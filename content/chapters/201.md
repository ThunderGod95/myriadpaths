# Chapter 201: Righteous Sword Palace

Ignoring the Minister of Works' pleas to stay, Li Qi left directly.

Everything was temporarily proceeding according to his arrangements.

The Vast Yang Minister of Works was cowardly and prized his life, but he wasn't genuinely stupid.

If he had tried to recruit a person like this, the man would never have believed him, nor would he have lifted a finger to help.

Thus, he stopped at just the right point.

First, he pointed out his own confidence and displayed his attitude. The other party would certainly look down on him, but simultaneously, they would fill in the blanks with their own overactive imagination.

Leaving things unsaid was the most effective approach.

The title "Young Master of the Shaman God Mountain" was enough to let this man conjure up all sorts of scenarios in his head.

Then, he told him some facts about Monk Chanzhi, mixing in indistinguishable lies.

For example, Li Qi said Chanzhi knew the Minister of Works' location, making his hiding meaningless.

Was it true?

Who knew.

In any case, the Minister of Works couldn't verify it. Weak, paranoid people who prized their lives feared this kind of uncertainty the most.

Coupled with his final, abrupt departure.

And the fact that the Minister of Works had no idea how Li Qi had found him in the first place.

Combining these two elements with Li Qi's rhetoric was enough to make a cowardly, life-preserving, yet cunning man scare himself silly, forcing him to agonize over it for a long time.

Li Qi was highly confident that this person would come seeking him out later.

Of course, if he didn't come, Li Qi had other methods.

Now, out of the four factions he had previously outlined, two would side with him.

Namely, the Vast Yang local faction, and Wang Boyan, who belonged to the Human Dao.

The remaining two factions were Monk Chanzhi and the Jianghu forces led by the Righteous Sword Palace.

Monk Chanzhi was an enemy, naturally requiring no further explanation.

And as for the Jianghu forces led by the Righteous Sword Palace, their time was coming soon too.

Li Qi returned to the secret manor of the Lower Nine Sects.

He sat down beside Shen Shuibi.

Shen Shuibi was zoning out. However, upon seeing Li Qi return, she tilted her head and spared a sliver of her attention. "You're back? Handled everything?"

"Only the Righteous Sword Palace is left. However, I left a letter for Zhang Lan before I departed; it shouldn't be long now." Li Qi nodded, then lay down next to the rabbit and stretched lazily.

The rabbit also flopped backward, lying next to Li Qi.

Rolling onto her side, she looked at him and said, "By the way, regarding that strand of Heavenly Devil dao rhythm... I thought about it for a long time and searched through many records. Although I couldn't find anything to confirm it, whatever desires a mortal holds in their heart will often manifest in their dreams. This is the definitive proof of delusion and bewilderment."

"While you are keeping that thing deeply hidden, be careful of your dreams."

"Alright, I'll be careful." Li Qi committed her words to heart.

Regarding that Heavenly Devil dao rhythm, he couldn't be too careful.

"So, are you free now?" Shen Shuibi asked again.

"Nothing pressing for now. I can also take a brief break from Monk Chanzhi's side," Li Qi answered.

"I see." Hearing this, the rabbit fell silent and closed her eyes.

She was getting ready to sleep.

Li Qi also let out a long sigh of relief. It was indeed time to rest.

Therefore, Li Qi—who hadn't slept a single minute over the past ten-odd days—grabbed a quilt, covered them both, and fell asleep.

Meanwhile, his true body inside the five-story Buddhist Pagoda naturally fell asleep as well.

A long-overdue rest.

In the hazy state of his slumber, Li Qi faintly felt his body become light and buoyant.

But this was considered a normal phenomenon.

He slept for a full eight to ten hours until he woke up naturally.

After a good sleep, he felt completely refreshed.

By the time he got up, the rabbit had long since awoken and was meditating nearby.

Her posture was quite elegant—sitting cross-legged with both hands resting on her knees.

Li Qi knew this was a part of her cultivation, known as "Basking in the Sun's Warmth."

On a sunny and windless day, one would sit by a south-facing window with their back to the sunlight. The slight warmth on the spine could make the entire body feel smooth and comfortable. The sun was the essence of the supreme yang; its light bolstered a person's Yang qi and provided immense nourishment.

Gathering Yang qi... was she preparing to condense her Yang Spirit?

Never mind, he wouldn't disturb her.

Li Qi then got out of bed as well.

Calculating the time, they should be arriving soon.

After rising, his morning ablutions went without saying. The attendants of the Lower Nine Sects had even prepared Jiliang water for him to bathe in.

Jiliang water drew upon the precious nature of the Five Grains, infused with various high-grade incenses, and brewed from the essence qi of the Five Grains. In various bathing recipes, its fragrance could clear the orifices and dredge the primordial qi. Therefore, those who bathed with it could cleanse their dirt without draining their essence qi, making it naturally superior to other methods.

It was quite refined.

Of course, Li Qi usually didn't care about such things. He was accustomed to braving the wind and sleeping in the dew, completely unbothered. At most, he would just wash with clean water and be done with it.

But now that he was in the city and these people had started to fawn over him, the extravagant displays multiplied.

Not only was the water refined, but so were other things.

Take the food, for example.

The local palate in Vast Yang favored sweetness. Therefore, daily meals were never short of jujubes, chestnuts, maltose, and honey to sweeten the dishes. According to the chef, this was called: "Sweetness to please the spleen's nature, smoothness to soothe the spleen's yang, and richness to nourish the spleen's yin. Consuming this is equivalent to taking medicinal food."

Today's breakfast was exactly like this. The morning meal consisted of deer tail congee, accompanied by a few side dishes.

The deer tail was of top quality, air-dried with the fat and membrane removed. Inside was coagulated blood resembling tender liver—a single glance proved it was a delicacy. It was finely chopped and boiled into congee, resulting in a dish that was clear but not greasy, carrying a uniquely charming aroma.

Another side dish was large jujubes soaked in sugarcane syrup. Eating them nourished the spleen's qi, balanced the stomach's qi, moistened the lungs to stop coughs, replenished the five viscera, and harmonized all medicines. They were as tender as egg whites, with the boiled syrup being exceptionally sweet, perfectly quenching one's thirst and restlessness.

With the food being so meticulous, the post-meal routine was even more so.

After eating, tiny food residues would linger between the teeth, causing the most distress to one's dental health. The Lower Nine Sects specifically provided toothpicks carved from willow wood and strung with tiger whiskers, along with strongly brewed tea. Once cooled, the tea was used to repeatedly rinse and flush the mouth.

If Li Qi hadn't strongly demanded that they not send anyone to serve him, his clothing, food, housing, and transportation would have all been managed by dedicated servants. He'd probably have a few beauties just to pick his teeth after meals.

Unfortunately, Li Qi had little interest in such things.

He was too lazy to even use the prepared items. Like the toothpicks—food didn't even get stuck in his teeth, so why would he use a toothpick?

After finishing his meal and rinsing his mouth, he lay in the courtyard with his eyes closed to rest his mind.

In truth, he wasn't resting his mind, but rather freeing up his vital energy for his true body.

On the other side, his true body climbed back up and resumed researching Monk Chanzhi's devils and devilish thoughts.

Every bit of research meant a deeper understanding of Chanzhi.

Li Qi was making preparations in full swing.

Vast Yang City also seemed to have returned to normal. People went about their daily lives as usual.

The blood sacrifice and the invasion of the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest seemed to have absolutely nothing to do with these commoners.

Li Qi's routine continued for another day.

Then, finally, someone came to his door.

Still in the secret manor of the Lower Nine Sects, Li Qi—who had been lying there like a corpse—suddenly opened his eyes.

A guard suddenly rushed in from outside. Dropping to one knee and cupping his hands, he reported to Li Qi, "My Lord! Following your description of the dragon colt, we've found the person! He is currently waiting outside!"

Yes, over the past few days, Li Qi had been mobilizing the forces of the Lower Nine Sects to search for Old Ma.

Old Ma was the beacon Li Qi had left for Zhang Lan. With Old Ma found, locating Zhang Lan would be easy.

When he had made that decision back then, Li Qi had already planned out this step.

"Bring him in quickly." Li Qi's split soul stood up. Saying this, he didn't wait for them to be invited in, but instead strode outside himself.

After waiting for so long! Zhang Lan had finally arrived!

However, before he could go far—having only taken two or three steps out the door—Li Qi heard the _clippety-clop, clippety-clop_ of hooves.

It was the crisp sound of horse hooves striking the stone slabs.

Then, a loud whinny rang out.

Li Qi's heart leaped with joy.

This was Old Ma's voice; he was incredibly familiar with it.

Thus, he stopped and stood by the door.

Soon, he saw Zhang Lan leading Old Ma around the corner.

Upon seeing Li Qi, Zhang Lan's face was filled with surprise.

But Old Ma wasn't surprised; he was ecstatic.

His long horse face twisted into a joyful grin. Breaking free from Zhang Lan's reins, he accelerated and charged straight at Li Qi.

Seeing this, Li Qi broke into a wide smile.

_As expected of the horse I raised myself. He's just so affectionate!_

Li Qi stood in place, waiting for Old Ma.

Old Ma charged over with the speed of lightning, wisps of azure thunder coiling around his body—the physical manifestation of Old Ma's acceleration.

Li Qi smiled, standing firmly by the door while waving his hand, signaling the surrounding members of the Lower Nine Sects to step back.

The people from the Lower Nine Sects all withdrew.

Then, Old Ma cast a sidelong glance at Li Qi. Without slowing down in the slightest, he directly slammed into Li Qi, who was blocking the doorway, sending him flying.

Zhang Lan stopped in his tracks and covered his eyes.

_That looked painful._

Li Qi's golden body instantly shattered! He was sent flying, completely charred black by the azure thunder radiating from Old Ma!

If it were his true body, this would at most have made him look disheveled. But his split soul had absolutely no resistance. It was knocked flying by Old Ma. Fortunately, Old Ma hadn't used his full strength, so the damage was still repairable.

As for Old Ma, after knocking Li Qi out of the way, he wiggled his rump and trotted inside to find Shen Shuibi.

Woken by the commotion outside, the rabbit roused from her meditative state.

She looked at the charging Old Ma.

Old Ma hastily braked, coming to a halt in front of the bed.

Then, she pulled a basket of carrots out of her mustard seed pouch.

One carrot for herself, and a whole basket for Old Ma.

Old Ma raised his head and whinnied happily as Shen Shuibi stroked his mane.

And so, one horse and one rabbit began munching on carrots right there on the spot.

They actually looked incredibly happy!

Zhang Lan couldn't contain his amusement. Laughing heartily, he stepped forward and helped Li Qi up from the ground. "Haha, Brother Li, it seems you haven't been taking care of your horse much lately, huh? Has Miss Shen been doing all the work for you?"

Li Qi's golden body naturally mended itself, quickly returning to normal.

After all, he was formed purely from the incense fire qi and lacked an actual physical body. As long as that core wisp of incense fire wasn't damaged, it was only a minor injury.

Li Qi could only laugh awkwardly. "Hahaha... I've been busy lately, never quite able to find the free time. Hahaha."

However, he brushed himself off and gave two dry coughs. "_Ahem_, alright, let's not talk about that, let's not talk about that. Brother Zhang, have you read what I wrote in the letter?"

When the conversation shifted to business, Zhang Lan lost the mood to tease Li Qi.

He replied, "The commoners have been settled in the minor world. I've arranged for the White Rhino Army to break up into smaller units and disperse among the populace."

"Brother Li's thoughts are meticulous, and your reasoning is incredibly sound. I've completed all the remaining arrangements you requested. But I just don't understand—why didn't you let me go seek the Chief Swordmaster?"

"Our adversary is a Rank 7. If we don't seek an equivalent Rank 7 to aid us in battle, we stand absolutely no chance," Zhang Lan sighed.

The gap between Rank 7 and Rank 8 was simply too vast. Although it seemed like only a one-rank difference, when applied to a specific Dao path, it could be a difference of five or six realms.

Just like Martial Dao Rank 9 and Martial Dao Rank 8. Despite being separated by only one rank, there were actually three major realms between them: Tempering Intent, Tempering Heart, and Tempering Spirit.

One had to cross three major realms and then condense their Martial Dao divine will to be considered a true Rank 8.

The disparity was far more complex than just a single rank.

Ranks were merely convenient classifications; one had to clearly recognize the monumental gaps between them.

Yet Li Qi said, "Don't ask, Brother Zhang. It's best not to notify the Righteous Sword Palace right now. I understand your concerns, but now is not the time."

"Why isn't it the time? I'm afraid you don't know the strength of my Righteous Sword Palace's Chief Swordmaster. His cultivation has reached Martial Dao Rank 7. He has achieved the unification of Heart, Body, Spirit, Intent, and Technique. Although he is still one step—the Unification of the Personal Cosmos—away from Rank 6, he is absolutely a powerhouse among Rank 7s. It's just one monk; if he comes, there definitely won't be a problem," Zhang Lan said confidently.

"How does the Righteous Sword Palace's Chief Swordmaster compare to the Minister of War?" Li Qi asked.

"Even if he held back thirty percent of his strength, he could still take him down." Zhang Lan raised his head, brimming with pride. "If all the swordmasters gathered and formed the Starry Dipper Sword Array, even two Ministers of War attacking together wouldn't be a match for us!"

"Then let me tell you this: three experts on the level of the Minister of War besieged that monk together. The outcome was one dead and two fleeing. The dead one was even refined into a puppet. Furthermore, that monk emerged completely unscathed, without a single scratch on him. Are you still planning to call that Chief Swordmaster over?" Li Qi stated flatly.

As soon as these words were spoken, the atmosphere instantly chilled.

Zhang Lan's expression turned serious. He pondered for a moment, then shook his head in disbelief. "Impossible. They are both Rank 7. No matter how strong he is, there should be a limit!"

"Then how would you rate my strength among Rank 9s? How many Rank 9s could I kill at once?" Li Qi asked.

Zhang Lan replied, "Brother Li, you've got it wrong. The Rank 9 realm is too low, and the power levels vary wildly. It's perfectly normal for there to be a massive gap between cultivators with orthodox methods and eclectic rogue cultivators. But by Rank 7, most eclectic cultivators have been weeded out. Everyone has a strong backing, so the gap wouldn't be that massive."

Yet Li Qi countered with a question, "What if what you consider an orthodox method is also just eclectic in his eyes? From the perspective of that monk's Daoist Tradition, do you think your methods—which can only take you to Rank 6 or Rank 5—are considered advanced?"

This question stopped Zhang Lan dead in his tracks.

It seemed he had never considered this question before.

He had traveled the Jianghu, wandering through dozens of kingdoms. Across distances spanning hundreds of thousands of miles, the Righteous Sword Palace's methods might not be the absolute pinnacle, but they were at least upper-middle tier. No matter where they went, they were a faction worthy of respect.

But...

What Li Qi said actually made a lot of sense.

"That monk's background has already been verified. He directly inherited the lineage of Mahakala. To him, an ordinary Rank 7 is nothing but free food delivered to his doorstep," Li Qi said.

There was one more thing Li Qi hadn't mentioned: the terrifying battle record of fighting one against three, killing one and routing two, was achieved while Monk Chanzhi was plagued by devilish thoughts and in exceptionally poor condition.

In other words, unless Li Qi could bring in a Rank 6, casually asking a Rank 7 for help was just sending them to their death.

The Minister of Works was a local, and Monk Chanzhi already knew of his existence. Therefore, it didn't matter if Li Qi sought him out or not; he was already part of the board from the beginning.

But the people of the Righteous Sword Palace were outsiders. If he rashly dragged outsiders in, the commotion of a Rank 7's descent would not only alert Chanzhi, but Li Qi was even more afraid that their arrival would be completely useless.

For this reason, Li Qi had no intention of summoning any outside Rank 7s.

Zhang Lan gritted his teeth. "Then what if the Palace Master comes? That should work, right?"
