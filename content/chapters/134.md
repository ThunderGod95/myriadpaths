# Chapter 134: Rank 9

“So, that’s basically what happened. Which means we can go home,” Li Qi said with a shrug, spreading his hands to Shen Shuibi and Bai Di.

The three of them were sitting face to face in their hotel room, looking at one another.

It had been two hours since the incident. Li Qi had returned to his room to find the two of them waiting for him.

Shen Shuibi seemed to have expected this outcome. “Mm. So, are we leaving?”

Bai Di, however, stood up and bowed so low his body almost folded in half. “Bai Di thanks you, Young Master!”

He then straightened up and said, “Young Master’s Dao path is a clarion call that has enlightened me greatly. This is the only way I can express my gratitude. If there is ever a time you have need of me, please do not hesitate to ask. I will surely come to your aid.”

“Brother Bai, why such ceremony?” Li Qi asked, perplexed, as he quickly stood to help him up.

Li Qi rubbed his nose, a little embarrassed.

He hadn’t quite understood what kind of revelation his words had given the other man, so the sudden grand gesture had caught him off guard.

“Er… what happened?” Li Qi asked, feeling awkward.

“Young Master’s determination to seek the Dao is profound. Your journey of contemplating the path has been a sudden awakening for me. My previous confusion has been swept away, and I have finally made my decision,” Bai Di said, his eyes firm.

“I don’t know what you’ve decided, but… good luck,” Li Qi encouraged awkwardly.

“I will not fail!” Bai Di replied with conviction.

This made Li Qi a little nervous.

_I didn’t mislead this kid, did I?_

Regardless, their business in the Earth Oil World had come to an end.

From the final conversation between his teacher and the sovereign of Great Deer, Li Qi could tell that his teacher had used his actions to send a message to the sovereign, warning him not to be indecisive in his dealings with the Tang Empire.

His training was complete, his objective achieved. It was time to go.

As for the Earth Oil World…

The Heavenly Dao had its own way of operating. He had already left them a thread of life. What he could do was done.

So, it was time for sleep.

He would rest, and then it would be time to return to All Under Heaven.

The three of them dispersed. Shen Shuibi stretched lazily, the rabbit looking rather tired.

Just as Li Qi was leaving, he suddenly turned back and called out, “Miss Shen.”

“Hm? What is it?” Shen Shuibi tilted her head, looking at Li Qi with a blank expression.

“The way the Heavenly Dao operates, even I could figure it out after observing the cultures of a few worlds. It’s clearly not that profound. So, what is it that those of higher ranks contemplate?” Li Qi asked.

It was true. After his experiences on Earth, in All Under Heaven, and in the Earth Oil World, combined with his extensive knowledge from books, even Li Qi could deduce the impartial workings of the Heavenly Dao.

So, what more profound truths were those of higher rank contemplating?

Upon hearing this question, Shen Shuibi smiled beautifully and answered with a question of her own, “Shaman God Mountain stands at the edge of the world, so high it cannot be seen. To discern its shape from afar, is it difficult or easy? To enter the mountain and perceive its deepest subtleties and mysteries, is it difficult or easy?”

Li Qi was instantly enlightened. He bowed in thanks. “Thank you for your instruction, miss.”

What Shen Shuibi said was simple. The distant Shaman God Mountain stood at the far end of the land. Was it easier to see its silhouette from afar, or to enter the mountain and discern every single stone?

This made Li Qi immediately understand his current state.

He had merely been gazing from a distance, observing the outline of the Dao.

He was still far from truly understanding it.

The next day, Li Qi and the others left the Earth Oil World.

Before their departure, the heretical Human Dao methods there had been abolished. It seemed the Great Deer Kingdom truly intended to change its ways.

But that no longer had anything to do with Li Qi.

The only thing that gave him a slight pang of concern was the Child of Destiny.

He wondered if she would have the same good fortune as him now that she was in the outer realms.

But he put those thoughts aside.

After bidding farewell to the Earth Oil World, Li Qi and his companions returned to All Under Heaven and set off once more.

Old Ma had been well-fed and was now strong and stout, with dragon scales beginning to appear on his body. It seemed the grooms of White Snake Prefecture had taken good care of him.

He was overjoyed to see Li Qi, trotting up with his rear end swaying. But when he heard that they were leaving, his face fell, and he stubbornly braced himself against the doorframe, refusing to budge.

Of course. Here he had delicious food and drink every day, and even mares. Who would want to travel three million li?

However, seeing that he couldn't pull the horse, Li Qi actually stopped trying. He patted the horse, telling him to just stay here and enjoy his blessings, while he himself returned to his sect.

And then he left.

Fortunately, Old Ma came chasing after him a moment later.

It seemed that Li Qi was more important to him than good food and drink.

Li Qi, as if he had expected it all along, laughed heartily, then swung himself onto Old Ma’s back. With Shen Shuibi seated behind him, he cracked the whip and bid farewell to Bai Di and the Governor who had come to see them off, galloping towards the horizon.

---

Their journey through White Snake Prefecture was unimpeded, and even the surrounding counties dared not bar their way.

There were a few minor incidents along the way, but with two Rank 9s, Li Qi and Shen Shuibi, they were considered powerful in the mortal world. At the very least, the local Jianghu sects and gangs were no match for them.

Thus, their journey was peaceful. As winter drew to a close, the Azure Dragon was about to raise its head once more.

An entire year had passed since Li Qi had left the Li River to search for Lady Luofu.

He had already performed the ritual for the austere autumn, the so-called 'Golden Autumn' of the White Tiger, the season when all things wither.

And now, in the last month of the year, Li Qi was performing the final ritual.

_In the last month of winter, the sun is in the constellation Wunu. The constellation Lou is visible at dusk, and the constellation Di is visible at dawn. The days are Ren and Gui, the sovereign emperor is Zhuanxu, the attendant god is Xuanming. The creature of the season is the tortoise, the note is yu, and the pitch-pipe is dalü. Its number is six, its taste is salty, its smell is of decay. The rite to be performed is the Rite of Travel, and the kidney is the honored offering._

_In this month, the sun completes its course, the moon completes its cycle, and the stars return to their celestial positions. The count of days is near its end, and the year is about to begin anew._

Therefore, the ritual of this day was of particular importance.

Li Qi offered the Black Huang to the north, offered kidneys and stomachs to revere the shelled creatures, and a black silk cloth eighteen feet long as a supplementary offering.

The ritual was complete.

Li Qi felt the final moments of the year pass. He looked out at the snow-covered land and sighed, "At the winter solstice, the Big Dipper points due north, aligning with the central string. Yin qi reaches its extreme, and Yang qi begins to sprout... It really is exactly as described."

At this hour, the Northern Dipper pointed to the Zi-Chen position, aligning with the prime meridian. At this time, yin qi reached its extreme, and yang qi began to stir, just like the principle of yin and yang's rotation.

This was the turning of the year.

This was the end of one annual cycle and the beginning of another.

The earth was silent and still, but new life was gestating beneath, waiting for the return of yang qi, for the vibrant season of spring.

Shen Shuibi sat quietly behind Li Qi, waiting for the ritual to be completed.

Soon, the year would be over.

He had gathered the qi of the four seasons. Within his personal cosmos, the four seasons were now distinct. The world was in its nascent stage.

It was time to enter the ranks of the Shaman Dao.

Li Qi gazed out at the vast land.

He was on a mountain, maintaining the ritual, waiting for the completion of the "heavenly cycle."

The time had come.

As the stars completed their circuit around the heavens, Li Qi suddenly felt a jolt run through his body. He stared at the scene before him in disbelief.

The world had changed.

The entire world seemed to be undergoing a dramatic transformation.

Pure qi churned and coiled, then suddenly scattered and flowed like a phoenix taking flight, like a dragon ascending into the clouds.

Turbid qi sank and settled, filling every space, revealing mountains and rocks that manifested in all eight directions.

The various types of qi that had been intertwined for a whole year suddenly separated, returning to their original states.

The pure and turbid of heaven and earth were divided, the primordial chaos was split, north and south were established, east and west were set, and the four dimensions were illuminated.

This scene, while shocking Li Qi, also triggered a change within his own personal cosmos.

The ten thousand types of qi, each distinct, were said to manifest as phenomena in the heavens—the sun, moon, and stars; to form shapes on the earth—the Five Great Mountains and the Four Great Rivers; and to form life in humanity—the heart, liver, and the five viscera.

As heaven and earth were renewed, Li Qi’s personal cosmos, catalyzed by the qi of the four seasons, also naturally evolved.

Li Qi knew the time had come.

He closed his eyes and looked inward.

His personal cosmos was empty, a world of chaos and darkness.

The landscape was a vast, bottomless expanse, a chamber of profound gloom, a light of endless emptiness. There was no earth below, no sky above. There was nothing to see, nothing to hear. Beyond it lay a boundless ocean, impossible to discern.

Then, he drew upon the dual qi of yin and yang.

The yin and yang qi entered his body, giving birth to a sun and moon within, which illuminated the darkness below.

For the first time, light appeared in his personal cosmos.

Then, he drew the essence of heaven from above and the essence of earth from below.

And then, all manner of qi began to surge in.

The qi of the four seasons was born. The internal qi from his Martial Dao cultivation flowed forth. The qi of the five elements, represented by the five viscera, appeared. The essence qi, pure qi, turbid qi, nutritive qi, and defensive qi within his body all manifested.

In his previously empty personal cosmos, various things began to take form, each born from a different qi.

The turbid qi became the earth, the pure qi became the sky.

The five elemental qi of the five viscera became the Five Great Mountains within his body, the mountains, rocks, and ley lines.

The flow of nutritive and defensive qi became the Four Great Rivers within his body, the water veins and root systems.

The divine qi of the Four Phenomena became the Four Phenomena of the heavens, the constellations and stars.

The internal qi from his Martial Dao filled the space, causing all things to thrive. Sweet dew fell from the heavens, and sweet springs welled up from the earth.

Though there was no life, only inanimate objects, it was a scene of burgeoning prosperity, the beginning of all things.

Li Qi knew his breakthrough was complete.

He opened his eyes and let out a long breath.

Rank 9. He had made it.

“Whew. With this, my divine abilities…” Li Qi felt the power within him.

He had truly created a small cosmos within his own body. From now on, he would no longer have to 'store' qi for use. Instead, he could generate different types of qi himself and use a variety of divine abilities.

His physical strength had become even more formidable, his stamina had increased, and his Shaman Dao cultivation had even merged with his Martial Dao cultivation, each complementing the other.

He even had a faint, indescribable feeling... that his 'status' had been elevated.

Not just entering the ranks, but more like a certain... cosmic standing?

It would now be even more difficult to divine his fate, though it had been difficult before.

Furthermore, things like curses, karma, and resentment would have a harder time latching onto him. If they were not strong enough, they would be naturally dissolved by the small cosmos within him.

"Congratulations. To enter the ranks in just one year, that's top-tier talent even on Shaman God Mountain. Very impressive," Shen Shuibi said with a smile, walking over to congratulate him now that he had regained his senses.

"Phew. I didn't expect this. This feeling… it's really…" Li Qi savored the memory of the moment he broke through. It felt like… he had just split heaven and earth.

"It's wondrous, isn't it? From now on, you have transcended the mortal world," Shen Shuibi said, covering her mouth with a giggle, as if she found Li Qi rather endearing.

"Why do you say I've only transcended the mortal world now? I was already at Rank 9 of the Martial Dao," Li Qi said, a little confused.

Shen Shuibi explained, "The Martial Dao is different. The Martial Dao and the Human Dao have no concept of transcending the mortal world. Even if they cultivate to Rank 5 or Rank 6, they are still within the mortal realm."

"I see. Then why is Rank 9 of the Shaman Dao considered transcending the mortal?" Li Qi asked.

Shen Shuibi explained, "The Human Dao and the Martial Dao cannot exist independently of the outside world. The Human Dao needs to gather the power of the masses, and the Martial Dao needs to constantly eat and temper the body. So no matter what, they need to exist and travel within the mortal world."

"But we of the Daoist Sects and you Shamans are different. We take ourselves as the center. When we reach a high level of cultivation, we can exist freely and eternally, without relying on the outside world. The personal cosmos can sustain itself in a cycle," Shen Shuibi said. "This is the difference between transcending the mortal and not. It's also the source of the conflict between the Human Dao and the Shaman Dao. Er, I remember the Human Dao people have always wanted to capture a few of the Great Shamans who have created their own worlds and use their personal cosmoses as resource worlds, because the output of such Great Shamans is astonishing."

"Because the Rank 2 of the Human Dao said that for a Great Shaman to absorb the qi of heaven and earth to create a self-sustaining cycle for their own use is too selfish. The Human Dao has always believed that a Dao that does not serve humanity is a heretical Dao, and a god that does not serve humanity is a wild god, and both should be slain…"

As Shen Shuibi spoke, she seemed to be getting a headache.

Li Qi wiped away a bead of sweat and nodded, indicating that he understood.

It seemed… the conflict between the Human Dao and the Shaman Dao was truly irreconcilable.

_Isn't that just robbery?_

The Shaman Dao seemed much more peaceful.

The Shaman Dao sought to maintain the purity of the natural environment, so that the nature spirits and all living things could grow in peace. They believed that humanity was a part of the universe, equal to all other things.

The path of the Invocator was just that, dedicated to communicating between heaven, earth, humanity, gods, and ghosts to maintain natural harmony.

From his reading, Li Qi already knew that the territories of Shaman God Mountain looked rather primitive, but it wasn't true primitivism. It was simply a lifestyle that maintained a balanced and peaceful attitude. In reality, they had everything they needed.

The Human Dao, on the other hand, needed to expand rapidly, to conquer nature and transform all things for human use. That was why they were in conflict with Shaman God Mountain, with the nations of the Baiyue’s Hundred Thousand Mountains as their battlefield.

"Let's not talk about this. Let's just keep moving. We've been on the road for almost a year. I've practiced my spell arts to about thirty or forty percent proficiency, and I've entered the ranks. Let's find a place to rest," Li Qi said, shaking his head, pushing aside thoughts of the Dao conflict.

For now, it had nothing to do with him.
