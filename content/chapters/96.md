# Chapter 96: Skill and Dao

Within the encampment of the Jing family's chamber of commerce caravan.

A piece of news spread like wildfire.

"What? That tigress made a wager with someone? With that newly arrived young hero?"

"Ah? Someone actually dared to argue with that tigress? Oh, he's also a ranked expert, right? We're in for a good show."

"Did you hear? Apparently, that woman's sword was snapped in half. He even called her proud Sword Dao a mere minor skill, infuriating that tigress half to death!"

However, contrary to Li Qi's expectations of being isolated by the entire caravan for provoking their original guard, everyone in the Jing family's chamber of commerce seemed to be taking pleasure in her misfortune. Some even ran over to cheer Li Qi on.

He instantly transformed from an unknown guard hired by the manager into everyone's hero.

He even hallucinated hearing someone shout, "Don't lose! Big brother with the long blade!"

_Damn it, just how terrible was Jing Yan's popularity usually?_

Given her incredibly stubborn attitude towards swordsmanship, she was likely quite arrogant on a normal basis. Even Manager Jing had described her as such: "Her mother died early, and her father was exceedingly strict, resulting in a withdrawn personality. Aside from practicing martial arts, she has almost no other hobbies."

Fortunately, despite being withdrawn, she wasn't unreasonable. Therefore, to prove the worth of her cherished swordsmanship, she had agreed to Li Qi's wager.

What was the wager?

The situation at the time went like this.

"A wager? Wager what?" Jing Yan pulled back her hand, eyeing Li Qi with suspicion.

"I have already said that my little sister is not without a cultivation base; she simply isn't adept at combat. However, the path she cultivates excels at seeing through weaknesses and flaws. Why don't you fetch a new sword and demonstrate your swordsmanship... your Sword Dao, for my little sister? I will return and rest for the night. Tomorrow, we will test it. If I can break your technique, it will prove whether or not my little sister was speaking arrogant nonsense. What do you think, Elder Sister Jing?"

"If I lose, I will certainly have her offer you a formal apology, Elder Sister Jing. I hope you will be magnanimous and merely issue a light punishment, treating it as a lesson for a child so she will not dare speak such nonsense again."

"If I win, it will prove my little sister's words were true. I won't ask for an apology. Instead, I will present you with the flaws we uncovered to help elevate your martial art to the next level. How does that sound?"

Li Qi's words were clear and logical, his attitude neither humble nor arrogant. This caused Jing Yan to view him in a slightly better light.

Therefore, she didn't refuse. Instead, she asked, "Will you use a blade or your fists?"

"How could flesh and blood block a weapon? Naturally, I will use a blade," Li Qi replied.

"Your blade work is far inferior to your unarmed combat," Jing Yan said, a hint of mockery in her voice.

Li Qi smiled helplessly.

It couldn't be helped. He didn't actually know any blade techniques. Even with the Carp Stream Intent, he only knew the training method, not the fighting method, nor did he know its accompanying weapon cultivation method.

It wasn't that Shen Shuibi was hiding secrets from him; it was simply because she didn't know them either.

For a technique that was practically used to prop up wobbly tables on Mount Luofu, her taking the time to learn the training method already meant she had too much free time. A fighting method? Did she even need one?

As a dignified Rank 5, why would she ever need to use the fighting method of a Rank 8 cultivation method?

As a result, Li Qi was still using the Wave-Shattering Force's fighting method. Relying on the progress bar and the instantaneous explosive power of the Carp Stream Intent, he forcefully interrupted his opponent's movements. This was the only reason he had earned the praise of having "decent unarmed combat methods."

As for his blade work...

Let's just say that when Li Qi used a blade, even if he saw the other person's progress bar, he couldn't interrupt their attack.

To him, this long blade was roughly equivalent to a wood chopping knife with a sharpness enchantment. It provided a bit of extra attack power at crucial moments, but most of the time, it was just a fire poker or a walking stick.

As for why he constantly carried it in his hand?

To show off, of course.

It also served as a convenient misdirection, making others believe he was a swordsman. Then, at a critical juncture, he could whip out his willow branch and lash them—guaranteed to be highly effective.

However, helplessness aside, Li Qi still cupped his hands and answered, "Naturally, it is better to use a blade. If my clumsy blade work can break your Sword Dao, wouldn't it serve as even greater proof that my little sister's words were true?"

"Fine! Then I will demonstrate it for you once! Tell your little sister to watch closely! If you lose, I want her to kneel and apologize!" Jing Yan declared arrogantly. She then had someone fetch an ordinary longsword and began demonstrating her Sword Dao.

Shen Shuibi had listened to the entire conversation from the sidelines and understood her responsibility. Moreover, since she was the cause of this incident, she naturally watched the demonstration with the utmost seriousness.

Honestly, Li Qi had never seen the rabbit look so serious before.

That was how the situation had unfolded at the time. Once the wager was set, the caravan couldn't delay their schedule just for this, so they departed that evening as planned.

Li Qi even found time to check on Old Ma one last time. He discovered the beast was eating well, sleeping soundly, and living in unparalleled lazy comfort. He munched on fruits in the morning, grains at noon, supplemented with forage and beans in the afternoon, and even enjoyed different midnight snacks.

Li Qi had to top up his tab with another thousand copper coins. Because the horse's appetite was so massive, the prepaid amount was no longer sufficient.

Forget it. The goal was to let him fatten up anyway, so this was fine.

After taking care of this matter, he hurried back to depart with the caravan. Once on the ox cart, he began conversing with Shen Shuibi.

Since he was a Rank 9 expert, the caravan specifically cleared out an ox cart for Li Qi and Shen Shuibi.

Well, not entirely cleared out—there were still some goods on the cart—but unlike the others who were crammed together, their cart was far more spacious.

"Tomorrow, I will definitely help you defeat that braggart!" Shen Shuibi said viciously, waving her tiny fists, her face full of indignation.

Though 'vicious,' she actually just looked incredibly cute. After all, she completely lacked expressions of killing intent or hatred. She looked more like a wronged child holding in her grievances.

"Alright, alright, I will definitely make her apologize to you! However, what did you manage to see today?" Li Qi asked.

"It was nothing but flaws! Nothing but weaknesses! There were no redeeming qualities whatsoever, yet she dares call it a Sword Dao... Does a mere thing like a sword deserve to be called a Dao?" At this point, Shen Shuibi grew even angrier.

"It is merely a man-made implement. Just because many people wield swords, they dare falsely claim it is a Dao? It is a minor skill!" Shen Shuibi stated in extreme displeasure. "A Dao must possess representative concepts, a comprehensive system of thought, and a method to achieve its ideological goals. It must possess its own unique theories and analysis regarding heaven and earth, all creation, and all living beings. Only then can it be considered a Dao!"

"How can anyone just dare to call anything a Dao! Really, I'm furious!" Shen Shuibi even stomped her feet. This was the first time Li Qi had ever seen her so angry.

She was even more furious than when he had petted her rabbit ears.

It seemed that someone showing contempt for the concept of "Dao" truly provoked her profound dissatisfaction.

However... she made a good point.

In Li Qi's understanding, the "Dao" represented rules; it was the natural law governing the operation of heaven and earth.

A sword was nothing more than a man-made weapon. The ability to wield a sword could, at best, be called swordsmanship. Vainly attempting to label it a Sword Dao was simply an insult to the word "Dao."
