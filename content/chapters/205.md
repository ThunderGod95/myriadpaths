# Chapter 205: Killing the Demonic Monk (2)

A sword struck out.

Like a great river surging from the west to shatter mountain ridges, roaring across ten thousand miles to strike the Dragon Gate, the waves arrived first!

Killing intent surged south, shaking the very axis of the earth. A single breath bound all of creation, stripping it of form. A Heavenly Soldier descended to strike and behead the demonic monk!

Without a shred of hesitation, holding nothing back, the formation-empowered Zhang Lan unleashed the ultimate sword strike!

He had completely abandoned his defense.

Having prepared for a full month, condensing his qi and blood for just as long, he erupted with a power that surpassed all limits!

Over a month ago, when Zhang Lan and Li Qi first met, he had faced two Rank 8 experts alone.

In that battle, he had only used sword qi and never drew the blade from its scabbard.

At that time, Zhang Lan had said, "I will make my move using the methods of the Daoist Sects I cultivate on the side, causing them to misjudge my strength. At the critical moment, I will use my Martial Dao cultivation to close the distance and draw my sword. This is my sliver of hope for survival. I will kill one first, then fight the other to the death. By my estimation, I have a thirty percent chance of turning the tables and saving these over a hundred thousand people. Moreover, I am certain of perishing with both of them."

From that moment on, Li Qi had anticipated Zhang Lan's explosive sword draw.

Zhang Lan's sword was the sword of a knight-errant.

The sword of a knight-errant harbored no regrets once drawn, courting death with no thought of survival.

Pressing forward with unmatched momentum, what could it not overcome? Eradicating the root of all evil, it sought to grant Vast Yang a lasting peace!

Having exerted too much force, the massive sword qi grew increasingly heavy under the formation's empowerment. The pressure made his bones tremble, his skin split, and his muscles tear apart from the inside.

Even his heart and lungs showed signs of failing as he gasped endlessly in deep, ragged breaths.

Yet, the sword qi was unbelievably fierce!

The boundless sword intent pierced through all the devilish qi coiling around Monk Chanzhi! It aimed to sever the demonic monk's head.

However, Chanzhi merely smiled.

He did not even pay attention to the sword qi. Instead, he shook his head. "The sword of a knight-errant—with disheveled hair and bristling temples, glaring eyes and struggling speech, clashing in the vanguard, severing necks above and rupturing livers and lungs below. It bears the name of a knight-errant, but it is truly the sword of a brute. It is no different from a fighting cock. Once its life is extinguished, it becomes useless."

"I have a Buddhist Kingdom surrounding me. Can the sword of a brute slay a kingdom?"

As Monk Chanzhi finished speaking, the space around him began to expand.

The Buddhist Kingdom within his heart manifested. Perhaps the lands of the Buddha were impure and uneven, arising in such a state from the vexations of all living beings.

The Buddhist Kingdom expanded, enveloping the three inches of space around Monk Chanzhi.

A three-inch Buddhist Kingdom seemed as though it could be broken with a single finger.

However, looking closely, one could see the vast and sweeping manifestations of a world appearing within it.

This three-inch space seemed to gather three thousand worlds. Within it lay various mountains—snow mountains, steep peaks, fragrant hills, jewel mountains, golden mountains, and black mountains—forming a celestial mountain range.

There were also great rivers, flowing streams, and bubbling springs, collectively building a sea of golden light.

The sun, moon, stars, heavenly palaces, and the divine shrines of exalted deities all appeared within the three-inch Buddhist Kingdom. Within this kingdom were the Buddhas of the ten directions. As the Buddhas preached the dharma, monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen all gathered to sit and listen.

A city of Dharma Protectors, established by the majestic divine power of the Buddhas, sat at the outer edge of the three-inch Buddhist Kingdom.

Inside the city of Dharma Protectors were throngs of heavenly beings. They solemnly held various weapons, riding all manner of celestial mounts. They stood in majestic formation, clad in magnificent, jewel-adorned celestial armor and wielding precious staves, guarding the Buddhist Kingdom.

Zhang Lan's resolute sword qi broke through the surrounding devilish qi, slaying the devils, and finally struck the gates of the three-inch Buddhist Kingdom.

Then, the sword qi pierced the Buddhist Kingdom.

A massive boom echoed, resounding like thunder.

War drums shocked the mountains, threatening to topple them.

Sword intent shrouded the surroundings, transforming into violent winds and torrential rain!

The sword of a brute could slay a person.

But could it slay a kingdom?

Zhang Lan's entire body was split open, covered in blood. The sword qi entered the Buddhist Kingdom, wreaking wanton destruction.

The earth cracked open, mountain ranges were severed at their waists, the oceans parted, and the city of Dharma Protectors was cleaved apart by the sword qi.

The sword intent continuously expanded within the Buddhist Kingdom. Magma resembling blood constantly surged out, making it look as if the earth had been torn open and was bleeding.

The earth shook, and the mountains swayed. The colors of the world changed as the Buddhist Kingdom fractured.

The sword qi slithered erratically through the three-inch Buddhist Kingdom like silver snakes. Countless sharp, thread-like strands of sword qi continuously pierced into the kingdom, spreading relentlessly as they attempted to crush the city of Dharma Protectors.

These tendrils plowed the ground over and over. Every sizable rock and building ruptured and shattered into dust. The ceaseless explosive sounds were like raindrops hammering the earth, continuing without the slightest pause.

However—

When a torrential downpour strikes the earth, it appears incredibly imposing and might even trigger raging flash floods or mudslides.

But could it truly inflict any substantial damage to the earth itself?

Evidently, it was impossible.

Even though the Buddhist Kingdom had been battered and cracked, and countless heavenly beings had fallen, bringing the three-inch Buddhist Kingdom to the brink of crumbling by two inches, Chanzhi merely pinched his fingers together lightly and chanted in a low voice, "With this heart clear and translucent as dew, the inner light shines brightly, turning the ten directions into the color of Jambu-nada gold[^1]. This is dyed by the spiritual awakening of the heart and soul. The light of the mind shines brilliantly, illuminating all."

As soon as these words were spoken, the Buddhist light around him flared brilliantly. His devilish dharma avatar retracted, and in its place, a third dharma avatar emerged.

The third dharma avatar was a cross-legged Buddha statue. It possessed compassionate brows and kind eyes, radiating a gentle aura. A faint light filled the area around the gold-adorned statue.

Precious vases and golden bells hung all around it. When a gentle breeze blew, the bells and vases chimed in harmony. Their sonorous ringing could be heard for over ten miles.

In a short span of time, Monk Chanzhi had displayed three dharma avatars.

A Mahamayuri dharma avatar, a Heart-Eating Devil God dharma avatar, and now this Compassionate Buddha Heart dharma avatar.

The precious vase around the dharma avatar flew up and tilted toward the three-inch Buddhist Kingdom.

A drop of sweet dew flew out, falling into the Buddhist Kingdom.

That mere drop of sweet dew suddenly multiplied into hundreds of billions of droplets, enveloping the entire three-inch Buddhist Kingdom!

A sweet rain descended upon the world. Its water was delicious, possessing the eight merits[^2]. The moment it touched the ground, it vanished, soaking into the earth without causing any flooding.

The heavy rain fell, clearing away the clouds and mist. A cool breeze rose from the marshes. Its moisture drifted outward, blowing upon the populace. All who were touched by it experienced peace and joy, nourished by the sweet dampness.

Thus, the soil within the Buddhist Kingdom became fertile, vibrant, and glossy. It was like a well-tended worldly garden, sprinkled with water to make its flowers bright and beautiful.

Likewise, when the Buddhist light appeared in the world, all the land within this Buddhist Kingdom naturally became fertile, lush, and brimming with life, as if someone had coated it with fragrant oil, leaving it rich and deeply nourished.

The heavenly beings who had previously suffered due to the sword qi were also saved. Now moistened by the sweet rain, their former agony was eased.

The Buddhist Kingdom was completely restored.

Without a single trace of damage left behind.

However, the sword qi was at the end of its strength, lacking the power to thrust forward even another half-inch.

In this clash of methods, it was clearly an overwhelming victory for Monk Chanzhi.

The sword of a brute could not rival a kingdom.

The blade that severed all things was completely suppressed on a conceptual level when facing an entire kingdom, left without the slightest ability to fight back.

Having neutralized his opponent's desperate, formation-empowered sword strike, Monk Chanzhi no longer held back. He threw out another palm strike, aiming for Zhang Lan.

This palm was exactly the same as the one he had used earlier to carve open the mountain range.

He would slap this martial artist to death, snatch Shen Shuibi away, and then return to complete his business with Li Qi.

_Hmm... However, this was likely Li Qi's trap, wasn't it?_

Monk Chanzhi had already realized it.

Li Qi truly had not given up yet.

Deceiving him into coming out—did he really think an ambush like this could hurt him?

Did he think these two Rank 7s could pose a threat to him?

It was truly... a bit laughable.

Much like the temper tantrum of a child.

Then he would teach him a thorough lesson and let him know what a true Rank 7 was.

Rather than the motley Rank 7 before him, or the fake Rank 7 boosted by a formation.

However, that formation did have some merit.

_How was it done?_

As Monk Chanzhi struck at Zhang Lan, he felt a twinge of doubt in his heart.

Originally, this should have just been a grand formation for regulating the qi of the mountains and rivers. Although such formations were massive in scale, they were difficult to use for offense; their functions were fundamentally for civilian use.

They could regulate the weather and adjust the earth qi to ensure a bountiful harvest. They could also avert less severe natural disasters. Ordinary floods, earthquakes, blizzards, and the like could all be neutralized in advance by this kind of formation.

Of course, lightning tribulations, heavenly punishments, or disasters like the inversion of yin and yang were beyond its scope of control.

Therefore, aside from being 'large,' this formation had no redeeming qualities at all.

To regulate as vast an area as possible, this formation was designed to be extraordinarily massive. Just three of them could cover the territory of an entire province.

Thus, they were able to hide here. By mobilizing the earth qi in this place, the sheer scale of the power meant they only needed to disrupt a small fraction of it to easily mask any other qi. To the outside world, it would merely look like an ordinary fluctuation of earth qi.

But...

How could such a formation provide empowerment? It should have been impossible, unless someone had modified it.

However, modification required someone proficient in formations.

To accomplish a modification of this degree, their mastery over formations would have to be incredibly profound. Was it someone by Li Qi's side?

Monk Chanzhi pondered for a moment and felt it was unlikely.

How could someone with such mastery over formations still be at Rank 8? And there was no one above Rank 8 here, aside from that fake, and the Minister of Works who had long lost the will to fight and was currently fleeing for his life.

_Forget it. Thinking so much is useless._

In Vast Yang, there was nothing that could threaten him. No matter how many schemes they hatched, it was all futile.

In the face of absolute power, he would let his little friend Li Qi take a good look at his true strength.

He would make him truly give up hope, completely shattering his will to resist. Then, Li Qi would obediently work for him, contributing his knowledge and intellect to build a solid foundation for Chanzhi's path to Rank 6.

He would crush Li Qi's mind, sever all his fleeting hopes, and pulverize his pride. Using absolute strength, he would make this quick-witted genius understand—

Geniuses were called geniuses because they were not yet worthy of being called the strong.

Arrogance and pride in the name of a genius, relying on intellect to play games, and manipulating conspiracies—these actions were utterly meaningless before a true powerhouse.

While it seemed like a lot to process, these thoughts merely flashed through Monk Chanzhi's mind in an instant. The attack in his hand was not delayed in the slightest.

He threw his palm strike. Zhang Lan, already exhausted from executing that sword strike, had no ability to resist. It looked as if he was about to be smashed to pieces, dying without a burial site.

However, at the exact moment Monk Chanzhi unleashed that palm, another sword descended from the sky.

Monk Chanzhi's pupils shrank!

Where did a second sword come from?

A third Rank 7?

How could it be?!

But this was not a question of whether it was possible.

It was a reality that had indisputably occurred.

A third Rank 7 had appeared!

This suddenly appearing third Rank 7 was a... woman?

But Chanzhi quickly realized that this sudden sword thrust was not delivered by a person.

Not a person, but a dharma avatar!

Another dharma avatar whose strength had reached Rank 7 descended upon the scene.

It was a female dharma avatar, hundreds of feet tall. Ethereal mist coiled around her, bathed in the clear, cold moonlight. She possessed an elegant and solemn bearing, a cinnabar mark on her arm. Her very form seemed condensed from pure light and dew, a bright, untainted soul, coldly beautiful.

Behind her hung a pure white moon, gazing down upon the mortal world for a thousand years.

Monk Chanzhi prided himself on his vast experience and broad knowledge, but he genuinely could not recognize whose dharma avatar this was.

Generally speaking, a dharma avatar represented that a certain technique had been studied to its utmost depths and completely mastered. Only then could one mobilize the full power of that technique.

A dharma avatar was the physical manifestation of a dharma. Only by fully comprehending and integrating the technique could a dharma avatar naturally form.

In other words, if one failed to fully integrate it, a dharma avatar could not be formed.

Therefore, by observing a dharma avatar, one could glean information about the opponent's techniques. For example, seeing a Mahamayuri dharma avatar revealed that the technique originated from Mahamayuri.

But no matter how Chanzhi looked at it, he could neither find nor deduce the origin of this dharma avatar.

This meant the opponent's technique was an obscure, unknown minor art. That was why he did not recognize the resulting dharma avatar.

Although it was somewhat surprising, it was nothing more than that.

He shook his head. The three-inch Buddhist Kingdom still covered his body, and he paid no heed to that sword.

The extraordinarily brave sword of the knight-errant from before hadn't posed the slightest threat to him. Now, this ordinary dharma avatar was even less likely to hurt him.

His palm strike continued its descent.

Just as he was about to smash Zhang Lan to death, the sword thrust by that female dharma avatar reached Chanzhi's body.

The three-inch Buddhist Kingdom around him shattered, and the sword pierced into his flesh!

Monk Chanzhi's physical body was incredibly tough. Yet, this sword pierced straight through his flesh.

His attack was interrupted.

Monk Chanzhi was sent flying backward.

He tried to defend, but it was completely useless. He was pierced through again, his entire body blasting backward.

A sword strike from south to north destroyed all the defensive arrangements of the three-inch Buddhist Kingdom. The leaves of the seven treasures fell, the sweet dew dried up, and Monk Chanzhi's dharma avatar was shattered.

Chanzhi was furious.

He let out a low, angry shout.

"Benefactor Shen! This poor monk's patience is running out!"

"If you do not surrender without a fight, I fear it will be difficult to keep you in one piece."

Then, he reached out again, flipping his hand to press down!

Within that golden palm, a world manifested!

The three-inch Buddhist Kingdom compressed into a miniature realm, spanning the heavens and earth. With world-shattering force, it suppressed Shen Shuibi's dharma avatar!

But a sharp sword intent pierced the firmament, slicing open the wind gates[^3]!

A surge of Supreme Yin qi soared into the sky, overturning the heavens and toppling the mountains. As oceans boiled and peaks collapsed, it forcefully carved a hole right through the center of Monk Chanzhi's palm, breaking all suppression and seals with a single sword strike!

Then, it shattered the remnants of Monk Chanzhi's dharma avatar.

The way of the sword is to show illusion, to open with an advantage, to strike after but arrive first.

Zhang Lan was merely bait.

This second sword was the hidden killing blow.

The sword of a brute could not rival a kingdom.

But this sword was the sword of an immortal.

The sword of an immortal was enveloped by the four seasons, governed by the five elements, and opened by yin and yang. When drawn, it reversed space, usurped cosmic time, and redefined chaos.

Even a realm could be broken by it.

Could a mere kingdom block the sword of an immortal?

[^1]: **Jambu-nada gold** is considered the finest and most radiant form of gold in Buddhist cosmology.

    The name is derived from the Jambū river, which flows through Jambudvīpa (the "Rose-Apple Continent," which represents the world inhabited by humans).
    
    According to legend, this gold is found in the sands of the river that flows beneath the giant Jambū tree. It is said that the juice of the Jambū fruit falls into the water and over time, produces this superior gold.
    
    It has a distinct reddish-purple or deep yellow glow. It is often described as having a purple-gold luster.
[^2]: A reference to the Water of Eight Merits (八功德水) found in the lotus ponds of the Buddhist Pure Land. This divine water possesses eight distinct qualities: it is pure and clear, cool and refreshing, sweet-tasting, light and soft, moistening, tranquil, cures hunger and thirst, and nourishes the body and soul.
[^3]: The term corresponds to Xun (巽), one of the Eight Trigrams (Bagua), which governs the element of wind and the southeastern direction. "Slicing open the wind gates" signifies an attack so profoundly powerful that it ruptures the elemental fabric and directional boundaries of the world itself.
