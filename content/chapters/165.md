# Chapter 165: Attack

Inside the carriage, the General and Li Qi sat facing each other.

Shen Shuibi sat behind Li Qi with a blank expression. She seemed as if she were sleepwalking, merely sitting there without making any movements.

Outside the carriage, Old Ma was busy competing with the General's horse. One would raise its head, and the other would raise its head even higher. Their incessant neighing nearly escalated into a fight, terrifying the stablehand into hurriedly separating the two dragon colts.

Meanwhile, inside the carriage, Li Qi was conversing with the General.

"So, to summarize your point, General: the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest is currently pulling back its forces for an unknown reason? And it's highly likely they are preparing for the next demonic surge, which is why you abandoned the garrison?"

"It is not abandonment. The garrison simply cannot hold. If the demon nest truly launches a massed assault and forms a demonic tide, stubbornly defending the garrison would only be a dead end," the General explained.

"Therefore, the State Preceptor has made the decision to evacuate the nearby populace and relinquish the garrison. Afterward, we will lay down formations using the layout of the mountains and rivers, relying on the geographical advantage to mount a steadfast defense."

"Because the demon nest is on the verge of striking, even though you are pursuing righteous deeds, young hero, I had to offer a word of warning to prevent you from throwing your life away," the General said.

"Has the General considered the possibility that... they might actually be on the defensive?" Li Qi suggested.

Although a sudden withdrawal of forces was indeed suspicious, the ruler of the Green Mountain Kingdom had already mentioned that this was likely due to the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord traveling beyond the realm.

However, this was information known only to a Rank 5 monarch. These people likely remained in the dark.

It was even possible that the ruler of the Hundred Forests Kingdom was unaware. The Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord leaving All Under Heaven to travel beyond the realm was undoubtedly a heavily guarded secret.

_Just how did the ruler of the Green Mountain Kingdom obtain this information? His reach must be vast indeed._

"Some have indeed thought of that, but we cannot gamble on it. If a demonic tide does surge forward and disaster strikes, who will bear the responsibility? It is better not to joke with lives. Why not travel with us? When the fiends emerge in full force, you will naturally have ample opportunity to slay demons from within our army," the General advised.

"Thank you for the warning, General, but I have my own methods of self-preservation. You need not worry. Furthermore, apart from slaying demons, I have my own necessary reasons for entering," Li Qi politely declined.

"If that is the case, I will not force you to stay, young hero." The General shook his head, having said all he needed to say.

Their meeting was merely a chance encounter, so there was no need for excessive words. Even if driven by good intentions, ultimately, everyone had to walk their own path. Since the other party had made up his mind, there was nothing more to add.

Li Qi bid farewell to the army. Pulling along Old Ma, who was still eager to brawl with the General's mount, he and Shen Shuibi departed.

The army did not linger either, departing swiftly. Before long, the two parties lost sight of each other as the distance between them grew.

Li Qi continued his journey toward the garrison.

On the road, he spoke to Shen Shuibi behind him, "The Hundred Forests Kingdom doesn't seem very eager for war. Their first reaction to an anomaly from the enemy is actually to run."

Shen Shuibi thought for a moment before answering, "It's probably because the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord is too strong. They've been beaten into fear, haven't they? I heard that between the three kingdoms here and the Righteous Sword Palace, there are a total of three Rank 5 experts. The Palace Master of the Righteous Sword Palace also possesses immense lethal power, comparable to half a Rank 5. All four joined forces to ambush the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord. Even with the advantage of a surprise attack, they returned without success. The Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord remains perfectly alive and well. Even I've heard of this."

"Fighting four alone? He's that fierce?" Li Qi was stunned.

He hadn't heard of this. Wasn't the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord also Rank 5?

Shen Shuibi explained, "There are already vast gaps within each rank itself. If you were to fight ordinary Rank 9 cultivators, taking on dozens at once wouldn't be a problem, right?"

"While you can fight dozens at the lower ranks, and the gap between individuals shrinks at the higher ranks, experts like the Palace Master of the Righteous Sword Palace or Jin Chi, the Green Mountain God—both Rank 6—can still effortlessly fight over a dozen ordinary peers simultaneously. The Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord is the same."

Li Qi suddenly understood. It did make sense.

The power disparity between ranks was already immense—whoever devised this system had made each rank a world of its own—so it stood to reason that there would be significant differences within a single rank as well.

A cultivator who had just entered Rank 6 facing a ferocious powerhouse like the Green Mountain God—someone capable of fighting a Rank 5 head-on—would likely be instantly killed in a single exchange. It would take a group of over a dozen attacking together to even stand a chance of fighting to a draw.

"That's true. But what do you mean by the gap shrinking at the higher ranks?" Li Qi asked again.

These things were common sense to Shen Shuibi, so she didn't hide anything and spoke truthfully. "I've never seen what the true high ranks look like, so let's just talk about Rank 5. By that stage, cultivation base doesn't matter much in a fight. What truly matters are resources, divine abilities, and dharma treasures."

"After all, Rank 5 experts live for a very long time. As long as they don't meet an unexpected end, they can basically slowly grind their cultivation base up to the limit. However, breaking through to Rank 4 cannot be achieved through mere grinding. Therefore, the vast majority of Rank 5 cultivators have already reached the so-called 'peak' of their realm."

Reaching this point, Shen Shuibi seemed slightly troubled over how to explain it, her words becoming a bit disjointed.

However, a moment later, she said straightforwardly, "You can understand it like this: if you break through to Rank 5, wouldn't you need to consolidate your realm?"

"Of course," Li Qi nodded.

"For most Rank 5 cultivators, during the stage of consolidating their realm, they will have already elevated their cultivation base to a bottleneck. After breaking through to Rank 5, their very first action would be to enter secluded meditation. By the time they emerge, their cultivation base is already fully at the limits of Rank 5, with no difference compared to others. Since there is no gap in cultivation base, the only things that can reflect a difference in strength are weapons, divine abilities, and the like."

"But this cannot be done at Rank 9, because you are currently in a process of accumulation, and your limited lifespan urges you to improve quickly. Once your rank increases a bit and your accumulation reaches a certain level, you will realize that accumulation is merely a matter of course. What is required for a breakthrough is far from accumulation; it requires a flash of insight."

Shen Shuibi was not skilled with words, so her explanation was somewhat messy, frustrating even herself.

Fortunately, Li Qi was quite clever.

He clapped his hands. "Oh, I get it!"

Li Qi helped Shen Shuibi straighten out her thoughts. "Going from zero to one is an infinite increase. Going from one to two is a twofold increase. But going from one million to one million and one thousand is only a minuscule improvement; there won't be much difference between the two."

"Cultivation is just like that. The higher the cultivation base, the harder it is to increase the percentage of growth, making it harder to widen the gap. So, the difference in combat strength must rely on multipliers, right?"

"In the early stages, the base number is the most important. Because if your cultivation base increases from one to two, you are twice as strong as someone at one. But in the later stages, even if your cultivation goes up by a thousand, it does little good; you have to rely on the bonuses from multipliers."

"Mhm, mhm! That's exactly what I meant!" Shen Shuibi nodded repeatedly. He had finally made her meaning clear.

It still required Li Qi's words to explain it clearly.

In truth, it was simply the relationship between a base number and a multiplier.

Once the base number had increased significantly in the later stages, continuing to increase it became too difficult.

In the early stages, if one's cultivation base was ten, and they possessed a divine ability with a multiplier of 10%, their combat strength would be eleven. Because ten increased by 10% is eleven.

Rather than going out of one's way to acquire such a divine ability, it was better to hurry up and cultivate until the base reached twelve. Not only would combat strength be higher, but it would also increase one's lifespan.

But in the later stages, if the cultivation base was one million, cultivating behind closed doors for an extra thousand points would only amount to one million and one thousand. The improvement was far too low.

However, if one had that same divine ability with a 10% multiplier, their combat strength would become 1.1 million. Instantly gaining one hundred thousand points of combat strength was much stronger than bitterly cultivating for a mere thousand.

Therefore, combat strength in the early stages relied primarily on the base number of one's cultivation. In the later stages, it relied primarily on the multipliers provided by divine abilities.

However, at this point, Shen Shuibi reminded him, "But combat strength isn't the only pursuit. Cultivation isn't just for fighting and killing. A cultivation base can be used in other areas too. If you only chase after combat strength every day, you'll stray onto the wrong path."

"Mm, I naturally understand that. The existence of combat strength is only to guarantee that one can walk their chosen path, not to increase combat strength just for the sake of it," Li Qi nodded.

Cultivation, at its core, existed for a purpose.

And the path and methods used to achieve that purpose formed one's Dao.

Combat strength was meant for self-preservation, but the ultimate goal of one's cultivation could never be merely to increase combat strength.

Of course, there were people who sought to grow stronger simply for the sake of growing stronger, but they were generally called martial lunatics, distinct from the vast majority of cultivators.

Take Shen Shuibi as an example. Among Rank 5 experts, she was particularly unskilled in combat; one could even say she completely didn't know how to fight. But she was a highly skilled physician and alchemist, and she commanded the respect of others just the same.

Even so, Li Qi understood in his heart that while combat strength wasn't everything, it was still indispensable.

All peace, harmony, and beauty, as well as the pursuit of the Dao itself, had to be built upon a foundation of sufficient strength. Otherwise, one would only be throwing their life away.

The two chatted about these trivial matters of cultivation. This was roughly their daily routine together.

Asking questions and learning from one another. Shen Shuibi frequently asked Li Qi about worldly affairs and various stories, while Li Qi mostly asked about difficult problems in cultivation.

Although the value of their topics was quite different, it was clear that both were quite happy.

Perhaps asking questions was merely an excuse to talk.

Setting that aside, just as Li Qi and Shen Shuibi were chatting while heading toward the garrison, both of them suddenly raised their heads at the same time.

"There's human qi. A village? Didn't they say everyone had evacuated?" Li Qi frowned.

That General had explicitly told him the Hundred Forests Kingdom was preparing a total evacuation. How could there still be human qi in a place like this?

"It's not just human qi. There is also death qi and blood qi. Oh... and demon qi and military qi too. It looks like a battle," Shen Shuibi's perception was far sharper than Li Qi's, allowing her to sense much more.

"The demon nest actually dared to launch a surprise attack? And their timing is this precise?" Li Qi's frown deepened.

After Shen Shuibi described the situation, he almost instantly understood what was happening over there.

Military qi, blood qi, demon qi, human qi, and death qi all mixed together. Wasn't this a battlefield?!

A massive amount of human qi meant a massive number of commoners. Was the Hundred Forests Kingdom in the middle of evacuating the local residents?

Had they been ambushed by fiends during the evacuation? Was the garrison army responsible for the evacuation currently fighting?

Although it took many words to explain, Li Qi's realization took only a split second. He immediately made a decisive choice: "Let's go. We're going to take a look."

"Mm," Shen Shuibi nodded. The two changed directions and rushed toward the source of the human qi.

Even before arriving, Li Qi peered into the distance and laid eyes on the battlefield.

It was exactly as he had expected.

Huge throngs of commoners were undergoing a forced migration, their numbers reaching roughly a hundred thousand.

Furthermore, an army of over ten thousand soldiers was at the front lines, resisting the fiends.

The fiends, hailing from an unknown origin, were led by two full Rank 8 fiends. Below them were dozens of Rank 9 demons and tens of thousands of minor demons, howling as they charged the army.

Casualties had already begun to appear among the civilians. Losing all order, they fled in every direction, throwing the army's formation into complete disarray. The officers were forced to kill a few as a deterrent, strictly ordering the rest to stand their ground and not move.

A foul stench rose from the dark red, blood-soaked earth. A massive barrage of spell arts unleashed a form of artillery fire unique to this world.

Bolstered by military qi, numerous combat spell arts stirred up radiant flames and thick, cloud-like smoke across the battlefield. Every synchronized volley caused the earth to violently shudder. The lingering flames scorched across the sky, and dense, acrid black smoke spiraled upwards like beacon fires.

However, there were simply too many fiends. Moreover, their own military qi hung over their heads, resembling dark beasts with azure tails or herds of cattle and sheep under a dome. The energy was continuous like a loom's shuttle, murky and opaque.

This military qi heavily weakened the soldiers' spell arts, sharply reducing their lethality and area of effect. Some spell arts were even completely negated by the military qi.

Accompanied by their thunderous footsteps, the cracking of nearby stones, and piercing, shrieking laughter, the fiends endured the bombardment of spell arts. Losing only a few dozen in numbers, they plunged into the soldiers' positions like a sharp blade.

_Bang!_ They violently collided.

The scene was like two clay pots brimming with blood smashing into each other, producing a crisp shattering sound and sending countless sprays of fresh blood flying.

The soldiers and the fiends had officially engaged in close combat.

Flesh and blood splattered alongside battle cries and shrieks, as severed limbs and broken bodies scattered across the ground.

The frontline consisted of a spear array. Facing the charging fiends head-on, their spear shafts sparked with fire as they thrust forward in tight formation.

The wind of the spears roared mercilessly in the narrow combat zone, attempting to shatter the fiends' charging formation. But the fiends possessed thick hides and durable flesh. Forcing their way through the thrusts at the cost of heavy casualties, they tackled the spearmen blocking their path, throwing them to the ground and scattering their equipment.

The battle formation devolved into total chaos, transforming from a head-on clash into a disorganized melee.

Above the horde of fiends stood the two Rank 8 fiends. They unleashed their own methods to suppress the army, firing off endless divine abilities. Yet, they held back a portion of their strength, ensuring the commoners remained unharmed.

It appeared their objective was to abduct the populace.

The army only had one Rank 8 expert. He struggled bitterly, barely managing to sustain the existence of the military qi. Driven to his limits, he found it difficult to fend off the enemy.

In large-scale warfare, an army without military qi acting as its backbone was doomed to be slaughtered. If the military momentum collapsed, they would scatter like loose sand, no longer forming an army.

This was the difference between an army and a disorganized mob.

"We need to hurry over, or else these people won't survive," Li Qi said.

"Mm," Shen Shuibi nodded firmly.
