# Chapter 159: Clear Mist Grotto-Heaven

When Li Qi arrived at the Clear Mist Sect, they had long since made preparations.

A hundred people stood around to respectfully welcome him, and a red carpet had been rolled out all the way to the foot of the mountain.

The main branch of the Clear Mist Sect was situated on a small hill, the original site of the Fox Ward. There were no towering peaks, only a few modest hills.

In the very beginning, this place was just an ordinary little ravine. After all, it was merely a gathering of unranked foxes under the leadership of two Rank 9 fox demons, Hu Xian'er and Hu Mei'er, relying on the dual traits of gluttony and greed to build up a mountain brothel.

The exact process of how the Fox Ward transformed into the Clear Mist Sect was no longer known. After all, it had happened nearly a thousand years ago. Even for cultivators, this was a lengthy span of time, to the point where the very first batch of foxes had long since reached the end of their lifespans and died.

Only Hu Mei'er and Hu Xian'er, who had ascended to Rank 7, had survived to the present day.

Rank 9 cultivators enjoyed slightly longer lifespans, though not to an incomprehensible degree. Upon reaching Rank 8, cultivators of most paths could live for several centuries, roughly the duration of an entire mortal dynasty.

However, a Rank 8 of the Martial Dao would only live for one or two centuries, not much longer than a mortal.

Only upon reaching Rank 7 would one stop dying. According to the classification Li Qi pondered in his own mind, this could be considered attaining immortality. The concept of a limited lifespan no longer applied.

It truly was immortality. According to Shen Shuibi, in some lower-grade worlds, one might be able to linger at Rank 8, but upon reaching Rank 7, they were absolutely guaranteed to undergo [Ascension].

This was because the existence of a Rank 7 would place an immense burden on worlds like the Earth Oil World. Their mere presence, breathing, and movements would stir the qi, disrupting the original flow and destroying the normal operations of the entire world. Therefore, the world would exert a repulsive force against them, and they would leave of their own accord. This was what was known as [Ascension].

In a place like the Earth Oil World, even the Lord of the World was merely Rank 8. Even the Child of Destiny supported by the Heavenly Dao had a limit of Rank 8. Once reaching Rank 7, one had to leave.

The lifespan of a Rank 7 was infinite, and the reason was quite simple.

What was lifespan?

In truth, it was merely the process of an isolated system degrading from order to disorder.

If the personal cosmos underwent no adjustments and was simply left to develop naturally, then in Li Qi's terms, the entropy within it would continually increase.

This entropy would cause the personal cosmos to gradually fall into chaos, rendering it unable to maintain normal functions. What followed would be decay, collapse, and ultimately, death.

Neither Rank 9 nor Rank 8 could solve this problem. Although they slowed the decay of the personal cosmos, they could not prevent the aging of the overall structure.

However, upon reaching Rank 7, with the elevation in cultivation base, the personal cosmos was no longer an isolated system. At that stage, almost all cultivators—aside from those of the Martial Dao—could take control of their personal cosmos, expelling the chaos from their bodies to reconstruct an orderly physical form.

It was much like a ship.

Everyone was born with an innate ship that carried them through the rise and fall of the mortal realm.

Mortals could do nothing about this ship. They could only slowly wait and watch as the vessel that accompanied them throughout their lives gradually rotted away. Then, having lost the ship that carried them, they would fall into the water and die.

Rank 9 and Rank 8 cultivators could repair this ship to a certain extent. For example, if there was a hole, they could patch it; if the deck fell off, they could reinstall it. Although this patching and mending could not make the ship as good as new, it could effectively delay the decay and extend their lifespan.

And upon reaching Rank 7, as long as no accidents occurred, they could replace the rotted parts of the ship at any time. By then, witnessing the imperishable divine, their lifespan would naturally be endless.

However, the infinite lifespan of a Rank 7 was built on the premise that the outside world had enough materials to supply them for ship repairs.

In other words, for a Rank 7 cultivator to maintain longevity, they needed the outside world to constantly supply them with materials, various kinds of qi, and even many profound and esoteric substances.

As long as there were enough materials, and they didn't just give up and stop making repairs, their lifespan was endless.

But as external resources depleted, a Rank 7's ship would still gradually rot away.

This was why Rank 7 entities in those lower-grade worlds were so madly obsessed with [Ascension].

Because a lower-grade world could not support their existence. The qi they absorbed to repair their ships could even drain a lower-grade world dry.

Only by undergoing Ascension to a higher-grade world could they find an endless supply of materials to sustain their lifespans.

However, as far as Li Qi knew, the final destination for [Ascension] from all worlds was this very All Under Heaven.

Therefore, Rank 7s born in All Under Heaven were quite fortunate. They did not need to travel across various worlds searching for materials. They were already here, naturally possessing nearly infinite materials to sustain their eternal life.

In Li Qi's view, this truly counted as becoming an immortal.

An endless lifespan, living eternally without death.

Yet, Shen Shuibi seemed to scoff fiercely at this kind of eternal life. But when asked for the reason, she refused to say.

Perhaps it was simply beyond the scope of what Li Qi should be exposed to. For some matters, knowing them too early was not a good thing.

However, thinking of this, Li Qi suddenly realized something.

Shen Shuibi's cultivation had plummeted; how was her lifespan calculated now?

Li Qi knew that her personal cosmos had basically shattered into puzzle pieces, and could only be described as being barely glued back together. Going by this logic, what was the state of Shen Shuibi's lifespan at this moment?

The ship carrying her must have been reduced to nothing more than a few broken planks of wood.

Realizing this, Li Qi became even firmer in his resolve. Coming to the Clear Mist Sect to collect the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord's reward was undoubtedly the most important task at hand.

Li Qi's mind was preoccupied with these thoughts, but his body did not stop moving. Surrounded by a large crowd, he walked toward the main branch of the Clear Mist Sect.

People around him constantly offered compliments and flattery. Li Qi continuously exchanged pleasantries, but since most of his energy was focused on the thoughts in his head, he appeared somewhat absent-minded.

The crowd from the Clear Mist Sect seemed to notice this as well, but they acted completely unbothered, carrying on as usual. There was no shortage of handsome men and beautiful women surrounding Li Qi, accompanying him the entire way, emphasizing their sheer attentiveness.

Finally, after passing through the mountain gates, the crowd surrounding him began to thin out.

Because Hu Mei'er had appeared by Li Qi's side.

She had changed into a new outfit, yet her aura remained as enchanting as ever.

With a Rank 7 powerhouse personally accompanying him, Li Qi naturally couldn't continue to space out. He immediately pulled himself out of his own thoughts, cupped his hands, and said respectfully, "I am merely here as a guest. There is no need for Senior to come personally."

"It is precisely because an honored guest has arrived that I must be extra careful. Otherwise, wouldn't it seem neglectful?" Hu Mei'er said with a smile.

"We are about to enter the mountain gate. This is the perfect opportunity for me to properly introduce the sect's situation to you, sir."

Li Qi cupped his hands again. "Then please lead the way, Senior. Allow this junior to broaden his horizons and see the world."

Hu Mei'er repeatedly waved it off. "You flatter us, sir. Gracing our gates with your presence, it is enough that you do not despise our sect for being crude and our etiquette for being lacking. How could I dare say I am broadening your horizons?"

Exchanging pleasantries, Li Qi stepped into the Clear Mist Sect.

However, with a single step inside, he saw the surrounding scenery shift, like <Flowers in a Mirror, Moon in the Water>.

From the outside, it was nothing more than a few small hills.

But upon entering, the picture drastically changed. Suddenly, it was as vast as the sea and sky, evoking the feeling of discovering a hidden paradise just when the path seemed to end.

This wasn't Li Qi's first time encountering this sensation, so he was already accustomed to it and showed no expression of surprise.

Hu Mei'er introduced, "This minor grotto-heaven is named the Clear Mist Grotto-Heaven. It is also the reason the Fox Ward originally changed its name to the Clear Mist Sect. It was personally bestowed by the Kingdom Lord and refined from the wreckage of several minor worlds. It is fundamentally a dead object, lacking a Heavenly Dao and devoid of gods, so it cannot generate life force or various kinds of qi. However, it is perfect for a sect headquarters. Everything inside can be custom-defined, making it incredibly suitable for setting up formations."

Hearing this, Li Qi took a look around.

He indeed got that feeling.

Within this minor grotto-heaven, there was no Heavenly Dao, meaning life would not be born. No bizarre or exotic qi would naturally generate; it was purely an empty space. If left alone, even after millions of years, it would remain a lifeless void, incapable of evolving on its own like a proper world.

_So this is a grotto-heaven?_

Although Li Qi had read about them in books, this was his first time seeing a real grotto-heaven.

Just as he was observing his surroundings, the light faded, and Li Qi suddenly found himself standing atop a mountain peak.

At the center of this grotto-heaven lay a massive mountain. Atop its peak sat a few pavilions—this was where Li Qi stood. It was extremely tranquil, with practically no one around.

But looking down, he saw a lake encircling the foot of the mountain. With faint smoke and light cosmetics, illuminated pavilions and bustling crowds, it was a thriving scene of revelry.

It was as if an island had suddenly risen in the middle of a lake, and that island was a towering mountain. All the water and land in the surroundings existed in orbit around this central peak.

On the ring-shaped lake below, countless multi-storied pleasure boats and painted barges floated upon the water.

The intricately carved barges featured canopies overhead hung with horn lanterns, and winding balustrades below. Small tables and couches were arranged within. The sides of the boats lacked windows to allow for unobstructed views. He could see that most of the pleasure boats were rowed with twin oars, filled with the sounds of wind instruments and singing, dancing figures, and women adorned in white powder and dark green eyeliner, exuding a lingering, romantic charm.

Even though it was daytime outside, it was night here. There was no sun, moon, or stars, only a black sky.

Yet, despite the black sky, there was still a faint underglow, creating a hazy atmosphere. It was akin to an obsidian crystal glowing softly from within—the color was black, but light was still present, preventing the entire grotto-heaven from plunging into total darkness.

Under this hazy ambient light, the barges on the lake below and the carved towers along the shore were brightly lit, presenting a breathtakingly beautiful scene.

Waterside pavilions dotted the shores, while pleasure boats drifted along the currents. The glow of lanterns and the sound of flutes and drums created an indescribably dazzling atmosphere. The lake's surface was as smooth as a mirror, reflecting the glazed lights and making it as bright as day. Countless courtesans clustered in groups, displaying a myriad of graceful postures. They showed off their melodious voices, taking turns singing to one another. Skirts fluttered and hairpins danced. Every onlooker was utterly mesmerized, their ethereal souls captivated and their hearts swayed.

White boats with red curtains, hundreds and thousands of illuminated barges on the lake, with music and singing piercing the night. They scattered across the water like the stars of dawn. One could only imagine the endless pleasures contained within.

"What a fine view," Li Qi remarked, standing at the summit and looking down at the scene.

_To elevate the world's oldest profession to such a standard... their aesthetic tastes are absolutely flawless._ Though decadence and debauchery hid within, the outward appearance was undeniably, breathtakingly beautiful.

It seemed the operations within Clear Mist City could only be considered fringe business—the simple selling of flesh.

The true patrons were hosted here. One glance was enough to tell that this place was on an entirely different level of luxury compared to the outside.

"You mock us, sir. These are all unpresentable things~, it's best not to sully your eyes. Let us head down and talk there." Hu Mei'er appeared quite humble, quickly urging Li Qi to descend from the pavilion.

"As a guest, I naturally follow the host's arrangements." Li Qi agreed. He stopped observing the Clear Mist Sect's primary business venue and descended from the pavilion, arriving at their living quarters.

After coming down from the pavilion, they passed through several disciple residences. Everyone they met along the way was exceedingly respectful, and Li Qi naturally returned their greetings one by one. The atmosphere was highly harmonious. However, these core disciples of the Clear Mist Sect were different from the people on the outside.

Though all possessed extreme beauty, they lacked any seductive intent. Instead, they resembled daughters of noble houses, and their cultivation bases were solid and grounded.

Passing through these areas, they finally arrived at the residence of the two sisters, Hu Xian'er and Hu Mei'er.

Unlike the brilliant lights, jade branches, and flawless moon outside, this true core location was extremely cold and monotonous.

A few thatched huts, some tables and chairs, an oil lamp, and nothing more.

As soon as he entered, Li Qi saw a tall, magnificently handsome man dressed in a stately robe.

This man exuded a profound nobility. He was currently writing at a desk. Beside him, Hu Xian'er was grinding ink and adjusting the lamp, attending to him with the utmost care.

The moment Li Qi arrived, he immediately bowed deeply. "This junior greets the Kingdom Lord! I was entirely unaware that it was the Kingdom Lord who invited me. For such negligence, I beg the Kingdom Lord's punishment!"

Yes, the man before him was none other than the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord!

Li Qi had never seen the man in person, but the aura he emanated was identical to the one he had felt in that palace back then.

_What virtue or ability do I possess to warrant a personal visit from the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord!?_

No wonder Hu Mei'er had adopted such a lowly posture toward him. No wonder she had been so vague, terrified of saying too much. So this was the truth!

_But what is this bigshot thinking? Why would he personally come all this way to see a nobody like me? Is he that bored?_

"Haha, we meet again, Li Qi," the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord laughed heartily. "Come, sit here."

He invited Li Qi to sit opposite him. Though his tone was hearty, it was actually the grand magnanimity of someone in a high position. It wasn't the boisterousness of a Jianghu outlaw, but a natural, expansive open-mindedness.

Seeing this, Li Qi stopped acting coy. Although any of these three could squash him like a bug with a single hand, he decisively walked forward and sat across from the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord.

The moment he sat down, Hu Xian'er and Hu Mei'er—two prominent figures and peerless beauties in the eyes of outsiders—consciously stood at attention on either side. Standing left and right, they washed the cups and poured tea for the two men, behaving immaculately without a hint of impropriety.

Li Qi felt a wave of hesitation in his heart, unsure why the other party was treating him with such solemnity.

It was just driving away Jin Buhuan. The Green Mountain Kingdom Lord could have handled that himself.

The only reason the Kingdom Lord hadn't done so was out of fear of sparking an all-out war with Jin Chi. Hence, he needed to borrow someone else's blade.

Li Qi was, at best, that blade. He merely represented a fabricated third-party faction he had postured into existence.

If the leader of that fictitious faction were to step forward, it would be worthy of the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord's grand treatment. But Li Qi was just a lowly Rank 9 pawn.

The gap between Rank 5 and Rank 9 was akin to the difference between a god and an ant.

Even tens of billions of Li Qis piled together could be wiped out by a single slap from this man.

_So why go to such lengths?_
