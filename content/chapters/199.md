# Chapter 199: A New Dao Rhythm

Li Qi's heart tightened. He immediately put on a panicked expression and said, "I don't know, perhaps Grandmaster saw wrong? I will bare my divine soul, please take a look for me, Grandmaster!"

He looked utterly flustered, as if he were genuinely worried that his divine soul had been damaged.

This actually gave Monk Chanzhi pause.

Li Qi had always appeared sharp and courageous before. Why was he suddenly in such a panic now?

Furthermore, baring his divine soul to him?

Was he not afraid of death?

The Shaman Dao possessed a multitude of methods. Their spell arts were miraculous and layered with profound techniques, but this was all predicated on the fact that their physical bodies were frail and their divine souls fragile.

The personal cosmos of the Shaman Dao could be considered among the most fragile across all paths.

To voluntarily offer up one's divine soul was akin to placing one's life and death entirely in the hands of another. It made it nearly impossible to defend against someone tampering with it.

Unless forced, one would typically only do such a thing for someone they trusted implicitly.

Monk Chanzhi frowned, unable to gauge Li Qi's attitude for the moment.

However, to Li Qi, this wasn't a problem at all.

Did he think his life wasn't already in someone else's hands just because he didn't reveal his divine soul?

What a joke. Every second he spent in this pagoda, his life belonged to Monk Chanzhi.

Therefore, he decisively bared his divine soul and did something even Monk Chanzhi wouldn't dare imagine.

He absorbed the devilish thoughts directly into his divine soul!

As Li Qi revealed his divine soul, a hundred ghosts paraded through it, and a horde of devils danced wildly!

A dense layer of black qi shrouded his divine soul. These were the very devilish thoughts gradually cannibalizing Li Qi's divine soul.

Seeing this, Monk Chanzhi's expression tightened. "My young friend, you researched these devilish thoughts for this poor monk, yet I didn't expect you to infect yourself as well. This poor monk has no way to cure this, but I can help you suppress it somewhat."

Chanzhi had never expected that Li Qi would fail to properly protect himself and become infected by the devilish thoughts!

In that case, it was only natural that his divine soul was damaged.

He only hoped this kid wouldn't die here—or at least, it would be best if he waited until the cultivation method was fully researched before dying.

Chanzhi immediately made his decision. He pressed his palms together, and a brilliant Buddhist light erupted around him. "My young friend, you will soon enter the Rakshasa Ghost Kingdom. They will tempt you with worldly pleasures, food, and drink—be careful not to indulge. Through all places of devilish obstruction, march bravely forward without retreat. If you encounter a treasure pool and drink its sweet dew, doubling your physical strength and preparing you to tread upon wondrous realms, do not linger in greed. Only by advancing with a single-minded focus can you suppress the devilish thoughts and guard your mind. I will perform the rite for you now."

Li Qi nodded repeatedly, looking utterly frantic and out of his wits.

Monk Chanzhi sat down and began to chant sutras. Suddenly, the image of a dignified figure in white appeared as he muttered, "Entering Samadhi through hearing, contemplating, and practicing. From the faculty of hearing, I entered the stream and forgot the source. Since the source was silenced, the two states of motion and stillness crystallized and ceased to arise. As this gradually increased, both the hearing and the heard exhausted themselves. Not stopping where they were exhausted, the awareness and the object of awareness became empty. When the emptiness of awareness reached ultimate perfection, the emptiness and the object of emptiness both vanished. Once arising and ceasing were extinguished, Nirvana manifested before me. Suddenly, I transcended the worldly and the otherworldly. Li Qi, attain perfect illumination! Above, unite with the profound, fundamental enlightened mind of all Buddhas, sharing the same power of compassion! Below, unite with all sentient beings in the Six Paths, sharing the same sorrowful yearning!"

Upon hearing the Buddhist chant, Li Qi's consciousness uncontrollably dissipated!

As if hypnotized, his divine soul shuddered, and he fell directly into a deep slumber.

In a daze, he seemed to plummet into a mysterious kingdom.

There, he felt as though he experienced countless events.

He and a group of people seemed to be trekking over mountains and wading through rivers.

They crossed basins, hills, canyons, plains, and deserts.

In the basin, there was fruit. The hungry left to forage, and never returned.

Among the hills, there were golden deer. The greedy left to hunt, and never returned.

At the bottom of the canyon, there were medicinal herbs. The sick left to gather them, and never returned.

On the plains, there were campfires. The exhausted left to rest, and never returned.

In the desert, there were oases. The parched left to drink, and never returned.

Li Qi ignored them, allowing them to leave.

He vaguely remembered someone warning him.

_Devils will tempt you with worldly pleasures, food, and drink—be careful not to indulge._

_Through all places of devilish obstruction, march bravely forward without retreat._

_Even if your physical strength doubles, preparing you to tread upon wondrous realms, do not linger in greed._

He kept these words firmly in his heart.

After walking for an unknown amount of time, his heart grew increasingly anxious.

Perhaps ten years had passed, or perhaps a hundred. Li Qi couldn't remember clearly.

His consciousness had blurred, and even his concept of time had been completely shattered.

He had absolutely no idea how much time had passed at this exact moment.

Despite feeling hollow, exhausted, drained, and tasting a bitter tang in his mouth, he still walked on with all his might.

He truly exerted every ounce of his strength. Every sliver of available energy was mobilized, even though he could feel an endless wave of agony wash over him with the slightest movement.

It was true agony—as if his ears were filled with snickering and screaming, with things constantly jumping around him.

His soul was being ground away.

The oppression of the divine soul was more agonizing than any physical torture.

Such suffering lasted for... countless years?

Exactly how long had it been?

He didn't know.

But it didn't matter.

Be it ten thousand years, a hundred thousand years, or even a million years, he would eventually reach the end.

Eventually...

Li Qi's mind had long since grown hazy. Only a faint trace of persistence supported his actions.

At this point, his actions couldn't even be called conscious behavior. It was more like the violent kicking of a skinned frog—an instinctual persistence.

Then, he reached the end.

Something yanked him.

Something grabbed him. Countless demons suddenly seized his heart and tore it to shreds!

An intense surge of pain hit him!

Every nerve ending was trembling, screaming at the top of its lungs!

Unbelievable agony swept through his entire body, making him curl up and shiver like a shrimp just plucked from the water. He could barely move!

Under such immense pain, Li Qi suddenly snapped awake.

Then, he saw it—

An incomparably magnificent existence.

A devil.

A true _devil_.

Through the entirely new senses provided by the True Knowledge dao rhythm, he saw it. In the endless void, there was a colossally massive black sphere.

A black sphere of unfathomable proportions.

It extended countless tentacles.

Truly countless.

Like the sands of the Ganges, beyond calculation.

Billions upon billions could not account for even one hundredth of them.

Beside it, even stars seemed like mere motes of dust.

Galaxies near it resembled nothing more than caterpillars.

Its countless tentacles pierced into countless worlds.

A burst of enlightenment rose in Li Qi's heart, forming a single name.

_The Paranirmita-vasavartin Devaputra Mara_.

Merely looking at it once and having this thought emerge caused Li Qi to suddenly feel as if he had been crushed flat. It felt as though something had pierced his heart!

Then, as if rapidly surfacing from the deep sea, he violently snapped his eyes open and gasped for air!

His gasping sounded like a working bellows. His lungs and heart pounded fiercely!

After taking a breath, he collapsed onto the floor and coughed violently. "_Cough, cough, cough! Cough!_ Fuck!"

Even Li Qi couldn't hold back his profanity.

"What... was that thing?" Li Qi raised his head. He was completely drenched in sweat. Every nerve in his body was trembling and groaning, and his entire figure was engorged with purple, blood-congested veins, on the verge of exploding.

At that moment, Monk Chanzhi's voice drifted over. "Your will is resolute, my young friend. I didn't expect you to actually endure the pain of my suppressing your devilish thoughts. You just journeyed to the Rakshasa Ghost Kingdom, experiencing the same torment this poor monk endures when devouring devilish thoughts."

"I used this method to help you seal the devilish thoughts. They won't affect you anymore, but the damage to your divine soul is a foregone conclusion. After you leave this place, you can heal yourself using Shaman Dao methods. It shouldn't leave any lasting side effects."

Hearing this, Li Qi forced himself up, staggering as he stood. He pressed his hands together, his tone weak. "Thank you... for your help, Grandmaster."

"It is of no consequence. You were infected by devilish thoughts for my sake. Rest and recover peacefully. I won't disturb you further. There is no need for you to research cultivation methods for me over the next two days. Focus on stabilizing your divine soul so you don't suffer irreversible damage." Chanzhi nodded, then headed back upstairs.

After Chanzhi left, Li Qi wiped the sweat from his brow.

He was drenched to the bone, looking as though he had just been fished out of the water.

Li Qi tapped two fingers together. Water qi surged forth, forming a large sphere of water that smashed heavily against him, clearing his mind significantly.

He then stumbled and collapsed onto the bed, unable to move a single muscle.

He had finally... fooled him.

Just now, to conceal the matter of his split soul, he had directly infected his own divine soul with devilish thoughts. He manufactured the illusion that his incomplete divine soul was the result of those devilish thoughts!

He hadn't known if the trick would actually work, but the situation had been urgent, forcing him to resort to such an extreme measure.

Fortunately, he had managed to hide the truth.

Furthermore—

"What... was that thing? Paranirmita-vasavartin Devaputra Mara?" Lying on the bed, Li Qi's heart was still filled with astonishment.

It was incomprehensible, indescribable.

Just what kind of existence... was that?

Li Qi felt it.

That Heavenly Devil had been giving him a hint.

A hint pointing toward an extremely crucial secret.

It was urging him.

It evoked an unanalyzable emotion within his heart.

It was hard to describe, akin to vaguely expressing a spiritual significance through tangible objects. This significance was incredibly bizarre; any intelligent creature could only barely articulate it using wholly inadequate symbols.

It was as if Li Qi had found a beautiful pattern within the chaos of the cosmos—a pattern that belonged to him, and him alone, sketched out using the tool of thought.

Yet, this pattern was so exquisite that any clumsy attempt to trace it would be foolish.

The very moment he laid eyes on that massive black sphere, Li Qi felt a bizarre sensation: the weight of his physical body began to lighten, and a purely spiritual essence flooded into his physical form.

It was a spirit that made one feel uneasy, yet strangely novel. It guided his fantasies down uncharted paths, transporting him to a hazy, empty realm—a mysterious exploration of the unknown, illuminated only by eternal stars. He felt his soul unburdened, freely experiencing all sorts of adventures.

"I can't think about it... I can't think about it. Bury it, bury it..." Li Qi muttered to himself, burying his face in his pillow.

That was the Heavenly Devil.

An existence that devoured one's Dao Heart.

He absolutely could not take it to heart.

This sensation.

It was fake.

But...

Li Qi slowly raised his hand.

Suddenly.

The devilish thoughts still entwined within his divine soul dissipated completely.

These devilish thoughts—which he had voluntarily infected himself with to deceive Chanzhi—instantly left him of their own accord.

Even the devils suppressed beneath the Arhat statues nearby simultaneously changed their postures.

These devils shifted from a state of forced suppression to one of terrified, kneeling submission.

Yet, even Monk Chanzhi failed to notice this change.

It was as if all devilish thoughts, all devils, had submitted to Li Qi.

But Li Qi knew that he wasn't the object of their submission.

Rather, it was a single strand of dao rhythm resting in his hand.

It was the physical manifestation of that wondrous emotion, that spiritual significance he had felt.

A dao rhythm bestowed by Paranirmita-vasavartin Devaputra Mara!

For some unknown reason, after he witnessed that black sphere with his own eyes, the other party had actually gifted him a strand of dao rhythm!

Relying solely on the aura of this single strand of dao rhythm, he could easily suppress all kins of the Heavenly Devil and eradicate all devilish thoughts. He could cure Monk Chanzhi's chronic ailment in a single day.

It felt like a fake illusion.

Li Qi didn't want to believe it was real, but the existence of this object was undeniable.

Curling up on the bed, he pulled the covers over himself.

Li Qi hid away this strand of dao rhythm.

Then, he closed his eyes.

An unknown distance away, his split soul opened its eyes.

Then, he shouted loudly, "Miss Shen! Miss Shen!"

When in doubt, find Shen Shuibi!

---

An hour later.

Inside the secret room of the Lower Nine Sects.

Shen Shuibi wore a serious and earnest expression, her eyes filled with distress.

Taking a deep breath, she finally threw her hands up and said, "I don't know. I've never heard of what you're describing."

"Even you don't know, Miss Shen?" Li Qi was astounded.

This was the very first time Shen Shuibi had ever said she didn't know something.

Shen Shuibi explained, "Based solely on your description, the discrepancy with the Heavenly Devil recorded in texts is far too vast."

"Her Ladyship once told me that Paranirmita-vasavartin Devaputra Mara had attained the Rank 1 Dao, with kins spreading across endless worlds. It once caused a massive catastrophe, dragging several Sons of Buddha into the Devil Dao, and even striking out against Mahavairocana Tathagata. Later, it was suppressed and banished from All Under Heaven. Since then, it could only wreak havoc Beyond the Boundary, leaving absolutely no influence on All Under Heaven, save for a few surviving kins—which make up the current Devil Dao."

"Therefore, I've actually never seen the Heavenly Devil myself. And... to be honest, I don't think you possess the qualifications to look at such a being and survive."

Choosing her words carefully, Shen Shuibi told Li Qi, "A Rank 1... what level of existence that is, neither you nor I can even imagine. Moreover, Paranirmita-vasavartin Devaputra Mara is renowned for deluding the hearts of men. It's not that I don't believe you; it's just that, if you truly saw it with your own eyes, I feel you would have been annihilated in an instant."

Although her words sounded quite impolite, Li Qi deeply agreed.

He nodded and said, "It is indeed so. Back then, I nearly died just looking directly at the true form of a Rank 6 demon king. If I had truly perceived a Rank 1, I'm afraid I would have been directly reduced to ash."

"But I didn't make direct contact. I saw it indirectly through my dao rhythm," Li Qi added.

"That's still unclear. To be honest, your dao rhythm is also extremely bizarre. I, for one, have never seen anything like it. Perhaps the Grand Invocator would know." Shen Shuibi shook her head, indicating her helplessness.

Li Qi ventured a guess. "Then... could it be a trick? What if this Heavenly Devil dao rhythm was actually fabricated by Monk Chanzhi to deceive me? Perhaps this object is a fake, intentionally created by him to confuse me, or even to monitor me?"

Shen Shuibi remained silent. She was never good at these kinds of scheming mind games, so she resumed staring blankly into space.

Li Qi knew this as well, so he could only offer a bitter smile and return to pondering the matter on his own.
