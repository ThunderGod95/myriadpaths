# Chapter 190: The Serious Bunny

Over on Chanzhi's end, Li Qi was currently analyzing the demonic thoughts of the hundred devils.

Meanwhile, on the other side, Shen Shuibi had already rushed to Vast Yang City.

Unlike the cautious and prudent Li Qi, she sprinted the entire way, rushing directly into Vast Yang.

When Li Qi reached the city outskirts, he would always honestly land on the ground and proceed on foot. But Shen Shuibi didn't know about this taboo, and even if she did, she wouldn't care.

She rode the wind the entire journey, transforming into a streak of rainbow light that carved a trail across the sky.

At this very moment, the commander of the city defense force was yawning while playing Pai Gow[^1] with his colleagues.

Hardly anyone ever caused trouble in Vast Yang City. On the contrary, the merchants passing through would desperately curry favor with the city defense force.

'Lots of money, little work' was the perfect description for this position.

In the past, he had harbored quite a few grand ambitions. But after sitting in this position for over thirty years, he had long since grown a potbelly, and his Rank 8 Martial Dao cultivation had fallen into neglect.

However, right at that moment, a soldier outside came stumbling and crawling over.

"Commander! There's... there's an escape light heading toward Vast Yang City!"

The soldier hastily dropped to his knees, shouting at his commander who was currently at the card table.

The fat officer's face turned livid as he berated him, "What are you yelling for? What escape light? Haven't I told you all before? Anyone who dares to fly through Vast Yang City's no-fly zone definitely has substantial backing. Who knows, it might be the young master of some prominent family. Do you understand!?"

Scolded like this, the soldier immediately grasped his commander's meaning. He asked tremblingly, "Then, Commander, we just ignore it? Pretend we didn't see anything?"

"You're new here, aren't you? Then I won't hold it against you for ruining my mood. I'll give you a piece of advice: close your eyes and plug your ears—that's the only way you'll live a long life!" the fat officer said.

Hearing this, the surrounding card players all laughed merrily, and the atmosphere instantly turned cheerful.

The fat officer, embarrassed and angry, quickly waved his hand and told the soldier, "Scram! You've completely ruined my luck at the table! Do something this mood-killing again, and I'll strip you of your post!"

The soldier hurriedly scurried away, terrified of becoming the target of displaced anger.

Consequently, the escape light—which was visible to the entire city—casually charged right into Vast Yang City. Shockingly, not a single person in the entire city stepped up to stop her.

It wasn't that there were no other Rank 8 cultivators around, but they all felt that since the city defense force wasn't stopping her, the intruder must possess special permission.

The city defense force, meanwhile, felt that since the person dared to enter like this, they absolutely had powerful backing.

With both sides turning a blind eye, the bunny flew right in as if there was nobody around.

However, Shen Shuibi didn't sense anything amiss at all.

She had always done things this way in the past, and no one had ever tried to stop her.

_Find... Wang Boyan. He's from the Golden Wave Sect,_ Shen Shuibi thought to herself.

"Wang Boyan, Wang Boyan, Wang Boyan. Golden Wave Sect, Golden Wave Sect, Golden Wave Sect..." she kept muttering aloud, afraid she might forget.

However, halfway through her muttering, she suddenly slapped her forehead, remembering something. "Oh right, I can't let anyone see me. Li Qi said I need to be stealthy. This is Vast Yang City, someone else's territory."

With that thought, she cast a spell art upon herself.

A qi-concealing art. Back when she first encountered Li Qi, this was the exact technique she had used to hide within the black mist.

This move could mask all the qi fluctuations from her body, making her seem as if she didn't exist.

Similar to a stealth bomber, the naked eye could still see her, but it was incredibly difficult to detect her using investigative spell arts. Even Li Qi had only managed to sense her by relying on the power of his dao rhythm.

Originally, if Li Qi hadn't discovered her, she would have secretly slipped away along the edges of the black mist. She never expected Li Qi to grab her right out of it.

Still, it could be considered a good thing, she supposed.

No, it would be more accurate to say it made no difference.

Either way, that Grand Invocator wouldn't have let her leave.

While turning these thoughts over in her mind, she was simultaneously simulating techniques to restore her Yang Spirit. Using less than one ten-thousandth of her brain's processing power, Shen Shuibi began searching for the right path and the right person.

The bunny was never stupid.

It was just that for almost all her waking hours, her mental capacity was dedicated to pondering academic matters regarding Daoist arts.

Her Yin Spirit had already recovered with the help of the Moon Corporeal Soul fragments Li Qi had retrieved for her. However, transforming her Yin Spirit into a Yang Spirit still required a great deal of effort.

The Yang Spirit commands the internal deities of the brain palace, guiding the primal spirit to roam the high heavens; through movement, life is generated.

The Yin Spirit utilizes stillness to stabilize the physical form, born from the void. Through this harmonious interplay, the Yang Spirit is coalesced and born.

This requires Yang qi to project outward and Yin qi to cultivate inward. These two states interweave, conjoining with the primordial spirit[^2]. Inner truth stands guard, allowing the nine orifices to receive vitality[^3]. By protecting the ethereal soul and consolidating the corporeal soul, the myriad internal deities settle peacefully. Once the three passes[^4] are secured, spiritual radiance flourishes abundantly. Ascending alongside these deities, one collectively reaches the realm of Jade Purity[^5].

After all these steps are completed, one must swallow qi thirty-nine times to fortify the thirty-nine inner gates[^6]. This lustrous qi will thoroughly moisten and circulate throughout the entire body. If one can cast aside all burdens, remain unfettered by worldly dust, stabilize the spirit, fortify the soul, purge death qi from the nine orifices, and secure the palace of life at the upper pass... only then can the Yang Spirit be successfully forged.

The current problem was that her thirty-nine inner gates were practically shattered into ninety-three.

As for securing the three passes and nine orifices? Forget about it—those things were completely gone. Right now, even the meridians within her body were broken into countless severed segments. She relied entirely on constantly devising new methods every single day just to maintain her baseline vitality.

If her condition weren't so severe, her body wouldn't be this frail, to the point where she was even physically weaker than Li Qi.

If her body had been fully intact and only her cultivation base was gone, then relying solely on her Rank 5 physical vessel, she could clench her tiny fist and pummel Chanzhi to death with a single punch.

The gap between Rank 7 and Rank 5 was so vast it defied verbal description.

Unfortunately, there was no use talking about any of that. This was her current reality, and she could only take things slowly.

Therefore, over ninety-nine percent of Shen Shuibi's mental energy was spent configuring different Yin and Yang hexagram lines, combining them into various techniques, and then testing them one by one to see which would be effective for her.

Only a tiny fraction of her mental energy was allocated to processing external matters. This was what caused her to constantly appear so absentminded.

Just like she was doing right now.

Shen Shuibi landed in an unpopulated area and, much like a ghost, drifted aimlessly through the streets, searching.

She planned to conduct a carpet search, looking at every single signboard along the street until she found the Golden Wave Sect.

_That should work fine, right?_ She thought to herself.

And so she walked aimlessly, traversing street after street.

She walked for about two hours, wandering through over a dozen streets. She inspected every single signboard and storefront, yet she still hadn't found the so-called Golden Wave Sect.

Shen Shuibi started to grow a bit anxious.

Under normal circumstances, she would never panic.

To her, time was completely worthless. It was a common occurrence for her to zone out for decades at a time.

But things were different now. Li Qi had been captured by someone, and yet she couldn't find the method he had instructed her to use to save him.

_What is going on?_

Consequently, the bunny grew anxious.

So, she halted her mental deductions regarding the restoration techniques.

The previously absentminded bunny wandering the streets suddenly regained luster in her eyes; one could even faintly perceive a sharp glint flashing within them.

She had closed her background work applications. Now, all that occupied memory space was completely freed up.

_Golden Wave Sect... Li Qi came to Vast Yang City for the sake of grain and fodder. Operating from this motive, the people he would proactively seek out should also be involved in the grain business. Therefore, it's safe to deduce that the Golden Wave Sect is at least connected to the grain industry._

Her mind immediately began to spin with agility.

_If they are connected to the grain industry, then they definitely handle massive amounts of transportation and warehousing._

In less than a split second, a map of all Vast Yang City appeared within Shen Shuibi's mind. During the instant she flew over from the sky, she had already scanned the entire city using her peripheral vision. She had cast it out of her mind previously, but now that her mental memory was freed up, she effortlessly retrieved that complete recall.

Photographic memory was the most basic of functions for her.

The entirety of Vast Yang City—every street, every intersection, all the landmarks, the exact square footage—everything surfaced with absolute clarity in her mind.

_Warehousing and logistics operations, the required area and road access..._

She murmured internally, instantly laying out a series of routes and comparing them.

Two breaths later, she locked onto her targets.

Through statistics, analysis, and arranging all possibilities, she rapidly mapped out an investigative route that boasted the highest probability of success with the shortest distance traveled.

In a mere four or five seconds, Shen Shuibi transitioned from aimless wandering to a highly purposeful, targeted search.

She strode briskly down the street, practically kicking up a gust of wind in her wake.

She didn't choose to fly or use spell arts to hurry along, because the moment she started using her brain, she realized something was wrong with that Buddhist Pagoda in the distance.

That should be the location of Chanzhi, exactly as Li Qi had mentioned.

A Rank 7 cultivator of the Buddhist Sect.

Troublesome. It was best not to alert the other party. She shouldn't have been discovered during her rapid flight just now.

She arrived at this conclusion through professional analysis. That Buddhist Pagoda was currently still sealed inwards. Though it appeared unchanged from the outside, in Shen Shuibi's eyes, it was actually in a state of complete isolation from the external world. The true main body of the Buddhist Pagoda was currently situated Beyond the Boundary, cut off from everything outside.

It could be considered luck that she hadn't been seen.

She would need to be even more careful from now on.

The other party had no reason to stay their hand if they saw her. And if he did make a move, she would have no way to counter it.

In that case, she should avoid using her own cultivation base as much as possible and rely more on the locals to get things done.

These arrangements were all calculated in a flash. The bunny had finalized her entire plan in a mere instant.

A few minutes later, she ran up to her first destination.

"A fabric shop... No." She merely swept a glance over it, and upon confirming it was a fabric shop rather than a granary, she turned and left.

However, before she had even walked for a minute, she was intercepted.

Several shifty-eyed, shabbily dressed, and slouched street thugs blocked Shen Shuibi's path.

The leader looked Shen Shuibi up and down, a covetous expression appearing on his face. "Miss, I've watched you running around in a hurry to quite a few places. Are you lacking a place to stay, or lacking something to eat?"

The man beside him added with a sleazy grin, "If you don't have a place to stay, why not come with us? We can even find you a job to do."

Such an exquisitely beautiful woman, out showing her face in public like this.

It was likely because of the situation over at the border pass.

These thugs had been active lately.

Over at the brothels, the average prices had recently dropped by more than two hundred coins, entirely thanks to their 'hard work.'

And right now, looking at Shen Shuibi was like looking at solid gold to them as they surrounded her.

They had never seen such a beauty in their entire lives.

If they delivered her over, she would likely be the top courtesan for the next ten years. The commission they'd earn would flow like water.

Thus, they had observed her for a full two hours. Only after confirming that Shen Shuibi had no one by her side, possessed no cultivation base, and was running around aimlessly like a headless fly, did they finally reveal themselves.

The instant Shen Shuibi saw them, her eyes lit up.

Searching alone was far too slow, after all.

Therefore, Shen Shuibi nodded. "Really?"

Those men clearly hadn't expected this beauty to be so agreeable. Chuckling gleefully, they immediately said, "Of course!"

Following that, they led Shen Shuibi away from the area.

After this, they planned to dress her up. If she was obedient, everything would be easy. If she wasn't, they naturally had their own methods to deal with her.

The punishments they inflicted left no physical scars, but would leave most women wishing for death out of shame and indignation.

No woman could withstand it.

It was currently late afternoon.

The sun was setting in the west. The twilight rays were warm, bathing one's body in absolute comfort.

Wang Boyan was presently within his own mansion.

He wore a languid, unhurried expression, appearing utterly relaxed.

Writing and painting, dusting and washing inkstones, burning incense and brewing tea, arranging flowers in vases, hanging curtain hooks—he occupied himself with minor, effortless chores.

He didn't hand all these tasks over to the maidservants because doing them himself was good for the body. By occasionally engaging in light physical labor, the bones and blood vessels would not stagnate. It was exactly as the saying goes, 'Running water is never stale, and a door hinge never gets worm-eaten.'

While brewing tea, he sat on a recliner, waiting for the hot water to slowly steep the flavor from the tea leaves. Closing his eyes to rest his mind, he muttered to himself, "Hmm... judging by the time, a conflict should have erupted on Li Qi's end by now, right? That demonic cleric has probably reacted as well. Yes, with things progressing like this, the landscape grand array can be broken. This Vast Yang Prefecture is going to face the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest head-on~ I've struck it big, truly struck it big!"

It was obvious he was in an excellent mood.

However, right at that moment, a domestic servant came running in from outside and said to Wang Boyan, "Master! The Horse King of the Lower Nine Gates is asking to see you by name!"

Hearing this, Wang Boyan frowned and asked, "What do you mean, 'asking to see me by name'? Doesn't he know me? If he really wants to find me, couldn't he just stroll over here himself?"

The Lower Nine Gates did not refer to a single sect, but rather an alliance forged by nine gangs engaged in illicit, vile, and filthy deeds. Their power was considerable, akin to the Grain Alliance. They also had a Rank 8 cultivator secretly backing them from the shadows, and they controlled a whole slew of enterprises, including beggars, human traffickers, brothels, civil construction, gambling dens, and more.

The leader of the Lower Nine Gates was called the Horse King. No one knew how this title had originated anymore; regardless, the moniker had just been passed down over time.

The Horse King was the underground emperor of Vast Yang City. Much like this 'Master' behind the Grain Alliance, he grasped a corner of Vast Yang Prefecture's livelihood.

Everyone living in Vast Yang City was inevitably entangled with their businesses.

The servant replied in a panic, "No, they've changed to a new Horse King!"

"A new Horse King?!" Wang Boyan was shocked.

The forces of the Lower Nine Gates were deeply entrenched and complex; non-insiders couldn't even begin to comprehend their transfers of power. He also knew the previous Horse King—a man of immense prestige who had thoroughly convinced the masses to submit to him.

How could he have suddenly been replaced?

"Replaced by who? Which of the gate leaders within the Lower Nine Gates? No, that's impossible. The Lower Nine Gates have strict, layered rules; usurping power is absolutely out of the question. Just what in the world happened? Explain it to me in detail!"

"We aren't clear on what happened either! But the new Horse King is a woman. And the moment she sat on the throne, she specifically asked for you, Master!"

[^1]: A traditional Chinese gambling game. It literally translates to "Make Nine."
[^2]: The spirit starts as Yin (ethereal, formless, associated with ghosts and stillness). To achieve immortality, a cultivator must temper this Yin Spirit with Yang Qi (vitality, life force, movement). By anchoring the stillness of the Yin Spirit to the physical vessel and actively feeding it Yang Qi, the spirit undergoes a qualitative metamorphosis. It "coalesces," birthing a Yang Spirit—an astral projection that is no longer a mere phantom, but an entity with real mass and power that can interact with the physical world.
[^3]: The "nine orifices" are the physical openings of the human body: the two eyes, two ears, two nostrils, mouth, urethra, and anus.

    In Daoist alchemy, these orifices are considered "leaks." Every day, a mortal’s vital essence (Jing) and Qi bleed out into the world through seeing, hearing, speaking, and bodily functions. Furthermore, "evil qi" (pathogens, demonic energy) can enter through them.

    **"Inner truth stands guard, allowing the nine orifices to receive vitality":** This means that once a cultivator achieves a state of perfect mental clarity and Daoist enlightenment ("inner truth"), they can consciously seal these leaks. Instead of bleeding energy outward, the orifices draw ambient spiritual energy inward, trapping the vitality inside the body to fuel the Yang Spirit.
[^4]: **The Three Passes (三关)**

    This refers to the three major bottlenecks along the body’s primary meridian (the Governing Vessel / Du Mai), which runs up the spine.
    
    * **Tailbone Pass (Weilü):** Base of the spine.
    * **Spinal Pass (Jiazhi):** Middle of the back.
    * **Jade Pillow Pass (Yuzhen):** The base of the skull.

    To circulate Qi effectively, a cultivator must force their qi through these three incredibly tight bottlenecks. "Securing" them means the pathways have been blasted open and fortified, allowing a massive, unimpeded flow of energy from the base of the spine directly into the "Brain Palace."
[^5]: **The Realm of Jade Purity (玉清)**

    In Daoist theology, the cosmos is ruled by the Three Purities (Sanqing), the highest manifestations of the Dao. "Jade Purity" is the highest heaven among them, the residence of the Primeval Lord of Heaven. In the context of this passage, "attaining the Jade Purity" is a metaphorical way of saying the cultivator has achieved the absolute zenith of perfection, ascending to true divinity.
[^6]: **The Thirty-Nine Inner Gates (三十九户)**

    According to texts like the Yellow Court Scripture, the human body is a microcosm of the universe, inhabited by thirty-nine specific "internal deities" residing in specific "gates" or acupoints (organs, energy centers, joints). Fortifying these gates means ensuring that every single qi node in the body is functioning in perfect harmony.
