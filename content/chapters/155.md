# Chapter 155: Flowing Like Water

Jin Buhuan no longer hesitated, nor did he care about any potential losses. Surrounded by two streaks of Rank 8 metal qi, he thrust toward Li Qi.

One yin, one yang. One in the front, one in the back.

The former posed as a feint to test for weaknesses, while the latter carried the true weight of slaughter.

Yang shifted to seven, Yin remained at three. This was no longer a reckless, all-out thrust. Because yin and yang were intertwined, it birthed a myriad of variations, making it far more difficult to deal with.[^1]

However, the reason Li Qi had previously asked to spar with Jin Buhuan was precisely for this very moment.

In his eyes, Elder Si was illiterate.

Was Jin Buhuan any different?

Li Qi lashed out with his willow branch, using it to temporarily entangle the two streaks of metal qi and establish a defense.

This defense wouldn't hold for long. After all, the willow branch wasn't specifically meant for this, but it could still buy him about ten seconds.

Within those ten seconds, Li Qi initiated a grand rite. He cupped his hands, executing the ritual of sacrificing to the West.

Then, he opened his mouth and reprimanded Jin Buhuan, "Metal belongs to the West, where all things reach completion; it is the origin of killing intent! When ancient kings mobilized their armies and raised their banners, it was to punish wicked thieves, forbid tyranny, and bring peace to All Under Heaven! Slaughter must correspond with righteousness to align with the metal qi!"

"Metal represents punishment and warfare. If punishment is inflicted upon the innocent, and weapons are not laid to rest, propriety is lost, turning into arrogance and overbearance. Thus, the metal qi will be destroyed!"

He delivered this direct reprimand, utilizing the spirit of his words to rebuke him with universal principles.

Why did a Shaman's rite require preparation? Why did it necessitate quoting classics and invoking elaborate, historically significant incantations?

It was because the lengthy chants, the magnificent incantations, and the solemn rites possessed power in and of themselves.

This was the power of words and language.

In ancient times, when Cangjie invented writing, the heavens rained millet, and ghosts wailed in the night.

Even the heavens were terrified by these written words, causing grain to fall from the sky, while thousands of ghosts on earth wept endlessly into the night in inexplicable dread.

Because words and language inherently possessed astonishing power, all sorts of spells and talismans required written characters to bear their weight.

The saying, _When the brush falls, wind and rain are startled; when the poem is completed, ghosts and gods weep_, was actually an application of the power of words. It was said that within the Human Dao, there were several factions specializing in this path, known as the Calligraphy lineage and the Confucian lineage.

However, their utilization of words and language was actually learned from the Shamans.

Regarding the authority to interpret rites, the Confucians had even fought numerous battles with the Shamans. Both sides believed that sacrificial rites belonged to them, yet no definitive conclusion was ever reached.

But in the end, it was the Shamans who first utilized these elements. In the earliest sacrificial rituals, Shamans would loudly chant incantations to call upon the gods they wished to commune with.

Right now, Li Qi was wielding the power of the incantation to condemn the tyrannical metal qi. Through this, the metal qi itself would gain awareness and naturally begin to rebel against Jin Buhuan's innocent-slaying, warmongering, and unrighteous actions.

Invocators were skilled at communing with all things. This didn't just refer to entities like Heavenly Gods, Earthly Divinities, and Human Ghosts, but truly all things. Anything possessing a spirit—be it the Heavenly Dao or a mountain spirit—could be communed with by an Invocator.

In fact, even Li Qi seeking out people for discussions and rushing between the major factions of Clear Mist City all night could be considered a form of communion.

However, this kind of communion required the Invocator's own virtuous conduct as a guarantee.

If an Invocator frequently went back on their word, saying one thing and doing another, how could all creation believe their claims?

Therefore, an Invocator could be evil, but they could never break their trust with all creation. Even if they were wicked, they still had to treat sacrificial rites with absolute solemnity and seriousness; they could never go back on their word.

Along with the power of an Invocator, Li Qi conveyed his righteous incantatory words to those two streaks of metal qi.

Upon hearing his words, the metal qi—despite lacking true sentience—felt its spirit begin to waver.

Furious, Jin Buhuan forcefully drove the metal qi forward.

Fortunately, a Shaman's words were not absolute law and could not forcefully manipulate objects. Thus, the metal qi merely hesitated; its might was no longer as flawlessly sharp, but it still retained over eighty percent of its lethal power.

Fierce sharpness intertwined with tyrannical killing intent. Jin Buhuan no longer held anything back in his manipulation of the metal qi. Instead, he let it fully erupt, detonating with a thunderous boom right in front of Li Qi.

The previously undamaged willow branch actually splintered under the combined assault of the two streaks of Rank 8 metal qi.

This caused a portion of the metal qi to leak through.

The violent friction between the metal qi and the air sent sparks flying. Because it was still entangled by the willow branch, the core attack had not yet struck Li Qi, but the sheer shockwave of the forward thrust pierced right through him!

Li Qi didn't even have time to dodge before the surging force punctured his chest.

The metal qi pierced his chest, even injuring his heart meridian, dealing Li Qi a grievous blow!

Yet, at this moment, despite wincing and baring his teeth in pain, a gleam of success shone in Li Qi's eyes.

Heartblood flowed out, wrapping around the metal qi.

The heart belonged to the fire element. Fire was the extreme manifestation of Yang, and its Yang qi was incredibly potent. Using his heartblood as a catalyst, Li Qi guided this extreme Yang. Where fire triumphs, metal wanes. Within Li Qi's personal cosmos, his five internal organs had manifested as the Five Great Mountains, and among them, the Southern Mountain representing his heart suddenly erupted with power that surpassed its limits!

At the same time, the four seasons within Li Qi's personal cosmos naturally began to lose their balance.

It was only natural. The four seasons could only cycle properly if the five elements were functioning normally.

Currently, Li Qi's heart meridian was severely injured, causing the fire element to erupt in massive quantities. Although it temporarily enveloped the metal qi, how could a Rank 9 personal cosmos suppress Rank 8 metal qi?

Furthermore, Jin Buhuan was exerting his full strength to control the metal qi, intending to quickly restore its momentum and tear Li Qi apart from the inside.

The two sides were locked in a fierce struggle of strength.

Therefore, the suppression was only temporary, and it would also result in a massive drain on the fire qi within his body.

However, at this exact moment, Li Qi violently yanked his willow branch back.

A willow branch, without a doubt, belonged to the wood element.

The splintered willow branch released a massive amount of wood qi, along with scattered traces of the Grand Invocator's divine qi.

Without hesitation, Li Qi absorbed this massive influx of wood qi into his body.

Normally, fire and wood restricted and generated each other. Under a balanced state of the five elements, excess wood qi would be incinerated by fire qi, maintaining internal stability.

But now, almost all the fire qi within Li Qi's body was being used to suppress Jin Buhuan's metal qi.

Wood qi flourished immensely, while fire qi was practically nonexistent; even the heart's fire qi had been thoroughly depleted.

His personal cosmos was originally only lacking one element, but now, with one element missing and another overwhelmingly dominating the rest, the entire system completely lost control and fell out of balance.

The qi and blood of his internal organs, the waxing and waning of the sun and moon within his body, communicated and resonated with the righteous qi of heaven and earth. Such a drastic alteration caused the temporal seasons within his personal cosmos to plunge into chaos.

Jin Buhuan witnessed all of this and sneered coldly. _As expected. Although he possesses a Rank 8 dharma treasure, I have two! Now that I am forcefully driving two Rank 8 divine weapons without regard for the future consequences, how could Li Qi possibly match me?_

Once his personal cosmos collapsed due to the temporal imbalance, Li Qi would die instantly. He had absolutely zero chance of survival.

Thus, he forcefully drew in a breath. Veins bulged across his body as he ignored the damage to his own liver and lungs, pushing the metal qi even further. He made it emit the humming _shang_ note, attempting to shatter Li Qi from the inside out.

Barely twenty seconds had passed since their battle began, yet both sides had already reached the point of risking their lives.

But suddenly, Jin Buhuan felt something—

_What's going on?_

_The metal qi... it's out of control?_

_Impossible! Why is it out of control? Why won't it listen to my commands?!_

He didn't understand.

But Li Qi did.

Because at this very moment, the temporal imbalance within his personal cosmos had shifted its seasons from an early spring day that normally aligned with the outside world, to the tail end of deep summer and the very beginning of golden autumn.

This was commonly known as the Three Fu Days.

What exactly were the Three Fu Days?

_Fu_ meant hiding or concealing; these were the days when the metal qi was forced into hiding.

The four seasons cycled through mutual generation: at the Beginning of Spring, wood replaced water, as water generated wood; at the Beginning of Summer, fire replaced wood, as wood generated fire; at the Beginning of Winter, water replaced metal, as metal generated water. But at the Beginning of Autumn, metal replaced fire. Since metal feared fire, it was forced to hide on the Geng-Metal days.

In other words, at the end of summer, the autumnal metal qi would begin to rise, but the fire qi still retained its final, most intense burst of momentum. During this time, the fire would burn itself out, utilizing its final surge to suppress the metal qi one last time before finally yielding to the metal-dominated autumn.

This was why the third Geng day after the Summer Solstice was the First Fu, the fourth Geng day was the Middle Fu, and the first Geng day after the Beginning of Autumn was the Last Fu. Together, they were called the Three Fu, hence the name Three Fu Days.

During the Three Fu Days, the fire qi of heaven and earth was at its absolute peak, marking the hottest time of the year. Only with such intensity could the Three Fu suppress the autumnal metal qi.

Li Qi's recent massive eruption of heartblood fire qi, fueled by the massive influx of wood qi, perfectly matched the final, explosive combustion of fire qi during the Three Fu Days. His personal cosmos had lost its balance and its temporal seasons had fallen into chaos, landing precisely on the Three Fu Days.

During the Three Fu Days, metal qi was forced into hiding. It was the period when the natural timing countered it the most.

Using fire to overcome metal, across three consecutive Fu periods.

As a result, Jin Buhuan's streaks of metal qi had been continuously suppressed by Li Qi four or five times over!

First, he reprimanded it with logic, weakening it by thirty percent. Then, he drenched it in heartblood, weakening it by another thirty percent. Finally, utilizing his personal cosmos, he borrowed the temporal suppression of the seasons to weaken it by a final thirty percent.

That left it with only ten percent of its power.

Li Qi grabbed the metal qi and directly thrust it deeper into his own body.

Using his personal cosmos to completely suppress it!

Every step was perfectly calculated.

Flowing like water, from arrangement to execution, there wasn't a single flaw.

Li Qi had deduced it before: Jin Buhuan's cultivation method required an instrument to serve as a physical vessel.

By using his own body to suppress the two streaks of metal qi, he left his opponent without a vessel to use.

Then, he charged at Jin Buhuan.

Even while grievously injured, his physical constitution and martial arts techniques were still superior to Jin Buhuan's.

Seeing his metal qi temporarily suppressed, Jin Buhuan's heart tightened. He immediately pulled two small bells from his belt.

He calculated the time it would take for Li Qi to reach him and prepared to ring the bells, aiming to generate a murderous sound to disrupt Li Qi's footwork.

But Li Qi vastly exceeded his expectations.

Suddenly, Li Qi's entire body expanded as if he were being inflated.

Billows of white smoke spewed from his mouth and nose. His muscles bulged, his skin turned blood-red, and he grew an entire head taller.

It was the Ox Strength Art.

Since advancing to Rank 9, Li Qi had rarely used this spell art. It was primarily a practice technique used by unranked Invocators to tentatively attempt communing with gods—essentially, training wheels.

But right now, the sudden addition of six thousand pounds of raw physical strength pushed Li Qi's speed just a fraction faster.

Jin Buhuan had no time to react before Li Qi smashed a fist directly into his face.

The once-handsome face of the aristocratic young master instantly caved in, his nose bridge visibly crushed.

Stripped of his instruments, ninety percent of Jin Buhuan's combat prowess vanished.

He was completely outmatched.

It came back to the same old saying.

The Shaman Dao had provided Li Qi with knowledge.

The Martial Dao, on the other hand, rendered one nearly invincible in close-quarters combat among peers of the same rank.

Against a Jin Buhuan lacking ninety percent of his power, even a heavily injured Li Qi was more than enough.

_Thud!_ Another punch landed squarely on his nose, sending blood spurting as Jin Buhuan's nose skewed to one side.

Li Qi's physical strength surged, and he unleashed his combat techniques with the fluidity of water. While he lacked deeply forged, instinctual combat experience, pummeling a defenseless opponent was effortless.

Truthfully, if it weren't for the faction backing Jin Buhuan, killing him would have taken only a few minutes. None of his elaborate prior scheming would have been necessary.

This was the advantage of possessing a complete, orthodox inheritance.

_Crunch!_ Another punch directly shattered his skull.

Jin Buhuan instantly lost consciousness and completely lost his ability to move.

Yet, Li Qi didn't stop. He threw another punch.

However, right before the fourth punch landed, a sudden tolling of a bell erupted from within Jin Buhuan's body.

The bell's chime carried a rumbling resonance. The sound was sonorous, melodious, and thunderously loud, rich with a rhythmic cadence.

Accompanying the tolling from Jin Buhuan's body, an answering chime seemed to echo from the distant horizon.

Li Qi's heart skipped a beat!

An intense, suffocating sense of pressure crashed down upon him!

Looking toward Green Mountain, he saw a visible shockwave surging toward him!

A bell chime! A deafening, resonant tolling!

Despite being mere sound, it struck at a speed far exceeding the speed of sound.

Even from two thousand miles away, Li Qi could still vividly see that surging torrent of power.

It tore through the earth, obliterating the air currents with violent, berserk fury. An explosive tremor erupted toward the heavens from the direction of Green Mountain, its brilliance illuminating the entire firmament. Not even the sun and moon could obscure its radiance!

Though it sounded terrifying, the scene was breathtakingly beautiful. Streaks of golden light continuously tore across the sky, as if the firmament itself were expanding and fracturing.

Witnessing all of this, Li Qi felt a profound shock in his heart.

This was but a casual strike from the Rank 6 Green Mountain God. It had been passively triggered simply by the call of the protective metal qi within Jin Buhuan's body.

Yet even so, it appeared incredibly terrifying and magnificent.

Could a Rank 6 of the Divine Dao truly achieve such a feat?

Then what about him, when he reached Rank 6?

Li Qi closed his eyes, vividly recalling the time in the Great Deer Kingdom when he first activated his qi observation vision, watching the human qi of the entire city awaken with his own eyes.

That same sense of awe seemed to reappear at this very moment.

Yes, this majestic spectacle—wasn't this exactly why he cultivated?

To climb to the absolute peak, to witness more, grander, and more magnificent scenes with his own eyes. To gaze upon all of All Under Heaven, upon all of creation.

This scene was far more spectacular than that initial mortal county town.

Li Qi didn't even attempt to resist. He knew that in the face of such mighty power, he possessed absolutely no ability to fight back, and any attempt would be futile.

He simply wondered—how much more spectacular would the future be?

Brimming with anticipation, Li Qi raised his hand.

The battlefield trembled slightly.

A power that seemed to warp and invert space-time descended. Countless substances disintegrated upon impact, shattering into fundamental particles, revealing their most subtle structures in the void. Even space-time itself seemed to be unraveling.

Li Qi felt a power capable of decomposing and pulverizing everything surging from his fingertips.

However, wherever it passed, nothing was destroyed. Everything remained completely tranquil, as if the surging power was nothing more than a hallucination.

In reality, aside from him, no one else had noticed a thing.

Yet, a certain distant power, suspended high above the flow of time, had manifested here.

It seemed as though the past and future had merged into one.

No energy was released; there wasn't even a gust of wind.

However, Li Qi saw it with absolute clarity.

The event of the Green Mountain God attacking had been erased.

Within cosmic time, the event of the Green Mountain God attacking no longer existed.

This event of the Green Mountain God attacking had collided with itself at a different node in time.

The event collided with another possibility of itself from a different timeline, and the two mutually evaporated.

Time did not turn back, but the event itself was interfered with, causing its existence within cosmic time to be obliterated.

_Is this the power of 'Chen'?_

**Footnote:**

This passage explains a real-world concept from the traditional Chinese calender called the **Sanfu Days** (the Three Fu, or the "Dog Days" of summer).

In Chinese elemental theory, each season is ruled by an element: Spring is Wood, Summer is Fire, Autumn is Metal, and Winter is Water. Usually, the seasons transition harmoniously (e.g., Spring's Wood provides the fuel to generate Summer's Fire).

The transition from Summer to Autumn is unique because their ruling elements are hostile. Summer's Fire naturally melts and destroys Autumn's Metal.

The ancient Chinese calendar used a system called the Heavenly Stems to count days, assigning an element to each day. A "Geng" day is simply a day ruled by the Metal element.

When a Geng-Metal day occurs during the absolute peak heat of late summer, the residual Fire energy of the world is too overwhelming. To avoid being destroyed by the lingering Summer Fire, the Autumn Metal must go into Fu (hiding/dormancy).

Li Qi weaponizes the calendar itself. By artificially shifting his internal bodily environment to the "Sanfu Days"—the hottest, most oppressive time of the year where Fire forces Metal into submission—he creates a specialized temporal zone that perfectly counters and suppresses Jin Buhuan's metal-based divine weapons.

[^1]:
    - Yang (Seven Parts): Represents the "solid" or overt aspect of the attack—the aggression, the lethal power, and the killing intent.
    - Yin (Three Parts): Represents the "empty" or covert aspect—the reserved energy, the feints, and the defensive flexibility.
    
    An inexperienced fighter might strike with 100% force (ten parts Yang), which is devastating but completely commits their momentum, leaving them wide open if they miss or are parried. By keeping "Yin at three," Jin Buhuan is deliberately holding back 30% of his power and momentum. This reserved energy acts as a buffer, granting him the fluidity to instantly change his trajectory, launch a follow-up attack, or fall back into defense depending on how Li Qi reacts.
