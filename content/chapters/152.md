# Chapter 152: The "Chance" Encounter

It was as if the end of the world had arrived.

Amidst a mountain of corpses and a sea of blood, even Green Mountain had been dyed crimson.

Only scavenger birds perched alone atop the carnage, unaffected as they pecked at the remains of cultivators who were once so high and mighty.

Unknown years of bitter cultivation were reduced to mouthfuls of flesh and blood, gnawed upon by wild beasts.

Within Green Mountain, Jin Buhuan coughed up a mouthful of blood.

Even Elder Si appeared somewhat lethargic, seemingly having sustained rather heavy injuries.

"Phew, that was close. But to think Li Qi's words were actually true," Jin Buhuan said with a smile.

He wasn't worried about his own safety; that was the exact reason he dared to return.

This was Green Mountain, his home turf.

Here, he could lose, but he absolutely would not die. In the split second before his death, his father would undoubtedly sense it.

While others had to hold back thirty percent of their strength just to stay alive, only he and his brothers could afford to go all out without any reservations. How could their opponents possibly match that?

Thus, after a bloody slaughter, things had played out exactly as Jin Buhuan had planned.

Elder Si had already masked his weakness, returning to his usual demeanor. "This item is indeed genuine. However, just as that man said, it requires a streak of moderate, peaceful qi to act as a mediator. Only then can the yin and yang circulate smoothly and merge into one."

"It's just that there's one problem, Young Master. How could that Li Qi know so clearly? How could he even pinpoint the exact night this divine weapon would manifest?" Elder Si asked, carefully weighing his words. "Such matters elude even me. How could a mere Rank 9 cultivator possibly know?"

"It matters not. Everyone has their own methods; what's the point of digging deeper? The man remained unarrogant and unagitated even after I provoked him earlier, and he still chose to build a friendly rapport with me. This clearly shows he has no intention of coming into conflict with me."

"If he knows this much, he surely knows my father is on the verge of breaking through to Rank 5. Given my father's immense killing power, if he achieves Rank 5, even Shaman God Mountain will likely send an emissary down to confer a title upon him. By then, he won't just be the pillar of a mere kingdom, but a great god renowned throughout the Hundred Thousand Mountains of Baiyue. He would rank highly even among the Earthly Divinities of Baiyue."

As Jin Buhuan spoke, his face filled with pride. "The fact that he was willing to offer such information to build rapport after I provoked him proves he is well aware of my father's prestige."

Jin Buhuan had always been a sensible person, but when it came to his faith in his father, he had never wavered.

Throughout his entire life, from birth until now, no matter where he went or who he faced, his father had always stood tall and imposing. The concept of 'his father failing' simply didn't exist in his mind.

He had never even considered it.

His father was undefeated. Even now at Rank 6, he could withstand the frontal assault of a Rank 5 mighty figure in the outer realms. The aftershocks of their battle had even destroyed two minor worlds.

Having accompanied his father to the outer realms, Jin Buhuan had witnessed his father's divine might on those battlefields with his own eyes.

So majestic. So incredibly powerful.

He was so powerful that Jin Buhuan simply couldn't fathom anyone defeating him.

Jin Buhuan took a few deep breaths and wiped the bloodstains from his body. "Since he intends to show goodwill, it is time to mend our relationship. Once I return tonight, I will pay him another visit."

Jin Buhuan summarized, "Mending ties with him this time might elevate my foundation in Clear Mist City to another level. I believe he has the sense to accurately judge the situation as well."

After all, compared to the aging lord of the Green Mountain Kingdom—who hadn't progressed in an unknown number of years—the Green Mountain god, on the verge of breaking through to Rank 5, was clearly the more worthwhile figure to curry favor with.

Starting from his own logic, Jin Buhuan arrived at this conclusion with crystal clarity.

"Young Master is wise. Shall we depart and return now?" Elder Si asked.

"En. Let's go; we return to Clear Mist City," Jin Buhuan declared.

He then wiped down the metal qi he had snatched, prepared to ride the wind and clouds, and departed the area to head back to Clear Mist City.

---

Dawn was approaching, yet Li Qi was still rushing about through the night, navigating between various factions.

Overnight, relying on the spell arts of the Clear Mist Sect Master, Li Qi darted throughout the entire city, lobbying everywhere.

This was the plan Li Qi had devised.

What was the foundation of Jin Buhuan's power and faction?

Li Qi's analysis yielded three points.

First, his personal strength. He had two Rank 8 experts under his command, Lord Yan and Elder Si, which was already a force not to be underestimated in Clear Mist City. He himself controlled a streak of Rank 8 metal qi. Although he was only Rank 9, his combat prowess was extraordinary. Relying on the might of his weapon, he could briefly contend with Rank 8s and rapidly reap the lives of numerous Rank 9 cultivators.

Furthermore, he had many retainers, including hundreds of Rank 9 combatants. This was an exceedingly terrifying force.

A ranked cultivator was already a one-in-a-thousand warrior.

Standing at a high vantage point and looking down, Li Qi could finally see the whole picture.

Every single ranked subordinate controlled their own set of resources. They were like the boat tracker gang leaders or market bosses Li Qi used to deal with.

Hundreds of ranked cultivators meant hundreds of urban bosses, gang leaders, and foreman-like figures. These were all individuals of status and face. This was Jin Buhuan's grassroots strength, the tiny tentacles through which he controlled the city.

This was the primary component of Jin Buhuan's power, his very foundation.

That was the first point.

The second point was his supporters.

Through various means—be it bribery, coercion, or the exchange of interests—Jin Buhuan had a tightly knit group of factions rallying around him.

These factions were even more deeply entrenched; they were the local powers of Clear Mist City, the native tyrants.

These factions were only united because of Jin Buhuan himself. Their alliance was built on his personal prestige, or perhaps the prestige of his family.

Only with Jin Buhuan present could these people temporarily join forces. If he were replaced by one of his brothers, they wouldn't be able to achieve the same result.

To shatter these factions, Jin Buhuan had to be eliminated.

Once he was gone, they would naturally disperse on their own.

Finally, the third point was the existence of the Green Mountain God.

Why was Jin Buhuan able to consolidate his own faction while simultaneously recruiting a group of allies?

Aside from his own methods, it was because of his backer and background: the Green Mountain God.

Without the Green Mountain God, it would have been incredibly difficult for him to establish a foothold in Clear Mist City.

To dismantle Jin Buhuan's faction, these three points had to be neutralized simultaneously. Li Qi referred to them as Jin Buhuan's three legs.

First, his direct subordinates. Li Qi had already made arrangements for this.

Second, his peripheral allies. This required killing Jin Buhuan himself.

Third, his father's influence. This was the most difficult point. It was precisely because this issue couldn't be resolved, and the consequences were too heavy to bear, that Jin Buhuan had been left to operate unchecked for so long.

These three legs formed a triangular pillar, firmly propping up Jin Buhuan and establishing him as one of the de facto rulers of Clear Mist City.

And tonight, Li Qi intended to break all three legs.

The first leg—his direct subordinates, those tentacles—would be dealt with by a specific group of people.

These were the people Li Qi had assembled, persuaded by the promises he secured from the Clear Mist Sect.

Thus, tonight, Clear Mist City would likely be washed in a storm of blood and gore.

These factions would tear away all pretense of cordiality and violently suppress Jin Buhuan's tentacles.

As for the forces beyond the tentacles—Jin Buhuan's high-end combat power, namely the two Rank 8s and Jin Buhuan himself...

Li Qi would handle those personally.

Moving on to the second leg—the factions attached to Jin Buhuan—Li Qi had entrusted them to the Clear Mist Sect.

Yes, these were local tyrants, difficult to deal with.

But when it came to being a local tyrant, who could possibly compare to the Clear Mist Sect?

The very reason Li Qi sought out the Clear Mist Sect Master was to ask her to suppress these factions for a single night. Come morning, after Jin Buhuan's death, these clinging vines would naturally scatter like monkeys fleeing a fallen tree.

Finally, the third leg: the prestige of the Green Mountain God.

There was no easy solution for this. It was exactly because of this great god's existence that neither the lord of the Green Mountain Kingdom nor the Clear Mist Sect Master had dared to make a move against Jin Buhuan.

After all, Jin Buhuan was an extension of this great god's power, his method of keeping Clear Mist City in check. Jin Buhuan's presence signified that the mountain god also held a seat of power within the city.

In Li Qi's eyes, Clear Mist City was actually a chess piece used by the Green Mountain Kingdom's lord to check the Green Mountain God.

If the Clear Mist Sect or the kingdom's lord directly acted against Jin Buhuan, it would mean the kingdom's lord was declaring outright war on the mountain god. The two most powerful figures of the Green Mountain Kingdom would tear away all pretense, leaving absolutely no room for mediation. It could very well trigger an immediate civil war and the kingdom's schism.

That was why Li Qi was needed.

Li Qi voluntarily took on the role of the knife, drawing the wrath of the Green Mountain God. As an outsider, his appearance signaled the intervention of other factions.

When two tigers face off, one must die. But a battle for supremacy among three tigers introduces a multitude of variables.

With this layer of lubrication, if the Green Mountain kingdom's lord stepped in to protect Li Qi, the conflict wouldn't be as explosive. Moreover, it would protect Clear Mist City—the territory originally intended to be used as a balancing weight.

But that was only regarding the conflict between the kingdom's lord and the mountain god.

As the sharp knife itself, Li Qi would inevitably have to face the wrath of the Green Mountain God directly.

The sharper the blade, the easier it breaks.

This was exactly why the Clear Mist Sect Master had been suspicious of Li Qi's motives. In her view, Li Qi was simply seeking his own death.

The kingdom's lord could protect Li Qi *after* the fact, but in that very first instant, Li Qi would have to face the direct fury of the Green Mountain God, Jin Chi.

That first instant had to be faced by Li Qi alone.

The Clear Mist Sect Master believed it was certain death.

A Rank 9 facing a Rank 6, and not just any Rank 6, but Jin Chi, who had already stepped into the ranks of the strongest... No matter how she looked at it, she couldn't find a path to survival for him.

The disparity between them defied description.

Back when a Rank 9 Li Qi faced the Curse Demon King, merely catching a glimpse of his true form had nearly crushed him entirely, pulverizing him from flesh to divine soul.

If he truly had to withstand a split-second attack face-to-face, there was absolutely no logical reason he would survive.

And it was impossible to have the kingdom's lord prepare an interception in advance, or else wouldn't that just prove Li Qi was sent by the lord?

In other words, Li Qi had to rely on himself to withstand the Green Mountain God for a single instant before the kingdom's lord could step in to protect him. Only through this could the illusion of a three-tiger supremacy battle be created.

If Li Qi couldn't even hold on for an instant, it would prove there was no one backing him, and the third tiger simply didn't exist.

Although, in reality, there really *wasn't* a third tiger.

Conversely, as long as Li Qi managed to hold on...

Then, Jin Buhuan's third leg would also be severed. The appearance of a third tiger, allying with the kingdom's lord, would hold off Jin Chi's pressure.

With that, all three legs would be broken consecutively. All of Jin Buhuan's power within Clear Mist City, including his own life, would vanish like smoke and scatter like ashes.

Only then would Jin Buhuan truly be considered "toppled."

Every trace of this young master would be erased by others.

Even if someone else took over later, it would be one of his other brothers and have nothing to do with Jin Buhuan.

Li Qi had said he wanted to topple Jin Buhuan.

From the very roots.

Breaking down the goal of toppling Jin Buhuan meant severing his three legs. Breaking it down further, it was the lobbying and maneuvering he had spent the entire night running around doing.

Ultimately, two tasks fell upon Li Qi's shoulders.

First, eliminate Jin Buhuan himself and the Rank 8s by his side. Furthermore, the Clear Mist Sect would provide no assistance; this was to prove that Li Qi was acting on his own and not under orders from the Green Mountain kingdom's lord, thereby creating the illusion of three tigers vying for supremacy.

Second, face the Green Mountain God head-on for at least an instant, again without assistance, for the same reason.

Pondering all of this, right up until he left the final faction, Li Qi hurried outside the city.

He sat on a rock outside the city gates, waiting quietly.

As he waited, the surrounding weather and climate gradually began to change.

The influence of his personal cosmos was steadily expanding.

The wind grew gentler, the ground leveled out, and the surrounding qi gradually shifted to accommodate Li Qi.

This was the method of using one's personal cosmos to alter the local environment. If a Great Shaman were to execute this move, their mere presence could force the local heaven and earth to yield, dictating that all surrounding rules revolve around them.

Around Li Qi, the sunlight slanted downward, its color bright and clear. The azure sky sparkled like crystal, serene as deep autumn waters.

The atmosphere of the entire world grew more peaceful and gentle.

Li Qi took a deep breath, adjusting his condition.

Jin Buhuan was no weakling.

He also had Elder Si and that maidservant by his side.

In other words, one Rank 8 and two Rank 9s.

Honestly, Li Qi wasn't a hundred percent certain.

But then again, what in life ever comes with a hundred percent certainty?

Two hours later, the morning sun rose.

Li Qi opened his eyes.

A voice called out.

"Brother Li, why are you out here so early in the morning? Cultivating?" Jin Buhuan asked with a chuckle as he floated down from the sky, snapping his folding fan shut.

"Not cultivating, really. I was mostly waiting for you, Brother Jin. After all, I am quite curious—did Brother Jin succeed or not?" Li Qi stepped forward, asking with a smile.

"Thanks to you, everything went smoothly. After today, I must certainly host a banquet to thank Brother Li for his guidance. If you hadn't warned me, this item truly would have fallen into my brother's hands~." Jin Buhuan answered with a grin.

"As long as everything went well, that's what matters. I actually have another matter to discuss with Brother Jin. Could you step forward for a moment?" Li Qi asked.

"Haha, no problem. We're all friends here; there's no need to hide anything," Jin Buhuan said nonchalantly, seemingly unbothered.

"No, no, no, this matter is far too important. Otherwise, why would I come all the way out here specifically to wait for you?" Li Qi said in a highly serious tone.

Jin Buhuan frowned, then stepped forward.
