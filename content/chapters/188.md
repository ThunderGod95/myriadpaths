# Chapter 188: An Anomaly

What should have been a pitch-black journey was suddenly torn open by the empowerment of his dao rhythm, revealing the magnificent spectacle of this boundless space.

This was Beyond the Boundary.

It was the true appearance of this universe.

Li Qi felt deeply gratified, yet simultaneously, an even greater insatiability gnawed at him.

A large part of his purpose in cultivation was to witness an even grander path.

He had laid eyes upon such a magnificent sight now, but what lay above it? What kind of scenery existed at an even higher vantage point?!

Infinite worlds drifted within this sea of the void, operating methodically according to some profound, esoteric laws.

Ordinarily, Li Qi should not have been able to see any of this, for darkness had shrouded him.

But the progress bar upon Li Qi—the Outer Dao dao rhythm that Young Master Zhu had spoken of—helped him pierce through the darkness to behold the majestic landscape around him.

However, the anomaly occurring within the progress bar did not stop there.

In this moment, the progress bar... seemed to let itself go entirely?

Li Qi did not know how to describe it, but he could sense that upon leaving All Under Heaven, a certain 'restraining' or 'suppressing' force had vanished.

Young Master Zhu had once said that his progress bar was a manifestation of the Outer Dao dao rhythm.

Fundamentally, it was the presence of a dao rhythm providing him with information about his surroundings through a method he could comprehend.

If this same dao rhythm were placed upon someone else, it might not take the form of a progress bar. Instead, it might manifest as ethereal book pages floating in the void, or perhaps bamboo slips.

Whether it was a progress bar or something else, it was merely a type of manifestation.

But now, this outer skin of 'manifestation' was being ruptured by a power bursting forth from within.

It was like a long spring that had been compressed into a flat disc and wrapped up tight, appearing from the outside to be nothing more than a solid plate of iron.

But now, the weight compressing that spring had disappeared.

The spring was restoring itself, tearing its packaging apart from the inside out. It reverted to its original form, stretching its body to the fullest.

The progress bar flickered, and then, it shattered.

At first, it had only torn apart the surrounding darkness, allowing Li Qi to witness countless wonders.

But now, after shattering, the progress bar vanished completely.

"This dao rhythm... is it integrating into my body?" Li Qi sensed that his personal cosmos was actively absorbing the dao rhythm.

In other words, his personal cosmos was gaining some laws that did not originally belong to it.

Li Qi did not know whether this was a good thing or a bad thing.

But he had no way to stop it.

It was as if the dao rhythm had long since become one with him; at this moment, it was merely revealing its true form.

Li Qi could feel it... he was developing extra senses.

So it turned out that in the past, the progress bar had merely taken what this sense perceived and presented it to him in the visual format of a bar.

The essence of this dao rhythm was... an entirely different perspective.

The sensation was bizarre.

It felt as though a blind fish had suddenly grown eyes.

Blind cave fish possessed no eyes. They were born sightless, relying entirely on touch and hearing to perceive the world around them.

Naturally, they grew accustomed to a world composed only of sound and solid matter, utterly lacking the concept of 'sight'.

By the same logic, humans were no different.

Humans were accustomed to a world composed of sound, light, touch, and taste. To the vast majority, this was the fundamental nature of reality.

However, only when a blind fish grew eyes and truly saw 'light' for the first time would it realize that the world held far more than just touch and sound.

Li Qi was this blind fish.

The dao rhythm that had completely merged with him was this newly opened eye.

To Li Qi's senses, the world had fundamentally shifted.

Within those magnificent scenes from earlier, he now saw the presence of something more.

First, there was gravity.

To his astonishment, Li Qi found that he could actually perceive the existence of gravity.

He closed his eyes, immersing himself in this strange, brand-new sense.

It wasn't something he saw, but rather... another profound kind of perception. It was a wondrous sensation he had never experienced as a human being, entirely distinct from the five traditional senses.

He could feel gravity acting like chains, locking these myriad worlds together.

Gravity pierced through all layers, penetrating all dimensions.

Li Qi could sense that the geometry created by the presence of mass in spacetime formed gravity.

Therefore, as long as spacetime existed, gravity was an absolute certainty. And since dimensions and planes inevitably possessed spacetime—as well as the means to describe it—gravity could cross all dimensions.

Yet, there was another force actively pushing these worlds apart. Li Qi did not know what it was, but he could feel it.

The two forces remained locked in a stalemate, ensuring that these countless worlds were neither cast away nor crushed together.

Li Qi realized with shock that he could feel the ripples in space, the spatial curvature caused by mass which subsequently generated gravity, and the very state of his current existence.

It seemed this novel sense of his could perceive space itself. Beyond that, it almost felt like it could... predict?

Wait. Space and time... they appeared to be a single entity.

Time and space were inseparable.

So, the essence of the progress bar was observing space, comprehending time, and then... formulating a prediction?

Then, once the progress bar filled up, where did the ability to instantly memorize books and master certain skills come from?

Li Qi was consumed by doubt from head to toe, scrutinizing the ability he had grown so accustomed to using.

He was used to the progress bar's presence. But now, the transformations that occurred after truly leaving All Under Heaven left him deeply bewildered, to the point where he almost forgot he was in the midst of peril.

Since he had no control over his situation anyway, he figured he might as well spend the time pondering what this truly was.

And so, like an infant opening its eyes for the very first time, Li Qi began to experiment with his brand-new senses.

First of all, where was he right now? What was this darkness?

Thus, he began to inspect the gloom shrouding him.

The darkness had not vanished, and his eyes still could not see. But through his Outer Dao dao rhythm, he was able to bypass these physical limitations. The dao rhythm provided him with perception through an alternate method, allowing him to witness the grandeur from earlier.

This darkness... did it resemble a tunnel?

He was currently situated within a... narrow tube.

A slender, dimensional conduit that pierced through space had enveloped him, and it was currently hurtling him away toward some unknown destination.

He was shuttling through this conduit, and it was through its walls that he had glimpsed the many wondrous sights of Beyond the Boundary.

Wait a minute, if that was the case! His ability had nothing to do with space!

Li Qi instantly realized the truth.

The only reason his attention had been entirely captivated by space was because space was the only thing here!

The progress bar... or rather, the true essence of his Outer Dao dao rhythm, was the analysis of all existing things in his surroundings!

Once the analysis was complete, he would receive the corresponding information!

Upon realizing this, Li Qi immediately began testing the other items on his person.

First were the two strands of metal qi accompanying him.

Next was the mustard seed pouch.

Then his clothes, and finally, himself.

After about two or three minutes, Li Qi confirmed it. This was the fundamental nature of his progress bar.

No, he couldn't call it a progress bar anymore.

This was his very own dao rhythm.

It was just like Jin Buhuan’s 'Listening to the Heart’s Voice' back in Clear Mist City, or the'Devil Seed' wielded by the Grand Diviner’s disciple, Bo Yue, in the Earth Oil World.

It could probe the objects around him, judge their conditions, and analyze them. Once the analysis concluded, it could even categorize and directly input the resulting information into his mind.

The progress bar was merely the visual manifestation of this dao rhythm.

Only now did this wisp of dao rhythm truly unveil its authentic form before Li Qi.

Probe, analyze, and then catalog into memory.

It seemed that everything in creation fell within the analytical scope of this dao rhythm, from rudimentary movements to highly complex structural compositions.

"Since that's the case, I shall name you 'True Knowledge'. This way, when I introduce it to others in the future, I'll have a proper name to give, just like Bo Yue did."

"Truly miraculous. What exactly is the underlying principle behind this thing? How does it accomplish this?" Li Qi marveled aloud.

At the same time, he realized something else.

Without a moment's hesitation, he calmed his mind and centered his focus.

He could sense it. Condensing his primal spirit no longer required that so-called treasure from the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

Before reaching Rank 5, absolutely no treasure could compare to this single strand of dao rhythm!

This was a dao rhythm that even the Grand Invocator had praised. Although his teacher was incredibly unreliable as a person, Li Qi had absolute faith in the old man's discerning eye!

He was going to use this to condense his primal spirit!

As for his safety, he wasn't worried in the slightest.

The monk Chanzhi definitely wouldn't kill him. The monk still had a use for him, so Li Qi harbored no fears for his own well-being.

To think that venturing Beyond the Boundary this time would yield such an unexpected boon.

The Outer Dao dao rhythm, freed from the suppression of All Under Heaven, had reverted to its true form, ironically making it his most optimal choice.

He cleared his mind and suppressed all stray thoughts.

Gathering his focus and stilling his qi, his personal cosmos began to actively embrace the complete 'True Knowledge' dao rhythm.

The condensation of the primal spirit followed its own specific process.

As the saying goes, 'All who possess blood and qi inherently harbor the Primal Unity[^1] as their essence, draw upon Yin and Yang to establish their nature, and embody the Five Elements to manifest their physical form. Provided a physical form exists, one may yet seek and attain it.'[^2]

Harboring the Primal Unity as one's essence was the critical key to condensing the primal spirit.

Within the body, the Five Great Mountains, the Four Major Rivers, and the Twin Luminaries each possessed their own unique traits. They formed the internal deities: the mountain gods of the Five Great Mountains, the water gods of the Four Major Rivers, and the Heavenly Gods of the Sun and Moon.

As the saying goes, _The Five Great Mountains govern the Five Elements, the Four Major Rivers connect the four cardinal directions, and the Twin Luminaries clarify Yin and Yang._

The primal spirit, however, stood independent of them all. Its greatest defining characteristic was its complete lack of characteristics.

A primal spirit lacking characteristics valued balance and harmony above all else.

An essence of balance and harmony must be plain and flavorless. Because of this, it could harmonize the five materials, facilitate the shifting of the four seasons, and align with the rhythms of Yin and Yang. Thus, it was capable of occupying the supreme position and held the right to be called _Primal_.

Regarding the primal spirit, its essence was nameless. It was salty yet not abrasive, plain yet not mundane, substantial yet unadorned, refined yet not ostentatious. Therefore, it could wield authority while remaining compassionate, speak eloquently while knowing when to hold its tongue, and adapt to external changes, achieving absolute impartiality.

Serving as the central coordinating internal deity, the primal spirit was condensed within the brain, perfectly synchronizing with the intrinsic traits of the _True Knowledge_ dao rhythm.

A well-functioning primal spirit was akin to a flawless central mechanism. It allowed a person’s Yin and Yang to be clear and harmonious, ensured the constancy of their five essences, and granted inner wisdom paired with outward clarity. In motion, it illuminated the exterior like the blazing sun; in stillness, it reflected internally like tranquil water over gold.

The _True Knowledge_ dao rhythm completely transitioned from being an 'Outer Dao dao rhythm' into an integrated part of Li Qi's body, transforming into a fundamental law of his personal cosmos.

The familiar progress bar was gone forever.

Because the true essence of this dao rhythm now belonged solely to Li Qi. It was no longer an external object, but a literal piece of his own being.

And through this, his primal spirit was successfully formed.

By the time Li Qi opened his eyes again, the condensation of his primal spirit was fully complete.

Li Qi's foundational accumulation was incredibly deep. Condensing an internal deity was meant to be a natural progression where water flows and a channel is formed; he had merely been dragging his feet and biding his time previously.

Now that he was actively pushing the process forward, everything fell seamlessly into place.

The primal spirit was formless yet possessed substance. It lacked a concrete shape; instead, it permeated his entire body, beginning to coordinate the intricate operations of his personal cosmos.

However, upon its successful condensation, Li Qi immediately felt that his body had become far more harmonious and responsive to his will.

The principles of life and nature—profoundly clear, genuine, and constant—flowed and evolved naturally. This harmonious integration permeated his entire body. From the inside out, there were no longer any divisions; it was an absolute, flawless whole.

The flow of qi within his body moved without danger or urgency, undulating like a snake gliding across the water's surface—vibrant and alive, retaining stillness without stagnation. It spiraled without rigidity, forming perfect, boundless circles. His physical form was relaxed, his qi was harmonious, and all traces of discord were completely shed.

Li Qi clenched his hand into a fist, then released it.

His mastery over his own strength had elevated by... he didn't even know how much.

Li Qi felt that he could now throw a punch from a hundred meters away, and the resulting wind from the strike would perfectly peel the shell off a hard-boiled egg without damaging the egg white in the slightest.

Moreover, he was willing to bet that this feeling was absolutely correct.

Because, with the assistance of the _True Knowledge_ dao rhythm, he now possessed near-absolute control over his own body!

His primal spirit now possessed a total, comprehensive understanding of his physical vessel!

Every single muscle fiber within his body, every inch of space within his personal cosmos—he perceived it all with unparalleled clarity. All of his body's secrets had been unraveled. There was no longer a single aspect of his physiology beyond his control.

His stomach, intestines, heart, and blood vessels had all been brought under the direct, subjective manipulation of his conscious mind.

If he so desired, he could even bring his cellular metabolism to a grinding halt.

His control had reached the absolute microscopic level.

He had also completely mastered those two mouthfuls of metal qi.

With an internal deity condensed, the durability of his personal cosmos increased proportionately, rendering his cultivation base even more refined and solidified.

"The _True Knowledge_ dao rhythm is still here, and its effects remain. It's just that there is no longer a progress bar. But this is actually better; my perception is far more acute now," Li Qi muttered to himself, before opening his eyes.

Upon opening his eyes, he realized that the transit through the void had long since concluded.

And sitting directly in front of him was the monk Chanzhi.

Sometime during his cultivation, he had arrived atop Monk Chanzhi's five-tiered Buddhist Pagoda.

Monk Chanzhi seemed to have been waiting for Li Qi for quite some time.

Seeing Li Qi awaken, he took the initiative to speak. "Little Benefactor possesses commendable concentration. To be summoned by this poor monk and show not a shred of panic, but instead cultivate along the journey—your mental fortitude far surpasses that of ordinary men."

"Furthermore, your innate talent is utterly astonishing. To assimilate a dao rhythm into your own body while only at Rank 9... When my respected master bestowed a dao rhythm upon me in the past, this poor monk could barely utilize it during my time at Rank 9. It was only after reaching Rank 7 that I could completely integrate and manifest it to form this Buddhist Pagoda. For you, Little Benefactor, to achieve complete integration at Rank 9, your aptitude is truly top-tier."

"Exceptional mental fortitude, outstanding concentration, and top-tier talent. I can only assume you hail from an illustrious lineage. Would you care to divulge a thing or two to this poor monk?"

Listening to Chanzhi's gentle words, Li Qi's vigilance instantly spiked to the absolute maximum.

Yet, Li Qi put on a simple, honest smile and replied earnestly, "I won't hide it from you, Great Master. This humble one hails from Shaman God Mountain, an orthodox lineage of the Shaman Dao. I will definitely be able to assist Great Master in expelling those devilish thoughts and restabilizing your Buddha heart."

Li Qi didn't utter a single syllable about the unpleasantness that had transpired between them earlier, acting completely as if it had never happened.

[^1]:
    **Primal Unity (元一 - Yuányī):** In Daoist and traditional Chinese philosophy, the "Primal Unity" (also referred to as the Primordial One) is the original, undifferentiated state of the universe before it split into Yin and Yang. It represents the purest, foundational energy or essence that makes up the core of all existence. In the context of cultivation and internal alchemy, it is the unblemished, fundamental root of life that cultivators seek to understand and harness.
[^2]:
    This passage is a direct quotation from the Renwuzhi (人物志, often translated as The Study of Human Abilities), a 3rd-century philosophical text written by Liu Shao during the Three Kingdoms period. Originating from the chapter "The Nine Signs" (九徵), it summarizes the classical Chinese cosmological view: all living beings are born from a singular fundamental essence, their natures are shaped by the dual forces of Yin and Yang, and their physical bodies are constructed by the Five Elements.

    The crucial final line—"Provided a physical form exists, one may yet seek and attain it" (苟有形质，犹可即而求之)—means that although a person's inner essence and potential are formless and hidden, they inevitably manifest through a tangible physical vessel. Therefore, by observing, studying, and refining the physical body, one can trace back to and master the intangible inner essence. In the context of this chapter, it explains why Li Qi is able to use his physical, structured "personal cosmos" (which has form) as the foundation to successfully seek out and condense his Primal Spirit (which is inherently formless).
