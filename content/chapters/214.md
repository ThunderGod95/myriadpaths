# Chapter 214: Things Go Awry

Forty-six days.

A full forty-six days had passed.

Li Qi had tread upon every inch of Vast Yang.

Ever since then, he had buried his head in his work, sorting out and meticulously deliberating over every procedure required to construct a Grand Rite.

This was an exceedingly difficult endeavor.

His hair was now a disheveled mess, and his eyes were heavily bloodshot, yet they still gleamed with piercing clarity. His face bore the marks of exhaustion, but his movements remained swift, and his reflexes were entirely unimpaired.

Buried amidst countless scrolls and dossiers, he painstakingly carved out every detail of what this sacrificial rite must entail.

Burning firewood to sacrifice to Heaven; burying offerings to sacrifice to Earth; offering red calves to the seasons; `raising pit-altars for cold and heat`; erecting palaces for the sun; illuminating the night for the moon; retreating into the obscure for the stars; praying at rain-altars for floods and droughts; establishing the `four pit-altars for the four seasons`; and venerating the hundred gods of All Under Heaven. 

This was the essence of ⟦Sacrifice⟧.

Those who impart laws to the people are to be venerated; those who toil unto death in their duties are to be venerated; those who stabilize the realm through their labor are to be venerated; those who ward off great calamities are to be venerated; those who defend against immense disasters are to be venerated. The heavens, the earth, the four directions, the mountains, and the rivers—they are all to be venerated in accordance with their proper seasons. 

This was the essence of ⟦Veneration⟧.

Only by clearly distinguishing these differences and scaling them according to the specific circumstances could one proceed. A Grand Rite required the assembly of countless individual, minor sacrifices and venerations. Furthermore, it demanded that all parties be coordinated according to precise specifications to complete the ceremony together. It was astonishingly complex.

Back when Li Qi was in Li Prefecture City, the annual River Breaking ceremony was, in truth, a formalized sacrificial rite.

The entire city, over a million people, would be mobilized. Everyone had their own designated duties—what each individual did, what each gang did, and the exact times they were to act—all was arranged.

Even what they ate on that day was dictated by custom.

Only through such meticulous orchestration could the River Breaking ceremony achieve its grand scale, bringing substantial benefits to both the God of the Li River and the Governor of Li Prefecture. Even those at the very bottom, like Li Qi in his days as a tracker, could partake in a share of the blessings, receiving rewards and meat.

Similarly, during festivals like the Ghost Festival, all of All Under Heaven would perform ceremonies simultaneously on that single day. Everyone would burn paper money, venerate their ancestors, and offer tributes.

A Grand Rite performed by an untold number of people concurrently was even more incomparably intricate. Depending on the region and the nation, the rituals performed by the people varied, yet these very variations were exactly what the overarching Grand Rite required.

Just thinking about it was enough to realize the sheer magnitude: mobilizing the populace of All Under Heaven to do the same thing at the same time, with each group carrying out different roles, and then solidifying all of this into tradition...

He had no idea how it had been popularized across all of All Under Heaven... but regardless, it had solidified into custom.

What Li Qi needed to do now was the exact same thing, albeit on a slightly smaller scale.

When Shaman God Mountain promoted the Ghost Festival, they promoted it to all of All Under Heaven.

Li Qi, on the other hand, had to orchestrate everything that the tens of millions of people in Vast Yang would do within a single day.

On that day, every single person had to follow his arrangements, performing all manner of rituals to produce various effects and generate different types of qi.

Through Li Qi's coordination, all of this qi would converge at the main sacrificial altar in the center. There, the different strands of qi would undergo subtle reactions, ultimately achieving his desired outcome.

Just hearing about it conveyed its immense complexity; the calculations required were beyond measure.

What kind of ritual would produce what kind of qi.

Which types of generated qi would merge, what reactions they would trigger, and what effects those reactions would subsequently yield.

This infinitely expanding chain reaction contained hundreds of millions of nodes. Each node would produce a distinct effect, which would then trigger chain reactions with other nodes.

Relying solely on reading made it exceedingly difficult to avoid even a single omission. He had to conduct small-scale experiments, step by step.

As Li Qi organized this knowledge, he arranged the steps according to the actual conditions of Vast Yang, refining his plans down to specific villages and individual blocks within the county towns.

In truth, it shouldn't have been this granular; over-complication often led to errors. But Li Qi could only prepare for the worst. He had to be precise first to confirm the effects, and then gradually modify the plans later to increase the margin for error.

This increased his workload tenfold, but it effectively mitigated the risks.

The consequence, however, was that he had to hurry.

Over forty days had already passed.

If he truly dragged this design process out for a year, the number of people who starved to death by then would not stop at a mere hundred thousand.

Moreover, once the design was complete, it still had to be implemented, which would consume even more time.

He had to be fast, as fast as possible. If he waited until everyone was dead, everything he had done prior would become a bitter joke.

Thus, aside from the bare necessities of eating and resting, Li Qi poured almost all of his time into this endeavor.

While Li Qi was diligently working, the sound of knocking suddenly echoed from outside the door.

"Mister Li... it is time for lunch." A maidservant walked in, carrying some meat broth and meat pies.

Such fare was far too austere for lunch, entirely unfitting for Li Qi's status.

However, this was Li Qi's explicit request. If every meal were prepared according to normal standards, eating would simply take up too much time.

Meat pies, meat broth, and a few vegetables mixed in—a meal that could be finished in two or three bites. It was highly efficient.

"Oh, thank you." Li Qi lifted his head from his endless reading of documents and accepted the food with a smile.

Li Qi had been burying his head in his work here for over a month.

Yet, during all this time, aside from eating, Li Qi spent every moment sitting here, reading through materials gathered from all directions.

These materials included more than just the Book of Invocation. They also encompassed documents and ancient texts he had requested Senior Le Ya to gather, including confidential records from within Shaman God Mountain. Among them were key sacrificial points, layout methods, and myriad experiences penned personally by Grand Invocators.

This was the advantage of a great power.

These books alone—even just a few isolated phrases from them—would be enough to trigger a storm of blood and slaughter in the outside world.

Yet Li Qi could casually flip through and even nitpick such precious treasures.

These materials were vastly more voluminous than he had anticipated, totaling perhaps one or two hundred million words. Furthermore, every word was a pearl; the text was refined to the absolute extreme. Each sentence had to be thoroughly chewed and savored to be understood. He could not simply skim ten lines at a glance.

Some even came with illustrations and recordings. Some were even holographic illusion explanations, directly projecting the historical scenarios into his mind for practical demonstrations. It was incredibly advanced.

But this made it even more time-consuming.

He had to extract the crucial components from them as quickly as possible. This could not be delayed.

Thus, just as he was doing now, aside from meals, Li Qi read through these materials without sleep or rest, not daring to miss a single word.

Even while eating, he read simultaneously.

He casually tore the meat pie apart and swallowed it whole with the broth, finishing everything in a little over a minute. This counted as a meal.

The maidservant standing nearby watched Li Qi with a hint of worry. "Mister Li... you are pushing yourself too hard. Why don't you rest for a while?"

"There is no need to worry. You may take your leave," Li Qi shook his head and replied casually.

"Yes." Not daring to say more, the maidservant retreated from the room.

Outside the room, Shen Shuibi was sitting beside a stone table in the courtyard.

Seeing the maidservant emerge, she turned her head and asked, "The same answer as before?"

"The same, Milady... I am afraid you will still have to persuade him yourself," the maidservant replied with trepidation.

"No need to persuade him. What is the point of that?" Shen Shuibi tilted her head. "You may go. There is no need for you to come back later. Just leave things here to me."

"Yes." The maidservant nodded, then backed away in fear and reverence.

It was for the best if she did not have to come here.

She had heard that Young Master Li was a man who accomplished great deeds. Every time she stood near him, she felt a palpable pressure. Whenever he spoke, her heart would thump wildly in her chest.

She was very nervous. If she did not have to come back, although she might feel a slight sense of loss, she would also be able to relax considerably.

However... she had heard many people say that this Mister Li was like a sage. Thus, she likely wouldn't be beaten to death for making a mistake.

She heard that his current state of intense busyness was for the sake of all of Vast Yang.

But she found it somewhat hard to believe.

Vast Yang in its entirety... was so massive. What could the meager strength of a single person possibly achieve?

The maidservant retreated, her heart harboring a bellyful of questions.

On the other hand, Shen Shuibi tidied herself slightly and pushed the door open to enter.

Li Qi frowned, not even lifting his head. "Is there a matter? Unless it is urgent, it is best not to enter outside of the two daily meals."

However, the voice that answered Li Qi was not the maidservant's, but Shen Shuibi's.

"It's me," she said.

"Ah?" Li Qi paused his work and looked up at Shen Shuibi. "Ah, Miss Shen, why have you come?"

Shen Shuibi walked up to Li Qi, pulled over a stool, and sat beside him. Picking up a book, she said, "I came to help you."

Li Qi couldn't help but laugh softly. He shook his head. "But you don't know how to..."

"That is why you must teach me. I am a fast learner," Shen Shuibi replied as a matter of course, flipping the book open.

Li Qi immediately realized that Shen Shuibi was entirely serious.

He hastily said, "These are confidential records of Shaman God Mountain. These books are all—"

Yet, before he could finish, Shen Shuibi pressed a finger against his forehead.

Suddenly, an inherited divine will appeared directly in his mind. He didn't have time to read the contents, but he could make out the characters on the cover.

Celestial Pole Golden Core Art

This was... the fundamental cultivation method of Mount Luofu?!

It had been said before.

The heavens have the Celestial Pole. It is the center of the firmament, eternally unmoving, while the myriad stars revolve around it. Every full revolution is termed a heavenly cycle.

Mount Luofu used the Celestial Pole as a metaphor for the Golden Core: the self was the center of the cosmos, and all phenomena revolved around oneself.

It emphasized tempering the self to pure mastery, remaining unmoved by external forms. If the heart remained still, the tempering of the self was complete, forging one's own being into the Celestial Pole of all creation.

This—

While Li Qi was in a state of shock, Shen Shuibi spoke up, "This is the foundational cultivation method of Mount Luofu. With this, the Grand Invocators and Grand Diviners should have nothing left to say."

Hearing this, Li Qi was left speechless, not knowing what to say.

He, too, had nothing left to say.

Shen Shuibi paid no mind to all that. Instead, she opened a book and immediately began studying it.

Even starting from absolute scratch, she felt certain her speed would outpace Li Qi's.

Li Qi took a deep breath.

If Shen Shuibi had gone to such lengths, there was no need to say anything more. He just needed to speed up and hurry.

In the deep of the night, only the sounds of the two reading and studying echoed within the study.

Occasionally, Shen Shuibi's voice would chime in with a question.

"What governs the sacrificial beasts?"

"The rites dictate the scale of the offering."

"Oh."

Time trickled by, bit by bit.

Another month passed.

In the course of their time together, Li Qi truly realized just how... intelligent Shen Shuibi was.

Her aptitude in this field was unbelievably high.

She grasped everything at the slightest hint and mastered it instantly.

Drawing inferences from a single instance was effortless for her. In most cases, she could achieve profound research entirely by reading. Out of a hundred thousand choices, she could guess the correct answer through sheer intuition and deduction.

Li Qi couldn't even remember how many scenarios she had accurately predicted. Relying purely on numerological deduction, she foresaw numerous potential errors, helping Li Qi avoid countless wrong turns.

Here, everything was so peaceful and steady.

---

Another twenty days passed.

However, on Le Ya's end, the situation had grown rather delicate.

The mission of this Rank 5 shaman, who had hurried over from Shaman God Mountain, was to act as Bo Yue's Dao Protector.

Of course, because he had wrongfully blamed Li Qi earlier, he had made a promise to Li Qi to help him complete this Grand Rite.

Initially, he hadn't thought this would be a problem. His only job was to ensure that this very promising Young Master could wholeheartedly complete his masterpiece—a Grand Rite that could be considered the absolute zenith within Rank 9.

Such an achievement would likely score perfect marks back at Shaman God Mountain.

Whatever help or materials this Young Master needed, he would provide.

Le Ya believed that with his strength, he could easily protect the other party as he completed this Grand Rite.

He could ensure that no external factors could possibly interfere.

Be it the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest or some war between nations, if they dared to interfere with the Grand Rite at this juncture, he would pluck off their heads to serve as a warning to others.

With his strength, it would not be difficult.

The Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord's strength was not inferior to his own, but as long as the other party possessed a functioning brain, they would never come here at such a time.

As for that batch of so-called Kingdom Lords of Baiyue, they were nothing but earthen chickens and pottery dogs—completely useless.

The problem, however, was that all of this was predicated on the premise of a normal situation.

The current situation had become somewhat troublesome.

That Shen Shuibi of Mount Luofu... had actually handed over the Celestial Pole Golden Core Art.

What was the meaning of this?

It gave him a headache.

Shaman God Mountain wouldn't stoop so low as to shamelessly demand that a Young Master offer up his personal property. That would be far too degrading.

It was like a child earning two hundred pieces of copper as pocket money outside; any parent with a shred of dignity wouldn't ask their child for that money.

Even if the Celestial Pole Golden Core Art was the method to activate the Sun and Moon True Lead Cauldron, it didn't matter.

The Sun and Moon True Lead Cauldron was a Rank 2 dharma treasure controlled by Lady Luofu. However, while the shamans could be insidious when doing business, they were still rather upright in their personal conduct. They wouldn't go so far as to forcefully rob someone while they were down.

In short, whether it was the Young Master's or Lady Luofu's personal property, Shaman God Mountain would absolutely not touch it.

But the problem was... the Tang Empire was a different story.

A secret was hidden within the Sun and Moon True Lead Cauldron. Le Ya didn't know the specifics; he only knew that it was the root cause of Mount Luofu's destruction, the very reason the Tang Empire had resolved to annihilate them.

Now that the Celestial Pole Golden Core Art had been leaked, Shaman God Mountain possessed the means to activate the Sun and Moon True Lead Cauldron.

Without a doubt, given the methods of the Tang Empire's Directorate of Astronomy, they had definitely already gleaned this matter from the threads of karma.

If this had occurred within the mountain sect's gates, it would be easy to handle. At worst, they would completely tear off all face and drop pretenses. Could the Tang Empire really attack Shaman God Mountain itself?

But now, this had happened in Baiyue...

Le Ya covered his face in agony.

He had already reported the matter to the mountain sect.

What happened next was no longer for him to decide.

As for the Young Master's Grand Rite...

He could only resign it to the heavens and fate.
