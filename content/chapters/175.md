# Chapter 175: An Unexpected Encounter

Listening to this, Li Qi felt a fatal dose of second-hand embarrassment, his toes curling tightly.

This man sure knew how to boast.

His cultivation method might genuinely be able to sense vitality, but Li Qi knew his own vitality also melded with nature and wasn't that easy to detect.

But it was likely because he possessed this perceptual ability that he noticed a slight discrepancy, prompting him to step out and shout into the void to see if anyone was actually there.

After all, if they were truly, undeniably certain someone was following them, Li Qi reckoned this group would have set up an ambush and counterattacked, rather than standing out here talking nonsense.

But Li Qi didn't plan on pursuing the matter. After two dry coughs, he walked up to the crowd and said, "I only came seeking some grain this time, but the distribution center wasn't enough. I figured following you would lead me to a larger grain production area to figure out a solution. I didn't expect you to possess a wondrous method to discover me, causing a bit of a misunderstanding."

"I absolutely harbor no ill intent, which I'm sure everyone here can see," Li Qi added.

Rising from his kneeling posture, the burly man answered, "That is natural, we do not doubt it at all!"

What nonsense. If this person truly had ill intent, they wouldn't have survived a single breath.

This person's strength was absolutely on the same level as their sect master; both were transcendent existences!

"So Senior wishes to do business. Then my Golden Wave Sect can provide a few introductions for Senior. May I ask how much grain Senior needs? Shall we board the carriage to discuss it in detail?" the burly man said to Li Qi.

"That's fine. We'll go up and talk. I wonder, can your Golden Wave Sect produce eighteen thousands tonnes of grain?" Li Qi asked as he followed him onto the carriage.

Eighteen thousands tonnes!

This number gave the burly man a fright.

A full eighteen tonnes of grain, this was no small amount!

A simple calculation made it clear: eighteen thousands tonnes was roughly over thirty million catties.

Assuming one person ate only one catty of grain a day, a population of a hundred thousand would need a hundred thousand catties, roughly eating fifty tonnes a day. It would be enough to last over three hundred days, effectively a year's rations for a hundred thousand people.

Of course, the people in this world had larger appetites due to cultivation methods and other reasons. In reality, they could eat three catties of grain a day, and big eaters could eat five or six catties. In other words, eighteen thousands tonnes was approximately a hundred days' rations for a hundred thousand people, or enough to feed everyone in Vast Yang City for two days.

Assuming there were a hundred thousand people, even if they started eating right now, it would be enough to last until the next spirit rice harvest!

But the burly man didn't know that Li Qi was, in fact, preparing to feed over a hundred thousand people until the next harvest.

"Senior, sixty kilogram of grain is roughly a hundred copper coins. Calculating it out, eighteen thousands tonnes of grain would cost thirty thousand gold. I'm afraid few grain merchants can cough up this much capital at once. Even if they raised it, it would take over a month. I wonder if Senior can wait? And is your funding sufficient?" the burly man asked cautiously.

He deliberately ignored the option of 'giving it to Li Qi for free'.

Eighteen thousands tonnes of grain. No one in Vast Yang City could afford to give that away.

"Thirty thousand gold..." Li Qi frowned. He did indeed possess items worth thirty thousand gold on him, but none of those were things that could be easily sold. As for thirty thousand gold in cash, who the hell would carry thirty thousand gold in cash on them?

One gold was a gold biscuit weighing about half a catty. Thirty thousand gold meant over ten thousand catties of gold. This was enough to show just how valuable eighteen thousands tonnes of grain was.

Li Qi's frown sent a chill of terror into the burly man, who feared Li Qi couldn't afford it but desperately needed it, and would simply resort to malicious intentions.

Yet he also found it somewhat strange. Why would a great expert like this start raising grain?

There were generally two reasons for raising grain.

First, they were supporting a faction of subordinates, with several thousand mouths waiting to be fed. But in such cases, grain channels were usually fixed, making a sudden demand for a massive amount of grain unlikely.

The second reason was disaster relief. But were there any refugees recently?

There seemed to be.

He had heard there were refugees near the border pass, but not many. The number that truly flooded into Vast Yang City was only a few thousand to ten thousand, barely enough to make a splash.

Aside from a few more beggars on the streets, slightly better business for the human traffickers, and significantly cheaper prices at the brothels, there hadn't been any special changes.

To the massive Vast Yang City, this was absolutely nothing.

Wait.

Reaching this point in his thoughts, the burly man suddenly realized something. He looked up and asked Li Qi, "Could it be that Senior to aid the refugees? Eighteen thousands tonnes is exactly enough to feed over a hundred thousand people until the next spirit rice harvest. Could Senior be...?"

"You're quite astute. It is indeed for this purpose. Rest assured, I won't forcefully rob you. Take me over to negotiate with your sect master. Although I don't have thirty thousand gold, I should be able to offset the cost with other items," Li Qi said.

Deep respect welled up within the burly man. Cupping his hands, he said, "Senior is highly righteous. Saving over a hundred thousand people... with such karma, who knows how many people will build living shrines and sculpt divine statues for you, praying for Senior day and night."

"What's the use of saying all that? Just hurry and take me to your encampment," Li Qi said. "On the way, could you introduce the grain market of Vast Yang City to me, so I can make a judgment?"

"Of course. Please ask any questions you have, Mister," the burly man replied.

The convoy resumed its journey, and Li Qi conversed with the burly man in the carriage.

Along the way, perhaps out of fear or perhaps out of admiration, the man answered Li Qi's questions truthfully and completely. However, Li Qi didn't entirely believe him. He simply memorized the information silently to use as reference later.

The burly man claimed his name was Di Danyi. He was one of the stewards of the Golden Wave Sect and cultivated the most common Martial Dao. His cultivation had reached the Tempering Viscera realm, and he had already refined two internal organs: his kidneys and lungs.

In other words, with three more, he would become an Innate martial artist and reach Rank 9.

This cultivation level was considered top-notch within the Golden Wave Sect. Even in Vast Yang City, he was a ranked expert of the Jianghu. That was why he had the qualifications to be the steward of a grain transport convoy, handling several tonnes of grain daily and millions of tonnes annually.

As for the sect master of the Golden Wave Sect, he was an Innate expert—a Martial Dao Rank 9. He and over twenty other Rank 9s had formed a Grain Alliance. Over twenty gang factions joined hands, each controlling a portion of Vast Yang City's grain business.

And it was this Grain Alliance that monopolized all the grain business in Vast Yang City.

In a person's life, the four essentials—clothing, food, shelter, and transportation—were absolutely indispensable. From this, one could see the power of the Grain Alliance. Behind them stood the Minister of State!

In other words, they were backed by a Rank 8 official!

This showed how massive their influence was. In Vast Yang City, they could be considered omnipotent.

However, despite the Grain Alliance's vast power, they weren't entirely united internally. Under the control of the Rank 8 Minister of State, while outwardly devoid of conflict and cooperatively maintaining grain price stability without causing trouble...

In reality, the channels controlled by each faction within the Grain Alliance differed.

For instance, the Golden Wave Sect could meddle in the grain trade because they were backed by dozens of villages. These villages were their die-hard supporters.

The village elders were mid-level members of the Golden Wave Sect. When the youths left the village, they basically became ordinary gang members for the sect. The grain planted by the villagers was mostly sold through the Golden Wave Sect.

Relying on these villages, their influence radiated outward, drawing even more villages to supply grain exclusively to the Golden Wave Sect. This even included a county seat, which fell within their territory. With these areas as their foundation, they secured a place in Vast Yang City.

This counted as their background.

The twenty-plus factions of the Grain Alliance each occupied a portion of the grain production. The smallest produced only sixty thousand tonnes a year. The largest was a family known as the Zhang Family, with a yield approaching six hundred thousand tonnes.

The total yield of all twenty-plus factions combined was roughly between three and forty million tonnes. This was enough to satisfy Vast Yang City's annual needs, with some surplus remaining.

And the Zhang Family were the ones who erected the pillar.

The twenty-feet tall pillar Li Qi had seen earlier in Vast Yang City, engraved with ten thousand bats and named "Ten Thousand Blessings Come to Pay Homage," had been raised by the Zhang Family.

As for the Golden Wave Sect, their yield was about two or three hundred thousand tonnes, placing them in the upper-middle tier.

Di Danyi stated, "Meaning, Senior, with this single stroke, you want to take a tenth of the Golden Wave Sect's total annual yield. This is truly not something easily handed over. At least the vast majority of the Grain Alliance's factions couldn't produce it."

"Perhaps only that faction with the six hundred thousand tonnes yield can satisfy your demand." Di Danyi spread his hands at Li Qi, indicating that he was truly out of options.

Hearing this, Li Qi rested his head on one hand and rubbed his temples. "How can the gap be this large? According to you, the personnel, experts, and power of each faction aren't vastly different. If someone truly swallowed such a massive share, how could the rest of you tolerate it?"

Di Danyi nodded in agreement. "I find it strange too. The Golden Wave Sect is very clear about their sphere of influence; it's absolutely impossible for them to produce that much grain. Yet they somehow can bring out that amount. It's just that the Sect Master refuses to tell us. Regarding these matters, I'm afraid Senior will have to ask him personally."

Upon hearing this, Li Qi only felt a headache.

It seemed there was finally a clue regarding the grain. However, at this moment, ten hours had already passed. He hoped the White Rhino Army had received his message and wouldn't descend into panic because he hadn't returned.

Over a hundred thousand people without grain. Just thinking about it was terrifying.

The most urgent task was to contact the Golden Wave Sect Master first, to see what means could be used to have the other party help him reach out to the Zhang Family.

With the Zhang Family's massive annual yield of six hundred thousand tonnes, withdrawing eighteen thousand tonnes of grain shouldn't be too difficult. Squeezing a bit should yield it. At worst, he could have the Golden Wave Sect make up the rest. It would always be enough.

As for whether they would be willing to sell it to him, Li Qi wasn't too worried.

While he didn't have much gold on him, he had plenty of things far more valuable than gold. Scrounging around, he should still be able to cobble together items worth thirty thousand gold.

Furthermore, exchanging these items for gold was easy.

Buying these items with gold, however, would be difficult. The phrase 'priceless and unmarketable' was quite reasonable.

Except for special situations like Li Qi's, no one would likely sell items useful only to cultivators in exchange for gold that only held meaning in the mundane world.

Their conversation continued all the way. They traveled from morning until noon. By the time lunch approached, they finally arrived at the Golden Wave Sect's external encampment.

It appeared to be a granary built against a mountain, though it was currently empty.

Of course, Li Qi didn't think this was Di Danyi's group's original destination.

He guessed that while chatting, Di Danyi had already signaled to change destinations, switching the original location to this empty granary, ensuring that even if Li Qi had malicious intent, he couldn't act on it.

Immediately after, Di Danyi said to Li Qi, "Senior, please wait here for a moment. I will go notify the Sect Master at once. He will definitely arrive within two hours at the latest!"

"I'll trouble you, then." Li Qi nodded and said.

"There is no trouble at all. The Golden Wave Sect will definitely not miss out on this huge deal. We will definitely handle it beautifully for Senior." He patted his chest as he spoke, then quickly ran out.

It was apparent that he was close to his breaking point. Although he acted very natural, being around Li Qi clearly brought immense pressure to this Golden Wave Sect steward.

Before long, Li Qi was the only one left in the room. It seemed even the servants nearby had been evacuated.

Li Qi stood up and looked around.

Although it was merely a manor built next to a granary, it had everything it should.

One side of the manor faced a lake. Over there was a pond of about an acre planted with lotuses and water caltrops, with hibiscuses lining the embankment.

There were three small rooms equipped with a tea stove, and the courtyard was sparse and desolate.

Not a single person could be seen.

This made Li Qi inwardly start to mutter to himself.

They didn't run away, did they? Putting him here and then packing up all their belongings to move?

That shouldn't be the case. The monk might run away, but the temple remains. The Golden Wave Sect's operation was massive.

With this thought in mind, Li Qi simply sat down, quietly waiting for the boss of the Golden Wave Sect to arrive.

This also made him sigh somewhat.

Once upon a time, his attitude when facing a steward of a business had to be one of bowing and scraping. But now, the situation had completely reversed.

Things were no longer what they used to be.

In the past, the greatest good deed he could do was give the neighbor's child a bowl of meat soup.

Now, he was already capable of shouldering the lives of over a hundred thousand people.

However, to Li Qi, there was no difference between the two.

Both were merely doing a good deed within his capabilities.

The only difference was that his power was not the same, so the scope of 'within his capabilities' was also different.

To this world, Rank 9 was already considered an expert of a region, capable of occupying a high position and being surrounded by tens of thousands of people.

However, Vast Yang City seemed unable to compare to Clear Mist City in the Green Mountain Kingdom.

The number of Rank 9s in Clear Mist City was far, far higher than here. He had no idea what the reason was.

If Rank 9s in Clear Mist City were one in a thousand, here they were one in ten thousand.

Was Clear Mist City particularly strong? Or was there something special about it?

Li Qi thought about this, simultaneously calculating in his heart how to handle the aftermath.

What would be best to use in exchange for eighteen thousand tonnes of grain?

What items on his person were unnecessary yet could be used for exchange?

As Li Qi pondered this, time slowly passed.

Soon, an hour passed.

Li Qi raised his head and looked outside the room.

However, he saw a carriage driving up outside the door.

Di Danyi was leading the way in front.

Seeing this, Li Qi sat back down, waiting for the other party to enter.

But he saw a person step down from the carriage.

Clad in white robes, he was romantic and suave, holding a folding fan in his hand, looking handsome and dashing.

Li Qi had met this person before.

Wang Boyan!

The man from atop the fifth floor of the Buddhist Pagoda!
