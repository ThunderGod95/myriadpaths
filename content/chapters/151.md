# Chapter 151: Communication

Jin Buhuan had already set out for Green Mountain.

With Elder Si manipulating the heavenly qi, the group could ride the wind. The two-thousand-mile journey took a mere two hours.

Upon reaching Rank 8, cultivators attained a rudimentary ability to fly, allowing their travel speed to increase by leaps and bounds. This was why Young Master Zhu had been so confident when he declared that Li Qi would definitely make it back to Shaman God Mountain within ten years.

It was also why Jin Buhuan could so confidently state that he would return by tomorrow.

Arriving at Green Mountain, his first order of business was to head home.

Most parts of Green Mountain were green, interspersed with patches of red.

This was a mountain range composed almost entirely of metal, resembling a massive, chaotic hunk of metal spanning thousands of miles across.

Here, rivers of mercury, rains of gold, snows of silver, and winds of copper flowed ceaselessly.

Veins of ore were everywhere! Besides the common five metals—gold, silver, copper, iron, and tin—there were countless other minerals, such as lead, mercury, and cinnabar, too numerous to calculate.

All the minerals were clumped together within the veins, tangled and intertwined. Everything was readily available. One merely needed to strap a basket to their back and toss the minerals in; a pickaxe wasn't even required.

This practically inexhaustible 'mountain of gold' was the key to Green Mountain's rise.

The Green Mountain god was also a naturally born anomaly nurtured within this environment. As a White Tiger, he was the physical manifestation of a streak of metal qi from within the mountain itself. After cultivating, he eventually chose the Divine Dao and became the mountain god of this place, as well as the most powerful Earthly Divinity in the Green Mountain Kingdom.

However, the utter metallization meant there was almost zero vitality here. Aside from the occasional metal-element creature, cultivators ascending the mountain in search of treasure, and miners coming to excavate the ore veins, almost no living beings existed here.

There weren't even any trees.

The rivers here were either liquid mercury or filled with rust water, completely undrinkable.

This was also one of the reasons Jin Buhuan chose to reside continuously in Clear Mist City.

Compared to the deathly silent Green Mountain, he vastly preferred the bustling, vibrant mortal realm of Clear Mist City.

Jin Buhuan hurried toward his destination without a moment's pause.

He knew his father disliked being disturbed by others, so he didn't even bother paying his respects. Instead, he headed straight for the location where the metal qi was rumored to be manifesting.

Along the way, he passed by a valley.

This was the only place in all of Green Mountain where 'wood' was known to exist.

Countless lanterns were hung throughout the valley. Sharp wooden poles formed structures that were painted with bright red cinnabar and draped with patterned brocade. The decorations were incredibly luxurious. Although the architectural style was rather rugged, the materials used were anything but cheap.

These frameworks formed the buildings nestled within the valley. A stone-paved path ran along the valley walls, trailing from the entrance all the way up to the highest peak, completely illuminated by lanterns hanging the entire way. Not a single spot was left in darkness. The entire mountain gleamed brilliantly, as bright as day.

There were so many lanterns that, when viewed from the foot of the mountain, it looked like a reversed galaxy pouring downward in a blazing inferno. It was as if Heavenly Gods were out on a night stroll, emptying bushels of fireflies into the valley, where they clung to the grass and trees, refusing to leave.

Looking further up, one could see an unknown number of beautiful women, clad only in inches of silk, serving their master barefoot, allowing him to casually slip off their sheer garments and engage in debauchery. The women's shoes hung from the wooden frames, swaying alongside the lanterns like autumn leaves rustling in the wind.

Observing this scene, Jin Buhuan shook his head.

_This brother of mine is truly as useless as ever._

_Nothing but mud that cannot be propped up to build a wall._

He only spared it a single glance before turning his head and walking away.

He continued all the way to a mine.

Here, people were packed shoulder to shoulder.

Because this was the place where the metal qi would manifest, and Green Mountain did not forbid outsiders from fighting over it, it was common for large crowds to gather and wait for the emergence, followed by a bloody free-for-all.

Jin Buhuan felt somewhat nervous. After all, the emergence of Rank 8 metal qi wasn't something he had complete certainty in securing.

Usually, Jin Buhuan didn't bother with these matters because a significant portion of Green Mountain's human qi was reliant on these treasure-seeking cultivators.

Therefore, his father considered intentionally leaving some lower-grade metal qi behind for them to claim as a fortuitous encounter to be a good thing. Not only did it attract more people, but it also formed karmic ties.

Thus, if any of the sons wanted their own Rank 8 metal qi, they had to fight for it themselves to demonstrate the Green Mountain God's impartiality.

Consequently, after obtaining his own streak, he had never intended to seek out more.

However, he had to personally observe the situation with today's streak of metal qi.

If it truly was the convergence of yin and yang as Li Qi had described, he would claim it and offer it to his father once he emerged from seclusion.

His father surely wouldn't accept it, but would only view him with greater favor. It was killing two birds with one stone.

One of his older brothers had already given up; he just needed to see what the other's situation was.

His eldest brother hadn't given up hope and definitely desired a streak of metal qi to contend against him, so he was bound to show up, wasn't he?

As Jin Buhuan pondered these matters, he suddenly sensed a divine qi of the same origin as his own.

Looking up, he saw his eldest brother standing on the other side, looking right back at him.

Their gazes crossed, and they exchanged smiles.

It seemed almost coincidental, but right at that moment, the metal qi manifested! Its sharp aura pierced the firmament, impossible to conceal.

The thousand-plus onlookers attacked simultaneously.

Jin Buhuan shook his head.

A bunch of Rank 9s; he had no idea why they came to throw their lives away. Did they truly think they were children of destiny? With hundreds of thousands of Rank 9 cultivators in Green Mountain and the surrounding Clear Mist City, only these few thousand had smooth brains.

Perhaps it wasn't that they were stupid, but rather they had reasons compelling them to gamble their lives. After all, there had been successful cases of Rank 9s succeeding in the past.

But it didn't matter.

Jin Buhuan glanced back at Elder Si.

Elder Si nodded.

In the next instant, over half of the thousand-plus Rank 9s were killed or injured. The survivors each deployed their methods: some focused on self-preservation, some decided to take one last desperate shot, and some seemed to have finally realized they were nothing but ants and fled for their lives.

But none of that was important. From the surroundings, four or five Rank 8 experts attacked simultaneously, causing the ground to detonate with a deafening boom!

They were the true main force fighting over this item.

---

Almost at the exact same time.

Li Qi was sitting upright within a palace. Before him was a spread of exotic delicacies, accompanied by the wafting aroma of fine wine. The surroundings were draped in light gauze curtains, illuminated by flickering candlelight.

Furthermore, a beauty was seated right in his lap.

She was an elegant and exquisite woman; a single backward glance from her could generate a hundred different charms.

Her appearance was exceptionally beautiful, her features delicate, and her skin as white as snow.

Her eyes were clear as jade, rippling like autumn waters. Her figure swayed as gracefully as a lotus flower struggling against the wind. She was like an elegant jade herb standing out in a forest, her red tongue playfully rolling a black pearl between her lips.

She was truly a great beauty whose resonant charm soared to the clouds, possessing an allure as slender and elegant as a crescent moon. Every smile and frown was fitting, and her entire body emanated the fragrance of orchids.

In terms of appearance, she was on par with Shen Shuibi. But compared to the absent-minded rabbit who possessed virtually zero presence, her temperament was a step above.

Especially when her gaze turned affectionate and filled with charm, one could only feel her deliberate, lingering tenderness and profound affection, making it incredibly difficult to extricate oneself.

Honestly, Li Qi almost failed to maintain his self-control.

However, he calmed his mind, stabilized his spirit, and closed his eyes, doing his utmost to ignore the various soft and warm sensations pressing against his body. "Senior is a dignified Rank 7. Why must you lower yourself like this?"

Yes, Rank 7. This beautiful woman before him was the true ruler of Clear Mist City.

Hu Xian'er, who cultivated both the healing arts and the Demon Dao, having achieved the Dao at Rank 7.

"With a piece of soft jade like myself in your arms, is Sir's heart truly not moved?" Hu Xian'er gently stroked Li Qi's chin, exhaling softly against his skin.

"This junior has come to discuss serious matters. I ask that Senior cease this playfulness." Li Qi kept his nose to the grindstone and his mind focused inward, silently reciting numbers in his head to suppress his instincts.

"So serious~, but if your heart wasn't moved, why would you close your eyes?" Hu Xian'er's voice brushed against his ear; he could almost feel the heat of her breath.

"Being moved is one thing, but matters must be prioritized. Until the important issues are settled, this junior is unwilling to discuss anything else," Li Qi replied honestly.

Having said that, he forced himself to suppress all distracting thoughts.

He had no choice. It wasn't that his willpower was weak; the gap between them was simply too massive.

She was a Rank 7 vixen spirit, and she probably wasn't even taking this seriously. If she actually unleashed a charm technique, could he really withstand it?

Since it was absolutely impossible to resist from the start, it proved this fox wasn't genuinely interested in hooking up with him. Rather than daydreaming, it was better to hurry up and handle the serious business.

Indeed, after finishing his discussion with the anti-Jin faction, Li Qi had rushed non-stop to visit the Clear Mist Sect.

He relied on the numerous mysteries surrounding his identity to secure an audience with Hu Xian'er. With his enigmatic background and his feud with Jin Buhuan, it was unlikely Hu Xian'er would refuse to see him.

For Li Qi, completely uprooting Jin Buhuan required the power of the Clear Mist Sect. The factions he had just met with, along with himself, were merely knives; they could not serve as the hand wielding the knife.

A knife without a sufficiently powerful hand to grip it cannot injure a person.

But for the wielder, not having a knife makes it easy to injure oneself.

The two were indispensable to each other.

Just as Li Qi was pondering this, a sudden, chilling dampness surrounded him, as if a cold wind were blowing.

The scenery around him abruptly shifted. The opulent, overwhelmingly glamorous palace transformed into an ordinary little garden.

The decorations were elegant and refined. Fruit trees bordered the area, encircled by tangerine, plum, pear, date, goji, and chrysanthemum plants.

Only then did Li Qi open his eyes.

_Damn it, my guess was right._

It had been an illusion from the very beginning. He hadn't touched a single inch of her skin from the start.

After opening his eyes, he realized that the fine wine and delicacies were still arranged on his table, but Hu Xian'er was seated ten paces away, half-hidden inside a thatched cottage within the orchard.

_That was close. At least the food she provided was real, and she didn't leave me eating air._

Upon truly seeing this widely renowned vixen spirit, Li Qi discovered that her temperament was actually transcendent, like a lone skylark soaring in the heavens, devoid of any seductive charm.

Noticing Li Qi observing her, Hu Xian'er stepped out from behind the door and sat across from him. "Since Sir truly came to discuss serious business, then please speak~."

Hearing this, Li Qi immediately put away all stray thoughts and instantly focused on the matter at hand.

"Senior, do you not feel that Green Mountain has been meddling in Clear Mist City's affairs for far too long?" Li Qi asked.

Hu Xian'er chuckled lightly. "Hehe, the Green Mountain god and I can be considered colleagues. We simply co-govern this area; how can there be any talk of meddling? Sir must not attempt to sow discord, or else I might hand you over to the government officials~."

Li Qi replied respectfully, "Then I ask that Senior allow this junior to sow some discord. You can hand me over to the officials after you've listened."

"It's widely known that the Green Mountain god, Jin Chi, acts domineeringly. But his killing power is massive, and his strength is extraordinary. Even the kingdom's lord can only suppress him by virtue of being one rank higher, but cannot truly make him submit, so he can only let him act as he pleases."

"Wait, you know Jin Chi's name?" Hearing Li Qi's words, the Clear Mist Sect Master's tone gained a hint of seriousness.

"Naturally," Li Qi answered.

"What is your background? Why do you know these things?" the Clear Mist Sect Master inquired.

"Please forgive this junior for being unable to speak clearly on the matter, but I do indeed hail from the Great Deer Kingdom. Senior should also be aware that Jin Chi not only possesses immense killing power but also immense killing intent. If this were exposed, I'm afraid the implications would be quite severe," Li Qi replied respectfully.

Hu Xian'er extended her slender, fair hand. With an elegant posture, she poured out the fine wine, rinsed a cup, and poured tea for Li Qi. While doing so, she said, "But if it isn't exposed, won't you simply be killed? Based on Sir's ideas, Jin Chi will definitely view you as a scheming, rat-like gossiper harboring malicious intent. When that time comes, you'll still be unable to escape death. Why bring this suffering upon yourself? Why must you risk your life to provoke him? You have no deep-seated enmity with Jin Buhuan, do you?"

"To be honest, I've been keeping an eye on Sir these past few days. I don't quite understand why Sir insists on putting Jin Buhuan to death. Your actions indeed align with the interests of the Clear Mist Sect, but I fail to understand your motives," the Clear Mist Sect Master asked.

Li Qi replied, "A true man acts on certain principles and refuses to act against others."

"Those things I refuse to do—even with a myriad of temptations before me and swords and halberds pressed against my back, I will absolutely not do them," Li Qi said in a serious tone.

The Clear Mist Sect Master nodded, offering her approval.

She had tested him just a moment ago. She had adjusted her illusion to a level that wouldn't genuinely affect his sanity but would certainly leave him infatuated. Yet, he had remained completely unmoved, covertly biting his tongue several times rather than giving in.

"But if there is something I must do, even if tens of thousands stand against me, I will still press forward!" Li Qi raised his head, his voice resolute and decisive.

Although his words seemed impassioned, the Clear Mist Sect Master didn't appear to be particularly moved.

Still, she offered her praise. "Sir is a remarkable man. With such determination, your path ahead is already smooth and flat."

"However, I now understand Sir's determination and motives. But what will Sir rely on to achieve this? There is so much injustice in the world. If Sir were to involve yourself in every single one, I'm afraid you wouldn't be speaking these words to me. After all, while the Clear Mist Sect maintains a bottom line and isn't as deranged as Green Mountain, to say we are truly fair... is probably a stretch."

"Why must Sir slay Jin Buhuan, yet treat the Clear Mist Sect as an ally?" the Clear Mist Sect Master questioned.

In the Clear Mist Sect Master's view, such matters were difficult to articulate.

If he claimed he was doing it to uphold justice? Only a ghost would believe him.

Jin Buhuan was not a good person.

But were the others any better?

Why target Jin Buhuan specifically?

If he truly intended to help whenever he saw an injustice, how could he even walk down the street?

There was injustice everywhere in the world; could he possibly manage it all?

If he truly acted as he described, he would have died long ago. How could he have survived until now?

Therefore, Hu Xian'er didn't believe half of Li Qi's self-professed explanation. She wanted to hear the real reason.

And Li Qi knew this as well.

Li Qi had his own internal scale; he knew his own standards for conduct.

He was very clear on what matters he should involve himself in, and what matters he could involve himself in.

But he also knew that very few people would believe him.

Therefore, he answered, "Besides that, this junior naturally has a second reason."
