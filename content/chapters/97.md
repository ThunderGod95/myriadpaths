# Chapter 97: Eating Humble Pie

The Dao is a rule.

When manifested in the external macrocosm, it takes the form of natural laws and the fundamental rules of heaven and earth.

When manifested within the personal cosmos, it becomes a person's code of conduct and ideological activities.

Such a massive concept obviously could not be encapsulated by the mere ability to wield a sword.

Therefore, as a true Dao seeker, Shen Shuibi was extremely indignant.

In her eyes, methods of using a sword were merely skills and could never be called a Dao. Hearing Jing Yan proclaim her technique a Sword Dao angered her even more than Li Qi stroking her fur.

Thus, she had to slap her face! Snatch away her sword! Make her understand the profound weight of the word 'Dao'!

"Her swordsmanship overly pursues trivial details. It has far too many flaws—a full one thousand five hundred and sixty-two of them!" Shen Shuibi spread out a piece of cloth and began drawing on it with a charcoal pencil.

Li Qi was stunned.

_Holy crap, the rabbit is taking this so seriously!_

Furthermore, he hadn't expected Shen Shuibi to be skilled at drawing. With just a few simple strokes, she accurately depicted the sword moves. Viewed sequentially, they even possessed a dynamic, animation-like fluidity.

Small dots were used to mark the different weaknesses and flaws within the various movements.

Shen Shuibi explained to Li Qi, "Look here. Her sword moves appear masterful, but in reality, she focuses too heavily on trivial details at the expense of the core foundation. Her internal qi is a chaotic mess. It is an utter flashy facade. She claims to have reached the realm of meticulous detail, but aside from being meticulous, she has nothing else. A simple push will topple it."

"As the saying goes, 'To know its comings and goings is to know the reverse, smooth, full, and empty states of qi; to anticipate it is to know the moment when qi can be seized!' I will now explain the changes in her qi dynamics—its reverse, smooth, full, and empty states—as it circulates while she executes her swordsmanship. As long as you grasp this, you can beat her face swollen in just two punches!" Shen Shuibi was aggressive and highly enthusiastic.

Li Qi looked at the rabbit.

_She is so excited..._

_Forget it, I'd better learn it. This is a good thing, after all._

Li Qi studied continuously late into the night.

---

The next morning, even Manager Jing hurried over to watch the spectacle.

It seemed... everyone from the Jing family was extremely eager to see Jing Yan eat humble pie.

Li Qi silently criticized Elder Sister Jing's terrible popularity in his heart before gripping his long blade and stepping into the crowd.

The ox carts parked for rest formed a circle, creating a small arena out of a human wall. A crowd of people—some guards from the Martial Affairs Hall, some merchants from other halls, laborers, accountants, various handymen, and even managers like Manager Jing—had all gathered together, brimming with excitement.

They were just missing a handful of melon seeds to snack on.

Li Qi couldn't shake the feeling that he was being treated as cheap entertainment.

Did this group of people really have no other forms of entertainment normally?

"Do your best, Warrior Li!" A group of laborers waved their fists and cheered for Li Qi from behind.

Some of the women were more reserved. They hid in the back, whispering among themselves, though their gazes were anything but reserved. They sized Li Qi up and down, as if trying to see right through him, curious about what kind of man dared to provoke that tigress.

Seeing Li Qi, Manager Jing chuckled and walked over. "You truly possess both wisdom and courage, Warrior Li. To think you came up with such a method to deal with Deacon Jing. This is far more effective than simply marching up to her door and demanding a spar. You truly are a man who wanders the Jianghu alone!"

Li Qi was bewildered.

_Forget it. If everyone believed that, then so be it._

However, wouldn't Jing Yan fly into a rage out of humiliation over this? Her own family members had actually come out to support an outsider?

Harboring these thoughts, Li Qi walked into the ring formed by the crowd.

Shen Shuibi stood on the outside, her hands clasped against her chest. She watched Li Qi with absolute confidence and great anticipation.

She wasn't worried in the slightest. She had completely seen through Jing Yan's sword moves. Operating from a vastly superior vantage point, she absolutely would not make a mistake!

And so, bearing the weight of everyone's gazes, Li Qi stepped forward.

However, to his surprise, Jing Yan didn't seem affected at all.

On the contrary, after arriving, this elder sister actually apologized to Li Qi first.

"Your name is Li Qi, correct? Then I will address you directly by your name." Stepping forward, Jing Yan took the initiative to speak. "Li Qi, yesterday I was a bit hasty in attacking your little sister. Fortunately, you intervened in time and no one was hurt. This was my mistake, and I hope you can forgive me."

Li Qi hadn't expected the other party to be so polite. He immediately cupped his hands and replied, "It was my little sister who spoke out of turn first. Having your art critiqued, it was only natural for you to grow anxious, Elder Sister. There is truly no need to apologize for this."

"Hold on. I am only apologizing for attacking so hastily. I never said she was right." Jing Yan cut off whatever Li Qi was about to say next.

"As for the arrogant nonsense she spouted, we will settle that account after I win." As she spoke, she drew a new longsword—also a fine blade forged from hundred-refined steel.

It appeared she didn't possess a Rank 9 sword herself. Otherwise, knowing that Li Qi held a weapon advantage, she wouldn't have chosen to wield an ordinary, unranked fine sword.

Looking at her sword stance, Li Qi could almost feel her pride—pointing straight to the heavens, just like her blade.

She really was... a withdrawn and eccentric person.

However, after sighing in amazement, Li Qi drew his long blade and assumed an odd stance.

Marveling was one thing, but they still had a fight to settle.

"That is not a stance for wielding a blade," Jing Yan frowned.

"This is a stance for breaking a sword," Li Qi explained.

With that, neither said another word. Jing Yan raised her hand, and her entire figure vanished in a flash!

_Swish!_ With the sound of a sharp sword tearing through the air, a vigorous burst of qi swirled like a vortex, yanking at Li Qi's blade as if trying to rip it right out of his hands!

Li Qi's initial stance was instantly ripped apart. His entire posture twisted awkwardly, his balance completely shattered by the sudden, howling gale.

_Holy crap! I thought we were just sparring casually! How did she just directly shatter my technique with brute force right from the start?!_

Yanked by the massive force, Li Qi nearly tumbled over, but the tip of the longsword was already thrusting towards him!

The billowing sword qi stung Li Qi's skin, making him feel like a helpless goat facing down a predatory tiger!

Li Qi had only truly been cultivating for a little over a month. He was vastly outmatched in both experience and technique.

Fortunately, he immediately erupted with Carp Stream Intent, temporarily unleashing double his power. In a split second, he forcibly stabilized his body and blocked the incoming thrust with a sharp _clang_ of his long blade.

But this strike was merely the beginning.

Jing Yan's speed exploded. Her already incredibly fast sword strikes now rained down like a violent storm.

Even though he was facing a single swordswoman armed with just one blade, Li Qi felt as though he were being strafed by a machine gun. The tip of the sword thrust relentlessly like a barrage of bullets, striking dozens of times every second!

Relying purely on his physical constitution and reflexes, Li Qi forcibly resisted for two or three seconds before being forced to erupt with Carp Stream Intent once more. He hastily retreated, withdrawing several meters away to dodge the frontal assault.

Having unleashed Carp Stream Intent twice in such a short span, Li Qi felt his heart pounding wildly against his ribs, and the world before his eyes took on a reddish hue.

Among the five viscera, the bitter consequences of his untempered heart began to show.

His heart simply could not support the massive vitality brought by his liver. The moment he engaged in combat, it was forced to operate in severe overload.
