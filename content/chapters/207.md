# Chapter 207: Slaying the Demonic Monk (IV)

Monk Chanzhi shrank his body. By doing so, he could better protect himself amid the large-scale explosion. With a smaller surface area, he would bear less of the destructive force.

It was like how a hammer could shatter a solid boulder but would struggle to smash a speck of dust. The dust was simply too small to absorb the impact.

Conversely, shrinking his body brought the disadvantage of increased vulnerability to single-target attacks.

The logic was simple. If a hundred-meter-tall giant was stabbed by a sword, it would feel like a prick from a toothpick—completely painless and entirely ineffective.

However, if a child was stabbed by that same sword, it would be a fatal wound.

This was exactly the scenario playing out now.

Li Qi had not orchestrated all of this. After all, even he could only plan the broader strokes. The specific tactical details, including the setup of the arrays, were entirely arranged by Shen Shuibi.

She had never been a fool.

Her usual absent-minded demeanor was merely a result of devoting the vast majority of her focus to researching techniques.

Compared to her vast, complex, and deeply fascinating research, mundane worldly affairs were simply dull and taxing.

Yet, whenever Shen Shuibi willingly applied her mind to matters outside her studies, it became obvious that she could instantly turn sharp and astute, never missing a single opportunity.

Just like right now.

Monk Chanzhi instantly found himself caught in a dilemma.

The Vast Yang Minister of Works did not possess overwhelming combat strength. As a cultivator on the path of the Reason Dao, his true expertise lay in craftsmanship—laying down arrays, forging dharma treasures, constructing earthworks, and shifting mountains and rivers.

This suited his official duties perfectly; such work was the very purpose of a Minister of Works.

Still, no matter what, he was a Rank 7 expert.

The river of stars in the sky—anchored by seven Rank 7 dharma treasures and supported by tens of thousands of lower-grade items—carried immense destructive power.

Under normal circumstances, such an attack wouldn't even scratch Monk Chanzhi.

If he manifested his Mahamayuri dharma avatar, the innate properties of the peacock—impervious to all spells and nourished by all poisons—would render the starry river of dharma treasures completely ineffective against him, just as before.

The problem was that the array below was currently self-destructing.

That explosion carried the same lethal force of a Rank 7 attack, and swirling within it was an obscure energy that Monk Chanzhi could not see through.

Monk Chanzhi deduced that while assuming his Mahamayuri dharma avatar would grant immunity to the dharma treasure river, the self-destruction from below would exact a heavy toll on him.

Weighing his options, he chose to defend against the blast below. As for the river of dharma treasures...

Let it hit.

What could a piece of trash accomplish, anyway?

Ordinary wounds would have no effect on him whatsoever.

Having made his decision, he stood his ground, pooling all his strength into defense as he quietly awaited the descending strikes.

At the same time, he began brewing a lethal counterattack.

The attacks arrived.

Within the starry river, the seven Rank 7 dharma treasures morphed into seven blazing stars, arranging themselves into the shape of a ladle.

Reflections pierced the waves as constellations descended; dense clouds and clinging mist highlighted their lofty forms.

The number seven is the culmination of Yang. Thus, the seven stars were said to mark the exhaustion of Yang qi. An old saying went, “The Southern Dipper governs life, the Northern Dipper governs death. All mortals, upon conception, pass from the Southern Dipper to the Northern Dipper; all prayers are directed to the Northern Dipper.”

The Northern Dipper acted as the throat and tongue of the heavens. In this very moment, it was time for the Northern Dipper to pronounce death!

Stars as massive as the moon slotted into the constellation, accompanied by deafening thunder!

This was the Minister of Works' desperate, ultimate killing strike—a method centered around the heavenly Northern Dipper.

The killing intent of the Northern Dipper was known as Heavenly Slaughter!

The common mortal curse, "May Heaven strike you dead," originated from this very concept.

An immense killing intent descended, shrouding everything.

This was not the killing intent of mortals.

It was the killing intent of the Heavens.

What constituted the killing intent of the Heavens?

When the handle of the Northern Dipper points east, All Under Heaven experiences spring. When it points south, summer rules. When it points west, autumn arrives. And when the handle points north, All Under Heaven freezes in winter.

The Dipper pointing north marked the season of absolute slaughter.

The dead of winter, when all creation withered and perished, embodied the true Heavenly Slaughter.

The entire world would plunge into death!

Leaves withered, vegetation shriveled, water veins ran dry, and mountains and rivers were sealed in snow. Even mortals lost their vitality as the Yang qi between heaven and earth was drained away, leaving all things devoid of life.

This was Heavenly Slaughter!

The Minister of Works’ method utilized this very law of Heavenly Slaughter to execute an inescapable death blow!

Yellow leaves danced in the western wind as heaven and earth unleashed their grim harvest.

The surrounding mountains darkened like splashed ink; the bleakness of the Supreme Yin cast a desolate pall.

The rustling autumn wind mourned while the fragile willows were the first to wither.

Heaven's will prioritized slaughter, and all creation was destined to perish!

Monk Chanzhi’s body was suddenly afflicted by a bizarre effect.

His skin wrinkled, his muscles atrophied, and his pupils turned a sickly, withered yellow.

Mortal slaughter began with conflict, manifested through weapons, and ended in blood and flesh.

Earthly slaughter began with tremors, manifested as earth fire, and ended in ash and dust.

Heavenly slaughter began with the autumn wind, manifested through the bitter winter, and ended in barren stillness.

Bearing Heavenly Slaughter with a mortal body meant rapid aging and depletion. Like vegetation in the dead of winter, life force would be severed until death claimed the victim.

There were no external wounds, and there was no way to resist it.

Yet, there were always exceptions.

Plum blossoms and pine trees could still remain verdant and bloom in the winter.

Animals could hibernate, and humans could huddle inside their homes for warmth, quietly waiting for spring to arrive.

Therefore, Monk Chanzhi held his breath, plunging into a state of deep hibernation like a resting turtle.

What was the art of hibernation?

In the freezing depths of winter, when the heavenly qi lay dormant, creatures like turtles and snakes would hibernate without eating, only awakening when spring returned and vitality was restored.

Spring brought the first thunder. When that thunder roared, shattering the stillness to release vital qi, all hibernating beings would wake.

This was why one of the spring solar terms was named the Awakening of Insects.

It signified the call to rouse all dormant life.

This was a natural part of heaven and earth's cycle.

This was the essence of a battle of arts—a true test of comprehensive ability.

All things in creation generated and countered one another. Everything had its bane. Merely mastering a single path—unless one's cultivation was profound enough to entirely bypass the laws of generation and restraint—guaranteed eventual defeat by a counter-attribute.

Naturally, sheer overwhelming strength bypassed this issue entirely. Breaking ten thousand spells with a single sword was not an impossibility.

However, the vast majority of people could never achieve that.

Thus, the strategy for most was to prevent themselves from being countered while suppressing their opponent as much as possible. This was the fundamental nature of battling with spells.

If duels were merely a contest of whose dharma power was greater, whose cultivation was deeper, and whose spell arts were more destructive, there would be no need to fight at all. Everyone could just flash their aura to determine the victor. Why risk their lives?

Li Qi's previous battle with Jin Buhuan, and the current situation, were perfect examples of this.

Hence, hiding one's foundation from the enemy—or ensuring that even if discovered, no countermeasure could be found—was of paramount importance.

Regrettably for Monk Chanzhi, Li Qi had thoroughly probed his foundation.

The monk had been overconfident, believing Li Qi posed absolutely no threat, or perhaps assuming that even if a threat existed, nothing could be done about it.

For the sake of his path, to solidify the foundation required to breakthrough to Rank 6, he had been forced to reveal his roots to Li Qi.

It was like seeing a physician for an illness; one had to explain their physical condition clearly, and if it remained unclear, examinations were necessary.

Monk Chanzhi believed he had handled the situation masterfully.

He would endure the Vast Yang Minister of Works' assault head-on, using the art of hibernation to weather the devastation of Heavenly Slaughter.

Meanwhile, his shrunken body and his devil kingdom would resist the array’s self-destruction from below.

It was a foolproof plan.

He waited at ease, confident that this wave of attacks would inflict light injuries at worst.

The Seven Stars Heavenly Slaughter was easily endured.

The explosion from below detonated with a thunderous roar. Across dozens of miles, the ground shattered inch by inch. A massive mushroom cloud surged upwards, violently scattering the river of dharma treasures in the sky. For a hundred miles in every direction, a layer of the earth was forcefully shaved away.

This region of Vast Yang was reduced to a scorched wasteland...

No, not a white scorched earth, but a red one.

Rather, it became blindingly white before turning a brilliant red.

Initially, there was the intense shockwave and scorching heat.

The extreme heat birthed a blinding light that rapidly expanded like a second sun, washing out the entire area. From afar, it seemed as if the very ground had turned a pure, searing white.

The radiance far surpassed that of the sun. As the supersonic shockwave swept out, the sheer pressure instantly compacted the soft soil and gravel, turning dozens of miles into solid earthen plates.

The ensuing inferno then scorched these plates into crystalline brilliance, melting them into glass. The entire earth transformed into a sheet of glass, possessing a bizarre, terrifying beauty.

Now, the molten glass glowed with a dazzling, vivid red hue, looking like a surreal dream from a distance.

However, beauty and brutality were not mutually exclusive.

Within the blast radius, everything—whether they were devils, wild beasts, vegetation, or even things buried deep underground—was reduced to flying ash, or melted into the flowing sea of glass.

Fortunately, no one else was nearby.

However, Monk Chanzhi's devil god physique was also injured.

Two consecutive, unexpected killing moves had left him with moderate injuries.

Still, it was useless. Even with only a single breath left, he was not someone these people could threaten.

Moreover, as the lingering force of the explosion faded, he effortlessly grabbed the Vast Yang Minister of Works by the throat with one hand and hoisted him into the air.

The Minister of Works, who had rushed back into the fray, lasted only a single exchange. After barely scratching Monk Chanzhi, he was instantly captured alive.

The strength of this monk was truly terrifying!

Gripping the Minister of Works by the throat and lifting him high, Monk Chanzhi smiled. With an elegant, refined tone, he said, "This poor monk happens to lack skill in artifact refinement. Having you here perfectly makes up for a major shortcoming."

Though he smiled, his devil god form was profoundly horrifying. With gloomy, chilling eyes, thick drool leaking from his maw, and rows of dense, needle-like teeth protruding outward, he presented a nightmarish visage.

Yet, the Minister of Works merely twitched his lips, forming an ugly grin. "Master Chanzhi... aren't you curious?"

Chanzhi tilted his head. "Curious about what?"

Smiling worse than if he were crying, the Minister of Works said helplessly, "Aren't you curious why a coward like me, someone who clings to life and fears death, would suddenly return to fight you to the bitter end?"

Monk Chanzhi frowned. That was indeed strange.

The man had already escaped, so why come back? Chanzhi hadn't even planned to bother with him, yet he willingly returned to throw his life away.

Was it necessary?

"True enough," Monk Chanzhi replied. "Why? Did someone behind you force you to return? But this poor monk did not sense—"

However, halfway through Chanzhi's sentence—

Suddenly, his body split open.

It literally split wide open.

As if gnawed on by some unseen entity, his flesh fractured inch by inch. In a fraction of a second, his lower half was chewed to pieces. Blood sprayed as slices of meat flew through the air, shaved as thin as cicada wings, like rolls of finely sliced beef.

Chanzhi was stunned.

Who was it? What was that?!

Ignoring the minister in his hand, he casually tossed him aside. Pressing his palms together, his devil god form erupted. Five hundred rays of light cascaded downward as countless ghost-gods swarmed around him, projecting a massive repulsive force to shove away whatever hidden entity was continuously biting him.

Yet, it was futile.

The repulsive force blasted out, sending the Minister of Works flying over ten miles. He crashed miserably into a pool of molten glass, battered but ultimately alive.

But Monk Chanzhi's body continued to be endlessly shaved into thin slices. Blood splattered everywhere as meat fell like drifting cicada wings.

The mysterious entity was completely unaffected.

Monk Chanzhi was horrified!

His eyes flashed with cold resolve, and he instantly brought his hands together.

The three-inch devil kingdom manifested once more.

But this time, it was not directed outward, but inward.

Monk Chanzhi's body was instantly plunged into the Nether Prison!

From his four arms holding bowls, he produced yet another bowl. It reeked of an overwhelming stench and was filled entirely with rotting pus.

He chanted loudly, "The Pus and Blood Nether Prison spans five hundred yojanas, and within it lies the natural pus of the damned! It scalds the lips and tongue, burns from the throat down to the belly, and rots everything it passes through! The agony and bitterness are unbearable! Almsgiver, fall into the Nether Prison with me!"

As his words fell, boiling heat surged from the bowl. The boiling pus and blood were like scalding soup, instantly causing Monk Chanzhi's limbs, head, and face to fester and decay!

Then, he scooped up the pus and blood and consumed it himself. The boiling liquid surged through him, circulating endlessly from mouth to stomach, and back up again, rotting him inside and out!

Just looking at the sight was enough to make one's scalp go numb!

This bastard was not only ruthless to others, but ruthless to himself!

This drastic move immediately forced a golden-furred rat, about the size of a small dog, to rapidly retreat from beneath him.

"Cough... ptui, ptui, ptui!" The golden-furred rat spat violently, its mouth filled with vile, rotting pus. Disgusted to the point of gagging, it cursed, "You damn monk! Do you have any shame left?!"

"Rat King?" Monk Chanzhi paused the Nether Prison aspect of his three-inch devil kingdom, his gaze turning dark.

The moment he saw the Rat King, he understood everything. All of Li Qi's arrangements were laid bare to him.

Monk Chanzhi sneered, "If you are here, it means Li Qi secretly tipped you off. You want to use me as a blood sacrifice?"

The Rat King floated in the air, chuckling sinisterly as it replied, "Naturally. Back when we negotiated, we crossed nearly half of All Under Heaven to reach the territory of Shaman God Mountain. For what?"

"You coveted the Shaman Dao method of balance, seeking to remove your devilish thoughts as a hindrance. As for me, I wanted sacrificial incense fire to shed the Rat Soil Mound's demon physique and become a divine rat."

"But what about now? You have your Shaman Dao method of balance, but where is my incense fire? Where is the sacrifice you promised me?" The Rat King spoke with a grin, though its tone was icy cold.

This was the opportunity Li Qi had spoken of!

Ever since he discovered the Gu Shaman arts, he had deduced that an irreconcilable conflict must exist between Monk Chanzhi and the Rat Soil Mound!

This was the third killing move.

"But who will host the sacrifice? In all of Vast Yang, apart from Li Qi, no one is capable of hosting such a ritual. If someone at Rank 8 or above had appeared, I would have sensed them long ago. Even if you kill me, there is no one to perform the sacrifice for you," Monk Chanzhi said with a gloomy expression.

"Is there no one?" the Rat King asked, tilting its head.

The moment its voice fell, a thousand mountains rose in the surroundings!

Throughout Vast Yang, the countless gods of its mountains and rivers cried out in unison.

For the landscape grand array, originally designed to defend against the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest, had been activated!

Monk Chanzhi was aghast!

Impossible! There was no one in Vast Yang capable of controlling the landscape grand array!

In his mind, only Li Qi possessed the ability!

But Li Qi was currently trapped within his Buddhist Pagoda. He couldn't possibly get out!

Who was hosting this sacrifice?

Meanwhile.

Thousands of miles away, the host of the sacrifice was gazing in their direction through a special method.

No one in all of Vast Yang recognized this host.

But Li Qi knew him.

The disciple of the Grand Diviner, the other young master of Shaman God Mountain.

Bo Yue.
