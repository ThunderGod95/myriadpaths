# Chapter 158: A Gracious Invitation

Clear Mist City, the second floor of the inn where Li Qi was staying.

Hu Mei'er sat sideways on the edge of Li Qi's bed. Li Qi lay under the covers, a tray positioned before him loaded with food, though the spread did not look particularly elegant.

After all, there were far too many animal phalluses.

As Hu Mei'er sat there, her hands and feet appeared delicate and slender, her skin as flawless and smooth as a heart-cooling snow lotus root. She was a vision of radiant beauty.

With her fragrant clothes and elegant hair, speaking in a soft, captivating whisper, she displayed the utmost grace and charm. She was clearly an incredibly powerful expert, yet at first glance, she resembled a freshly bloomed lotus rising from the water, as gentle as a little bird resting against a person. One would not sense any danger from her, only a pitiful and endearing loveliness.

"I have come this time to ask about your future plans, sir," Hu Mei'er said.

"Now that Jin Buhuan has been toppled and the Jin family's forces have withdrawn from Clear Mist City, you are naturally an honored guest of our Clear Mist Sect. My sister and I have discussed the matter, and we would like to invite you to stay at our sect for two or three days. Although our Clear Mist Sect engages in some unpresentable businesses, our cultivation method is rooted in the orthodox Medicine Dao. We can help treat your injuries and nurse your body back to health." She extended the invitation to Li Qi with perfect politeness.

As she spoke, a fragrant breeze assailed his senses. Even though the beauty before him made no superfluous movements and spoke with respectful courtesy, her every glance and gesture exuded a captivating allure, making her seem almost shy.

To this, Li Qi could not help but sigh inwardly.

It was no wonder these vixen spirits had managed to build such a successful business. They truly had their methods.

He prided himself on his deep concentration, able to withstand even the rabbit unconsciously acting cute in front of him. But whether it was Hu Xian'er or Hu Mei'er, he found them almost too much to handle.

This had nothing to do with his inner thoughts. The disparity in their strength was simply too vast; they were entirely on different levels.

This was merely her natural aura. If she truly intended to cast a charm spell on him, Li Qi would not be able to resist, even if it cost him his life.

At that point, his subjective will would be completely irrelevant. His perseverance and resolve would be nothing but a joke. If either of these two simply beckoned with a finger, his sanity would shatter. He would be capable of nothing but crawling over to them, even if his lower body was practically paralyzed right now.

Fortunately, his body currently suffered a severe lack of Yang qi, and his fire qi had been entirely drained. He was in such a lethargic state that he couldn't even muster any wicked thoughts. Moreover, the other party wasn't intentionally using charm spells on him. Under these combined factors, Li Qi managed to successfully steady his mind.

Could this be considered a blessing in disguise?

"I thank the Kingdom Lord and the two seniors for your profound kindness. However, I have no intention of lingering in Clear Mist City. I have been entrusted with a mission by my master, so I beg your understanding." Li Qi raised a hand and spoke honestly.

"Naturally, I know you cannot stay for long, which is why we merely ask you to visit for two or three days. After all, given your current condition, travel must be difficult. You still need quiet recuperation. If you rest here, it will take at least ten days before you can set out. But at our sect, my sister and I are Rank 7 in the Medicine Dao. We naturally have our ways to ensure your rapid recovery."

"Secondly, we have prepared generous gifts for your visit. I have heard that your dragon colt still lacks an outstanding cultivation method. Our Clear Mist Sect can barely be considered a Demon Dao sect, but we do have some collections regarding demon race cultivation methods. It is simply inconvenient to bring them out of the sect. However, you may come and browse them yourself, taking whatever catches your eye."

"Finally, did you know... the reward the Kingdom Lord promised you is currently being kept at the Clear Mist Sect~." Hu Mei'er leaned in close, whispering softly into Li Qi's ear.

Hearing this, Li Qi shuddered.

Of course, the shudder was mainly because her warm breath had brushed against his ear.

_Damn these vixen spirits!_

Suppressing his fluttering heart, Li Qi frowned and asked, "At the Clear Mist Sect?"

"Naturally. You need only come and collect it yourself. Before I set out, my sister was already preparing the gifts and sweeping the guest paths, eagerly awaiting your visit," Hu Mei'er said.

Li Qi nodded at her words. He coughed dryly. "Senior, please step back a little. It is improper for men and women to be so close. Rest assured, since that is the case, once I am able to get out of bed and walk, I will personally pay a visit!"

"Then my sister and I shall throw our doors wide open and await your arrival~." Hu Mei'er stepped back a few paces, letting out a low chuckle before taking her leave.

Li Qi watched her go.

Then he began to eat.

Even matters as massive as the heavens could wait until he finished his meal, even if that meal was phallus soup.

However, as he ate, Li Qi pondered the situation.

Jin Buhuan was dead. He no longer had any reason to remain in Clear Mist City. But why was the Clear Mist Sect so insistent on him visiting in person?

Were there gifts that couldn't be delivered? Were there matters that couldn't be discussed right here?

Why must he go and collect them himself?

The Clear Mist Sect must be dealing with something specific.

A good thing?

A bad thing?

A conspiracy?

He didn't know, and he had no way of knowing. With a severe lack of clues, there was no way to reach a useful conclusion. He could only make thorough preparations before going. Still, he figured his life wouldn't be in danger.

If they truly wanted him dead, they could strike right now. No matter what, Li Qi was no match for a Rank 7 expert. In fact, even defeating a Rank 8 was highly unlikely.

Fighting across ranks was simply unrealistic. Every rank was an entirely different world. The gap between Rank 9 and Rank 8 was like the difference between Foundation Establishment and Transcending Tribulation. They weren't on the same level at all.

He mulled over these thoughts while continuously shoving food into his mouth—food that was undeniably delicious, but whose primary effect was boosting male virility.

Although he was incredibly weak right now.

Surely all this nourishment wouldn't cause any _issues_, right?

By the time he finished eating, a constant stream of warmth surged through his stomach, warming his frail body. Only then did he believe that Shen Shuibi genuinely thought eating like this was good for him.

Speaking of which, where was Shen Shuibi?

Where had that rabbit run off to? Why hadn't she shown up yet?

Li Qi felt a bit puzzled.

So, he clapped his hands and called out a few times.

However, the one who walked in wasn't the rabbit, but Piaoxu.

"Miss Piaoxu, why is it you? Where is Miss Shen?" Li Qi asked.

Piaoxu replied, "Mister Shen is discussing some matters with Elder Hu. She probably didn't hear you. What do you need, Mister Li? If it's anything, just give me the order."

Shen Shuibi was discussing matters with Hu Mei'er?

Li Qi frowned.

What in the world was the Clear Mist Sect scheming?

But there was no need to display his suspicions in front of Piaoxu. Thus, Li Qi said, "I've finished eating. Since you're here, Piaoxu, come. Take this."

Reaching out, Li Qi picked up the tang saber resting by his bed.

"This is?" Piaoxu stepped forward, spotting the blade in Li Qi's hand.

"This is a military saber produced by the Tang Empire. It is a Rank 9 dharma artifact, containing a sliver of metal qi, a sliver of baleful qi, and a sliver of military qi. Its power is considered top-tier among Rank 9 items. Aside from lacking a self-destruct function, it is not inferior to your throwing knife in any way," Li Qi explained.

"I gifted your throwing knife to Jin Buhuan, and later they were directly destroyed and fused into that stream of metal qi. I have no way to retrieve them and give them back to you, so I'm offering this to you as compensation."

Piaoxu quickly declined. "You saved my life, sir! Such a massive debt of gratitude cannot be repaid. How could I possibly have the nerve to accept compensation from you? I absolutely cannot take this item."

She already knew the whole story. This disaster had been purely brought about by that tiger demon and had nothing to do with anyone else. If Li Qi hadn't intervened, she would already be dead, and her throwing knife would have been snatched away by Jin Buhuan. Thus, she resolutely refused to accept the saber.

Li Qi shook his head. "Your logic is flawed. With Jin Buhuan dead, who knows how many people in Clear Mist City I've saved? Should they all be giving me a reward?"

"Since I don't require compensation from the others whose lives I saved, why would I accept it from you? What is right is right. Take it."

Piaoxu remained resolute, continuously refusing the offer.

Left with no choice, Li Qi said, "You are currently standing in the eye of the storm here in Clear Mist City. While the big shots won't care about you, there's no guarantee some underlings won't set their sights on you, suspecting you possess something valuable. You aren't skilled in combat, you hold no dharma treasures, and you are tied down by this inn. How are you supposed to survive properly in this city?"

"If you refuse this now, how will you be able to protect Qi Ya when people come looking for trouble after we leave?"

Hearing this, Piaoxu's face filled with conflict. Ultimately, she accepted the military saber.

Upon taking it, tears welled in her eyes. She dropped to her knees, kowtowing repeatedly while crying out her thanks for his grace.

Li Qi waved his hand. "Stop kowtowing. The food you cook is quite delicious. When we leave, make some extra portions for us to eat on the road. Consider that your repayment."

Unsure of what else to say, Piaoxu could only bow out and leave the room.

Leaving Li Qi lying alone on his bed.

Since he was just lying there anyway, he might as well continue reading.

He closed his eyes and began reading again, studying spell arts and learning.

Knowledge truly was power.

He could also take the opportunity to ponder how to condense his primal spirit.

With his eyes closed, he repaired his personal cosmos while absorbing the texts.

And just like that, time began to slowly slip by.

Time spent reading always passed extraordinarily quickly. Perhaps because she saw Li Qi hadn't opened his eyes, Piaoxu merely set aside the food she had brought and left.

By the time Li Qi opened his eyes again, it was already deep into the night. The food had grown cold.

He had been too engrossed in his reading, losing track of time entirely.

He was addicted to learning, unable to extricate himself.

However, when he turned his head, he saw Shen Shuibi right by his bedside, zoning out alongside him.

Outside, a bright moon hung high in the sky. The moonlight was as pure and pale as frost.

The cold, white luminescence poured in, spilling across the floor and onto Li Qi's bed, casting a faint, hazy pale-blue veil over the room.

This radiance illuminated Shen Shuibi's face, making her look all the more ethereal, elegant, and noble.

Indeed, at some unknown point, Shen Shuibi had silently taken a seat by his bed, her head tilted as she simply gazed at him.

The pure glow descended, illuminating the room and the two within it—

The old osmanthus carried a frigid scent, the mountains and rivers cast their shadows. Like snow resting upon one's robes, it felt utterly free, a myriad of winds sweeping clear.

Pure whiteness condensed like dew, crystallizing the myriad sceneries. The tranquil light lingered, filling the courtyard with shimmering ripples.

The atmosphere was so surreal that it almost made Li Qi suspect he was dreaming. Thus, he didn't speak for a moment, silently admiring the picturesque scene where beauty and setting blended into a single painting.

However, Shen Shuibi, who had never possessed a sense for romantic atmosphere, saw Li Qi open his eyes.

Thus, she spoke without hesitation, shattering the tranquility. "Li Qi, I heard from Piaoxu that you were calling for me? What's wrong? Do you need something?"

It seemed she had just been waiting for Li Qi to open his eyes...

As the dreamlike scene shattered, Li Qi felt a sudden pang of regret. He only wished he could have sealed Shen Shuibi's mouth shut so she would maintain that quiet grace for a little longer.

But that was merely a passing thought. He quickly collected his thoughts and replied, "Ah, it's nothing. I just wanted to ask, what did Hu Mei'er want with you?"

"Oh, that. She asked if I had any interest in joining the Clear Mist Sect as a guest elder. I told her I wasn't interested, so she went on about a bunch of random things. I refused them all, and then she left. Why?" Shen Shuibi tilted her head. "You called me over just to ask that?"

"I see. I understand then." Li Qi nodded and asked another question. "By the way, what are your plans moving forward?"

"Me? Aren't I here to accompany you?" Shen Shuibi asked in confusion. "Naturally, whatever you do, I'll do."

Shen Shuibi felt quite baffled as to why Li Qi would ask such a thing.

She could have returned to Shaman God Mountain ages ago. She only came to accompany him. Of course she would go wherever he went. What kind of plans would she have?

Making plans was something Li Qi was supposed to do. After all, this three-million-li journey was his path, a trial prepared for him by the Grand Invocator.

"I see. Then let's rest up. I'll make a trip to the Clear Mist Sect soon, and afterward, we'll continue our journey toward Shaman God Mountain. How does that sound?" Li Qi suggested.

"Sure, as long as you have a plan." Shen Shuibi agreed without hesitation.

"Mm, then it's settled." Their brief conversation ended. Li Qi continued to play dead in bed, while Shen Shuibi walked out the door, once again immersed in her own world, thinking about who knew what.

Whatever it was, she definitely wasn't thinking about Li Qi.

Li Qi felt a flash of irritation.

Why was the rabbit getting increasingly aloof lately?

Was she getting too familiar with him? Thus reverting to the way she used to act on Mount Luofu?

It was possible. She had mentioned before that she had once gone several centuries without speaking a single word. Now that she was growing more familiar with him, she was becoming more relaxed, naturally slipping back into that zoning-out state she had held for hundreds of years.

_This won't do. I need to think of a way to inject some novelty between us, or else we'll really just end up as platonic friends._

Fortunately, he was quick-witted and had prepared in advance.

_Recapturing the freshness of our first meeting hinges entirely on this!_

---

Leaving aside Li Qi's romantic scheming, by the time the second day arrived, he was finally able to get out of bed.

A martial artist's body truly healed rapidly. After two or three meals, combined with pills, he had essentially regained his mobility.

However, he had only regained his mobility. His physical injuries simply could not support any martial combat. Moreover, his personal cosmos showed almost no signs of recovery. He could only nurse it back to health slowly.

One could say he currently had absolutely zero combat ability.

The very first thing Li Qi did upon getting out of bed was prepare for his visit to the Clear Mist Sect.

However, the moment he opened the door, he was practically blinded.

The entire lobby of the inn was stuffed to the brim with gifts.

Pearls and jewels sparkled, radiating a myriad of colors. Countless exotic treasures crammed the inn's lobby to bursting.

Obviously, these were offerings from various factions who had come to pay Li Qi a visit.

Piaoxu had long since categorized and arranged the gifts, waiting for Li Qi to deal with them himself.

Just looking at the sight gave Li Qi a headache. He turned to Piaoxu and said, "Miss, I must trouble you to notify these people to take their gifts back. Note down anyone who refuses to accept their return in a ledger. I will personally visit their doors to deliver them back later!"

With that said, he set out without delay, heading straight toward the Clear Mist Sect.

Compared to these material items, the Clear Mist Sect was far more worthy of his time and energy.
