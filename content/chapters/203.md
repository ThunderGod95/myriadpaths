# Chapter 203: Wish

Another arrangement was set in place.

Everything was ready; all they needed was the eastern wind.

Zhang Lan knew exactly what he was doing. When facing an overwhelmingly powerful opponent, the best course of action was for each person to fulfill their duties and work together in perfect unison. Only then could they grasp that microscopic sliver of a chance at victory.

Disciples of the Righteous Sword Palace frequently amassed into armies, striking from all sides within the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest. Like a swarm of army ants, they devoured the fiends inside the nest. Thus, they were highly adept at teamwork and dividing labor.

They walked alone as knights-errant, yet gathered as a disciplined army; they were not mere Jianghu wanderers accustomed only to solo skirmishes.

Now that someone had assumed the role of strategist, his job was to perfectly execute his orders.

Thus, after listening to the rest of Li Qi's analysis and instructions, he took his leave with incredible speed. He didn't even ask Li Qi for compensation or rewards.

When a disciple of the Righteous Sword Palace performed acts of chivalry, what other reason did they need?

A demonic monk was bringing ruin to the nation. Tens of millions of commoners in an entire province had been exposed to the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest because of him. The sheer malice of his actions was evident.

Eradicating this man was the true path of righteousness! The one who struck him down would be a true grand hero!

Therefore, Zhang Lan joined the operation against a Rank 7 powerhouse without a moment's hesitation.

He did so despite knowing the harsh truth: if they failed, Li Qi likely had a trump card to save his own life, but Zhang Lan would almost certainly die.

If he died, he died. Was sacrificing one's life for justice a rare occurrence?

Zhang Lan departed, exuding heroic spirit.

Remaining behind, Li Qi watched him leave the courtyard.

Now, all the preparations were complete.

All that was left was the final fallout.

---

Time ticked by.

In the blink of an eye, a month passed.

Li Qi's clone rarely left its residence, hiding in the shadows like a spider, slowly and meticulously weaving an enormous web.

His tendrils extended across all of Vast Yang. Not just Vast Yang City, but the other county towns, and even the desolate wilderness, gradually fell under his control.

Meanwhile, his true body had successfully analyzed thirty-six devils.

On this day, Li Qi continued analyzing the devils and developing their corresponding counter-methods.

Before him were overwhelmingly complex devilish thoughts. Much like human consciousness, these thoughts were constantly shifting and changing.

They were incredibly intricate—far more complex than the vast majority of spell arts.

They resembled a massive string of garbled code. Just when the analyzer thought they had found the key, they would inevitably realize it was merely another of the devil's misdirections.

And if you failed to realize it was a misdirection...

Congratulations. The devilish thoughts would quietly worm their way into your own mind, completely undetected.

Analyzing a devil was simply that difficult and dangerous.

It was no wonder Monk Chanzhi had been completely helpless against them.

Analyzing these devils was like staring up at a sky full of complicated constellations. There seemed to be no single core—every point was a core, interconnected with all the others, weaving together into a dazzling starry sky that offered no entry point. It felt as though touching a single star would cause the entire structure to collapse.

However, he was already highly proficient at this.

By employing the True Knowledge dao rhythm for analysis and leveraging Shaman Dao cultivation methods to develop countermeasures, he achieved what ordinary cultivators found impossible. In just ten to twelve hours a day, he could fully analyze one devil.

"The thirty-seventh one. The final piece." Li Qi tossed the thirty-seventh devil aside and stood up.

He looked as though he had stayed awake for an entire month straight. His eyes were bloodshot, his veins bulged, and his face was bruised purple, giving him the appearance of someone suffering from a severe terminal illness.

He had no choice.

To claim he could interact with these devilish thoughts without being affected was simply impossible.

He _could_ use that single strand of Heavenly Devil dao rhythm to disperse the thoughts, but that might be inviting a tiger in to chase away the wolf.

Sure, the wolf would be gone, but how would he deal with the tiger?

Having finished dealing with this devil, Li Qi completed the counter-method for it and, as always, headed upstairs.

Monk Chanzhi was sitting in his usual spot. Seeing Li Qi arrive, he opened his eyes.

His gaze was warm, his voice gentle. He spoke to Li Qi with deep affection, "My young friend, which devil have you finished parsing this time?"

The monk seemed almost impatient.

This was entirely because his condition had recovered immensely over these past few days.

Thirty-six devils could now be suppressed. As long as he simultaneously circulated the thirty-six different methods, he could keep them suppressed at all times.

This greatly relieved Monk Chanzhi's pressure. His cultivation base, which had been stagnant for so long due to the devilish thoughts, was even beginning to loosen.

However, there was still a minor flaw in the methods Li Qi presented: they were all independent. They only suppressed the devils while actively utilized, meaning Chanzhi had to constantly split his focus to circulate different methods.

If all one hundred and eight methods were developed, Monk Chanzhi would need to run one hundred and eight methods simultaneously for complete suppression.

This would likely occupy five percent of his total cultivation and constantly distract him.

But as long as he stabilized his foundation, none of these devilish thoughts would be a problem once he broke through to Rank 6.

Being able to alleviate the crisis at his current Rank 7 was enough to thoroughly satisfy Chanzhi.

As expected, compared to those Gu Shaman arts, only the orthodox Shaman Dao possessed such miraculous capabilities.

He was incredibly lucky to have stumbled upon an orthodox inheritor of Shaman God Mountain passing through. Although the boy had tried to hide, he ultimately couldn't escape Chanzhi's dharma eyes.

It had only been a month, and already thirty-six devils were dealt with.

In at most a year, he would return to normal, restabilize his cultivation base, and forge a solid foundation to step into Rank 6.

With his Great Dao at stake, Monk Chanzhi was naturally incredibly kind to Li Qi. Aside from forbidding him to leave, the monk essentially granted his every request.

Nevertheless, whenever Li Qi demonstrated a method, he would carefully scrutinize it.

There was absolutely no chance for Li Qi to slip in any hidden traps to harm him.

Although he didn't understand the Shaman Dao and couldn't balance the devilish thoughts himself, he could easily discern a method's specific function. Thus, Li Qi could play no tricks on him.

Li Qi walked over and said, "Master, the one I analyzed this time is the Killing Intent Devil. It embodies the urge of perpetually recurring murderous intent within the heart. The suppression method is complete. Please observe my demonstration, Master."

Chanzhi instantly sat up straight and focused on Li Qi.

Li Qi began his demonstration, displaying how to suppress the devil.

It was just another routine exchange.

After this demonstration, Chanzhi closed his eyes, preparing to slowly digest the method's logic and familiarize himself with its circulation, just as he always did.

Suddenly, however, he noticed that Li Qi had not left as usual.

This caused Chanzhi to frown slightly.

Normally, Li Qi would hastily scurry away the moment the demonstration was over. He was clearly terrified of him. It was precisely this fear that allowed Chanzhi to control the boy and force him to create these cultivation methods.

What was going on today?

Li Qi looked rather apprehensive. Seeing Chanzhi looking at him, he hurriedly cupped his hands and said, "Master... I have stayed here for over a month, researching methods for you day and night. Surely, this can be considered doing my utmost, right?"

"Naturally. But why do you speak of this so suddenly, young friend? Is there something you need?" Chanzhi asked.

Li Qi immediately bowed deeply with trepidation. "To be honest, Master, I was always afraid of delaying your grand matters, so I never dared mention this. But it has been so long, and I believe I have done my absolute best. Only now do I have the courage to bring this up."

"The truth is, I have a female companion. She grew up isolated from society, is poor with words, and has an aloof temperament by nature. Since I have been gone for over a month, I worry she may have suffered some grievance out there. I beg Master to grant me two days of leave so I can go soothe her. If something untoward were to happen, it might inadvertently ruin Master's Great Dao," Li Qi pleaded.

Hearing Li Qi's words, Chanzhi smiled. "So that is the case. This poor monk understands. Young friend, simply tell me your companion's name, appearance, and characteristics. Then, provide me with a token of trust. I will naturally arrange for someone to bring her here so the two of you can stay together."

Li Qi smiled bitterly. "I fear it will require Master to act personally. Master should have seen my companion before. Her cultivation base is also at Rank 8, and her strength is extraordinary. Since she is withdrawn and also a demon, ordinary people wouldn't be able to invite her; they would only cause unnecessary casualties."

Monk Chanzhi nodded. "This poor monk will keep your warning in mind. However, you need not worry your head over such trivial, mundane matters. Since I have promised to bring your companion here, I will not break my word. How I do it is my concern. You should focus your mind on developing the cultivation methods; that is the true path."

With Li Qi mentioning it, he did recall the event.

When he struck to intimidate Li Qi, there was indeed a woman fighting alongside him. Furthermore, her spell arts had been exceptionally profound, reaching the absolute pinnacle of Rank 8.

Very rarely did anyone utilize spell arts with such flawless precision, which had left a deep impression on Monk Chanzhi.

So it was that woman.

Seeing Chanzhi agree, Li Qi bowed slightly. "Then, thank you, Master."

He then pulled out a piece of paper he had prepared in advance and handed it to Chanzhi. "Here is her name and characteristics. As for her appearance, she is an exceedingly beautiful woman dressed in plain white clothes. I have no token on me, but she always carries a white jade hairpin styled with a Jade Rabbit motif. Just tell her it was bought in Clear Mist City, and she will know it was me."

"Very well. Just await the good news, young friend," Chanzhi said, taking the paper and watching Li Qi depart.

After confirming Li Qi was gone, Monk Chanzhi lightly patted the ground.

The entire fifth-tier Buddhist Pagoda vibrated imperceptibly.

The surroundings of the pagoda began to fill with some sort of... flesh and blood?

This flesh should have been disgusting, akin to rotting meat, yet it simultaneously evoked a scorching, fervent desire. It vaguely brought to mind the sacrifices dedicated to Mahakala.

The demonic rites constructed from mountains of blood and flesh, infused with the Five Nectars[^1].

They were utterly vile, yet surged with an indescribably terrifying power—unbelievably radiant and overflowing with shocking vitality.

Flames burned upon the flesh, the union of the two creating a piercing, vivid crimson.

It delivered the shock of fire, the malice of flesh, and the absolute deterrence of raw power.

Upon witnessing this crimson, even the ambient light seemed to soften. The surrounding world took on a bubble-like tenderness, intoxicating the mind and spirit, bringing with it the fragrance of spring and the clarity of a bubbling mountain spring.

What agonizing delusion could possibly manifest such imagery?

These colors seemed alive, lacking any fixed form. Fleshy and plump, they radiated a dense aura of life, as if imbued with a unique, melancholic emotion.

They had been infected by something. Anyone who looked upon them would find doors opening to unknown secrets of the soul, allowing them to step into the mystical palaces of illusion.

They birthed unpredictable dangers. A single glance might twist a person into a wild beast, or perhaps elevate them to a god.

The coloration of everything was profoundly bizarre, inducing a deep-seated unease that was impossible to articulate clearly.

Absurd and eerie, yet somehow feeling perfectly natural.

A primordial, brutal entity brimming with vitality and power manifested before his eyes, inciting an involuntary wave of terror in any who beheld it.

This flesh and flame enveloped the fifth-tier Buddhist Pagoda floating in the void.

It was gripped within a formless terror, impossible to escape.

This was Mahakala's divine ability.

Enveloped by this divine ability, Li Qi had absolutely zero chance of escaping, even if another Rank 7 expert intervened.

No one below Rank 6—no matter how heaven-defying their talent or unparalleled their heroism—could possibly break through this blockade.

With this in place, there was no logical way for Li Qi to flee.

Now, it was time to find that Miss Shen.

Li Qi's words held some truth. When facing a peak Rank 8 cultivator, the entirety of Vast Yang might not have a single suitable person to send with an invitation.

If anyone else went and she refused, they wouldn't be able to defeat her, making coercion impossible.

The puppet of the Minister of War could go, but that puppet had a grudge against her, which could easily spark a conflict.

If a battle erupted due to her aloof nature and that underlying grudge, he would be forced to use his overwhelming might to suppress her, which would be highly counterproductive.

After all, he was supposed to be _inviting_ her. Li Qi had been honestly helping him research cultivation methods, working himself into a state neither human nor ghost. To ensure the boy remained diligent in the future, he couldn't push him too far.

Because ultimately... this was a direct descendant of Shaman God Mountain.

However, he guessed Li Qi wasn't a Young Master, merely a standard direct inheritor.

If a Rank 7 expert had truly imprisoned a Rank 9 Young Master of Shaman God Mountain, the mountain would have dispatched someone immediately, without a single second of delay.

The ascension of a Young Master usually involved monumental fanfare and the mobilization of massive forces.

Great Shamans were extremely cautious when selecting disciples, much like Buddhas choosing theirs. They usually underwent rigorous trials; it was never an easy process.

Chanzhi knew of one such example. Once, to select a single disciple, a Buddha had observed a true spirit and sent it through ten lifetimes of reincarnation, meticulously arranging ten completely different lives.

Across all ten lifetimes—whether impoverished or wealthy, male or female, no matter what they experienced—this disciple's heart always turned toward the Buddhist Sect. They always returned before the Buddha, dedicating their life to the blue lamp and wooden fish[^2] until they naturally passed away.

Only in the eleventh lifetime were they judged to possess the aptitude of a Son of Buddha and finally accepted as a disciple.

While he didn't know how Shaman God Mountain conducted its internal selections, a Young Master dying at Rank 9 would be a waste of apocalyptic proportions.

Merely being chosen was proof enough of many things.

Therefore, after testing the waters multiple times by feigning attacks on Li Qi and seeing no protector emerge, it was clear the boy wasn't a Young Master, just a direct descendant.

So there was no need to fear retaliation. He just had to wait and let the boy slowly toil away for him.

Having completely sealed the pagoda, Chanzhi's true body left the area.

He was off to fetch Li Qi's woman to keep the boy company.

[^1]: In the context of Vajrayana Buddhism, the "Five Nectars" have two distinct interpretations depending on the nature of the ritual.
    
    - **Peaceful Rites:** They represent pure, auspicious offerings, typically listed as milk, ghee (clarified butter), yogurt, honey, and sugar.
    - **Wrathful Rites:** In the left-hand path or wrathful deity practices—such as those associated with Mahakala—the Five Nectars refer to five deeply taboo bodily substances: feces, urine, blood, semen, and marrow (or human flesh/brain). The practitioner visualizes transforming these ultimate impurities into pure, transcendent nectar, symbolizing the non-dualistic nature of reality where purity and impurity are one.
[^2]: In Chinese Buddhism, the "blue lamp" (a simple oil lamp used to read scriptures late into the night) and the "wooden fish" (a hollow wooden percussion instrument struck to keep rhythm while chanting) are fundamental symbols of temple life.
