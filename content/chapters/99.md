# Chapter 99: Inquiry

"I... I lost," Jing Yan said, staring in disbelief at her fallen longsword.

"Whoa! Look! That tigress actually lost!" someone gloated.

"What? The Hall Master clearly said that within Rank 9, Jing Yan should hardly find a match!" Jing Yan's subordinates seemed unable to comprehend the sight, all staring in dumbfounded shock.

"Hardly finding a match doesn't mean she has no match. I'd say Young Hero Li is a supreme expert; this journey is in the bag!" Some people had already taken Li Qi's side, clapping and cheering.

It was likely they had been disciplined by Jing Yan more than a few times in the past.

As for Shen Shuibi, she let out a long sigh of relief.

_Thank goodness. Although Li Qi is a massive idiot, I am still very formidable. As long as he does exactly as I say, even a pig could beat that arrogant woman to a pulp._

_Hmph!_

Shen Shuibi placed her hands on her hips, looking incredibly pleased and proud of herself. Her face practically screamed, "Hurry up and praise me!"

Unfortunately, Li Qi didn't even look at her, robbing her proudly puffed-out chest of any sense of accomplishment.

Inside the ring.

"I concede," Li Qi said, withdrawing his long blade. He pulled out a scroll of cloth—the diagram of weaknesses Shen Shuibi had drawn the night before. "Then, according to our wager, I present you with the weaknesses my little sister perceived, to help elevate your martial art to the next level, Elder Sister..."

"Wait, Li Qi. You said before... your little sister actually has a cultivation base, but her path simply isn't suited for combat, so she only appears frail, when in reality she can see through my weaknesses." Jing Yan stopped Li Qi, not rushing to take the scroll of cloth.

"Exactly," Li Qi replied.

Jing Yan nodded, picked up her sword, and sighed. "I didn't lose to you... I lost to your little sister, and to my own arrogance."

Then, to Li Qi's shock, she sheathed her sword, walked over to Shen Shuibi, dropped to one knee, and cupped her hands in a formal apology.

Shen Shuibi was completely flustered, forcing Li Qi to step forward and help Jing Yan up in her stead.

"There's no need for this, Elder Sister Jing. To err is human; there's truly no need for such a grand gesture," Li Qi said as he supported Jing Yan to her feet.

Jing Yan, however, shook her head. "It is precisely because I erred that I must correct it. Full of arrogance and complacency, I nearly made a grave mistake that could have ruined my path. Your little sister awakened me to this fact; she is my benefactor."

"Now, with the weaknesses you have pointed out, I know exactly how to improve. This is a massive favor I can hardly repay. Words of gratitude are certainly not enough. I hope the two of you will accept this as compensation." As Jing Yan spoke, she produced a red stone.

She then turned to Li Qi and said, "I do not know what your little sister needs, but I observed that your five viscera tempering is incomplete. Your heart is weak and unable to bear the burden of your vitality, making the circulation of your qi and blood extremely difficult. The heart belongs to the fire element. I obtained this stone by chance. Although I do not know exactly what it is, I consulted an expert who said it contains a sliver of pure fire qi. If you can find an expert from the Daoist Sects to extract it and temper your heart, it might help you remedy this flaw."

"Just as you have remedied my flaws," Jing Yan added.

Li Qi and Shen Shuibi looked at each other in dismay.

This Jing Yan... was perhaps a bit too upright.

The events that followed unfolded naturally.

Jing Yan accepted the diagram of weaknesses, but she didn't begin practicing immediately because she still had a duty to fulfill: guarding the caravan.

As for Li Qi, he naturally had to work as well. After all, a wage of one gold piece a day—ten thousand copper coins—wasn't given to him for doing nothing. He naturally had to guard the caravan alongside Jing Yan.

The rest of the caravan members dispersed to attend to their own tasks after the spectacle concluded.

Everything returned to normal, incredibly peaceful.

There were a few wild beast attacks along the way, but under the protection of Li Qi and Jing Yan, the beasts were easily dispatched, providing extra meals for the group. The pelts and bones were collected by the caravan, treated as additional goods.

After the day ended, Li Qi returned to the ox cart he shared with Shen Shuibi as they set up camp for the night.

"Li Qi, Li Qi! This is a piece of Fire-Refined Stone Essence!" Shen Shuibi said, tugging at Li Qi, looking exceedingly happy.

"What is that?" Li Qi asked, staring at the red stone, completely lost.

"As the saying goes, 'There is no utility greater than this; stones attain perfection through fire-refinement.' That means once a stone has been refined by fire, it gains wondrous uses." Shen Shuibi immediately began to explain.

"Alum manifests the forms of the five metals, and sulfur is the general of all stones; both are transformed by raging fire. Some Art Masters possess the method of fire-refining stones.[^1] By using the technique of 'fire-refinement' to refine stones, ancient texts record that 'having been refined by fire for use, once its substance is formed, it will not decay even if submerged in water for an eternity.' Because of these properties, fire-refined stones have many uses."

"Although this stone isn't a fire-refined stone itself, it is a clump formed from the remnants of extensive fire-refinement It is the condensed fire qi left over from an Art Master refining countless stones. With this, I can help you temper your heart, and then you'll only have your spleen left to temper," Shen Shuibi explained, holding the red stone.

"Is it really that useful?" Li Qi took the stone. It felt cool to the touch and just looked like an ordinary red rock.

"Alright! I'll help you temper your heart right now!" Shen Shuibi began rolling up her sleeves.

"Wait! Don't take off my clothes!" Li Qi panicked.

"How am I supposed to use it if I don't take off your clothes!"

"I'll do it myself!"

---

The night passed.

Li Qi felt his heart thumping powerfully, filled with robust strength.

Now, he was only missing the spleen tempering. But Shen Shuibi said this would naturally temper itself over time.

Because the spleen belonged to the earth element, it seemed useless among the five viscera, yet it assisted in the operation of the other four organs.

As the earth element, it was something that could simultaneously participate with the other four viscera, just as the earth encompassed all creation.

The earth could conceal metal, water flowed upon its surface, wood could grow from it, and fire could erupt from its depths.

Correspondingly, the liver produced blood, but the spleen stored it. The heart's Yang qi could reach the spleen. The spleen's transformation of water and grain essence qi could benefit the lungs. And the spleen and kidneys worked together to metabolize water and dampness.

Once the other four viscera were perfected, the spleen would naturally grow robust after a period of time.

Therefore, as long as he waited, within half a year at most, Li Qi's Rank 9 would reach the standard of a true Martial Dao Rank 9.

Feeling all of this, Li Qi began his second day of work.

He patrolled around the caravan with Jing Yan, looking around as they moved forward.

As they crossed paths during a patrol, Li Qi suddenly remembered something and called out to her.

"Elder Sister Jing," Li Qi called out.

"What is it?" Jing Yan turned her head, unsure of what Li Qi wanted.

However, her attitude remained very good. Li Qi and his sister were her benefactors, and she always remembered favors owed.

"The Jing family is an aristocratic family of the Song Kingdom, correct?" Li Qi asked.

"Indeed. Why do you ask?" Jing Yan was perplexed.

"What is the current situation in the Song Kingdom?" Li Qi asked again.

He wanted to learn about the situation in the Song Kingdom from a member of the Jing family.

The Song Kingdom, after all, was at war with the Tang Empire and had become their bridgehead in the confrontation against Shaman God Mountain.
