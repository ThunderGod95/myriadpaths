# Chapter 140: The Flute and the Gallbladder

Inside that private box, there were a total of three or four people: a leading young noblemen, a hunched old man, a middle-aged man with red hair and a red beard, and the young nobleman's personal maid.

The hunched old man heard the young nobleman's summons, stood up from his chair, closed his eyes to sense for a moment, and then said, "Young Master, it is indeed the ghost qi of the malicious ghost I raised. It didn't return when the curse was cast earlier; it seems someone has bound it."

The young nobleman frowned. "Who is meddling in other people's business? Stopping me from cursing a tiger demon to death? Are they deliberately trying to make things difficult for me? Perform a divination to see who stopped it. Have him come to my door and apologize, and I will spare him this once."

"Young Master, please continue to enjoy the show. I will handle this immediately." The old man known as Elder Si immediately rose. Right where he stood, he laid out chicken bones and tortoise shells to begin his divination.

He manipulated the milfoil stalks and prepared the tortoise shells, boring into the shell for divination and sorting the stalks for augury.

First, he scorched the chicken bones, but the bones cracked violently. The fissures spread wide, directly shattering them into two halves.

Elder Si's eyes widened in surprise. He immediately switched to forty-nine slender bamboo sticks, representing the forty-nine heavenly laws, to divine once more. Yet, the slender bamboos directly withered, turned dry and yellow, and then crumbled into flying ash.

He was instantly stunned, his gaze turning solemn, but he did not stop.

He took out a roof tile and struck it to split it apart, intending to observe its textures. However, the interior of the tile was as smooth as a mirror. There was no trace of it being struck at all; it looked as if it had been sliced cleanly.

Now, he began to panic. He hastily took out the tortoise shell, bit the tip of his tongue, and spat out a mouthful of fresh blood. With a stern face, he held the shell and faced south. He then used the qi of blazing fire to burn the tortoise shell, but the shell seemed flawlessly whole, completely unchanged within the roaring flames.

Holding the tortoise shell in both hands, he immediately walked over to another person in the room and said gravely, "Lord Yan, please assist me."

The man known as Lord Yan was a bit taken aback, but he immediately cooperated.

He formed a hand seal with both hands and declared, "I will scorch this tortoise shell with the worldly fires of men, using the providence of the Human Dao to help you divine the result."

Then, a ball of fire ignited in his hands. Amidst the spreading flames, the entirety of Clear Mist City appeared within the fire. One could even clearly see the officials and their subordinates, dancing troupes and musicians, traveling merchants, and roadside peddlers.

The whole of Clear Mist City was captured as a scene within the fire. Looking into the flames was like looking at the entire city. The fire entered the tortoise shell, flickering and transforming within the light, shadows, smoke, and flames, blurring the line between whether it was the fire within Clear Mist City or Clear Mist City within the fire.

However, Lord Yan was surprised to find that the tortoise shell still remained completely unresponsive.

"I will raise the stakes. I'll use the spirit beasts I raised to burn it again!" Lord Yan said. He then slapped a cage at his waist, and several pigeons flew out from within.

The flock of pigeons danced in the air, their bodies engulfed in blazing flames. The tips of every pigeon's wings bore fire, burning like torches.

These pigeons spewed out sparks, diving into the worldly flames and causing the fire to roar even brighter.

Yet, the tortoise shell remained unmoving and unshaken within the fire, as smooth and clean as new.

"Since human fire and beast fire are insufficient, I will use earth fire and try again!" Frustrated and angry, he declared, then exerted all his strength to pull at the void.

A small rift tore open in the void, and Yin qi overflowed in all directions!

Lord Yan's face flushed deep red, and sweat poured down like rain. He had clearly used every ounce of his strength. Through that small rift, a scene of hell revealed itself!

Dark clouds billowed, and smoke and dust rolled. It rained incessantly from the sky, but it was not water; instead, it was a multitude of blazing hail. Intense flames roared upon them, radiating a fierce and terrifying heat.

There were also hellish beings and countless ethereal souls bound tightly in place, unable to move, forced to endure the blazing hail. They were struck almost every passing second. The hail burned their skin, seared their flesh, charred their sinews, scorched their bones, and incinerated their marrow. The agony was unparalleled. The massive smoke and flames pierced right through their bodies, combusting their flesh and blood.

The beings of Nether Prison suffered extreme torment within the flames, begging for death but unable to obtain it.

Lord Yan completely ignored all of this. He reached through the small rift and grabbed a piece of blazing hail that had just fallen. Immediately after, the rift closed, and the Yin qi ceased to leak.

His hand instantly caught fire. Even his face twisted dramatically in unbearable pain. The flesh and bone in the palm of his hand charred, nearly burning straight through.

Grabbing this hellish blazing hail, he swiftly tossed it into the tortoise shell.

The worldly fires of men, the heart fire of a spirit beast, and the fire of Nether Prison—the three combined into one, jointly scorching the tortoise shell.

The tortoise shell could not withstand such extreme temperatures and finally began to tremble.

Seeing this, both Lord Yan and Elder Si breathed a sigh of relief.

However, the tortoise shell shuddered repeatedly, cracked open from the center, and then directly exploded, shattering into several fragments!

It was the maid standing to the side whose reaction was the fastest. With a wave of her hand, a roll of silk flew out. Splendid layers overlapped, weaving in and out to form a ring that locked the explosion inside.

The explosive force was not very large, but the power of Lord Yan's flames contained within was formidable. It incinerated the entire roll of silk and left Lord Yan and Elder Si covered in ash and dirt.

As for the melons, fruits, delicacies, soups, tablecloths, seat cushions, wine pots, and cups and chopsticks inside the private box, they were all overturned by the shockwave of the explosion, leaving the entire room in a state of chaotic ruin.

But Elder Si couldn't care less about his ash-covered face. He jumped up in shock, hastily bowed, and said to the young nobleman, "Young Master! Either this person is of a very high rank that I cannot provoke, or they possess a protective treasure that prevents divination!"

The young nobleman frowned. Having watched the entire process, he naturally understood what had happened.

Both Lord Yan and Elder Si were Rank 8 experts. This was especially true for Elder Si. Although he hadn't had the fortune to join Shaman God Mountain, he had luckily obtained fragmented orthodox cultivation methods of the Shaman Dao. Whether in divination or cursing, he possessed a fraction of the bearing of an orthodox Diviner, making him more than ten times stronger than ordinary shamans. He was a highly trusted subordinate.

"It is unlikely that they are of a very high rank. If that were truly the case, your tortoise shell wouldn't have just exploded; the other party would have directly peered back at us. But there's no need to worry. If he really saw me, he would naturally back down. At least within the Green Mountain Kingdom, even if the Kingdom Lord came, he would at most mete out a minor punishment on my father's account. He wouldn't go overboard," the young nobleman said, tapping the table.

The Lord of the Green Mountain Kingdom was a Rank 5 mighty figure, already the strongest existence in this region. As for anyone higher...

He hadn't even considered it; it was utterly impossible.

Those above Rank 5 were already masters of a nation. Their mighty power was astonishing; with a wave of their hands, they could shatter a minor world, and the shockwaves of their battles could easily lay waste to ten thousand li around. Plucking the stars and grasping the moon was well within their means. Moving up to Rank 4, even he had never seen such an existence.

That level of majesty had reached an unimaginable degree. An entity like that wouldn't bother bickering with him.

"Therefore, it's likely they have a treasure protecting their karma and lifelines, causing Elder Si's curse to fail and making divination impossible... Tsk tsk, such a treasure should belong to someone fated for it, which suits me perfectly. How could it be left to a tiger demon?" He stood up, not even bothering to watch the Ever-Victorious deathmatches he usually enjoyed.

"Let's go, we're heading down. There's no need for divination; we will simply follow the ghost qi to see what's going on with that tiger demon. We'll pick up that dust-covered treasure while we're at it. This will allow it to regain its glory instead of suffering the indignity of being stranded in the hands of a demon." The young nobleman rose and walked out of the private box.

Lord Yan, Elder Si, and the maid all followed suit, heading downstairs.

---

As for Li Qi, at this very moment, he felt absolutely nothing.

There was no helping it. As an orthodox Rank 9 of the Shaman Dao, he had transcended mortality and birthed a personal cosmos within his body. He could now go a whole year without food or drink without suffering any ill effects. Even breathing was no longer strictly necessary; he could hold his breath for four or five days and act normally, save for the inability to speak.

After all, without breathing, there was no airflow to vibrate his vocal cords, rendering speech impossible.

When the Shaman Dao was cultivated to profound levels, the personal cosmos could even produce living creatures. The body would form a world unto itself, free, eternal, and completely independent of external resources.

Attempting to divine Li Qi was, in reality, attempting to divine a minor world. Even though it was currently very small, its tier was still significantly higher than that of an individual lifeform.

Furthermore, he was someone of the Outer Dao, making divination exceedingly difficult.

However, Li Qi had recently acquired some relevant knowledge. He knew that not everyone of the Outer Dao was like him, a person whose traces were difficult to capture even for a Grand Diviner.

To be precise, the further one was from All Under Heaven, the harder it was to perform a divination. This was because it involved the Heavenly Dao of the corresponding world; divination did not possess an effect that transcended distance.

For instance, a Rank 9 Diviner could easily calculate and divine matters within the Earth Oil World.

To make it difficult even for a Grand Diviner to calculate... In any case, Li Qi had no idea how far away Earth was.

But these were all aimless thoughts. Li Qi's primary focus right now was still on selecting a Windwalk Talisman.

Because... there were simply too many.

Yes, far too many.

The barrier to entry for learning the Windwalk Talisman was low, making it an entry-level product for talisman masters. The cost was almost nonexistent; it only required ordinary talisman paper, ink, and the talisman master's own qi. Essentially, they were just selling their craftsmanship.

Therefore, nearly every shop here sold Windwalk Talismans. Moreover, depending on the maker's style, the effects varied greatly.

The books hadn't mentioned this. It was the first time Li Qi had learned that Windwalk Talismans possessed different properties.

The heavens possessed the Eight Winds, each with different properties. Depending on which type the maker was adept at and familiar with, the Windwalk Talisman would exhibit different special effects.

Some simply made one run faster, some lasted longer, others granted the ability of brief flight, and some even combined two or three different effects.

If it were a talisman master who had mastered all Eight Winds, they could even craft a Windwalk Talisman that simultaneously possessed eight different properties.

Of course, the price would certainly soar right along with it.

This was also Li Qi's first time visiting such a market. Since it wasn't written in the books, he certainly wouldn't have known, so he was slowly learning as he went.

Shen Shuibi, on the other hand, had an excellent eye despite not being a talisman master. She could always point out the flaws, greatly assisting Li Qi in making his choices.

Piaoxu also frequently chimed in to remind them of certain shops' reputations and the like, making the selection process exceedingly smooth.

Soon, Li Qi picked out three talismans that met his needs. They were all geared toward extreme acceleration. Furthermore, by pointing out the flaws Shen Shuibi had spotted, he managed to haggle the price down by nearly a third, acquiring them for two gold coins each.

At this point, Li Qi only had one gold coin and a tiny bit of loose change left on him.

Fortunately, as long as he didn't overspend, it was fine.

However, he was well aware of the production cost of Windwalk Talismans. At two gold coins apiece, aside from the manual labor, it was practically pure profit. Furthermore, Windwalk Talismans were highly susceptible to damage and were considered consumables; the slightest bump during combat or travel would destroy them. They relied entirely on bulk sales. A skilled talisman master could easily draw ten or twenty of them a day.

But for him, this was a good thing.

The cheaper, the better.

But...

"To think two gold coins is considered cheap..." Li Qi sighed emotionally.

Just a year ago, he was still struggling for a few copper coins, yet now he actually felt that 'something costing two gold coins is truly too cheap, simply an excellent bargain.'

Two gold coins equaled twenty thousand copper coins!

Piaoxu cast a glance at Li Qi, somewhat puzzled. That an expert of his caliber would haggle was already surprising enough to her; what was the meaning behind this emotional sigh now?

Shen Shuibi, on the other hand, had no reaction whatsoever. She had reverted to her usual dazed state, completely immersed in her own world.

It was likely because of this personality that she hadn't stepped foot outside Mount Luofu in several thousand years, allowing Li Qi to string her along with half-truths from the very beginning...

_Hmm... I bought the Windwalk Talismans, but for a cultivation method, I'm afraid my money won't be quite enough. I need to figure out a way to earn some money. I don't have any supplies in my pockets either. Should I make a trip to the Green Mountain?_ Li Qi pondered his upcoming itinerary in his heart.

A thing like a cultivation method was bound to be outrageously expensive. He probably wouldn't be able to buy it with just gold.

"Well, we've bought the items, so let's head back to avoid holding up your business. We've been out for almost three hours now; going back now is just in time for a meal," Li Qi suggested after making his purchases.

"As you say, sir." Piaoxu appeared much more relaxed now. She offered a polite curtsy and led the way forward.

Shen Shuibi naturally had no objections. She had already activated her follow mode anyway; she just needed to walk along, paying no mind to anything else.

However, at this very moment.

Shen Shuibi, who had been absentmindedly trailing along, suddenly sprang into action. She stepped forward and grabbed Piaoxu, giving the fox a fright.

"Madam, what is it?" Piaoxu highly respected this female expert who had saved her, so she hurriedly asked, assuming the other party had something to discuss.

However, the moment Shen Shuibi pulled Piaoxu back, Li Qi immediately made his move.

The qi of the Five Great Mountains surged from within his body. It was incomparably steady, sinking into the earth like the roots of a mountain, forming a thick shield of the Five Great Mountains.

Yet, accompanied by the sound of a bamboo flute, the steady qi that composed the Five Great Mountains was pierced right through.

Li Qi furrowed his brow.

The bamboo flute represented the qi of the center. All things were born from silence and manifested from the formless. It was austerity, a formless killing intent. The mournful whistling of the flute governed bleak desolation. The sayings 'mournful and desolate' and 'the rustling, desolate trees' were derived precisely from this concept.

_The other party planned a sneak attack from the very start? And with lethal intent?_

Just as sound could penetrate a solid shield, the qi of the flute could easily pass through Li Qi's internal qi of the Five Great Mountains. Normal defenses were useless against it.

But this did not stump Li Qi.

He fiercely swung his fist, striking the position of his liver and gallbladder!

The qi of the gallbladder represented tremors. A person's indignation would vibrate outwards, unleashing the sound of utmost virtue.

The qi of the liver represented life. It sensed the qi of harmony, its roots lying at the origin of vitality.

Just as the vitality brought by a thunderclap could dispel the aura of bleak desolation, Li Qi pounded his liver and gallbladder while bellowing loudly, a resounding roar bursting forth: "Haa!"

The other people in the surroundings scrambled to dodge, and that flute's whistling was shattered by Li Qi's roaring voice infused with the qi of his liver and gallbladder. However, Li Qi coughed slightly.

Punching himself hurt a lot. He had probably sustained a minor injury.

"Who is it?" Li Qi dispersed his defense of the Five Great Mountains and looked toward the origin of the flute's sound.
