# Chapter 160: Demon Nest

The main branch of the Clear Mist Sect, situated on a tranquil mountain.

Inside a thatched hut, a few cups of tea were poured, accompanied by the fragrance of beauties.

At the table, a few words were exchanged, heroes conversing with laughter.

The Green Mountain Kingdom Lord put away his brush and paper, smiling as he said, "Truly, heroes emerge from the youth. When I was at Rank 9, I would tremble in the presence of a Rank 8, not daring to utter a single word out of turn. Yet you, Li Qi, can remain completely composed while facing me. You even dared to cross swords with Jin Chi. If news of your courage were to spread, who knows how many young heroes would gaze upon you in admiration."

Li Qi broke out in a sweat. Wiping his brow, he picked up his teacup and took a tactical sip. "The Kingdom Lord praises me too highly. I merely acted as a middleman, navigating between factions. The Kingdom Lord was the one who bore the true danger. What is there to praise about this junior?"

"Too modest, far too modest." The Green Mountain Kingdom Lord appeared exceedingly magnanimous, completely devoid of any domineering airs. "I recounted your deeds to a few of my friends, and they were all amazed. You must understand, an ordinary Rank 9 would likely die of a ruptured gallbladder out of sheer terror just from witnessing a battle of our level. Yet you managed to execute Jin Buhuan without batting an eye. That is no ordinary feat."

These words were entirely true. Li Qi knew it from personal experience. 

Back before he had even ranked in the Shaman Dao, he had witnessed the battle between the Bear Slaughter King and the Curse Demon King. Just feeling the killing intent radiating from them had nearly killed him on the spot.

That sliver of killing intent possessed no actual energy; it was purely an aura. Yet that very aura had nearly frightened Li Qi to death. Only through immense willpower and a semi-suicidal technique to tear his own divine soul had he barely managed to hold on. 

The disparity between their levels was simply too vast.

However, after experiencing such things frequently, one could build up a resistance. Li Qi's luck was exceptional, his background was prominent, and he had witnessed far too many grand spectacles. As a result, he had grown somewhat accustomed to it...

But naturally, he couldn't divulge such details. Instead, he replied, "This junior has always been somewhat slow-witted. I am not particularly sensitive to things like killing intent, so I suppose I took quite a bit of an advantage in that regard."

Hearing this, the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord let out a hearty laugh. "I suppose that counts as a unique innate talent. But let us put that aside. The main reason I called you here today was to speak with you face-to-face and to bestow the reward you requested."

With a wave of his hand, he instructed the beautiful woman beside him, "Xian'er, heal Li Qi's injuries. Dragging around a wounded body makes it impossible to fully enjoy the tea!"

Receiving her orders, Hu Xian'er immediately nodded. She stepped forward and extended a hand invitingly. "Mister Li, please extend your hand so that I may examine and treat you."

"Then I must trouble Senior," Li Qi said properly, putting on no airs of being an arrogant genius.

He simply wasn't worthy of such arrogance.

Just because someone else elevated him didn't mean he had the right to look down on others.

He understood this basic truth perfectly.

He extended his hand, and Hu Xian'er grasped it.

Upon taking his hand, Li Qi felt as though his skin were gliding through warm spring water. Her touch was soft and fragrant, her skin like flawless, delicate snow.

He felt as though this place was constantly testing his Dao Heart...

However, this sensation didn't last long. The moment she took his hand, he suddenly felt a ball of fire ignite within his body.

It wasn't the fire of lust, but rather a massive influx of Yang qi surging into his frail, cold, and Yin-dominated internal organs.

With Yin suppressed and Yang rising, the severe exhaustion and internal damage he had previously sustained were being subtly mended.

Was Hu Xian'er... performing surgery on him?

She wasn't simply pumping him full of Yang qi. Such a method was far too crude; it might make him feel better in the short term, but it would be like pouring water into a shattered vessel—it would all just leak out.

Shen Shuibi's approach had been slow dietary supplementation, having Piaoxu feed him massive amounts of vitality-boosting foods while personally refining pills to replenish his Yang qi and mend his body, allowing the vessel to slowly heal itself.

That method had no side effects and suited Shen Shuibi's current lack of vital energy.

But Hu Xian'er employed a far more direct method. Completely disregarding the drain on her own vital energy, she performed an operation directly inside Li Qi's body.

Li Qi could feel countless infinitesimal streams of qi swimming through his system. Some acted as needles, some as threads, and others as scalpels.

Wherever his organs, muscles, or bones were damaged beyond repair, they were cleanly excised. Wherever they could be saved, they were mended.

Needles threaded, scalpels cut, and wounds were stitched together, yet he felt absolutely no pain.

It was a wondrous sensation.

He suddenly understood Hu Xian'er's methods.

This was... surgery at the molecular level?

It was as if a swarm of nanobots was repairing the wounds inside him, even patching up his personal cosmos in the process.

Such a technique was nothing short of miraculous!

As the surgery progressed, Hu Xian'er's expression gradually grew solemn. She even began to sweat, her body trembling slightly.

It was evident that directly performing such delicate operations at the molecular level placed a tremendous burden on her.

After about fifteen minutes, Hu Xian'er let out a long breath. "Fortunately, I did not fail my task... Your injuries are largely healed, sir."

Li Qi stretched his limbs.

Indeed, he was healed.

His entire body had been restored to its original state. All of his injuries, even hidden traumas, had been cured. Fractured bones and torn tendons had been reconnected, and the cracks in his personal cosmos had been completely sealed.

"Thank you, Senior!" Li Qi bowed respectfully.

An injury that would have required months of slow recuperation had been fully treated in a mere fifteen minutes. As expected of a Rank 7!

"You need not be so formal, sir. This was merely my duty." Not daring to accept his bow, Hu Xian'er stepped aside to avoid it and retreated to the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord's side.

"Alright, now that your injuries are mostly healed, here is the reward you requested. Take it, and be sure to hide it well. This item is somewhat too precious for a Rank 9," the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord said with a smile, handing over a small bottle.

It was a porcelain bottle that looked entirely ordinary.

Li Qi accepted it. "Thank you, Kingdom Lord. If I had to search for this item myself, I truly do not know how long it would have taken."

"It is nothing. You helped me expel the Jin family's forces, saving me a great deal of time," the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord waved his hand dismissively.

He then asked, "However, Li Qi, now that your injuries are healed, you likely won't stay in Clear Mist City for long. Have you decided where you will go next?"

"This junior will likely continue heading southwest, though I do not have a specific destination. It is a journey of experience, after all, so I haven't set a concrete goal," Li Qi replied.

This wasn't a lie. He truly had no specific target; as long as he moved southwest, he would just follow his instincts.

"In that case, I can point you toward an opportunity. I wonder if you would trust my word?" the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord smiled.

"How could I not trust you? The Kingdom Lord has treated me with profound kindness. If I cannot even trust the Kingdom Lord, who else could I trust?" Li Qi answered quickly.

"Then hear me out. If you travel about forty thousand miles to the southwest, you will find a demon nest."

"The demon race there is not peaceful like the demons in this region. The demons gathered in that nest are incredibly bloodthirsty. Almost all the surrounding kingdoms have relocated, setting up heavy defenses along their borders. Massive battles break out there frequently."

"Within that demon nest resides a Rank 5 demon known as the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord. Under his command are six Rank 6 demons, six sworn brothers. They are mutated toads who became demons by absorbing the filthy qi of the heavens, granting them the divine abilities of the Six Afflictions."

Li Qi nodded, listening intently to the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord's explanation. The place being described likely held immense benefits for him.

Although the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord didn't elaborate further, Li Qi understood exactly what he was referring to.

As the ancient sayings went, Heaven has six qi: Yin qi, Yang qi, wind qi, rain qi, dark qi, and bright qi. 

The six qi descend to become the five flavors, manifest as the five colors, resonate as the five sounds, and when in excess, give rise to the six afflictions.

Excess Yin qi causes cold afflictions, excess Yang qi causes heat afflictions, excess wind qi causes extremity afflictions, excess rain qi causes abdominal afflictions, excess dark qi causes delusion afflictions, and excess bright qi causes heart afflictions. When the six afflictions go too far, they become outright disasters.

These six Rank 6 demon kings likely based the very foundation of their cultivation on the negative filth born from the over-accumulation of the heavenly qi.

He just didn't know the foundation of the Rank 5 Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord, though Li Qi figured the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord probably didn't know either.

Why would anyone casually reveal the foundation of their cultivation? That was a matter of life and death.

If someone grasped your roots, they could easily set up formations perfectly tailored to counter you.

Wasn't that exactly how Li Qi had defeated Jin Buhuan? During their spar, Li Qi hadn't exposed a single thing, whereas Jin Buhuan's foundation had been completely laid bare.

Perhaps Jin Buhuan thought he had hidden it well, but Li Qi's observational skills far surpassed Jin Buhuan's ability to conceal himself. This directly led to a situation in their duel where, even when Jin Buhuan risked his life to drive the two streams of metal qi, he was still hopelessly countered by Li Qi.

Of course, this also depended on one's level of knowledge. One couldn't generalize. Even if your foundation was exposed, your opponent might still have absolutely no idea how to counter it, right?

After all, knowledge was precious. Not everyone was like Li Qi, with an encyclopedia he could memorize at will, and who would likely have access to an entire library once he returned to his sect.

A rural, wild shaman like Elder Si had been completely ignorant of knowledge that Li Qi considered foundational.

"Is there something special about this demon nest?" Li Qi asked curiously after hearing the introduction.

The Green Mountain Kingdom Lord replied, "The nest itself isn't particularly special. But because no one in the surrounding area is strong enough to suppress them, that region lacks the order you've seen along your journey. Over there, demons run rampant, and righteous heroes frequently venture there to exterminate them. It is a land of endless conflict."

Li Qi nodded, still unsure why the man was recommending this specific place to him.

"However, I recently received news. The Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord will soon travel to the outer realms for a serendipitous encounter, leaving only those six toads behind to guard the nest. Consequently, a gap in their defenses will appear."

"That alone wouldn't mean much, but it just so happens that the Righteous Sword Palace is preparing to mobilize its inner elders to intercept and slay one of those Rank 6 toads, both to set a righteous example and to exact revenge."

"With all this back-and-forth, the Righteous Sword Palace might not succeed in killing the toad, but the ensuing chaos will inevitably create a massive vulnerability in the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest. This is useless to me, but to you, Li Qi, it is highly valuable. You can use the opportunity to slip inside. Hidden within Bright Lake is an incubating treasure of the primal spirit, which might aid you in condensing your internal deities."

Li Qi finally understood the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord's intentions. Realization dawned on him.

The man was pointing him toward a fortuitous encounter.

To the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord, this treasure was likely garbage he wouldn't even bend down to pick up, but to Li Qi, it was incredibly precious.

He wasn't handing him the world, simply pointing out the direction. It was a very thoughtful gesture.

"Thank you for the information, Kingdom Lord. Might I ask what the Righteous Sword Palace and Bright Lake are?" Li Qi asked further, having truly never heard of them. They were likely factions and locations near the demon nest.

"Bright Lake is a lake within the demon nest, occupied by water demons. However, I imagine they won't pose a problem for you," the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord explained. "As for the Righteous Sword Palace, it is a cultivation sect founded upon the Martial Dao. Their sect rules are upright, and their members are mostly chivalrous heroes. The Palace Master has achieved Rank 6 in the Dao, which is quite respectable. Previously, the Righteous Sword Palace meddled in the affairs of the demon nest, and a demon king stepped in to intercept and kill one of their elders. The Righteous Sword Palace was already displeased with the nest's existence, and now they are openly hostile, frequently allying with neighboring kingdoms to launch rescue and extermination missions."

"This time, the Righteous Sword Palace also received word that the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord is heading to the outer realms. They plan to mobilize their entire force to sever one of his limbs, so to speak. This is precisely your opportunity."

"There is no benefit in me saying any more about the specifics. It would be better for you to go and see for yourself. You will naturally discover many things. With your skills, I assume it won't be difficult?" The Green Mountain Kingdom Lord winked at Li Qi.

It was obvious that the Kingdom Lord firmly believed in Li Qi's ability to see the big picture from small details. After all, within less than two days of arriving in Clear Mist City, Li Qi had seen through the local political landscape with a single glance and successfully exploited it. He had analyzed the relationships between dozens of factions and lobbied them individually. Although he had borrowed the momentum of the Clear Mist Sect, he had single-handedly united a force strong enough to suppress Jin Buhuan's remaining influence.

For Li Qi, a simple hint was enough; gathering intelligence once he arrived there would be a breeze.

"I understand. Thank you for the guidance, Kingdom Lord. I was just agonizing over how to condense my primal spirit. I will certainly not forget the grace of your guidance." Li Qi immediately stood up, offering his solemn thanks and making a point to emphasize that he would not forget this favor.

Li Qi was well aware that his current self amounted to nothing. The only reason the Kingdom Lord treated him so amicably was because he saw Li Qi's potential. Therefore, promising to remember this kindness was the best possible response.

"Haha, consider it a reminder from an elder. Since our business is concluded, I will take my leave. You still need to collect some cultivation methods, so feel free to look around. The Clear Mist Sect doesn't have many profound Demon Dao arts, but they should be sufficient for a beginner." Having finished his tea, the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord smiled and stood up, preparing to depart.

Li Qi, who was already standing, watched as the man took a single step and vanished into thin air. He then turned to Hu Xian'er and Hu Mei'er, offering a bow. "Since the main business is concluded, Seniors, regarding the cultivation methods..."

"Please follow me, sir. I will take you to the scripture pavilion right away," Hu Mei'er replied immediately, gesturing for Li Qi to follow her.

Hu Xian'er watched the two of them leave.

Afterward, she let out a soft sigh and waved her hand. The tea set from earlier automatically flew up, washing itself and resetting its position.

Then, speaking softly into the empty air, Hu Xian'er asked, "Master, why go to such lengths for a mere Rank 9 child?"
