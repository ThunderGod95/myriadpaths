# Chapter 135: Clear Mist City

The Green Mountain Kingdom was located a hundred thousand miles li from the Great Deer Kingdom. It was the latest nation Li Qi had arrived at, and it was exactly here that he successfully advanced and entered the Ranks.

Along the journey, he had concealed his identity as a Young Master of Shaman God Mountain. Li Qi had learned this lesson well in the Earth Oil World.

Status stemmed from oneself. Without personal strength to back it up, any number of titles were nothing but hollow words.

This place was a top-tier copper-producing region. The so-called Green Mountain Kingdom was, in truth, an enormous copper vein stretching for over two thousand li. The ore was so abundant that it burst from the earth, lying exposed in the open air. It required no mining; one merely had to stoop and pick it up.

The reason it was called Green Mountain was not due to verdant hills, clear waters, or leafy trees. It was because the sheer volume of exposed copper bred thick layers of verdigris whenever the rain and dew fell.

The entire mountain range was covered in this copper rust. Washed by the rain, it created a literal landscape of "green mountains and green waters." As a result, scarcely a single blade of grass grew anywhere near Green Mountain.

Metal qi governed death and slaughter. Where metal qi concentrated, all life withered into a barren wasteland. Only a rare few demons or spiritual creatures that thrived on the metal could exist there.

However, a high concentration of metal qi was not necessarily a bad thing. The exceedingly sharp metal qi gave birth to numerous metal-attribute heavenly materials and earthly treasures deep within the copper mines. Occasionally, innately formed divine weapons would resonate from within, luring wave after wave of people into Green Mountain to hunt for treasure.

Two thousand li west of the Green Mountains stood a city named Clear Mist City.

And it was in Clear Mist City that Li Qi now found himself.

"How luxurious. It truly lives up to its reputation as a den of indulgence," Li Qi marveled as he walked down the street.

Through his eyes, he could see that almost every building in this city was covered in colored glaze.

The glaze embellished the walls, dazzling like stars and reflecting the ambient light. Under a scorching sun, one could see the sparkling gleam. Even without the sun, the surrounding candlelight and luminous pearls illuminated the entire city as brightly as day.

This was a city that never slept.

He had heard of it early on his journey. The great name of Clear Mist City had spread across a radius of fifty thousand li.

The City Lord of Clear Mist City, Hu Xian'er, was charming and alluring. Her true form was a Rank 7 demon fox, and it was she who had founded this city.

The story of the city's founding was quite legendary and widely circulated. Almost everyone who came to Clear Mist City knew of its origins.

In the beginning, this place was nothing more than a fox den operating purely in the flesh trade[^1]. Because Hu Xian'er and her younger sister had taken in several fox demons, the foxes acknowledged her as their matriarch.

The foxes, however, had little concept of financial management and loved to indulge themselves. Although they had ways to make money, their extravagant spending eventually outpaced their income. Falling into debt, their creditors came knocking at their door.

After a round of negotiations, it was unknown exactly how silver-tongued Hu Xian'er had been, but she actually managed to persuade the creditors to accept payment in the flesh from the vixen spirits.

A month later, the vixens began to ponder. If money came this quickly and effortlessly, why continue their old ways—gaining nothing and giving their bodies away to penniless scholars? Furthermore, Hu Xian'er realized this business made it easier to manage the inherently promiscuous fox demons, solved their financial woes, and even allowed them to cultivate along the way. Was it not the best of both worlds?

Hu Xian'er acted on her words. The vixens immediately built a settlement right then and there, establishing the foundations for what would become the Clear Mist City.

At first, it was merely a small district named Fox Ward, and its only customers were local demons. However, the location was excellent, situated right on the periphery of Green Mountain.

The cultivators emerging from Green Mountain had all lined their pockets with immense wealth, yet they suffered heavy internal injuries from the overflowing metal qi.

By some unknown stroke of genius, Hu Xian'er devised a dual cultivation method that used the fire of lust to conquer metal. By drawing upon the fire qi born of carnal desire, the technique secretly aligned with the principle of fire subduing metal. After a bout of bone-melting pleasure, the imbalanced metal qi accumulated on Green Mountain would be pacified. It healed injuries, offered relaxation, and had practically no side effects.

In an instant, Fox Ward exploded in popularity.

A constant stream of customers poured in. Their brothel business boomed, and they even hung up the shingle of a medical clinic, specializing in treating injuries caused by overflowing metal qi.

Hu Xian'er was quick-witted. She immediately expanded the business, forging ties with the ruler of the Green Mountain Kingdom and turning Fox Ward into a state-sponsored enterprise. With a powerful backer and ample resources, other luxurious and extravagant trades began to proliferate. Eventually, they built a proper city, and the Green Mountain Kingdom bestowed upon her an official seal, making her the Lord of a city.

Today, this place had become the largest den of indulgence within a fifty-thousand-li radius. The variety of entertainment options was too numerous to count.

For the worldly, there were traditional wrestling matches, duels, arena fights, gambling dens, fine food, exquisite wine, and beautiful women.

For the refined, there was music, clear tea, and even various academic institutions where people could preach and demonstrate the Dao.

Whether one was coarse or cultured, they could always find what they desired here. No matter what Daoist Tradition one followed, their needs could be met. Even cultivation chambers and auction houses were readily available.

This place had become a massive, comprehensive commercial trading hub. Dining, drinking, and entertainment were all accounted for. Merchants large and small brought rare treasures from the four seas. Whether for pleasure or bulk goods trading, everything passed through here. Even the copper ore and various metallic spiritual items excavated from Green Mountain were primarily brought to Clear Mist City for trade.

City Lord Hu Xian'er and her younger sister, Hu Mei'er, had even established a sect named the Clear Mist Sect.

Ninety-nine percent of the sect's official disciples were female. Only rarely did a man become an official disciple. In truth, the sect rules were relaxed, emphasizing fate and free will, with no restrictions on gender or race. Consequently, men frequently joined. However, very few successfully entered the Ranks. Most ended up with their lifeblood drained dry, failing the assessments and being expelled from the sect.

At the time of entry, male applicants outnumbered females. Yet, after several rounds of elimination, women made up ninety-nine percent of the roster.

Male disciples who could not control themselves simply could not withstand those vixen spirits.

Despite this, once the Clear Mist Sect's training concluded and one became an official disciple, they were guaranteed a position within Clear Mist City. Thus, the sect flourished immensely.

In a mere six hundred years, Fox Ward had transformed from a petty fox den into a prestigious major sect within the Green Mountain Kingdom. Sect Master Hu Xian'er and her sister had even achieved Rank 7. With the Green Mountain King backing them, outsiders dared not suppress them, allowing the sect to expand rapidly to this day.

Now, Clear Mist City was divided into four main districts: East, South, West, and North. Each district contained thirty-six wards, with countless smaller establishments beneath them. The headquarters was located in the suburbs, at the original site of Fox Ward.

"These things are quite luxurious," Shen Shuibi remarked, following behind Li Qi and looking around the bustling city.

In her eyes, the decorations here weren't worth much, but they certainly looked impressive.

"Moreover, there are so many cultivators." Li Qi observed his surroundings, noticing that the average strength of the people here was a cut above the rest.

Clues could already be seen here. Unlike the cities in the Great Deer Kingdom, which were predominantly populated by mortals, this place was no longer the front lines.

Back on the front lines, the conflict over the Dao meant the laws of the Human Dao did not prosper, making it difficult to enter the Ranks. The Shaman Dao also struggled to progress because the gods of the mountains and rivers were suppressed.

But this area could be considered the directly administered territory of Shaman God Mountain, so the number of cultivators surged.

Cultivators could be seen everywhere, filling the streets.

Sure enough, the closer one got to Shaman God Mountain, the more profound the foundation became. Conversely, the closer one was to the borders of two major powers, the weaker it was.

However, entering the Ranks remained a massive chasm to cross. Rank 9 cultivators made up roughly one in a thousand people, so Li Qi could still be considered a rare expert.

Yet, Li Qi could hardly spot any cultivators from the orthodox Shaman Dao or the Daoist Sects, which he was most familiar with. The vast majority he saw on the streets belonged to minor Daoist Traditions.

He occasionally ran into Shaman Dao cultivators, but the difference between them and Li Qi was like night and day. Most of them were practitioners like Ghost Shamans, vastly inferior to the orthodox Shaman Dao.

There were also martial artists, practitioners of the Reason Dao studying artifacts, and various members of the Clear Mist Sect.

Funny enough, if one were to classify the Clear Mist Sect's cultivation method, it surprisingly fell under the Medicine Dao.

_I guess that counts as... saving people?_

Trailing behind Li Qi, Shen Shuibi asked, "Li Qi, what are we doing here? Do you even have that much money?"

This was a gold-squandering den. Li Qi only had a dozen or so gold pieces on him. Under normal circumstances in other places, that would definitely be enough, perhaps even considered wealthy. But in a place like this, it amounted to nothing.

"I definitely don't have enough money. But after entering the Ranks, I had a premonition. I felt like I was supposed to come here," Li Qi replied innocently, throwing his hands up.

Shen Shuibi nodded in realization upon hearing this and didn't press further.

It was quite normal. Shamans were just like that, occasionally experiencing sudden whims.

Because shamans maintained excellent relationships with nature and the myriad gods, various figures would occasionally send them reminders, guiding them to avoid disasters or seek out fortuitous encounters.

This was simply one of the quirks of this Daoist path.

Of course, one could also ignore it. After all, it was merely a suggestion, not a manipulated compulsion to go.

But Li Qi figured he had nothing else going on anyway. He might as well come and see. What was the worst that could happen, right?

The two continued walking, scouting for lodgings along the street and comparing prices.

It was astonishing once they actually looked. The inns and taverns here charged over twenty times the rates found elsewhere—though, naturally, they were also far more luxurious.

The dozen or so gold pieces Li Qi carried truly wouldn't be enough to stay here for a month.

However, Li Qi wasn't worried about money.

Although a gold-squandering den drained money quickly, earning money here was bound to be just as fast.

While walking down the street, Li Qi turned to Shen Shuibi and asked, "By the way, Miss Shen, I just remembered something. I can understand using gold as currency right now since we are still in the mortal realm. But surely gold isn't used once you reach the higher Ranks, right? That seems unlikely."

"Of course not. We only use gold now. Later on... hmm, it should be qi and worlds, right?" Shen Shuibi mused, poking her cheek with a finger in thought.

"Qi... and worlds?" Li Qi was stunned. The answer was so grand that he couldn't quite process it.

"Yeah, you should be able to understand qi, right? No matter what type of cultivation it is, it ultimately boils down to different types of qi. Therefore, various heavenly materials and earthly treasures that harbor qi serve as currency," Shen Shuibi explained.

"I can understand that, but... worlds?" Li Qi found it hard to imagine.

"There are so many outer-realm worlds. Conquering them and refining their Heavenly Dao takes a lot of effort. Once cultivators reach that level, qi is no longer of much use to them. At that point, worlds are generally used for trade," Shen Shuibi said. "I actually own a dozen or so worlds. Even though I never really managed them, their output was handled by dedicated personnel from Mount Luofu..."

As she spoke, the mention of Mount Luofu made her trail off. She fell silent, saying no more.

Li Qi understood.

Mount Luofu had most likely fallen.

Even Lady Luofu, a mighty Rank 3 expert, had fled to Shaman God Mountain in exile. How could Mount Luofu have fared any better?

However, looking at the bigger picture from this small glimpse, Shen Shuibi possessed over a dozen worlds. It seemed all the resources produced by those worlds had belonged to her.

_She really is a wealthy woman._

_Too bad she's bankrupt now._

_But... it's not just bankruptcy. Not many of her friends or fellow disciples from Mount Luofu likely survived..._

_She bears a blood feud of sect annihilation against the Tang Empire._

Tactfully, Li Qi did not press the issue.

"Ah, let's stay here. What do you think, Miss Shen?" Seeing her demeanor, Li Qi immediately picked an inn at random without even looking, using it as an excuse to change the subject so Shen Shuibi wouldn't dwell on her memories for too long.

"I'm fine with anything, let's stay here," Shen Shuibi agreed without being picky.

Now that they had decided on their lodgings, Li Qi led Old Ma and entered the inn.

Upon entering, they found the interior exceedingly elegant. It lacked the complex, ostentatious decorations of the outside world, radiating a refined taste instead.

The decor was serene and distant, devoid of worldly clamor or the stench of extravagant wealth. It felt as though it belonged in a completely different aesthetic than everything else in the city.

Such interior design wasn't particularly strange. Although it clashed with the general vibe of the masses, this was Clear Mist City after all; it had every kind of establishment imaginable.

What Li Qi found most surprising was that the owner here was actually a fox.

A fox demon with fox ears and a tail dragging behind her was currently managing the business.

Having seemingly just finished dealing with the previous batch of customers, the proprietress walked over right as Li Qi fortunately timed his entrance. "Guest, are you here for lodging or a meal?"

"Lodging. I'll stay for two days first. Serve us some food, and take good care of my horse while you're at it," Li Qi said, handing over a gold cake. This was worth one gold piece, enough to stay here for three days.

The excess money would cover their meals, as well as Old Ma's feed and stable fees.

As Li Qi spoke, he sized up the beautiful fox demon.

The reason he considered her a fox demon rather than a seducing vixen spirit was because the other party was... far too plain and elegant.

With lips tinted like cinnabar, a figure like a jade branch, and a strangely serene fragrance, she possessed a touch of quiet elegance to complement her flawless features.

She was completely different from those flashy, cheap seductresses, prompting Li Qi to take a few extra glances.

"Sir, you've arrived at the perfect time. This is our very last room," the fox demon said with a smile, accepting the money. "Arriving specifically at this time, are you also here for the innate spiritual item, Sir?"

"Hmm? What thing?" Li Qi raised an eyebrow.

_What is she talking about? I haven't heard of anything._

"Oh, it's not so rare an occurrence. It happens once every decade or so. Green Mountain has nurtured another surge of innate metal qi that naturally formed into a weapon. It seems to be a saber, so quite a few martial artists have come to fight over it. I assumed you were here for the same reason, Sir." The fox demon smiled, seamlessly explaining the situation to Li Qi.
