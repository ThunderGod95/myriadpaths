# Chapter 116: Universe

Hearing this, Li Qi also wanted to hurry over and take a look, to study it a bit. What if he could gain something from it?

However, Shen Shuibi pulled him back.

"What is the use of you looking at this fingerprint? You cannot understand it right now anyway. By the time you can understand it, you won't lack this kind of thing, right?" Shen Shuibi found Li Qi's choice somewhat strange.

"You possess four wisps of divine qi bestowed by the Grand Invocator. This is genuine Grand Invocator fundamental essence. Later on, once you have the ability to study it, your teacher will definitely keep you by his side to learn slowly. Won't these things be easily within your reach in the future? Why be anxious now?"

After listening to Shen Shuibi's words, Li Qi could not help but sigh with emotion once again.

The governor of White Snake Prefecture and his subordinate officials were nothing more than owls fighting over a stinking, dead rat.

Meanwhile, Shen Shuibi and Li Qi were like aloof and noble phoenixes—resting only on parasol trees, eating only bamboo seeds, and drinking only from sweet springs. And as it happened, people were delivering exactly these things right to them.

No wonder the others were so terrified the moment they heard the name of Shaman God Mountain.

The hierarchy of this world truly was starkly defined.

But then again, in the world of cultivation, each rank was an entirely different heaven. They could barely even be considered the same species anymore.

Since Shen Shuibi had already stated that such a fingerprint was actually of little use and amounted to nothing for the two of them, Li Qi naturally stopped caring about it.

Shen Shuibi would not deceive him, after all.

Just like that, Li Qi simply sat where he was and resumed his reading.

_One still had to read. Studying was the only true way forward._

---

This reading session lasted for four whole days.

Four days later, the governor finally hurried back.

In fact, there were even some people who failed to return at all.

Li Qi secretly guessed in his heart—did a few of them die attacking each other while those owls fought over the dead rat?

Or did they simply fail to make it back in time?

He did not know, but it did not matter.

This was exactly like the scenes often seen in those novels, where a fortuitous encounter appears and everyone rushes in to fight and slaughter one another for it.

Unfortunately for them, Li Qi had no intention of going to such a place to court death.

Just as Shen Shuibi had said, in the eyes of these people, he was absolutely considered a favored son of heaven.

The fortuitous encounter these people viewed as a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity was, to Li Qi, nothing more than a dead rat on the ground. He could not even be bothered to spare it a glance.

The disparity in this world was even greater than he had imagined.

Back when he was a boat tracker, even a few petty thugs could bully him.

But now, the governor of an entire prefecture had to treat him with the utmost respect.

Yet he had basically done nothing. He was called an Invocator, but in reality, he had not even entered the ranks. The only difference was that he had a Grand Invocator for a teacher.

_A single step to the heavens..._

But then again, Li Qi surprisingly did not feel overly conceited about it.

This was because he had experienced transmigrating, enduring three years of bitter hardship, sharing everyday life and conversations with Shen Shuibi, and then witnessing the great battle a few days prior.

All of these experiences made Li Qi realize that the flattery he was currently receiving was nothing but an empty illusion.

The moment he first witnessed the qi of heaven and earth, and the qi of the city—that sheer grandeur and awe.

Then, there was the time he first fulfilled his duties as an Invocator, communicating with ghost-gods and achieving harmony between human and ghost.

And after that, witnessing Yang Ning in combat, with the surging dao rhythm and myriad forms of qi.

All of this allowed Li Qi to remain unfazed by the superficial things currently before him.

Ultimately, this wealth and status only truly belonged to him if he earned them with his own two hands.

Knowing himself clearly—this was perhaps what Shen Shuibi meant by, "Remaining calm in the face of change, and maintaining composure through both honor and disgrace."

Therefore, for these entire four days, Li Qi peacefully read and cultivated, taking solid steps and moving steadily forward.

His teacher had already given him a true spell art anyway, so he just needed to cultivate it slowly.

His path as an Invocator was far slower than Martial Dao cultivation, but it grew increasingly profound and solid.

After the governor returned, he did not strike camp. Instead, he continued to station his forces there, claiming he needed to wait for the others.

During this period, there were naturally banquets every day.

Li Qi did not refuse the banquets, but upon returning to his quarters, he steadily continued his cultivation and reading.

His behavior surprised the officials somewhat. They withdrew their contempt, no longer daring to treat him merely with the etiquette reserved for a spoiled young master, and instead began praising his steady composure.

As for how much truth there was to their praise, that was hard to say.

But Li Qi did not care.

He did not cultivate for the sake of their praise.

They waited like this for another seven days.

Seven days later, people gradually returned, safe and sound.

Others were picked up completely insane, their cultivation ruined.

Out of curiosity, Li Qi asked Shen Shuibi why their cultivation would be ruined.

Shen Shuibi did not care much, casually replying, "Because after carefully studying the Grand Diviner's dao rhythm, they discovered that the Grand Diviner's understanding of the Dao was different from their own. Under the weight of anxiety and fear, their Dao Heart probably collapsed."

"These kinds of people have no future. A path is one's own path. Even if it is the Dao of a Grand Diviner, it is only suitable for him alone. To become so anxious simply because one's own Dao differs from a mighty figure's... if they die, then so be it."

The rabbit felt no pity; in fact, she seemed to deeply despise such people.

On the contrary, she seemed to quite like his type.

A good thing.

With matters here concluded, the cultivators who had achieved their goals, along with the officials, all took their leave. Only Li Qi, Shen Shuibi, and the governor remained to travel together to the provincial capital of White Snake Prefecture.

Li Qi had actually wanted to decline, preferring to leave alone with Shen Shuibi.

However, the governor urged him to stay, saying, "Young Master, why the rush to leave? You have bestowed an opportunity upon us this time; how could we possibly dismiss you with mere banquets? Why not accompany me to the White Snake Prefecture's capital? When we arrive, I will naturally have a generous gift prepared for you."

Li Qi agreed.

Who wouldn't love a generous gift?

Thus, the carriage set off once again.

Less than two hours into the journey, the governor invited Li Qi for a private meeting.

Having no reason to refuse, Li Qi went over to the governor's side.

Inside the carriage, the long table had already been removed.

In its place was a sandalwood desk.

The two sat facing each other.

"Does Young Master know what kind of generous gift I have prepared for you?" the governor asked, pouring Li Qi a cup of tea.

"How could I possibly know? Governor, there is no need to keep me in suspense." Li Qi maintained perfect etiquette, receiving the tea with both hands.

"Then I shall not hide it. As a distinguished disciple of Shaman God Mountain, Young Master should be aware of the realms beyond our territory," the governor said, his gaze confident, as if Li Qi undoubtedly knew.

Did Li Qi know?

Nonsense, of course he knew. He had come from there.

But... what did the other party mean by this?

Li Qi frowned, remaining silent as he waited for the rest.

"Looking at Young Master's expression, you must be aware. I imagine that even for disciples of Shaman God Mountain, the qualification to enter the extra-territorial realms before entering the ranks is still quite rare, yes?" The governor smiled.

"Young Master gave me an opportunity, so I am returning the favor with another opportunity. I hold a handwritten letter from the Lord of the Great Deer Kingdom, which can recommend an outstanding talent to enter an extra-territorial realm. It will absolutely be of great benefit to you, Young Master."

"This extra-territorial place is called the Pacing Realm. It is a Rank 8 world, and its master is merely Rank 8, which should pose no problem for you. This world has long served as a resource realm for the Great Deer Kingdom and has already been tamed. I wouldn't dare boast about its resources, as you certainly lack nothing in that regard, but the different dao rhythms of an other world will absolutely be of immense help for you to enter the ranks."

The governor explained.

This conversation did not last very long. In truth, it was just the governor attempting to return the favor, allowing Li Qi to obtain an opportunity of his own.

Li Qi also understood that this was likely not just simple reciprocity, but also the governor's investment and show of goodwill towards him.

After all, the governor of White Snake Prefecture could distinguish between an owl clinging to a dead rat like himself, and a phoenix that had a high probability of taking flight in the future. Befriending him now carried no downsides.

Li Qi would not refuse such a generous gift either. After all, no matter how he looked at it, the other party had no reason to harm him.

If he really wanted to harm him, the governor was a Rank 6, on the exact same level as the Curse Devil King and the Bear Slaughter King.

If an existence like that truly wanted to harm him...

Then he would likely have no way out; his death would be certain.

Therefore, Li Qi gladly accepted it, but he still needed to go back and consult Shen Shuibi.

After this conversation ended, Li Qi returned and found Shen Shuibi.

"Miss Shen, what are the extra-territorial realms?" Li Qi asked Shen Shuibi.

"Ah? Didn't you come from beyond the territory?" Shen Shuibi asked, stunned.

_Wait, the fact that I am someone of the Outer Dao has already spread, hasn't it?_

Li Qi smacked his forehead. "But I have no idea what these extra-territorial realms are actually about... Miss Shen, please enlighten me."

"Hmm... let me think. Actually, there's nothing particularly special about it."

Shen Shuibi pondered for a moment before explaining it to Li Qi.

The so-called extra-territorial realms, as the name implied, were other worlds outside of this heaven and earth.

Here, the concept of the 'Universe' had to be introduced.

The four cardinal directions, up, and down were called 'Yu'; the past, present, and future were called 'Zhou'. 'Yu' represented all of space, while 'Zhou' represented all of time.

The universe was so vast that it was boundless and inexhaustible. Within it existed countless heavens and earths, each born adhering to different 'Daos', interacting through qi to manifest all living things.

And extra-territorial simply referred to the other places outside of their own world.

Shen Shuibi said, "According to the nine-rank classification system proposed by the Sages, different heavens and earths are also divided into nine ranks. The baseline is the strongest existence a given world can support. Any world capable of supporting the emergence of a Rank 9 is a Rank 9 world."

"Just as you have seen, our world can support multiple Rank 1s, and even existences surpassing Rank 1, such as Sages, True Immortals, and Shaman Gods. Therefore, our heaven and earth can be called 'All Under Heaven'."

"This name signifies that it is solely beneath the heavens. Up to now, countless extra-territorial realms have been explored, and temporarily, we are the only 'All Under Heaven'. However, I've heard the Lady mention that the True Immortals all say that while there are no traces within space, there are traces of other 'All Under Heavens' within the flow of time. Presumably, other such worlds do exist."

"The influence of 'All Under Heaven' can radiate outward to countless other worlds. You might even hear the same rumors and myths in many different worlds. It should be the same for you, right? You were able to read the moment you arrived, and you have a partial understanding of certain existences. The world you came from was definitely influenced by this 'All Under Heaven', and legends of it are passed down over there as well."

"Ahem, I digressed. Anyway, that's how it is. These extra-territorial realms are mostly like this, essentially existing as appendages to All Under Heaven. They even have a concept of 'Ascension', which involves using one's own power to break through the world's shackles and travel to a higher-ranked world. As for the final destination of this Ascension, that is naturally our 'All Under Heaven'."

"Correspondingly, our All Under Heaven can also travel beyond our territory. Sometimes it is to conquer certain natives, and other times it is to gather resources. After all, while All Under Heaven is vast and rich in materials, no one ever complains about having too much wealth..."

"This time, it should be an extra-territorial realm controlled by the Great Deer Kingdom itself, right? Since it is only a Rank 8 world, it means the strongest entity the world can support is only Rank 8. There shouldn't be any danger to speak of. Go ahead. Taking a trip beyond our territory will indeed be of great benefit for you to enter the ranks," Shen Shuibi finally summarized.

Thus, Li Qi set his mind at ease and went back to his cultivation.

He didn't need to travel on foot himself anyway. He had plenty of time on the journey to White Snake Prefecture, so it was better to seize the moment to cultivate and read, striving to enter the ranks as soon as possible.

---

During these days of Li Qi's cultivation.

Shen Shuibi was not idle either.

Her accumulated foundations were sufficient; she was preparing to enter the ranks.

Ever since she had crippled her own cultivation base a few months ago, the rabbit had already started preparing for her re-cultivation.

Following the incident with Yang Ning, and after those two demons had died and Li Qi was taken away by the Grand Invocator, she had gone to Yang Ning to ask for some other resources.

Finally, as of today, all of these preparations were fully complete.

"Mhm, this way I'll return to Rank 9, and I'll have a bit of ability to protect myself." Shen Shuibi hummed a tune as she busied herself beside the alchemy furnace.

Kneading the medicinal cakes, filtering the liquids, preparing the rituals, and then, igniting the alchemy furnace.

Alchemy was an important aspect of cultivation for the Daoist Sects. In fact, there was even a specific cultivation method within the Daoist Sects that was known as the 'Dao of the Golden Core'.

This 'core' did not merely refer to the pills one consumed; it also referred to the cultivator themselves.[^1]

As the saying went, in the Dao of the Golden Core, refining oneself came first.

The heavens had the North Star, the center of the sky, eternally unmoving while all the stars revolved around it. Every complete rotation was called one 'heavenly cycle'.

Mount Luofu used the North Star as a metaphor for the Golden Core, emphasizing the perfection of self-refinement. By remaining unmoved by worldly temptations, an unmoving heart meant self-refinement was complete. One would temper themselves to become the 'North Star' of all things in the universe.

This was the focal point of Shen Shuibi's cultivation, and also the core of Mount Luofu's cultivation method:

She herself was the North Star, she was the center of the universe, and all myriad things revolved around her.

In this kind of cultivation, one's state of mind was of primary importance, followed by resources. This was why Shen Shuibi had been stuck for so long.

If, at the very end, she had not shattered her own Unsullied Frame, then given her state of mind—she was intrinsically a Rank 5—advancement would have happened naturally as soon as she made up for the alchemy resources.

However, after the Unsullied Frame was damaged, things were no longer so simple.

Although it was said that her Unsullied Frame had been restored, a shattered mirror was hard to make whole again. Having been damaged, it was no longer perfect. Therefore, she still had to slowly nourish her Unsullied Frame step by step on her own.

But she was not in a hurry at all.

The Lady was already safe, so there was no longer any lethal threat looming over her. What came next was merely a matter of patient, grinding effort.

To her, time was not valuable.

To enter the ranks in the Daoist Sects, one needed to forge a 'Golden Core'.

This was not a physical entity, but rather referred to a state of being for the cultivator.

The Golden Core did not mean there was literally a golden bead sitting inside one's body. If there was, it could only be called a gallstone.

The Golden Core meant that upon inner reflection, the primal spirit was flawlessly condensed and unified into a single whole, radiating a brilliant golden light. This was what was known as the 'Golden Core'.

In this state, the cultivated true qi would not leak, allowing one to resist external qi, prolong their lifespan, and cast certain divine abilities.

However, Shen Shuibi could not achieve this. Her physical shell and primal spirit were covered in cracks brought about by her injuries. Previously, her Unsullied Frame had shielded her, but now even that was cracked. Even though it had been forcefully restored by the Grand Diviner, it was far from being 'perfect' and was barely usable.

Under these circumstances, if she wanted to recover quickly, she could only take an alternative path.

And that was Outer Alchemy.

Which was exactly what she was currently refining inside the alchemy furnace.
