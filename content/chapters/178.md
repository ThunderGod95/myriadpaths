# Chapter 178: Descending to the Mortal Realm

Li Qi slapped one hand onto the ground. Earth qi from within his personal cosmos surged in, and mountain qi flew out.

The dark, howling sandstorm rushed toward their faces, on the verge of burying the sacrificial altar. Even Old Ma found the blowing sand somewhat uncomfortable.

A cluster of water qi condensed in Li Qi's hand, transforming into clear water. He then boiled it using fire qi and scattered the scalding broth.

He had used this move back in the Earth Oil World; it was a rain-praying ritual.

Next, he tapped the ground with his foot and released a resonant, impassioned trill from his throat.

Up in the sky, a great wind arrived in response.

Li Qi took another mouthful of water and sprayed it outward.

White qi rose from the water mist like smoke from a furnace, shooting up several feet before drilling into the celestial vault.

Holding water to command the heavens, clouds and rain instantly gathered with a resounding boom. Amidst the roaring and howling, thunder and lightning darkened the day. The gathered qi let out a long whistle, kicking up sand and dust.

Despite being in a desert, rain suddenly poured down in torrents.

The heavy rain fell, and before long, the sandstorm was completely extinguished. Merely dozens of seconds later, the torrential downpour stopped.

It didn't seem like rain at all, but rather as if someone had dumped a basin of water out of the sky.

The entire process lasted only a minute or two, barely managing to suppress the sandstorm.

Wang Boyan watched from the side, clicking his tongue in wonder. "Impressive. Rumor has it that shamans all possess the ability to manipulate celestial phenomena, and it is truly wondrous. But why didn't the rain last longer?"

Li Qi replied in annoyance, "The rain-praying method is fundamentally borrowing the water qi of heaven and earth. This is a vast desert; how much water qi could there possibly be? I only managed this brief rain by using earth qi to draw up subterranean water. Stop gawking. There is human qi in the distance. Go see what's going on."

A shaman's qi-observing ability could intuitively distinguish between various types of qi. Thus, while Wang Boyan remained oblivious, Li Qi had already spotted the human qi far away.

It seemed they were attracted by the sudden, anomalous thunderstorm, as a large number of people were gathering and heading in their direction.

As Li Qi urged Wang Boyan to investigate, he also found it strange. To think there were so many people in such a barren desert.

_Shouldn't deserts be entirely devoid of human life?_

However, Wang Boyan replied, "There's no need. I know what it is. It should be a tribe residing here."

"This minor world is divided into two factions, residing at the northern and southern extremes. One is akin to a civilized nation, ruled by a self-proclaimed King of Thousand Mountains. Their domain is similar to the various kingdoms of Baiyue. That is also the place I am after, as they practice agriculture, live peacefully in their respective villages, respect the king's rule, and submit to cultural edification."

"Within this desert, there is another tribe. Their people braid their hair, wear gold rings in their earlobes, and let the hair on the back of their heads grow long, tying it with colored silk. They revere the blade and the bow, and survive through nomadic herding and plundering."

"We can completely ignore the people here. Once you finish communing with the Heavenly Dao, we will head straight south."

Lighting the altar, Li Qi asked, "Herding in this barren desert? What could they possibly herd?"

Wang Boyan answered, "It's only barren desert right here. Just a few hundred miles to the south lie the grasslands. We are currently at the very edge of this world. I have already thoroughly scouted this place. You just focus on communicating with the Heavenly Dao. I will handle the local customs and practices."

Li Qi sighed. "Then have you ever considered why they would appear here, when it takes a journey of hundreds of miles to reach their grasslands?"

"Why bother thinking about that? It's merely an unranked minor world. If not for the protection of this Heavenly Dao, we could take whatever we wanted at our leisure. Even if ten thousand of them came, they would be no match for you and me. Let us hurry and begin the sacrifice," Wang Boyan urged.

But upon hearing this, Li Qi immediately stopped setting up the altar and looked at Wang Boyan.

He said, "Brother Wang, the people of this world are still human beings."

"To save the hundred thousand people of the Hundred Forests Kingdom, I would have to deliver millions, perhaps even tens of millions, of this world's people into your hands to suffer your oppression. How do you think I will choose? Do you really think I would act to save a hundred thousand, yet allow millions to suffer?"

Hearing Li Qi's words, a look of embarrassment crossed Wang Boyan's face. He immediately cupped his hands in apology. "Brother Li is absolutely right. I was wrong. I will go and investigate those people right now."

"No need, I will go see for myself," Li Qi said directly, no longer trusting Wang Boyan's words on the matter. He then mounted Old Ma and flew off into the distance.

That's right, he had Old Ma fly directly over. It was much faster that way.

The distance was a mere ten or twenty miles. Old Ma arrived in the blink of an eye.

Upon arriving, he saw a mortal army. Both men and horses wore leather armor and carried curved blades. Bows were slung across their backs, and their arrowheads were six or seven inches long, shaped like chisels. Everything was utterly mundane.

This entire army was composed of mortals; only a small handful of officers had cultivated some crude Martial Dao methods.

However, this mortal army was covered in wounds and utterly exhausted, looking very much like the battered remnants of a defeated force.

Within that mortal army, the leading General had lost an arm. The wound was bandaged, but blood still seeped through. Most of the other soldiers were also injured. Stranded in this desert, they looked like an army doomed to die.

At this moment, within that army.

"Rain in the great desert! Haha! The heavens have not forsaken me, Balahan! Move toward that direction!" The leading General laughed uproariously. "Boys! The rain is pouring in torrents over there, and thunder crashes! There is definitely a water source! We won't die of thirst!"

The injured soldiers also experienced a massive boost in morale. Summoning the last shreds of strength from their bodies, they trekked desperately through the sea of sand, limping toward the site of the rain.

However, the rain stopped after less than a few minutes. The formerly dense thunderclouds dissipated like an illusion, leaving the remnant army stunned in place.

An advisor put on a mournful face, his voice filled with sorrow. "I have heard that within the great desert, there is a strange phenomenon called a 'mirage.' It is a fleeting illusion, half-clear and half-obscured, vanishing in the blink of an eye as if waking from a dream. I fear we have just encountered such a phenomenon. How could it possibly rain in this vast desert?"

The General named Balahan also took on a sorrowful expression, even showing a trace of cowardly despair.

But he quickly gathered himself. Waving his curved blade with his remaining hand, the weapon flashed like a full moon, producing a loud thunderclap that echoed across the desert and startled the dispirited soldiers.

"A mirage is nothing but light and shadow! But earlier, we clearly heard the sound of thunder and water! It absolutely could not be fake! It is likely just because water is scarce in the desert, so the rain was brief, but there are definitely puddles left on the ground! Keep moving! The heavens always leave a path for survival! There must be a way out ahead!" he roared loudly, then took the lead, rushing toward where the rain had fallen.

Was there truly a way out ahead?

He didn't know.

He spoke with such absolute certainty that it wasn't a mirage, but did he actually know that?

Of course not. How could he possibly know? He had never even heard of a mirage before; it was all total nonsense.

However, he had to give his soldiers false hope, or else they would never march on.

To march was to retain a sliver of hope for survival.

To stay meant certain death, their bones bleaching in the desert sand.

But after they had taken only a few steps, the soldiers suddenly threw their heads back and cried out in panic, "There's a flying horse!"

Balahan also looked up, terror gripping his heart.

There really was a flying horse!

The horse was pure black from head to toe, standing perhaps eight feet tall at the shoulder. Certain parts of its body were covered in azure scales. It trod upon auspicious clouds, striding proudly with outstanding majesty. Its flight produced a rumbling commotion, accompanied by twisting snakes of dark-azure lightning. Amidst the explosive claps of thunder, the surviving horses of the remnant army all dropped to their knees, trembling uncontrollably and not daring to raise their heads, as if subjected to absolute suppression by a superior existence.

"Is it an Immortal!?"

"Has a Heavenly God descended to the mortal realm!?"

"Look! Quickly, look!"

The dragon colt's appearance triggered waves of commotion.

Balahan stared at the sky, his heart filled with doubt and astonishment.

_Are there truly immortals... in this world?_

Leaving aside the reactions of the remnant army, Li Qi, flying high in the sky, had his attention focused far into the distance.

From above, Li Qi spotted traces of spell arts trailing behind these defeated soldiers.

_Does this world also have rudimentary cultivators?_

They all appeared to be unranked methods, though none of these people had noticed them.

Even that General only practiced a very ordinary Martial Dao cultivation method. A punch from him would generate at most eight or nine hundred pounds of force—no different from when Li Qi worked as a boat tracker.

It was a garbage cultivation method, inferior even to the Wave-Shattering Force. If placed back in All Under Heaven, no one would even spare it a glance.

Practicing the Wave-Shattering Force for three years could yield seven or eight hundred pounds of strength. Yet, judging by that General, he possessed at least twenty years of accumulated effort.

Having confirmed the situation, Li Qi turned around to leave.

If they were refugees, he would have definitely lent a hand.

However, an organized army ending up in such a state was the result of a clash between two military forces. Seeing the baleful qi condensed around them, Li Qi knew they were unlikely to be some righteous army. Victory and defeat had already been decided on the battlefield; unauthorized interference would be unjust.

So, this remnant army would just have to rely on their own luck.

Li Qi turned back and left, leaving the army below to lament.

Just before he departed, however, he heard the General shouting at his soldiers below. "Hahaha! I, Balahan, have roamed unchallenged my entire life! My only regret is suffering a single bitter defeat! Yet, for an immortal to guide me at this very moment—it must be the will of the heavens! Boys, march along the path the immortal pointed out! There is definitely a way to survive ahead! That shall be the place where Balahan rises again!"

Hearing this, Li Qi glanced back.

_This man truly has some nerve._

Scanning his surroundings from the sky, Li Qi noticed a patch of water qi in the distance. It was likely an oasis.

He flew toward the oasis, disappearing from sight.

The heavens help those who help themselves.

_Perhaps my appearance here is also the will of heaven._

Then, he changed direction and returned to Wang Boyan's side.

However, the moment Li Qi touched down, both he and Wang Boyan immediately sensed that something was amiss.

"Brother Li, what... what did you do?" Wang Boyan asked in astonishment.

Li Qi examined himself. "I didn't do anything special, but what is going on?"

Because both of them could feel that the suppression of heaven and earth acting upon Li Qi had vanished.

Previously, his strength had been suppressed to eighty percent, but now, there was absolutely no suppression at all. It was as if this world's Heavenly Dao had fully accepted him.

Li Qi frowned, speaking with confusion. "I just... slightly helped those remnant soldiers over there... Wait."

"A Child of Destiny!"

"A Child of Destiny!"

The two men cried out in unison, both turning to look in the direction of the remnant army.

Wang Boyan was overjoyed. "Good deeds truly do bring good karma! A problem I tried every possible method to solve, yet failed, has been effortlessly handled the moment you arrived, Brother Li! Now the Heaven-Sacrificing Rite is guaranteed to succeed! Our plan to plant grain here will definitely go through! This world has a population of tens of millions; they can easily scrape together a mere eighteen thousand tonnes of grain! Haha, I never expected things to go so smoothly!"

By saving a Child of Destiny, Li Qi was no longer rejected by the Heavenly Dao of this minor world. Thus, communing with it would certainly become much smoother. This venture might actually succeed!

Li Qi shook his head. "Don't celebrate just yet. The Heavenly Dao is ruthless. It holds no likes or dislikes; it only makes the best possible choice for the world under the current circumstances. If it deems your grain-planting unacceptable, we will still have to fight it out."

"Refining a Heavenly Dao is out of the question—neither of us has the capability for that. We would at least have to beat it until it didn't dare stop us."

"True enough. Please, act as you see fit, Brother Li. I will absolutely follow your every instruction!" Wang Boyan immediately said.

It had been less than fifteen minutes since Li Qi arrived, yet he had already solved the problem that had troubled Wang Boyan for years. Wang Boyan realized that he needed to follow Li Qi's lead on how to act in this minor world.

Listening to Li Qi was definitely the right move. He was an expert at this sort of thing.

When operating in a minor world, it was better to conform to the will of heaven.

"As expected of a true shaman..." Wang Boyan sighed in admiration.

"Huh?" Li Qi, who was tinkering with the altar, suddenly looked up. What was that supposed to mean?

_Are there fake shamans in this world?_

"Nothing, nothing! Keep busy with your work. If you need anything, just tell me! I have plenty of bizarre items in my mustard seed pouch," Wang Boyan waved his hands repeatedly, hastily changing the subject.

Li Qi lowered his head and continued working.

Thirty minutes later, the sacrificial rite was completed.

Spiritual qi rapidly gathered around them, and auspicious shadows clearly manifested. Illusions briefly surfaced, only to vanish in the blink of an eye.

Standing in place, Li Qi let out a long breath.

"How did it go? Have we reached an agreement?" Wang Boyan rushed over, deeply concerned about the outcome.

"I wouldn't call it an agreement, but I sensed a vague intuition from the unseen world. This world faces a grave, existential threat. The Child of Destiny we just helped was driven out of the Central Plains."

"It seems someone from the Outer Dao, similar to you and me, came to this world. Using their unique power, they wish to become the ruler of this world. And unlike our direct descent, it appears they arrived as a remnant soul and possessed an inhabitant here. Because of this, the Heavenly Dao cannot target him like it targeted you. After all, the Heavenly Dao lacks sapience and can only act according to its own laws."

The Heavenly Dao, much like many natural gods, possessed spirit but lacked sapience. It could react to stimuli, but everything was instinctual—as natural as a baby crying—lacking the will or ability to take proactive action.

"To stop that person, this Heavenly Dao chose a Child of Destiny to oppose him, but the Child of Destiny was defeated. That person from the Outer Dao is on the verge of conquering this world and becoming its de facto ruler."

"If we help resolve this matter, we might just earn the approval of the Heavenly Dao here." Recalling his communion, Li Qi shared his findings with Wang Boyan.

Wang Boyan was ecstatic upon hearing this. Clapping his hands together, he said joyfully, "Then this will be easy! Hahaha, Brother Li, it truly looks like heaven is helping us! Now, both of our problems are solved! Let's go! You have a dragon colt, and this world is pitifully small. The Central Plains are only two or three thousand miles away; we can reach them in a single day. The two of us will strike and annihilate that man with a flick of our fingers! Then you'll have your grain, and this minor world can become the Golden Wave Sect's second base of operations!"

"Rest assured, I will definitely treat this place well! I will only use it for agriculture and mining, and I will absolutely not interfere with anything else!"

Wang Boyan was overjoyed, but Li Qi's expression was rather subtle.

It was obvious that Li Qi was already plotting something in his mind, but Wang Boyan remained seemingly oblivious.

However, after a moment's thought, Li Qi nodded. "Let's go. We'll check it out first."

**Translator's Note: Generating Rain**

Standard rain-praying rituals gather _water qi_. Because the desert air is completely dry, Li Qi improvises by slapping the ground and injecting _earth qi_ and _mountain qi_ from his personal cosmos. He uses this connection to forefully draw deep, subteranean water to the surface.

Once he gathers this subterranean moisture as _water qi_, he condenses it into phyical water. He then immediate;y applies _fire qi_ to boil it into scalding broth, preparing to be vaporized.

The physical actions he takes—tapping his foot on the earth and releasing a resonant, trilling chant—are classic Shamanic methods to commune with the "unseen world" and command natural phenomena, in this case summoning a massive updraft of wind.

Finally, he takes the water into his mouth and sprays it outward into the wind. This creates a pillar of steam ("white qi") that shoots directly into the sky. This acts as a form of rapid cloud seeding, instantly forming heavy thunderclouds that dump the drawn-up water back down all at once.

Because he is recycling a limited amount of underground water rather than utilizing a naturally humid atmosphere, the resulting downpour is violently heavy but lasts for only a minute or two.
