# Chapter 211: ,,,,

Vast Yang shook violently.

Everyone of capability in the entirety of Vast Yang cast their gazes toward this location.

Those Rank 8 experts who had stayed aloof and out of the fray all felt an intense, oppressive might.

The peak forces of the noble clans and powerful sects each deployed their methods to look this way.

In truth, people had already been looking over earlier, because the landscape grand array was not activated casually.

But what was even more terrifying than the activation of the landscape grand array was...

The entire landscape grand array had been snapped in half. A severe earthquake wracked all of Vast Yang. Thousands of mountains and rives dieties connected to the array died violently on the spot, and even more suffered grievous injuries.

Such a grandiose rite had been forcefully shattered by a single person's strength.

The Rat King's pupils shrank! Trembling all over, he directly reverted to his true form, turning into a ball of rat and hiding deep into the earth.

_Rank 5! Is that... Chanzhi's master?!_

_Fuck! Why would he come here!_

_How are we supposed to fight this?_

The Rat King hid, but no one paid him any attention.

After the landscape grand array shattered, that giant black hand slowly dissipated.

Then, abruptly, space distorted. It was as if someone was tearing and crumpling a sheet of transparent plastic, causing even the light to warp.

Within this distortion appeared a black-skinned cleric with a face full of righteousness.

He had black skin, a string of skull prayer beads hanging around his neck, and wore a tall hat. Yet, he possessed kind eyebrows and a benevolent gaze, faintly radiating Buddhist light.

Shielding Monk Chanzhi with one hand, his gaze gently swept the surroundings. "Who was it that nearly severed my disciple's Dao path?"

His words carried no oppressive force.

However, a sense of despair shrouded the area.

The faces of everyone in the White Rhino Army, including Lie Kedi, who had fought bitterly and suffered heavy injuries, were filled with despair.

A feeling of powerlessness flooded everyone.

Except for Shen Shuibi. She merely tilted her head.

But in the next second, a skinny old man wearing hemp clothes suddenly popped up in front of this cleric.

And then, with a backhand, he slapped the cleric across the face.

_Smack!_

A crisp sound rang out.

Everyone was stunned.

The soldiers of the White Rhino Army were collectively dumbfounded.

The Rat King of the Rat Soil Mound opened his mouth wide.

Even Wang Boyan halted his steps as he prepared to run away.

He had arrived long ago but hadn't shown himself, hiding with his men to observe the situation. Seeing a Rank 5 appear, he immediately turned to flee, but this slap made him stop in his tracks.

Wang Boyan's eyes nearly popped out of their sockets.

And Zhang Lan—who had rested for a brief moment after withdrawing from the battlefield before immediately plunging back in to fight alongside the White Rhino Army—had no reaction. He was still continuously swinging his sword, clearly having killed until his eyes were red, completely oblivious to the outside world.

There was also the exception of Shen Shuibi.

Only Shen Shuibi, who was outside the battlefield providing technical assistance for the White Rhino Army's numerous dharma treasures, was not surprised.

Because she knew the matter was over.

Sometimes, when the strong bullied the weak, they ended up dead.

That black-skinned demonic monk who had suddenly appeared was slapped right across the face, the sound incredibly crisp.

Even he was somewhat astounded, failing to react for a moment.

But he was a Rank 5, after all.

He immediately snapped back to his senses. However, he didn't strike back; instead, he stared intently at the other party. "Who might you be, sir?"

The skinny old man who had suddenly appeared merely looked at him with cold eyes. "Sacrifice thirty percent of your cultivation base to feed back into heaven and earth, and restore the water and soil of this region to normal."

"Once you've done that, you may leave."

The black-skinned cleric first looked grave, carefully sizing up the old man.

Then, he suddenly let out a sneer. "You are merely Rank 5 as well, sir. Your words are too arrogant."

Having been slapped earlier, he was worried this person was some formidable figure, so he hadn't dared to act rashly.

Looking closely now, he found that the other party was also Rank 5.

Since they were both Rank 5, and he himself was an orthodox inheritor of the Mahakala lineage, how dare this person be so insolent?

_I'm really being looked down upon._

The black-skinned cleric raised a hand. Suddenly, a dharma hall descended upon the area.

This was his dharma treasure.

This grand hall was resplendent with gold and jade, adorned with the seven precious treasures, and draped with silken banners and canopies. Statues stood in chariots, attended by two Bodhisattvas serving as heavenly attendants, all carved brilliantly from gold and silver, suspended in the void.

The structure as a whole was sixty miles long and thirty miles wide, majestically decorated with the four treasures. Its foundation was flat, blotting out the sky and covering the sun.

Seven tiers of precious bricks paved its steps. The dharma hall boasted eighty-four thousand pillars: gold pillars with silver brackets, silver pillars with gold brackets, and similarly with colored glaze and crystal.

Surrounding the hall were four balustrades, all crafted from the four treasures. The four staircases were likewise made of the four treasures. Upon the dharma hall stood eighty-four thousand precious pavilions. The golden pavilions had silver doors and windows, the silver pavilions had golden doors and windows, and the same applied to those of crystal and colored glaze.

Golden beds resided in silver pavilions, and silver beds in golden pavilions. Exquisitely soft fabrics woven from golden threads draped the seats. The hall's radiance was blinding, as bright as the midday sun, rendering everyone present unable to look directly at it.

As the grand hall descended, the people around immediately realized this was not a battle they could interfere in. Anyone capable of moving desperately retreated.

But no one paid them any heed.

The black-skinned cleric's dharma hall had eighty-four thousand doors, which opened simultaneously!

Countless entities walked out from within.

They saw, eighty-four thousand elephants, ridden by strongmen with bulging muscles, marching and shaking the earth.

Eighty-four thousand horses, precious steeds ridden by martial artists, brandishing their might.

Eighty-four thousand chariots with golden wheels, bearing golden-eyed archers who aimed their bows at the surroundings.

Eighty-four thousand divine pearls illuminating the area, dispelling all miscellaneous qi and defending against spell arts.

Eighty-four thousand jade maidens, virtuous and benevolent, attending at the flanks to heal wounds with miraculous hands.

Eighty-four thousand lay practitioners expounding on the Buddhist Dharma, proclaiming righteous fruits, and chanting the great name of the hall's master.

Then, the grand hall unfurled, its Buddhist light shining everywhere.

There were also four thousand nagas, four thousand garudas, four thousand evil paths, seven thousand great trees, and eight thousand great hells.

Later, numerous Yakshas appeared, wielding tridents as they surged forward like a tide.

From the void sprang a forest of ghosts and beasts. Countless venomous creatures—mosquitoes, horseflies, flies, lice, snakes, vipers, bees, maggots, and more—gathered into black clouds that blotted out the sky.

Then, the surrounding space was instantly sealed.

The black-skinned monk chanted sutras:

"Four gates face the four directions, lanes and paths perfectly aligned."

"Iron forms the prison walls, covered above by an iron net."

"Iron forms the ground below, from which flames naturally erupt."

"For those of grievous sins, they dwell here unmoving."

"Falling into the Great Wailing Hell, enduring suffering beyond measure."

Having finished his chant, he extended a hand in invitation to the skinny old man. "Benefactor, this poor monk has prepared a hell for you. It only awaits your thorough tasting."

The earth transformed into purgatory. The previous dharma hall hovered in the sky, suppressing everything. Those martial artists, strongmen, jade maidens, nagas, and garudas were Rank 8 at the very lowest, and there was no shortage of Rank 7s among them!

Just this army contained within a dharma treasure could easily flatten the entirety of the Hundred Forests Kingdom.

And this was merely a single dharma treasure.

As a Rank 5, he hadn't even personally made a move.

After all, they were currently in All Under Heaven. The Heavenly Dao of All Under Heaven did not permit Rank 5 experts to fight with their full might; otherwise, the affected range could not be measured in mere hundreds of miles.

It would truly ruin a kingdom, causing billions of casualties and turning the entire Hundred Forests Kingdom into a barren wasteland.

Almost everyone present was utterly intimidated.

Fighting Monk Chanzhi, a mere Rank 7, had already exhausted all their methods and required countless sacrifices.

Yet this black-skinned cleric before them had summoned thousands of Rank 7s with a light wave of his hand.

This disparity... made one tremble.

It was simply indescribable.

A short distance away, the old man glanced at the black-skinned cleric.

The cleric had confidently summoned that dharma hall and was now sitting atop it.

On the dharma hall rested a grand chariot draped with white curtains. Golden bells hung from the four sides of the curtains, alongside various fragrant flowers, precious canopies, and banners.

Exquisite sweet delicacies and all sorts of entertainment were present. The four legs of his seat were made of pure dark blue glaze.

Behind his seat were reclining chairs fashioned from the seven treasures. Trees of the seven treasures served as lamps, illuminated by various precious pearls, while delicate heavenly flowers covered the ground. Numerous heavenly maidens served and offered to him with pious hearts.

Sitting upright upon it, a hint of mockery playing at the corners of his mouth, he quietly awaited his opponent's reaction.

He was very confident.

This dharma hall had been personally bestowed by a Cakravartin[^1].

In the past, he had served the Cakravartin and received his grace. At that time, the King led eighty-four thousand followers to the Buddha to become sramanas[^2].

Those eighty-four thousand people all achieved Dao fruits, became Arhats, and proved their Rank 5 status.

As one of them, he was the only one from the Mahakala lineage.

Out of compassion, the Cakravartin bestowed a dharma treasure upon him to protect his Dao path.

Residing within the dharma hall eliminated greed, wrath, and ignorance. This was called: 'The tainted are not tainted, the evil are not evil, the ignorant are not ignorant.'

Later, when the Cakravartin fought against the Dragon King Upananda[^3], he joined the battle on behalf of the Sage King.

Midway through the battle, the Dragon King Upananda coiled his body around Mount Sumeru seven times, then uprooted the mountain and hurled it. He had been in the mountain at the time, and immediately brought out his dharma hall to fortify himself within.

Mount Sumeru was hurled past sixty-two thousand worlds. Being attached to it, he traveled through those sixty-two thousand worlds alongside it. Upon impact, the shockwaves shook a billion stars. Although the dharma hall was severely damaged, he suffered no injuries inside it, which spoke volumes of its quality.

Now that the dharma hall had descended, it was like a divine needle calming the seas, sufficient to suppress the vast majority of situations.

Although the others didn't know its origins, they could see the terrifying nature of this item.

Almost everyone looked fearfully at the old man who had delivered the slap, hoping he could withstand it.

Even though, in truth, no one knew him.

Of course, that was 'almost everyone'.

There was one exception.

Shen Shuibi was not afraid in the slightest.

She saw through the current situation with a single glance.

She even had the leisure to sit down and begin sensing the qi dissipating into the surroundings from the battle.

These were all refined and processed qi, very useful for repairing her body.

As for that black-skinned monk...

From the moment he summoned his dharma treasure but didn't attack, instead choosing to flaunt his might on the sidelines, he was already a dead man.

There was no need to look anymore.

On the other side, the old man quietly waited for the dharma hall to finish descending.

Very quietly and indifferently, he waited for the other party to finish preparing.

Then, suddenly.

The black-skinned cleric's head tilted to the side, and he quietly passed away in a seated posture.

The power of the dharma hall instantly diminished. The old man then reached out and caught it as it shrank to the size of an egg. The strongmen, jade maidens, archers, and martial artists within were all collected along with it.

The old man muttered, "All flash and no substance... Have you never been to Baiyue? Acting like a complete idiot."

He then stored the dharma hall away. "This dharma hall is rather decent, though. It can be considered top-tier among Rank 5 items. This trip wasn't made in vain."

The reason he said this was because the black-skinned cleric had truly underestimated his enemy.

This was Baiyue.

It was the peripheral territory of Shaman God Mountain. Even though it bordered other regions, it was effectively under the direct jurisdiction of the Shaman Dao.

What was the characteristic of the Shaman Dao?

Shamans were renowned for their deceit, their myriad methods, their infinite spell arts, and how difficult they were to defend against.

Was his dharma treasure so strong, having helped him evade so many risks in the past, that it had made him lose his mind?

Coming to Baiyue and acting so arrogantly towards a Diviner...

It was baffling.

A Diviner excelled at two things.

One was divination.

The second was cursing...

Did he really think he could withstand curses without putting up the slightest personal defense, relying entirely on that dharma treasure?

Did he think that slap earlier was just to vent anger?

_What a fool..._

That slap was an 'insult'.

Therefore, when rites and music are abandoned, perverse sounds rise—this is the root of danger, ruin, and insult.

As it is said: 'To be trapped where one should not be trapped, one's reputation will inevitably be insulted. To occupy what one should not occupy, one's body will inevitably be in danger. Being both insulted and in danger, the day of death approaches.'

The ancients also had a saying: "The method to kill a scholar is to insult him."

This meant that if one wished to kill a scholar, insulting him was a way to bring about his death.

Being both insulted and in danger, the day of death approaches.

This was the curse.

Silently and without a trace, falling short by a single move, the black-skinned cleric, a dignified Rank 5, died an inexplicable death.

The danger and eeriness of shamans were evident.

In all places within All Under Heaven, shamans were notoriously terrifying.

The moment a shaman appeared, they were always the primary target.

This was because shamans were generally not very skilled at head-on combat, but they were absolutely adept at killing you when you least expected it.

Whether using Gu insects or curses... Invocators excelled at travel, diplomacy, and communicating with all places, while Diviners excelled at analyzing, scheming, and shaping the future. All of them preferred killing people from the shadows.

Shaman God Mountain was absolutely the place with the most secrets in all of All Under Heaven, and shamans were the people who loved hiding things the most.

Even Li Qi was like this.

Without realizing it, Li Qi had also become the type who liked to secretly cause trouble behind the scenes and backstab others.

Whether in Vast Yang or Clear Mist City, he always operated behind the scenes, connecting different parties to ultimately achieve his goals.

This old man was much the same.

For the old man, if that black-skinned cleric had launched a full-power assault without saying a word the moment he appeared, it might have caused some trouble and made it difficult to parry.

But that was impossible.

This was All Under Heaven.

A Rank 5 absolutely could not attack with full force here, or they would face heavenly punishment.

With the power of a Rank 5, a battle between two of them would easily lay waste to a kingdom, with casualties easily numbering in the billions.

One must know that when Rank 5 experts fought Beyond the Boundary, the shockwaves alone could shatter several unlucky minor worlds. Even though All Under Heaven was much sturdier, it still couldn't withstand the full force of a Rank 5.

Thus, the old man was certain from the start that the other party wouldn't launch a fierce assault immediately.

And by striking unexpectedly, even if he were blocked, he had prepared four other contingency plans, including at least three different curses, ensuring that a curse would latch onto the opponent.

In other words, as long as the opponent didn't flee the moment he appeared or immediately suppress the shaman with full force...

...then the death of this black-skinned cleric was already set in stone.

Shen Shuibi understood this very well, which was why she hadn't wasted any time, choosing to simply sit down and wait.

As for the others...

They were just irrelevant personnel, nothing to worry about.

Shaman God Mountain had already dispatched a Rank 5; therefore, everything was over.

And that skinny old man was also well aware of this.

With a grab of his hand, the Buddhist Pagoda in Vast Yang City instantly shattered, and Li Qi's main body was directly pulled over.

Even his split soul was rapidly merged back in, landing right in the center of the battlefield.

At the same time, Bo Yue was pulled by the same force, flying over from thousands of miles away at incredible speed.

Although the speed was immense, it wasn't jarring at all. It seemed as if all acceleration had been negated by some force.

After Bo Yue and Li Qi stood in place, both looking utterly bedraggled, the skinny old man respectfully cupped his hands and bowed right in front of countless people.

Then, he said with utmost seriousness, "I am Le Ya. Greetings, Young Master Yue, Young Master Qi. I arrived half a step late; I hope you can forgive me."

[^1]:     
    In ancient Indian political and religious thought, a Cakravartin is the ideal universal ruler who governs the entire world (or the entire universe) with justice, wisdom, and benevolence rather than through force or violence.
    
    **Cakravartin** literally means "One whose wheels are moving." This suggests a ruler whose chariot wheels can roll anywhere in the world without obstruction, signifying undisputed sovereignty.
    
    In Buddhist tradition. a Cakravartin is considered the secular counterpart to a Buddha.
[^2]: **Sramanas**

    Quite difficult to explain without the cultural context, but a rough translation would be: "one who labors or exerts themeselves," usually in the context of spiritual practice.

    It was a religious movement in India, challenging the established Brahmanical order and the authority of the Vedas during the mid-1st millennium BCE.
[^3]: Another mythical figure in Hinduism.
