# Chapter 213: Preparations

Riding Old Ma, Li Qi raced toward the site of their previous battle.

At the center of the former battleground, the remaining uninjured soldiers were clearing the grounds.

In truth, there was little left to clear. 

Amidst a battle of such intensity, corpses rarely survived intact; even divine souls struggled to endure.

Li Qi was here now to help soothe the few remaining divine souls. Along the way, he had brought these soldiers a multitude of medicines and... spirit tablets.[^1]

They were all blank, requiring only names to be carved upon them before use.

Seeing Li Qi arrive with such items, the soldiers of the White Rhino Army did not know whether to laugh or cry. They were unsure if they should praise him for his thoughtfulness or curse him for his lack of tact.

However, no matter how one looked at it, at this very moment, there was indeed a dire shortage of such things. 

The omen was simply inauspicious, yet the reality remained that their supply of spirit tablets was vastly insufficient.

Soon, a quartermaster stepped forward to receive the goods, expressing his profound gratitude to Li Qi.

Shortly after bidding the quartermaster farewell, another high-ranking officer came to pay a visit.

It was evidently the current commander of the White Rhino Army, Lie Kedi, whom Li Qi had previously fished out of prison.

This General, visibly bearing severe injuries, walked up to Li Qi, dropped to one knee, and cupped his hands in greeting.

"Lie Kedi greets the Young Master... This must be our first meeting, yes? The Young Master's divine calculations not only saved my life but rescued the common people of an entire prefecture. Lie Kedi is eternally grateful. I merely wonder, what business brings the Young Master here today?"

Li Qi hastily helped him up. "General Lie, please do not speak like this. Our actions were all for the sake of the people; there is no distinction of high or low between us, much less any master-servant dynamic or debt of gratitude. Rather, it is I who am profoundly awed by the General's unyielding loyalty and righteousness. I entreat the General not to demean himself—treating me as an equal will suffice."

Seeing that Lie Kedi still refused to rise, Li Qi added, "I merely ask myself, have I not done the exact same thing as the General? According to military regulations, those who work together share the same rewards and the same punishments. By demeaning yourself, General, do you imply that we did not handle this matter well enough?"

With Li Qi phrasing it thus, Lie Kedi could no longer insist. He could only bow deeply once more. "The Young Master is of lofty righteousness. If not for you this time, Vast Yang would truly have been plunged into abyss of suffering. Please accept this bow; though it signifies little, it expresses the depths of my heart."

Left with no choice, Li Qi accepted the bow and then helped Lie Kedi to his feet.

He then invited Lie Kedi into a military tent and offered him a seat. "Very well, please sit, General. You asked earlier what brought me here. In truth, besides delivering supplies, I have a request."

"Please speak, Young Master. Are we to ride out to war once more?" Lie Kedi asked.

Other than waging war, he truly could not fathom what else a group of rough soldiers like them could do to assist the other party.

"That is not it. The White Rhino Army has already suffered disastrous casualties; how could I ask you to take the battlefield again?" Li Qi denied the notion, then asked, "Has the General seen the current situation in Vast Yang?"

"The situation?" Lie Kedi pondered the words for a moment. "Is the Young Master referring to the matter of the earth qi?"

"I have noticed it as well. Following the activation of the landscape grand array, there will inevitably be a period of diminished harvests. However, if we mobilize the many prominent clans and wealthy households of Vast Yang and compel them to open their granaries, we should be able to endure it."

Lie Kedi continued, elucidating the situation for Li Qi. "The Young Master has only newly arrived and may not be clear on the dynamics of Vast Yang. There is an organization here called the Grain Alliance, which controls the food trade. Numerous wealthy households and aristocrats hold shares within it. They generally stockpile enough provisions to last two to three years. If managed properly, a famine will not occur."

"I am currently considering dispatching troops back to the city. If necessary, martial law may be enforced. If the Young Master shares this intent, that is perfect. As long as this matter is executed cleanly, no one in Vast Yang should starve to death for the next two years."

Lie Kedi was naturally no fool. He possessed some understanding of the landscape grand array and knew that it would guarantee a reduction in crop yields. Thus, he was already laying out plans for the aftermath.

However, his thoughts were still too shallow. After all, he did not realize that the landscape grand array had not merely been used normally, resulting in an overdrawn earth's vitality.

Rather, it, alongside the mountain and water veins, had been entirely shattered by that dark-skinned cleric.

For the next ten years, across all of Vast Yang, the myriad trees would wither, the farmlands would yield nothing, the wild beasts would turn barren, and the rivers would run dry.

Relying solely on emergency rations would be absolutely impossible to endure.

If they waited for a natural recovery, the entire population of Vast Yang would likely be forced to flee from famine. Tens of millions of refugees would flee to the other prefectures of the Hundred Forests Kingdom.

A man far from home is as cheap as a dog. If a mass exodus truly began, a death toll of five or six out of ten would likely be a conservative estimate. 

Even if they managed to escape to other provinces, the majority would only be able to sell themselves into servitude. Otherwise, with no grain and no land, upon what would they rely to survive? Perhaps only those from wealthy households, abandoning their ancestral estates, might manage to settle as affluent residents in another prefecture.

Li Qi naturally understood that Lie Kedi was entirely ignorant of this reality. He let out a bitter smile. "I am afraid the matter is not as simple as the General believes."

"In truth, the earth qi of Vast Yang will not merely cause a diminished harvest. General, follow me." Li Qi stood up and whistled. Old Ma trotted over in response.

Lie Kedi also summoned his mount. It was a tall and sturdy steed; while not a dragon colt, it was still a rare and exceptional thousand-miles thoroughbred.

Dragon colts were a rare sight, and a dragon colt like Old Ma was even rarer. Once Old Ma truly stepped into Rank 9, he could no longer be called a dragon colt. At that time, he would undergo his transformation.

Naturally, he would not transform into a human form. He would take on the form of a dragon.

Typically, when demons transformed, they assumed the form of a race possessing innate spiritual wisdom. This allowed them to glean a portion of that wisdom, augmenting their own intellect. Generally speaking, demons would choose a human form, for transforming into a human was the simplest path, and humans were the most common. 

Yet there were other races possessing innate spiritual wisdom, such as the Golden-Winged Great Peng, the dragon, the phoenix, and even natural anomalies like the Jade Rabbit and Golden Crow. They were not nearly as common as humans, but in truth, they too were viable choices for transformation.

As a dragon colt, once Old Ma reached Rank 9 and initiated his transformation, he would undoubtedly lean toward the path of a dragon. 

The trend was already apparent. The azure scales emerging upon his body, as well as the auspicious clouds and thunderbolts that trailed him in flight, were all draconic characteristics.

Traveling alongside Lie Kedi, Old Ma refrained from flying, instead dutifully galloping across the ground. Riding their steeds, Li Qi led the way, swiftly traversing the plains.

Originally, this path wound through the mountains. Following the battle, however, the peaks had been essentially flattened. The ground was littered with shattered glass-like fragments and strewn with massive boulders. These were newly formed rocks, birthed from cooling magma.

About an hour later, after the two horses had galloped for over a hundred miles, mountains untouched by the devastation finally came into view. 

Upon arriving, Li Qi vaulted from his saddle, landing gracefully upon the earth. Lie Kedi followed suit, bewildered, unable to fathom why Li Qi had brought him here.

"Observe, General." Li Qi stepped forward and applied a slight pressure with his foot. The ground ruptured, blowing out a crater roughly a meter deep.

Lie Kedi walked over to inspect it. His pupils contracted, and his expression instantly stiffened.

Normally, a meter beneath the surface, the soil should be profoundly moist and dense. Beneath the earth, water qi naturally swirled and gathered. Plants drove their roots downward to absorb this water qi to sustain their own growth. When water qi and earth qi merged into one, they formed a rich, moist, and loamy black soil. This earth was abundant in nutrients, teeming with various forms of qi, and capable of nurturing robust plant life.

But if the water qi was lost, and the earth qi existed in isolation, the soil would take on a withered, yellowed hue—the raw color of pure earth qi. This withered yellow earth signified the absence of water veins below or in the vicinity. Without the blending of water qi and earth qi to create fertile soil, the land became unfit for cultivation.

Lie Kedi frequently encountered such wastelands—barren tracts harboring only earth qi and lacking water qi. This was entirely normal.

Yet, this was his very first time witnessing pallid, whitened soil.

The soil here looked almost like an alkaline wasteland. It was a full meter deep, where the soil ought to be damp and heavy, yet it presented a deathly, chalky white appearance. 

Not only was there no water qi, but even the earth qi had dried up completely.

Bending his knees, Lie Kedi reached into the crater and scooped out a handful of soil. He gave it a gentle rub between his fingers, and the clump immediately crumbled away.

It was less soil, and more sand.

"This... this, this?" Lie Kedi stammered. He jerked his head up, staring at Li Qi.

Li Qi sighed. "Monk Chanzhi's master, the dark-skinned demonic monk, shattered the landscape grand array. The mountain veins and water veins were obliterated. In present-day Vast Yang, both the earth qi and water qi have utterly run dry. According to our estimates, for the next ten years, not a single grain will be harvested. There will be severe drought and widespread famine."

Rain, too, was born from the transformation of mountain qi. Thus, an extreme drought was inevitable; not a single drop of rain would fall.

"Furthermore, watch this." With those words, Li Qi gestured to Old Ma, directing him up the mountain.

Old Ma nodded, then leapt into the air, soaring rapidly toward the summit. A short while later, Old Ma descended, clasping a mountain eagle the size of a grown man in his jaws. Tossing the great eagle at Li Qi's feet, the steed then retreated a few paces.

Li Qi picked up the corpse of the mountain eagle and unfurled its wings, revealing a span of over five meters. It vastly exceeded the proportions of a mundane eagle; it had clearly become a demon. Yet, its demon qi was exceedingly faint, and strands of incense fire lingered around it instead.

It had abandoned the Demon Dao to walk the Divine Dao.

However, any observation was moot, for this mountain eagle had already been reduced to a lifeless corpse. There were no wounds upon the carcass, nor any traces of battle. It had simply dropped dead in a sudden, inexplicable manner.

"Is this... the mountain god of this land? How did it die here? Was this also because of the landscape grand array?" Lie Kedi stared at the eagle, his fingers and lips trembling slightly in disbelief.

"The backlash following the collapse of the landscape grand array was catastrophic. Even Bo Yue, who presided over the rite, nearly perished. It required the intervention of a Rank 5 being to heal his injuries. As for the other mountain gods and water gods, though they did not endure a backlash as violent as Bo Yue's, seven or eight out of ten were either killed or severely wounded. Ten thousand gods wail in despair. Relying solely upon the deities of the mountains and rivers, I fear restoration will be insurmountably difficult."

Lie Kedi fell silent.

He realized now that, under these circumstances, every scenario and contingency plan he had envisioned was entirely useless. If they proceeded according to his methods, the devastation of Vast Yang and a mass exodus of starving refugees were an absolute certainty.

The atmosphere remained heavy with silence for several minutes. When he finally lifted his head to look at Li Qi, it was as if he were grasping at a life-saving straw. "For the Young Master to seek me out, you must have a solution, yes!? Is there anything I can do to help?"

Li Qi nodded. "I have thought of a method that can resolve this crisis."

"Presently, the mountains and rivers of Vast Yang are withered, and the ghost-gods are grievously wounded. Only humanity has remained relatively intact. I wish to initiate a Grand Rite, gathering the collective strength of the populace to soothe the scars of the earth. Therefore, I require the General's assistance."

"Preparations for such a sacrificial rite cannot be accomplished by me alone. Thus, I must entreat the General to lend his aid," Li Qi said solemnly.

"It is my bounden duty!" Lie Kedi immediately clasped his hands together, accepting without hesitation.

Li Qi nodded. "I will arrange the specific details in due time. For now, the General should attend to the aftermath within the army. A Grand Rite cannot be prepared in a day or two, and I still need to notify others."

"At the Young Master's summons, I shall arrive," Lie Kedi pledged, watching as Li Qi departed.

In this manner, Li Qi sought them out, one by one.

The next was Zhang Lan.

With the matters here concluded, Zhang Lan had originally intended to slip away in silence. He was preparing to depart Vast Yang and head in the direction of the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

The demonic monk had been executed, yet were the fiends of All Under Heaven numbered at a mere one or two? It was time for him to embark on a new journey.

He had sneaked away quietly, without even bidding farewell to the others. 

This was exactly the kind of conclusion Zhang Lan desired most. To brush off his robes and depart once the deed was done, burying his identity and his name.

There could be no greater path for a knight-errant!

It was utterly, unfathomably dashing!

Zhang Lan's temperament had always been thus. From the moment he and Li Qi first met and engaged in mutual flattery, one could tell that he secretly relished this feeling. He cared not for grandstanding before the masses; he preferred leaving behind a silent legend, only to revel in the quiet satisfaction of it.

However, despite his somewhat eccentric demeanor, he possessed a genuine passion for public virtue and justice. He was always willing to draw his blade to aid those suffering from injustice—a true knight-errant.

Except, this time, he had barely made it halfway when he suddenly spotted auspicious clouds and azure thunder sweeping across the sky, barreling straight in his direction.

"That is... Li Qi's dragon colt? Why is it coming toward me?" Zhang Lan reined in his horse, halting in his tracks.

He felt a measure of delight, yet also a twinge of displeasure.

He was delighted that Li Qi had actually made a special trip to see him off; this put him in rather good spirits. He was displeased because his flawless exit was now stained by a minor blemish. 

It was meant to be a grand deed accomplished, a silent departure unfettered by mundane dust or lingering karma, taking not a single cloud, but leaving behind only a name and the legend of a knight-errant. Introducing a farewell scene made it all feel a bit sluggish and far less dashing.

Still, since the man was already here, he could hardly chase him away. Thus, he reined in his horse and waited for Li Qi to descend.

Li Qi arrived swiftly, touching down upon the earth. "Brother Zhang!"

"Brother Li, why the sudden visit? Could it be a farewell? When did you become such a dawdling, mother hen of a man?" Zhang Lan struck first, feigning a look of sheer impatience.

Li Qi chuckled. Naturally aware of Zhang Lan's disposition, he refrained from bandying words on the matter and cut straight to the purpose of his visit.

He wanted Zhang Lan's help to coordinate the figures of the Jianghu.

A Rank 8 wanderer of the Jianghu would certainly command respect across its many factions. He was intimately familiar with its rules; entrusting this matter to him brought Li Qi far greater peace of mind.

"So that is how it is. Then leave it to me!" Hearing this, Zhang Lan patted his chest and accepted the charge.

Li Qi offered his thanks, mounted his steed, and turned back to retrace his path.

It was time to find Wang Boyan and the Vast Yang Minister of Works.

[^1]: **Spirit Tablets:** Traditional wooden memorial pacards utilised in funeral rites and ancestor reverence. Once inscribed with the name of the deceased, the tablet serves as a sacred focal point and resting place for their soul.
