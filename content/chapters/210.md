# Chapter 210: Sudden Change

Two hours before Li Qi made his move.

The battlefield was locked in a bitter struggle, and blood flowed across the ground almost instantly.

The asuras and hungry ghosts clashed in a slaughter with the soldiers of Vast Yang.

They had to use every means at their disposal to kill every existence they saw; otherwise, they would be the ones to die.

This was the rule of this battlefield.

No one could retreat, and no one could escape. The result of retreating or escaping was death.

Monk Chanzhi panted heavily. The battle below had reached a white-hot intensity.

The soldiers advanced in formation. The long spears in their hands formed an array, thrusting straight ahead. Officers led the charge, desperately blocking the advancing tide of asuras and hungry ghosts.

Monk Chanzhi's strength was limited. The gate of the six paths he had opened could only allow a very small number of Rank 8 and Rank 9 entities to appear. The vast majority of the asuras and hungry ghosts were unranked existences.

But even so, if left unchecked, they would truly be able to devour the entirety of Vast Yang.

Because whether it was the asuras or the hungry ghosts, both could rapidly elevate their power through combat and devouring.

Yet, the White Rhino Army forcefully held them back.

Even knowing they would ultimately be outmatched, they did not retreat a single half-step.

Accompanied by thousands of deafening spell arts flashing past, the radiance of dharma treasures tore the ground apart. Arrows rained down like a torrential storm, shattering the black qi barriers of the asuras and piercing through the blindingly white explosions.

Volley after volley of spell arts slammed into the ground, crashing into the enemy forces.

High, resounding war cries rang out. Various cultivation methods and divine abilities surged forth, scattering across the sky before spiraling down to blanket the earth.

A row of shield-bearing warriors held the front line. Clad in heavy armor and wielding incredibly thick greatshields, they formed a military formation that flickered with layer upon layer of spell art protection. Military qi wrapped around their bodies, mitigating the impact of external damage.

A Rank 8 asura stood at the very front, unleashing terrifying blasts of qi. Its imposing aura was so majestic that a single strike could likely crush a hill.

But at this moment, it unleashed hundreds of moves in succession, yet completely failed to break the established military formation.

All offensive power was evenly distributed across every person by the barrier, simultaneously dispersing into their armor and heavy shields. Even qi blasts strong enough to crush small mountains still had no way of harming the formation as a whole.

While defending, the mid-level officers rapidly issued orders, simultaneously leading by example and meeting the enemy head-on!

Order after order was relayed down by messengers, then executed by the soldiers under the command of lower-level officers. Layer by layer, they were neat and orderly, moving and halting as one!

The reason elite legions could surround and kill powerful experts relied entirely on military qi and flawless coordination.

There was only one way for these unranked soldiers to resist powerful experts: ironclad discipline and a fearless spirit!

The battle was not without casualties, but when it was their turn to die, no one took a step back.

The military qi upon the White Rhino war banner grew increasingly fierce. What should have been blue and white military qi was now dyed with a layer of blood.

Military qi arrayed like the dawn, killing intent blazing like a rainbow.

Military qi gathered like storm clouds, shattering the pale cliffs with a roar.

Beneath the clear sky and across the vast wilderness, they fought bitterly, their war cries shaking the heavens and making the winds howl!

This interception battle had already lasted for over two hours.

Raging fires burned in the air. Ash and flame coalesced into massive plumes of black smoke that choked the combatants, making them cough violently.

Yet one could only see treasured blades snapping, life and death decided, ethereal souls and corporeal souls tangled, ghost-gods gathering, bones exposed to the sand and gravel, and corpses littering the wilderness.

Rivers intertwined, and mountains tangled together.

Gloomy and desolate, the wind wailed as the sun grew dim.

Tumbleweeds broke and grass withered, as bitterly cold as a frosty dawn.

Birds dared not fly down, and beasts fled in panic from their herds.

Corpses collapsed along the long roads, and blood filled the plains.

Generals fell from their horses, and soldiers died in battle.

Neither noble nor lowly mattered; all became withered bones alike.

The casualties were disastrous.

Even though the White Rhino Army fought to the death, the sheer number of asuras and hungry ghosts was simply too great, backed by endless reinforcements. In these two hours, the White Rhino Army had already lost over half its numbers.

"Rat King! Are you still going to stand in my way!?" Monk Chanzhi struck out with full force, his massive oppressive might crashing down. At this point, there was no longer any talk of life, death, or elemental counters.

Every move was pushed to the absolute extreme; every strike was made with full strength.

His devil god physique ignited with black flames. Fire spewed from every joint and limb, like a strongman walking against the wind while holding a massive grass torch. The flames were blindingly intense, blazing fiercely as if to incinerate everything.

This was the karmic fire of a Nether Prison devil.

However, none of these attacks landed on the ground.

A golden-furred rat, about the size of a small dog, was continuously blocking all of Monk Chanzhi's attacks.

It had absolutely no intention of fighting Monk Chanzhi head-on. Everything it did was focused entirely on defense, dragging out the time as much as possible.

It continuously defended and constantly dodged. If an attack couldn't be blocked, it would deflect it as much as possible.

What rats excelled at the most was burrowing and hiding.

Furthermore, the Rat Soil Mound possessed a unique divine ability.

It was recorded in the original texts of legends, as both Shen Shuibi and Li Qi had mentioned.

'Horse saddles, human clothing, bowstrings, and armor plates; any straps or ties, the rats will gnaw them all apart.'

That meant gnawing on dharma treasures!

Monk Chanzhi was even forced to fight using only his physical body. His three-inch devil domain was also a dharma treasure. In truth, he still had many dharma treasures he hadn't brought out.

But now, he couldn't take any of them out.

The Rat King of the Rat Soil Mound feared no dharma treasure that could be produced. With those large front teeth, it would gnaw metal when meeting metal, chew wood when meeting wood, and munch earth when meeting earth.

Among the five elements, only water and fire were not countered; the other three would be eaten as snacks. Water and fire lacked physical substance, rendering its large front teeth useless against them.

It was precisely because of this that Monk Chanzhi ignited the karmic fire of a Nether Prison devil, using the fire element to bypass the Rat King's large front teeth.

However, the Rat King's coat of golden fur was also a divine ability.

When standing upright, the golden fur was like steel needles; when smoothed down, it was like armor. It was impervious to water and fire, invulnerable to swords and spears. It could even burrow into the earth, parting the soil naturally without any resistance.

Facing such a stubborn and slippery nuisance, even Monk Chanzhi was at a loss for what to do.

If it were a true battle to the death, Monk Chanzhi felt he could easily take down the Rat King. It would require the entire Rat Soil Mound attacking together to fight him to a draw.

But he couldn't overcome how slippery this rat was. It dodged swiftly and couldn't be caught. It simply stalled him, refusing to fight and forcefully dragging out the time.

This made Monk Chanzhi furious, yet he was utterly helpless.

As his fury gradually intensified, devilish thoughts began to rise in his heart.

It wasn't until after fighting for two whole hours that Monk Chanzhi finally lost control.

Focused upon by devil qi, he became deceitful and tyrannical.

He immediately realized what was happening, promptly halted his attacks, sat cross-legged, and roused the Buddha heart within him to suppress the devilish thoughts.

Monk Chanzhi chanted sutras, "Disperse all bonds to the gates of affliction! Subtle afflictions shall be instantly severed. Once removed, it is called returning the spirit to dwell in the empty cave, also known as liberation from the bonds of devils!"

"Chant the unequaled mantra; no mortal devils shall come, no Hinayana devils shall come, no Bodhisattva devils shall come, and even no Buddha devils shall come!"

"Just like the Formless Realm, avoid falling into the paths of the two vehicles, avoid falling into the path of devilish subjects!"

He was doing everything in his power to suppress the devilish thoughts born from his fury!

However, just as he was doing his utmost to suppress them, he suddenly struck empty air.

Yes, he struck nothing but air.

The devilish thoughts were gone.

Someone had helped him suppress the devilish thoughts.

But the sutras had already been chanted, and his Buddha heart had already been roused.

The balance between Buddha and Devil was breached in an instant.

Monk Chanzhi only felt a crisp, resounding crack echo within his brain.

It was as if he had thrown a punch with all his might, only to hit cotton, ending up spraining his own waist from the backlash.

His Dao foundation... had developed a crack.

This even caused his devil heart to fracture instantly, sending his mind reeling.

Monk Chanzhi's Dao Heart was divided into a Buddha heart and a Devil heart. Merging the two and maintaining their balance was the Dao path of the Mahakala lineage.

He had always carefully maintained this equilibrium.

Just moments ago, his devilish thoughts were surging, so he had elevated his Buddha thoughts to counterbalance them.

But the devilish thoughts had vanished bizarrely.

The Buddha thoughts were left completely unchecked.

Monk Chanzhi always talked about his devilish thoughts losing balance, acting as if only the devilish thoughts were capable of running out of control.

Could his Buddha thoughts not do the same?

Li Qi had recognized this point with utmost clarity.

Therefore, when laying out his plans, he had arranged everything perfectly.

The first link: bluff Monk Chanzhi into heading to the array's location.

The second link: a sneak attack by Zhang Lan and Shen Shuibi, followed by their withdrawal.

At this point, Monk Chanzhi would inevitably harbor doubts.

The third link: Wang Boyan, who had long been prepared on the perimeter, would hand Bo Yue's reply letter to Li Qi over to the Minister of Works, who had escaped thanks to Li Qi's schemes.

The Minister of Works only needed one glance to know that two young masters were present. A cowardly figure like him naturally knew what choice to make; he would undoubtedly turn back without hesitation.

Because if he didn't risk his life now, he would surely lose it later.

The fourth link: the cooperation between Bo Yue and the Rat King.

Regarding the Rat King of the Rat Soil Mound, a single young master's promise was useless. Therefore, Li Qi had also pulled Bo Yue in, giving the Rat King the confidence of certain victory and making it resolve to stall Monk Chanzhi.

With the Rat King in place, it could coordinate with the White Rhino Army to hinder Monk Chanzhi's progress. This prevented him from dealing with the enemies before him wholeheartedly, nor could he focus entirely on dealing with Bo Yue's landscape grand array. Forced to split his attention, the wrath within his heart would only grow stronger.

The fifth link: following the previous four layers of ambush.

During the previous exchanges, Monk Chanzhi would inevitably realize the issue regarding Li Qi.

By this point, he would undoubtedly be burning with anger.

Although this was of little use since nothing had escaped Monk Chanzhi's control yet. He might be angry, but he could still endure it.

However, when Bo Yue initiated the rite, took charge of the landscape grand array, and condensed the power of ten thousand gods, he truly possessed the strength to execute Monk Chanzhi.

At that point, anger would transform into terror.

Because it was entirely possible he could actually die.

Therefore, he would directly commit all of his reserve forces—namely, the Rank 7 puppet of the Minister of War.

Monk Chanzhi would absolutely try to stop Bo Yue from truly taking control of the landscape grand array.

Thus, he would be forced to divide his attention: stopping Bo Yue on one hand while countering the restraints on the frontal battlefield on the other.

At this point, terror would transform into anxiety.

Finally, Li Qi firmly believed that Monk Chanzhi would definitely be unable to break through the arrangements he had set up in a short amount of time.

He would only grow increasingly desperate.

Doubt, anger, terror, and anxiety—in the end, they would all condense in his heart, transforming into killing intent.

The interlocking setup forced Monk Chanzhi to step-by-step approach the weakness in his Dao path.

Doubt, anger, terror, anxiety, and murder—these five poisons would gnaw at his heart, and his devilish thoughts would naturally surge to their peak!

As long as he seized the moment when Monk Chanzhi's devilish thoughts swelled and he was forced to elevate his Buddha heart to suppress them, he could use the Heavenly Devil dao rhythm to suppress the devilish thoughts. In this way, the Buddha thoughts would naturally lose their balance.

Monk Chanzhi immediately fell for the trap, and his Dao Heart collapsed!

However, to him, this wasn't an overly serious injury.

Buddha thoughts were different from devilish thoughts; Buddha thoughts were very easy to control.

Yet, no matter how easy they were to control, it still required a few seconds to stabilize them.

The Rat King would not give him that chance.

Bo Yue, even less so.

---

At the very instant Monk Chanzhi's mind faltered, the Rat King violently exploded into action!

The Rat King's large front teeth acted like a carpenter's plane, rapidly slicing open and chewing apart Monk Chanzhi's physical body!

Against teeth capable of shattering dharma treasures, a devil god's physique possessed absolutely zero resistance.

At the same time, Bo Yue seized the opportunity.

Monk Chanzhi's brief loss of focus allowed him to instantly launch a counterattack.

It took barely two or three seconds.

_Landscape grand array, rise!_

A muffled boom could be heard across the entirety of Vast Yang!

Countless mountain and earth veins instantly erupted with majestic power. Along pre-planned conduits, they formed a near-perfect structure. Boundless energy surged through it as emerald brilliance cascaded from above. It was as if the river of stars had plummeted from the vault of heaven, connecting heaven and earth.

An awe-inspiring grand array manifested in the area.

Simply making this grand array operate normally required an unfathomable amount of colossal energy.

Every second of its operation extracted an incalculable amount of power from the earth veins.

A single use would leave ten thousand gods sluggish for a year. The entirety of Vast Yang Prefecture would suffer three years of disharmonious feng shui. Crop yields would decrease or fail entirely, countless animals would struggle to reproduce, and the people would become listless.

This was a grand array constructed at the cost of severely draining and devastating the earth qi and mountains and rivers divinities. It was the ultimate trump-card weapon of an entire prefecture.

Bo Yue manipulated the landscape grand array, bringing its power crashing down.

A broad and pristine sky suddenly appeared before their eyes.

Because... everything around them had vanished!

The surrounding mountain ranges... had been completely melted down. The resulting rivers of magma rushed over the cliffs, forming massive, spectacular waterfalls of fire. Splashing magma rained down everywhere—it was simply a boiling torrential storm...

The ground turned into a crimson sea that incinerated everything it touched. Searing magma currents swept in all directions, radiating a might sufficient to shake the earth... Even hundreds of miles away, one could feel the descent of this indescribable power.

The soldiers of the White Rhino Army were protected by a peculiar force and remained unscathed.

As for Monk Chanzhi, the asuras, and the hungry ghosts, they remained where they were, facing this power head-on.

The Rat King was terrified. Its reaction speed was incredibly fast; the instant the landscape grand array activated, it immediately used earth traversal and curled into a ball underground.

It was merely grazed by the shockwave.

Even so, its skin was scalded bald. A patch of black fur appeared on its golden coat, making it squeal in heartache.

However, it was still worth it.

Because the blood sacrifice, centered around the landscape grand array, had begun.

Monk Chanzhi, the asuras, the hungry ghosts, and the beasts were all offerings for the blood sacrifice.

And those enjoying the fruits of this rite were the demon rats of the Rat Soil Mound.

_Shedding our demonic nature and transforming into gods happens today!_

The Rat King ignored its scorched fur, joyfully feeling the power of the blood sacrifice diffusing throughout its body!

However, suddenly, the Rat King froze.

Because a massive black hand descended from the sky, shielding Monk Chanzhi's divine soul.

Only his divine soul, for his physical body had already been shattered.

The blood sacrifice was forcibly halted and interrupted!

The landscape grand array shattered in response.

Ten thousand gods wailed in agony. Tens of thousands of mountains and rivers divinities instantly suffered grievous injuries! Some of the weaker ones even died on the spot.

The earth veins of all of Vast Yang howled in anguish!

As the one presiding over the array, Bo Yue spat out a mouthful of blood. His personal cosmos suffered a violent backlash, and his entire being grew sluggish.

He hurriedly took out a vial of potion and swallowed it, but as he drank, his heart was filled with absolute horror.

His gaze swept the surroundings, seeing countless earth veins directly snapped in half. In this region of Vast Yang, all trees would likely wither, rivers would dry up, and the land would yield no crops for more than ten years before it could slowly recover!

To be able to forcibly shatter the landscape grand array... was this... the descent of a Rank 5 expert!?
