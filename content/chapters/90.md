# Chapter 90: White Snake Prefecture

Great Deer Kingdom, White Snake Prefecture, Snake Tail County.

White Snake Prefecture was situated next to Li Prefecture. Just as Li Prefecture took its name from the Li River, White Snake Prefecture was named after White Snake Mountain.

It was a towering mountain range that stretched for thousands of miles. Because of its sheer height, the peaks were perpetually crowned with snow, resembling a massive white snake slithering across the earth. Hence, it was named White Snake Mountain.

The entirety of White Snake Prefecture was bisected by this very mountain.

A full fifteen days had passed since the incident in Li Prefecture.

For fifteen days and nights, Old Ma had galloped without rest. They had finally made it out of Li Prefecture, putting an end to any worries about being hunted down by official constables.

But still... crossing into another prefecture.

This was Li Qi's first time embarking on such a long, cross-prefecture journey. Even in his original world, he had never traveled this far.

A distance of ten thousand li.

_To put it in perspective, it would be like running all the way from Beijing to New Delhi._

Li Qi could not help but marvel at the sheer scale of this world.

_Earth was fucking... about the size of the Great Deer Kingdom, and nothing more._

Yet, the Great Deer Kingdom was merely a remote, impoverished backwater. Rumor had it that the entire kingdom was only the size of a single county in the Tang Empire. And to Shaman God Mountain, petty nations like the Great Deer Kingdom numbered in the thousands.

It was an unfathomable size.

According to Shen Shuibi, they had already covered about half the distance to Lady Luofu. It seemed they were drawing near.

Because of this, Shen Shuibi had been in high spirits recently, eagerly gazing westward every single day.

However, Old Ma could not hold on much longer.

Galloping continuously for fifteen days on a meager diet of beets, oats, and grass was simply not enough. The beast had lost weight rapidly during this relentless journey, visibly shrinking by a full size.

Anyone who didn't know better might have assumed Li Qi had provided him with a harem of twenty mares.

Therefore, Li Qi planned to stop in Snake Tail County. He needed to restock their supplies and give Old Ma a chance to rest, recover some nutrition, and put back on some weight. He also intended to earn some coin to replenish their dwindling funds.

_How should I earn money?_

He didn't know yet; he would figure it out once they entered Snake Tail County.

Snake Tail County, true to its name, was situated at the very tail end of White Snake Mountain. It marked the outermost edge of the mountain range bordering Li Prefecture. Here, the boundless snows of White Snake Mountain melted and rushed down the slopes, forming mountain streams and waterways that merged to become the source of the Li River.

"Li Qi, do we really have to stop here..." Shen Shuibi asked, her face a picture of misery. She was desperate to fly straight to the Lady's side and felt deeply distressed by Li Qi's plan to halt and rest for at least a week.

"Miss Shen... we have no choice. Look at Old Ma; can he even run anymore?" Li Qi patted the horse's belly. Instead of the usual firm slap, his hand hit ribs with a dull thud.

Old Ma snorted. With sunken eye sockets, he turned his head and gave Shen Shuibi a pitiful look.

At this, Shen Shuibi was left speechless.

Even a dragon colt couldn't withstand fifteen days of non-stop, day-and-night travel. The poor creature had lost at least two hundred jin. Who could endure that?

"Alright..." Shen Shuibi lowered her head, reluctantly agreeing.

With the plan settled, Li Qi rode Old Ma toward the city gates.

Since they had already reached White Snake Prefecture and the primary goal was to let Old Ma recover his strength, Li Qi opted to ride openly.

Besides, Li Prefecture was thousands of miles away. The crimes committed there had no bearing on them here.

Yet, despite bracing himself, Li Qi was astounded before he even entered the city.

A saber hung at his waist, he was mounted on a dragon colt, and seated behind him was a pristine, ethereal beauty who was curiously glancing around. Though his attire wasn't extravagant—just the simple cloth garments of a commoner—the entire picture projected an undeniable air of grandeur.

_Grandeur, my ass._ It was entirely propped up by the dragon colt and Shen Shuibi.

Put any random peasant up there, and they'd look majestic, too.

The only real advantage was his cleanliness. He didn't look like someone who had been roughing it in the wilderness for over a dozen days.

This was perfectly normal. Li Qi's daily cultivation method required finding a water source, and soaking in it for six hours a day made it quite difficult to get dirty.

Furthermore, he had been reluctant to wear his good clothes in the wild, opting for rough cloth instead. He had only just changed into his better garments before approaching the city. At first glance, they still looked new, completing the imposing image.

In short, this traveling party looked quite intimidating.

The only flaw was the massive woven basket hanging off Old Ma's back, which looked rather crude. But it couldn't be helped.

They didn't have a spatial ring or a mustard seed pouch. Without the basket, there was nowhere to store their luggage!

Nevertheless, it was what it was. Even with the unsightly basket, the sight of a man riding a dragon colt, wearing a long saber, and accompanied by a beautiful woman stood out like a crane among a flock of chickens in a mere county city.

It was so effective that when Li Qi reached the city gates, no one dared to stop him.

It was Li Qi who took the initiative to dismount and pay for a Chuanyan. Only then did the city guards bow and scrape as they processed his entry.

Experiencing this blatant differential treatment, Li Qi couldn't help but sigh inwardly.

It was bizarre. Pleasing him offered them zero tangible benefits; they could have just done their jobs normally. Yet, they insisted on bowing and scraping.

Conversely, back when Li Qi was in Li Prefecture wearing rough cloth and sporting tanned skin, making things difficult for him offered no benefits either. Yet, the guards had delighted in finding trouble.

People were simply like that. It was hard to explain.

In any case, a smooth entry was a good thing. Li Qi still had a dozen or so gold coins on him, so there was no need to stay in a shabby inn. He immediately found an establishment with a stable, paid a thousand copper coins upfront, and ordered the best possible fodder for Old Ma. Beans, beets, oats, and even mountain forage and fresh fruits were arranged.

This old brother had worked hard. He deserved to eat well and put some meat back on his bones.

Seeing Li Qi's generosity and the imposing stature of his steed, the innkeeper's attitude was exceptionally obsequious. He arranged for a stablehand to tend to the horse, giving Old Ma a thorough scrubbing.

As for Li Qi, he looked wealthy on the outside, but his heart was bleeding.

Keeping a horse was incredibly expensive.

A dragon colt like Old Ma was said to be worth a thousand gold pieces—literally a thousand gold. If equated to modern luxury cars, it would be a Bugatti Veyron at the bare minimum. The annual maintenance fee alone would be over a hundred thousand, wouldn't it?

Fortunately, as long as it didn't display the ability to generate cloud qi, it would only be seen as a tall, ordinary spirit horse rather than a dragon colt, sparing them from unwanted attention.

Thinking about it, Old Ma had really suffered following him. He was restricted to mortal fodder when he should have been feasting on rare heavenly treasures.

Thankfully, whenever Li Qi sought out a water source, Old Ma managed to absorb a bit of pure water qi. This replenished his depleted cloud qi, so his condition wasn't entirely miserable—his physical stamina simply couldn't keep up with the exertion.

After all, the water at the roots of mountains could give rise to clouds, and cloud qi itself was formed from the evaporation of this mountain-root water qi. Because of this, he wouldn't weaken to the point of losing his ability to tread on clouds.

As Shen Shuibi had quoted from the _Shuowen_, "Clouds are the qi of mountains and rivers, and they bring forth rain."

Having settled Old Ma, Li Qi took Shen Shuibi upstairs to book their rooms.
