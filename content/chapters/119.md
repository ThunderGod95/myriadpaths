# Chapter 119: Ambush

"Therefore, the primary goal of this expedition is to venture deep underground and observe the ley lines of this world, which are entirely different from those in our All Under Heaven. These ley lines are the external manifestation of the 'Dao' within this resource world. By studying them and experiencing a dao rhythm distinct from our own, we can broaden our horizons and deepen our comprehension of the 'Dao'," Bai Di said.

"Through past journeys, we have deduced several optimal vantage points for observing the shifting courses of these ley lines. The Frontier Industrial Company we are heading to right now controls the very best observation spot," Bai Di explained to Li Qi and Shen Shuibi.

"Well, do the people of this world know about our existence and the Great Deer Kingdom?" Walking through a desolate wasteland, Li Qi felt a pang of curiosity and voiced his question.

At this moment, the trio had emerged in the wilderness and was heading toward a highway.

With their respective cultivation bases, all three moved at an astonishing speed.

Li Qi and Bai Di were both at Rank 9 of the Martial Dao. They utilized lightness movement arts to traverse the terrain swiftly, practically gliding over the grass and skimming across water.

Shen Shuibi, having recovered her cultivation to Rank 9 of the Daoist Sects, appeared much more ethereal. She rode the gentle breeze, drifting behind them like a leaf caught in the slipstream of their rapid advance.

Indeed, she wasn't flying. She had simply neutralized her own body weight, allowing herself to be towed along like a kite—an effortless and highly convenient method.

"The vast majority of the populace is naturally oblivious, though the upper management of the few companies backed by the Great Deer Kingdom are aware. However, none of that concerns us. We are merely passersby. After a week of observing this foreign dao rhythm, we will take our leave." Bai Di spoke with a relaxed tone, making it seem as though this trip truly posed no danger.

"It's that simple? Then why did the two of you act as if this was going to be perilous?" Li Qi asked, his confusion growing.

"If I came alone, I would certainly have to fight for the best observation spot. Things could easily come to blows, with strength deciding the victor. I’d also have to watch out for ambushes and the like. While my life wouldn’t be in danger, failure would inevitably mean injury or ridicule," Bai Di answered with a smile. "However, with Young Master leading the way this time, the others have undoubtedly caught wind of it. I fear few would dare to contest with you. Therefore, the path ahead is naturally unimpeded and free of danger."

"I see." Li Qi nodded in understanding.

_Now this is proper treatment._ When he used to read web novels, the protagonists were always fighting tooth and nail, risking their lives over petty scraps while every faction under the sun seemed determined to make their lives miserable.

_That was a life fit only for beasts._

His current situation was clearly much more normal. Aside from blood feuds, why would so many people want to fight to the death? Even if relationships were exceedingly poor, the worst that usually happened was refusing to speak to one another upon meeting. Why resort to violence?

Li Qi was highly satisfied with this arrangement. Since Bai Di had declared them mere passersby, he could fully dedicate his mind to preparing for the observation of this foreign dao rhythm.

The trio conversed as they sped forward. Soon, under Bai Di's guidance, they arrived at the edge of the highway.

It appeared this was a prearranged meeting point, as a crowd of people was already waiting for them.

Li Qi witnessed a rather bizarre scene: a man adorned in antique, flowing robes with wide sleeves was leading a dozen or so individuals dressed in practical, short-hemmed martial attire, all waiting in respectful silence.

Given this aesthetic, one would expect to see horse-drawn carriages or ox carts nearby. Yet, despite their classical garments, the vehicles parked beside them were bona fide luxury cars.

They were the kind of dignified, low-chassis luxury vehicles that, aside from a different emblem, bore a striking resemblance to Rolls-Royces. The only difference was the elaborate designs adorning the chassis—coiling dragons, dancing phoenixes, and intricate architectural carvings.

It was a blend of modern industrial design and technology with classical attire and aesthetics.

_Is this what the Earth Oil World looks like?_

Furthermore, the primary inhabitants were still human, though they appeared quite tan. They all possessed brown skin—was this a defining trait of living in this world?

Seeing Li Qi, Bai Di, and Shen Shuibi hurtling toward them, the man in the flowing robes hastily bowed. "This lowly one greets the Exalted Immortals!"

_Exalted Immortals?_

_What kind of title is that?_

Li Qi was a bit bewildered.

Bai Di leaned in and whispered an explanation, "To win over certain prominent figures in the Earth Oil World, we imparted a few life-extending cultivation methods. Those who cultivate to Rank 9 can live for two hundred years, which is why they address us as Exalted Immortals."

_So that's how it is._ Didn't that mean his group's status was practically akin to divine beings?

_That's actually pretty cool._

"We have arrived. According to the prearranged schedule, first take us to your city, and then escort us to the earth oil excavation site. The rules remain the same as before: if you perform well, you will be rewarded with an opportunity to study a cultivation method. Once mastered, it can extend your lifespan by a century." Bai Di stepped forward and waved his hand, his demeanor projecting absolute authority.

To be completely honest, Bai Di stood nine feet tall. With his long legs, loose flowing robes of pristine white, and a cascade of dark hair blowing freely in the wind, he was so devastatingly handsome it was almost unfair.

Thus, as he spoke, everyone present naturally assumed him to be the leader and immediately began fawning over him.

The group was ushered into the vehicles. A chauffeur took the wheel, Bai Di sat in the passenger seat, and Li Qi and Shen Shuibi occupied the back. The rest of the entourage boarded the other cars in the convoy to serve as escorts.

Soft, tranquil music played inside the cabin. The interior had been immaculately cleaned and exuded a faint, pleasant fragrance.

The chauffeur continuously tried to curry favor with Bai Di. Since Bai Di was not particularly arrogant by nature, he engaged in casual conversation with the man.

Li Qi, on the other hand, was perfectly happy to enjoy the peace and quiet. Sitting in the back, he glanced around the cabin, relishing a feeling he hadn't experienced in a very long time.

It truly had been an eternity. Riding in a car, listening to a music player.

He never expected to experience this again in his lifetime.

_Does this mean that if my cultivation base reaches a high enough level, I might be able to find Earth?_

At that thought, Li Qi suddenly realized something.

"By the way, Brother Bai," he called out to Bai Di from the backseat.

Bai Di instantly dropped his current conversation and turned his head from the passenger seat. "Young Master, what is it?"

The chauffeur in the driver's seat was inexplicably stunned.

_This inconspicuous Exalted Immortal is actually the one in charge?!_

This realization sent a cold sweat pouring down his body, instantly soaking his clothes.

Li Qi paid no mind to the driver's reaction, nor did he care. He simply asked out of curiosity, "Brother Bai, what is the technological level of the weapons and equipment in this world? You mentioned the world can only support cultivators up to Rank 8 at most, but what about ordinary weaponry?"

"For example, firearms and the like." Li Qi gestured with his hands, mimicking the shape of a pistol.

In his view, while cultivators were formidable, those within the Rank 9 realm likely couldn't withstand a concentrated barrage from a large cluster of modern firearms. If they took a direct hit from an artillery shell or a missile, they would most certainly die.

However, he had once witnessed a battle involving a Rank 6 demon. The sheer magnitude of that clash had easily spanned over ten li. He reckoned that at that level, even nuclear warheads would be ineffective.

"Oh, you mean those. They do possess such weapons. If one were to stand completely still, they would indeed pose a threat to anyone below Rank 8. However, it's incredibly difficult for those weapons to actually land a hit..."

"The speed at which I draw my sword is already faster than their bullets. Their projectiles are far too slow, and their stopping power is minuscule. Even if I were shot a dozen times, it wouldn't be fatal."

"As for artillery shells, a direct hit might indeed prove lethal. But how could one possibly be struck by such a thing? They are far too cumbersome. Unless it's a sheer coincidence, scoring a hit is highly improbable," Bai Di said, scratching his head.

Li Qi nodded, sighing in agreement. "That makes sense."

_True enough._ The destructive power of firearms could threaten his current self, but only on the condition that they actually hit him.

His current instantaneous burst speed was entirely imperceptible to the naked eye of an ordinary person. They wouldn't even be able to aim.

If someone possessed the dynamic vision and reaction speed to keep up with him, then using firearms could indeed pose a genuine threat to Li Qi.

_But what would be the point?_

If an opponent could already keep pace with Li Qi, they could just as easily threaten him with cold weapons.

The greatest advantages of firearms—their speed and destructive power—were completely nullified in the face of a Rank 9 cultivator. They were reduced to nothing more than ordinary weapons.

Furthermore, these ordinary weapons couldn't scale in power alongside their wielder's growing strength. Consequently, it seemed very few cultivators were willing to use them.

At this point, Shen Shuibi chimed in. "It's not that no one uses them. A weapon is merely a weapon; there is no inherent superiority or inferiority. Over in the Tang Empire, there are those who excel in the use of firearms, though their weapons are far from these mortal implements."

"The principle is actually the same. A cold weapon forged from ordinary mortal iron cannot withstand the intensity of your battles either, right? A wooden sword is still a sword, but if someone strikes you with it, the only thing that will break is the wooden sword itself."

"Therefore, these firearms operate on the exact same logic. You feel they lack power simply because they are cast from mortal iron. I have witnessed truly formidable firearms. Those in the Tang Empire who specialize in such weapons are all practitioners of the Reason Dao. Their ammunition can pierce through almost anything, and their speed is staggering—some can even approach the limits of the Heavenly Dao."

"The limits of the Heavenly Dao?" Li Qi frowned.

"Hmm... roughly six hundred thousand li per second, I believe?" Shen Shuibi pondered for a moment before calculating a figure.

"I have been enlightened." Bai Di cupped his hands in gratitude. Because of his experiences in the Earth Oil World, he truly had harbored a sense of contempt for firearms. However, he was also an eager learner; after Shen Shuibi's explanation, he firmly etched this knowledge into his mind.

A weapon was merely a weapon; there was no inherent superiority or inferiority.

As for Li Qi, his expression became rather subtle.

His attention was entirely captivated by the phrase _limits of the Heavenly Dao_.

_Bullets flying at near the speed of light?_

_Do the effects of relativity still apply here? Could a thing like that trigger time dilation?_

_Damn. It goes to show, you really can't underestimate the heroes of All Under Heaven._

Just as the trio was deep in conversation.

Suddenly, Shen Shuibi shivered. Her hands formed an incantation seal, and she slammed her palm toward the exterior of the car!

Li Qi reacted instantly. He kicked open the car door, grabbed the chauffeur, and leaped out.

Though half a beat slower, Bai Di also realized what was happening before disaster struck. He immediately followed Li Qi out of the vehicle, slamming his foot on the brakes on his way out. In a flash, he was outside, drawing his sword and dropping into a defensive stance.

It takes long to describe, but it happened in an instant. Less than a tenth of a second after the three made their respective moves, a deafening boom echoed through the air! A colossal explosion tore through the silence!

An artillery shell had plummeted from the heavens, smashing directly into the car Li Qi and the others had just evacuated.

Fortunately, Shen Shuibi's spell art had already erected a defensive barrier in advance. The barrier, a translucent aquatic blue densely covered in talismanic scripts, firmly repelled the shell and its resulting shockwave. This allowed the vehicle to come to a steady halt without any casualties.

"Enemy attack!" The lead car in the vanguard screeched to a halt, and several escorts clad in short-hemmed martial attire shouted in alarm.

Then, everyone looked up to see a massive volley of artillery shells raining down from the sky.

This wasn't just one or two stray shots; it was a concentrated, synchronized bombardment from a dense array of heavy artillery!

The thunderous roar of hundreds of cannons firing in unison finally reached their ears, delayed only by the speed of sound!

"Save them." Given the urgency, Li Qi kept his words brief.

Bai Di and Shen Shuibi sprang into action immediately.

Shen Shuibi formed a seal with both hands and stirred her spiritual imprint. In an instant, a wisp of blue light materialized on the collars of the dozen or so mortals. As Shen Shuibi took a light step backward, an invisible force yanked all of them back with her.

Bai Di and Li Qi's efficiency, however, was somewhat lower. The two Rank 9 Martial Dao cultivators erupted with their maximum speed. Smashing car windows and dragging people out in one fluid motion, Li Qi grabbed three men with both hands while Bai Di snatched one in each arm. They too began a frantic retreat.

The convoy was abandoned in its tracks as the people were hauled away.

Only then did the artillery shells finally hit the ground.

Gunsmoke billowed in all directions as shockwaves violently shook the earth. Everything within a hundred-meter radius was entirely flattened, the ground completely pockmarked with craters. Miraculously, there were no casualties.

"It's... it's the rebel army! It's those terrorists!" the man in the wide-sleeved robes who had greeted Li Qi gasped out, his voice shrill with absolute terror.

"Terrorists?" Li Qi raised an eyebrow.

_Now that's a novelty!_

"Since when did the Earth Oil World harbor such disruptive elements? And how were our movements exposed? Why were they able to set up an ambush?!" Bai Di frowned, demanding answers from the robed man.

"E-Exalted Immortal, we truly don't know!" the robed man cried out in distress.

"Capture the attackers, and we'll naturally find out," Li Qi stated. "These people must know this poses a threat to us. For us, a direct hit from an artillery shell can inflict fatal wounds."

"Not to me," Shen Shuibi raised her hand.

"Ahem, just grasp the general spirit of my words," Li Qi choked back a cough, glaring at Shen Shuibi.

"In short, this group wants us dead. It could be a coincidence, but that's highly unlikely. If we want to observe the ley lines in peace, I'm afraid we'll need to eliminate this threat in advance," Li Qi said. "I'll go capture them."

"I'll go as well!" Bai Di immediately volunteered.

"Mmm, I'll stay here and keep an eye on them." Shen Shuibi glanced at the ordinary humans around her, who were still coughing uncontrollably from the shockwaves and largely incapacitated. She chose to stay behind and tend to them.

After all, she lacked the raw speed to keep pace with two Rank 9 Martial Dao cultivators.

As for Li Qi, he and Bai Di were already accelerating at full speed, sprinting furiously into the distance.

In the blink of an eye, their speed approached the speed of sound.

However, less than twenty seconds into their dash, before they even reached their destination, another figure suddenly hurtled toward Li Qi and Bai Di at an identical velocity.

_A cultivator!_

_Another Rank 9 of the Martial Dao!_

_No, not the Martial Dao._ The opponent's body was exceedingly bizarre. It clearly hadn't been tempered by a Martial Dao cultivation method, and their method of exerting force lacked the fluid grace characteristic of the Martial Dao.

Yet, regardless of the origin, this individual could definitively match the speed and power of Li Qi and Bai Di!

Almost instantaneously, this sudden intruder clashed fiercely with Li Qi and Bai Di!
