# Chapter 177: Heaven-Sacrificing

Six hours later.

Li Qi rode out of Vast Yang City, his expression exceptionally grim.

Without a doubt, he had been thrown out by the Zhang family.

All signs indicated that Wang Boyan had spoken the truth.

The Minister of War was a useless piece of trash who cared for nothing, holding a sinecure and utterly neglecting his duties.

The Minister of State, on the other hand, was inhuman garbage. To think he would actually blood sacrifice the town pass, and for the flimsy excuse of 'guarding against potential demons.'

This Vast Yang Prefecture was rotten to the core.

Naturally, it was impossible for the Zhang family to sell him grain. He had been practically chased out.

A full twenty-six hours had passed since he first set out.

Over a day.

He had very little time left to waste. Thus, Wang Boyan's proposal remained his absolute only option.

Li Qi didn't believe Wang Boyan genuinely wanted to help him; the man definitely had his own hidden agenda. But it didn't matter. You couldn't expect everyone to act entirely without ulterior motives.

As long as the other party could truly secure required grain, it was acceptable.

Galloping through the desolate wilderness on horseback, Li Qi's mind churned, calculating his next steps and what precautions he needed to take.

But as he rode, he abruptly yanked the reins, halting right in the middle of the road.

The surrounding woods were deadly silent.

Li Qi sighed. "Who is it? Someone sent by the Minister of State?"

Following his words, the atmosphere immediately shifted as a middle-aged man in gray robes drifted down from above.

"To have stepped into the Innate realm at such a young age, truly, heroes emerge from the youth. However, how does your newly minted Innate cultivation compare to mine?" The gray-robed man stood balanced upon a treetop, a blade of young grass clamped between his teeth and a sword hugged to his chest. He looked down upon Li Qi from his high vantage point.

"And you are?" Li Qi raised his head to look at the man.

The man had a weather-beaten face covered in scruff. His sword blade was wrapped in tattered linen, and the tips of his feet rested on the leaves without causing the branches to bend.

"You haven't heard of me? Well, it's normal for a young man like you not to understand the Jianghu." The gray-robed man shook his head. "I am Liu Sanlong, the Drifting Catkin Sword."

"I learned the sword at three, tempered my bones at five, and at seven, I slew the Five Bandits of the Barren Mountains with five strikes. By twelve, my name was known across five thousand miles. At twenty, I stepped into the Innate realm, and to this day, twenty-three years have passed."

"Twenty-three years. I have traversed the martial world for far too long. Being a Innate in your twenties, you must be a genius just as I once was. The only difference is that I have twenty-three more years of accumulated experience than you."

"Normally, I should be protecting a junior like you and watching you grow. Sadly, you provoked people you shouldn't have and did things you shouldn't have done. Today, I have been commissioned to take your life. Do you have any last questions?" The gray-robed man delivered his monologue with grave solemnity.

Meanwhile, Li Qi was just glancing around.

_Is it really just this one guy?_

"Glancing about while facing a formidable foe. It seems I overestimated you," the gray-robed man said, shaking his head.

Since that was the case, he would simply have to collect his life.

The longsword cradled in the man's arms slid from its scabbard.

With a flash of sword light, a crisp _pop_ resounded.

A golden thread swept past. The half-drawn sword and the man were both cleanly sheared into two.

"I thought there were others hiding... I didn't expect it to just be him," Li Qi muttered, urging Old Ma into a gallop once more.

As for the man's long-winded introduction, he hadn't listened to a single word.

It was just a newly ascended Rank 9 Martial Dao cultivator. What threat could he possibly pose?

He had expected an ambush, not a lone fool.

_I really am being looked down upon._

The rest of the journey was peaceful and uneventful.

Well, perhaps not entirely uneventful.

There were still a few minor pests.

But two golden threads trailed closely beside Li Qi like shadows, cleanly decapitating every ambusher. He killed around ten people along the way. Roughly two or three were Rank 9, while the rest were essentially small fry.

Yet, it could still be considered peaceful, seeing as not even Old Ma's pace had been hindered.

After that, no one else came.

Very quickly, he returned to the Golden Wave Sect's manor.

Dismounting, he shouted loudly, "Brother Wang, I have returned! When do we depart?"

Wang Boyan stepped out. "Everything is prepared! We leave at once!"

With a mutual understanding, the two immediately set off without a moment's delay.

Shortly after the pair departed.

Within Vast Yang City, at the Minister of State's estate.

The scenery here was exquisite, surrounded by yellow-inlaid stone walls over ten feet tall. Dozens of buildings sat within, designed to catch the sweeping mountain breezes and fracture the moonlight upon the streams. The layout featured three chambers in the front and three in the rear, connected by a central alleyway. Corridors wove through the trees in intersecting patterns of long and short lines.

Right now, within the central-most room.

The Vast Yang Minister of State clenched his fists as he listened to his subordinate's report.

"The Drifting Catkin Sword, the Shannan Brothers, Iron Halberd Lu, He Yuxiu... all of them dead?" the Minister of State asked.

"Yes..." The trembling spy below reported, "Their corpses have all been collected..."

"How is that possible? Isn't he also Rank 9? Judging by his cultivation base, he has barely entered the rank. As a shaman, he doesn't even possess a single internal deity. How can this be possible?" The Minister of State was completely baffled. Using 'how is that possible' twice in one breath perfectly showcased his utter astonishment.

A completely ordinary Rank 9 shaman, lacking internal deities and without any apparent extraordinary dharma treasures, had somehow consecutively slain several Rank 9 experts!

It had to be known that those Rank 9s were all figures of renowned prestige in the Vast Yang Prefecture's Jianghu. None of them were mediocre fighters; they were at least sect masters of local factions and considered top-tier within Rank 9, possessing formidable strength.

"This man still has hidden cards. Let's not send anyone else to throw their lives away. Right, have that ancestor of the Zhang family take action. He is just a hair's breadth away from condensing his Martial Dao divine will and has reached the absolute apex of Rank 9. He should be able to effortlessly handle this person."

"Wait, where is he now?" the Minister of State suddenly asked.

"Unknown. The rest of our men haven't returned yet," the spy answered.

"Haven't returned?" The Minister of State frowned.

Because he knew that if they hadn't returned by now...

Then they were more than likely never coming back.

"Tch! Fine! Very well! I was wondering what was going on, but it turns out he has backing. The direction he headed was the Golden Wave Sect's external granary. So the one standing behind him is that 'Boss'?"

"I originally thought he was genuinely just a kind soul trying to save people. Though ignorant of the grand scheme, he could be considered a good person, so I hadn't planned to kill him. Who knew he was just another stray dog from outside? Seeking to obstruct the formation's construction? Delusional! Pass down the order! ...Nevermind, cancel the order. I will go personally!" At the thought of that Boss, the Minister of State was overwhelmed with fury. He immediately stood up and stormed out the door.

He left the spy behind to wipe cold sweat from his brow, terrified of suffering collateral wrath.

Seeing the Minister of State vanish into the distance, the spy finally breathed a sigh of relief. Unable to hold back, he lamented, "Constructing this great formation truly is difficult..."

With that, he muttered an incantation. His body turned ethereal, and he stealthily slipped away.

A good spy never easily revealed their true form.

---

Setting those matters aside, Li Qi was riding the dragon colt at breakneck speeds. In less than fifteen minutes, he had already entered the cave dwelling.

As expected of a dwelling left behind by a Rank 7 expert, it possessed an inner grotto-heaven. Stepping inside revealed a vast, expansive realm that felt like an isolated utopia.

However, it was completely barren. Everything had long since been stripped clean. Not a single item remained—even the potted plants, the lawns, the fish, and the birds had been thoroughly cleared out.

_They really dug three feet deep into the earth..._

"To think you were still able to discover a hidden minor world in a place like this, Brother Wang... How truly... sharp." Li Qi looked up at the cave dwelling that seemed to have been scraped clean down to its foundations, the corner of his eye twitching.

"Haha, without such a thorough plundering, how could that minor world have been uncovered? Follow me, Brother Li, it is right over here." Wang Boyan laughed and walked forward, stopping before a mural painted on the wall.

Following him, Li Qi looked closer and discovered that the mural was a painting of a hundred landscapes.

There were oceans, deserts, cities, and towering peaks. There were rivers and hills, military formations, and celestial phenomena.

The boundless heaven and earth, the bustling mortal realm, spectacular natural wonders, and magnificent expanses—countless beautiful sights were all captured within.

Before he could finish examining it, Wang Boyan lightly flicked his wrist. The painting split open, revealing a swirling vortex inside that exuded a tearing force, trying to drag Li Qi in.

The force wasn't strong, however, and Li Qi easily steadied his body.

But Wang Boyan actively took two steps forward, stepping inside and vanishing into the vortex.

Seeing this, Li Qi sent out his senses.

His intuition told him there was no danger.

He chose to trust it.

After all, a shaman's intuition was no joke; it was a very real connection to the unseen world.

Therefore, Li Qi also took a step forward and walked in, with Old Ma following closely behind.

Upon stepping out on the other side, Li Qi felt a brief wave of dizziness.

Then, he found himself standing in the midst of a vast desert.

The scorching sun beat down on the sands, whipping up wave after wave of blistering heat that made it nearly impossible to keep one's eyes open.

Staying here any longer would likely cause one's skin to split under the wind.

The blazing sun above, the skin-flaying winds around them, and the burning sands underfoot that could roast meat all gave the profound sensation of walking straight into the Nether Prison.

It was exactly high noon. The punishing sunlight forced every living creature in the desert, aside from cacti and sea buckthorns, into hiding. Even the insects had buried themselves deep beneath the thick dunes to escape the heat.

"This place..." The moment Li Qi arrived, he felt a strong repulsive force.

_So, this is what it feels like._ Li Qi reminisced, comparing the differences between this place and the Earth Oil World.

The Earth Oil World hadn't repelled them at all. That world's Heavenly Dao had already been refined and was thus perfectly restrained.

But this world's Heavenly Dao was different. It still brimmed with a wild, primordial aura, frantically attempting to forcefully expel them.

However, this was nothing more than an unranked minor world. It couldn't push them out; it could only suppress a portion of Li Qi's power.

According to the classification of outer-realm worlds, a world's rank was determined by the highest grade of cultivator it could sustain. A Heavenly Dao could not support the existence of another entity at its exact same rank.

Therefore, a world's native Heavenly Dao was generally one rank higher than the world itself. This meant that in a Rank 8 world like the Earth Oil World, the Heavenly Dao's capabilities could reach Rank 7.

In an unranked world such as this one, the Heavenly Dao was Rank 9.

The Heavenly Dao here was at the exact same rank as Li Qi and the others.

Still, Li Qi knew one more thing: a Heavenly Dao, at any given rank, was the absolute cream of the crop.

It was truly the highest tier. The texts stated that a native Heavenly Dao, aside from facing the rarest of heaven-chosen prodigies, would generally steamroll any opponent at the same rank.

What did it mean to steamroll?

It was roughly what Li Qi had looked like slaughtering those other Rank 9 cultivators earlier that noon.

Of course, the Heavenly Dao possessed spirit but lacked true sapience, so it generally wouldn't initiate an attack.

Having assessed his surroundings, Li Qi discarded all extraneous thoughts and turned to look at Wang Boyan.

"Brother Wang, what percentage of your combat strength remains?" Li Qi asked.

Wang Boyan clenched his fists, testing his strength, before replying, "Fifty percent."

"I am faring a bit better. I still have eighty percent," Li Qi said.

Wang Boyan nodded upon hearing this. "That's good. Inviting you was clearly the right choice. Shamans possess a natural affinity with heaven and earth. Although this Heavenly Dao despises outsiders, it is still somewhat lenient toward you."

Then, he continued.

"Then please set up the Heaven-Sacrificing Rite, Brother Li. Help me communicate with this Heavenly Dao. Previously, I was so heavily repelled that I couldn't accomplish a single thing here. Any grain I planted would wither and die. If I tried to plunder the nations here, a Child of Destiny would emerge to oppose me, and I would always lose. Even if I just tried mining for ores or hunting animals, accidents would constantly occur. I truly had no way to deal with this world."

"I'll give it a try first," Li Qi nodded.

Most likely, all of this had been within the man's calculations, including the fact that he was a shaman.

Looking at it this way, had Wang Boyan been targeting him for a long time?

Li Qi silently pondered what exactly Wang Boyan was scheming in the shadows, but his hands didn't stop moving.

The Heaven-Sacrificing Rite required an offering of wine. Upholding the etiquette of formal reporting, one had to sweep the ground clean before offering sacrifices. The finest vinegars and minced meats had to be presented alongside high-quality refined salt. Wood would then be burned upon the altar before the petition was finally declared to the heavens.

Li Qi had also performed a sacrifice to heaven in the Earth Oil World, but he hadn't prepared a single thing that time. He had only intended to mock it, so extra offerings were unnecessary.

Now it was different. This time, he genuinely intended to have a proper conversation with this world's Heavenly Dao, so he needed to properly prepare the sacrificial tools and offerings.

Fortunately, Wang Boyan seemed fully prepared.

Surprisingly, whatever Li Qi needed, he could immediately produce it.

"Brother Wang, aren't your preparations a little too complete...?" Li Qi couldn't maintain his composure, shooting him a sideways glance.

But Wang Boyan simply explained it away. "I am a grain merchant, after all... It's perfectly normal to carry these in my mustard seed pouch, isn't it? Aren't they all just food and drink?"

"What about the sacrificial firewood? Could that be prepared in advance too?" Li Qi asked while setting up the altar.

Wang Boyan flicked his fan open and chuckled. "Haha, coincidence, pure coincidence! I happened to pick some up earlier. I never expected it would actually come in handy now."

Li Qi remained noncommittal.

He continued to assemble the altar.

Right at that moment, however, a violent gale abruptly flared up.

"A sandstorm is coming... Sigh, see for yourself. I told you, being forsaken by the Heavenly Dao means you will constantly encounter this sort of thing." Wang Boyan sighed.

While the sandstorm posed no lethal threat to them, it could completely disrupt the sacrificial ceremony. It was obvious that Wang Boyan was utterly powerless against these natural phenomena.

But Li Qi was different.

"It's fine," Li Qi stated.

Following that, he pressed lightly against the ground, carving out a pit in the sand.
