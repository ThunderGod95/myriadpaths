# Chapter 209: Crap!

On the other side, Bo Yue's forehead was covered in sweat.

Things had gone terribly wrong.

Li Qi had tricked him!

Wasn't this supposed to be an unowned landscape grand array? Why was someone fighting him for control?

Bo Yue prided himself on his sharp mind and exceptional calculation skills. Coupled with his superb inheritance, he felt no fear. Even if the opponent was Rank 8, he felt he could easily take them down.

Li Qi's intelligence had explicitly stated that he would stall all the Rank 7 experts.

_That bastard! How hateful!_

However, it was too late for complaints.

The most pressing matter right now was to hold the line.

The battle had begun.

Almost visibly, two distinct wills began to spread across the entirety of Vast Yang.

Oblivious to the struggle, the animals felt their spines tingle and their fur stand on end, as if the mountains and forests beneath their feet had suddenly gained a consciousness of their own.

Woodcutters in the mountains were suddenly startled. They felt the earth trembling, yet at the same time, it seemed completely still.

Many woodcutters took out their waterskins to check if the water's surface was vibrating.

It wasn't. The earth remained perfectly stable. But... why did it feel like it was moving?

The experienced woodcutters and hunters who frequented the mountains were all bewildered.

As the acting mountain god, Li Zhufang felt it far more clearly.

Every Earthly Divinity—the mountain gods and water gods—could sense it. The power they contributed was supposed to merge into a single entity, gathering together with the help of the landscape grand array. It should have pooled in the hands of the person presiding over the array, before being directed to specific locations and transformed into various spell arts.

But at this moment, it was as if a giant's left and right hands had stopped listening to commands and started brawling with each other.

The result of this was that the landscape grand array was fracturing.

The intense internal conflict caused the earth qi to churn uncontrollably.

If this continued, the landscape grand array would soon dismantle itself.

This was likely the enemy's exact plan. He had no intention of taking control of the array; he wanted to destroy it!

As long as the landscape grand array was destroyed, no one in the entirety of Vast Yang would be able to stop Monk Chanzhi.

He could easily handle a Rank 8, but what about a Rank 7?

If it was a Rank 7 who specialized in rites, he would probably lose very quickly.

No matter how much of a genius he was, there was a gap in sheer hardware.

In a battle of who could seize more nodes of mountain and water divinities, combat power was not the most crucial factor; mental prowess was.

However, an excessive gap in cultivation realms meant a severe disparity in calculation power.

For a Rank 7 existence, their personal cosmos was much stronger, and their minds processed information far faster.

In other words, the opponent's calculation power completely crushed Bo Yue's.

It was like a supercomputer going up against an ordinary desktop.

It was a qualitative difference.

"But... I still have a chance." Bo Yue gritted his teeth.

As a young master of Shaman God Mountain, he was a true favored child of heaven.

Unlike Li Qi, who had been left to grow wild, he had received the highest quality of education his entire life.

Therefore, he understood exactly what the word 'genius' truly meant.

Cleverness, and wisdom.

These two things had always been completely distinct.

A clever person might learn incredibly fast, possess a photographic memory, and boast unparalleled mental arithmetic speed, but none of that had anything to do with wisdom.

A computer could process countless basic arithmetic equations in a split second, but it could never invent calculus.

True genius represented wisdom!

Innate root bone, the brain's prowess, physical talent, or even an innate dao rhythm—even one's bloodline and race—could all be easily altered!

With enough heavenly materials and earthly treasures, one could elevate their root bone. Countless rituals existed to swap out a bloodline.

Even if there were truly unfixable flaws, the worst-case scenario was simply reincarnating. With a Great Shaman personally protecting the process, one could guarantee reincarnation into a body with the absolute best talent.

All of those were merely external factors.

But wisdom alone belonged to oneself!

When the Great Shamans selected their disciples, they cared nothing for so-called sacred physiques or innate dao rhythms. Shaman God Mountain could easily grant such things after birth, even forcefully reversing the congenital with the acquired.

Those things were all hollow.

Wisdom, or rather, one's way of thinking, was forever the primary standard for a genius. This was the only unalterable talent.

In other words—

_If my mental prowess isn't enough, then I'll beat you with a better way!_

_If my current way can't win, I'll deduct a new one right here on the spot!_

Bo Yue's resolve hardened. Even if the opponent was Rank 7, it was obvious they could only brute-force their mental prowess. He had already noticed that this person knew absolutely nothing about rites or untangling the veins of mountains and rivers.

_I am the real expert!_

_Then, a Rank 9 slaying a Rank 7 against all odds... happens today!_

---

Meanwhile, on the main battlefield, Monk Chanzhi erupted with terrifying combat prowess!

In a full-force outburst fueled by the sacrifice of his own cultivation, the Rat King alone couldn't hold him back!

Calamity descended upon the area!

The beings of the Hungry Ghost path, driven mad by starvation and thirst, devoured everything in sight. Whether it was dirt, rocks, the glass-like ground beneath, or any lifeforms that had miraculously survived—they refused nothing!

The beings of the Asura path, brimming with murderous intent, were blinded by a dense forest of ignorance. They pursued false and inverted paths, following the malicious thieves of the Devil Dao. Driven by greedy desires and calamitous urges, they even began slaughtering each other. However, the vast majority surged outward in all directions!

They wanted women, they wanted wealth, they wanted to slaughter and plunder everything. All of Vast Yang was their target, and they would continue to spread even further!

The beings of the Beasts path existed in ignorant darkness, suffering the agony of decapitation and the endless torments of the evil realms. Dazed, lacking wisdom and sentience, they stampeded wildly in all directions following pure instinct. Yet, due to their sheer numbers, they formed a crushing tide.

These beings of the Beasts path constantly died off, turning into flesh and blood while their divine souls wailed incessantly. They became food for the Hungry Ghosts and the Asuras, simultaneously replenishing Monk Chanzhi's exhaustion!

Interlocking in an endless cycle, not only did they possess an unparalleled ability to spread, but they could also sustain themselves.

A great black wind whipped up! Anyone caught in it had their flesh and bones instantly pulverized.

Searing sands swirled, and flames billowed into the sky.

Countless hungry ghosts and asuras marched through the inferno, lunging in every direction.

Darkness and terror had descended upon this place.

The Rat King's fur stood on end as he fled frantically!

He had no intention of fighting Monk Chanzhi to the death!

However, Monk Chanzhi didn't want to let him go.

He sat down cross-legged.

The sound of sutra chanting drifted out.

"In all the worlds, the living must return to death."

"Though lifespans may seem boundless, they must eventually reach an end."

"The wheel of suffering is infinite, turning without rest."

"Fearsome are the various evils: old age, sickness, death, and decay."

"This body is a collection of suffering, entirely impure."

"Sever all bindings forever, and enter nirvana today."

Monk Chanzhi opened his eyes. His face was twisted in a savage grimace, yet he spoke to the Rat King in a kind and gentle tone, "Since we are acquainted, allow me to send the Rat King into nirvana. Let me sever your bindings forever and liberate you from the suffering of life."

The Rat King was horrified, but he gritted his teeth and forced a smile. "You damned monk, since you're putting your life on the line, how could I possibly fight you head-on? Someone else will be the one to kill you anyway! I just need to stall you!"

As soon as the words left his mouth, the Rat King tunneled deep underground to evade the calamity.

Monk Chanzhi merely shook his head and sighed, "Cowardly rat..."

With that, he ignored the Rat King. While maintaining the gateway, he changed direction, forcing the vast majority of the asuras and hungry ghosts away from the area and driving them toward a location thousands of miles away!

He could vaguely sense that the person presiding over the landscape grand array was over there!

Although the puppet of the Minister of War hadn't entered the battlefield, it had served as a perfect reserve force, successfully blocking Li Qi's lethal move just as he expected.

That being the case, his victory was already secured.

Kill that person.

Sever Li Qi's final hope.

However, right at this moment.

Monk Chanzhi saw a surge of qi rising up not far away, right in his inevitable path, seemingly preparing to stop him.

That qi was spirited and vigorous, cascading down in azure threads of light that illuminated the nine directions. Its sharp radiance cast a glow for five hundred fathoms, as dense and profound as the azure sky. The intense light surged majestically, faintly taking the shape of an auspicious beast.

What auspicious beast was it?

The beast of sincerity and benevolence that illuminated a hundred ghosts—the White Rhino.

Beneath the war banner of the White Rhino Army, a Rank 8 cultivator stood a short distance away, watching the incoming tide of hungry ghosts, asuras, and beasts.

He sighed in admiration. "That Mr. Li Qi truly predicts things like a god. He ordered us to station here in advance, and sure enough, that demonic monk is heading right this way. I have no idea how he managed to calculate this."

Beside this Rank 8 general stood the lieutenant general of the White Rhino Army—the very same one who often communicated with Li Qi. He looked at the general with worry. "Commander, you've only just been freed from prison... Please mind your health."

As it turned out, this person was the imprisoned commander of the White Rhino Army, Lie Kedi!

At some unknown point, Li Qi had actually managed to bail him out. Furthermore, aside from him, there seemed to be other legions scattered around. In total, they numbered roughly twenty to thirty thousand troops.

Lie Kedi waved his hand, gesturing that he was fine. Then, he gripped the war banner and slammed it heavily into the ground.

A loud thud rang out, echoing into the surroundings.

Every soldier of the White Rhino Army instantly stood at solemn attention, awaiting their commander's orders.

Holding the war banner, Lie Kedi spoke in a calm voice,"Mr. Li Qi and Mr. Zhang Lan have already spearheaded the vanguard. Now, as an ambush force, we must block that demonic monk's path. Do not worry; the Rank 7 Rat King will intercept any Rank 7 attacks for us. Our enemies are those hungry ghosts and asuras."

"This battle is not only to repay the grace of Mr. Li Qi and Mr. Zhang Lan, but it is for the entirety of Vast Yang! If we do not stop this villain, Vast Yang will undoubtedly be plunged into hellish ruin!"

"Gentlemen, are you willing to die with me!?"

The soldiers drew their blades and stamped their spears, shouting in unison, "Ten thousand victories to the General!"

"Ten thousand victories!"

The deafening cries shook the ears. The White Rhino war banner snapped in the wind, condensing military qi that spiraled into the sky and formed a radiant glow.

The soldiers of Vast Yang were going to stall the footsteps of a Rank 7.

Just over a month ago, the lieutenant general of the White Rhino Army had once defied orders. He had said it back then.

Thirty years ago, Vast Yang saw the rise of a great rebel, Boyue. Relying on generations of accumulated resources and coercing the masses of an entire prefecture, he raised an army and marched on the capital. Unstoppable, he intended to offer the land to the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

Lie Kedi sent out a call to arms to suppress the rebel. Sacrificing his family's wealth and risking his life, he stalled the rebel Boyue for ten days until the Hundred Forests Kingdom Lord descended, finally averting the great disaster.

Recalling these memories, Lie Kedi raised his head and looked at the great terror on the horizon.

The surging hungry ghosts and asuras brought with them bitter agony and hardship. Their mournful wails echoed as myriad poisons gathered. A terrifying hell had descended upon this place.

Stalling the footsteps of a Rank 7.

He had already done it thirty years ago.

Now, he was simply doing it one more time.

And so, he stamped the war banner again, roused his vitality and blood, and roared furiously.

"Pass down the order! Marshal the troops! Engage the enemy!"

---

Just a few minutes ago.

Inside the five-level Buddhist Pagoda, the true mastermind behind the scenes, Mr. Li Qi, was currently sitting on the first level of the five-level Buddhist Pagoda, watching the arhats subjugate the devils.

Here, one hundred and eight arhat statues and the corresponding one hundred and eight devil statues they were suppressing flashed with alternating radiance.

The arhat statues and the devil statues formed a perfect balance.

Whenever a devil showed signs of going berserk, the arhat statues would increase their power proportionately to maintain this equilibrium.

Monk Chanzhi was maintaining this balance with great ease.

Although Li Qi didn't know how the battle outside was faring, he could easily deduce that the monk was nowhere near his absolute limit, nor was he completely drained of spare energy.

Though ignorant of the external situation, he had already set everything he needed to arrange into motion.

Having done all that was humanly possible, the rest was up to the mandate of heaven.

In truth, Li Qi also wanted to watch the battle. However, his split soul was a golden body forged from incense fire qi. It was as fragile as paper. Talking was fine, but getting close to the battlefield to watch was utterly impossible. If he drew even slightly close, he would be blown away like dust.

Therefore, from beginning to end, he had no idea what was happening outside, nor did he know how events were unfolding.

However, judging by the Buddhist Pagoda, Monk Chanzhi still retained more than half of his power, using it to maintain the balance between Buddha and devil.

Monk Chanzhi was afraid, and at the same time, he was on guard against Li Qi.

This five-level Buddhist Pagoda was the foundation of his Dao attainment.

When he completed the seventh level of the Buddhist Pagoda, it would be the exact moment his Dao reached Rank 6.

In other words, this Buddhist Pagoda was a place more important to him than his own heart.

He had laid down an extreme number of defenses to guard its safety. He even lived here most of the time, terrified that something might go wrong.

Moreover, there was a strange strand of qi here.

Through his True Knowledge dao rhythm, he could sense that the essence of this strand of qi was not inferior to the Grand Invocator divine qi within his own body.

He guessed that it was Monk Chanzhi's trump card. After all, the monk was a disciple of a major sect and had cultivated to Rank 7. It was perfectly normal for an elder to bestow a life-saving trump card upon him.

The monk hadn't even taken it outside with him, instead leaving it here constantly to serve as a guard.

This meant that it would be incredibly difficult for Li Qi to use his Grand Invocator divine qi to shatter the monk's Dao foundation.

_Yes, very well defended._

Worthy of being the demonic monk who easily suppressed an entire prefecture, he handled everything impeccably. The opponent was no amateur; he was a figure whose schemes and wisdom were both highly commendable.

But much could be gleaned from this person's layout. Extrapolating backward from the currently known intelligence, one could easily arrive at a conclusion.

Monk Chanzhi had set his sights on Li Qi a long time ago.

However, despite all his calculations, Monk Chanzhi was still one move short.

Monk Chanzhi miscalculated one single detail.

Li Qi, through sheer good luck, possessed a strand of Heavenly Devil dao rhythm.

When Li Qi first obtained this dao rhythm, he tested it out. This thing... could easily make all devilish thoughts submit and render them useless.

Originally, before he had this item, Li Qi's plan was to throw out all his Grand Invocator divine qi and use sheer quantity to suppress Monk Chanzhi's trump card.

But now, he had a much better method, even if it came with a bit of risk.

Pinching this strand of dao rhythm, Li Qi waited in silence.

He waited for a very long time.

He watched as the power of the arhat statues and devil statues continuously fluctuated.

Sometimes the devil rose a foot taller; sometimes the Buddha rose a foot taller. But overall, a balance was constantly maintained, and Monk Chanzhi was clearly holding back his strength to sustain it.

It wasn't until a full hour and a half had passed that the intensity of the battlefield finally reached its absolute peak.

Because Li Qi saw that Monk Chanzhi had finally been pushed to his limit, pouring every last drop of his power into the struggle!

_Now is the time!_

The dao rhythm unfurled, suppressing the devilish thoughts!

The devilish thoughts dissipated, but all of Monk Chanzhi's Buddha thoughts remained completely intact.

On the first level of the Buddhist Pagoda, the arhat statues instantly grew incomparably massive, while the devil statues shrank directly to the size of rice grains.

The balance between Buddha and Devil... was broken!
