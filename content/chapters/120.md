# Chapter 120: Resistance

"Has the Platoon Leader gone out?" a person dressed in crisp black martial attire asked.

"Yes, let's retreat quickly. The Platoon Leader has already gone to intercept them. She's a Type-A modified human. Ordinary exalted immortals are no match for her, but we can't hold her back either." Another woman in black martial attire climbed into an off-road vehicle and slammed on the gas pedal.

The group consisted of about seven or eight people, both men and women. Each was exceptionally agile. They swiftly dismantled their artillery formation, loaded it back onto the vehicles, and drove off to make their escape.

"Qing, the Exalted Immortals this time seem a little different. Our artillery strike didn't do any damage; everything was either dodged or blocked," someone holding binoculars said, watching the battlefield in the distance.

"According to the intel from higher-ups, there are two 'Young Masters' among the Exalted Immortals arriving this time. Could we have run into one of them?" another person guessed.

"I don't know what a Young Master is, but judging by the Platoon Leader's situation, she should... Crap, the Platoon Leader is in danger! She's losing!" the observer exclaimed.

"Turn around! Go save the Platoon Leader!" ordered another person who appeared to be in charge.

Three off-road jeeps swiftly whipped around, driving straight toward Li Qi and Bai Di.

At that very moment, Li Qi and Bai Di were besieging the 'Platoon Leader'.

However, Bai Di seemed somewhat constrained in his movements.

This was primarily because their opponent was a woman. With his gentlemanly demeanor, Bai Di found it difficult to strike a heavy blow.

As for Li Qi, he had no such reservations. He didn't care about his opponent's gender. If the other party wanted to kill him, he would certainly return the favor.

That was exactly what was happening now.

Besides, truthfully speaking, it was genuinely hard to see this person as female just by looking at her exterior.

Her entire body was covered in scars, as if she had undergone countless surgeries. Her muscles were knotted and bulging. Standing nearly two and a half meters tall, she resembled a humanoid Tyrannosaurus rex. She was completely hairless—bald, with no eyebrows—because her skin looked as if it had been entirely grafted, losing the ability to grow hair.

Aside from the fact that she still had breasts, which identified her gender, there was absolutely nothing else feminine about her.

In fact, she barely even possessed human characteristics.

For example, she had massive canines that no human should possess. Combined with her savage musculature and the vertical slits of her pupils, she looked more like a frenzied lioness.

"Young Master, she doesn't seem to have any inner qi. She's fighting purely with vitality and blood..." Bai Di suddenly remarked.

As he spoke, he easily deflected a straight punch from the savage lioness and smacked the inside of her knee with the flat of his sword.

With a dull thud, the woman was forced to her knees, carving a trench into the ground as she slid.

If Bai Di had wanted to, that strike could have completely severed her leg.

Although the lioness could keep up with their speed and exchange blows, against someone like Bai Di—who was merely a hair's breadth away from Rank 8—she was only on the receiving end of a beating.

"I know, so can we wrap this up quickly?" Li Qi sighed. "People are coming from over there."

Saying so, he erupted with Carp Stream Intent and blasted a punch into the lioness.

Tens of thousands of jin of immense force sent her flying directly backward. The sickening crunch of breaking bones echoed from her body.

"I'll handle that side," Bai Di stated. His body glided forward, sprinting toward the oncoming jeeps.

However, at that moment, the lioness who had just been hammered away by Li Qi's punch suddenly struggled to her feet. Ignoring her spurting wounds, she charged at Bai Di!

_Their bond is quite deep. They still want to rescue each other even at a time like this,_ Li Qi thought as he watched.

He immediately put his thoughts into action and dashed forward.

Although Li Qi's own fighting method was rather poor—mostly relying on the progress bar to exploit timing—the lioness before him had an even worse fighting method.

She relied on practically nothing but brute strength. Without the aid of inner qi, she had to increase her body size to increase her power. That was why she was now two and a half meters tall with arm muscles the size of basketballs.

Given the same speed and power output, a larger frame would inevitably be more cumbersome.

Furthermore, having been besieged by two people, she had sustained heavy injuries, even if Bai Di had been holding back.

Li Qi attacked directly, throwing two consecutive punches, intending to capture her alive.

However—

"Roar!" The lioness let out a furious bellow. Her already formidable physical power surged to its absolute limit. It was her true limit. Completely disregarding the damage to her own body, she allowed her muscles and bones to crumble and tear under the strain of unleashing overloaded power. Without hesitation, she threw a straight punch right at Li Qi.

Relentlessly advancing and devoid of any flair, it was perhaps the most monotonous of all attack methods. It lacked skill or tricks. It was the simplest way to project energy, something any beginner could execute.

Absolute power overwhelming all techniques—such a concept truly existed.

Unfortunately, without an insurmountable chasm in sheer strength, technique still prevailed.

Li Qi caught her fist and twisted his body.

Like flowing water.

Carp Stream Intent specialized in advancing against the current amidst crashing waves; it was exceptionally suited for neutralizing straightforward, linear force.

This lioness was simply too weak.

It could only be said that she had reached the level of Rank 9. But without inner qi or technique, she was absurdly weak.

After his twist, he locked her arm joint. Carp Stream Intent erupted, and he snapped it.

With a sharp crack, her entire arm was twisted backward.

Then, he delivered a kick to her abdomen, pressed a hand against her skull, and slammed the lioness headfirst into the ground with a resounding crash.

The earth cracked and trembled incessantly.

The current Li Qi was already considered very strong among Rank 9 cultivators. Up until now, he hadn't used a shred of his power as an Invocator. That was his trump card, and he couldn't use it casually.

Seeing this from afar, the jeeps immediately whipped around.

"Run! Run! Even the Platoon Leader is no match for them! We've definitely run into a 'Young Master'!" someone shouted at the top of their lungs.

"What do we do! Are we not going to save the Platoon Leader!?" another person asked, his knuckles white from clenching his fists.

"How many seconds could you last against the Platoon Leader? Even she couldn't hold them off. If we try to save her, we'll bury our entire operation right here. Have you forgotten why we gathered in the first place?!"

"The Exalted Immortals control our world. If we want to liberate it, we must preserve our vital forces. Sacrifices should be made where they count." The man who seemed to be issuing commands made the final decision.

With their leader speaking, the others said no more and swiftly turned their vehicles around.

However, it was too late.

Bai Di's speed was far faster than a jeep.

Seeing the situation, the man who had been giving orders frowned. "I'll go back and hold him off. You run, and don't come back to save us."

The others wanted to argue, but he cut them off bluntly: "That's an order!"

With that, he leapt straight out of the car window, rolled nimbly on the ground, and pulled out a grenade launcher, firing a shot right at Bai Di.

Bai Di was forced to dodge backward.

At times like this, the advantage of firearms became apparent. Because the energy was stored within the weapon itself, there was no need to exert one's own power. Pulling the trigger with an index finger could unleash tremendous destructive force.

It was just like a pre-crafted talisman.

The grenade launcher forced Bai Di back.

The martial-attired man who had jumped down immediately reloaded.

It was obvious he was no mortal either. Jumping from a vehicle traveling at over a hundred kilometers per hour, he had instantly steadied his stance without his hands trembling in the slightest.

Bai Di frowned and immediately began weaving in a serpentine pattern to prevent himself from being targeted.

The grenade launcher could only fire one shot at a time before needing a reload. The opponent was merely an unranked individual, so dealing with him wouldn't be much trouble.

It was just that he needed to finish this quickly and then chase after the rest of them.

Right at this moment, Li Qi's voice suddenly echoed from behind. "No need to chase. Let them go. Capturing these two is enough."

"Yes, Young Master." Bai Di nodded.

_Young Master!_

_He really is a 'Young Master'!_

The man's heart tightened. His group had indeed kicked an iron plate!

He saw the man in flowing white clothes stop attacking a short distance away.

_Is he planning to capture me alive?_

_Impossible!_

_I will..._

Before the thought could fully form in his mind, a wave of dizziness washed over him.

He saw stars, and his mind went completely blank.

By the time he regained consciousness, he found that he had already been restrained. His hands and feet were bound, his weapons were stripped away, and he was being dragged across the ground by the white-robed man. He had already been dragged quite a distance.

"What... what was that?" Filled with inexplicable horror, he couldn't help but ask in a panic.

Unexpectedly, the white-robed man actually replied.

The other party said earnestly, "A curse art of the Shaman Dao. It doesn't work against a group of people, and it requires standing still to gather power, but it's very effective against a single target."

"Shaman Dao... what is that?"

"I can't explain it clearly to you in a short amount of time, so stop asking. Let's just see how the Young Master wants to deal with you." The white-robed man shook his head, seemingly finding it too much effort to explain.

Only Li Qi knew what was really going on.

As the nephew of the White Snake Prefecture's Governor, the path Bai Di was meant to walk in the future was, of course, the Shaman Dao. Practicing the Martial Dao was merely a secondary pursuit.

It was just that the difficulty of entering the Shaman Dao was absurd. Thus, even though his Martial Dao was on the verge of breaking through to Rank 8, his Shaman Dao remained unranked.

That was why he sincerely believed Li Qi was a genius.

"Young Master, I've brought him back," Bai Di said, dragging the man and dropping him in front of Li Qi.

"Good, thanks for your hard work. Go help me call Miss Shen over for a moment!" Li Qi replied through gritted teeth.

He was exerting all his strength to pin the massive, two-and-a-half-meter-tall woman to the ground.

Even though she was severely injured, she was still struggling relentlessly, preventing Li Qi from letting go even for a second.

"I'll go call Miss Shen," Bai Di immediately said.

When it came to bindings, Daoist arts were far more suitable.

Soon, the man and the woman—if she could barely be considered a woman—were both restrained.

Out in the barren desert, beside the battered convoy.

"Didn't you two say you were going to chase them all down? Just these two wasn't enough to stop you, right?" Shen Shuibi asked the two men behind her while inscribing a talisman.

Her talisman emitted an aqua-blue glow. Thin, silken threads wrapped around the lioness and the martial-attired man like a cocoon, rendering them immobile.

"The Young Master said these two were enough, and there was no need to chase the rest," Bai Di answered honestly.

Li Qi smiled and added, "Yes, these two are enough."

Then, Li Qi looked at the robed man standing beside them, the one who had come to receive them. He was acting extremely cautious, practically shrinking into himself. "And how should I address you?"

"T-this lowly one is Zhang Deming. The Young Master can just call me by my name," the man replied immediately in trepidation.

"Alright then, Brother Zhang. You should know what these people are, right? Why don't you tell me about them?" Li Qi asked with a smile.

"I wouldn't dare hide anything from the Young Master. These people are rebels. They commit acts of violence under wicked ideologies, disturb the peace, and sabotage production. They often cause the populace to go without food or clothing. They are all heinous criminals."

Zhang Deming pointed at the lioness and continued, "Furthermore, they abduct children, brainwash them, and modify them. Look here, Young Master, does this even look human anymore? She doesn't have a single piece of good skin on her entire body!"

But at this moment, the martial-attired man loudly rebuked him, "Bullshit! You're the ones who grovel like slaves, handing this world over on a silver platter, letting these Exalted Immortals plunder it as they please!"

The lioness, however, tugged at him, gesturing for him to be quiet. Only then did he indignantly shut his mouth.

Bai Di was somewhat puzzled. Just what exactly was the Young Master trying to do?

Li Qi, on the other hand, was full of interest. "You guys are a resistance army? Whose orders are you following to mount this resistance?"

Neither the lioness nor the martial-attired man spoke.

"I've been suspicious from the beginning. Judging from the descriptions along our journey, the Great Deer Kingdom and these industrial companies should have formed absolute control over this world. From education to channels of knowledge, everything is in their grasp."

"How could anyone develop a resistance organization? Unless the higher-ups of the industrial companies harbored treacherous intentions themselves. Only they would have the capability to cultivate such people, right? Look at her, she has the combat prowess of a Rank 9." Li Qi pointed at the lioness.

"Young Master! Our company absolutely harbors no treacherous intentions!" Terrified to death, Zhang Deming dropped to his knees, kowtowing repeatedly as he defended himself.

"I know. I also believe you don't have the slightest rebellious intent. This world can at most birth Rank 8 experts, which means it must be backed by a much greater power. A world that can only produce Rank 8 experts, and is constantly being mined on top of that, cannot possibly harbor thoughts of rebellion. I know how the strong operate. To them, a single glance is enough to easily see through a person's heart."

"So under these circumstances, how could a resistance possibly exist? Aren't you curious?" Li Qi asked the people around him.

Bai Di looked thoughtful, but his mind was like a tangled ball of yarn; he just couldn't find the thread to unravel it.

Shen Shuibi, however, quickly said, "Li Qi, stop keeping us in suspense and just spit it out. What did you sense? The thing the Grand Invocator gave you?"

She was a Rank 5 and knew many things, so she had guessed the answer.

At this, Li Qi smiled and replied, "Yes, I felt it."

"The four wisps of divine qi bestowed upon me by my teacher—the qi of the Sun, Moon, Stars, and Constellations—felt an inexplicable tremor just now when I faced these people."

"The qi of the Sun, Moon, Stars, and Constellations stirred? Doesn't that mean this world is about to be finished?" Shen Shuibi was stunned.

"Yes," Li Qi said with conviction. "I felt it. The Heavenly Dao of this world is in pain. Its blood is being drained, its flesh is being gnawed away. This world is wailing in agony as it marches toward death, yet the children it nourished and raised are aiding the tyrants, helping outsiders excavate its few remaining foundations."
