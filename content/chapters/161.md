# Chapter 161: A Monumental Event

When Hu Xian'er asked this question, the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord had already arrived within his own palace.

Yet he still heard the question.

He frowned and waved his hand. The space directly folded, and Hu Xian'er stepped out from within, a look of utter astonishment on her face.

She was genuinely surprised. She hadn't expected her casual complaint to warrant an answer from her master, much less that he would take the initiative to summon her over.

So, she immediately knelt and bowed low. "This servant deserves death..."

But the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord replied bluntly, "You do deserve death, but you don't even know why."

"This servant does not understand... Even if he has a backer behind him, how is it worth Master stooping to such a degree?" Hu Xian'er asked in confusion.

"This is exactly why you've accomplished nothing in Clear Mist City over the past hundred years." The Green Mountain Kingdom Lord walked out of the palace and sat on a stone bench in the elegant garden outside.

He reached out his hand, producing a few spirit seeds. Tossing them into the air, the numerous spiritual birds and birds of prey soaring in the sky immediately scrambled to snatch them, squabbling in a chaotic flock.

Although his tone held a hint of anger, he maintained excellent composure. He didn't resort to beating or scolding her, but instead prepared to explain.

"Think carefully. What was the reward he requested?" the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord asked.

"It was... I remember it being Moon Corporeal Soul fragments," Hu Xian'er replied.

"Under what circumstances would one use Moon Corporeal Soul fragments?"

"They are used as materials when one needs to extract Yin qi. However, because they are overly condensed, the extraction process is extremely difficult, and the extracted qi is merely ordinary Supreme Yin qi."

"If one's techniques are exquisite, they can also be used for artifact refinement. Although they are rare, they aren't of much use, so their value isn't considered high. Oh, the powder could perhaps be used to treat illnesses, but Supreme Yin qi is too illusory. It wouldn't be as effective as directly using Earthly Yin qi." After a moment of thought, Hu Xian'er gave this answer.

She was fundamentally correct. The Supreme Yin Moon's Corporeal Soul hung high in the sky. It was naturally formed from the convergence of the natural Yin qi of heaven and earth. Its status was extremely high, and it contained a virtually inexhaustible amount of Yin qi.

However, because of its massive volume, a portion of it would naturally shatter at regular intervals, only for another portion to condense anew, much like a metabolic process.

Some of these fragments would directly turn ethereal and dissolve into Yin qi. Many cultivators who based their cultivation on the moon would gaze at it while cultivating at night, absorbing this naturally dissipating Supreme Yin qi.

There was also a minuscule fraction of remnants that were too condensed to shatter completely. These would remain and fall onto the lunar surface—these were the Moon Corporeal Soul fragments.

The Supreme Yin existed beyond All Under Heaven. Even for mighty figures, traveling there required covering quite a distance. The act of going up there to pick up Moon Corporeal Soul fragments was roughly equivalent to jogging ten miles just to pick up an ordinary rock.

Its status was high, its quantity was low, and harvesting it was a hassle. Most importantly, it was practically useless, as there were almost always cheaper and superior alternatives available.

Therefore, this material barely circulated in the markets. Ninety-nine percent of the Moon Corporeal Soul fragments sat quietly on the surface of the Supreme Yin moon, no different from weeds.

The ones Li Qi now held were obtained by using his reward opportunity to ask the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord to make a trip to the Supreme Yin and bring them back for him.

"Then think. Why would he want a material that is virtually useless?" the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord pressed.

"He must have his own methods. There are tens of thousands of cultivation methods in this world; it's entirely possible one of them requires this item," Hu Xian'er guessed uncertainly.

"What a fool. I don't even know what to say to you anymore. You know he hails from the Great Deer Kingdom. The Great Deer Kingdom, and he needs Moon Corporeal Soul fragments. Can you really not connect the dots?" the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord scolded.

Berated like this, Hu Xian'er immediately began scouring her mind for intelligence regarding the Great Deer Kingdom and the Moon Corporeal Soul.

Prompted by her master, she carefully pondered the recent major events concerning the Great Deer Kingdom.

The Great Deer Kingdom was extremely far from here. She usually didn't pay much attention to it; after all, separated by over a hundred thousand miles, there was practically no connection to speak of.

However, a monumental event had indeed occurred in the Great Deer Kingdom recently—one that had shocked All Under Heaven.

Lady Luofu had received a sword strike from the Human Emperor, leaving her heavily injured and on the verge of death. She had fled from Jiannan to Baiyue. Two Rank 3 Paragons[^1] of the Human Dao had relentlessly pursued her all the way to Baiyue. In the span of just three days, they had utterly destroyed the Song Kingdom, the Stone Mountain Kingdom, the Bright Celebration Kingdom, and the Lesser Arrayed Kingdom. Two of the four Kingdom Lords were slain on the spot, and the remaining two lost half their lives before barely managing to escape.

Afterward, a Grand Invocator arrived. Fighting one against two, the battle was pushed all the way to the heavens beyond the heavens. Miraculously, the Grand Invocator defeated both Paragons, forcing them to flee in miserable defeat. They had even been forced to sever their own tails like geckos, abandoning a Rank 4 dharma treasure to cover their retreat just to escape successfully.

Following this, the standoff at the border between Shaman God Mountain and the Tang Empire grew increasingly severe. Rumor had it that in that region, even natural mountain gods and water gods were unable to be born. At the same time, various laws of reality began to fail. This was the result of the Shaman Dao and the Human Dao clashing directly against each other. It had become a completely lawless land, where even ranked cultivators were a rare sight.

This was likely the most severe situation the Great Deer Kingdom had encountered, because the battlefield where the two Paragons and the Grand Invocator fought was located in the Great Deer Kingdom, right on its border with the Song Kingdom.

Aside from this, Hu Xian'er had no particular knowledge regarding the Great Deer Kingdom.

No, there was one more thing.

It had happened just last year. An opportunity had emerged at White Snake Mountain in the Great Deer Kingdom, attracting countless people like Hu Xian'er to scramble for it. It was a fingerprint left by a Grand Diviner, containing inner dao rhythm. The White Snake Mountain god had made an absolute fortune off it.

It was also related to Lady Luofu. Apparently, Lady Luofu's hidden tracks had been exposed. A Grand Diviner from Shaman God Mountain descended personally to escort her, and fought off a great demon who had received the news and rushed over to take advantage of the crisis. Not only was Lady Luofu successfully rescued, but they had also snatched back a Rank 2 dharma treasure—the Sun and Moon True Lead Cauldron, a legacy artifact of Mount Luofu. It had been a fully triumphant return.

The battlefield left behind from that encounter became a new land of opportunity. People continuously flocked there, attempting to observe the Grand Diviner's dao rhythm to advance their own cultivation, sparking numerous violent conflicts in the process.

Was there anything else?

Although all of this happened in the Great Deer Kingdom, what did it have to do with Moon Corporeal Soul fragments?

Seeing Hu Xian'er remain silent for a long time, the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord looked at her with bitter disappointment and snapped harshly, "Use your brain! Who escaped from Mount Luofu? Who were the individuals the higher-ups ordered the entirety of Baiyue to search for back then?"

Hu Xian'er immediately jolted!

Yes, there was such a thing! Shaman God Mountain had once ordered all the kingdoms in Baiyue to conduct a sweeping internal search to find Lady Luofu's tracks!

Naturally, the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord had also instructed his subordinates to carry this out, plastering numerous notices everywhere.

At that time, the clues handed down from above—aside from a bunch of Lady Luofu's physical characteristics and cultivation methods—also included her maidservants as secondary clues.

Because her master had specifically pointed this out as a matter of importance, she had paid close attention and remembered it clearly. It was said that when Lady Luofu escaped, she was accompanied by the Essences of Sun and Moon, her handmaidens. If these two were discovered, it meant Lady Luofu was nearby.

These two were the Moon Essence, Shen Shuibi, and the Sun Essence, Yang Ning. They were sun and moon spirits birthed by the Sun and Moon True Lead Cauldron, innate anomolous entities.

"This person... is connected to the Moon Essence?" Hu Xian'er raised her head, finally grasping her master's implication.

"Likely more than just connected." Seeing that Hu Xian'er had finally understood the most basic point, the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord pushed the topic a bit further. "Being connected to the Moon Essence is one thing. But if he were merely someone connected to the Moon Essence, would he forgo practicing the cultivation methods of the Daoist Sects and instead choose to practice the Shaman Dao?"

Upon hearing this, Hu Xian'er felt as if she had been struck by lightning! She stood frozen in place.

Of course! She should have thought of it earlier!

It was just that the answer was too terrifying and horrifying, so much so that she had subconsciously excluded it from her mind.

She couldn't be blamed. No one would easily bring out that specific answer.

Because, in the hearts of all Baiyue cultivators above Rank 7, that answer could explain every doubt and satisfy every question, yet it was absolutely impossible for it to appear in reality.

It was like the ultimate truth. Theoretically, it could explain everything, and everyone knew of its existence, but no one would blindly apply the ultimate truth to every single problem.

But sometimes, certain problems just happened to fit perfectly within that ultimate truth.

"Sha... Shaman God Mountain," Hu Xian'er answered, trembling, cold sweat pouring down her face.

A Rank 7 could easily control their metabolism, yet she was unable to stop herself. The cold sweat soaked through the thin gauze she wore, making her entire body appear semi-transparent.

"You've figured it out, right? Do you see why I've told you all this?" Seeing Hu Xian'er so terrified, the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord scoffed coldly. "Consider this a lesson. Remember, use your brain when dealing with things in the future. Don't be like Jin Chi, who only knows how to rely on brute strength."

"This... this servant understands," Hu Xian'er replied with a shudder.

The Green Mountain Kingdom Lord shook his head and continued tossing spirit seeds into the sky to feed the birds. Without looking back, he said, "Go back. Do not reveal this matter to anyone, not even to Li Qi. If you don't say anything, he will still remember my kindness and know that I helped him."

"If you say it, then it becomes me making an early investment, granting a favor while expecting repayment. Even though he definitely wouldn't leave it unpaid, the sentiment would be far weaker. Do you understand?"

"This servant understands." Hu Xian'er had calmed down considerably. She bowed her head in agreement, though who knew what she was thinking inside.

"Furthermore, I added a few extra things to the reward he requested. The next time we meet, he will definitely thank me in person," the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord stated.

With that, he waved his hand. The space around Hu Xian'er instantly warped, and she vanished from her spot.

He then closed his eyes.

_The gift given through this reward should be enough to make this young master remember my favor, right? It should be._

Pondering this, he controlled his palace, beginning to accelerate and sprint through the outer realms.

He still had matters to attend to.

Jin Chi was rushing to get his son reincarnated.

Was it really that simple?

Jin Chi was brainless. He had finally managed to sire a son with half a brain like Jin Buhuan, treating him like an absolute treasure and viewing him as his future successor.

How could he possibly allow him to actually successfully raise Jin Buhuan?

_Tampering with the cycle of reincarnation—if Jin Chi can do it, why can't I?_

---

Meanwhile, on Li Qi's end, he had wandered around the Clear Mist Sect for a day. His main activities were touring the Clear Mist Sect's properties and taking the opportunity to select a cultivation method.

There wasn't much to say about the Clear Mist Sect's properties. While they were certainly pleasing to the eye, Li Qi had no interest in those women and had no intention of trying them out for himself.

After a simple lap around to broaden his horizons, he focused entirely on hunting for a cultivation method. That was far more important.

He picked out a cultivation method for Old Ma with a moderate level of strength and price. The limit of the cultivation method allowed one to reach Rank 8. It was titled [Song of the Solitary].

The name was derived from the saying, 'Fierce beasts do not flock; birds of prey do not sing.' It took the fierce beast qi within a dragon colt's body as its core, refining it by absorbing and tempering the righteous qi of heaven and earth. Its roar could contain a trace of righteous qi. By using this method to become a demon, one would be like a hero among beasts, brimming with righteous qi. Tossing its head and galloping, its breath would sound like thunder, its aura unstoppable. It could devour tigers and ward off weapons, its eyes like pure gold, roaming the earth without boundaries.

These characteristics suited Li Qi perfectly. Therefore, after careful consideration and browsing through many texts, he still chose this one.

Although he had no idea why the Clear Mist Sect—which primarily relied on the flesh trade to amass wealth—would possess such a cultivation method, Li Qi happily accepted it anyway.

Originally, the Clear Mist Sect intended to gift him the original copy directly, but Li Qi firmly refused. He asked for a blank book and stayed up all night transcribing the cultivation method's circulation routes, various qi ingestion methods, and key points to note. As for the core divine will inheritance, he honestly committed it to memory, preparing to pass it down to Old Ma later.

Once all these matters were settled, Li Qi bid farewell to the Clear Mist Sect and headed straight back to his inn.

Along the way, many people still tried to exchange pleasantries and strike up conversations. Li Qi brushed them off with superficial politeness.

Back at the inn, the rabbit was crouching by the door, drowsy and half-asleep.

The moment she heard Li Qi's voice, she immediately lifted her head and walked outside.

"Ah, Miss Shen, I'm back! Everything's sorted out. Let's set out today!" Li Qi quickened his pace upon seeing Shen Shuibi, walking right up to her and declaring cheerfully.

However, Shen Shuibi furrowed her brows. She pulled Li Qi close, then stood on her tiptoes and patted his head.

Li Qi actually thought she had missed him and was feeling a bit smug about it, but Shen Shuibi proceeded to peel back his eyelids to check the whites of his eyes, then pried his jaw open to take a sniff.

Then, she grabbed his wrist and carefully checked his pulse.

Following that, Shen Shuibi spoke to him in a very serious tone, "Li Qi, did someone tamper with your body recently?"

Li Qi replied, "Uh... the Sect Master of the Clear Mist Sect helped heal my injuries. Her methods were incredibly miraculous. It was like she was performing incisions and sutures on microscopic parts inside my body, fixing all the damaged—Urgh!"

Halfway through his sentence, Shen Shuibi completely ignored his words and slapped a palm directly onto his chest.

Intense agony tore him apart from the inside out!

Li Qi instantly collapsed flat onto the ground. He felt as if he had just plummeted from a twenty-meter-high platform and smacked face-first onto concrete.

His entire body felt as though it had been sliced open, chopped to pieces, and thrown into a meat grinder until he was nothing but minced meat.

Even with Li Qi's formidable willpower, he couldn't hold back a scream as he crashed to the floor.

It took him quite a while to recover. Drenched in sweat, he gasped for breath.

"Miss... Shen... what are you... doing..." Li Qi barely managed to prop himself up, on the verge of total collapse.

Furthermore, his injuries had 'recovered.'

Meaning, they hadn't healed. Rather, he had reverted from his healthy state back to his previously injured condition.

"Don't let quacks touch you. Thankfully you came back in time. If you had actually let her treat you like that and allowed it to slowly heal, once you fully recovered, your personal cosmos would have lost its natural state. The lingering side effects for your Shaman Dao path would be massive. You would have to expend a hundred, or even a thousand times more effort to restore it later," Shen Shuibi said very strictly while patting his head.

"Okay..." Aside from that single word, Li Qi was too drained to say anything else.

_Whatever the case... it isn't like she would harm me._

_Let it be. I don't want to do anything right now except play dead._

_It hurts so much._ _Ah, I'm dying._

[^1]:

    **Paragon (至人 | zhì rén):** 至人 signifies the "Ultimate Person" or the "Person who has reached the peak."
    
    - It can be also be translated as:
        - The Perfected Man
        - The Utmost Man
        - The Supreme Man
        - The Sage of the Highest Order
    
    In the writings of Zhuangzi, the Zhiren is one of the several terms used to describe an enlightened being, alongside Zhenren (True Person | Daoist Master).
    
    A 至人 is characterized by:
    
    - As the text states, "The Perfect Man has no self" (至人無己). They have transcended the ego and personal bias.
    - They do not struggle against the natural flow of the universe; they "ride the clouds" and remain unaffected by life and death.
    - They no longer distinguish between "this" and "that," "good" and "bad," or "useful" and "useless."
    
    "The Perfect Man is spirit-like. Were the great lakes burned, he would not feel the heat; were the Milky Way frozen, he would not feel the cold."
    
    -- **Zhuangzi**
