# Chapter 129: Rain

In the desert, within the convoy camp.

Li Qi was being watched with extreme wariness by a crowd of people. He had already been thoroughly searched to ensure he carried no weapons whatsoever.

Currently, he was surrounded by a group of people, with at least ten guns trained on him.

The only person not holding a gun was the middle-aged leader. He was scrutinizing Li Qi with a probing gaze, attempting to see right through this city dweller.

Yet, try as he might, he could detect absolutely no hint of fear or similar emotions from the other party.

Therefore, he abandoned his scrutiny and addressed Li Qi. "I'm warning you, if you don't produce the clean water you promised right here on this spot, I will bury you in the ground."

Following this, he sat down cross-legged. "Speak. What is it you want to ask?"

Seeing that the other party was finally willing to communicate, Li Qi breathed a sigh of relief. Fortunately, these people were still receptive to reason.

Thus, he opened his mouth and asked, "I want to ask, how long has this kind of life lasted for you all?"

"Pfft..." a young man scoffed disdainfully.

"Silence. My voice is the only one needed here. Go watch the perimeter and stay on guard," the middle-aged man frowned as he spoke to the youth.

The young man dared not say more, merely glaring unpleasantly at Li Qi before taking his gun outside to guard against potential enemies.

It was evident that hostility toward Li Qi still lingered here.

No, it wasn't hostility toward 'Li Qi', but rather hostility toward 'city dwellers'.

"How long? Since birth, obviously. Although we were over near the poles before, and only recently moved next to these satellite cities belonging to the Zhang family," the man answered.

"Satellite cities?" It was the first time Li Qi had heard this term.

"Don't you city dwellers call them that? I actually learned this name from your lot. Anyway, it refers to those few major cities, you know, the ones that orbit the earth oil extraction sites like satellites," the man replied.

"Since birth? Do you know exactly how long that has been?" Li Qi asked again.

"Probably since my grandfather's generation. When I was little, I heard my grandfather say that there used to be cities everywhere. These roads out here in the desert were all built back then. But starting from my grandfather's generation, they began to disappear."

"After that, there wasn't enough to support so many people. Only the people in the satellite cities could survive. As for us... we were just thrown outside," the man said in a casual tone.

It was clear he truly didn't care about these things. His only desire right now was to quickly finish answering the questions and receive the clean water this city dweller spoke of.

However, Li Qi could discern the horror hidden within.

Of all the people in the entire world, only those in the satellite cities survived? The rest... were all abandoned?

How many people... died?

The mere thought of it made his skin crawl.

No wonder this outside world looked like a complete wasteland...

"So you all are the descendants of the survivors from back then?" Li Qi inquired.

"Yeah, there's no other way. People have to survive, after all. But it's different for us than for you. There's no water out here, and nothing can grow. But thankfully, the satellite cities still need to exchange goods with each other, so we just live off your transport convoys," the man stated.

As he spoke these words, there seemed to be a subtle hint of threat in his voice.

"I see." Li Qi was not intimidated, merely committing this information to memory before continuing with his questions.

Seeing that Li Qi remained composed, the man curled his lip, not expecting this city dweller to be so bold.

"If that is the case, then where did your weapons come from? Even if you can steal them now, where did the very first weapons come from?" Li Qi asked again.

"That's something I can't tell you. But don't you even think about using this as an excuse not to give us water. As I said before, I won't answer every single one of your questions," the man emphasized.

"I understand. Then we'll skip this topic. By the way, have you ever heard of a rebel organization?" Li Qi continued with his next question.

To his surprise, the man's reaction to this question was explosive!

He instantly grabbed his gun, pressed it against Li Qi's forehead, and pulled the trigger without hesitation!

However, he was merely an ordinary man.

And Li Qi was faster, instantly dropping his head as the bullet grazed past in a heart-stoppingly close shave.

Fortunately, the ground consisted entirely of nutrient-deficient, loose soil, so the bullet embedded itself deep into the earth without causing a ricochet.

As their leader fired, the others reacted immediately, disengaging their safeties and preparing to execute Li Qi on the spot!

But Li Qi stood up at this moment, raising his hands and shouting loudly, "Hold on! I heard this news from Sha'er. I saved Sha'er's life!"

The man waved a hand, preventing the others from firing.

"Just who are you? What is your purpose?" He grabbed Li Qi by the collar, staring intently at him.

"I said, I saved Sha'er's life and heard about you drifters from outside from her own mouth! That is why I came to ask!" Li Qi immediately answered without the slightest hesitation.

"How do you prove it!" The man gripped Li Qi tightly, shouting his interrogation.

"By the very fact that I am standing here alone, am I not?" Li Qi stated.

"You had best produce the water right now, and then I will let you roll out of here alive. Otherwise, even if you have contingencies arranged, I will make sure they only recover your corpse!" The man threatened Li Qi.

Yet his attitude had already revealed many answers.

Sometimes, silence is an answer in itself.

Li Qi sighed. "Very well, I understand."

Suddenly, the man felt a blur before his eyes!

What followed was excruciating pain radiating throughout his body.

Li Qi's body twisted into an exaggerated angle as his feet executed several rapid steps. With a violent rotation, he shifted his center of gravity to his rear foot, his muscles subtly twitching as his entire being seemed to slither like a snake... no, rather, like a carp attempting to leap through the Dragon Gate, he suddenly surged forward in a preemptive attack! He effortlessly extended his body to the very limit where balance could still be maintained.

Almost everyone present was caught completely off guard, too slow to react. They were struck a single blow upon their gun-wielding hands, forced to retreat repeatedly.

The leader, bearing the brunt of the assault, was directly struck several times in quick succession, his entire body hunched over like a cooked shrimp, his face incredibly flushed as he nearly coughed up bile.

They simply hadn't expected Li Qi to be able to reach them from such a distance. He clearly seemed quite far away, yet his attack arrived before them almost instantaneously!

By concentrating his power and aiming precisely, Li Qi completed over a dozen attacks in a single flash. After striking everyone down, his body directly vaulted into the air and vanished in the blink of an eye.

When accelerating with full power, his rushing speed could approach the speed of sound for short durations, far exceeding what the human eye could distinctly perceive in the darkness!

And so, in an instant, Li Qi struck everyone down and fled.

He left behind only a group of astounded people.

Only now did they realize why this person possessed the courage to venture inside alone.

"Do not pursue!" enduring the pain, the man shouted loudly. "Never mind that! Pack everything up, we are relocating! Run!"

Pondering that person's purpose and origins could be left for later.

Running came first!

Ensuring no fatalities was paramount!

The group spent their entire lives scrambling through the desert, walking amidst death. Even the children understood the importance of swift actions.

Thus, within a few seconds, almost all the vehicles roared to life. Amidst a cacophony of engines, the entire camp rapidly evacuated.

"Damn it! We should have just opened fire immediately back then!" The man massaged the bruised and purple areas on his body, cursing loudly.

"How could a person possibly execute such movements? How could they run this fast!"

"Was that person... just like Sha'er, their body has been augmented?"

The crowd whispered amongst themselves, constantly speculating about that terrifying event over the roar of the car engines.

Was that kind of speed and power really something a human could possess?

He said he knew Sha'er; could it be true?

After all, looking at his power and speed, it really was similar to Sha'er.

The convoy roared forward through the night, the group fleeing in terror, continuously discussing Li Qi from earlier.

Especially the few children; besides fear, there was also the excitement of witnessing something incredible. They constantly attempted to mimic Li Qi's movements from just before.

The adults, however, were mostly panic-stricken, ceaselessly peering outside and aiming their weapons, petrified that the monster would pursue them.

As soon as the middle-aged leader felt slightly better physically, he immediately asked, "Number Two, he's not pursuing, is he?"

"No, but... Leader, there is a bit of a problem." Number Two's tone carried hints of panic.

"What kind of problem?"

"From the sky... water is falling?" Number Two's voice trembled slightly.

"How is that possible? Water only ever exists on the ground. How could it come down from the sky?" The middle-aged man frowned.

"It is real! Look!" Number Two pointed outside the window.

The leader raised his head.

He beheld small, pitter-pattering drops of water falling from the sky, dripping onto the car window.

The convoy fell into utter disarray, narrowly avoiding colliding with each other.

However, the people shifted from their previous panic into a collective silence.

"Stop the vehicles!" the man bellowed over the vehicle radio!

Then, without waiting for the car to come to a complete stop, he directly opened the door and charged out, bathing in the small water droplets falling from the sky.

He howled loudly. As a man already over fifty and the eldest in the entire convoy, he erupted with energy and a voice far surpassing what should be possible for his age.

"Everyone, stop quickly, get down, collect the water!"

"I remember now! My grandfather once said! Water falling from the sky, this, this, this thing is called 'rain'!"

A shower of rain fell upon this region.

Countless people rejoiced because of it.

---

And at this very moment.

The young girl known as Number One was walking within a satellite city.

She was beautiful, though only to the extent of being called beautiful—she hadn't reached the level of a peerless beauty, perhaps around the level of a school beauty.

Immediately after she spoke with Sha'er, she had rushed to this satellite city.

Because she had a premonition that she would encounter those two young masters here.

At that time, it would be the moment to take action.

Number One walked rapidly on the streets. Before the young masters made their move, she still had a few things to attend to.

They weren't particularly important matters, just something to relax her mind and body.

She arrived at a long-neglected and dilapidated area of the satellite city.

This place was still some distance from the city's edge, yet the filthy and foul-smelling environment forced people to cover their noses.

Narrow streets delineated a collection of squalid compounds in the open space.

Within these compounds and the rooms inside, many people lived—men, women, some clothed, others naked.

It was currently deep into the night. Some still had the energy to move, but many more were huddled together chaotically, sleeping.

Yes, they slept on the ground.

Most of these compounds were completely tattered and dilapidated, with piles of garbage accumulated everywhere.

The environment inside these compounds depended on how many of the residents had not yet grown accustomed to garbage and rot.

Every satellite city had places like this.

This was a den of vice, filth, and contagious diseases.

The corporations never cared about them. Most of these individuals had physical disabilities and were 'culled' from the earth oil extraction sites.

Yes, the corporations used the word 'culled'.

And they were the 'city dwellers' that the convoys outside simultaneously envied and feared.

Not all city dwellers had good lives; only those belonging to the corporations had normal food and water supplies.

Number One walked to this place.

A young girl appearing in such a place at night would likely not meet a good end.

But surprisingly, almost no one noticed her. She was like a ghost; even if she walked directly before their eyes, she would be overlooked.

"Sigh..." She looked at these people, then gazed far into the desert outside.

The corporations, and the Exalted Immortals...

They were the root of this world's chaos.

Number One walked to the center of this place, sat down, and then bit her finger, drawing blood.

A pale light circled her body. The qi drawn forth by her blood swirled around her, resembling swarming fireflies, yet also like a galaxy fallen into their midst.

Just like the galaxy she had created within the room before.

A refreshing qi spread outward.

Whether awake, asleep, or muddled, everyone suddenly felt their bodies become lighter, and it seemed their breathing had also become much smoother.

*With this, the sickly qi of this satellite city can be temporarily alleviated for a time, but... it cannot be eradicated.* Number One thought this to herself.

It was impossible to eradicate.

To eradicate it, the only way was to drive all the Exalted Immortals from this world.

Then, both corporations must be destroyed, order re-established, and rest slowly allowed, only then could this world recover.

As she silently dispelled the sickly qi, Number One suddenly raised her head and looked toward the distant horizon.

Those were... clouds?

Why would there be clouds?

Clouds should be the water qi of mountain ranges. The Earth Oil World no longer had such things. She had only heard of the existence of clouds and rain from other people from the outer realm.

Yet at this moment, black clouds truly appeared in the sky.

Then, water fell down.

Was that rain?

Why would such a thing appear?

Wait, Number One suddenly stood up, stopping her blood flow, her expression instantly becoming solemn.

"Young master...!" She grit her teeth!

It absolutely was the young master; only the young master could manipulate celestial phenomena to this degree!

Although she did not know why the opponent wanted to cause rain, clearly her premonition had come true.

Almost immediately, she pressed one hand against her temple and contacted the others. "The young master has appeared. At Satellite City Four, east side, approximately twenty li away."

After speaking, she actually flew straight up, soaring toward the place where it was raining.
