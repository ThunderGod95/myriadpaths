# Chapter 144: Divine Weapon

On the other side, that 'passing traveler' did not appear anxious at all.

Li Qi sat outside the inn, teasing the little fox, looking entirely relaxed.

Shen Shuibi, on the other hand, was bustling about. The rabbit was engraving talismanic seals all over the inn, setting up formations, burying various types of qi, and muttering continuously.

"Six inches per breath, up to two feet and four inches makes a stroke. Twenty-four feet, containing four _gui_. Two hundred and forty feet, six _zhu_... Yin gentle fire for one hundred and eight breaths, Wu-Ji fire for sixty-four, Jia-Bing fire for seventy-two... Ah, drew it wrong."[^1]

Then, in a flurry of motion, she clanked and chipped away the mistake to redraw it. She ran all over the building, making it so that no corner was peaceful.

Fortunately, she was very cute.

So no one had any complaints.

However, even if they did, no one would dare say anything.

Everyone knew that this seemingly cute and absent-minded girl was actually a hidden expert who had nearly slain a Rank 8 existence.

But in truth, there weren't many people around anyway—just the original staff and helpers. There wasn't a single guest left.

The entire inn had long since been cleared out. After returning that day, Piaoxu had politely asked every guest to leave one by one, compensating them in the process. Since then, she had refused to receive any new customers.

Even Piaoxu herself knew that her place would likely become the center of a vortex, so she quickly emptied the inn.

As for the guests, they had been eager to flee for a while.

Knowing that a hidden Rank 8 expert lived nearby... it was a terrible feeling, especially when she had offended someone powerful.

Therefore, the only guests left in the inn right now were Li Qi and Shen Shuibi.

Li Qi held a smooth-furred, red-haired little fox in his arms, saying, "Don't worry, Qi Ya. We know this whole affair started because of me. I won't just pat my butt and leave. Why are you worrying so much?"

This little fox he was teasing was named Qi Ya. She was Piaoxu's younger sister—not a blood relative, of course, but an awakened little fox that Piaoxu had picked up.

Although her spirit had awakened, she had not yet transformed into a demon. She still retained some habits of a wild beast and could not speak.

Demons didn't care about bloodlines; they considered those with sentience to be kin. Thus, their relationship was no different from that of real sisters.

It was said that those two from the Clear Mist Sect, the foxes Hu Xian'er and Hu Mei'er, weren't biological sisters either, because demons completely disregarded such things.

Hearing Li Qi's words, Qi Ya wagged her tail and softly bit his cuff, looking extremely happy.

Li Qi also quite liked this little red fox. As he petted her large, fluffy red tail, he pondered what to do next.

Li Qi hadn't actually been idle these past few days either.

Shen Shuibi was responsible for setting up defenses and the like, while he gathered quite a bit of information from the people who came to visit, either by asking them directly or using methods of the Shaman Dao.

First, Jin Buhuan's identity was repeatedly confirmed by multiple parties. Many visitors seeking an audience mentioned that Jin Buhuan was the most highly regarded son of the Green Mountain's mountain god.

This person was extravagant and domineering, with a capricious temper. Relying on his father's power and boasting of his status as a divine son, he kept countless concubines and lived a life of boundless debauchery. He frequently abducted women to be his concubines, sometimes never even touching them, doing it entirely for his own twisted amusement.

He also freely gave away women from his harem. During banquets he hosted, if a guest took a fancy to someone, they could take her away, and he wouldn't mind in the slightest.

Beyond this, he engaged in massive construction projects to build estates. In Clear Mist City alone, he owned three or four manors. Each was unimaginably luxurious, built with countless quantities of gold, jade, and an unknown number of heavenly materials and earthly treasures.

Yet, despite having so many estates, they were basically left vacant. Locked tight year-round and guarded only by a few old servants, he hadn't visited them even once in twenty years.

He also loved exquisite artifacts and enjoyed collecting beautiful jade. In short, whatever burned through money, he liked. He neglected any real work, leaving everything for his maidservants to handle. Every day, he was either banqueting, debauching in his harem, or participating in outside gambling rings. He indulged in sensual pleasures, extravagance, and lust—a textbook example of a second-generation heir's lifestyle.

Everyone who heard of him cursed him bitterly.

But Li Qi discerned a faint hint of something else from all this.

Even with such reckless squandering, he didn't seem to be getting poorer; in fact, he threw money around more and more lavishly.

Upon Li Qi's questioning, most of the people who visited him had cursed Jin Buhuan for using insidious methods to plunder and extort them, causing them heavy losses.

He also noticed that the majority of the city's wealthy merchants and magnates hadn't come to visit. Even the Clear Mist Sect acted as if they hadn't seen him at all, completely failing to show up.

These various signs made Li Qi sigh incessantly.

Although no one had a good word to say about him, with the help of these clues, Li Qi was still able to deduce a few things.

First, while Jin Buhuan was domineering, arrogant, and acted recklessly—even killing randomly and daring to incinerate a small market just in a momentary fit of rage—in reality... this person seemed highly capable. It was true that he squandered wealth limitlessly, but he certainly didn't lack in earning it.

No matter what he bought, he always used liquid assets, circulating money and currency.

Some tried to copy him, playing cards, gambling, collecting beautiful maids, boating on lakes, and engaging in all sorts of games. Any scion of the gentry who abandoned their studies to compete in such things would completely exhaust their family fortune within a few years. Yet, no matter how Jin Buhuan squandered, he only seemed to grow richer. There was no sign of his wealth being depleted.

This was because he never lightly touched his means of production—his true industries. Each one was perfectly preserved and never tapped into casually. His bottom line was incredibly clear.

Furthermore, this lifestyle of his had offended many people. That was why, when Li Qi merely had a minor clash with him, took a little advantage, and gained the upper hand in a single encounter—such a trivial matter—people immediately came to Li Qi's door, trying to prop him up and pull him to their side to oppose Jin Buhuan. It was obvious that these people had been at odds with Jin Buhuan for a long time.

However, there were other wealthy families and major merchants who stood firmly by his side.

Giving away concubines sounded debauched, but in reality, wasn't it truly just a form of currying favor and bribery?

He frequently gave in to sudden whims, running into various shops and throwing away thousands of gold coins in wild extravagance. But what if this was merely his way of expressing a stance?

And, no matter how one looked at it, the numerous industry chambers of commerce under Jin Buhuan's control firmly tied another group of people to his side.

He wasn't some despised, isolated second-generation heir; he was a genuine, major faction within Clear Mist City.

Lastly, there was the attitude of the Clear Mist Sect, which was extremely delicate.

The Green Mountain's mountain god was indeed Rank 6, but the local tyrants were ultimately the Clear Mist Sect. Hu Xian'er and Hu Mei'er were both Rank 7. Clear Mist City was also officially decreed and sealed by the Lord of the Green Mountain Kingdom. Here, even the mountain god of the Green Mountain likely couldn't act too brazenly.

Yet, up until now, there had been no news of the Clear Mist Sect reacting to any of Jin Buhuan's past behaviors. They neither showed favor nor made an enemy of him, adopting an attitude of total blindness.

What was the Clear Mist Sect thinking?

Layer upon layer of clues intertwined, preventing Li Qi from rashly sticking his neck out. Originally, he had wanted to just leave and go somewhere else, but since Piaoxu was involved, if he just walked away, she would undoubtedly die a graveless death.

This went against Li Qi's righteousness, preventing him from packing up and moving on.

"Hmm... I still need to keep observing. While ensuring my own safety, I'll see how Jin Buhuan reacts," Li Qi pondered for a long time before finally deciding to wait and see.

Now, he had one more goal in Clear Mist City.

That was to help Piaoxu escape danger.

Once he did that, he would quickly make a run for it.

No matter how powerful the local tyrants were here, they were still just local tyrants. Once he ran out of the Green Mountain Kingdom, he might never see them again in his lifetime.

"Old Ma's cultivation method, helping Piaoxu escape danger..." Li Qi silently recited his goals and committed them to memory.

Old Ma's cultivation method could wait. Cultivation methods were available everywhere; he didn't necessarily have to get one here.

Just as he was thinking about this, a massive sound suddenly erupted outside.

It wasn't a sudden explosion, but rather... musical instruments.

First drums beat, then horns blared, followed by more drumming, and finally a cacophony of singing and playing of strings and woodwinds. A jubilant melody rang out.

It scared the rabbit so much she shuddered all over and carved another mistake, forcing her to bitterly scrape it off and start over.

Setting up formations was a meticulous process. Unless one used pre-made dharma treasures like formation disks or formation flags that could be quickly adapted according to the time and terrain, one had to do what Shen Shuibi was doing: honestly survey the feng shui, examine the ley lines, determine the celestial phenomena, and then slowly lay out different formations based on the specific circumstances.

Although formations had things like diagrams and blueprints, those were essentially just formulas. Truly knowing how to apply them to a specific environment required a lot of hard work and study.

The rabbit buried her head and continued carving.

After hearing the music, Li Qi frowned, set Qi Ya aside, stood up, and walked out.

Piaoxu, who had also heard the noise, hurriedly came out, but Li Qi waved his hand, signaling her to go back inside.

Piaoxu had no choice but to step back, leaning against the doorframe to watch Li Qi walk out.

Just as he stepped outside, he was greeted by the sight of a whole convoy of carriages parked at the door!

Each carriage in the convoy was pulled by two date-red steeds. They were all top-tier spirit horses. The carriages behind them were loaded to the brim with large, red-lacquered leather trunks that looked incredibly heavy.

In the front, there was a grand palanquin, golden lanterns, towering torches, and offerings of fruits, vegetables, livestock, and wine, all attended to by servants forming a ring in front of the carriages.

Beside the spirit horses stood dozens of attendants. There were musicians singing and playing, creating a lively and boisterous atmosphere. With canopies scattered and red silk draped, it presented a thoroughly jubilant scene. If he didn't know better, Li Qi would have thought someone had come to propose marriage.

Li Qi walked out and looked up, spotting a ceremonial official standing at the door. Upon seeing Li Qi, the official immediately bowed low. "Greetings, Mister Li! I am a servant of the Jin family. I have come on the orders of the young master to offer gifts regarding the previous matter!"

Li Qi hadn't even processed this yet.

But after speaking, the official immediately straightened his posture. Without waiting for Li Qi to react, he ordered the servants beside him to unfurl a long scroll.

Two servants each grabbed one end of the long scroll, walking to the left and right, slowly rolling it out.

The red scroll was a full thirty feet long; it required two people holding each end to unfold, as one person could never manage it alone.

The ceremonial official walked to the far right and read aloud, "Today, we specifically offer to Mister Li—!"

"Gold, three thousand pounds!"

"Colored silk, six thousand bolts!"

"Spirit flowers, eight hundred blooms!"

"Xuande censers[^2], twelve pieces!"

"Calligraphy and paintings, fifteen carts!"

"Fresh fruits and vegetables, eight and a half carts!"

"Iron pear wood, thirty-three feet!"

"Red coral from the East Sea, eighteen feet!"

"Uncut jade stones, thirty pounds!"

"Beauty-shaped wine goblets, six pieces!"

"Mutated peonies 'One Foot of Snow', two flowers!"

"Insect-encased amber orbs, twenty pieces!"

"Emerald Ruyi scepters, twelve pieces!"

As he read each item, someone would step forward to open a trunk.

With every trunk opened, a precious light seemed to flash.

Within the trunks on the carts, a hundred treasures appeared at once. Pearls tumbled with amber, white snow produced smoke, coral formed trees, red clouds spat radiance, luminous pearls shone like stars, and emeralds seemed to spew azure flames together, like a thousand layers of fire. Countless rare treasures were piled up like a mountain; one could only see the glowing light they emitted, making it difficult to take in all the treasures within.

Li Qi was dumbfounded.

He had never seen such a... such a terrifyingly extravagant scene before.

This sight truly intimidated him.

_Is this what it means to be rich?_

However, it wasn't over yet. That gift list was a full thirty feet long; just reading it took a quarter of an hour.

The open treasure chests stretched halfway down the street.

Finally, the ceremonial official roared out once more: "A damaged Rank 8 metal qi innate divine weapon, one blade!"

The chest at the very back of the carriages was opened, revealing a long broadsword resting inside. The moment it was exposed, it felt as though blood and flesh were splattering all around, accompanied by the screams of slaughter and scattered severed limbs!

Immediately following, a thread of crystalline light pierced out from within the sea of blood. Enduring thunderstorms and endless chaos without shifting in the slightest, it pierced straight through the bloody sea.

Before this item, the multitude of treasures all completely lost their luster!

The countless precious lights from before were all thoroughly suppressed.

Even Li Qi was shocked.

Rank 8!?

Are you kidding me!

Is this something that can be given away as a gift!?

What kind of concept was Rank 8? Even in a massive city like Clear Mist City, with a transient population of tens of millions, how many Rank 8s could there be?

Li Qi estimated there were only about a hundred or two hundred of them.

In the entire Clear Mist City!

If Rank 9 was one in a thousand.

Then Rank 8 was one in a million!

'Each rank is a different heaven' was no joke. Every time one ascended a rank, it was as difficult as scaling the heavens.

A Rank 8 item and a Rank 9 item simply couldn't be mentioned in the same breath. Attempting to fight across realms was practically impossible, unless you were a big shot like Shen Shuibi who had fallen in rank and still held high-tier dharma treasures.

Li Qi boasted that he could fight dozens of ragtag Rank 9s without orthodox inheritances. But facing a ragtag Rank 8 who had self-taught their way up, he still only had one thought in mind: run away.

This was absolute suppression, an uncrossable chasm.

Back then, the first Ghostly God he had communicated with, that Old Man Li in the Great Deer Kingdom, was exactly that kind of extremely inferior Rank 8.

Yet his capabilities were enough to allow him to set up a scheme after his death, ensuring his memories continued to exist as he switched to cultivating the Ghost Dao. He could also easily curse a wealthy family into ruin and death, severing their providence.

Li Qi currently couldn't do any of that. He was still keeping the ghost qi Old Man Li had given him hidden as a trump card.

At this thought, Li Qi couldn't help but take two steps forward to look at that divine weapon.

It was a long broadsword.

Its sturdy body was like the spine of a snake, curving and tensed, exuding a fiercely furious aura.

The hilt was like grabbing scales and claws, fiercely unyielding. The spine bristled like needles, flaring out angrily like halberds, seemingly possessing blood and qi.

Just a single glance was enough to feel the sharpness hidden within!

[^1]: Shen Shuibi is calculating the "firing process" of her formation. Gui and zhu are ancient Chinese micro-measurements of weight, used in Daoist alchemy to represent precise increments of qi and time. Yin, Wu-Ji, and Jia-Bing refer to the Heavenly Stems and Earthly Branches, dictating the exact elemental nature (Wood, Earth, Fire) and astrological timing of the energy she is applying. "Gentle fire" refers to a slow, steady output of energy, measured in "breaths" (cycles of inhalation and exhalation).
[^2]: Named after the Xuande Emperor of the Ming Dynasty, these are legendary bronze incense burners. Historically, they were crafted from prized imported copper that was smelted up to twelve times to achieve absolute purity, resulting in a flawless, gold-like luster. In this context, they represent an antique of unimaginable value and the ultimate status symbol.
