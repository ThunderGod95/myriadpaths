# Chapter 115: Scrambling

"That will not do. You have made a great contribution this time, and it would be against the rules not to reward you. Although I cannot bestow a reward upon you on behalf of my true self, I still must show some token of appreciation," the star said.

"While your talent is absolute, you are still young—barely in your early twenties—and your cultivation is shallow. A lavish reward would be inappropriate. I also cannot think of a fitting gift on the spot. Why don't you tell me what you desire?"

The Grand Diviner tossed the choice to Li Qi.

He seemed quite relaxed.

But Li Qi was breaking out in a cold sweat.

_If I say something that displeases him, will I die?_

However, he needed to answer immediately. If he hesitated and overthought it, the other party might feel he wasn't speaking from the heart, but rather carefully calculating how to curry favor.

Therefore, Li Qi only weighed his words for a moment before bowing. "This junior has no other wishes. I only ask to trouble the Grand Diviner to inform my teacher that I have not failed my mission and have successfully found Lady Luofu. That will be enough."

After saying this, Li Qi subtly stole a glance at the Grand Diviner.

Unfortunately, the other party was currently just a star. It lacked any facial features, making it impossible to read his expression.

"Very well. What is your teacher's name?" the Grand Diviner asked.

_Crap._

Sweat beaded on Li Qi's forehead.

_What was Young Master Zhu's name again?_

_I have no idea._

However, he quickly recovered and offered up the willow branch. "I dare not ask my teacher's taboo name, nor do I dare address him directly. But this willow branch was gifted by my teacher. The Grand Diviner should be able to recognize it."

The Grand Diviner took one look at the willow branch and swayed slightly. "Oh, it's that brat. He actually took a disciple? Are you an apprentice, or an inner disciple?"

"Naturally, I am an apprentice," Li Qi replied.

"Makes sense. That brat has never had a disciple before. Even as an apprentice, you are the first. Alright, I have already notified him. Now, I will be taking Luofu and leaving."

Before leaving, he asked Shen Shuibi once more, "Moon Essence, are you... are you going to return together with Luofu?"

"Lady Luofu should be... fine now, right? I hope the Grand Diviner can inform me how long it will take for her true spirit to awaken," Shen Shuibi asked in return.

"Naturally, she is fine. Do you think my Shaman God Mountain cannot even protect Luofu? As for the awakening of her true spirit, it will take at least ten years, and at most a century. We will have to slowly plan for that once we return," the Grand Diviner answered proudly.

Hearing this answer, Shen Shuibi glanced at Li Qi and whispered, "Li Qi, do you want to return to Shaman God Mountain to cultivate as well?"

"My cultivation has scattered, and I have to start over from the beginning anyway. Where I cultivate doesn't make much of a difference," Shen Shuibi said softly, stealing glances at Li Qi as she spoke. "If you are returning to Shaman God Mountain, we can go back together. If you are not going, then I... still want to stay by Lady Luofu's side and wait for her to awaken."

Hearing this, what else could Li Qi think?

_If I don't go to Shaman God Mountain now, am I supposed to wait until I'm dead to go?_

_Since I've taken a master, returning to the sect is perfectly normal, right?_

Li Qi hurriedly said, "Grand Diviner, could you bring me back to the sect as well?"

"It matters not. Decide amongst yourselves." The Grand Diviner appeared quite casual, not putting on any airs.

However, right at that moment, a voice echoed out.

"Who said you could go back? Is he your teacher, or am I?" Young Master Zhu's body emerged from the void, still retaining that scholarly appearance.

The Grand Diviner had only just notified him, yet he had instantly appeared here.

"Young Master," Li Qi immediately bowed.

He didn't dare call him teacher, so he still used 'Young Master'.

"You completed the mission quite well. It's just that wanting to return to the mountain right now is a bit spineless," Young Master Zhu said to Li Qi.

As soon as he appeared, the gazes of everyone present uniformly turned toward him.

Before anyone could respond, Young Master Zhu looked at the Grand Diviner and said in dissatisfaction, "Old ghost, what is the meaning of this? Taking my student back to the mountain now—are you trying to ruin his future?"

"You have a sharp tongue, boy. This old man won't argue with you. He is your student, after all. Do as you wish" Upon seeing Young Master Zhu, the Grand Diviner simply left.

_He left..._

_Hey, you left Shen Shuibi and Yang Ning behind! And what do you mean by ruining my future?!_

Li Qi, Shen Shuibi, and Yang Ning all wore expressions of utter bewilderment.

_Is he really that unreliable? How could he just leave?!_

"Alright, you two idlers disperse. I need a few words alone with my student." With a wave of Young Master Zhu's hand, Li Qi felt his body lose control.

In an instant, the surrounding scenery abruptly changed, transforming into the chaotic space he had seen within the altar.

Shen Shuibi and Yang Ning had vanished, leaving only Li Qi and a smiling Young Master Zhu.

Li Qi respectfully lowered his head, waiting for the other party to speak.

"Mm, well done. Although you are a person of the Outer Dao and I couldn't deduce what you were up to during this time, being able to find Lady Luofu and summoning the Grand Diviner while enduring the pressure of two Rank 6 demons shows that you truly put your heart into it. Your capabilities are not bad either. No, since you haven't even entered the ranks yet, I should say you are remarkably outstanding," Young Master Zhu praised with a smile.

"Young Master praises me too highly." Li Qi naturally had to be modest.

"No need to call me Young Master. You may call me teacher now."

"Disciple greets Teacher!" Li Qi immediately bowed deeply, shouting without a hint of hesitation.

_I've finally latched onto this thick thigh!_

Calmly accepting Li Qi's bow, Young Master Zhu continued, "Do you know why I wouldn't let you return directly to Shaman God Mountain?"

Li Qi naturally didn't know, so he answered truthfully, "Disciple does not know."

"If you return to Shaman God Mountain now, because of my seniority, you wouldn't cultivate slowly like an ordinary disciple. Instead, you would directly enter the inner mountain. Moreover, with the Moon Essence Shen Shuibi by your side, you would attract far too much attention. Your current cultivation is insufficient; if you go back, you'll be beaten to death. Even if you survive, your Dao Heart might collapse, turning you into a cripple," Young Master Zhu explained.

Li Qi sweated nervously and boldly asked, "Teacher, dare I ask... are your interpersonal relations very poor?"

Young Master Zhu's expression didn't change. "There are too many mediocre people in Shaman God Mountain. It's completely normal that they don't get along with me."

Li Qi wiped his forehead.

_Right, understood._

_You've offended too many people. As your student, with my inadequate level, going back would easily lead to trouble..._

"And then? What arrangements does Teacher have for me?" Li Qi inquired.

_As a teacher, you have to arrange something, right?_

"Of course I do! This place is roughly three million li away from Shaman God Mountain. I will give you a scroll of spell arts. You will walk all the way back to Shaman God Mountain from here, and finish studying the scroll along the way. Once you return, you naturally won't fear them anymore!" Young Master Zhu clapped his hands, speaking with immense pride. "Such an arrangement is very safe for you."

Li Qi was dumbfounded upon hearing this.

_Sir, walking 1.5 million kilometers on foot... Old Ma travels 800 li a day, which means it will take ten years._

_Are you trying to kill me?_

"Why are you looking at me with that expression? Could it be that you'll only ever be able to travel 800 li a day? Can't you improve? Enough, it's settled," Young Master Zhu finalized the decision upon seeing Li Qi's face.

"Rest assured, how could I really make you walk for ten years? If nothing goes wrong, after you enter the ranks in the first year, you'll be able to ride the wind. Once you reach Rank 8, you'll cover this distance in a year or two."

"Teacher, do you think I can reach Rank 8 in two or three years? I've heard along the way that some people are trapped at Rank 9 for their entire lives..." Li Qi was speechless at first, then said this to Young Master Zhu.

"That's some people, not you. With the aid of the Outer Dao dao rhythm, how could Rank 8 be difficult for you?" Young Master Zhu raised an eyebrow, completely unconcerned.

Li Qi, on the other hand, felt like he was about to dehydrate.

"Outer Dao... dao rhythm?" he repeated, his voice somewhat dry and hoarse.

"What? You haven't noticed it? I can definitely feel that it's there." Young Master Zhu leaned in and reached out his hand to grab at the air.

Li Qi immediately felt his vision fill with static.

The progress bars he had grown so accustomed to suddenly began flashing violently!

Then, they were abruptly and forcefully ripped right out of his field of vision!

"Do you feel any change? Look, this is your Outer Dao dao rhythm. It's a trace of innate dao rhythm you accidentally acquired when crossing the Great Thousand Worlds. It generally exists in a form that aligns with your own thoughts. It can be considered a decent fortuitous encounter, capable of demonstrating miraculous effects for anyone below Rank 6. Once you reach Rank 5 and can cross realms, this kind of thing won't amount to much. By then, it will be available at your fingertips," Young Master Zhu casually explained, grasping a cluster of something completely invisible to Li Qi.

Following that, he patted it forward. Li Qi felt another wave of disorientation. When he opened his eyes again, everything was restored.

_Right, confirmed._

_My golden finger really isn't worth a damn. It can just be casually kneaded like this._

_Weren't other people's golden fingers supposed to be the kind that even the most top-tier big shots fall prey to? How come the very essence of mine was analyzed by someone else, and even evaluated by my teacher as 'available at your fingertips'?_

_A fortuitous encounter... my hidden cheat is actually nothing more than an ordinary fortuitous encounter._

_Sure enough, there's no such thing as a protagonist in this world._

"I will strictly obey Teacher's decree. I will depart from here and hurry to Shaman God Mountain," Li Qi replied respectfully.

"Don't panic, I haven't given you your reward yet." Young Master Zhu stopped Li Qi.

"Although I don't know what you experienced on the road this time, to actually face two Rank 6 demon kings head-on and endure it... not bad. You did extremely well, truly worthy of being my student," Young Master Zhu praised.

_You say that as if you've ever taught me anything..._

Li Qi slandered internally.

_Thud._ Li Qi's head was suddenly struck by a rock, making him dizzy and nearly causing him to lose his footing.

However, he wasn't injured.

"Slandering your teacher deserves a beating. Although I haven't taught you, you still can't say it out loud, nor can you even think it in front of me. Did you not know? To a cultivator of Rank 7 or above, your inner thoughts are like loud shouting right next to my ear. If you ever dare to think like that in front of someone Rank 7 or above again, you might not make it back to Shaman God Mountain. Consider this a lesson," Young Master Zhu said lackadaisically.

Saying so, he reached out his hand.

Instantly, the chaotic space directly split apart!

"When heaven and earth first opened, neither men nor objects existed between them; thus, it was called chaos."

"Later, the light and pure ascended, bringing forth the sun, the moon, the stars, and the constellations. Therefore, the sun, moon, stars, and constellations are called the Four Phenomena," Young Master Zhu murmured.

As soon as his voice fell, the primal, chaotic sky all around abruptly cleared. The sun, moon, stars, and constellations hung together in the sky, orbiting the earth!

Then, the sun, moon, stars, and constellations collapsed once more, each transforming into a wisp of pure qi that fell into his hands.

"I bestow upon you the qi of these Four Phenomena: sun, moon, stars, and constellations. You get one wisp of each, and each has its wondrous uses. Study them thoroughly, and you might reap some gains. You can also use them directly when facing danger; they can help you escape a life-and-death crisis four times, providing some assurance for your journey," Young Master Zhu said to Li Qi, then waved his hand.

Four wisps of pure qi surged into Li Qi's body and lay dormant within.

Having done this, Young Master Zhu drifted away, leaving Li Qi with only his back.

He actually left without even saying goodbye.

Li Qi stood in the chaotic space, respectfully bowing toward the retreating figure for a full five minutes.

Then, he raised his head, and the words he had been holding back for so long finally bubbled up.

_Damn it, there's a reason your interpersonal relations are so poor!_

_He should be gone by now, right? He didn't hear that, right?_

_This teacher is truly unreliable!_

---

However.

Young Master Zhu wasn't completely unreliable.

As a reward for Li Qi handling the matter well, and to ensure a smooth journey, Young Master Zhu had given Li Qi four wisps of divine qi derived from the sun, moon, stars, and constellations. They should be quite formidable.

Furthermore, along with these four wisps of divine qi, Li Qi had also received his master's lineage. Young Master Zhu had given him spell arts; a new book appeared in his mind, filled with various spell arts. There were a full one hundred and twenty of them, presumably what he was meant to learn over the next ten years.

Subsequently, Li Qi left the chaotic space and returned to White Snake Mountain.

Standing on the mountain, there was no one around.

It seemed Shen Shuibi and Yang Ning had already gone back.

"But... I suppose this counts as a good thing?" Li Qi smiled bitterly, muttering to himself.

Compared to those lofty immortal figures, he was truly as lowly as dust. To even form a connection, he had to thank that cheap teacher of his, Young Master Zhu.

He didn't seem to have told him his name yet.

Whatever, calling him 'Teacher' would do.

After this, he would have to walk to Shaman God Mountain all by himself.

Wait, no, he still had Old Ma. A brother was still the most thoughtful. Unlike women, who just left the moment they said they would.

Wait, where was Old Ma?

_Where the hell is Old Ma?!_

Li Qi panicked. He looked around and found that Old Ma was truly gone!

_Now I'm really screwed._

_I won't actually have to walk 1.5 million kilometers on foot, will I?_

_Damn it, the distance between the Earth and the moon is only 300,000 kilometers. I have to walk the equivalent of five round trips between the Earth and the moon on my own two legs?!_

Li Qi hastily searched everywhere for Old Ma. He hadn't fallen off the mountain, had he?

Just as he looked down, he saw Old Ma carrying Shen Shuibi, slowly walking up from below.

Shen Shuibi was still the same, wearing a dazed expression. Her entire being exuded an ethereal quality, as if she didn't belong to this mortal world.

Li Qi looked at Old Ma, then up at Shen Shuibi.

The two stared at each other in dismay.

Then Old Ma trotted over gleefully, nuzzling Li Qi and snorting, seemingly very happy that Li Qi was safe.

Li Qi also noticed that Old Ma seemed to have grown some scales around his mane?

But that wasn't important. The important thing was that Shen Shuibi was still here.

"Miss Shen... why didn't you return to Shaman God Mountain with Miss Yang Ning?" Li Qi suppressed his emotions and asked politely.

"Ah... well, I asked that Grand Invocator how he planned to deal with you." Shen Shuibi seemed a bit embarrassed. Sitting on the horse, she twirled a strand of hair around her finger and said, "He said he planned to make you walk back to Shaman God Mountain as a form of tempering."

"So I asked how long it would take to walk."

"He said roughly four or five years."

"Then, he said it didn't seem too long. Lady Luofu needs ten to a hundred years to awaken anyway, so he suggested I delay going to Shaman God Mountain and simply walk back with you. It wouldn't really hold anything up, right?" Shen Shuibi didn't quite know how to explain it, making her logic a bit muddled.

"I thought about it and felt it made sense, so I agreed."

Having finished, Shen Shuibi looked at Li Qi.

"I see." Li Qi, on the other hand, nodded calmly after hearing this. "Then let us depart."

_Calm my foot! Slandering Teacher just now was entirely my fault! You are truly my great benefactor! If others can't get along with you, it's definitely their fault!_

"Mm!" Shen Shuibi nodded. The two looked at each other in silence.

But then they both smiled. Li Qi led the horse with Shen Shuibi sitting atop it, and the two headed down the mountain together.

Li Qi didn't know what to say. His mind felt muddled, probably knocked silly by Young Master Zhu, completely lacking his usual sharpness and wit.

He didn't even ask why Old Ma had grown scales. However, thinking about it, Old Ma must have received some benefits too. Young Master Zhu wouldn't be that stingy, right?

_Right?_

They continued down the mountain just like that. Halfway down, they spotted flags fluttering in the distance. It was actually a convoy of carriages!

The main carriage was extremely gorgeous, pulled by four horses, all of which were eight-foot dragon colts. It was adorned with bound silks, cinnabar, lacquers, silk threads, cotton wadding, varying sizes of bamboo, and gold.

As the saying goes, 'four horses make a chariot'. This could be considered a full chariot, befitting the etiquette of a high minister.

Furthermore, an entire fleet of carriages trailed behind, forming a complete ceremonial procession. It looked as though they were specifically waiting for a distinguished VIP.

"What is that?" Li Qi raised his head, his gaze regaining its clarity.

Stimulated by the outside world, his lost IQ immediately reclaimed the high ground.

"Looking at those flags, it seems to be the Governor's carriage. Wait, the Governor of White Snake Prefecture?" Li Qi frowned, unable to make up his mind for a moment.

But before he could decide, a voice suddenly drifted over.

The voice rang in his ears out of thin air, speaking thus, "This humble official has personally come to welcome the Young Master. Today, my humble prefecture was graced by the presence of the Grand Diviner and the Grand Invocator. I am deeply ashamed that my arrangements of wine and food were crude, and my welcoming etiquette was negligent. I had no face to ask the two esteemed figures to stay. However, seeing that the Young Master is still here and has not departed, I believe the Young Master must possess innate compassion and a foundation of goodness. With your benevolent heart and magnanimous tolerance, I trust you will not fault me for this. Therefore, I have specially prepared a modest banquet here, daring to invite the Young Master and the gentle lady to take a seat!"

Hearing this, Li Qi understood.

_Well then, Teacher's face is truly enormous._

He had merely passed by without even staying, yet he had already scared the Governor of White Snake Prefecture silly. The Governor must have rushed here madly just to greet him.

Thus, Li Qi spoke up, "The Governor does not need to go to such lengths. The two of us are merely passing through. Let us forgo the banquet!"

Just as he finished saying that, Shen Shuibi suddenly leaned down and told him, "This kind of banquet will have a lot of heavenly materials and earthly treasures..."

Li Qi blinked upon hearing this.

At that moment, the voice spoke again, "The Young Master need not be so modest. I imagine the Young Master is heading to Shaman God Mountain. Why not ride with this humble official? Not only will it be faster, but it will also allow me to fulfill my duties as a host and express my sincerity!"

Listening to the other party's tone, Li Qi couldn't help but sigh in awe.

_Is this the feeling of having backing?_

Even though it seemed Shaman God Mountain was at war with the Tang Empire, which made him quite fearful, in this territory governed by the Shaman Dao, having a Grand Invocator as a teacher instantly changed his entire standing.

However, Li Qi was very self-aware. He knew the other party didn't respect him, but rather feared his backer.

Therefore, he didn't dare to be too arrogant. Added to Shen Shuibi's reminder, he softened his tone and said, "Since the Governor has prepared in advance, then this junior would be disrespectful to decline!"

"Haha, why does the Young Master say such things? Being able to travel with the Young Master is this old man's good fortune," the Governor's voice replied.

Li Qi twitched his mouth.

_A moment ago it was 'this humble official', and now it's 'this old man'. These people really know how to push their luck._

But since he had made his decision, there was no need for further pleasantries.

Li Qi waited in place.

Not long after, the carriages came up the mountain.

It was only then that Li Qi saw that this carriage was truly... massive.

Rather than a carriage, it was more like they had carried a fifty-square-meter studio apartment up the mountain.

Four dragon colts, slightly shorter than Old Ma, pulled the cart, with beasts of burden supporting it from below. The carriage itself was inscribed with floral talismans and patterns that seemed to channel the presence of certain qi, keeping this ornate room incredibly stable.

An honor guard followed behind, their ranks strict and clear, setting up camps and tents. Some held flags, some carried musical instruments, some bore umbrellas, and some danced with fans. They were equipped with pennants, dragon umbrellas, and ceremonial robes. Banners fluttered, cheers filled the road, and they were adorned in pearls, jade, and splendid brocades. Their attire was exquisitely fashioned, playing elegant music and singing, bright and orderly.

Further back were soldiers wielding bows, arrows, sabers, lances, crossbows, and axes. They wore lacquered leather armor and used bone for arrowheads. Each stood nine zhang tall with heroic countenances. They marched in strict formation, stirring up dark dust and thick fog as they moved, looking immensely awe-inspiring.

Just a single glance was enough to reveal the richness of the Governor's prestige and the sheer extravagance of his glory.

Another official led the way to Li Qi. He stopped the carriage, lowered it, and opened the door. "Please, Young Master and gentle lady, board the carriage and travel with the Governor!"

Li Qi asked, "What about my horse?"

"The Young Master's mount will naturally be handled by the grooms. There is no need to worry."

Hearing this, Li Qi helped Shen Shuibi dismount. Without bothering with luggage, he held his head high and walked into the carriage.

Upon entering, Li Qi felt a wave of dizziness.

_Damn it, how is this a studio apartment? It's a massive villa inside!_

The interior of the carriage was vastly larger than the exterior. From the outside, it looked like a fifty-square-meter room, but inside, it was a magnificently carved multi-story pavilion.

Dazzling crystal light filled the interior. Li Qi felt as if he had stepped into a grand hall. Inside stood a redwood tree over ten meters tall, its branches dotted with pearl flowers, jade flowers, and agate flowers—a feast for the eyes.

Looking around, the lattice windows were partially hidden, and railings encircled the area. The five-colored crystal light was dazzling, and the myriad jewels each displayed their own bizarre wonders.

Layers upon layers of emerald green light shone, accompanied by various breathtaking flowers and plants. One couldn't even see to the end of it with a single glance.

As he walked inside, over a dozen maids bowed respectfully, calling out, "Respectfully welcoming the Young Master!"

More than a dozen beauties, each possessing elegant and exceptional looks, breathed fragrant breaths and smiled with blooming faces. Wearing red skirts and blue capes, they exuded a sweet aroma. With every shift of their gaze, it was as if flowers filled the entire air.

Looking further into the hall, there was a long table already full of people. Some were civil officials, some were military generals, and some didn't look like they held official posts, likely belonging to various cultivation sects.

Arrayed on the long table were numerous rare delicacies emitting mouth-watering aromas. Maids stood by, serving gracefully.

Above all of this sat a desk. A middle-aged man with a dignified face and official robes sat at the head of the long table. He reached out to invite Li Qi, "Come, Young Master, please take a seat!"

Li Qi, however, stayed where he was, unmoving.

To be honest, he was stunned by this scene.

However, Li Qi had seen countless CG movie effects in his time, so he had at least a shred of resistance to such fantastical scenes. He was only dazzled for a split second before returning to normal the moment the Governor of White Snake Prefecture spoke to him.

"Many thanks for the Governor's invitation." Li Qi cupped his hands in greeting. Then, leading Shen Shuibi, he sat at the other end of the long table, facing the Governor from across the table in a very natural manner.

As Li Qi sat down, the others also chimed in one after another.

"For the Young Master to personally attend this banquet truly brings us boundless joy!"

"I once read about the might of the Grand Invocator in ancient texts. Seeing it today, it truly shakes the soul!"

"Those two demons, the curse demon and bear demon, have occupied White Snake Mountain for a long time. Fortunately, your esteemed master eliminated them, truly saving the millions of residents of White Snake Prefecture from their dire suffering!"

All sorts of flattering words rushed toward Li Qi.

This scene was like a mayor inspecting a neighborhood committee.

Fortunately, Li Qi could still handle it. Exchanging pleasantries, he said, "I am a newcomer here and do not yet know the names of the esteemed gentlemen of White Snake Prefecture. Could you enlighten me so that we might interact in the future?"

"Why must the Young Master refer to himself so humbly? How are we supposed to conduct ourselves if you do? I am Qin You, the Bandit-Suppressing Inspector of White Snake Prefecture," one person took the lead and said.

"Exactly. If the Young Master speaks humbly, what face do we have to sit here? Wouldn't someone like me, who holds no official position, feel even more awkward? My North Gate Gang merely handles some logistics." Someone echoed, awkwardly slipping in their identity.

In a flash, Li Qi was subjected to an even greater barrage of introductions.

He navigated the conversations smoothly, responding appropriately. Even if he made an occasional breach of etiquette, no one would mind.

With everyone lifting the sedan chair and adding fuel to the fire, after such a scene, even though Li Qi was a trash cultivator who hadn't even entered the ranks, at this moment, he truly felt like some distinguished noble.

Shen Shuibi shrank to the side. With her cultivation entirely lost, unless someone had sufficient observational skills or a unique detection technique, it was very difficult to discover that her true essence was as high as Rank 5.

She was very beautiful, but right now she was cowering and looked timid. Without any accessories or makeup, compared to the beautiful maids, she was rather inconspicuous, merely taken by the others to be a maid or a similar attendant of Young Master Li Qi.

Therefore, no one paid her any attention, just as no one bothered with the surrounding servant girls.

This gave her the opportunity to observe her surroundings.

However, the person she looked at the most was still Li Qi.

Even though Li Qi was currently surrounded by a crowd and seemed to be floating on air, Shen Shuibi could sense that he was deeply self-aware. Although he was being elevated, his gaze remained clear, and he hadn't let it go to his head.

This made Shen Shuibi quite happy.

At least he was better than his previous state of looking like he'd been smashed by a rock.

Still, Shen Shuibi would occasionally glance up at the Governor of White Snake Prefecture.

Apart from inviting Li Qi at the very beginning, he hadn't done anything else. He just sat up there watching the pleasantries below.

_Mm, Li Qi is still inexperienced. Now they'll underestimate him, won't they? If Li Qi pays too much attention to the people below, he'll appear somewhat frivolous._

Shen Shuibi pondered these things in her mind.

But it didn't really matter. There was no need to have any deep interactions with these people to begin with.

After conversing like this for a while, Li Qi grew a bit tired.

These people were certainly enthusiastic about forging connections, but enthusiasm was just that—no one was actually pulling out money or offering gifts.

Chatting for a bit was fine, but a whole crowd chattering endlessly was truly becoming irritating.

Fortunately, everyone here was shrewd. They quickly let Li Qi be, not wanting to become the target of his annoyance.

Seeing this, the Governor clapped his hands. "How can we just idle chat? Let us eat first, and we can talk while we eat!"

The crowd voiced their agreement in succession, and the banquet commenced.

Following a gesture from the Governor, the waiting maids sprang into action, serving dishes and pouring wine to attend to the esteemed guests.

Besides the serving maids, a row of female performers flooded out. Fully dressed and heavily made up, they were like weeping willows and flying petals. As far as the eye could see, every one of them possessed a delicate countenance, as beautiful as celestial maidens.

Dozens of dancers bowed in unison with respectful postures, then stood divided into groups. Each wore long-sleeved palace dresses, their figures graceful and enchanting. Moving with light, lotus-like steps, their delicate voices were melodious.

Once settled, they slowly began to play tunes and dance gracefully. Seven or eight performers moved like butterflies fluttering through flowers, each performing their own part.

First came the musicians, a row of beauties. Those who played strings played, and those who played winds blew. Straightening their sleeves, they slowly extended their slender hands—playing jade flutes, striking sandalwood clappers, and strumming jasper zithers. The melodious songs harmonized perfectly, the decadent sounds lingering endlessly in the ears.

Then came the dancers. Upon hearing the elegant music filling the air, the dancing women twirled their fluttering skirts in clusters. Lifting their robes and rolling up their sleeves, they appeared and disappeared in the dance, their bright laughter ringing out clearly.

Occasionally, the cries of bluebirds harmonized with the elegant music. Looking at the venue, the young maidens danced wonderfully, as if drifting on the wind.

When the maiden dancers drew near, at times they exuded a chilling sensation across their entire bodies, like ice jade in the height of summer, helping to quench the fire three inches below the navel—only, the cooler it was, the more fiercely the fire raged.

At other times, their skin was incredibly warm, like the breath of spring, causing a surging fire of passion to flare up uncontrollably.

The maidens were like petrels darting through flowers, while the myriad treasures also radiated brilliant splendor, dancing alongside them. Bathed in the lamplight, tens of thousands of rosy rays rippled along with them. Countless lamps burned, creating a sight too beautiful to behold, like descending stars, manifesting as five-colored clouds, flickering incessantly.

Music echoed all around. The beauties and the countless previously arranged treasures moved in tandem, like a sea of crystal, dazzling the eyes so much that one wouldn't know where to look.

Suddenly, the giant coral in the center of the hall began to sway, and countless flower buds blossomed upon it.

The radiant flowers bloomed, a mixture of five colors, each as large as a bushel.

Within each blooming flower, a beauty appeared in the center, adopting various postures. Some played jade flutes, some strummed jasper zithers, some blew jade fifes. In an instant, a fragrant breeze permeated the air, and flowers covered the ground. Among them, butterflies fluttered like orioles, dancing amidst the blossoms, as radiant as a pile of brocade.

In an instant, the entire hall looked as if special effects had been applied to it. Spots of light danced, petals scattered, and butterflies weaved through it all.

Looking closely at these butterflies, they were actually tiny, exquisite beauties. Carved from jade and powder, apart from their size and the butterfly wings on their backs, they were no different from humans. Incredibly beautiful and entirely unclad, they darted and dodged in the air, making it hard to see them clearly, yet rousing a deeper desire to grab them, play with them in one's hands, and clearly examine their bodies.

Li Qi watched in absolute astonishment.

At this time, a maid beside him timely explained, "Young Master, please look. This item was crafted by the Tang Empire's Reason Dao and originates from the East Sea. It is called the 'Mirage Tree'. It can project lifelike illusions and is perfectly suited for entertaining at banquets."

Li Qi nodded, indicating he understood.

_This thing... if brought to the battlefield, wouldn't it be an active holographic camouflage?_

_For the Governor to use it just for banquet entertainment feels like a bit of a waste._

Beyond these, the myriad of delicious delicacies on the table also left his eyes dizzy with choices.

This made Li Qi sigh internally.

_The boat trackers and these high-ranking officials truly live in completely different worlds._

He appreciated the song and dance while savoring the fine food.

Li Qi realized that he had never seen most of the food on the table before...

Even before the banquet started, the throngs of servants had already set the tableware. It was very ancient and classical. There were no plates or such things; instead, there were _jue_ (wine vessels), _zun_ (goblets), _zu_ (meat stands), and _shang_ (drinking cups) that Li Qi often saw used in sacrificial rites.

In front of everyone's table, there was even a small bronze tripod. Inside the tripod boiled an unknown meat, exuding a tangy, exotic aroma.

The desks were covered entirely in goldware and lacquerware.

Carved red lacquerware with exquisite craftsmanship, sporting wooden cores and red bases embellished with gold leaf.

As for the food, it was truly a myriad of delicacies and exotic dishes.

The meats Li Qi recognized—such as beef, mutton, chicken, duck, pork, fish, donkey, and horse—were all available.

Besides those, there were many more items that tasted even better but were completely unidentifiable. They must be some rare ingredients.

But just looking at the organs on these creatures—some with wings and feet, some with fins and scales—it was obvious they came from every sort of habitat.

This banquet was clearly a gathering of everything that flew in the sky, ran on the earth, and swam in the water, braising the beautiful bounties of the four seas and five lakes into a culinary masterpiece.

The deliciousness of the delicacies was beyond words. Maids took turns filling the cups, wine and fruits were arrayed, fine brews filled the desks, and clear songs and wonderful dances made it feel like the life of immortals.

However, Li Qi politely declined the maids who tried to cozy up to him to pour his wine and serve his food.

He still felt it wasn't quite right. Besides, Shen Shuibi was sitting right beside him.

Catching his gaze, the maid understood perfectly and quickly withdrew, leaving space for Shen Shuibi.

Shen Shuibi didn't stand on ceremony. Sitting beside Li Qi, she began instructing him on how to eat.

When Li Qi picked up a piece of unknown meat, she would explain.

"This is the Fei Yi. It looks like a quail, with a yellow body and a red beak. Eating it can kill Gu insects in the body. You travel through the mountains and have likely picked up some. Eating this to dispel hidden ailments is a good idea."

"Don't eat that. That's Dan Wood fruit. It tastes very good, but eating just one will make you full for three days. Save it for the end."

"This is a sauce made from Pin Grass. You can dip your food in it. Eating it makes one unable to feel sorrow. It's placed at banquets in case someone is in a bad mood, but don't eat too much, or you might lose your composure."

"This is the Qi. Eating its meat prevents nightmares and repels fierce, wicked qi. Eat more of it. You were invaded by the wicked qi of those two Rank 6 demons, and I worry there might be lingering effects."

"That round one—yes, that's the one. That's the fruit of the Cup Tree. If a person eats it, it increases their physical strength. You have Martial Dao cultivation, so you can eat more of it."

Li Qi listened while using his chopsticks, and sometimes a knife and fork, exclusively picking out the useful things to eat.

He didn't just eat by himself. He also served Shen Shuibi whatever she wanted. This caused the crowd at the banquet to unanimously praise him for his benevolence towards his subordinates, and praise Shen Shuibi for her beauty and sensible nature.

Hearing these praises, Li Qi looked over at them and found that, as expected, the maids only served and didn't touch a single bite.

Even though these people praised Li Qi's benevolence in sharing food with those beside him, not a single one of them seemed to emulate him. It was evident that deep down, they simply didn't care.

After the meal was finished, another round of pleasantries ensued.

Having endured it for so long, Li Qi began to grow weary of this endless, empty politeness.

_Do these people never cultivate?_

_How did they even enter the ranks?_

This continued for a while. About two hours later, Li Qi suddenly felt the carriage come to a halt.

"Good, we have arrived at our destination. Our serendipitous encounter has also arrived. Will the Young Master accompany us?" The Governor suddenly spoke with a smile, looking at Li Qi.

"Naturally." Li Qi cupped his hands. He watched as all these guests suddenly lost all interest in the beauties and fine food. One by one, they all stood up and rushed outside at breakneck speed.

Even the Governor stood up and walked shoulder-to-shoulder with Li Qi. "The Young Master is magnanimous, granting us a serendipitous encounter. Alas, we all owe the Young Master a favor."

_Huh?_

Li Qi had no idea what he meant.

However, when you don't know what's going on, you just have to pretend you do.

He immediately replied, "The Governor is too polite."

"Haha! The Young Master is truly someone from a major sect, so straightforward! I will express my gratitude upon my return. I won't delay here any longer. The Young Master can wait for us here. Afterwards, we will head to the prefectural capital together!" The Governor laughed heartily, then hastily sprinted out, disappearing from Li Qi's sight.

Li Qi was completely baffled.

But he followed them out of the carriage anyway.

Then, he understood.

He understood why these people had been so attentive, and why the Governor had tried to curry favor with him.

Because the place they had arrived at was not some city, but the previous battlefield!

On the battlefield lay a circular mirror, spanning a full hundred li in radius.

This was the trace left behind by the Grand Diviner when he suppressed the two demon kings, the curse demon and Xiong Sha. That single finger had flattened a hundred li of land until it was as smooth as a mirror. But upon closer inspection, one could make out a giant fingerprint on it.

Now, those bigwigs whom Li Qi had once considered lofty and unattainable were all sticking their butts in the air, desperately observing the fingerprint. They were even getting into fights over it, excitedly discussing it amongst themselves.

It seemed their earlier pleasantries with Li Qi had merely been them suppressing their impatience.

"Ah! So that's how it is!" Shen Shuibi, standing beside him, suddenly tapped her head in sudden realization.

"Those with profound cultivation naturally carry dao rhythm within their bodies. They came for the fingerprint left by the Grand Diviner!" Shen Shuibi had figured it out as well.

_Holy crap, to these Rank 6, 7, and 8 people, a single fingerprint left by the Grand Diviner is probably a fortuitous encounter and a resource they'd struggle to find in their entire lifetimes!_

But they were afraid that coming here without permission would anger the Grand Diviner. Thus, they dragged Li Qi along. This way, even if the Grand Diviner sensed it, he wouldn't make things difficult for them.

Li Qi smiled bitterly. He had thought this bunch were just bootlickers, but it turned out they had all come prepared. Was he... being used as a human shield again?

_Sigh, I've suffered a loss for being uneducated._

"...Forget it. Even if I told them to leave now, I probably couldn't chase them away. Might as well cut my losses. Miss Shen, you're also Rank 5. Aren't you going to study it a bit?" Li Qi immediately decided on damage control.

_If you guys can use the Grand Diviner's fingerprint, can't I?_

"I have no use for it. That Grand Diviner's cultivation is still somewhat weaker than Lady Luofu's. I have already spent day and night studying the dao rhythm on her body in detail. This single fingerprint is of no use to me."

Saying so, Shen Shuibi began to explain.

For Lady Luofu or that unnamed Grand Diviner, their very existence could alter the 'Dao' of a region of heaven and earth. Their personal cosmos could already tamper with reality.

A single hair or a single fingerprint was an external manifestation of this 'dao rhythm'. Although it was only a very shallow trace, not even a trillionth of their true essence, for those with lower cultivation, even a single glance could yield immense benefits, perhaps even causing their cultivation to advance by leaps and bounds.

The officials and the Governor before them were likely all of the Shaman Dao, so the Grand Diviner's fingerprint was extremely useful to them.

But as an orthodox member of the Profound Sect, and with the true form of Lady Luofu available for her to study at any time over thousands of years, these things were meaningless to Shen Shuibi.

It was just like... how an owl would fight over a dead rat, while a phoenix only consumed the fruit of the bamboo.

This was the gap between them.
