# Chapter 195: Summoning

Although he wanted to escape, Li Qi did not attempt to use the qi of the sun, moon, and stars given to him by his teacher.

He knew very well that the reaction speed of a Rank 7 was far faster than his own.

In the split second it would take him to activate it, Monk Chanzhi could kill him a thousand times over.

The reason he had managed to escape the Green Mountain God last time was that the deity had been two thousand miles away, delivering an absentminded, casual strike.

If the other party deliberately intended to kill Li Qi, he wouldn't even have the chance to react, let alone make preparations.

Therefore, drawing upon that qi right in front of him would be tantamount to suicide.

He still needed to think of another way.

Monk Chanzhi began his seclusion to experiment with cultivation methods.

Meanwhile, Li Qi fell into a deep sleep.

The position where he had placed his bed silently began to take effect.

A divine soul was not like a yin spirit or a deity's golden body. Both yin spirits and golden bodies were incredibly condensed, possessing highly conspicuous protective energy that could be easily detected.

The divine soul, however, was exceptionally fragile. A mere gust of wind could inflict severe damage, and exposure to sunlight would cause it to dissipate on the spot. Even the faint Yang qi and breath emanating from ordinary living mortals could force a divine soul away, lest it be burned.

Weightless, delicate, and practically devoid of resistance—such was the constitution of a divine soul.

However, this fragility also brought about a specific trait.

For most people, their ethereal and corporeal souls were practically undetectable. They exerted no influence on the mortal realm at all. Aside from Yin Bailiffs and those who exclusively practiced similar cultivation methods, they were impossible to find.

It was precisely because of this trait that Li Qi dared to let his soul leave his body. The likelihood of a solitary soul being discovered was virtually nonexistent. The ones that were typically noticed were massive congregations of yin spirits found in graveyards or ancient battlefields.

Li Qi initiated his soul's wandering.

The moment his soul departed his flesh, he felt a colossal pulling force surge toward him!

Li Qi was deeply familiar with this suction. He had faced it directly before.

Back in the Great Deer Kingdom, when he had risked his life to slay several Tang Empire soldiers, he had nearly died. On that occasion, he had felt this very same pulling force sweeping over him.

This was the pull of reincarnation!

It permeated all realms and filled the endless cosmos. It was the attractive force of reincarnation that all the dead were bound to obey.

The last time Li Qi managed to resist it was entirely due to the protection of Shen Shuibi's Unsullied Frame, combined with the medical arts she used to anchor his life force. That was how he had survived.

Now, Li Qi had no intention of resisting this pull.

The suction was so immense that the seal of Monk Chanzhi's five-tiered Buddhist Pagoda could not contain it.

Although a Rank 7 was already akin to a god or devil, expecting to easily contend with the pull of reincarnation was nothing more than a pipe dream.

And so, Li Qi's divine soul was dragged straight out of the Buddhist Pagoda.

However, halfway through the pull.

Li Qi caught sight of Beyond the Boundary.

This five-tiered Buddhist Pagoda was fundamentally rooted in Beyond the Boundary. When he first arrived, he had been directly transported through a slender, inexplicable conduit.

The Buddhist Pagoda situated near Vast Yang City was likely nothing more than a projection.

Li Qi arrived at Beyond the Boundary, yet the pulling force continued to tug at him.

Moreover, the surrounding scenery no longer possessed its previous magnificence.

The prior grandeur seemed to have been a vision glimpsed only from within the conduit. In that place, there was an existence capable of transcending light and transmitting information, allowing Li Qi to perceive it through the True Knowledge dao rhythm. That was why he had witnessed those majestic spectacles.

Now, Li Qi had arrived in Beyond the Boundary entirely alone.

And the true Beyond the Boundary...

Held only profound desolation.

There was no light. His eyes could perceive absolutely nothing.

Not only was he blinded, but he was also deafened. The surroundings were pure void. Aside from a rudimentary sense of touch that conveyed an endless, biting cold, all his other senses seemed to have completely failed.

But it wasn't a failure of his senses; there was genuinely nothing around him.

No light, no sound, no warmth.

If he reached out a hand, he would only grope aimlessly through the boundless darkness, feeling nothing. It was as if even his sense of touch had vanished. Only when his fingers brushed against one another could he affirm his own existence.

This was the true Beyond the Boundary.

An expanse of absolute nothingness.

A hollow, empty realm.

Lacking light and color, devoid of scent and sound, absent of any medium whatsoever. It was an utter, uncompromising dead zone.

Even the closest matter or world was situated at an unfathomable distance away.

The magnificent grandeur of Beyond the Boundary required a truly macroscopic perspective to be witnessed.

If one entered this realm with the perspective of a mere ant, they would only exhaust their entire lifespan within the endless void, unable to traverse even the most negligible of distances.

This caused Li Qi to recall the unit of measurement known as a "light-year."

Such distances evoked nothing but pure despair, entirely snuffing out any desire to challenge them.

However, Li Qi refused to believe that Monk Chanzhi could truly detach himself to a place unfathomably far from All Under Heaven. He didn't possess that kind of capability.

Thus, while he was undoubtedly some distance away from All Under Heaven, it certainly wasn't an insurmountably desperate distance.

At that moment, Li Qi silently roused the faint traces of incense fire qi within his body.

This was the incense fire qi coalesced from the reverence and gratitude of the mortals who had established shrines to worship him and earnestly prayed for his well-being.

Previously, Li Qi had never considered it useful, simply because he had no need for it. As long as one did not walk the path of the Divine Dao, incense fire qi was essentially useless.

It wasn't a matter of simply possessing "qi." All myriad things in the world were composed of qi. Countless variations of qi intertwined to form matter and all manner of wondrous phenomena. By utilizing qi, one could alter the world, much like how fire qi could ignite into flames. This was the foundational basis of "arts."

However, the applications of qi were infinite. For example, water qi in its purest form was not actual water. In Li Qi's mind, it was no different from the distinction between the chemical element of oxygen and the air we breathe; one was a fundamental building block, while the other was physical matter.

The same held true for qi. It was merely the fundamental element that constructed the world, not physical matter itself. The method of utilizing different types of qi, blending them to create the specific, functional energies one desired—this was the essence of "arts."

Taking water qi as an example once more: when combined with mountain qi, it formed cloud qi. Merging with earth qi produced marsh qi. It could even be fused with fire qi; the convergence of water and fire qi would give birth to vital qi.

The practice of combining these varied forms of qi, creating unimaginable effects, and achieving extraordinarily miraculous abilities was known as "methods." The two were often spoken of interchangeably, collectively termed "spell arts."

Therefore, while the varieties of qi were boundless, cultivators typically only gathered the specific types required by their cultivation methods.

Incense fire qi, to Li Qi, was entirely useless. Retaining it within his body would even obstruct the normal operation of his personal cosmos, much like an accumulation of unwanted clutter.

Yet, to cultivators of the Divine Dao, incense fire qi was a supreme tonic, something they yearned for desperately.

Sometimes, the qi one required was viewed as worthless trash by another, while the qi one discarded was treated as a priceless treasure elsewhere. It all depended entirely on the cultivation method one practiced.

But circumstances were ever-changing.

Just like right now.

As Li Qi activated the incense fire qi and channeled a Divine Dao cultivation method, the incense fire cloaked his divine soul.

The heartfelt gratitude and worship of a hundred thousand people—this was a larger congregation than the combined following of many Rank 9 mountain gods.

Executing a Divine Dao cultivation method at this moment yielded immediate, profound results.

Marvelous phenomena manifested all around him.

Behind him unfurled a golden mountain rising from billowing clouds of mist. Beneath his feet, a pavilion of glazed glass ascended, culminating in a majestic throne upon which his form rested, serene and unbothered.

Talismanic script blanketed his surroundings, arranging into profound ritualistic formations.

Divine light flickered like kindled candles, burning as brilliantly as the noonday sun.

Auspicious winds filled the void, resembling twilight mists reflecting the moon's glow.

His ceremonial crown and regalia were dignified and solemn, their radiance intertwining like connecting flames.

Li Qi himself became a singular point of light within this boundless nothingness.

Yet, as the radiance extended outward, it was like a drop of water falling into the ocean. It failed to stir even the slightest ripple within the pitch-black, infinite void.

This speck of light was utterly insignificant.

However, the light had forged a _connection_.

Through the unseen world, Li Qi could sense people offering sacrifices to him, directing their prayers his way.

Some prayed for his protection, treating him just like a deity.

Others prayed for his well-being, hoping for his safety...

Li Qi didn't even recognize some of these people. They weren't part of that group of a hundred thousand. They were individuals he had helped a long time ago, in places he could no longer recall.

How should he describe it?

Li Qi's emotions grew somewhat complicated.

Though he had never sought any return for his actions, this sensation was undeniably profound.

It wasn't that deferential, face-to-face gratitude. He had seen too much of that. Having done so many deeds, there were always crowds of people thanking him directly.

But to feel the silent prayers echoing in their hearts from so incredibly far away, long after the deeds were done... this feeling was truly different.

It was like...

Donating money through your phone to help a middle school student in a remote mountain village, paying for their high school tuition.

And then completely forgetting about the matter.

Until, seven or eight years later, a stranger unexpectedly knocked on your door, having traveled all that way specifically to thank you.

This kind of feeling...

Was impossible to describe.

But it certainly wasn't a bad feeling.

That, however, was unimportant.

What mattered was that, through these prayers, Li Qi could clearly perceive the location of All Under Heaven.

More accurately, he sensed the locations of the praying individuals, who naturally resided within All Under Heaven.

Yet, amidst these prayers, Li Qi sensed another distinct summons.

It wasn't a beacon indicating a location, but a direct summoning.

A Divine Descent spell art!

Li Qi furrowed his brows, a surge of unease welling in his heart.

A Divine Descent spell art was, in essence, the summoning of a deity.

Generally, only a deity's most favored believers possessed the means to summon their respective god. This method allowed a fraction of the deity's spirit to split off and be transported to the designated location.

This was considered one of the foundational cultivation methods of the Divine Dao, akin to horse stance training in the Martial Dao—a basic fundamental.

The problem, however, was that Li Qi was not a cultivator of the Divine Dao.

This meant that whoever was casting this Divine Descent spell art lacked his divine title, a dedicated temple, or any of the myriad other tools typically used to anchor a deity's presence.

Under such circumstances, who could possibly summon him and construct the corresponding Divine Descent formation?

It was the equivalent of Li Qi having just registered a new phone number—representing his divine title and location—without telling a single soul. Absolutely no one should know this number existed.

Yet, at this very moment, a complete stranger was calling it.

Had they guessed the number?

Or had someone anticipated long ago that Li Qi would use a Divine Dao cultivation method at this specific time to forge a golden body and turn himself into a temporary deity, and thus made advance preparations?

Li Qi felt a twinge of hesitation. He had no idea what the summoner's intentions were.

Still, he extended his perception.

The location of the person calling for his Divine Descent... was within All Under Heaven.

Since the other party was in All Under Heaven, as long as he accepted, his divided spirit could immediately descend to that location. This equated to escaping his current predicament outright.

Even better, his primary divine soul could return to his physical body to continue acting as Monk Chanzhi's captive laborer, placating the monk and preventing him from discovering the escape!

_I can give it a try. The person summoning me right now must have some connection to me. Whether it's for better or worse, it's still better than being locked up by Monk Chanzhi._ Li Qi made his decision, preparing to accept the Divine Descent.

No matter what, it was vastly preferable to being trapped by Monk Chanzhi.

Furthermore, Li Qi still possessed an unused trump card: the four wisps of qi bestowed upon him by Young Master Zhu. Although he had expended one, three still remained.

However, unless driven to absolute desperation, Li Qi was unwilling to use them.

His current situation had not yet reached that point of absolute desperation.

Li Qi had never willingly placed his own life in extreme peril. As he had noted in the past, he was someone who greatly cherished his life. While he wouldn't claim to only act with absolute certainty, he required at least a fifty percent chance of success before making a move.

With this in mind, Li Qi willed his primary soul to return to his body while simultaneously allowing a splinter of his spirit to respond to the Divine Descent.

The main soul slipped back to lie dormant within his physical shell, while the divided spirit was whisked away by the summons.

Crossing an unfathomable distance in the blink of an eye, Li Qi's divided spirit vanished from the void, arriving within All Under Heaven.

Despite his divine soul being split, Li Qi felt no pain whatsoever.

A divided spirit was not a true tearing of the soul, and thus induced no agony.

However...

"This sensation... is incredibly bizarre." Li Qi experienced both perspectives simultaneously, finding it immensely awkward.

It was like possessing two distinct bodies and being forced to control both of them at the exact same time.

Two disparate viewpoints, two distinct sets of sensory inputs, and two entirely different physical forms.

Was this how deities with thousands of incarnations perceived the world?

Did they constantly experience billions of perspectives, pilot billions of bodies, and converse with billions of mortals simultaneously to answer their prayers?

For such divine beings, how many followers would be pleading for their Divine Descent at any given moment?

Take the Ox Strength Art he used to employ so frequently. How many people out there actively used the Ox Strength Art to draw upon the power of that Earthly Divinity, the Qiong Ox?

Fortunately, his current burden was manageable.

Li Qi lacked the capability to seamlessly command two bodies and could not adapt to having two overlapping visual feeds. Fearing he might confuse the physical actions of the two forms, he decisively abandoned control of his original body, leaving it to rest quietly.

Even then, it couldn't strictly be called sleeping—more like lying idle on a bed, as his consciousness remained fully awake.

Concurrently, he funneled all his vital energy and focus into the divided spirit.

Soon after, the divided spirit's Divine Descent concluded, and he opened his eyes.

The first thing he saw was Shen Shuibi.

He found himself standing directly in the center of a formation disk.

"Ah?" Li Qi stood frozen in astonishment.

_Why is it the rabbit?_

Shen Shuibi, upon seeing Li Qi materialize, allowed her tightly strung nerves to visibly slacken.

One could clearly sense an aura of sheer relief and joy radiating from her.

She closed the distance in a few quick strides, looking as if she were about to throw herself at him. However, she quickly realized herself, halting abruptly before Li Qi. Forcing a calm demeanor, she slowed her pace.

"You...! Where exactly did you run off to?" she asked as she took a slow step forward. Her tone was initially sharp with emotion, but she instantly masked it with feigned nonchalance.

"Uh, Miss Shen, how did you find my divine title? Wait, no. How did you even know I would attempt to use a Divine Dao cultivation method to escape?" Li Qi asked, his mind brimming with confusion.

Shen Shuibi had initially been harboring traces of delight, but upon hearing Li Qi's very first words to her be an interrogation, she immediately spun around. Turning her back to him, she puffed her cheeks and retorted, "How should I know? I certainly don't."

"You don't know?" Li Qi was bewildered.

It was only then that he took a closer look at the formation disk beneath his feet.

Engraved upon its surface were thousands of different spell arts.
