# Chapter 162: Memories

Shen Shuibi seemed extremely displeased with Hu Xian'er tampering with Li Qi's body. Consequently, she took over all of Li Qi's subsequent treatments.

Muttering complaints like "Quack!" and "How did it end up like this!", she slung Li Qi over her shoulder and carried him to the kitchen.

At first, Li Qi didn't understand what was happening.

Then, Shen Shuibi emptied a large wok and dumped him right into it.

"Miss Shen, what is this?"

"Shut up! I finally managed to clear your meridians, and you actually let a quack make a complete mess of them again! Don't talk to me; I need to focus on untangling this!" Shen Shuibi snapped at Li Qi with a rare tone of sheer annoyance.

Li Qi fell silent. It wasn't like he could move anyway.

However, this feeling truly was...

A wave of lingering fear washed over him. If another highly skilled medical expert saw his body, could they also, like Shen Shuibi, shatter and injure the flesh that the Clear Mist Sect Master had so carefully sutured with just a light tap?

This kind of risk... and the fact that it would affect his future cultivation. No wonder Shen Shuibi was so angry.

It seemed he really couldn't trust outside doctors anymore.

Their intentions might be good, but their medical skills genuinely couldn't compare to the rabbit he had at home.

Pondering this, Li Qi lay in the wok as Shen Shuibi poured in a potful of water to soak him. She then cast a Fire Ignition Spell to boil the water, effectively iron-wok-stewing Li Qi.

Afterward, she bustled up and down, summoning the Three-Five Cauldron to begin alchemy. She tossed the refined medicines into Li Qi's wok, letting them bubble and boil together.

Fortunately, the current Li Qi was completely immune to ordinary boiling water, so he obediently lay there waiting to be cooked.

As Shen Shuibi cooked him, she scolded, "It's not like you don't know about the personal cosmos. Why did you let someone tamper with your body?"

"Within the personal cosmos, the head is like heaven, and the feet are like the earth. Heavenly qi connects to the lungs, earth qi connects to the throat, wind qi connects to the liver, thunder qi connects to the heart, grain qi connects to the spleen, and rain qi connects to the kidneys. The six meridians are the rivers, the intestines and stomach are the seas, and the nine orifices are the qi of flowing waters. Heaven has the four seasons and five elements, governed by the waxing and waning of Yin, Yang, cold, and heat. Harmony becomes rain, anger becomes wind, condensation becomes snow, and expansion becomes a rainbow. These are the constants of heaven. A human has four limbs and five internal organs; through breathing, the circulation of qi, and the continuous rise and fall of essence qi, life persists. The dual qi of Yin and Yang disperse to form nourishment and defense. The blood meridians manifest as complexion, and the outward projection becomes voice. These are the constants of humanity."

"When heaven and man no longer correspond, problems arise. Your personal cosmos was already unbalanced. Repairing it artificially might make it look healed, but in reality, subtle discrepancies with the external heavenly timing have been created. Your personal cosmos was born using Shaman Dao methods, borrowing the qi of heaven and earth, and completed by relying on the laws of the four seasons. Within it, the mountains, rivers, soil, water, metals, stones, grass, and trees—all myriad things—have already grown. Even every hair, patch of skin, muscle, blood vessel, meridian, and bone marrow must perfectly correspond with the external heaven and earth. If they don't, the internal deities you condense will be distorted. When it comes time to advance in rank, you'll have to scrap everything and refine it all over again. Messing around all day—do you truly not cherish yourself?"

"Furthermore, that person who helped mend you possesses mere craftsmanship but doesn't understand the true principles. It serves her right to be stuck at Rank 7 for the rest of her life!"

The more Shen Shuibi spoke, the angrier she got. Eventually, she simply cranked up the firepower of her Fire Ignition Spell. Instantly, steam flew everywhere, and the entire wok of water evaporated in two or three seconds.

"Hot!" Li Qi almost flipped out of the water.

Unfortunately, he couldn't move.

"Consider this a lesson!" Shen Shuibi said. She formed a Water Attraction Spell, and with a swoosh, a fresh ring of cold water soaked Li Qi again. It directly submerged his nose and mouth, making him blow bubbles in the water.

But despite her words, she diligently refined the medicine and placed it in the water, meticulously untangling the chaotic qi within Li Qi's body.

She even rolled up her sleeves and got hands-on, giving Li Qi a comprehensive massage and bone-setting session from head to toe.

Lying in the wok, Li Qi narrowed his eyes, feeling her hands gliding back and forth over his body.

Could this be considered... a blessing in disguise?

In short, Li Qi was forced to recuperate for another day. It wasn't until the second day that he finally regained his mobility.

Still, they were finally setting off.

Old Ma had eaten extremely well these past few days. He had fattened up again, turning back into a horse with a double chin.

Li Qi also gained a good item: a mustard seed pouch[^1].

He had bought it using the gifts Jin Buhuan had sent.

The gifts he had delivered were truly valuable.

The mustard seed pouch wasn't very large, roughly the size of a single room.

In truth, there wasn't actually any space inside the item itself; it was merely an entrance. The underlying principle involved demarcating an area in the outer realms—similar to a cage—protecting it with talismans, and then opening a small portal leading to the mouth of the mustard seed pouch. Tossing something inside simply meant placing the item into that extra-dimensional cage.

Because this cage in the outer realms was directly exposed to the vast void, it could only store inanimate objects, or living creatures with vitality so overwhelmingly strong that they could survive in the outer realms. Time flowed normally inside, so food placed within still had a shelf life. In essence, it was an easily accessible safe. Furthermore, the internal environment was incredibly harsh; the outer realms were a vacuum without a trace of temperature, devoid of light or shadow. Water placed inside would inevitably turn to solid ice.

When Li Qi bought it, he also learned an amusing piece of trivia.

Originally, a mustard seed pouch required a mighty figure of Rank 5 or higher to refine.

Demarcating space in the outer realms was simply too difficult, and opening a stable spatial rift for transmission was not something an ordinary person could achieve.

However, a certain mighty figure later turned this into a business. He staked out a massive tract of space in the outer realms—who knew how many millions of miles wide—and divided it into countless smaller sections. He then rented out these small pockets of space to various artifact refiner sects, allowing low-ranked artifact refiners to open mustard seed portals within his demarcated zone. Only then did low-ranked mustard seed pouches come into existence.

By doing this, that mighty figure collected rent from countless factions. He operated much like a colossal cloud storage provider, selling different amounts of storage space to different accounts. You could store your belongings in the cloud and withdraw them whenever needed.

Low-ranked artifact refiners were merely utilizing this mighty figure's cloud space; they had absolutely no ability to manipulate the space themselves. Different mustard seed pouches were simply different accounts, possessing varying capacities of storage space.

This couldn't help but make Li Qi sigh in awe.

_What a brilliant business model..._

Depending on the amount of space their sect had rented, the size of the outer realm area these artifact refiners could demarcate varied—meaning the cages came in different sizes. Li Qi's was considered moderately priced, though that price was enough to buy a tang saber. It was exorbitantly expensive.

But it was still incredibly convenient. At the very least, he no longer needed to carry a bamboo basket on his back.

He had Young Master Jin to thank. Without the eight hundred spiritual flowers he had sent, Li Qi's injuries wouldn't have healed so quickly. Without the other jewels and gold he had gifted, Li Qi would never have been able to afford a mustard seed pouch.

However, while renting space on a network cloud drive was convenient, if one didn't want to use the cloud, they could opt for a portable hard drive—namely, a grotto-heaven dharma treasure.

Grotto-heaven dharma treasures were infinitely more high-end than mustard seed pouches. Although grotto-heavens were inherently empty voids, they could be freely modified.

For example, the main sect gates of the Clear Mist Sect resided within a massive grotto-heaven. Everything inside had been transported from the outside world and then sculpted into shape.

As long as the internal environment was suitable, a grotto-heaven dharma treasure could store absolutely anything, and its volume was vastly larger. However, its price was unfathomable. In any case, Li Qi couldn't afford one, and Clear Mist City didn't even sell them to begin with.

After tossing a pile of frozen culinary creations from Piaoxu's kitchen into the mustard seed pouch, and buying some of Piaoxu's homemade cured meats and sausages, his food provisions for the journey were ready.

Leading a reluctant Old Ma, who was still constantly chewing on Piaoxu's cooking, Li Qi took Shen Shuibi and bid farewell to Clear Mist City.

Qi Ya actually followed them along the way. After pondering it over, Li Qi gave her a copy of the _Song of the Solitary_ as well.

Consider it a gift born of fondness; after all, this little red-haired fox was quite adorable.

With all this done, they had thoroughly bid their goodbyes to Clear Mist City.

They set out on their journey.

However, upon reaching the desolate wilderness, Li Qi suddenly pulled the reins and brought the horse to a halt.

"Hm? What's wrong?" Sitting behind him, Shen Shuibi tilted her head, wondering what Li Qi was up to.

Li Qi looked around, confirming that there was truly no one nearby.

Then, he pulled out a porcelain bottle.

"Right, Miss Shen, this is a gift for you. It wasn't convenient to take it out in the city, but it shouldn't be a problem here." Li Qi handed the porcelain bottle to Shen Shuibi.

Shen Shuibi looked at the porcelain bottle, unsure of what Li Qi was scheming.

Then, she popped the cork.

Suddenly, even though it was broad daylight, a radiant, spiritual moon slowly rose into the air.

A dense surge of Supreme Yin qi swept over them.

A graceful crescent hook, ethereal light mist. The deep Yin had just coalesced, like the residual rain in the sky, casting a cool, clear moonlight and an air of desolate elegance.

Li Qi's eyes widened in shock.

_Ah, the books never mentioned that Moon Corporeal Soul fragments would cause a commotion like this!_

_Did I misunderstand something?_

Confused, Li Qi looked at Shen Shuibi, who was frozen in place.

_No, this definitely isn't the work of mere Moon Corporeal Soul fragments!_

He reached out and grabbed Shen Shuibi.

However, the icy touch at his fingertips felt like being dampened by morning dew. As a clear breeze swept past, his entire body turned freezing cold.

The surrounding scenery changed, shifting into a faint blue halo, as if they were underwater.

Li Qi struggled to his feet and pulled Shen Shuibi.

The moment she moved, the surrounding world seemed to instantly sharpen. It was as if he had broken the surface of the water and returned to the shore. His body re-integrated, returning to its original state, and his senses were no longer blurred.

Yet, a moon continued to glow right beside Shen Shuibi.

Moreover, she was crying.

Clutching the tiny porcelain bottle, the rabbit began to sob.

Although she occasionally lost her composure, she had never cried like this before.

Tears fell like pearls, dampening her cheeks, as her jade hairpin tilted slightly. Her voice was tender, seemingly carrying a trace of sorrowful resentment.

The vast sky was pure and clear, the light rain fell bleakly. Her pitiful appearance evoked boundless sympathy and endless longing.

This unprecedented sight actually left Li Qi staring in a daze.

While his heart ached for her, he simultaneously only felt one thing.

_So beautiful._

Fortunately, he didn't space out for long. Snapping out of it, he stepped forward and held onto Shen Shuibi. "What's wrong?"

"No, it's nothing..." Shen Shuibi wiped her face, but no matter how much she wiped, she couldn't dry her tears. Finally, she simply plopped down onto the ground, unstoppered the bottle again, and took another look inside.

This time, it was no longer silent sobbing; she began to truly wail.

Although she tried her best to suppress the sound, it was still clearly audible.

Li Qi stepped forward again and crouched down in front of her. Without speaking, he simply waited, his face plastered with awkwardness.

The usually sharp-tongued and eloquent Li Qi found himself completely unable to utter a single word at this exact moment.

It took quite a while before Shen Shuibi gradually reigned in her tears.

With the tear tracks on her face still wet, she remained kneeling on the ground. Tilting her face up, despite the teardrops still continuously falling, she asked Li Qi, "Li Qi, you... you know everything?"

Li Qi was bewildered.

_What do I know?_

_I have no idea._

However, he didn't show it. Remaining silent, he leaned sideways, raised his sleeve, and gently wiped her face.

Shen Shuibi finally ceased her crying. Sitting on the ground, hugging the bottle, she spoke in a soft, weak voice that nonetheless carried a hint of joy, "Thank you, Li Qi... To think you would actually help me find and bring back things from Mount Luofu."

The radiant light in those eyes made Li Qi instantly turn his head away.

Li Qi felt that it was an expression extremely rarely seen on her.

"No... there's no need to thank me for this. It's no big deal," Li Qi reacted quickly, answering immediately.

He looked up at the sky and thought to himself.

_Green Mountain Kingdom Lord._

_My good brother! You truly went out of your way._

Having dried Shen Shuibi's face, touching her cheek made him feel wave after wave of soft tenderness.

Shen Shuibi didn't seem to notice that Li Qi was stroking her face. She opened the porcelain bottle and told Li Qi, "This object... is the Moon Corporeal Soul core of the Luofu grotto-heaven. It's my former home."

Li Qi also sat cross-legged on the ground in front of her, listening to her speak.

Unexpectedly, a flood of memories seemed to surge into Shen Shuibi's mind, and she actually began to recount tales of the past to Li Qi.

"Back then, Yang Ning hated me incredibly much. Because Yin and Yang are difficult to harmonize, the Supreme Yin qi on my body made her very uncomfortable, so she was always hitting me... Plus, I couldn't beat her in a fight; I was never good at fighting to begin with..."

"One time, after she hit me, I ran to Lady Luofu crying. The Lady called her over and asked why she hit me."

"She arrogantly raised her head and said to me, 'I've disliked her for a long time, and I've wanted to hit her for ages! If the Lady hadn't said it was bad to do so, I would have made my move two years ago!'"

"Pfft..." Li Qi couldn't hold it back and let out a laugh.

"It's funny, right? After hearing that answer, the Lady hung her up on the laurel tree and blew Yin qi at her for eight hours, completely extinguishing the fire on her head."

At this point, Shen Shuibi's tone turned desolate. "It's just that, later, when the Military Commissioner came to deliver the final ultimatum, Yang Ning and I were intercepted. It was she who threw me out and stayed behind to cover my escape."

"That's why I ended up wandering alone, separated from the Lady and the others..." Shen Shuibi trailed off, her voice filled with melancholy.

"Alright, let's not talk about that. Let me tell you some things from my childhood. When I was little, I lived inside this. This Moon Corporeal Soul fragment was personally fetched from the Supreme Yin by the Lady just for me."

"Inside this... I actually secretly carved quite a few murals. I'll take them out to show you. They were all carved when I was very young. This bottle is a small mustard seed bottle, wait for me to break its seal."

Shen Shuibi spoke enthusiastically about her childhood stories, while Li Qi sat beside her, listening attentively.

Flying clouds darkened amidst the setting sun, gazing at each other as the night scenery fell. How could one bear to count on their fingers the years that had slipped by, reminiscing about the times of the past.

Old Ma snorted and rolled his eyes, tactfully trotting far away to graze on grass.

It seemed Li Qi's itinerary would have to be delayed for another night.

[^1]: Mustard seed pouch (芥子袋): The term "mustard seed" originates from the Buddhist philosophical concept of "Mount Sumeru hidden in a mustard seed" (Xumi cang jiezi). In Buddhist cosmology, Mount Sumeru is the colossal mountain at the center of the universe, while a mustard seed represents the infinitesimally small. This concept illustrates the transcendence of physical dimensions—the idea that the macroscopic can be effortlessly contained within the microscopic. In Chinese fantasy, this spiritual metaphor is adapted into a literal spatial storage device, functioning much like a "bag of holding," where a tiny pouch can house an entire pocket dimension.
