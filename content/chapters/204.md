# Chapter 204: Slaying the Demonic Monk (I)

Monk Chanzhi swiftly traversed Beyond the Boundary and arrived in Vast Yang.

To the naked eye, it appeared as though he had simply pushed open the gates of his Buddhist Pagoda and stepped outside.

"Ah... the suppressive force of All Under Heaven. _Phew_." Monk Chanzhi sighed.

Those who had never been to Beyond the Boundary could never understand this oppressive sensation.

Compared to the unbridled indulgence of Beyond the Boundary, in this All Under Heaven, it felt as if every word and deed was being scrutinized.

Here, dao rhythms of too sublime a nature were suppressed.

Those with excessively high cultivation bases were suppressed.

The collateral damage of a battle could not exceed a thousand miles; otherwise, it would incur Heavenly Punishment.

No speed could surpass the absolute limits of the Heavenly Dao.

No one was permitted to delve deeper than thirty thousand miles underground.

Laws such as these were engraved upon the Heavenly Dao of All Under Heaven. They required no overseer; the heavens and the earth themselves enforced the rules upon all living beings.

'Rules' permeated the entirety of All Under Heaven.

By comparison, Beyond the Boundary was a place of true freedom.

Countless people in the outer worlds longed for 'Ascension' into this place, bestowing upon it countless names: the Immortal Realm, the God Realm, the Firmament, the Divine Dynasty, the Heavenly Kingdom, the Sacred Realm, the Supreme Land.

The ultimate goal of their cultivation was to undergo Ascension from their own worlds into All Under Heaven, becoming true gods, immortals, or whatever else they aspired to be.

Yet, the cultivators who actually lived here only wanted to escape to Beyond the Boundary.

It was stifling. Far too stifling.

It felt as though Mount Tai was pressing down upon one's chest, making even breathing difficult.

One's hands and feet were entirely bound.

However, it was fine. He was just returning to pick someone up.

"The location is... ah, found her." Monk Chanzhi smiled and took a step forward.

His body suddenly turned illusory, and with that single step, he arrived at a karst cave.

This happened to be the hiding place of the Vast Yang Minister of Works.

If he had known Li Qi would direct Monk Chanzhi here, he would likely have regretted not killing Li Qi right from the start.

As for Monk Chanzhi, after arriving, he let out a sigh of admiration.

"Hm? Is this the formation created by Vast Yang to regulate the mountains and rivers? No wonder it could mask auras so flawlessly that even I couldn't detect it. Truly impressive. Hiding that female companion here was indeed a cunning move."

"However, now that I am here, these defenses seem somewhat superfluous." Chanzhi muttered to himself. He then reached out a hand and pressed down lightly.

In the sky, a massive hand materialized.

The giant golden hand, completely translucent and formed in a flower-pinching mudra, pressed toward the ground.

Although it was clearly solid ground, it suddenly reacted like the surface of a lake, allowing the hand to easily sink into it.

No, it had not turned into water.

Rather, before the colossal power of that hand, solid matter behaved like a fluid.

He plunged his hand into the earth just as an ordinary mortal would dip their hand into a pond.

Without the slightest resistance.

It wasn't that there was zero resistance; it was simply that the resistance was negligible to him.

Mountain ranges were flattened.

The earth violently trembled!

The ground rippled outward like waves on the water!

The earth fractured, land and water were thrown into the air, and all living beings were struck with terror. Mountain gods were seen fleeing, and water gods hastily retreated!

Just by looking at this giant hand, the various gods of the mountains and rivers knew the sheer disparity in their strength and fled in panic.

Monk Chanzhi paid them no mind. The massive hand continued to press downward, leaving an enormous, deep crater in the ground. The surrounding earth continuously quaked. Displaced rocks and soil surged upward, burying the adjacent landscapes, only to form entirely new mountains and rivers in their wake.

The countless mountain gods and water gods who had just fled now found their golden bodies flickering unstably; some even perished on the spot!

He had no choice. This formation blocked all prying eyes and prevented the transmission of information.

If he wanted to go in and invite the person, he first had to shatter this array built by Vast Yang City.

Fortunately, it wasn't difficult at all.

Overcoming skill with absolute power would suffice.

However, the giant hand did not continue downward indefinitely.

After excavating several thousand meters deep, a barrier abruptly rose.

"Monk Chanzhi! You push things too far!" The voice of the Minister of Works rang out from below!

"Ah, there was a rat hiding here too? Very well, I shall take care of you both. This poor monk is truly blessed by fortune." Monk Chanzhi smiled. He seemed slightly surprised, but entirely unfazed.

"Then come and try!" the Minister of Works roared. Instantly, the barrier expanded violently!

Immediately after, countless dharma treasures materialized in the sky—numbering in the tens of thousands!

The Reason Dao excelled the most in artifact refinement and the deployment of formations.

Investigating the principles of all things—this was the path of the Reason Dao. They typically applied the truths they discovered into practical use, meaning they crafted dharma treasures capable of reproducing those methods.

Through this, the Reason Dao possessed its unique formations and vast quantities of mass-producible dharma treasures.

"Go!" The Minister of Works waved his hands. The myriad dharma treasures chimed and hummed before shooting toward Monk Chanzhi at blinding speeds!

Among these dharma treasures, there were several hundred of the same grade as Li Qi's golden thread. There were even seven or eight pieces that surpassed Rank 8 metal qi and reached Rank 7.

These dharma treasures linked together, forming a grand formation.

Golden bells chimed, churning the clouds and waters.

Jade chimes struck, startling ghosts and gods.

Incense censers burned, blanketing the sky in fiery clouds.

Feather fans waved, summoning dancing colorful phoenixes.

Soul banners fluttered, plunging the ghostly realms into panic.

Bows and heavy crossbows, golden swords and silver spears, purple mist and crimson clouds, black qi stretching out.

Overlapping halberds and pikes, layers upon layers of spears and blades, golden bells and jade chimes—an all-encompassing myriad of dharma treasures blotted out the sky as they surged forward!

No matter how unsuited for combat he might be, the Minister of Works was still a majestic Rank 7 expert. Occupying the geographical advantage and supported by the formation below, he unleashed unbelievable destructive power.

The pungent stench of sulfur filled the air. Waves surged to the heavens, the earth trembled, and the sky turned a gloomy gray.

Just the shockwaves of this formation threw the surrounding weather into chaos, disrupted the celestial order, scrambled the heavenly qi, and caused the earth qi to churn wildly.

Yet, Chanzhi merely stood in place, pressing his palms together in prayer.

He softly chanted a sutra: "This peacock, is my body."

With just this single line... no, just this half-line of scripture, Buddhist light began to radiate in all directions.

The peacock inherently loved devouring poisonous insects and toxic weeds. Not only could poisons not harm it, they even enriched the luster of its feathers, making it superior to all other birds and beasts. Hence, it held the reputation of being immune to all dharma.

In the Buddhist Sect, there was the honored Mahamayuri, the Peacock Wisdom King, known as the 'World-Protecting Vajra Steel.' Blessed by divine light, it repelled all things precisely because of the peacock's trait of remaining untouched by all matter.

In the past, there was a unique beast in the snowy mountains—a golden peacock. It diligently chanted the Mahamayuri Mantra, thus always securing peace and safety for itself.

However, on one occasion, consumed by the desire for pleasure, it traveled deep into distant mountains to frolic with a flock of peahens and failed to recite the mantra. Consequently, it was captured by hunters. While bound, it regained its clarity of mind and chanted the mantra, instantly breaking free of its restraints and regaining its freedom.

At this moment, what Chanzhi chanted was exactly a half-line of an altered and flawed Mahamayuri Mantra.

Following the chant, a Great Peacock dharma body manifested behind Monk Chanzhi. Mandala light radiated around him as a peacock dharma avatar condensed into a solid form at his back.

The peacock emitted Buddhist light. Like a solitary cloud drifting through the boundless sky, the dharma treasures completely lost their guidance and miraculously slipped right off the edges of the Buddhist light enveloping the peacock dharma body.

It was a bizarre sight. Like repelling magnets, countless dharma treasures swirled around Monk Chanzhi, yet simply could not draw near.

However, the Minister of Works had no intention of dragging out the fight.

The moment this move forced Monk Chanzhi on the defensive, he didn't hesitate to activate the formation, commanding this grand formation built from the resources of an entire prefecture to directly self-destruct!

Then, he quickly pulled back, preparing to flee.

Although his words earlier had been filled with absolute resolve, his immediate reaction was to run.

Launch an all-out offensive to force Monk Chanzhi to defend, detonate the array to block pursuit, and then escape without a trace.

He had made this decision the instant they met.

It was incredibly fast and exceedingly wise. This was practically the Vast Yang Minister of Works' sole chance at survival.

There was only one problem.

How had he been exposed?

This place was supposed to guarantee that no auras whatsoever would leak out. How had Monk Chanzhi found him?

No matter how he thought about it, only one answer came to mind.

That Young Master of Shaman God Mountain!

Aside from him, no one else could have tipped off Chanzhi!

That bastard!

Just because he hadn't agreed to act as his dog and horse, the boy actually resorted to this, intending to use Chanzhi to kill him with a borrowed knife?!

Despicable and shameless!

However, at that very moment, the dharma avatar behind Monk Chanzhi transformed.

Originally, it was the Great Peacock dharma avatar. Dazzling with the colors of the seven treasures[^1], its radiant light filled the surroundings, looking upon even mosquitoes and insects with the compassion one would show a newborn child.

The defenses of this dharma avatar were impenetrable, practically impossible to break.

After the formation self-destructed, he should have continued to defend.

If he didn't, he would be wounded by the explosion and would be unable to pursue the Minister of Works.

Yet, Monk Chanzhi—who ought to have maintained his defense to avoid injury—did not make the choice the Minister of Works expected.

From the Minister of Works' perspective, Monk Chanzhi directly abandoned his defense.

It was as if Monk Chanzhi wasn't afraid of getting hurt at all?

It was just that, the newly emerged dharma avatar was—

A devil.

It had been high noon.

But suddenly.

The sky went dark.

Something blotted out the sun across a hundred-mile radius.

A colossal nightmare descended upon the mortal realm.

A terrifying devil—whose upper body was human, while its lower body consisted of countless drooling, venomous snakes—towered behind Chanzhi.

These grotesque snakes looked more like some form of tentacles. Their eyes were pitch-black, and upon these tentacles writhed countless human faces, shrieking in pure agony.

Li Qi had seen Chanzhi in this form before.

Whenever Monk Chanzhi lost control, he assumed the appearance of this fiendish devil god!

From beneath the horrific devil god, black, smoking acid dripped incessantly.

In an instant, all the incoming dharma treasures, and even the self-destructing formation itself, began to dissolve.

Like wind blowing against light, or a knife cutting through water—they didn't even interact.

Monk Chanzhi's devilish qi was like boiling soup, while these external entities were like solid ice. The moment they touched, the ice rapidly melted and vanished.

In the distance, the frantically fleeing Minister of Works looked back in shock, only to see that Monk Chanzhi was completely ignoring him. Instead, he was using this devil god physique to forcefully suppress the self-destructing formation!

He was forcibly pushing an actively exploding bomb back into its casing!

Why would he do such a thing?

The Minister of Works couldn't make sense of it no matter how hard he thought.

Was he trying to prevent collateral damage?

Impossible!

How could this demonic monk ever entertain such a thought?

However, to his absolute disbelief, after suppressing the array, Monk Chanzhi genuinely reached out and protected the space beneath the karst cave. He then raised his voice and called out, "Young friend Shen, I was entrusted by Li Qi to seek you out and invite you to be a guest at my Buddhist Pagoda for a few days. Young friend Li Qi is also staying with me."

Only when Chanzhi spoke these words did the Minister of Works finally understand.

Monk Chanzhi wasn't here for him at all!

Someone had been hiding right beneath his formation! How could such a thing happen?! Which god-forsaken bastard had ruined him like this!

The Minister of Works was filled with grief and indignation, yet he also felt a stroke of luck.

In that case, the monk wasn't targeting him? He could actually escape?

Driven by this fluke, he didn't stop, sprinting madly until he was over a hundred miles away.

This was the boundary line. Once he crossed it, he would be out of the devil god's area of effect, and his chances of successful escape would skyrocket to over seventy percent!

As long as he crossed that line—

However, in the very next instant.

The Minister of Works froze.

Behind him, an aura of pure terror erupted, sending shivers down the spine of even a Rank 7 expert like him.

Rank 7 combat prowess. It was absolutely another entity with Rank 7 combat prowess!

What was going on?

He couldn't resist glancing back.

He saw a martial artist.

With the back of a tiger and the waist of a bear, standing over two meters tall. His muscles looked forged from iron, his bones cast from steel. Bare-chested and rubbing his fists, he grinned wildly. A torrential surge of vitality and blood erupted like a billowing smoke signal from his Baihui point[^2], shooting hundreds of feet into the air! It was visible even from a hundred miles away.

What a fiercely robust man!

That dense vitality and blood was practically a raging inferno! It was fine when restrained, but if pushed to its absolute limits, it could melt gold, liquify iron, and turn the very earth into a boiling sludge!

_Thump, thump, thump._ A muffled rhythm echoed out, sounding like the Lord of Heaven beating a war drum, or the deep, resonating pulse of the earth.

But the Minister of Works knew better. It was the sound of a heartbeat.

An impossibly powerful heart was pumping blood, surging it through his entire body to supply explosive strength to every single muscle!

It was this exact martial artist who had forcefully blocked Monk Chanzhi's descending hand.

In doing so, he had forcibly tanked a direct hit from Monk Chanzhi.

When Monk Chanzhi's massive hand struck him, a cacophony of cracking and popping resounded.

The martial artist's bones emitted sharp, crisp snaps, like iron beads clattering onto the ground—_clack, clack, clack_—or the clinking of jade stones. Yet, whatever it was, it sounded nothing like human bones breaking.

As he stretched and flexed, the thick tendons within his body snapped like the bowstrings of gigantic ballistae, producing explosive _twang_ sounds.

His skin was dense, taut, and lustrous, resembling fine silk or flowing quicksilver—flexible yet impossibly heavy. Sharp blades could not pierce it; flying arrows left not a single mark.

Just looking at that physique, anyone could tell he was an absolute martial artist!

"Rank 7? No, he's Rank 8, but temporarily boosted to Rank 7 by a formation. Who is this?" The Minister of Works muttered, continuing to back away while observing the newcomer who had intervened.

Not just anyone could elevate their cultivation realm through external boosts. Just like overinflating a balloon until it popped, relying on external power to forcefully raise one's realm without a rock-solid foundation was pure suicide.

To survive a boost from Rank 8 to Rank 7, what kind of heaven-defying genius was this?

Monk Chanzhi chuckled. "Someone from the Righteous Sword Palace? Boosted by a formation? Furthermore, a formation personally commanded by young friend Shen?"

He looked down and saw that, at some unknown point, Shen Shuibi was standing below. She had completely taken control of the Vast Yang City formation meant to regulate the mountains and rivers qi.

Without the Minister of Works even realizing it, she had usurped and modified his formation long ago!

Chanzhi couldn't help but laugh out loud. "Young friend Li entrusted me to fetch you, yet to think young friend Shen has such a strapping man by her side. It seems you've been enjoying yourself too much to return. Young friend Li was simply being overly affectionate."

Before his teasing words even landed, the burly man—Zhang Lan—sprang into action.

Empowered by the formation, he drew his sword.

The first phase of Li Qi's plan had officially commenced.

[^2]: Also known as "Hundred Convergences" acupoint, it is the intersection point where all the Yang qi meridians of the body meet.
    
    The point is located at the very top of the head.
