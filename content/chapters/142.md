# Chapter 142: Making a Name

Li Qi wasn't worried about Shen Shuibi in the slightest.

Because the rabbit had said she could handle it.

So, she definitely could.

She always told the truth, even though sometimes this trait could be quite infuriating. Young Master Zhu had been provoked by her precisely because of this in the past.

And right now, the small tripod in Shen Shuibi's hand might have looked unremarkable, but it was an item Yang Ning had given her.

A Rank 7 dharma treasure named the 'Three-Five Cauldron.'

Its name was derived from the concept that 'three and five make one.'

As the saying went, _Zi_ and _Wu_ align to make three[^1]; _Wu_ and _Ji_ are called five[^2]. When three and five are in harmony, the Eight Minerals form the core principles[^3], the Five Elements exchange their dominance, and mutual reliance breeds life. Three and five become one, representing the ultimate essence of heaven and earth.

It was forged entirely around the core concept of reversing the Five Elements. The cauldron enacted the art of inverting Yin, Yang, and the Five Elements. The fundamental principle was that only through the grinding and friction of this inversion could the essence qi of food and medicine resonate with the innate essence qi. Like attracting like, they would gather within the cauldron. This was also the process of 'refining pills' within this cauldron.

This was also the reasoning behind the common saying in alchemical texts: "Compliance makes a mortal; inversion makes an immortal." It was the profound mystery behind the Daoist Sects' mechanism of life and death.

What did it mean to reverse the Five Elements?

Fire melts metal, metal chops wood, wood flourishes while water dries, water thrives while fire perishes. When all four elements are extinguished, all things return to earth.

It broke the Daoist Sects' traditional mindset of "Earth births all things, and the Five Elements generate one another." By inverting the Five Elements and causing them to exchange dominance, it highlighted an effect primarily driven by mutual restraint. It utilized the characteristics of mutual restraint and mutual destruction until everything perished and returned to the deep earth. In doing so, it extracted the essence of all sorts of materials into the 'deep earth.'

After that came mutual reliance to breed life, where three and five became one.

The Five Elements would return to one. Within the element of earth, a brand new set of Five Elements would be nurtured, reverting to the original state of 'Earth births all things.' Through another cycle of mutual generation, a freshly produced 'pill' would be born.

This was the method of reverse refinement.

Unlike the water and fire refinement methods mastered by ordinary alchemists, this was the Daoist Sects' deeper, more profound method of alchemy.

And right now, Shen Shuibi had activated the method of reversing the Five Elements within it. Using a trap and employing the bright moon for targeting, she was refining Lord Yan as a material inside!

Lord Yan could never have imagined how a mere Rank 9 cultivator could manipulate a Rank 7 dharma treasure.

Furthermore, no one present could discern the true nature of this dharma treasure.

This was because the Three-Five Cauldron was not a physical object, but rather a set of stabilized ethereal qi directly branded within Shen Shuibi's body. It simply manifested when she needed to use it.

To others, it merely looked like her own spell art.

Seeing that he was about to be sucked in, Lord Yan immediately let out a loud roar. He then ruthlessly severed his own arm, allowing a portion of it to be sucked inside.

Shen Shuibi's expression, however, turned somewhat grave.

Although the Three-Five Cauldron was slightly stronger than these people, it wasn't an offensive item; it could only achieve miraculous results as a trap.

If its existence was exposed, it would be very difficult to use it effectively a second time.

_Hmm... but if the opponent is only at Rank 8, it shouldn't be a problem, right?_ Shen Shuibi tilted her head, thinking to herself.

Especially since the opponent was a Rank 8 of the Daoist Sects.

Yes, she could tell at a glance that the Lord Yan before her cultivated a Daoist Sect cultivation method, specifically the method of true fire and true medicine.

It was just... since they were both of the Daoist Sects, she didn't really want to fight.

Therefore, facing the panicking and furious Lord Yan who had just lost an arm, Shen Shuibi suddenly said, "When refining Inner Alchemy with true fire, the difficulty lies in regulating the true timing of the medicinal fire. When the inner storage is truly present, what is there seems as if it were not, causing it to return to nothingness, like an empty void of fire."

"This is why the _Scripture of Alchemy_ states: 'The profound mastery of using fire is like having it yet seemingly not having it. Using fire without fire, not clinging to the appearance of using it—this is having attained it.'"

After saying this, she advised, "Your use of fire is too intense. It may seem to match the description in the _Chapters on Awakening to the Truth_, which says, 'Inside there is natural true fire, the furnace is fiercely and eternally red,' but it loses the essence. Your success rate in refining pills must be very low, right?"

Lord Yan was originally both shocked and enraged, but Shen Shuibi suddenly spoke up, pointing out the flaws in his cultivation method.

His fury instantly turned into shock, but upon listening carefully, it felt like divine enlightenment washing over him! Many past bottlenecks in his cultivation suddenly opened up!

Amidst his shock, he immediately realized that this woman was absolutely not a Rank 9!

He had encountered an expert!

And he had dared to attack her!

Yet, she did not hold a grudge, even pointing out the flaws in his cultivation for him—

At this thought, Lord Yan completely forgot about everything else, not even caring about his severed arm. He hurriedly dispersed his flame body, knelt down single-handedly, and kowtowed. "Thank you for your guidance, Senior!"

But even as he bowed, he was scratching his ears and cheeks, fraught with unbearable anxiety.

This Senior had only spoken halfway! She had only pointed out the flaw without explaining how to resolve it!

However, he didn't dare to rush her, terrified of disturbing the Senior.

This scene shocked everyone present!

After speaking, Shen Shuibi pondered for a moment and continued, "Fundamentally, your cultivation method should emphasize the method of 'the fire of innate qi merging into the true breath to assist the primal spirit.' But the drawback of this is... Mmph! Mmm!"

However, halfway through her sentence, Li Qi ran up and covered her mouth.

_Are you kidding me? This is premium knowledge! How could you just give it away?_

_These are the insights of a Rank 5 powerhouse! Shen Shuibi might lack scheming, but did he?_

On the other side, the young nobleman also realized the situation.

Just as he had a Dao Protector, the person before him also had a significant background.

In that case, whether it was protecting that tiger demon or resisting the curse and divination, it must have been the work of this man.

His gaze darkened by three degrees, but a bright smile blossomed on his face. In the next blink of an eye, even his gaze had become radiant and genial. He cupped his hands to Li Qi and said, "Brother, you are exactly as I suspected. May I ask your family background and esteemed name?"

He didn't even pay attention to Lord Yan, who was kneeling on the ground in fear and awe, instead greeting Li Qi with a gentle expression.

Li Qi, covering Shen Shuibi's lips, leaned in close and whispered into her ear, "You go take care of Piaoxu. Leave the rest to me."

The breath from his words tickled Shen Shuibi's ear. She squirmed uncomfortably, hastily broke free from Li Qi's grasp, nodded, and then pattered away to the back.

It was better if she didn't speak anyway. She wasn't good at communicating to begin with. She once went three hundred years without saying a single word, just spacing out by herself and wandering around Mount Luofu like a ghost.

Because of this incident, Lady Luofu had once commented that she might as well practice a vow of silence. That way, it might yield a miraculous effect later on, adding another divine ability to her repertoire.

However, Shen Shuibi hadn't wanted to speak simply because of her mood, not to cultivate some method. If she deliberately pursued a divine ability by refusing to speak, it wouldn't be her anymore. So, she declined the suggestion.

Hearing her refusal at the time, Lady Luofu was very pleased. She said Shen Shuibi had grasped the true meaning of 'unfettered freedom,' and a future at Rank 5 was promising.

Sure enough, a few hundred years later, Shen Shuibi successfully broke through to Rank 5.

Thinking of this, the rabbit shook her head.

In this short period of barely a year, she had spoken more than she had in the previous thousand years combined.

_Why is that?_

_It's a bit strange._

But strange or not, things still needed to be done. Shen Shuibi returned to Piaoxu's side and picked up the fox, who had reverted to her true form.

_Fox tails are so warm and soft._

The rabbit touched her own tailbone.

_Should I grow one too? Or should I just pet this one more?_

The rabbit suddenly jolted in alarm, immediately recalling the times she had been petted.

_No! Wouldn't I just become someone like Li Qi if I did that?!_

Thinking this, she hugged the fox and retreated quite a distance.

From start to finish, she hadn't felt the slightest bit tense.

There was no need to be tense. She had seen far too many grand scenes; this was well within manageable limits.

Li Qi was much more infuriating.

_Hmph!_

_I'm stealing all his food tonight!_

However.

While things were peaceful on her end.

Everyone else present had their own thoughts swirling.

Although Li Qi knew Shen Shuibi used to be Rank 5, the combat prowess she had just displayed still took him by surprise, though it subsequently bolstered his confidence even more.

In response to the young nobleman's inquiry, Li Qi answered, "My name is Li Qi, just a man of the wilderness. I hail from the Great Deer Kingdom, which is quite a distance from here. I am merely traveling at the moment."

"Ah, I see," the young nobleman replied with a smile.

Though he smiled outwardly, he was secretly alarmed, and a sense of wariness began to rise within him.

Lord Yan was already a powerhouse among Rank 8s, but seeing him now, he seemed completely inferior to the woman beside Li Qi, even treating her with the grand courtesy owed to a senior. Was that his Dao Protector?

_Is she an expert at the absolute peak of Rank 8, just a hair's breadth away from Rank 7...?_

_Or is she simply a Rank 7?_

_But no matter how I look at it, she only seems to be Rank 9..._

_It must be a disguise. My eyesight is too poor to see her true depths._

With these thoughts in mind, even though Li Qi claiming to be a 'man of the wilderness' was clearly mocking him, he acted as if he hadn't noticed and maintained a smile full of warmth.

"My father is the mountain god of the Green Mountain. I am the third son of the family, Jin Buhuan. Brother Li Qi, this place is not convenient for conversation. Why don't we find a tavern and chat at our leisure?"

Hearing this, Li Qi also secretly found it troublesome.

_The mountain god of the Green Mountain... seems to be a Rank 6 existence as well._ Just like the mountain god of White Snake Mountain and the water god of the Li River, they were all mighty figures who dominated an entire region.

By now, he had profoundly realized the chasm between each rank. The saying 'each rank is a different heaven' was truly no joke.

Take the Martial Dao, for instance. Before entering the ranks, he could lift six thousand jin and sprint twenty meters in a second with ease.

Upon reaching Rank 9, his strength easily reached fifty thousand jin. The properties of Carp Stream Intent could temporarily double it to a massive force of a hundred thousand jin, and his speed could approach the speed of sound.

The gap between Rank 8 and Rank 9 was even larger...

However, he had Shen Shuibi as a safety net.

With this thought, Li Qi immediately made a decision. "There is no need for that, Young Master Jin. Time is of the essence. If there is any misunderstanding, it's best to clear it up right here."

He had no intention of going out drinking with these people. Who knew what kind of trap he might fall into? It was better to air out any issues right here; he didn't even know why the other party had attacked him in the first place.

And so, both sides simultaneously plastered on smiles, acting as if the previous conflict had never happened.

It was quite normal. Were they really supposed to fight to the death?

People with brains might bully the weak as a hobby, but they certainly wouldn't go mad and fight someone to the death.

Therefore, both the young nobleman and Li Qi tacitly chose to forget what had just occurred.

Any issues would be discussed later.

Since they currently lacked the confidence and ability to crush the other party, it was better to keep up appearances.

However, Li Qi cast a sidelong glance at the unconscious fox behind him.

_Did this young nobleman attack because of Piaoxu?_

_To think she was forced back into her original form just from being scolded. That's truly..._

After being rejected by Li Qi, Jin Buhuan's expression stiffened for a moment. But before he could speak, Li Qi immediately added, "Young Master Jin, I presume you came for this fox demon?"

"A fox demon? Wasn't it a tiger demon?" Jin Buhuan asked in confusion.

"Ah, I see. I think I know why now." Li Qi also wore a beaming smile. "So it was this kind of misunderstanding."

Li Qi cupped his hands. "This fox demon of mine previously encountered a dead tiger demon and absorbed a remnant ethereal soul from it. I assume this is what caused Young Master Jin's misunderstanding?"

Jin Buhuan also put on an expression of sudden realization. "I see! Then it truly was a misunderstanding. It was I who was rash! I offer my apologies to Brother Li."

Then, he walked over with a smile and helped the red-bearded, red-haired middle-aged man up. "Lord Yan, get up. If you wish to pay your respects to the Senior Expert, we will prepare gifts and return later. Why plead bitterly now?"

"This time, it was our rashness that caused a conflict with Brother Li. Brother Li, why don't you tell me where you are currently staying? I will take a few days to cleanly handle the matters here, then pay you a visit to dissolve today's unpleasantness!"

"Haha! What unpleasantness? I had a very pleasant conversation with Young Master Jin. As for my residence, just look at this fox, and you will know. I will await Young Master Jin there," Li Qi laughed heartily.

Both men appeared completely amicable, as if they were having a delightful chat.

Afterward, both sides took their leave, leaving behind a scene of utter devastation and countless swirling rumors.

Merchants were always the most well-informed group.

Soon, more than half of Clear Mist City knew about the incident.

Jin Buhuan, the son of the Green Mountain's mountain god, had caused a massive uproar in the market, causing hundreds of thousands of gold in damages all at once.

However, with his immense wealth, he quickly compensated everyone, shop by shop, and the matter was considered settled.

What else could they do? Make trouble for this second-generation young master?

The Clear Mist Sect did lose the physical bodies of three Rank 9 enforcers, so they went to make some trouble. However, they couldn't do anything to the young master. After all, strictly speaking, Clear Mist City also relied on the Green Mountain for its livelihood, making it difficult to completely fall out with them.

It was likely because Jin Buhuan knew this that he dared to be so arrogant and domineering.

Jin Buhuan was quite famous, so although what he did was a big deal, it merely became fodder for gossip in Clear Mist City. After all, that was just an ordinary small market covering no more than ten square li. Compared to the entirety of Clear Mist City, it was merely a tiny corner.

Compared to this, it was the other person mentioned in the rumors who truly captured the attention of the various factions in Clear Mist City.

Their motives were unknown, they could not be divined, and they claimed to be from the Great Deer Kingdom.

One had to know, the Great Deer Kingdom was over a hundred thousand li away from the Green Mountain Kingdom. This was an incredibly vast distance for a Rank 9 cultivator; even a Rank 8 wouldn't cross such a distance without a good reason.

Moreover, and more importantly, Jin Buhuan's guard—Lord Yan, a Rank 8 of the Daoist Sects specializing in fire-element cultivation methods—had his arm severed by one of them and nearly died!

Afterwards, Jin Buhuan even apologized publicly, completely disregarding his pride, and even promised to pay them a visit later.

It had to be known that this young master was usually someone who valued his face above all else.

All these events, combined with the complete unknown of their background and whereabouts, making it impossible to confirm any specific intelligence, cast a veil of illusion over this mysterious visitor.

[^1]: **Zi (子) and Wu (午):** These are terms from the Chinese Earthly Branches. Zi represents midnight, the North, and the Water element (utmost Yin birthing Yang). Wu represents noon, the South, and the Fire element (utmost Yang birthing Yin). In internal alchemy, they symbolize the exact moments and specific pathways for circulating qi through the body. "Aligning to make three" refers to the harmony of these opposing forces (Yin and Yang) creating a new, unified state.
[^2]: **Wu (戊) and Ji (己):** These are terms from the Heavenly Stems. Both represent the Earth element and the "Center." Wu is Yang Earth, and Ji is Yin Earth. In Daoist alchemy, Earth acts as the crucible or the "matchmaker" (representing true intent or mindfulness) that binds Water and Fire together. In traditional Chinese numerology (the He Tu or River Map), the Earth element is represented by the number five.
[^3]: **Eight Minerals (八石):** In traditional external alchemy, these were eight literal foundational minerals used to forge immortality pills (typically including substances like cinnabar, realgar, and mica). In internal alchemy—which is what Shen Shuibi is practicing here—they act as a metaphor for the essential energies and physical components within the cultivator's own body.
