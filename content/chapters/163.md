# Chapter 163: Half a Year

The Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

This was a lawless land spanning ten thousand miles, teeming with demons and covering an area as large as an entire kingdom.

Though it could not compare to the great empires, it was far more terrifying than some minor nations.

To this day, the identity and origins of the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord remained a mystery. It was only known that he possessed a divine ability to devour the heavens. When unleashed, it swallowed the sky and washed over the sun with overwhelming dominance, plunging the day into pitch-black darkness, as if a profound abyss had descended.

It sounded incredibly imposing, but as the signature move and representative divine ability of a Rank 5 mighty figure, it undoubtedly possessed its own unique profundities.

As the master of the demon nest—which was even named after him—the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord commanded the Six Demon Kings of Heavenly Filth. They were six toad spirits whose origins had been fully uncovered. They had achieved the Dao by harnessing the filthy qi born from the excesses of heaven. Having reached Rank 6 in the Dao, each wielded terrifying might, surrounding the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord with a fierce and imposing presence.

Under the power and prestige of these seven great demons, countless minor demons had pledged their allegiance. They fought their own battles, and though they nominally answered to the Six Heavenly Filth Demon Kings, most gathered their own factions, claimed mountains as their own, and carved out their own existence.

The Hundred Thousand Mountains of Baiyue were generally peaceful and orderly. While there were contradictions and friction between the various kingdoms, major conflicts rarely occurred. Thus, the existence of the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest was particularly glaring.

However, they were called wicked demons, rather than simple demons.

A simple demon was merely a member of a different race, possessing nothing particularly special. But to be called a wicked demon meant they were largely atrocious entities who committed every imaginable evil.

Furthermore, it wasn't just wicked demons; many Demon Dao cultivators and wanted criminals also made their home in the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest. The place had practically become the gathering ground for all the sinister forces in Baiyue.

The demons stationed in the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest caused black clouds to billow as they plundered the surroundings, leaving the people in abject misery.

Yes, the people were in misery. The Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest actually had "people" living within it.

From time to time, the demons would go out to pillage, competing to devour humans. After all... the human body contained the Pre-Heaven Three Treasures.

As a race born with innate sapience, the human race possessed the Pre-Heaven Three Treasures, making them far more nourishing than wild beasts. This was the primary reason most demons loved to eat humans.

It wasn't just man-eating demons; many human cultivators of the demonic path also favored using humans as materials. For example, refining a single Hundred-Soul Banner easily required over a hundred living souls.

The Pre-Heaven Three Treasures were true treasures, useful in a multitude of ways.

Fortunately, under the suppression of the Righteous Sword Palace and three other kingdoms, the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest was unable to expand outward.

Three kingdoms bordered the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest, and wars frequently broke out between them. The demon nest constantly raided these kingdoms for their population, forcing the three nations to continuously bolster their border defenses against the demonic incursions.

This was Li Qi's first time witnessing a state of "combat readiness" in this world. What's more, the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest was fighting a one-against-four war without losing ground; their expansion had merely been halted.

However, Li Qi also discovered along his journey that the basic strategy of the three kingdoms was strict defense. They resolutely stayed behind their walls, fending off the demons when they attacked, but adamantly refusing to step half a foot into the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

The only ones who truly dared to charge in and slay the demons were those from the Righteous Sword Palace.

These were mostly idle rumors Li Qi had picked up while gathering information on the road.

In reality, nearly half a year had passed. Li Qi had been traveling constantly toward the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest. Along the way, he continuously gathered intelligence regarding the Righteous Sword Palace and the demon nest.

After all, no matter what one did, one had to understand the situation before taking action.

---

Time flew like a shuttle. Since leaving Clear Mist City, Li Qi had traveled continuously and was now close to the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

At this moment, he was at the border of the Bailin Kingdom. Once he exited the border and crossed the frontier pass, he would be heading straight into the demon nest.

Right now, surrounded by a crowd, Li Qi was holding a sacrificial rite.

Wearing his usual clothes without any special attire, Li Qi held a spiritual wooden branch. Standing before an altar, he chanted loudly toward the sky:

"The people regard food as their heaven, food relies primarily on grain, and grain is rooted in water. Without grain, the people perish; without water, the grain cannot grow. Since this spring, the high gods have hoarded the rain, leaving the lower realm parched. The great rivers shrink, the streams run dry, the fields crack open, and the crops wither. This river basin suffers most severely. Early rice was barely planted due to the lack of water, and mid-season rice seedlings were damaged for the same reason. Now is the peak season for transplanting mid-season rice, yet this land remains blighted by drought. With no water to prepare the fields, it is a matter of life and death."

"If the mountain god blesses the common people, the common people revere the mountain god. If the mountain god ignores the common people, the common people will lose faith in the mountain god. The high and the low are interconnected; god and people depend upon each other."

"I implore the mountain god to see reason and cease hoarding the mountain qi!"

Li Qi recited the sacrificial litany. It was written plainly, without much overly flowery language, for fear the other party wouldn't understand.

He was addressing the mountain god because, in truth, making it rain was largely the job of the mountain gods.

Clouds were the qi of mountains and rivers, which led to rain.

Cloud qi was formed when the qi of mountains, rivers, and water veins evaporated into the sky; it was not true heavenly qi.

Therefore, among the six qi of heaven, there was no category for cloud qi.

Consequently, when it didn't rain, one either needed a mighty figure capable of transporting water fortunes—or even directly siphoning endless water from the ocean like a True Dragon—or it was best to simply ask the mountain god.

After all, mountains and waters relied on each other. A mountain god's reserves definitely contained water veins; otherwise, the mountain would not be a proper mountain and could not house a mountain god.

Under the hopeful gazes of the numerous villagers, Li Qi waited before the altar for a moment.

However, the rain still did not fall.

Li Qi felt somewhat embarrassed, while the villagers broke out in wails and even resentment.

However, Li Qi turned around and said, "Fellow villagers, please calm yourselves. I will be right back."

With that, he whistled.

Suddenly, a robust dragon colt descended from the sky. It was clad in black scales resembling dark armor and stepped upon auspicious clouds. Standing eight and a half feet tall at the shoulder, it was majestic and awe-inspiring, with a flowing mane crackling with faint currents of electricity. As it raised its head and neighed, a sound like thunder echoed. Descending from the sky riding the wind and clouds, its prestige was unparalleled.

As the dragon colt descended, the villagers fell to their knees in trembling awe.

Li Qi mounted the dragon colt, gave its flank a pat, and charged directly into the mountain they had been offering sacrifices to.

The villagers looked at each other in bewilderment.

But merely a few minutes later, the fierce beasts in the mountain seemed to show terror, fleeing in panic. A great wind howled, alternately churning clouds and spitting mist, plunging the world into obscurity. At times, sand and stones flew through the air amid rumbling sounds.

A moment later, pure qi suddenly rose from the foot of the mountain, condensing into a radiant glow that coiled around the peak without dispersing.

A strange wind slowly plummeted, spiraling through the mountain. Sometimes it tangled with the divine qi, darting up and down, turning relentlessly. A flash of lightning closely pursued the strange wind, mirroring its every twist and turn, refusing to let go.

Next, the roar of thunder shook the air, sounding as though the mountain itself were collapsing. The strange wind scattered, fleeing into the ripples and vanishing without a trace.

The lightning flashed once more, giving chase.

About fifteen minutes later, black clouds descended from the sky. Vital qi swirled through the firmament, and raindrops began to fall.

It started as a soft pitter-patter, like pearls rolling on a jade plate, before escalating into a torrential downpour. It was as if the sky itself had cracked open; like ten thousand horses stampeding out of their troughs, the sweet rain drenched the earth.

Then Li Qi reappeared, walking out of the mountain pass. His complexion was as calm as ever, his clothes as clean as new. He leisurely returned to the altar.

The villagers were still dumbfounded.

"Alright. The sweet rain has fallen, so I will take a sliver of your village's human qi, as agreed. That mountain god has promised me that for the next three years, he won't dare to withhold the water qi. Over these three years, I can't guarantee perfect winds, but you will certainly have favorable rain."

Only after Li Qi finished speaking did the villagers finally snap back to reality.

They immediately fell to their trembling knees. All their previous complex thoughts vanished, replaced by cries of "Great Shaman bless us!" as they kowtowed repeatedly.

"There is no need to bow. The mountain god was unrighteous, and as an Invocator, it is my duty to voice the needs of the people. But you must also remember: if a god is unrighteous, you may appeal, but you must not be disrespectful in your regular sacrifices. As I said, the high and the low are interconnected, god and people depend upon each other, and all things are equal." Li Qi waved his hand.

With that, adhering to their prior agreement, he reached out and extracted a single sliver from the massive convergence of human qi gathered by the crowd.

Then, he turned and headed back into the mountains.

He left behind a crowd of villagers who—whether they had truly listened or not—continued to shout "Great Shaman" and kowtow endlessly.

While passing through and temporarily resting here, he had heard that the local mountain god was withholding the rain. He took up the matter, but as compensation, he would collect a wisp of the local human qi.

Because he collected it *after* helping them, there were no karmic entanglements.

Gathering that human qi was Li Qi's very process of cultivation. Through this process, his cultivation base grew increasingly profound.

However, he had been deliberately suppressing the cultivation of his internal deities. He was waiting for the opportunity the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord had mentioned to condense his primal spirit. Doing so would be far more beneficial for his future cultivation.

Li Qi left the crowd and entered the mountain.

The moment he stepped inside, Old Ma approached, neighing softly. On the horse's back sat a weasel.

The weasel's expression was incredibly human-like. Upon seeing Li Qi enter, it immediately spoke in a human tongue: "O Shaman, living in this world, one must be reasonable, right? Cursed be those unreasonable mortals! I treated them with good intentions, but they returned it with malice. Whenever outsiders come, they only whine about their own grievances, completely forgetting how I treated them in the past. I merely hoarded the water qi to starve them for a year as a warning, and you come beating down my door! Where is the justice in that!"

As it turned out, this weasel was the local mountain god.

Li Qi looked at it and replied calmly, "No matter how great your grievances, withholding rain for a year is still wrong. When it doesn't rain in this region for a year, setting aside human life and death, what about the surrounding forests, the distant beasts, the fish and shrimp in the rivers? Which of them hasn't suffered terribly because of you? As a mountain god, do you merely enjoy the incense of the Divine Dao without performing your actual duties?"

"Severing the water qi of a mountain out of selfish desire is something you should never do, no matter the reason. My punishing you this time is precisely for that. Since you walk the Divine Dao, you must understand that gods exist because of the people. Receiving their sacrifices means you must take action on their behalf."

The weasel, originally full of righteous indignation, instantly lost a fraction of its momentum upon hearing this. It muttered softly, "The rivers drying up, the forests withering, the beasts languishing... that, well, that is naturally my fault. But that village was truly out of line!"

The weasel grew furious again. "They used to worship me constantly, and I naturally blessed them with favorable weather. But later, the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest expanded its reach. I sacrificed sixty years of my cultivation to shelter this area and keep the peace. However, my strength was truly lacking. The demons broke through my bewildering formation and snatched three to four hundred people from that village... Afterward, they smashed my idol and destroyed my shrine! They nearly severed my path in the Divine Dao! How could I tolerate that?!"

"How many people were in that village total?" Li Qi asked in return.

The weasel froze for a moment, recalling, "About a thousand."

"In a village of a thousand people, three to four hundred were snatched away. Thirty to forty percent of their population vanished, and you blame them for no longer sacrificing to you?" Li Qi asked again.

"You can't blame me for that! I lost sixty years of cultivation to lay down that bewildering formation; I did everything I could." The weasel was indignant, no longer even fearing Li Qi.

"Then talk to them. You severed the water qi veins without saying a single word; how could they possibly continue to worship you?" Li Qi waved his hand. "You have your difficulties, and they have theirs. Humans and gods depend on each other. How can you break ties over a momentary fit of anger? Without them, how will you walk your Divine Dao path? Do you think you're one of those great gods worshipped across ten thousand miles, who wouldn't care if one or two villages lost faith?"

"The foundation of the Divine Dao lies in power and sincerity. Think it over yourself. It's already difficult enough for the weasel clan to achieve the Dao, and you are relatively dutiful and kindhearted. Continue to bless them with favorable weather for the next three years, and see what happens then." Having said his piece, Li Qi grabbed the weasel, tossed it up the mountain, and without lingering, mounted his horse and galloped toward the main road.

There was nothing worth lingering for. This kind of scene was no longer a rare occurrence on Li Qi's journey.

Along the way, he had done this countless times.

Mediating between humans and gods, between humans and ghosts, and between ghosts and gods. Amidst all sorts of conflicts, as an Invocator, he stood at the center. He acted with uprightness and treated others with righteousness, resolving misunderstandings and contradictions, delivering fair judgments, and then collecting their corresponding qi.

This was one of the cultivation methods of an Invocator.

Of course, if one found this too exhausting, they could simply squat in the mountains and cultivate slowly, meticulously carving out their personal cosmos bit by bit. That could lead to advancement as well.

But compared to Li Qi's method, it would be much slower.

Every time Li Qi collected an external qi, he was filling his own personal cosmos. For example, this time, he had obtained the human qi of a village. Once his personal cosmos was perfected, a settlement similar to this village would appear within it.

According to the texts, if one's personal cosmos could bear it, and one gathered every type of 'qi' in the world, then their personal cosmos could reach a state of absolute perfection.

However, directly harvesting qi would taint one with karmic cause and effect.

For instance, if Li Qi had directly taken the human qi from that village instead of restoring balance beforehand, he would be tainted by the karma of the village, which would be highly detrimental to his future development.

Some rural wild shamans only realized they could harvest other types of qi without understanding the consequences. Consequently, their cultivation progressed by leaps and bounds early on, only for them to pay a thousand or even a million times over later to settle their karmic debts.

The Shaman Dao prized uprightness, so one had to remain constantly vigilant.

Otherwise, if the debts became insurmountable, one would likely have to change their path—for instance, by joining the demonic path, devouring karma as nourishment, and taking plunder as their sole duty.

Riding Old Ma, Li Qi soon returned to the roadside.

Standing by the side of the road was Shen Shuibi, dozing off.

Yes, standing.
