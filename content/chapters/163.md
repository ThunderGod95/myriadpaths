# Chapter 163: Half a Year

The Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

This was a land of chaos spanning ten thousand miles, teeming with fiends, covering a territory as large as a kingdom.

While it could not compare to the great empires, it was far more terrifying than some smaller nations.

The identity and origins of the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord remained a mystery. It was only known that he possessed a heaven-devouring divine ability. When unleashed, it swallowed the sky and eclipsed the sun with overwhelming dominance, plunging the world into pitch-black darkness as if a profound abyss had descended.

It sounded incredibly imposing, but as the signature move and representative divine ability of a Rank 5 mighty figure, it undoubtedly possessed its own unique profundity.

As the master of the demon nest—which was even named after him—the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord commanded the Six Demon Kings of Heavenly Filth. Their origins had already been thoroughly investigated: they were six toad spirits who attained their Dao through the filthy qi born from heavenly obscenity. Having reached Rank 6, each possessed immense power, surrounding the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord with a terrifying aura.

Under the power and prestige of these seven great demons, countless minor demons served as vassals within the nest. They fought amongst themselves. Although they nominally obeyed the orders of the Six Demon Kings of Heavenly Filth, most of them gathered their own factions, claimed a mountain as their domain, and fended for themselves.

The Hundred Thousand Mountains of Baiyue were generally peaceful and orderly. While there were conflicts and friction between the various kingdoms, major wars rarely broke out. Thus, the existence of the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest was particularly glaring.

However, they were, after all, called fiends, not simply demons.

Ordinary demons were merely a race, nothing more. But those labeled as fiends[^1] were almost entirely vicious, committing all manner of atrocities.

It was not just fiends; many cultivators of the Devil Dao and wanted criminals also stationed themselves in the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest. It had already become the primary gathering place for the sinister factions of Baiyue.

As the creatures of darkness settled in the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest, dark clouds constantly roiled. They pillaged relentlessly, leaving the people in abject misery.

Yes, the people were in misery, for there were actually mortals living within the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

The fiends would frequently go out to plunder, competing to devour humans. After all... the human body contained the Pre-Heaven Three Treasures.

As a race born with innate spiritual wisdom, humans possessed the Pre-Heaven Three Treasures, making them far more nourishing than wild beasts. This was why most fiends loved to eat humans.

It wasn't just man-eating demons; many human devils also took great pleasure in using humans as materials. For example, forging a single Hundred-Soul Banner easily required over a hundred living ethereal souls.

The Pre-Heaven Three Treasures were true treasures, useful in many different fields.

Fortunately, under the suppression of the Righteous Sword Palace and three other kingdoms, the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest was unable to expand outward.

The three kingdoms bordering the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest frequently engaged in wars with it. The nest would regularly invade these nations to pillage their populations, prompting the three kingdoms to continuously fortify their borders against the demonic incursions.

This was the first time Li Qi had seen a true state of war readiness in this world. Moreover, the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest was fighting against four forces simultaneously without losing ground; its expansion was merely contained.

However, Li Qi also noticed on his journey that the primary strategy of those three kingdoms was strict defense. They adamantly refused to sally forth. When the demons came, they defended, but they absolutely refused to set foot inside the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

The only faction truly daring enough to charge in and slay the demons and eradicate the devils was the Righteous Sword Palace.

These were mostly rumors and idle chatter Li Qi had gathered along the way.

In fact, over the past half year, Li Qi had been steadily traveling toward the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest. Along the journey, he continuously collected information about the Righteous Sword Palace and the nest itself.

After all, no matter the task, one had to understand the situation before taking action.

---

Time flew by. Since leaving Clear Mist City, Li Qi had traveled continuously and was now approaching the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

At this moment, he was at the border of the Hundred Forests Kingdom. Once he crossed the border and passed the fortress garrison, he would enter the territory of the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

Right now, surrounded by a crowd, Li Qi was performing a ritual.

He wore his usual clothes, lacking any special ceremonial attire. However, he held a branch of spirit wood in his hand. With an altar set up before him, he chanted loudly toward the sky:

"The people regard food as their heaven, food relies on grain, and grain is rooted in water. Without grain, humanity perishes; without water, grain cannot grow. Since this spring, the high gods have withheld the rain, leaving the lower realms bereft of streams. Great rivers shrink, small rivers dry up, fields crack open, and crops wither yellow. This river basin suffers worst of all. The early rice was sparsely planted due to the drought, and the mid-season rice seedlings are damaged. Now is the peak season for transplanting the mid-season rice, yet this land remains parched. Without water to prepare the fields, it is a matter of life and death."

"When the mountain god blesses the commoners, the commoners revere the mountain god. When the mountain god ignores the commoners, the commoners lose faith in the mountain god. The heavens and the earth are connected; gods and mortals rely on one another."

"I urge the mountain god to see reason and cease hoarding the mountain qi!"

Li Qi recited the sacrificial incantation. The wording was straightforward, avoiding overly poetic or archaic language, for fear the recipient wouldn't understand.

He was addressing the mountain god because, in truth, bringing rain was largely the responsibility of mountain gods.

Clouds were the qi of mountains and rivers, and from them came rain.

Cloud qi was formed when the qi of mountain and river water veins evaporated into the sky. It was not true heavenly qi.

Therefore, among the Six Qi of Heaven, there was no such category as cloud qi.

Thus, during a drought, unless there was a mighty figure capable of shifting water veins or a True Dragon that could directly siphon boundless water from the ocean, it was best to honestly ask a mountain god for it.

After all, mountains and waters were codependent. The reserves of a mountain god inevitably contained water veins. Otherwise, the mountain would not be a true mountain, nor could it sustain a mountain god.

Under the hopeful gazes of the villagers, Li Qi waited before the altar for a moment.

However, the rain still did not fall.

Li Qi felt a bit awkward. The villagers began to wail, some even showing resentment.

But Li Qi turned back and said, "Fellow villagers, please remain calm. I will be right back."

With that, he whistled.

A robust dragon steed descended from the sky. It was covered in black scales like dark armor and trod upon auspicious clouds. Standing eight and a half feet at the shoulder, it was majestic and awe-inspiring, flanked by trailing fur. Its mane crackled with faint electric currents. As it raised its head and neighed, it sounded like rolling thunder. Dropping from the air, riding the wind and clouds, its prestige was unparalleled.

As the dragon steed descended, the villagers fell to their knees, trembling in fear.

Li Qi swung onto the dragon steed, patted its flank, and charged directly into the mountain of the ritual.

The villagers looked at each other in dismay.

Only a few minutes later, the fierce beasts of the mountain fled in terror. Howling winds surged, churning clouds and spewing mist, obscuring heaven and earth. At times, sand and stones flew through the air, accompanied by thunderous rumbles.

A moment later, pure qi abruptly rose from the foot of the mountain, condensing into a brilliant light that coiled tightly around the peak.

A strange wind slowly descended, spiraling through the mountains, sometimes weaving alongside the divine qi, darting up and down without pause. Following closely behind was a flash of lightning that twisted and turned with the strange wind, biting tightly at its heels and refusing to let go.

Then, a clap of thunder shook the air, sounding like a collapsing mountain. The strange wind scattered, fleeing into the ripples of space and vanishing without a trace.

The lightning flashed again, giving chase.

About fifteen minutes later, dark clouds descended from the sky, life energy swirled in the firmament, and raindrops began to fall.

At first, it sounded like pearls rolling on a jade plate. Then, it transformed into a raging torrent, as if the heavens had split open, sounding like ten thousand stampeding horses. The sweet rain showered down universally, soaking into the earth.

When Li Qi reappeared, he was walking out of the mountain pass. His expression was as calm as ever, and his clothes were as pristine as new. He leisurely returned to the altar.

The villagers were still dumbfounded.

"Alright, the sweet rain has fallen. As agreed, I will take a wisp of your village's human qi. The mountain god has promised me that for the next three years, he will not dare withhold the water qi again. For these three years, I cannot guarantee favorable winds, but the rains will certainly be timely."

Only after Li Qi finished speaking did the villagers finally react.

They instantly dropped to their knees, trembling. All their previous doubts vanished as they repeatedly kowtowed and cried out, "Great Shaman, protect us!"

"There is no need for such formalities. The mountain god was unkind, and as an Invocator, it is my duty to voice the needs of the people. But you must also remember, while an unkind god can be reprimanded, you must never be disrespectful in your regular offerings. As I said before, the heavens and the earth are connected, gods and mortals rely on one another, and all things are equal," Li Qi said, waving his hand.

Having said that, he reached out and, in accordance with their prior agreement, drew a single wisp from the massive cluster of human qi gathered around the crowd.

Following that, he turned around and headed back into the mountains.

He left behind a crowd of villagers who, whether they had internalized his words or not, continued to loudly praise the Great Shaman and kowtow endlessly.

While passing through and taking a brief rest, he had heard that the local mountain god was withholding rain. He took it upon himself to resolve the issue, asking only for a wisp of human qi as compensation.

Because he collected it only after providing his help, there were no karmic entanglements.

Collecting these wisps of human qi was a part of Li Qi's cultivation process. Through these actions, his cultivation base grew increasingly profound.

However, he had been constantly suppressing the cultivation of his internal deities. He was waiting for the fateful opportunity mentioned by the ruler of the Green Mountain Kingdom to temper his primal spirit. Doing so would bring greater benefits to his future cultivation.

Li Qi left the crowd and ventured deep into the mountain.

As soon as he entered, Old Ma approached him, neighing softly. On the horse's back sat a weasel.

The weasel's expression was incredibly human-like. Upon seeing Li Qi, it immediately spoke in the human tongue. "Great Shaman, surely those living in this world should act according to reason? Those unreasonable mortals are truly detestable! I treated them with kindness, yet they repaid me with malice. Whenever outsiders visit, they only complain about their own grievances, completely forgetting how I treated them in the past. I merely withheld the water qi to trap them for a year as a warning, and yet you came knocking on my door with violence. What logic is this?"

As it turned out, this weasel was the mountain god of this place.

Li Qi looked at it and calmly replied, "No matter how great your grievance, withholding rain for a year is unjustified. Without rain for an entire year in this region, setting aside human lives, what about the surrounding forests, the wild beasts in the distance, and the fish and shrimp in the rivers? Which of them hasn't suffered miserably because of you? As the god of a mountain, do you only enjoy the incense of the Divine Dao without actually doing your duties?"

"Severing a mountain's water qi for selfish desires is wrong under any circumstance. That is why you were punished this time. Since you walk the Divine Dao, you must understand that gods exist because of the people. The very essence of divinity combines the altar of offerings with the forces of nature. Having received their sacrifices, it is only right that you fulfill your duties."

The weasel, originally self-righteous, immediately lost a portion of its confidence upon hearing this. It muttered softly, "The rivers drying up, the forests withering, the beasts languishing... that, that was naturally my fault. But that village was simply going too far!"

The weasel grew indignant again. "They used to worship me constantly, and I naturally blessed them with favorable weather. But later, forces from the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest arrived. I sacrificed sixty years of cultivation to protect the peace of this region! It’s just that my strength was lacking, and the fiends broke through my illusion formation, snatching away three or four hundred people from that village... Afterward, the people smashed my idol and destroyed my shrine! They nearly severed my path in the Divine Dao! How could I tolerate that?!"

"How many people are in that village total?" Li Qi asked in return.

The weasel froze for a moment, recalling, then answered, "About a thousand."

"In a village of a thousand, three or four hundred were taken. Thirty to forty percent of their people disappeared, and you blame them for not continuing to worship you?" Li Qi pressed.

"But you can't blame me for that! I lost sixty years of cultivation setting up that formation. I already did everything in my power," the weasel grumbled indignantly, briefly forgetting its fear of Li Qi.

"Then talk to them. You severed their water qi veins without a single word of explanation. How can you expect them to continue worshiping you?" Li Qi waved his hand. "You have your difficulties, and they have theirs. Mortals and gods rely on each other; how can you sever ties over a momentary fit of anger? Without them, how will you walk your Divine Dao? Do you think you are one of those great gods worshipped for ten thousand miles around, who couldn't care less if one or two villages lose faith?"

"The foundation of the Divine Dao lies in the balance of your divine power and the faith shown at the altar. Think it over. It is difficult enough for the weasel clan to achieve the Dao, and you are fundamentally kind-hearted. Continue to protect them and ensure their favorable weather for three years, and then see what happens." Having said his piece, Li Qi grabbed the weasel and tossed it back up the mountain. Without lingering any longer, he mounted his horse and galloped toward the main road.

There was nothing left to stick around for. Such scenes were no longer rare on Li Qi's journey.

Along the way, he had lost count of how many times he had done this.

Mediating between mortals and gods, mortals and ghosts, and ghosts and gods. Amidst all sorts of conflicts, as an Invocator, he stood at the center. Acting with righteousness and treating others with justice, he resolved misunderstandings, delivered fair judgments, and then collected their corresponding qi.

This was one of the cultivation methods of the Invocators.

Of course, if one found this too exhausting, they could simply hide in the mountains and cultivate slowly, carving out their personal cosmos bit by bit. That, too, could lead to advancement.

But compared to Li Qi's method, it would be far slower.

Every time Li Qi collected an external qi, he was filling his personal cosmos. For instance, he now possessed the human qi of an entire village. Once his personal cosmos was perfected, a settlement similar to this village would appear within it.

According to the texts, if one's personal cosmos could withstand the burden and gather every type of qi in the world, then their personal cosmos would reach a state of flawless perfection.

However, directly harvesting qi would entangle one in karma.

If Li Qi had simply taken the village's human qi without first restoring balance, he would have been tainted by the village's karma, which would be highly detrimental to his future progress.

Some untutored village shamans only realized they could harvest qi but failed to foresee the consequences. Thus, their cultivation would advance by leaps and bounds in the early stages, only for them to pay thousands or millions of times over to settle their karmic debts later.

The Shaman Dao valued righteousness above all, so one had to remain constantly vigilant.

Otherwise, if the debts could not be repaid, they would most likely be forced to change their path—for example... by joining the Devil Dao, turning karma itself into nourishment, and dedicating themselves to plunder.

Riding Old Ma, Li Qi soon returned to the roadside.

By the road stood Shen Shuibi, dozing off.

Yes, standing.

[^1]: Let's discuss the these three:

    - Demon (妖): This refers to the separate race of beasts who have awakened spiritual wisdom.
    - Devil (魔): This is not exactly Chinese. It was specifically created by Buddhist translators to render the Sanskrit word **Mara**—the celestial demon who attempted to tempt Gautama Buddha away from enlightenment. Essentially, this refers to wicked beings.
    - Fiend (妖魔): The literal translation is wicked demon.
