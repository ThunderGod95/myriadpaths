# Chapter 137: The Jade Rabbit Pounds Medicine

Saying so, Shen Shuibi immediately began the treatment.

The fire evil resided in the lungs. The lungs belonged to metal, so the curse used the fire evil to scorch the lungs and choke off her breathing.

Shen Shuibi targeted the outer acupoints of the chest and the back alongside the five viscera. Then, she condensed the cold qi of the Supreme Yin into needles to suppress the fire evil. She targeted the Tiantu acupoint right in the center of the collarbone to dissipate the wicked qi within the lungs.

Just like that, the first evil was dispelled.

The metal evil resided in the liver. The liver belonged to wood, so the curse used the metal evil to sever the liver qi. Liver qi governed vitality; severing it would extinguish the body's life force.

Shen Shuibi continued by targeting the Xingjian acupoint—the Ying-spring point of the Veiled Yin liver meridian of the foot—to guide the qi. She pierced the azure-net meridians between the ears and redirected the previously suppressed fire evil, using fire to combat the metal evil. Once the fire burned away the metal, the second evil was dispelled.

Just like that, the second evil was gone. The formation of the five elements and five evils was instantly broken. The remaining three streams of wicked qi were isolated and forced to fight alone.

The wood evil resided in the spleen. The spleen stored blood and governed digestion; eating generated all things to support the five viscera and four limbs. The curse used an overabundance of vitality to overburden the spleen and stomach, making it difficult for the patient to take medicine, and rendering any ingested medicine impossible to digest.

Therefore, Shen Shuibi used her needles to pierce the Zusanli acupoint, the point of Bright Yang stomach meridian of the foot, activating the qi and blood. Working in tandem with the liver, she guided the wood evil out of the body.

Just like that, the third evil was gone.

The earth evil resided in the kidneys. The curse used earth qi to block the kidney water, causing the circulatory system to collapse. The bloodlines became stagnant, circulation halted, and the body could no longer circulate qi to heal itself.

Shen Shuibi sliced open the skin on the fox demon's lower back, draining the corrupted kidney water. Supplementing this with a Daoist Sects' qi-guiding method, she suppressed the earth evil and reconstructed the circulation, reconnecting the nutritive qi. With this, the earth evil could no longer harm the body and would only be slowly digested by the body over time.

Just like that, the fourth evil was gone.

Finally, the water evil resided in the heart. It manifested as cardiac edema, causing the heart's blood to swell and overflow. This induced heart colic and blockage. The bloodlines would clog in an instant, and the victim would drop dead within two quarters of an hour. Even Shen Shuibi didn't have a perfect solution for this.

Fortunately, the other four evils had already been eradicated, so the fox demon's body was in much better condition. Shen Shuibi applied her needles, using the sturdiness of the spleen to guide the heart's blood. She utilized the suppressed heavy earth wicked qi within the spleen to bury and alleviate the water evil.

Just like that, all five evils were gone. Every consequence brought about by the curse was shattered.

The rabbit wiped her sweat-free forehead and said in relief, "Phew~, done."

"Whoever did this is incredibly ruthless. Any one of these five evils could take a person's life. Unleashing all five evils in an interlocking chain... If it weren't for me, an ordinary doctor truly wouldn't be able to break it," the rabbit muttered to herself.

After all, she was a Rank 5 expert. Furthermore, the classic idiom "the Jade Rabbit pounds medicine" existed for a reason. It was essentially referring to someone exactly like Shen Shuibi. Her medical skills were truly outstanding. The fact that she could purge all the qi from her own body and reconstruct her circulation back then was already proof of her strength.

This operation was basically equivalent to extracting all the internal organs and blood vessels, replacing them all at once, and ensuring the patient didn't die in the process.

Back when she reconstructed her own circulation, she was her own patient and had to remain fully awake throughout the entire ordeal. The sheer difficulty of such a feat is unimaginable.

Just as she finished her work, she suddenly turned her head and looked outside.

Five imperceptible streams of qi had silently crashed into the divine qi barrier of the five household gods that Li Qi had constructed.

Li Qi immediately opened his eyes. He had sensed it too.

"Coming back for a second try? Just how deep is this grudge? Then don't bother leaving." Li Qi frowned. The divine qi of the five household gods surged, violently snapping back over the ghost qi of the Five Ghosts.

Although the quantity was the same, the quality was worlds apart. The ghost qi of the Five Ghosts was instantly locked inside. Li Qi compressed it into a small sphere and pocketed it.

Although this thing would lose its wondrous properties and the attached spell art would dissipate once it lost its master's control, it was still very useful as a clue.

"Thank you, Sir and Madam, for saving my wife's life!" Seeing this, the man realized that these two were the ones who had intervened. He hastily fell to his knees, kowtowing loudly. "Though my family possesses no great wealth, we will surely offer a generous reward. Thank you both!"

"Get up," Li Qi said, pulling the man to his feet before looking around.

Quite a few people upstairs were watching the spectacle. It seemed there was a reason no one had stepped forward earlier.

But it didn't matter.

Regardless of what lay behind this, they had already saved the person.

"What happened? Did your wife offend someone? Why would they strike so ruthlessly?" Li Qi asked, hauling the man up so he could stand steady.

The man seemed to have some cultivation, but like most people, he hadn't entered the Ranks.

In contrast, the fox demon had entered the Ranks and was capable of taking human form.

Right after asking, Li Qi glanced at the surrounding onlookers and frowned. "Let's go inside the room to talk."

Their brilliant display—especially Shen Shuibi's lightning-fast series of medical procedures—had already caught the eyes of many. Some people were even looking eager to approach and strike up a conversation.

Therefore, Li Qi wanted to speak inside the room to avoid wagging tongues.

But at this moment, the fox demon woke up. Though weak, she forced herself to speak: "Husband, go tend to the business..."

Seeing this, Shen Shuibi crouched down and patted the fox demon's head. "Still thinking about business? Do you have a death wish? Although the five evils are gone, two of them are suppressed within your body. You need to recover slowly and let your personal cosmos digest them naturally. You won't be able to do any strenuous work for at least half a year. Just be a good girl and let your husband take care of you."

With that, the rabbit frowned, looking at the submissive man with a hint of exasperation. "Alright, why are you so useless? It was one thing to cry earlier, but now you're just standing there dumbly? Aren't you going to carry your wife inside? Tell us what exactly is going on."

The previously panicked man snapped back to his senses. He hurriedly bowed in thanks, fulfilling all the proper etiquette, before bending down to carry the fox demon on his back. He led Li Qi and Shen Shuibi into the back kitchen and firmly shut the door.

Finally finding a quiet place, Li Qi took a seat. "Alright, tell us. What's the situation? Seeing how polite your wife is, how she restrains her demonic qi, and how she keeps her head down, she doesn't seem like the type to offend people."

"Sir is perceptive... I truly have never made enemies. But... the reason is likely because I hail from the Clear Mist Sect," the fox demon said weakly.

"The Clear Mist Sect? Ah, did you offend someone from the Clear Mist Sect?" Shen Shuibi tilted her head, not understanding why the cause would stem from there.

"I see." Li Qi nodded, already having a rough idea of the reason in his heart.

"Are disciples of the Clear Mist Sect in such high demand? Or did you sign some sort of contract?" Li Qi asked.

"Li Qi, what did you figure out now?" Hearing his questions, Shen Shuibi instantly knew he had formulated a theory.

It had been like this throughout their journey. Shen Shuibi was highly intelligent, but she lacked guile. Her mind was pure, so she was no match for Li Qi when it came to deduction and navigating schemes.

Thus, Li Qi explained, "What is the foundation of the Clear Mist Sect? You don't even need to think about it; it relies on the flesh trade. Even though fox demons are inherently promiscuous and mostly don't view this as shameful—instead seeing it as a way to boost their cultivation—this lady here is clearly not like that, right?"

"My wife indeed left the Clear Mist Sect because she was unwilling to sell her body. But... the Clear Mist Sect has always been amicable about partings. They wouldn't pursue someone for leaving..." the man said in disbelief.

"That's exactly why I said she's in high demand. With your wife's temperament... such refined aesthetics, yet retaining that faint, almost imperceptible allure... yes, she's no ordinary fox."

"Being so unique, she was likely targeted before she even became an official disciple, right? Who knows, maybe her price had already been negotiated."

"Even though the sect rules state that members can come and go as they please, her sudden departure definitely caused some people to lose money. Perhaps this is why someone is targeting you?" Li Qi guessed.

At this point, Li Qi tapped his knee. "There's one more thing. Although I didn't expose you outside, I did save your life. There's no need to keep up this act with the ghost, is there?"

As soon as he finished speaking, the man's body suddenly flickered, turning translucent. He then became utterly dazed, as if he had lost his soul.

"Controlling a _Chang_ Ghost[^1], not bad. I didn't notice it earlier. I only realized your husband was a _Chang_ Ghost under your control when I was setting up the formation with the divine qi of the five household gods. You're probably not just a simple fox either, right? A half-breed?" Li Qi asked.

"Did you secretly delude this _Chang_ Ghost into thinking he's your husband so you could live in seclusion here? To hide from your enemies? If so, could I further guess that you actually have a different background, and you merely fled to the Clear Mist Sect for asylum, using this identity to evade your foes?" Another thought sprang to Li Qi's mind.

Shen Shuibi, on the other hand, had a face full of confusion.

Consequently, she interrupted Li Qi and whispered, "Ah... why can't a _Chang_ Ghost be with a human? Is there a rule about that? Is there a difference between humans, ghosts, and demons?"

It turned out she had long recognized it was a _Chang_ Ghost. Come to think of it, she naturally wouldn't be fooled by such things. It was just that she seemed to think a ghost living a normal life with a living person was a perfectly ordinary occurrence.

Li Qi lowered his voice and leaned into her ear. "It might be perfectly normal on Mount Luofu, but that's not necessarily true in the outside world. Stop interrupting me, you're making us look like we're sneaking around."

"Mhm, mhm!" Shen Shuibi nodded. Then she deliberately straightened her face, pretending to be serious, and said no more.

Li Qi glanced at her.

_Kind of cute._

The tense, investigative atmosphere that had previously filled the room completely dissipated. The entire scene warmed up, abruptly shifting from a suspense thriller to a sitcom.

"Sigh," he breathed.

_Now the serious mood is ruined. But it should be fine, right?_

Therefore, Li Qi abandoned the increasingly oppressive aura he had been building up and spoke frankly, "Well then, Miss. There are too many suspicious points surrounding you. Although I saved your life out of a sense of righteousness, if you don't explain things clearly, the two of us will have to take our leave."

The fox demon couldn't stand up, but she still forced herself to bow. "Please do not be angry, Sir... I had no intention of hiding anything. I was simply too severely injured earlier to explain."

"Alright, you can speak now," Li Qi said. He walked over and channeled his Great Forest Wood qi, patting her shoulder.

The circulating vitality of the wood qi stabilized her injuries a bit more.

"Thank you, Sir. May I know your name?" the fox demon asked.

"Li Qi. And you, Miss?" Li Qi replied.

"I was once a wild beast and never had a surname. I only go by the name Piaoxu," the fox demon named Piaoxu said.

"Piaoxu. Not exactly an auspicious name,"[^2] Li Qi commented.

"It was given to me by the senior who enlightened me, and his words proved prophetic. My life... has truly been like a drifting catkin, tossed about by the waves, never finding peace."

"Alright, let's get down to business. You should at least let us know who we just saved, right?" Li Qi interrupted her self-pity, wanting her to get straight to the point.

Piaoxu naturally stopped with the pleasantries and revealed her background.

Once, Piaoxu had been a wild fox in the mountains, belonging to one of the fox tribes surrounding Clear Mist City.

"However, I was not enlightened by the Clear Mist Sect. One day, while hunting at the foot of the mountain, I stumbled upon a senior expert who was hunting a man-eating tiger demon."

"This expert was the senior who enlightened me. He engaged in a fierce battle with the tiger demon. Boulders shattered, and the earth trembled. I was still a wild beast back then. I was crushed by a falling rock, losing one of my ethereal souls and two of my corporeal souls. I was at death's door."

"After the expert slew the tiger demon, its remnant soul saw my corpse and attempted to possess it to continue fleeing. It ultimately failed, but the ethereal soul and corporeal soul of the tiger demon remained inside my body."

"Seeing this, the expert enlightened me. He guided me to devour the tiger demon's remnant soul within my body to mend my own souls. This caused my body to mutate into the form I have today."

"He told me then, 'You had no aptitude to begin with, yet you died because of me. After your death, you coincidentally seized a sliver of the heavenly mystery, snatching this tiger's destiny. Although you have entered the gates of cultivation, your future days will likely be difficult. I shall name you Piaoxu, in the hopes that although you drift, you will eventually land in a place that belongs to you, just like a catkin.'"

"After that, I became a demon and cultivated all the way. I also inherited several of the tiger demon's _Chang_ Ghosts... I had a few fortuitous encounters until I eventually joined the Clear Mist Sect."

She continued her story, and Li Qi listened silently.

Later, Piaoxu joined the Clear Mist Sect. Due to her bizarre aptitude—being half-tiger and half-fox, capable of controlling _Chang_ Ghosts and laying down illusions, while also being able to completely conceal the natural allure of a fox demon—she was viewed as a guaranteed candidate for the inner sect and received heavy grooming.

Piaoxu had no objections to this. She knew the Clear Mist Sect wasn't solely about the flesh trade. The disciples with true potential cultivated orthodox methods that blended the Medicine Dao with demonic cultivation—it was nothing unpresentable.

But she later discovered that she had been groomed from the very beginning to be the top courtesan.

At that point, she refused to play along. Relying on the sect rule that members could 'come and go as they please,' she eventually managed to extricate herself safely.

Since then, she had peacefully opened an inn within Clear Mist City. She used her _Chang_ Ghost as a 'husband' to avoid harassment. Just like that, fifteen years had passed.

"Fifteen years? In other words, although the Clear Mist Sect initially had some unpleasantness with you, they didn't actively seek to harm you... That does align with their consistent peacemaker style." Li Qi stroked his chin as he listened.

The Clear Mist Sect truly never went out of its way to stir up trouble. They pursued harmony to generate wealth, honestly managing Clear Mist City to make money. And this inn had been open for fifteen years without incident.

_So why now of all times?_

[^1]: Chang Ghost (伥鬼, chāng guǐ): In Chinese folklore, a person who is eaten by a tiger cannot pass on; instead, their soul becomes enslaved to the tiger as a Chang ghost. These bound spirits are forced to serve the tiger, often acting as lures to trick other humans into being eaten (giving rise to the idiom wèi hǔ zuò chāng — "to play the jackal to the tiger").
[^2]: Piaoxu (飘絮) literally translates to "drifting catkins" or willow fluff. In Chinese literature, drifting catkins are a classic motif used to symbolize a rootless, unstable existence. Because they are weightless and lack a foundation, they are entirely at the mercy of the wind. To be named Piaoxu implies a tragic destiny of wandering without control over one's own life, making it a highly inauspicious name.
