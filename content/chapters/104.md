# Chapter 104: Run!

White Snake Prefecture, Snake Tail County, on the streets.

It was already the dead of night. The markets had long since closed, leaving the streets completely deserted. Only Li Qi and Shen Shuibi, riding on horseback, remained, locked in a standoff with an old man in brocade robes a short distance away.

Li Qi raised his head, his expression arrogant. "If you don't have eyes, you deserve to be beaten."

He projected an air of absolute confidence.

Because he knew this was exactly how he needed to act right now.

The old man's eyes twitched. He silently put away his jade box, squeezing out a sinister smile as he looked at Li Qi. "Young hero, acting like this on the roads of the Jianghu… it can lead to unfortunate accidents."

"I think that in the Great Deer Kingdom, there are very few people capable of causing me an accident." Li Qi smiled, showing no fear.

"You are merely at Rank 9, yet you dare speak such words?" The old man in brocade robes opened the jade box. Suddenly, over a dozen ghosts surged out from within!

All of them were malicious ghosts.

The horde of ghosts arrived, their hideousness defying description.

Some had faces torn and flayed to the bone, blood flowing endlessly.

Some had elongated tongues, their unkempt hair like a basket drooping down to obscure their faces.

Some resembled charred corpses from a fire, emitting an incessant crackling sound as oily fluids flowed endlessly over their bodies.

Some were nearly three zhang tall, wearing green shirts and white trousers, their skin a stiff purple.

Some were covered in blisters, oozing pus that sizzled.

Some had snapped tendons and broken bones, looking as if they had been beaten to death with wooden clubs, only able to crawl on the ground.

As the ghosts appeared, faint glows resembling fireflies dotted the air. In an instant, they multiplied into millions, impossible to count.

Over a dozen ghosts surrounded Li Qi, flying back and forth around him. They dispersed and converged in a scene straight out of purgatory.

Seeing this, Li Qi's heart quailed. He felt an icy chill wash over him, as if his very blood was about to freeze.

This scene was not just terrifying; the aura of death and Yin qi filling the surroundings nearly suffocated him.

Most of these malicious ghosts were unranked, but among them, three were at the Rank 9 level!

They were a drowned ghost, a burned ghost, and a bludgeoned ghost.

"Now, tell me. Am I capable of causing you an accident?" The old man in brocade robes looked at Li Qi with a smile.

But Li Qi remained dismissive. "Just this? Not enough!"

Having said that, he stimulated the divine qi of Shentu and Yulei within his body.

In the vast sea lies Mount Dushuo, upon which grows a giant peach tree with twisting branches spanning three thousand li. Between its northeastern branches lies the Ghost Gate, where all ghosts enter and exit. Above it stand two divine figures: one named Shentu, the other Yulei. They govern and inspect the myriad ghosts. Any evil or harmful ghost is bound with reed ropes and fed to tigers!

Therefore, sage kings of ancient times established rituals to drive them away at the proper times. They hung peach charms on their doors, painted with the images of Shentu and Yulei, and suspended reed ropes to ward off evil spirits, preventing the myriad ghosts from entering their homes!

Shentu and Yulei were gods who devoured ghosts!

In the eyes of the malicious ghosts, the divine qi of the two gods was like a fortress forged of golden light. In a split second, all the circling malicious ghosts let out wails of agony and retreated a full three zhang away, not daring to approach any closer.

The brocade-robed old man's face instantly darkened.

Yet Li Qi did not dare to act rashly either.

The two sides fell into a bizarre standoff.

The old man in brocade robes was wary of Li Qi's willow branch and the divine qi of Shentu and Yulei, realizing that this young hero before him was not someone easy to deal with.

Logically speaking, a Rank 9 martial artist would be quite afraid of ghosts. They were not yet powerful enough to obliterate a ghost-god relying solely on Yang qi, fist intent, or sword intent. Facing intangible and formless ghosts, their ability to resist was not strong.

It was precisely because of this that the old man in brocade robes had the confidence to confront Li Qi.

After all, a Rank 9 in the Martial Dao facing three Rank 9 ghosts would essentially have no way to fight back.

However, he never expected this young hero to possess an ability that countered ghosts!

_Is he not of the Martial Dao?_

_Or perhaps he has a dharma treasure on him?_

_Can that seemingly ordinary willow branch strike ghosts because of the Shentu and Yulei divine qi, or can it do so on its own?_

_And where did that Shentu and Yulei divine qi come from? Did he cultivate it himself, or is it from some treasure?_

Nothing was clear, so he dared not make a careless move.

Li Qi was in the same boat. As an Invocator, he was very clear on the terror of ghosts. If he carelessly allowed ghost qi to pierce his body, his soul would detach from his physical form, and his spirit would wander, resulting in certain death.

And while it seemed he had the divine qi of Shentu and Yulei, his status as an Invocator was unranked!

If he were a Rank 9 Invocator, he would definitely charge forward and smash the opponent's head in. But he was just an unranked Shaman; he had no confidence in whether he could truly rely on the divine qi of Shentu and Yulei to hold off these ghosts.

After all, there were three Rank 9 ghosts!

If they swarmed him, he would really be in trouble.

Both sides harbored their own hesitations, leading to a temporary stalemate where they stood frozen in place.

Shen Shuibi, however, observed for a few seconds before leaning close to Li Qi's face and whispering in a low voice, "Ghost Shaman..."

Hearing this, Li Qi's spirits rallied!

Subsequently, he raised his head and proclaimed loudly, "I have heard that Shamans harbor Yang qi, and thus the people from regions with abundant Yang qi often become Shamans. There are also Shamans who ally with ghosts, thus such Shamans are Ghost Shamans. Ghost Shamans are likened to children's rhymes; therefore, a discerning Shaman can determine good and ill fortune. If one can determine good and ill fortune, they are a disciple of good and ill fortune. Thus, the vengeful spirit of Shen Sheng appeared before a Shaman—"

As Li Qi finished, the old man picked up the chant: "Such is the nature of a Shaman. Thus, the words of a true Shaman have no basis or cause; their declarations of good and ill fortune come straight from the mouth, like a child's rhyme. A children's rhyme is spoken from ones own mouth; a Shaman's words emerge from their own intent..."

The two of them seemed to be exchanging secret passcodes.[^1]

After finishing the exchange, both men seemed to breathe a sigh of relief and bowed to one another.

The old man in brocade robes opened his jade box, and all the evil ghosts retreated into it, securely stored away.

Li Qi also withdrew the divine qi of Shentu and Yulei, returning to normal.

However, Li Qi kept a firm grip on the willow branch, and the old man did not set down the jade box.

The old man spoke first, asking Li Qi, "To reveal my roots with a single breath... may I ask where Junior Brother learned his trade?"

Li Qi smiled and spat out three words.

"Shaman. God. Mountain."

The old man shuddered, nearly falling to his knees.

However, he was a Rank 9, while Li Qi was unranked. This disparity allowed him to barely steady his knees.

"Ju... Junior Brother, making such wild claims will get you killed. Impersonating a disciple of Shaman God Mountain will be detected by the Grand Diviner, and you're likely to be executed on the spot." The old man's tone softened significantly.

"Well, four breaths have passed since I spoke those words. Has Senior Brother seen the Grand Diviner unleash his fury?" Li Qi asked.

The old man looked him up and down.

Then, he bowed deeply, his attitude respectful. "I was unaware of the arrival of a disciple from Shaman God Mountain. Forgive my failure to welcome you. Please pardon my offense, Junior Brother. Are you here on a mission?"

"I was entrusted by the Grand Invocator to find someone. I am merely passing through. I was preparing to leave, but then I discovered you setting up an ambush with ghosts, which led to our conflict," Li Qi said.

"I would not dare block an envoy of the Grand Invocator. It was my rudeness, please forgive me! The next time Junior Brother visits Snake Tail County, I will surely offer lavish hospitality to fulfill my duties as a host!" The old man dropped to both knees, performing a grand gesture of respect to see him off.

He was not bowing to Li Qi.

He was bowing to the status of the Grand Invocator's envoy!

Li Qi said nothing more. He kicked his heels into the horse's belly, and Old Ma immediately took to the clouds, speeding away.

He left behind the old man, who stared in astonishment at the soaring horse and exclaimed, "A dragon colt!"

Then, he shook his head and muttered, "I really... picked up a second lease on life."

[^1]: In ancient Chinese political culture, spontaneous songs sung by children in the streets were often interpreted as cryptic messages from Heavem regarding the fate of an empire. Why children? Because they were innocent, uneducated, and lacked political agendas. Heaven could use them as perfect, unthinking vessels to deliver a message.

    The old man and Li Qi are applying this exact logic to shamans.

    They are arguing that a true shaman does not use intellect, study, or deductive reasoning to predict the future. Like a child singing a rhyme they don't understand, the shaman's words "spill from their mouths". The lack of rational foundation is actually the proof of the prophecy's authenticity. If the shaman had to think about it, it wouldn't be a true omen.

    As for vengful spirit of Shen Sheng appearing before a shaman, that is just Li Qi giving an example to prove his point. It is irrelevant so you do not need to think too much about it.
