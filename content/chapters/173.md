# Chapter 173: Empty Warehouse

Li Qi rode without pause, swiftly rushing to the military ration warehouse.

The journey was entirely peaceful. After all, this was near Vast Yang City, making it difficult for fiends to survive in packs here.

Where a city stood, human qi gathered, and all things sprouted from it.

In places where human qi gathered, five colors linked together, reaching up to touch the heavens[^1]. Its qi stabilized all things, connecting with the four seasons, and bridging the qi of above and below.

Through the eyes of Li Qi, a shaman, the human qi of the city connected the heaven and the earth, standing firmly on the ground like a towering mountain, difficult to shake.

Fiends fundamentally couldn't shake this kind of city.

Even if the tens of thousands of fiends he had encountered earlier formed a formation and gathered military qi, that military qi would likely dissipate after just one siege charge against the human qi of this city.

Therefore, it was absolutely safe here, or at least it was from Li Qi's perspective.

Galloping all the way to the granary, there were soldiers standing guard all around, though they all looked to be dawdling without any of that elite baleful qi to speak of.

However, Li Qi could understand. After all, they were watching a granary in the deep rear. Even the most elite soldiers would grow weary after standing guard for a few years, so naturally, they couldn't be that elite.

But upon seeing someone approaching on horseback, they roused themselves and raised their spears. An officer shouted loudly, "Granary grounds! No galloping! Dismount and verify your identity!"

Hearing this, Li Qi immediately vaulted off his horse and sprinted over, holding up the official document to display it to everyone. "An edict from the Minister of War! I need to requisition grain!"

However, upon hearing Li Qi's words, the officer's expression became extremely peculiar. He asked in great astonishment, "An edict from the Minister of War?"

"That's right, a handwritten edict from the Minister of War! Both the seal and signature are here!" Li Qi held up the document, thrusting it right before the officer's eyes.

"Hahaha, now this is fresh. I haven't received a handwritten edict from the Minister of War in two or three years. Alright, the item is authentic, go withdraw it yourself~." The officer was amused, laughing loudly. He didn't lead the way, but merely burped with alcohol, stepping aside to make way for Li Qi.

"You can still drink while on guard duty?" Li Qi frowned.

"What's it to you? Pretty boy, you don't know shit. In this place, if you don't drink, there's nothing else to do! Get lost, go get your grain." Hearing Li Qi's words, the officer immediately became irritated.

Li Qi naturally couldn't be bothered to ask for a snub. He walked quickly into the granary and pressed the handwritten edict against the array formation.

The array formation upon it automatically recognized the special aura of the Minister of War's seal, then opened the large doors.

Li Qi walked inside, taking out the White Rhino Army's military-issue mustard seed pouch.

But halfway through drawing it, Li Qi froze.

The entire granary was much larger inside than on the outside; it must have utilized a 'Hair Swallows the Sea' spatial technique.

Yet, no matter how vast the interior was, it wouldn't have shocked Li Qi into a daze.

What truly left him stupefied was the fact that the granary was empty.

Dust covered everywhere inside. While there weren't any cobwebs, the entire massive granary, large enough to hold food for a million people, was now completely empty. There wasn't a single grain of rice.

Li Qi immediately charged out the door, shouting, "What's going on!? Was the granary robbed!?"

"Shout, shout, shout, what are you shouting for? Is there something wrong with you? Did you just come back from the border pass? Didn't you hear what I just said? It's been years since a handwritten edict came down. Without an edict, the grain in here only goes out and never comes in. How could there be any grain left? Did you think I was neglecting my duties by drinking? I'm just bored out of my mind!" The officer leaned outside the door, mocking Li Qi.

"If the central command has no grain, then what's the deal with the war at the border pass?!" Li Qi reined in his rage, forcefully suppressing his anger as he asked.

"Are you even a man of the military? Didn't you come down from the border pass? Did you not know that it's been decommissioned over there? The news is over three months old; the border pass was abandoned! I recall your leading general was already reassigned, right? Did he not tell you these things?" The officer dug his ear with his pinky finger, speaking impatiently to Li Qi.

As these words were spoken, Li Qi was dumbfounded.

The White Rhino Army, it seemed, truly did not have a leading general.

At the border pass, there was also truly no one else.

Damn it, had the Hundred Forests Kingdom given up resisting??

They threw away the commoners, threw away the border pass... The only army seen gathering commoners had a leading general who actually hid the news and abandoned his troops to flee!?

Leaving behind only a platoon of soldiers willing to fight to the death, starving in place, protecting the commoners as they waited for death?

If they truly escorted the commoners to White Peak Mountain, would the local government there even accept them? If they weren't registered in the household census and refused asylum, wouldn't all these people become refugees?

Over a hundred thousand refugees, wait...

The beggars he had seen in the city earlier were not isolated cases.

Was the Hundred Forests Kingdom truly just leaving the border pass and all the commoners near it to wait for death?

The officer watched as Li Qi's expression gradually changed, from forcefully suppressing his anger, to dumbfounded shock, to uncontrollable fury. Then he took a deep breath, and finally, his face turned complex, his expression difficult to describe.

His complexion went from white to red, and from red to green, before he let out a long breath and returned to normal.

This series of changes amused the officer. He took a swig from his wineskin, then offered it to Li Qi. "Alright, don't be mad, brother. At least you're in Vast Yang City now and don't have to wait for death out there. Consider it a good thing. Come, take a sip and cool off."

Hearing this, Li Qi finally regained his rationality.

He glanced at the officer here and the listless soldiers, finally understanding why they were so dispirited.

Under these circumstances, it would be a miracle if they weren't dispirited.

But he couldn't afford to be dispirited. A hundred thousand people were still out there waiting for him to return with grain.

"I appreciate the gesture, but I still have important duties and cannot drink. Farewell." Li Qi took several deep consecutive breaths, cupped his hands, and then turned to leave.

"Hey, hey, don't go. What military duties do you still have? You don't want to go back just to throw your life away, do you? You guys coming down from the border pass are just foolishly naive. I've seen a few idiots like you over these past few months. Don't be so stubborn." The officer watched Li Qi leave, drunkenly waving at him.

But Li Qi didn't even turn his head back. He swiftly walked over to Old Ma, vaulted onto its back, and galloped off.

Along the way, Li Qi remained silent, his mind constantly pondering over the situation.

"Neigh..." Old Ma whinnied and slowed its pace, seeming to worry about Li Qi.

"No need to worry. Keep going; we're going to pay a visit to the Prefect," Li Qi stroked Old Ma's mane to console it.

Old Ma shook its head, feeling that thinking about it for half a day was useless anyway, so it simply gave up and continued running.

Riding on horseback, Li Qi thought continuously.

The military logistics of Vast Yang City had likely rotted to the core. That Vast Yang Minister of War was obsessed with the Buddhist Dharma and completely ignored government affairs, neither organizing grain collection nor grain distribution. The current granary had already been emptied out.

So, did the Vast Yang Minister of State, who was responsible for taxes, and the Prefect, who oversaw the grand scheme, know about these things?

If they knew, yet still hadn't resolved it, it meant the issue had grown quite severe. It probably couldn't be solved simply and likely involved more of the court's messy politics.

If they didn't know, it meant it might just be poor communication on the Minister of War's part. After reporting it, it might get resolved, but the side effect would be offending the Vast Yang Minister of War.

Offending someone wasn't something Li Qi feared. At worst, he could just flee. He didn't live here, so he wasn't worried. Thus, in the best-case scenario, this was the situation.

But there was an even worse scenario.

That was, the Minister of State and the Prefect were cut from the same cloth as the Minister of War.

Then the problem would be a real headache. It meant that on an official level, Li Qi couldn't acquire any help; he would have to raise the grain himself.

Enough to feed a hundred thousand people for a month... No, that wasn't enough. To ensure those commoners could last until the next harvest after migrating, they needed an absolute minimum of eighteen thousand tonnes of grain.

Apart from going through official channels, where else could one possibly scrape together so much grain in a short period of time?

The more Li Qi thought, the more he felt the task was impossible. Yet with the lives of over a hundred thousand people pressing down on his shoulders, how could a mere 'task is impossible' be used to brush it off?

Li Qi closed his eyes, continuously pondering and unconsciously muttering to himself, simultaneously recalling the current state of Vast Yang City he had witnessed along his journey here.

"Think carefully, think carefully. Vast Yang City has millions of people. How much must their daily consumption of food be? The fact that Vast Yang City shows no signs of famine means it has its own methods for collecting and storing grain. If I can find this method, a mere eighteen thousand tonnes is just eight or nine days' worth of consumption for this city. Vast Yang City can definitely supply it," Li Qi murmured.

So, a two-step plan.

Step one, first go ascertain the true state of Vast Yang's officialdom.

Step two, uncover the source of the grain and meat consumed daily by Vast Yang City.

Once he found clues, he would formulate the next step of the plan.

After all, with too few clues now, his room to maneuver was small. The more he knew, the more he could do. Planning was actually about using known elements to try and achieve one's desired outcome. If he knew nothing, then he couldn't even talk about planning; in the end, he would only be able to charge in recklessly.

Thinking thus, Li Qi simply had Old Ma come to a halt.

Old Ma was puzzled, but still obediently braked.

Li Qi dismounted, then took out a finger from his robes.

Looking at this finger alone, one would find it very beautiful, tender like a scallion. Though it was severed, it seemed as if it were still alive. The cross-section was incredibly smooth, lacking any gory appearance, making one feel as if it were a finger made of jade.

It was truly beautiful. When touched, it carried a chill, like stroking some extremely high-end material, making one feel exceptionally comfortable.

However, it was ultimately just a severed finger, so it was somewhat horrifying regardless.

This severed finger belonged to Shen Shuibi.

Back at White Snake Mountain, she had pulled it off and given it to Li Qi.

After all, it was the body of a Rank 5 cultivator, an Unsullied Frame that was immortal and incorruptible. Thus, there was no difference from when it was attached to her hand; touching it felt exactly like holding her hand.

Of course, Li Qi pulling out the finger now was definitely not to grope it.

He landed on the ground because coincidentally, there was a small river near Old Ma. Li Qi didn't know the name of this river, but that didn't stop him from making a trip over.

Approaching the riverbank, he brought out the fine jade he kept prepared for daily sacrificial cultivation.

As the saying goes, fine jade and pure livestock are used to pay respects to the River God.

First, establish the altar, then offer the ceremonial jade.

Then run back over to Old Ma.

Old Ma snorted and rolled its eyes in a human-like fashion, looking at its mane with heartache.

One of these days, Li Qi was going to pluck it completely bald.

Li Qi used a small knife to cut off a small bundle of mane.

The so-called pure livestock referred to clean and pure animals.

Although it felt somewhat off to say it, Old Ma fit this requirement perfectly.

He offered the mane of a dragon colt as the pure livestock.

He used the ceremonial jade as the fine offering.

Intoning the sacrificial incantation, the sacrificial rite was completed.

He then saw bubbles rising, glug-glug, from the deep and secluded waters.

A small snake with greenish-black bands quietly floated up from the water.

Then, it swam from the water onto the shore, shook itself, and transformed into a beautifully made-up woman. Her features were comely, her posture enchanting, and her slender waist swayed gently, dazzling the eyes and intoxicating the mind.

But despite possessing such a temperament, her expression was incredibly cautious. Appearing before Li Qi, she bowed and solemnly asked, "Great Shaman has summoned me. Failing to set up a banquet in advance to welcome you is truly a breach of etiquette; I beg your magnanimity. May I ask what business you have in visiting late at night?"

"To disturb you so suddenly is an imposition on you, water god. It is simply that there is a matter I truly need the water god's help with. I present this sacrificial offering along with a wisp of pure water qi, hoping the water god will lend a hand." Li Qi cupped his hands and spoke courteously.

"If it is within my power, I absolutely will not decline," the water god said upon accepting the sacrificial offering.

"Please remember the aura upon this item, water god, and pass along a message for me." Li Qi brought out the severed finger, showing it to the water god he had summoned.

The water god raised her head, her pupils suddenly shrinking into vertical slits.

Then, as if struck by lightning, her body violently reeled back, and her entire frame flew backward outright. Her garments shed away as a greenish-black water snake drilled straight into the water and hid.

It was for no other reason than her sixth sense perceiving an intense terror.

Magnificent, immense, aloof... It was just like the full moon on the horizon, eternal and unchanging, suspended high in the sky.

It was a noble existence that absolutely must not be touched, a holy entity that could only be viewed from afar but never approached.

Were she to show disrespect, a single look might crush both her divine soul and physical body, perhaps denying her even the chance at reincarnation.

Merely sensing this aura terrified this little river's water god into directly reverting to her true form and fleeing.

A full several minutes passed before she fearfully swam back. In her snake form, she asked, "Great Shaman... W-what is this thing?"

Li Qi sighed, having overestimated the gods of these minor mountains and rivers. But he still said, "Has the water god remembered the aura on this item?"

The water god smiled bitterly. "I'm afraid I won't be able to forget it for the rest of my life."

"This item is a treasure; I won't introduce the specifics. The aura on it leads to a Rank 8 mighty figure... I wish to ask the water god to use your abilities to connect with the powers of the four directions and execute a mountain-and-river transmission art to carry a message to her for me," Li Qi stated once more.

"I dare not decline what the Great Shaman entrusts, it's just... what does Rank 8 mean?" the water god asked.

"Uh, the water god cultivates the methods of the Divine Dao. What realm are you currently in?" Li Qi felt a headache coming on upon hearing this, so he asked an extra question.

"For me, I'm currently at the Golden Body realm. Why does the Great Shaman ask this?" the water god asked.

"If it's the Golden Body realm, then Rank 8... is roughly the Divine Soul realm. Make an analogy, it's about that level," Li Qi estimated and said as much.

"Divine Soul!?" The water god was given a massive fright. This was a full three major realms higher than her! It was the type that could crush her to death with a single hand.

[^1]: The "five colors" directly correspond to the Five Elements: Wood (green), Fire (red), Earth (yellow), Metal (white), and Water (black). When observed as a manifestation of _qi_, these five colors linking together symbolize absolute harmony and balance.
