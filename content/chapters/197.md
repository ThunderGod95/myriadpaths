# Chapter 197: Roping In

Having finalized his course of action, Li Qi began to move.

First, he set the sleeping rabbit aside.

The rabbit didn't wake up.

Devilish thoughts surged wildly in Li Qi's heart.

There was something he had wanted to do for a long time.

Therefore, he placed his hand on the rabbit's tail.

And gave it a pull.

_Wow, a rabbit's tail can actually be pulled this long!_

Completely satisfied, Li Qi's devilish thoughts dissipated.

Putting such trivialities aside, he got down to business.

First, he set up the Covenant.

He had to manually prepare the sacrifices for the Covenant. Shen Shuibi's formation disk was strictly designed to summon him; it had absolutely no other function. It could only be considered highly technical scrap metal, perhaps holding some collectible value at best.

Naturally, Li Qi still stored it away.

With the Covenant established, he called out to a person far away.

Then, carrying Shen Shuibi in his arms, Li Qi stepped out the door.

The moment he emerged from the secret room, two guards outside startled and charged over.

Their blades carved chilling arcs through the air. Without even asking, "Who are you?", the guards immediately moved in for the kill.

This was the core of the Lower Nine Sects, where the Horse King resided. Any stranger appearing here was to be killed without mercy; there was no room for negotiation.

Li Qi, of course, knew this, so he drew out a jade pendant and waved it.

The guards instantly halted their strikes, staring in bewilderment and suspicion.

This was a token personally issued by the Horse King. Seeing the token was akin to seeing the Horse King in person; only her most trusted confidants possessed one.

_Who is this man? Why have we never seen him before?_

Despite their inner doubts, they immediately dropped to one knee in silent salute.

"Do not reveal my presence. Prepare a carriage and take me to the Grain Alliance's master. Publicly announce it as the Horse King's excursion," Li Qi ordered, his voice cold and sharp.

"Yes, sir!" the two guards complied, then backed away.

A short while later, everything was ready. Li Qi rode the carriage, the hooves clattering as he hurried to the manor where Wang Boyan resided.

Along the way, aside from the absolute core members of the Lower Nine Sects, no one caught sight of Li Qi.

A deity's split soul possessed a physical form, so he didn't need to worry about being ethereal. However, its combat strength was quite weak.

The combat prowess of Li Qi's current split soul was roughly equivalent to his days as a boat tracker.

Because it was a split soul, it lacked any equipment, nor did it have the enhancement of his personal cosmos. His Martial Dao cultivation was also bound to his physical body.

Therefore, he could only barely scrape by in a fight relying on temporary incense fire; he was practically indistinguishable from a mortal.

But right now, there was no need for him to fight.

Only pawns needed to charge forward and slaughter. The chess player merely needed to sit by the board and sip tea.

A chess player would only personally step onto the board as a pawn when they ran out of pieces, but doing so was a steep drop in status.

Li Qi wasn't afraid of losing face. It was just that maintaining an aura of high prestige made it easier to get things done.

After a while, still carrying the soundly sleeping rabbit, Li Qi finally met with Wang Boyan.

This was a secret room within Wang Boyan's manor, though despite its hidden nature, it was extravagantly decorated.

Books were neatly arranged on the desk, alongside brushes, inkstones, and an assortment of stationary.

The walls were crafted from Zhao stone—sturdy, clean, and lustrous—framed with sandalwood edges.

A large vat made of transparent glass sat in the room, housing goldfish and floating algae; gazing at it quietly could make one forget the summer heat.

Li Qi glanced around and noticed numerous talismanic scripts. It seemed a formation was also set up here, but he couldn't quite understand it. It wasn't any type of formation he had encountered before.

While Li Qi was still observing the room, Wang Boyan pushed the door open.

"Ah, Brother Li!" Wang Boyan's face lit up with joy. Looking utterly sincere, he quickly walked over. "It's wonderful that you're alright! That monk—"

Before he could finish his sentence, Li Qi placed the sleeping rabbit on the table, stepped forward, and threw a punch!

Wang Boyan neither dodged nor blocked. He took Li Qi's fist squarely to the face and slammed into the wall with a heavy thud.

The recoil force caused a portion of Li Qi's temporary golden body to disperse.

Wang Boyan, however, simply rubbed his nose, remaining fundamentally unscathed.

He climbed up from the wall with a bitter smile. "Is this really necessary, Brother Li? Aren't we good brothers who have braved life and death together?"

Li Qi merely shook his hand, using the incense fire qi to mend his golden body, and let out a cold sneer. "Life and death together? How about you go knock on the doors of that Buddhist Pagoda right now, and the two of us can have a heart-to-heart with that monk? Only that would truly count as braving life and death together, wouldn't it?"

"Hahaha, there's no need for that. If I went in too, who would help Brother Li mobilize the Tang Empire's resources? Right?" Wang Boyan stood up, walked to the desk, pulled out a chair, and sat down with a smile.

As he spoke, he flicked open his folding fan and fanned himself, his tone relaxed. "Besides, you can't blame me for this, can you? Everything I told you was the truth. There wasn't a single falsehood; I've never lied to you."

"Even if there were some discrepancies with the truth, that's because I was unaware myself. Wasn't I also led in circles by that monk? How can you pin that on my head?"

Those words weren't false. From start to finish, from Wang Boyan's perspective, he hadn't spoken a single lie to Li Qi. Even if some statements were incorrect, it was because Wang Boyan himself had been deceived.

"Telling only half the truth is a bigger problem than outright lying," Li Qi stated coldly.

Wang Boyan replied with an innocent look. "But Brother Li, weren't you also undeceived? You didn't go to destroy the landscape grand array, nor did you tear all pretense of civility with the Vast Yang government. Even after returning to the White Rhino Army, you didn't resort to more extreme measures to stop the blood sacrifice."

"If I were you, upon recognizing that monk's position and the circumstances, I definitely wouldn't have risked my own life to protect those refugees. Instead, I would have decisively activated the landscape grand array and confronted him. That way, wouldn't you have secured your own safety?"

"I shouldn't be wrong in guessing that Brother Li must have left a trump card within that landscape grand array, allowing you to use it for yourself, right? I calculated the time. It should only take you a little over two hours to rush back from Vast Yang to the garrison pass, but you took more than three. You couldn't have been sleeping in the middle of all that, could you?"

"But what happened? Brother Li, you didn't touch the landscape grand array at all. Instead, you cleanly went along with that monk. I was quite surprised when I heard the news later. It turns out that from the very beginning, Brother Li, you didn't believe a single word I said."

Li Qi shook his head, his tone hostile. "If I had activated the landscape grand array then, wouldn't I have fallen right into your trap? I'm afraid what would have followed was you colluding with the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest to launch an attack, taking the opportunity to swallow up Vast Yang, right?"

Hearing this, Wang Boyan gave a soft chuckle. Refusing to confirm or deny, he didn't pursue the topic.

Instead, he continued analyzing Li Qi's actions. "Moreover, what I expected even less was that you actually buried a trump card here in Vast Yang City as well. That woman from before took over the position of Horse King in half a day. She then proceeded to negotiate with me and even found a way to rescue Brother Li from that monk's clutches."

"Interlocking rings of plans, trump cards hidden everywhere. Brother Li's profound thoughts and far-reaching schemes truly fill this younger brother with admiration. You are indeed a high disciple of the Shaman God Mountain. Coming to find me again after escaping this time, I wonder what Brother Li has in mind?"

With a barrage of flattery, he glossed over the matters Li Qi had brought up about him, smoothly steering the conversation to the main point.

If Li Qi continued to harp on those past grievances, it would make Li Qi appear unreasonable.

Naturally, Li Qi could see right through these verbal tricks.

But he couldn't be bothered to care. He hadn't come here to interrogate and assign blame anyway; that was something to be done after the dust settled. Therefore, he cut straight to the chase. "I'm not counting on a Rank 7. You people probably can't deploy one in Baiyue anyway. Two Rank 8s—at least two Rank 8 combatants—appearing before me within a month. That shouldn't be a problem, right?"

Wang Boyan dropped his playful demeanor. With a snap, he closed his folding fan and frowned. "What are you planning to do? Don't tell me you want to use Rank 8s to challenge that monk? Are you looking for death? Even if a hundred Rank 8s charged together, it would still be a dead end. Do you think a Rank 7 is that easy to deal with?"

"Of course not. I know how powerful a Rank 7 is better than you do. I have another use for them; they won't be sent to the frontline," Li Qi replied.

Wang Boyan's expression turned stern as he questioned further, "Why engage in all this posturing? Since you've already escaped, why not seek help from the Shaman God Mountain? Instead, you come looking for me, someone from the Human Dao. It's completely baffling. If you notify the powers under your mountain's jurisdiction right now, Monk Chanzhi might be strong, but there's no way he can challenge a Kingdom Lord-level powerhouse. Driving him away wouldn't be difficult."

"You are a direct disciple of the Shaman God Mountain. Don't tell me you can't even call for people?"

Wang Boyan was deeply puzzled by Li Qi's attitude.

Just what did the concept of a direct disciple of the Shaman God Mountain entail?

You might not be able to find many across all of All Under Heaven. Their numbers were absolutely under a thousand, though no one knew the exact figure.

To the outside world, these individuals were all addressed as 'Young Masters of the Shaman God Mountain.' This meant their masters were high-ranking echelons of the Shaman God Mountain at Rank 3 or above. And the future goal of these proud children of heaven was to reach the level of their masters.

Even if they were only Rank 9, their status was on equal footing with a Rank 5.

As a scion of an aristocratic family in the Tang Empire, Wang Boyan knew in his heart that the Tang Empire had similar proud children of heaven to compare them to.

Namely, the students of the Imperial Academy and the Human Emperor's Palace Guards, all of whom were the absolute pinnacle of geniuses.

The Tang Empire had government schools established everywhere. People from the villages went to county schools, and students from the county schools could advance to higher levels. Step by step, passing through examinations and recommendations, they advanced, ultimately filtering out countless people. Across the entire Tang Empire, an unknown number of people competed, yet only a meager few dozen students could enter the Imperial Academy for advanced studies every ten years.

This was the selection process for the civil branch.

As for the Human Emperor's Palace Guards, that was the selection process for the martial lineage. A young martial artist's greatest glory was being selected to enter the Palace Guards, standing watch over the imperial palace and becoming the Human Emperor's personal guard. They would receive guidance from numerous elite imperial experts; serving internally as guards, and upon being sent out, they would become generals.

The Shaman God Mountain was much rougher in comparison. They had no systematic selection method in this regard. Mostly, it was just the various Great Shamans taking in disciples based on their mood. However, anyone capable of catching a Great Shaman's eye couldn't possibly be a true mediocrity.

This highlighted the difference between the two factions. The Shaman Dao was loose and relied on fate, adapting to the heavens and conforming to nature. The Human Dao, on the other hand, was strictly organized with rigorous laws and systems.

Li Qi had never publicly revealed his identity, but the reason Wang Boyan knew was that he had deduced it indirectly through Monk Chanzhi's reactions.

With a hypothesis in mind, he sought evidence. Wang Boyan quickly followed the trail all the way to Clear Mist City.

Referencing the sheer volume of esoteric knowledge and cultivation methods in the hands of the woman under Li Qi, and then learning that Li Qi had blocked a direct strike from the Green Mountain's mountain god in Clear Mist City.

Combining these three aspects, he finally confirmed Li Qi's identity.

Although Li Qi hadn't told him, Wang Boyan could infer the clues on his own.

To accomplish so many inconceivable feats, even if he wasn't a Young Master of the Shaman God Mountain, his status couldn't be much lower.

Upon hearing these words, Li Qi gave him a sidelong glance instead.

Wang Boyan certainly knew a lot.

But that was normal. This person must hold some significant standing within the Tang Empire; otherwise, it would be impossible for him to preside over the overall situation of a region with only a Rank 9 cultivation base, let alone mobilize Rank 8s to heed his commands.

The Rank 8s he mobilized were brought all the way from the Tang Empire. The underlying intricacies were easy to imagine.

Nevertheless, Li Qi explained, "It is precisely because I am a disciple of the Shaman God Mountain that I cannot casually order powerful experts to come. Otherwise, what would that make me?"

"The matter with Chanzhi started because of me, so naturally, it is best if it ends by my hands."

Wang Boyan was thoroughly shocked. "Brother Li?! You truly intend to clash with a Rank 7?!"

"A child of great wealth does not sit near the edge of the hall! Why risk your life?! For a Rank 9 to fight a Rank 8 is already the mark of an unparalleled genius. To fight a Rank 7—there has absolutely never been a precedent for this in all of All Under Heaven!"

Li Qi merely shook his head. "If I can defeat a Rank 7 with the body of a Rank 9, tell me, what will happen to me?"

Li Qi spoke lightly, but Wang Boyan completely lost his composure upon hearing those words.

He understood what Li Qi meant.

This sent a terrifying shock through his entire being, causing him to tremble uncontrollably. He abruptly stood up, unable to even suppress his cultivation base. The floor tiles beneath his feet cracked with loud bangs, shattered by the overflowing bursts of qi.

"Don't be so surprised, Brother Wang. Do you want to add your name to this monumental achievement? A scion of an aristocratic family from the Tang Empire, coming to the Baiyue region at such a young age—you couldn't possibly be here for retirement, could you?"

"I see you scheming against me, scheming against the Vast Yang Prefect, mediating for profit between Monk Chanzhi and the local factions, and even daring to plot around the external invasion of the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest. You wouldn't lack this little bit of courage, would you?" Li Qi asked with a smile.

Wang Boyan took a deep breath.

To be honest, he hesitated.

He had been maneuvering everywhere, but he had never truly entered the fray. The Wang Family's power could protect him. As the young master of an aristocratic family, he had multiple Rank 8s under his command, as well as some bottom-of-the-box trump cards. Essentially, he could preserve his life even against a Rank 7.

But it was strictly limited to preserving his life. Those ultimate trump cards were used to buy him time to escape. Wanting to genuinely clash head-on with a Rank 7 was utterly impossible.

As for stronger treasures, it wasn't that the family didn't have any, but even if given to him, he wouldn't be able to use them.

For a Rank 9 to power a Rank 7 dharma treasure was already the absolute limit. Although some supreme treasures had no cultivation requirements to use, selling the entire Wang Family wouldn't be enough to afford one, let alone giving it to him.

Therefore, his hidden cards could only save his life.

If he intervened, his life might be secure, but he might completely smash the entire operation he had built up in Vast Yang.

Furthermore, Monk Chanzhi wasn't actually just one Rank 7. He also controlled the puppet of the Minister of War. In other words, he effectively had the combat power of two Rank 7s.

To truly come into direct conflict with this monk...

Wang Boyan could only say that he couldn't find even half a fraction of a chance at victory.

However, that was just him.

He was the one who couldn't find a chance at victory.

But the unfathomable man standing before him was a Young Master of the Shaman God Mountain.

Could he do it?

He shouldn't be able to, right? No matter how great his title, could he bridge the massive chasm between Rank 9 and Rank 7?

Moreover, Monk Chanzhi wasn't just some random Rank 7 picked off the street. He possessed the orthodox inheritance of the Mahakala lineage. His strength was absolutely top-tier among Rank 7s; even the Minister of War, a fellow Rank 7, had been devoured by him and reduced to a puppet.

How many more puppets did he have after devouring their hearts?

Wang Boyan broke out in a cold sweat. He hesitated in place, completely unable to make a decision.

He lacked the courage to challenge a peak Rank 7, but he couldn't bear to give up the opportunity either.

If he gave up now, he feared he wouldn't find another Young Master of the Shaman God Mountain willing to cooperate with him in this lifetime.
