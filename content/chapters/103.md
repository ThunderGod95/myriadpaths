# Chapter 103: Ambush

It was now the second day, and they were taking a break during their journey.

Li Qi seized the moment to practice some light exercises. It didn't consume much stamina, but it allowed him to slowly digest some water qi, while simultaneously dedicating a fraction of his attention to reading.

Meanwhile, Shen Shuibi was busy bullying squirrels.

She was digging through a squirrel hole.

The squirrel beside her dared to be angry but dared not speak out.

Mainly, it was because she had suddenly developed a taste for nuts yesterday. She seemed to thoroughly enjoy their subtle fragrance. Li Qi had explained the concept of the melon-seed-eating spectators to her, which had instantly piqued her interest.

However, there were no melon seeds to be found here. Amidst the snowy expanse of White Snake Mountain, there were only acorns and pinecones.

Where were acorns and pinecones most abundant?

That was easy—inside pine tree hollows, of course.

Thus, she committed an unpardonable sin.

She mugged a squirrel.

And she succeeded. After a fierce struggle, the squirrel was defeated.

_Munch, munch, munch._ She sat beneath the tree, chewing on the winter provisions the squirrel had so painstakingly hoarded. She looked to be enjoying it immensely, appearing absolutely adorable.

Unfortunately, the squirrel did not share this sentiment.

But nature was cruel like that. In this clash of small herbivores, the rabbit had emerged completely victorious.

_Good stuff, good stuff._

Setting aside these minor interludes, Li Qi had no intention of resting further. Once his daily exercises were complete, he resumed their trek.

The journey remained peaceful. After all, it was just the two of them on the road, and one was a ranked expert.

There were no visible goods worth robbing, and making an attempt would only risk provoking a master. Few people with functioning brains would try such a thing.

Robbery required picking the right target. It was much like a wolf pack on the prowl: their strikes had to be fast, accurate, and ruthless. Even if they missed their mark, they couldn't afford to suffer heavy casualties.

When a wolf pack roamed the mountains and forests, though they appeared majestic and unafraid of fierce tigers, they never attacked indiscriminately. An injury meant becoming a target for other predators, leading to even graver consequences.

As such, large caravans were far more suitable prey. Snatch a cart or two and run—minimal losses and guaranteed spoils.

A lone pair capable of fighting back was like a hedgehog: all spikes, no meat. Wolves had no appetite for that.

Thus, the trip went without a hitch. By traveling through the night, they arrived back in Snake Tail County by the evening of the second day.

Li Qi pondered for a moment and decided they should head straight back, ready some fodder for Old Ma, and then immediately set out again.

Even though he wasn't sure if the Assistant Magistrate knew of his involvement, sticking around was definitely a bad idea.

An Assistant Magistrate... what a high-ranking official that must be.

Based on Li Qi's current understanding, a single county spanned thousands of li at the very least—almost the size of a province in his previous world.

They called him an Assistant Magistrate, but his actual authority was on par with a top official. The county seat alone housed anywhere from hundreds of thousands to over a million people. Tacking on the surrounding villages and scattered mountain strongholds, the total population easily reached ten million.

The County Magistrate was effectively the equivalent of a provincial party secretary.

Less trouble was always better than more. Fleeing a day early meant Old Ma couldn't fully fatten up, but it avoided potential headaches. Overall, it was a worthwhile trade.

At worst, they could just fatten him up at the next county seat.

With Old Ma's speed, they would reach the next county within a single day.

His mind made up, Li Qi returned to the city and rushed non-stop to the inn's entrance.

"Innkeeper, I'm checking out," Li Qi said as he knocked and walked in. He was straightforward and didn't plan on wasting words.

"Right away, sir. As for the stable and fodder fees you paid in advance, would you like them refunded in coin, or shall I just provide you with the fodder directly?" The innkeeper didn't ask any unnecessary questions, smoothly processing the checkout.

A guest staying for seven days was considered a long duration; checking out was perfectly normal, and they had long since prepared for it.

Besides, the manager had personally given orders to accommodate this lord's requests to the fullest extent and to avoid offending him at all costs.

Though he didn't know why the manager had said this, there was surely a good reason.

The accountant and the waiter quickly verified the bill, jotting down the exact expenses on a slip of paper for Li Qi to review.

Li Qi calculated it once over and confirmed it was correct. He paid what was owed, inspected what needed inspecting, and then the stablehand led the horse over and handed the reins to him.

Without another word, Li Qi mounted the horse, ready to depart.

Old Ma, however, wore a reluctant expression.

He had probably been living too comfortably these past few days.

"Alright, it's been a whole week, what more do you want? Getting cheeky, are we?" Li Qi kicked Old Ma lightly, scolding him with a laugh.

"A week? What is that?" Shen Shuibi asked curiously, having heard a term she didn't recognize.

"Uh... it's nothing, it just means seven days," Li Qi said, brushing it off.

"Oh..." Shen Shuibi nodded thoughtfully, though it was a mystery what she had actually comprehended.

He led Old Ma out of the stables and onto the street.

As always, Li Qi strapped on the bamboo basket, lifted Shen Shuibi onto the horse, and swung himself into the saddle.

_Time to run!_

But before a single hoofbeat could echo, Old Ma's entire body abruptly went stiff. He struck a posture ready to break into a wild gallop, yet he didn't move an inch.

Shen Shuibi instantly went on high alert, tugging at Li Qi's sleeve. "There's a sound, Li Qi."

Li Qi gripped his blade with one hand and secured the willow branch tied to his waist with the other. "Which direction? I don't sense anything."

It couldn't be helped. When it came to perception, aside from his progress bar, he had nothing worth boasting about.

"It's a ghost. And one that has deliberately concealed its form. Four steps directly in front of us," Shen Shuibi whispered, instantly calling out the position.

Hearing it was a ghost, Li Qi breathed a sigh of relief.

Fighting a person would leave him feeling a bit uncertain.

But fighting ghosts? That was an absolute sure thing.

Since Shen Shuibi had pinpointed its location, Li Qi pretended to be oblivious, urging Old Ma a few steps forward.

Then, he abruptly drew the willow branch and whipped it fiercely forward!

With a sharp _crack_, a wail echoed through the surroundings.

The ghost writhed frantically in midair from the sheer agony, Yin qi spilling out in all directions!

Now, having activated his qi-observing vision, Li Qi could finally see it too.

It was baring its fangs and brandishing its claws, its visage a terrifying shade of green with protruding tusks. Its body was wreathed not only in Yin qi but also in baleful qi!

This was no ordinary ghost; it was a malicious ghost!

A malicious spirit that had already manifested a physical form from the Five Elements, capable of harming and devouring humans.

Li Qi certainly wasn't going to leave it an avenue of escape. Since his sneak attack had succeeded, it was time for another lash.

The second strike was imbued with the divine qi of Shentu and Yulei.

A Rank 9 malicious spirit could have its soul scattered into oblivion with just two strikes.

A willow branch personally bestowed by a Grand Invocator provided a crushing advantage against lower-level entities.

However, this strike failed to connect.

The ghost was suddenly enveloped by some bizarre power and flew backward at breakneck speed. It didn't look like it was flying under its own power, but rather as if it had been bound by a rope and violently yanked away.

Li Qi looked in the direction the ghost had flown.

There, he saw an elderly man in brocade robes clutching a jade box. It was this very jade box that had snatched the malicious spirit away.

The old man who had suddenly appeared looked at Li Qi and spoke. "Young hero, why must you strike so ruthlessly? It was merely taking a look; did you truly intend to beat my ghost slave to death right here and now?"
