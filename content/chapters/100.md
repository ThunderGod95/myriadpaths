# Chapter 100: Tang Empire

"Song Kingdom? Why ask about that?" Jing Yan frowned.

"Nothing in particular. I've just never left the Great Deer Kingdom, so I wanted to ask about the local customs of the Song Kingdom," Li Qi said with a smile.

"Fine, but we're still on patrol. Let's chat tonight after we've set up camp," Jing Yan replied.

Li Qi nodded. This response was exactly what he had expected.

After spending the last two days together, he had come to understand Jing Yan's personality.

She was exactly like a sword.

Upright and stubborn, yet not inherently difficult to get along with. She was just far too serious, making her poorly suited for teamwork.

When dealing with someone who never lied, did everything strictly by the book, and possessed zero flexibility, conflict with others was inevitable. And when those conflicts arose, she was incredibly capable in a fight.

No wonder the others called her 'Tigeress.'

She seemed to treat everyone and everything with absolute earnestness, pouring her full attention into every matter she encountered. More than anything, she devoted all her vital energy, love, and who-knew-what-else into her sword practice. In short, she had cultivated herself until she was as sharp and unyielding as a blade.

The two continued their patrol. The day passed peacefully, without even a single wild beast crossing their path.

They did, however, encounter a few mountain-harvesting platoons.

At first, these mountain-harvesting platoons watched from a distance, sizing up the merchant caravan.

But as soon as Li Qi showed himself, they scattered.

Taking to the mountains to become a bandit was an easy enough path, but one still had to weigh their own strength.

Provoking a ranked expert was highly unwise; the two sides operated on completely different levels.

This was the benefit of spending money to hire Li Qi. The journey appeared uneventful, but danger lurked everywhere. Without someone offering protection, a caravan of dozens of carts would have met with disaster long ago.

After a safe day, Li Qi finished his meal, exchanged a few words with the rabbit, and made his way over to Jing Yan's carriage.

Jing Yan was practicing her swordsmanship.

Every movement was meticulous and utterly earnest. She had forgotten both the world and herself; in her heart and in her hands, there was only the sword.

Such focus. Such dedication.

No wonder Shen Shuibi had said she would definitely amount to something in the future. With this kind of personality and a bit of talent, it would be hard for her _not_ to succeed.

Having Shen Shuibi personally point out her weaknesses was equivalent to receiving direct guidance from a Rank 5 master. For Jing Yan, this was a heaven-sent opportunity. Breaking through to Rank 8 with this insight was entirely possible.

This was a good thing. It counted as forging good karma and making an early investment.

In Li Qi's estimation, he would inevitably be swept into the conflict between the Great Deer Kingdom and the Song Kingdom. Understanding the situation now while building some connections within the Song Kingdom would definitely prove useful later.

Li Qi waited on the sidelines for her to finish her routine. He watched as she slowly sheathed her sword and walked over to him.

"What did you want to ask? About the Song Kingdom?" Jing Yan got straight to the point as she approached.

There were no pleasantries whatsoever. Her bluntness gave Li Qi a slight headache.

After spending so long exchanging empty courtesies with all sorts of people, running into someone this straightforward was honestly a bit... jarring.

Still, this could be considered a good thing.

"It's nothing complicated. I just want to understand the local customs of the Song Kingdom. I heard you just fought a war with the Tang Empire. What's the situation like now?" Li Qi asked.

As he spoke, the two sat down beside an ox cart and began to talk.

"The war? That was already three or four years ago. It wasn't much, really. It had almost no impact on the common folk. It's just that those high officials and great shamans were utterly crushed. There was barely any commotion," Jing Yan explained after a moment of recollection.

"...The war had no impact on the civilians?" Li Qi was somewhat stunned.

"The Tang Empire conquered the entire Song Kingdom in three days, breaching three provinces in a single day. What impact could it possibly have?" Jing Yan offered a bitter smile. "Rank 9 martial artists like you and me might be considered experts in the Jianghu, but in the Tang army, we would only be a Platoon Leader. A single army has hundreds of platoons, and the Tang Empire has far more than just one army. Therefore, the battlefield never even reached the ground."

She tilted her head back. Her expression looked like fear, yet also like she was lost in a memory. She spoke with a heavy sigh, "On that day, ten suns blazed in the sky, and the moon eclipsed the five stars. Shooting stars streaked and fell. The military qi erupted, gradually forming the shape of mountains within the clouds. Dragons and tigers soared through the heavens. There were countless strange phenomena."

"A day later, everything returned to normal. Aside from the high officials being replaced by Tang Empire Generals, the Great Shamans being replaced by Daoist Masters, and all the original shamans becoming Daoist priests, nothing else changed. There wasn't even any chaos within the Jing family. Everything went on as usual."

Jing Yan's explanation made Li Qi's head throb.

This didn't sound like a battle between equals on any level...

Then again, this was likely exactly how the Tang Empire viewed the Song Kingdom.

In his original world, things were actually quite similar. A major nation's special forces unit could easily overthrow the regime of a small country.

"And afterward? Did the governance of the Song Kingdom change at all?" Li Qi asked.

"There were changes... For instance, they cracked down severely on corruption. They forcefully integrated all the scattered sects into the Song Kingdom's Daoist Alliance, placing them under unified official jurisdiction. They enacted laws that everyone, top to bottom, must strictly obey. Violators are punished according to the law. To establish the authority of these laws, they even slaughtered a Rank 6 mountain god."

"The god of the Western Peak, one of the Five Great Mountains of the Song Kingdom, had attained the Rank 6 of the Dao. Its colossal body stretched for five thousand li. It refused to submit to the Tang Empire's laws and ignored the mandate to regulate the earth qi to bless the local farmers. Instead, continuing its old practices, it demanded that the populace below offer human sacrifices. As a result, a Daoist Master suppressed and killed it on the spot. Even the mountain's roots were shattered, and its body was refined into a 'Five Peaks Seal'."

"Afterward, that Daoist Master conferred a title right there and then, appointing a fallen heroic spirit of the Tang Empire as the new mountain god. He bestowed the Five Peaks Seal upon this spirit, allowing it to receive the local incense offerings and become the new Western Peak of the Song Kingdom."

"Since then, the Song Kingdom's administration has been honest and efficient. The people live in peace and work happily. Furthermore, the government began establishing official schools. All children between the ages of six and fifteen are required to enroll. They do not collect tuition fees; instead, they distribute rations. The children must study for three years: reading and writing in the first year, laws in the second, and rites and music in the third. Only then are they allowed to leave the official schools."

"In the event of natural disasters, cultivators step forward to handle them. They regulate the climate, ensuring abundant earth qi and favorable weather for the crops. If there are man-made calamities, armed forces are immediately deployed to capture the wicked and punish them according to the law."

"In addition, numerous artifacts have flowed in. It seems they are manufactured by followers of the Reason Dao. They search day and night for the principles behind how objects function, creating many bizarre artifacts. For example, 'Flowers in a Mirror, Moon in the Water' appears to be nothing more than a round mirror, yet it can project scenery from thousands of miles away. You can watch distant song and dance performances or converse with someone holding the other half of the mirror. It has already become popular in the Song Kingdom, but it's too expensive. I can't afford one."

"It has been three years like this. The Song Kingdom... is probably much better off than before," Jing Yan replied truthfully after a moment of thought.

Sitting beside her, Li Qi listened in stunned silence.

_Holy shit!_

_Isn't this Tang Empire a bit too advanced?!_

_Weren't they supposed to be the villains?!_

_Why are they so civilized?!_
