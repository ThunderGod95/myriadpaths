# Chapter 148: Discussion

It was still that same main street.

The surrounding atmosphere felt incredibly off. Li Qi had arranged the area in advance. Thus it carried a gloomy and chilling vibe that made people feel quite uncomfortable.

Yet Jin Buhuan acted as if he felt nothing at all. He walked straight up and cupped his hands in greeting with a bright smile on his face. "Haha! I trust you have been well Brother Li? You do not look to be in the best of moods. Have you encountered some difficulties recently? Why not share them with me? I hold a bit of meager influence here in Clear Mist City. I can certainly be of assistance."

Seeing him Li Qi also smiled and stepped forward. He did not return the formal salute. He merely nodded in acknowledgment before replying. "It is nothing in particular. It is just a personal feeling of discomfort in my heart."

Jin Buhuan entirely dispensed with courtesy. He led his people directly into the inn. He looked around in all directions and spoke to Li Qi. "I consider myself the host of Clear Mist City. You can just speak your mind if you have any troubles Brother Li! Even if I cannot help I can at least lend an ear. Getting it off your chest will surely make you feel better!"

Jin Buhuan surveyed the surroundings after walking in. He comfortably took a seat only after confirming he could not spot anything unusual.

He cut straight to the chase the moment he sat down. "Why have you not accepted those gifts left outside Brother Li?"

Li Qi also walked inside.

He neither poured tea nor observed any proper etiquette. He did not even greet Elder Si or the maids. He treated them as if they were invisible. It was as if he were the only person in the entire inn.

Li Qi did not even sit down. This made the seated Jin Buhuan look rather foolish.

This was an incredibly disrespectful display. It could have easily sparked a feud between two factions had this occurred in a formal setting. Yet both parties acted utterly oblivious. They acted as if neither minded in the slightest.

Li Qi replied in a relaxed tone after hearing the question. "Those gifts are far too precious. I do not feel I am worthy of you going to such expense Brother Jin. Thus I naturally did not dare accept them."

"Ah! You are mistaken Brother Li. My sudden attack on you in the streets last time was a grave breach of courtesy. I ought to properly compensate you by all rights. Otherwise people might think I was raised without a mother to teach me manners. They would think I lacked both etiquette and upbringing." Jin Buhuan said with a grinning face.

Li Qi also broke into a smile upon hearing this.

The two of them laughed out loud together. This painted a rather harmonious scene.

It was obvious that Jin Buhuan was quite displeased by the lack of courtesy from Li Qi. This explained his unceremonious and mocking remarks.

They had to maintain a facade of civility for people like them. This applied no matter how viciously they fought in the shadows. It meant the situation underneath had already completely rotted away if they could not even keep up appearances.

Li Qi was effectively tearing away that facade by acting this way.

However they were still one step shy of the breaking point. Thus both sides exercised a degree of restraint. They at least had not started fighting right there and then.

Li Qi waved his hand just as the atmosphere grew awkward. He took the initiative to steer the conversation. "Alright! Enough about such mood-killing matters. Do you know why I invited you here this time Brother Jin?"

"Naturally I do not. Why do you not enlighten me Brother Li?" Jin Buhuan shifted his expression once more and leaned forward with keen interest.

"You have recently fallen into misfortune Brother Jin. However you might be able to turn this bad luck into good fortune if handled well." Li Qi spoke with an air of profound seriousness.

"Oh? What do you mean by that?" Jin Buhuan looked surprised.

"Are you aware that Green Mountain recently unearthed another burst of metal qi? Furthermore it is of Rank 8. And yet again it takes the form of a long saber." Li Qi said.

Jin Buhuan certainly knew of this. The matter had caused quite a stir in Clear Mist City over the past period. Many had flocked to Green Mountain for treasure hunting. The news had spread far and wide.

Piaoxu had even asked Li Qi if he had come for this very reason when he first arrived in Clear Mist City and stayed at her inn.

"Of course I know of this matter. But what does it have to do with me? Green Mountain produces a wisp of metal qi every dozen years or so. It is not uncommon. Even my father would not seek to monopolize it. He instead leaves it for those destined to claim it. In that case it has even less to do with me." Jin Buhuan replied while waiting for the subsequent explanation.

He knew Li Qi was simply keeping him in suspense.

However what he said was not the complete truth.

In reality the mountain god of Green Mountain typically only allowed ordinary Rank 9 metal qi to flow out into the world. He would likely intervene if an innate divine weapon of Rank 8 appeared. He would not forcefully hoard it though. He would gift it as a fortuitous encounter to form a positive karmic tie if someone truly had the capability.

The mountain god considered anything Rank 7 or above to be true treasures. How could he possibly give them to outsiders? He absolutely would not let them slip away.

Take the Rank 8 metal qi he had gifted to Li Qi for instance. It actually belonged to him. He obtained it only after the intervention of his father. It was just that his father had not acted personally that time. This allowed a tiger demon to seize the opportunity and steal it.

Later Jin Buhuan hired a master to slay the tiger demon and bring the metal qi back. He only discovered that it was damaged. The tip of the blade was snapped off.

Still later he recruited Elder Si. Elder Si discovered through divination that the tiger demon was actually still alive. Elder Si accidentally provoked Li Qi while attempting to curse-kill her.

However he had no intention of revealing these inside details to Li Qi.

One of them was definitely hiding his metal qi fragment between Li Qi and that fox demon.

It would be returned sooner or later.

Jin Buhuan thought to himself.

Li Qi simply continued unaware of his thoughts. "This is where you are mistaken Brother Jin. Did Green Mountain not also yield a Rank 8 long saber more than ten years ago?"

Jin Buhuan darkened his gaze. "Of course. But that metal qi was damaged. Its value plummeted. That was the very blade I gifted to you Brother Li. Did you not notice?"

"Indeed I did notice. And that is also the reason I did not accept it." Li Qi replied. "Hear me out Brother Jin. How often does Green Mountain normally produce a burst of Rank 8 metal qi?"

Jin Buhuan thought for a moment before answering seriously since it involved his own treasure. "Roughly once every two hundred years. Though it is not fixed. The production of metal qi is like a tree bearing fruit after all. It depends on the will of Heaven."

"You see here lies the problem. Green Mountain produced two bursts of Rank 8 metal qi in succession. Two Rank 8 treasures within fifteen years. Do you think this is normal?" Li Qi asked.

Jin Buhuan frowned. "What are you implying Brother Li?"

"I am implying that these two Rank 8 metal qi were originally a single entity!" Li Qi asserted.

"Heaven is round and the earth is square. They are rooted in Yin and Yang. All things are born of Yin and Yang. Yet Yin and Yang are fundamentally one. This is just as all things possess the qi of Yin and Yang. They are merely two sides of the same coin. But now Green Mountain has produced two pieces of metal qi. Yin and Yang are displaced. The `Two Modes` are divided. This is an omen of terrible misfortune! That is why I said you might be in trouble Brother Jin!"

Li Qi spoke with absolute conviction.

Jin Buhuan was initially noncommittal upon hearing this. He silently mulled over the words.

He no longer cared about the lack of manners from Li Qi at this moment. What the other party said did make a certain amount of sense after all.

Jin Buhuan immediately cupped his hands and asked after pondering for a bit. "Then what are your insights Brother Li?"

He first asked for insights before considering anything else. He was not thinking about being in trouble right now.

Rather if Li Qi was telling the truth then these two pieces of metal qi were the Yin and Yang sides of the same entity. Would their quality not increase astronomically if they could be merged back together!?

Could it even reach the level of a half-step Rank 7?

This was not something he could treat lightly. He had to carefully verify it even if Li Qi was spouting nonsense.

The matter was of grave importance. He had no choice but to be cautious.

"My insights are exactly as I just said. The division of Yin and Yang is a terrible omen. However opportunity lies within it. You see the central qi lies between the pure and the turbid of Yin and Yang. The central qi acts as the pivot for the rise and fall of Yin and Yang. If you can unify the Yin and Yang and then find a central qi to harmonize them Brother Jin... This item will surely turn calamity into blessing. It will become an omen of good fortune for you." Li Qi said while cupping his hands.

Jin Buhuan could no longer keep up his act with Li Qi laying it out so clearly.

He simply stood up and walked toward Li Qi. "I assume you also know the purpose of my visit since we have spoken to this point Brother Li. I refuse to believe you saw the gifts outside and still do not understand my intentions."

"Naturally. I presume you came for this Brother Jin." Li Qi smiled and pulled out an irregularly shaped throwing knife.

"You will possess an innate divine weapon that ranks among the finest of Rank 8 with this item in hand Brother Jin. Its quality and might will elevate even further by combining it with the other half and merging Yin and Yang. It would be a true treasure even for your esteemed father. He will be incredibly pleased if you can piece it together and offer it to him. He might even bestow it straight back to you. Gaining both his favor and the treasure kills two birds with one stone."

Li Qi said all this while clutching the throwing knife in one hand.

Clearly everyone knew this was an incredibly enticing prospect.

Even the slimmest sliver of hope was something Jin Buhuan took very seriously. This was let alone the fact that he already knew the power his metal qi would wield once restored.

"Ah. So you were aware of the existence of this item after all Brother Li. And here I thought you did not know." Jin Buhuan smiled.

Li Qi returned the smile amicably. "I did not know before. But how could I still be clueless after you dropped so many hints Brother Jin? Therefore my previous refusal of your gifts was not out of unwillingness. It was absolute inability. How would I have the opportunity to converse with you here and now had I accepted them?"

Jin Buhuan looked as if a realization hit him. "So that is how it is. It seems I misunderstood you Brother Li. I will not waste time on laughable theatrics since everything is out in the open. To put it simply... I will reward you handsomely if you can hand this item over to me Brother Li."

Li Qi was equally straightforward. "Naturally. Why would I call you here if not for this matter Brother Jin? I will certainly return the item to its rightful owner since you have come. However I have one unreasonable request Brother Jin."

"Please speak Brother Li!" Jin Buhuan replied with an extremely agreeable tone.

"I wish to spar a bit with you. And I want to use that weapon." Li Qi said.

"You wish to challenge yourself Brother Li? It seems you want to focus entirely on your path in the Dao. No wonder there were so many oddities in your behavior. I thought it was something else. It seems you simply did not care Brother Li." Jin Buhuan took the throwing knife from the hand of Li Qi. He then flicked his finger.

That long saber shifted slightly.

Then a piercing hum rang out!

The sound spun out like a razor-thin thread. It was capable of shattering stones and piercing clouds! It was so shrill that it caused all the surrounding rocks and porcelain to violently explode. Li Qi could even feel the thick and suffocating killing intent within it!

Madness. Murderous intent. Excitement. Fury. Sheer exhilaration. A myriad of intense emotions surged wildly from within the innate divine weapon!

It was clearly a lifeless object. Yet it housed an inexhaustible vortex of emotion. Its sharp hum actually carried a living will. It almost made one envision certain images.

However it was only almost.

Li Qi merely felt as though he saw something. This was much like the emotions evoked when listening to music. He did not physically see anything however.

It was the atmosphere conjured by the sound of clashing metal and weapons of war if he had to describe it.

Covering his ears Li Qi silently noted...

_Sound again._

_The first time was the sound of a flute. Then it was listening to the voice of the heart. And now it is the killing sounds of clashing weapons._

Even the dao rhythm of the opponent was related to sound.

Li Qi observed all of this.

Sure enough that innate divine weapon had been fully under the control of his opponent from the very beginning.

Not only would he have been in the wrong in terms of principles if he had accepted those gifts. He would have been constantly placed in a passive position moving forward. He might have even been directly beheaded by the opponent remotely activating the divine weapon.

Li Qi looked at Jin Buhuan before him. Although the man used a saber it was likely just a facade. The foundation of his cultivation method should be the Metal Sound among the Eight Sounds. This was just as the mountain god of White Snake Mountain had said.

The Eight Sounds were Metal. Stone. Silk. Bamboo. Gourd. Earth. Leather. Wood.[^1]

Heaven possesses the Eight Sounds. They are elements of different natures that often conflict. The Sages borrowed these natures to forge eight instruments. They used sound to summon qi. This compelled the Eight Winds to follow their rhythm. Such was the design of the Eight Sounds.

The Sound of Metal was clear and bordered on the elegant. Its instruments included cymbals and bells and chimes and iron flutes. As the saying went: The sound of metal shakes the valleys. The beating drums deafen the heavens. Righteousness pierces the profound. Reason aligns with divine covenant.

Furthermore it was frequently used on the battlefield amidst slaughter due to its clear and ringing tone. This gave rise to the saying: Hear the sound of metal and rise to war.

Right now Jin Buhuan was using this method to fuse the fragment back into his saber.

A metallic clang rang out. That burst of metal qi continuously devoured the shell that had once broken off from it.

The small throwing knife seemed to struggle. But it offered no resistance before the main body. It was completely absorbed into the long saber before long. The two became one again.

Jin Buhuan was highly satisfied.

His status within his family would be genuinely solidified only by truly obtaining this innate divine weapon.

Clashing with Li Qi was the result of his careful deliberation. His own backer could suppress the consequences even if the person backing Li Qi was a Rank 6.

All of his goals inherently needed to be built upon this foundation as a second-generation young master. He had to maintain his status. That was the bedrock of everything.

As for the possibility that the backer of the other party was a Rank 5? How could that be? He would have received a warning the moment he provoked him if they truly possessed such a level of backing.

It meant his father could suppress the consequences since his father had not come to warn him.

Of course there was another possibility. It was that his father could not investigate the background of the other party at all. His father only managed to find out that this person actually had no major backing. His profound methods were perhaps simply the result of fortuitous encounters.

However just thinking about it made him want to laugh.

What kind of person could possess a background his own father could not investigate? Rank 4? Rank 3?

Was that even possible?

Consolidating his own power and status was vastly more important compared to such an infinitesimally small probability.

[^1]: The Eight Sounds (Ba Yin / 八音) refer to the ancient Chinese classification of musical instruments based on their primary construction materials: metal stone silk bamboo gourd earth leather and wood. In traditional cosmology these are linked to the Eight Winds which represent the seasonal winds blowing from the eight cardinal and intercardinal directions.
