# Chapter 147: Invitation

Ultimately, Lord Yan still did not refuse this request. Instead, he bowed to Li Qi, turned, and left. He left behind an entire street's worth of gifts sitting where they were, radiating waves of dazzling treasure light.

Pity, no one dared to touch even a single piece.

Li Qi took a glance, the bright light briefly dazzling his eyes.

_So wealthy._

However, with this glance, he suddenly frowned. He quickly walked over, stopping before that so-called "defective Rank 8" metal qi.

It was a long saber. Its entirety seemed perfectly natural, bearing no traces of forging or casting. It lacked even the slightest engraving or ornamentation. It looked like a piece of iron that had naturally grown out of a piece of dead wood, resembling a lizard flaring its angry scales.

Yet, this piece of iron had grown into the exact shape of a long saber, without a single hair's deviation, while that piece of dead wood served as its hilt.

This was... an innate divine weapon?

The metal qi was incredibly sharp, seething like firelight illuminating the night. Red and white qi coiled around it, fiercely unstoppable like a flood dragon striking at a person.

However, it possessed a sliver of defect. It was broken right at the tip of the blade, causing its overall power to drop by a massive margin. No longer perfect, even its aura of sharpness seemed blunted by half.

With the tip of the blade no longer whole, its killing power was naturally reduced by more than half. No wonder it was called a defective Rank 8. This thing was vastly stronger than all Rank 9 dharma treasures, but it fell far short of any true Rank 8 dharma treasure.

But what attracted Li Qi wasn't the power of this metal qi. He hadn't yet reached the point of being bewitched by money or dharma treasures.

_If one accepts ten thousand bushels of grain without regard for propriety and righteousness, what benefit do those ten thousand bushels bring to me?_

If something wasn't obtained through proper channels, what appeal could it possibly hold for him?

What truly drew his attention was the gap on this defective metal qi.

This gap... no matter how he looked at it, it resembled the throwing knife Piaoxu had given him.

No, it didn't just resemble it—it was an exact match!

No matter how he looked at it, they would fit together seamlessly, perfectly identical. As long as it was filled in, this would be a complete Rank 8 metal qi, an innate divine weapon without the slightest flaw!

Which meant—!

Li Qi abruptly jolted awake to the realization.

Jin Buhuan had been hunting down a tiger demon for unknown reasons and purposes. It was because he was hunting this tiger demon that he tracked it down to Piaoxu.

Piaoxu was a wild fox who happened to pass by the corpse of that hunted tiger demon. She inherited a portion of its ethereal soul and corporeal soul, resulting in the deaths of both the original tiger demon and the original wild fox. From this, a brand new demon named 'Piaoxu' was born, inheriting things from both sides. Among those things was this throwing knife, considered the absolute pinnacle of Rank 9.

Could it be that the one who originally wanted to hunt down the tiger demon and retrieve the defective Rank 8 metal qi fragment was Jin Buhuan?

The reason Jin Buhuan suddenly attacked in the bustling market was to snatch back this fragment?

In that case, by gifting this metal qi to him right now, seeming generous on the surface—was he actually trying to test if that item was in Li Qi's hands? Or rather, in Piaoxu's hands?

Sending gifts and forcing him to accept them... besides being a show of dominance, it was also a probe? And... laying the groundwork for a hidden trap?

Just for the sake of his petty schemes... he committed such atrocities?

Li Qi took a deep breath. The surrounding ash was gradually being blown away by the wind.

Jin Buhuan...

_You deserve to die._

---

At this very moment, within Jin Buhuan's mansion, the young master had his legs propped up on a table while several beautiful maids massaged them. Meanwhile, he questioned the physician kneeling in the hall below.

Upon closer inspection, it was actually the same physician who had briefly crossed paths with Li Qi and the others—the one Qi Ya had called over to treat Piaoxu.

He had merely shared a brief encounter with Li Qi, yet Jin Buhuan had still managed to track him down and question him face-to-face.

"So, that person also possesses extremely profound medical skills?" Jin Buhuan picked his ear, asking absentmindedly.

"R-replying to Young Master Jin, it is indeed so. Compared to his, my medical skills are like a flower-pecking sparrow compared to a True Dragon in the clouds. The gap cannot be measured in miles," the physician said, trembling uncontrollably.

"What level? Rank 9? To someone like you, a Rank 9 cultivator of the Medicine Dao would be vastly stronger than an unranked individual, wouldn't they?" Jin Buhuan continued to press.

"A-although my medical skills are lacking, my sect still has a few ranked elders and masters. I have witnessed many ranked techniques of the Medicine Dao. While miraculous, compared to that senior expert, they still appear somewhat amateurish..." the physician stuttered as he shook, breaking out in a cold sweat. However, the sweat wasn't from nervousness.

If one looked closely, they would find fine needles embedded into all ten of his fingers. He had clearly been tortured. The cold sweat he was currently drenched in was undeniably from the pain—pain so intense that even his voice was shaking.

"Oh? Not even Rank 9 physicians can compare? Hmm, I suppose that makes sense. Elder Si is a Rank 8 no matter how you slice it. To neutralize his curse-killing, one would definitely need a Rank 8 physician for it to be possible." Jin Buhuan nodded.

"A Rank 8 physician... who can also guide Lord Yan. Most likely, they cultivate both the Daoist Sects' methods and the Medicine Dao. That's quite normal," Jin Buhuan mused, muttering to himself.

It was indeed quite normal. The Daoist Sects had originally sought long life and enduring vision, the path of agelessness. For countless years, many within the Daoist Sects had been dedicated to researching the aspects of life.

After all, the ultimate pursuit of the Daoist Sects was unfettered freedom and eternal life.

Long life was not difficult; what was difficult was eternal life.

Even some plants and animals could achieve long life on their own, existing perpetually as long as they weren't affected by external forces.

But eternal life was different. Eternal life required immortality—not being killed, not dying of illness, not being bound or suppressed, and existing eternally according to one's own will.

To achieve this goal, the Daoist Sects developed fighting methods and healing methods. Whether it was combat or medicine, the ultimate purpose was to protect one's eternal life.

A Rank 8 senior expert of the Daoist Sects concurrently cultivating the Medicine Dao was very normal.

It's just that...

Jin Buhuan narrowed his eyes.

The presence of someone from the Daoist Sects was very normal. After all, the Shaman Dao was not hostile to any Daoist Tradition; its attitude toward the Daoist Sects and the demon race was quite peaceful.

Even toward the Human Dao, the Shaman Dao was not actually very hostile. It had always maintained an attitude of passive resistance rather than active annihilation. It was just that the Human Dao was too aggressive, making the intensity of the war seem like it was escalating.

Shamans believed that all things in heaven and earth existed naturally. Their role was to maintain balance and harmony, not to serve any particular side.

However, what kind of faction would assign a Rank 8 powerhouse of the Daoist Sects as a Dao Protector to follow a Rank 9 Shaman?

This combination was far too strange.

Did the Great Deer Kingdom have such a faction?

Was he not from the Great Deer Kingdom?

Then where did he come from?

Jin Buhuan fell deep into thought.

Just at that moment, Lord Yan walked in from outside.

Jin Buhuan immediately flipped up and stood, kicking away a concubine in the process. Paying it no mind, he slipped on his shoes, straightened his collar, and rose to greet him. "Ah, Lord Yan, you've returned. Well? Did he accept?"

"No, Young Master. He did not accept. All the etiquette officials were killed, yet he appeared completely unfazed," Lord Yan stated calmly.

Upon hearing this, Jin Buhuan clicked his tongue in wonder. Snapping his folding fan open, he said exaggeratedly, "Wow, what a ruthless character! He can draw his sword to help a stranger facing injustice, opposing this young master without a second thought. Yet, he can also sit back and watch over a hundred people slowly die right in front of him without the slightest ripple in his heart. This proves the man has an extremely resolute Dao Heart. He only does what he believes is right, unswayed by anything else. Truly worthy of being a genius with a Rank 8 powerhouse as a Dao Protector! I wonder which sect or family he hails from? How very interesting."

After saying this, he asked again, "So? With your personality, Lord Yan, if he didn't accept, you should have just kept waiting. Why did you suddenly return?"

Lord Yan answered peacefully, "Li Qi asked me to come and invite you, Young Master. He wishes for you to visit his residence to catch up and discuss a few things."

"Hah? Hahaha! Interesting. Fine, then I'll make a trip. Lord Yan, you go rest. Elder Si, we are taking a trip!" Hearing this, Jin Buhuan raised an eyebrow and decisively accepted the invitation without a moment's hesitation.

Li Qi was inviting him?

How truly fascinating. This time, it would be a real face-to-face confrontation, wouldn't it?

Last time could only be called a chance encounter; this time was a true, direct clash.

Jin Buhuan really wanted to know exactly what the person who stopped him from hunting the tiger demon—the person piecing together his own Rank 8 metal qi—was thinking.

But just as Jin Buhuan was walking briskly toward the door, Lord Yan suddenly spoke from behind him.

His tone was low. "Young Master, that senior..."

Jin Buhuan waved his hand dismissively. "Rest assured, Lord Yan. If the flaws in your cultivation method aren't fixed this time, I will naturally ask my father later to find a grandmaster of the Daoist Sects with profound knowledge—someone no worse than that person—to fix them for you."

Lord Yan bowed deeply in gratitude, then watched Jin Buhuan leave.

His goal was simply just this.

Shen Shuibi's lecture being interrupted last time had caused him immense pain. However, since Jin Buhuan promised to make up for it, all was well.

---

The walk from Jin Buhuan's mansion to the inn where Li Qi was staying took only fifteen minutes.

If they were to put on airs, organize a procession, and have him carried over in a six-bearer palanquin, the time would have easily tripled or quintupled.

But he was already too impatient to wait to meet Li Qi.

He wondered how the other party planned to receive him?

With Elder Si following behind and his maids by his side, Jin Buhuan quickened his pace, rapidly arriving at Li Qi's inn.

However, the moment they stepped into the street, the three of them stopped simultaneously.

It was because all three felt that the atmosphere of this street was vastly different from the outside world.

The entire street felt as if it had been washed with water, cleansed of all flaws and filth. The weather and atmosphere felt completely renewed; even the smell of the air was different.

Beyond that, it felt as if countless eyes were watching them from all around, sending a chill deep into their bones and marrow.

The ground seemed to be faintly radiating heat. The colors of the sun and the sky also skewed somewhat white, completely unlike their usual appearance.

Though they had only taken a single step into the street, it made them feel as though they had entered another world.

"...Using a personal cosmos to reconstruct the yin and yang of an area, changing heaven and earth, and reversing the four seasons. What an impressive method. Could a Rank 9 truly lay down such a formation?" Elder Si, who also walked the Shaman Dao, felt somewhat alarmed.

Just what kind of genius was this? He himself had only learned this trick after reaching Rank 8.

"Interesting. What is the use of this move? Other than making people feel uncomfortable?" Jin Buhuan asked.

"It allows for small-scale manipulation of the flow of qi. It's equivalent to setting up a home field advantage in advance. While inside it, his strength will increase by a level, and ours will decrease by a level. Back and forth, when facing enemies of the same rank, it creates a two-level disparity in strength, practically securing victory. However, it's useless against this old man. If Young Master dislikes it, I can shatter it right now. It is merely a Rank 9 personal cosmos; breaking it will not be difficult," Elder Si explained.

"Since it can be broken with a wave of the hand, there's no need to pay it any mind. Let's go in. We'll just treat it as if he decorated the place for us." Jin Buhuan magnanimously waved his fan, showing his indifference.

Elder Si nodded, making no move to shatter the field. However, he added, "It's just that... Young Master, normally, when a personal cosmos is deployed, it doesn't take on such a gloomy and miserable appearance. Instead, it aligns more closely with the individual's traits. Whatever kind of person they are, that is what their expanded personal cosmos will look like."

"From what I have observed, this person is not of a sinister or malicious nature. Normally, his personal cosmos wouldn't look like this. He must have intentionally made it this way. Look, the surroundings are thick with ghostly qi, and there's the lingering intent of the dead's gazes. He is likely mocking your previous actions as lacking in virtue, Young Master. Shall we really not break it even so?" Elder Si asked.

"Lacking in virtue? Tsk. Virtues are what the strong use to restrain themselves, not what the weak use to restrain the strong." Jin Buhuan scoffed. "Using such petty methods to complain to me is like the angry glaring of a petty housewife. It lacks any masculine spine and only makes me look down on him. This young master actually thought he truly didn't care about the dead in the slightest."

Finishing his words, Jin Buhuan slung an arm around a maid's shoulder. "Let's go. We'll go broaden our horizons and see what other skills he has besides this little field of his."

As they walked along, the entire street remained deserted. Elder Si looked around, seemingly inspecting the profound mysteries of the place.

As a Rank 8 of the Shaman Dao, he was theoretically much stronger than Li Qi. Saying he could crush him would be an understatement.

But upon walking in and taking a closer look, he realized that while he might be able to defeat technique with sheer power and forcibly shatter this altered personal cosmos, in reality... he actually couldn't understand many of the profound mysteries woven into it.

_How is the qi here... changing like this?_

_Why does this personal cosmos possess such a 'thunderous and seething' momentum, yet remain hidden within the gloom? How was this achieved?_

_This posture... it's 'seeking a needle in a mustard seed'. Is this a method of musical temperament? No, that doesn't seem right... Then what is it?_

_This... is the solitary moon and solemn qi? No, no, no!_

The more Elder Si looked, the more alarmed he became. Of these numerous arrangements, he could fully understand thirty percent, slightly grasp thirty percent, and barely deduce another thirty percent. However, the final ten percent was completely incomprehensible to him, no matter how hard he tried.

They both walked the Shaman Dao. He had encountered fortuitous events, possessed several spell arts that had leaked from Shaman God Mountain, and had attained the Rank 8 Dao. He prided himself on his profound knowledge. Yet now, he couldn't even decipher the layout set up by a Rank 9 Shaman!

How was this possible?!

Just what was this person's background?!

Thinking of this, Elder Si stepped forward and stopped Jin Buhuan. "Young Master, be careful. There are some strange anomalies in this place. There are a few points where I can only use brute force to break them, but I cannot see through their specifics."

Jin Buhuan frowned. "Even you can't understand them?"

But before he could receive an answer from Elder Si, Li Qi walked out of the inn's main doors not far away.

"We meet again, Brother Jin!"
