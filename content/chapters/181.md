# Chapter 181: Lord Huangshan

Through Li Qi's shamanic vision, he could clearly see a thread tethering the butterfly, continuously extracting her essence qi.

It was a technique Li Qi also knew—the method used to lay down a landscape grand array.

The vast majority of shamans were almost entirely clueless when it came to the yin-yang calculation arrays, as they were overwhelmingly complex. However, they were highly proficient in another type of array: the landscape grand array.

The so-called landscape grand array fundamentally relied on borrowing the power of the mountains and rivers. It used individual Earthly Divinities of the terrain as nodes, weaving mountain qi and water qi into a network of veins to forge a unified whole.

This type of array lacked the miraculous profundity of the Daoist Sects' yin-yang calculation arrays. After all, those formations could upend heaven and earth, reverse yin and yang, and achieve unbelievable feats for a very small price.

But the advantage of a landscape array lay in its unmatched magnitude.

A formation linking one mountain or water god after another, governed by a shaman. Though this power lacked finesse, its scale was immense. It could mobilize colossal energy, and once unified, it was nearly indestructible. Unless a mighty cultivator intervened and directly pulverized the entire expanse of mountain and water veins, the array could not be dismantled by force.

Naturally, one could dismantle the connections between each individual node, but that required meticulous, time-consuming effort.

However, all of this hinged on a single prerequisite: the presiding shaman acting as a central mediator, persuading the various gods of the mountains and waters, the local spirits, and other forces to voluntarily contribute their power.

"Did someone make a deal with you? Your previous deficit of essence qi was clearly the result of someone forcefully extracting it. What exactly is going on!" Li Qi's emotions were running high, his tone taking on a sharp edge.

Hearing her benefactor's harsh words, the little girl was terrified.

Butterflies were an easily startled species to begin with. Being yelled at by her benefactor caused her to tremble uncontrollably, shivering as if she wore no clothes at all.

Oh, she _wasn't_ wearing clothes. She was just draped in Li Qi's robe.

Old Ma shot them a sidelong glance.

Then, he buried his head and went back to eating.

Li Qi was always meddling in other people's business; Old Ma was used to it. As long as it didn't interfere with his meal and rest, it was fine.

Seeing the young butterfly-girl's reaction, Li Qi took a deep breath to suppress his agitation. He found a large rock to sit on and patted the empty space beside him. "Come, sit here. Tell me, has anything unusual happened recently?"

"Ben... benefactor, are you angry?" the butterfly asked cautiously. "I wouldn't dare sit with you. I am fine kneeling right here."

Hearing this, Li Qi couldn't help but roll his eyes. He stood up, lifted her off the ground, and plopped her down beside him.

Before she could react, he pressed on, "Don't bother with empty pleasantries. Haven't you noticed? This thing on your body is draining your essence qi. I didn't spot it earlier, but when you entered the chrysalis and reshaped your form, your body should have been completely whole and unblemished. Instead, an extra thread appeared, and that's when I caught it."

"A thread?" The young girl was confused. Her initial nervousness was washed away by Li Qi's questioning, replaced by an effort to recall recent events.

After all, although she was considered long-lived for a butterfly, she had practically only existed for four or five years, and had possessed sentience for only about four. Her mind was immature; she was still a single-track thinker.

Thinking and feeling anxious were two things she struggled to do simultaneously.

The barrage of questions from Li Qi forced her to think, and in doing so, she forgot she was supposed to be afraid.

"Ben... benefactor... Earlier, the mountain king of this region summoned all the demons who had attained sentience. He asked us for a favor, requesting that we each offer a sliver of essence qi to help him forge a dharma treasure. In return, he gave us some spirit fruits as payment."

"Everyone was afraid he would turn hostile if we refused, so we all agreed. Could it be related to this?" the butterfly asked.

"The mountain king? Is he the mountain god of this peak?" Li Qi pressed.

"Yes... He calls himself Lord Huangshan. Benefactor, did he steal my essence qi?" she asked timidly.

"Perhaps. Let me snap it off for you first; then, you just need to show me the way." Li Qi reached his hand down the collar of the robe and felt along her back.

Since the butterfly was only draped in his robe, reaching in through the gap at the nape of her neck allowed him to easily trace the thread without obstruction.

His mind completely focused, Li Qi felt his way down past her shoulder blades, tracing the spine until he reached the third vertebra.

The third vertebra, located at the cervical spine, corresponded exactly to the passage where qi flowed from the throat.

The Fengmen acupoint lay at the back of the head, and the third vertebra housed the Feishu acupoint. Both were porous points, highly susceptible to wind infiltration.

"Take a deep breath and relax. Cast out all stray thoughts; let your eyes not wander, and guard your spirit within its dwelling," Li Qi reminded her.

Then, he exerted a sudden burst of strength, striking out with his Martial Dao inner qi.

The nature of Martial Dao inner qi was intensely pure. It devoured and assimilated all qi from foreign sources, refining them into a homogenous substance. Consequently, martial artists rarely possessed many divine abilities, but their inner qi could shatter most spell arts.

Because, at their core, all spell arts and divine abilities were simply different applications of the boundless 'qi' existing between heaven and earth.

The inner qi severed the thread.

The space previously occupied by the thread now exposed a tiny puncture, leaving a flaw in the butterfly's personal cosmos.

External qi flooded into the breach, instantly leaving her dizzy and disoriented.

When external qi invaded the brain, it clouded the vision and weighed heavily on the head—a symptom of excessive Yang.

Li Qi sealed the flaw with his finger, then channeled vital qi to mend the breach.

"Done. This thing won't drain your essence qi anymore. Now, where is this Lord Huangshan?" Li Qi asked.

With the thread plucked from her body, the little butterfly experienced a fleeting moment of dizziness before suddenly feeling refreshed and invigorated. Her body felt incredibly light.

She immediately realized that this man sitting before her had aided her three separate times in less than a quarter of an hour.

At this thought, the butterfly scrambled to her feet and fell to her knees in worship. "I will lead the way for my benefactor at once!"

Li Qi felt a sense of urgency as well. Though Wang Boyan had informed him of the landscape grand array's existence, seeing it with his own eyes was a different matter entirely. He was eager to investigate it.

"Old Ma, keep eating. I'll come find you later. If I'm not back in an hour, just keep resting," Li Qi called out.

Old Ma let out a whinny to show he understood.

Seeing this, Li Qi followed the little butterfly into the mountains.

Barefoot and swathed in Li Qi's robe, she guided him through the mountain paths.

Yet, along the way, she appeared hesitant, frequently glancing back over her shoulder at Li Qi.

"Are you worried about the consequences? Rest easy, I won't let them drag you into this." Seeing her indecision and assuming she feared retaliation, Li Qi offered words of comfort.

But the little butterfly shook her head, choosing her words carefully. "It's not that. It's just... I still don't have a name. I would like to ask my benefactor to grant me one."

"A name. I see." Li Qi finally remembered her earlier request.

"'Grant' is too heavy a word, but I can certainly help you pick one." Walking along the mountain trail, Li Qi began to ponder.

After walking a distance, he looked at his surroundings and found inspiration. He smiled. "Butterflies adore fragrant grasses. Dancing butterflies and fluttering flowers find harmony together, as their sweet fragrance brings joy to bees and butterflies alike. Thus, a gentleman engages not in sordid deeds, but chooses to associate with those of pure and fragrant character. With a pristine primal qi, never exposing one's sharp edges—this is a peaceful haven for conducting oneself in the world. Following the natural flow of things leads to an untroubled life, as harmonious as a full moon reflecting perfectly in a basin of water."

"Drawing from this meaning, I will name you Zhufang—Pursuing Fragrance. I hope that in the future, you will avoid the foul and impure, and seek out the fragrant orchids. May you never become a lowly fly drawn to filth, but instead continue to be a butterfly dancing among the flowers."

"Thank you, benefactor! May I boldly ask for my benefactor's name?" The butterfly was overjoyed, bowing repeatedly.

Li Qi hastily pulled her to her feet. "My name is Li Qi. And stop dropping to your knees over every little thing!"

"Zhufang understands! From now on, my name is Li Zhufang!" she declared gleefully.

"Ah? You're taking my surname?" Having just steadied her, Li Qi frowned at her words.

Adopting another person's surname was no trivial matter. It would impact her entire future. A name was something that accompanied a person for a lifetime.

"Zhufang is but a wild butterfly, naturally possessing neither name nor clan," Li Zhufang replied immediately. "My survival is entirely due to my benefactor's rescue. My ability to shapeshift relied on my benefactor's enlightening words. Even the hidden danger within my body was uprooted with your help. Such monumental grace is beyond my ability to repay; I can only engrave it into my heart! I hope that one day I will have the chance to return the favor!"

The little girl's face was etched with absolute certainty, giving Li Qi a slight headache.

The main issue was that she was simply too young. Having just taken human form, she was only twelve or thirteen years old, barely starting to grow.

Li Qi was exceedingly bad at dealing with children.

Thus, he decided to put on a stern face. "Don't overthink it. Helping you was nothing more than lifting a finger. I don't need your repayment. Just guide the way properly and help me locate Lord Huangshan."

"Yes!" Li Zhufang, undeterred and utterly cheerful, agreed instantly and resumed leading the way.

The mountain paths were narrow and treacherous, with hundreds of perilous obstacles blocking the route. However, Li Qi was long accustomed to traversing such terrain, and Li Zhufang, being a demon born from a butterfly, could simply fly. Consequently, they encountered no significant hindrances.

As in most parts of Baiyue, the scenery along the way was stunning, brimming with lush foliage. However, in certain areas, the vegetation was beginning to wither and yellow in an abnormal fashion.

_Is the landscape grand array beginning to drain the mountain qi, causing the trees to wither?_ Li Qi thought to himself as he surveyed the decay.

Rounding another bend, they came upon a deep pool formed by a plunging cascade. The spring water was a deep, crystalline azure. Within it swam a giant fish, a full twelve feet in length. It moved listlessly through the water, appearing utterly drained.

Seeing this, Li Qi knew instantly that this giant fish had also had its essence qi siphoned away, just like Li Zhufang.

But Li Zhufang was a butterfly, inherently fragile. Extracting even a sliver had nearly cost her her life.

The giant fish, however, shared a similar cultivation base to Li Zhufang—having attained spiritual sentience and standing on the verge of shapeshifting—but possessed a robust body overflowing with vitality. Losing some essence qi only left it fatigued; its foundation remained untouched.

Noting this, Li Qi didn't intervene further. He pressed forward to seek out Lord Huangshan.

They walked another three miles westward until they reached a towering peak. A perilous cliff stretched out in isolation, curling inward to form a gorge. Crossing the ridge and descending slightly, they skirted the dangerous precipice. The mountain landscape opened up once more, and they proceeded up the slope.

Upon entering this mountain, they found what felt like a colossal wind tunnel. The gale roared endlessly, tearing the drifting clouds apart and throwing them back together in chaotic patterns.

Stopping at this point, Li Zhufang spoke up. "Benefactor, Lord Huangshan is at the bottom of Tiger Roar Cliff... but I dare not go down."

"Tiger Roar Cliff. A fitting name." Li Qi peered downward, listening to the howling tempest.

The wind bellowed and rumbled like a tiger roaring in the forest, a sound capable of shattering a person's courage.

But occasionally, piercing through the gales, Li Qi caught the sound of a genuine tiger's roar—laced with savagery and violent rage, echoing in tandem with the wind.

The roar of the beast and the roar of the wind intertwined. The combined might shook the mountain forests, carrying a momentum that seemed capable of soaring into the heavens and plunging into the seas.

"This local mountain god... is a Rank 9 tiger demon? So it walks the Divine Dao," Li Qi deduced after observing for a moment.

He turned back to Li Zhufang. "Wait here. I'll go down for a moment and return shortly."

With that, Li Qi grabbed hold of the cliff face and carefully began his descent.

He, too, wished he could just leap down in a dashing display, but regrettably, he couldn't fly.

Tiger Roar Cliff was hundreds of feet high. If he simply jumped, his Rank 9 Martial Dao physique might save him from death, but snapping his legs would be practically guaranteed.

It was best to be honest and climb down.

Stepping nimbly from one rocky outcrop to the next, Li Qi swiftly reached the bottom of the cliff.

But before his feet even touched the ground, he sensed murderous intent radiating from the shadows below.

A pair of blood-red eyes glared out from a cavernous lair below, piercing through the howling mountain winds.

A massive, ferocious tiger bolted from the depths. Swishing its tail and shaking its massive head, it stretched its limbs. Its maw was like a basin of blood, its shoulders broad, its waist forged of steel. Armed with golden claws and an iron tail, it glared fiercely at Li Qi and roared, speaking human words: "Which arrogant whelp dares to disturb this great god's peace?!"

Listening to the outburst, Li Qi estimated the remaining distance. Seeing he had about sixty feet left to go—a fall that shouldn't break his legs—he simply let go and plummeted, crashing heavily into the dirt just thirty feet from the great beast.

"Lord Huangshan, I presume?" Li Qi asked, simultaneously sweeping his gaze across their surroundings.

This entire area was saturated with vicious qi and death qi. Countless bones were scattered about—some human, some animal. They were likely the leftovers of Lord Huangshan's meals, piled up outside his den.

Li Qi didn't harbor any particular revulsion over the tiger eating people. After all, it was a tiger. It ate humans just as it ate other animals; one couldn't exactly forbid a tiger from eating meat.

However, the arrangement of these bones...

Li Qi frowned. "Who taught you this? The placement of these bones... You don't have any chang ghosts around you. Have you shattered them all and mixed them into the skeletons? Using the essence qi of hundreds of demons in this mountain as a catalyst, combined with the chang ghosts you've accumulated, and utilizing the power of the landscape grand array to nurture them—are you trying to birth a bone demon? Moreover, the bone demon's intellect is being cultivated from the divine souls of your chang ghosts, ensuring it will naturally obey your commands. Such a ruthless method... is not something a practitioner of the ordinary Divine Dao would know. This is clearly a Gu Shaman art."

Indeed, it was a brutal method. This type of formation required the sacrifice of mountain and water qi, and there was no telling how many minor demons like Li Zhufang would be drained dry in the process. All of this just to breed a Rank 9 bone demon to serve as a retainer.

The most crucial point was that this method belonged strictly to the Gu Shaman lineage.

Unlike orthodox shamans, the Gu Shaman lineage specialized in refining Gu insects and breeding malignant demons to act as their bodyguards. They also specialized in casting curses. Like the Ghost Shamans, they were a branch of the Shaman Dao, and their methods were extraordinarily insidious.

This bone demon was one of the retainers that the Gu Shaman lineage could forge.

Li Qi sighed inwardly.

_As expected. Is this the handiwork of those from Vast Yang?_

There was just one problem.

A landscape grand array did not actually require blood sacrifices.

So why were they gathering blood sacrifices? Or was the blood sacrifice simply a lie fabricated by Wang Boyan to provoke him?
