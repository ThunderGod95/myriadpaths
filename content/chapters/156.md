# Chapter 156: The Kingdom Lord

'Chen' is a measure of the celestial phenomena.

Among the sun, moon, and stars, “chen” refers to the intersection points of the sun, moon, and stars in the sky at different times.

Li Qi had already reviewed this point when he was in the Earth Oil World. _(For details, see Chapter 121)_

A single 'shichen' refers to the time it takes for celestial phenomena to shift.

(TL Note: One _shichen_ is Two _hours_.)

This was... the Grand Invocator's Four Phenomena qi.

Back when Li Qi was taken in as a disciple by Young Master Zhu, Young Master Zhu, just like the Green Mountain God, had given his junior a life-saving item.

The Green Mountain God gave a wisp of his metal qi; once activated, it could summon a pre-set, casual strike from Green Mountain.

Li Qi's was the four qi of sun, moon, stars, and constellations, created for him by the Grand Invocator. Not only could they help Li Qi perceive celestial phenomena and discern good from ill fortune, but they could also be released at a critical moment to save his life.

But Li Qi never expected it to manifest in such a breathtakingly spectacular manner.

The Four Phenomena qi erased the very fact that the opponent had attacked from within cosmic time, returning everything to its original state.

The previously shattered firmament was restored, and all the damage caused by the aftershocks of the Green Mountain God's attack was completely healed.

While Li Qi stood in a daze, marveling at the mighty power of the Four Phenomena qi, far away in Green Mountain...

"How did my attack disappear?" The Green Mountain God awakened from his secluded cultivation.

He had been in seclusion all these years, attempting to break through to Rank 5. He rarely showed his face in the outside world and engaged in almost no communication. Only a few wisps of his divided spirit were keeping an eye on his children, especially Jin Buhuan.

When the metal qi on Jin Buhuan's body was activated, he awakened from his seclusion almost instantly.

Then, he clearly saw his own attack being neutralized.

"It was canceled out? Someone is protecting him? An unfamiliar aura, not that old fool." The Green Mountain God frowned. He sent out his senses—it wasn't the lord of the Green Mountain Kingdom.

Did another Rank 6... or even a Rank 5 intervene?

He hadn't clearly seen how his attack disappeared just now, and that qi had dissipated incredibly fast, making it impossible to trace back.

Did someone want to meddle in affairs here?

_That junior..._

Jin Chi gazed into the distance, using a spell art to clearly see Li Qi standing there in a daze.

He reached out his hand.

This time, it was no casual strike.

He intended to capture Li Qi alive and properly interrogate him about who wanted to stick their nose into the Green Mountain Kingdom's business.

With that thought in mind, he extended his hand.

Metal qi condensed into a massive hand, crossing a distance of two thousand miles in a mere instant, charging right in front of Li Qi!

Only then did Li Qi snap back to reality, letting out a chuckle.

He didn't even pay attention to the massive hand closing in on him.

Instead, he casually drew his tang saber and walked over to the unconscious Jin Buhuan.

Right in front of the Green Mountain God, he brought the blade down.

"You dare, you scoundrel?!" the Green Mountain God roared in furious shock.

He had already made a move, yet this person was still so arrogant!? Did he truly want an irreconcilable blood feud with him?!

A mere Rank 9 junior—even with a backer—how could he dare to do such a thing? Forging a grudge with him was a dead end, wasn't it? Where did a Rank 9 junior find the nerve?

But regardless, the Green Mountain God no longer held back his strength to capture him alive. Instead, he accelerated the strike directly, intending to swat Li Qi to death before he could finish the execution.

Compared to capturing him alive, his own son's life was far more important.

The massive hand of metal qi tore through the sky.

Then, the drifting clouds surged.

In the sky, a palace suddenly materialized.

It was a magnificent palace, with crimson and indigo glazed tiles and intricately painted pillars. Brocade curtains hung in the halls, and carvings of flying immortals adorned the walls. Willows and elms lined the paths, their colors complementing the distant mountains. It was a scene straight out of a painting.

Inside, green vines sprouted and flowers bloomed and withered. Jade dew collected on the twin towers, and the silver river of stars flowed with light.

Strange beasts roamed around the palace.

To the left were aquatic creatures: pythons, tortoises, and softshell turtles with distinct carapaces, along with brocade carp, salamanders, pearl rays, and the like. They swam without water, swaying as they hovered in the air beside the palace, appearing as part of the scenery.

To the right were avian creatures: forms of parrots, fishing owls, and turtledoves, endearing and lovely. Then there were owls, white crows, and wind falcons—incredibly ferocious birds of prey. They all flapped their wings, circling the right side of the palace like guards.

The palace stood stark and solitary in the center of the heavens. Below it, all the mountains seemed to bow in submission, and clouds gathered from ten thousand miles around.

It could only be said that outside the palace, the sky was like water, and the palace was a sky within the water.

This palace happened to block the path of the massive hand extended by the Green Mountain God.

The two collided with a thunderous boom.

It was like a million lions roaring at once, or a Heavenly God lashing the mortal world with a whip of thunder. A hurricane-force wind tore through the air.

Jin Chi’s full-force blow was like an explosion in a dragon’s pool, sending a blizzard of energy across the sky. The clouds for ten thousand miles were instantly cleared, revealing the vast expanse of the mountains and rivers below. It was a majestic sight.

And yet, the blow had been blocked. Aside from the spectacular aftershocks, all its power had been neutralized, causing no other effect.

In the instant the blow was blocked, Li Qi’s blade had already found its mark.

The sharp blade met almost no resistance as it sliced through.
Jin Buhuan was beheaded.

His divine soul seemed to want to flee his body, but Li Qi activated the baleful qi within his blade. The baleful qi surged forth, churning the divine soul to shreds.

It couldn't quite be considered a complete dispersion of his soul, but there were no memories left to speak of.

"Rat!!! You—!" Jin Chi roared in absolute fury! Even Li Qi heard it!

But the killing intent and power contained within the roar were casually blocked by that palace.

"Jin Chi, a dignified Rank 6, why must you get angry with a junior? In a struggle between the younger generation, for an elder to intervene makes you appear quite petty, does it not?" A lazy voice drifted from the palace.

However, although the voice sounded languid, anyone listening could hear the absolute satisfaction and pleasure within it.

He was definitely in a very good mood.

"This junior killed my beloved son; I must kill him. Kingdom Lord, please step aside, otherwise do not blame Jin Chi for being impolite." The Green Mountain God condensed his dharma body. Above Green Mountain, an imposing White Tiger dharma avatar manifested, confronting the palace from two thousand miles away.

"Alas, though Jin Buhuan’s divine soul is shattered, it has not been annihilated. If you take it back now and send it into the cycle of reincarnation, you can have a son again in a hundred years. If you insist on fighting for face now and let his divine soul be destroyed, you will truly have no son left," the Green Mountain Kingdom's lord sighed.

Then, he reached out a hand, grabbing Jin Buhuan's churned, fragmented soul. "I will not hold it against you for acting rashly. Take it. With your resources, bribing a Yin Bailiff or two should not be difficult. Though your son’s memories are gone, you can still salvage the rest."

"Or would you rather make a scene here over a junior? Have you not realized that it wasn’t me who blocked your first attack?" The kingdom lord's lazy tone vanished, replaced by a serious reply to Jin Chi.

This was a threat.

If Jin Chi insisted on fighting here, he would face a siege.

Because Li Qi had already proven that there was indeed someone backing him.

Although that person wouldn't actually care about him.

But Jin Chi didn't know that, nor did the kingdom lord. The Four Phenomena qi caused absolutely no destruction, and barely left an aura to anyone other than Li Qi. Thus, it only felt unexceptional—like it had merely blocked Jin Chi's casual strike.

Both of them firmly believed that if a fight truly broke out, a third party would intervene, turning the current situation into a two-versus-one.

That strand of qi from just now could not be faked.

This was how a battle between two tigers morphed into a struggle for supremacy among three.

And it was where Li Qi's chance of survival lay.

Although Li Qi knew that for his master, there was absolutely no need to intervene.

If the fights between Rank 9 juniors were like kindergarteners scuffling, then a Rank 5 or 6 intervening would be petty and shameful, like an adult storming into the school to beat up the other kid.

Then to the Grand Invocator, even a Rank 5 intervening would at most be like older kindergarteners bullying a newly enrolled kindergartener. If he had to personally protect his student against that, it would be even more disgraceful.

Furthermore, Li Qi figured he probably didn't even know what was happening here.

This person didn't seem like someone who would watch him all the time... It was more likely he was left to sink or swim on his own.

On the other side, gripping Jin Buhuan's remnant soul, Jin Chi ground his teeth.

However, while his killing power was immense and he was usually tyrannical, he wasn't stupid.

Thus, he clenched the remnant soul, turned around, and left.

The most pressing matter was to find a familiar Yin Bailiff and send his beloved son into the cycle of reincarnation.

This way, he could still be saved.

The White Tiger dharma avatar dissipated. Only, before leaving, he cast a deep glare at Li Qi, as if to brand his appearance and aura into his memory forever.

Li Qi was naturally aware of all this.

However, he wasn't nervous, because up until this point, everything was still within his calculations.

Of course, he also understood that if things spun out of control and deviated from his calculations, he would likely suffer a massive fall.

But it couldn't be helped. What in the world was ever guaranteed to succeed?

Man proposes, Heaven disposes; that was all.

Fortunately, his luck was good this time. Nothing went wrong, and everything played out exactly according to his script.

Therefore, he calmly collected Jin Buhuan's corpse, severed the head completely, hung it separately at his waist, and then dug a hole to bury the body.

He didn't touch the inheritance or belongings, leaving them all buried with him.

Only, those two streaks of metal qi were still suppressed within Li Qi's body. Although they couldn't be extracted temporarily, Li Qi gladly accepted them as a gift.

And the reason he took the head was simple: the matter wasn't finished yet.

Killing Jin Buhuan alone wasn't enough.

To kill a man, one must destroy his spirit.

Having done all this, Li Qi raised his head and looked at the magnificent palace floating in the sky.

It was truly massive. A single palace was comparable to the entirety of Clear Mist City.

_It's just... where do these people normally stay? The outer realms? How many outer realms could withstand the havoc these people could wreak?_

If All Under Heaven was filled with fierce men of this caliber, it wouldn't take long for this world to be shattered by them, right?

Because Li Qi noticed that when the two of them clashed, they clearly held back deliberately, ensuring the aftershocks wouldn't crash into the ground and harm the innocent.

There were probably some restrictions in place. If experts of this level could act freely, then despite how vast All Under Heaven was, how many inches of land would remain intact?

While Li Qi was pondering these matters, a voice drifted down from the palace in the sky. "Li Qi... right?"

"That is this junior. Greetings, Kingdom Lord." Hearing the voice, Li Qi immediately bowed.

"You have quite the nerve to make such a gamble. Uprooting the Jin family's power in Clear Mist City overnight—you are much more capable than that fox I'm keeping. She has wasted a century in Clear Mist City, only allowing the Jin family's influence to grow even stronger. Yet you executed it so efficiently." The voice from the palace spoke amiably to Li Qi.

"It is not the Clear Mist Sect Master's fault. The crux of toppling Jin Buhuan lay with the Green Mountain God himself. Had the mighty figure behind me not provided deterrence today, this matter likely wouldn't have concluded so easily. In the end, it was because I acted as a variable, not because the Sect Master is incompetent. If she truly were incompetent, how could I have rallied those factions overnight and suppressed Jin Buhuan's other helpers?" Li Qi kept his spine straight and answered methodically.

"Well reasoned. But that doesn't change the fact that you truly did a good job. Lobbying forty-five factions overnight, even running to the Clear Mist Sect—setting aside everything else, just this eloquence and thick skin is worth praising," the kingdom lord complimented.

"The Kingdom Lord praises me too highly," Li Qi replied.

"However, I have a question. Why did you intervene? Do not try to deceive me with such nonsense; I am not Hu Xian'er," the kingdom lord asked again.

"You and I both know, if it were for righteousness, there are so many unrighteous matters in All Under Heaven. Would you risk your life for every single one of them? There are so many affairs in this world; can you manage them all?"

Li Qi forced a dry chuckle and shook his head.

Speaking the language of men to men, and the language of ghosts to ghosts. Previously, to persuade the Clear Mist Sect Master, he had spun a tale of profit, convincing Hu Xian'er that his actions were truly driven by self-interest.

However, it was clear that the kingdom lord didn't buy that excuse.

Thus, Li Qi answered him seriously this time. "This junior has his own internal scale, weighing gains and losses, judging right from wrong, and using it to decide how I should act."

"There are so many unjust and unrighteous matters in this world. Some I can manage, some I cannot reach, and some I have no qualifications to interfere with. This is perfectly natural. So, just because the world is like this, should I do nothing at all?" Li Qi asked in return.

"If we do nothing, then why bother cultivating? Life and death are natural phenomena; why cultivate to resist them? Injustice and unfairness exist naturally; why interfere with human effort?"

Li Qi answered.

"Right now, my cultivation is lowly, so I manage the matters within my capabilities. When my cultivation becomes profound, I will manage even more matters."

"With so many affairs in the world, of course I cannot manage them all. Sometimes, things happen right in front of me, but I lack the ability and cannot intervene. But no matter how numerous or difficult these matters are, they are not an excuse to sit idly by and ignore the rest."

"Why does the Kingdom Lord only see the myriad of things in All Under Heaven that cannot be done, yet fail to see the myriad of things that _can_ be done?"

Li Qi looked up at the palace and solemnly declared his answer.

Yes, why only look at "so many things that cannot be done" instead of looking at "so many things that can be done"?

Hearing this, the kingdom lord threw his head back and laughed loudly, but said no more.

Li Qi continued his response, "I have heard a saying: 'Do not abstain from a good deed simply because it is small.' The scale in my heart determined that this matter could be done, so I did it. If it hadn't crossed that threshold, I wouldn't have acted."

Upon hearing this, the kingdom lord finally felt a trace of interest. He asked, "Fascinating. Then how do you define this threshold?"

"If it is something I can accomplish conveniently, then I do it. If a price must be paid, then I must weigh it again. It cannot be generalized."

The kingdom lord asked again, "What if two matters conflict, and you truly cannot resolve them?"

Hearing this question, Li Qi spread his hands helplessly. "Then I simply ignore it. I perform righteous deeds because it comes from my heart, not because it is a mandatory task forced upon me by someone else. So what if I don't do it?"

The kingdom lord's voice pierced through the palace. "Hahaha! Interesting, truly interesting! A junior like you has a boundless future. I wonder what kind of elders could raise a disciple like you. Were it not for the fact that you already have a master, I would be tempted to take you as a disciple myself."

"However, even without taking you as a disciple, merely helping me uproot the Jin family's influence is more than enough for me to reward you. What is it that you desire? Just say the word!"
