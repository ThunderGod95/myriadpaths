# Chapter 146: Inquiry

Piaoxu took Qi Ya and the other handymen and retreated to the rear courtyard to take shelter.

Li Qi had delivered his harsh words but took no further action. He simply stood at the door and waited in silence.

In reality, however, he was not idle.

He was observing the terrain, calculating the placement of his altar and its corresponding orientation.

He still lacked sufficient intelligence. For someone like Li Qi—who preferred to have a concrete plan before acting, a trait that often made him seem somewhat indecisive—he would absolutely avoid a direct confrontation with Jin Buhuan at this very moment.

Knowledge and intelligence were the foundations of action. To act without them was reckless impulsivity and the root of disaster.

Li Qi observed his surroundings for a while and confirmed the orientation. He then began to set up the altar, arranging the sacrifices used to 'knock on the door' and commencing the ritual with a reverent ceremony.

A moment later, a response emerged from the altar.

The ritual was complete.

The sacrificial rite, the Invocator, and the Earthly Divinity—these three elements had reached an alignment.

Li Qi felt his body lighten as he arrived at that familiar state of chaos...

Wait, this space wasn't familiar at all!

Where was that incomparably hollow, chaotic, and boundlessly dark expanse of the unseen world?!

How did this place have complete mountains, rivers, and water veins? Although it was devoid of life, a certain dormant vitality could still be felt within it.

The sky was clear and the earth was bright, brimming with the harmonious interplay of the Three Yangs. The former chaotic space had now transformed into a tangible small world.

Li Qi instantly realized the reason behind this transformation.

As it turned out, the chaotic space where he previously conducted his rituals was his own personal cosmos!

The space he entered to communicate during every ritual was actually within his own body?

Previously, his cultivation had been too shallow, not yet having entered the ranks. Thus, his personal cosmos had remained a chaotic blur. Now that he was ranked, a small world had evolved from the chaos, altering the surrounding scenery?

This was the first time he had clearly sensed the evolution of the small world within his body.

Li Qi tentatively exerted his power here. He found that a breath from his lips summoned the wind, sending sand and stones flying. Wherever his foot fell, dust bloomed from the void.

Pointing at the sky brought forth the sun, moon, and stars; pointing at the earth instantly manifested mountains, rivers, and lakes.

He could exhale water to form clouds, wave his hand to summon fog, gather soil into mountains, and pierce the earth to create abysses.

It was as if this world was entirely at his mercy.

Li Qi was still testing his control over the realm when he suddenly heard a dry cough.

Following the cough, a gentle and cautious voice rang out. "Cough, cough! Young Master, for what matter have you summoned this minor god?"

It was the deity he had invoked.

Li Qi felt a bit embarrassed.

He naturally hadn't forgotten his true purpose and knew exactly why he was here. However, he simply hadn't seen the other party's presence earlier, which was why he had started experimenting with his powers.

Even after the other party spoke... he still couldn't see them.

"Ahem... May I ask where you are, Senior? This junior truly hasn't noticed your presence." Li Qi looked around sheepishly, but he still couldn't spot the deity.

"Look down, Young Master. This minor god is down here," the voice said again.

Li Qi looked down at his feet.

Nothing had changed. Wasn't he just standing on the ground...?

Wait, the ground was moving.

An earthquake spread outward from beneath his feet!

Every tremor turned solid matter into a liquid-like state, rippling through dirt and stone as easily as sound traveling through the air!

Countless mountains were violently birthed into existence. The earth itself was uprooted. Viewed from the sky, it looked as if infinite spikes were surging up from the ground to pierce the very heavens!

Shockwaves rolled in like an endless tide, threatening to collapse the very core of the earth!

The world was thrown into upheaval as a thousand mountains rose all at once!

From Li Qi's perspective, hundreds of thousands of cubic kilometers of land were being overturned!

An earthquake spanning a thousand miles!

Then, a white snake reared its head from the midst of the chaos, practically tearing the earth's crust asunder.

This white snake was thousands of miles long and dozens of miles wide. A single one of its scales was large enough to build a city upon. At a glance, it stretched on endlessly, occupying almost the entirety of Li Qi's personal cosmos!

Li Qi's heart trembled with awe and fear.

No wonder he couldn't see it.

With it being this massively gigantic, it would be a miracle if he could see it!

After raising its head briefly, the gargantuan white snake buried its body back into the earth.

That gentle voice spoke again, "Young Master's cultivation is still quite shallow. Your personal cosmos cannot yet accommodate the projection of my dharma body. Therefore, I had no choice but to bury myself below in order to come here and converse with you."

As for Li Qi, he was so utterly astounded that he began to stutter.

"T-then... then how did Senior descend in the past?"

Indeed, the target of Li Qi's altar and invitation was the mountain god of White Snake Mountain, an entity he had a prior connection with.

He just never expected that the true form of White Snake Mountain's mountain god would actually be a white snake—and such an incredibly colossal one at that!

"Ah, is Young Master referring to the first time you summoned me? Was the space you saw back then a chaotic blur?" the massive white snake asked.

"No, I recall it being a beautiful garden," Li Qi replied.

"That explains it. The place you visited back then was not your personal cosmos, but a space I expended my own power to construct. Young Master should understand now, shouldn't you? Why is it that when sacrificing to overly powerful deities, one might not necessarily receive a response? The reason is simply that their personal cosmos cannot withstand the burden." The white snake laughed.

"During that time, I willingly spent my energy to build a stable realm to descend into. The main reason was that your sacrifices gave this minor god quite a fright. If it were an unfamiliar deity, they likely wouldn't have gone through the trouble," the white snake explained peacefully.

Li Qi nodded. "So that's how it is."

"However, for someone who has merely just reached Rank 9, the fact that your personal cosmos can barely accommodate my will means your innate talent is already of the highest caliber. Even within Shaman God Mountain, I imagine few disciples could achieve this. You have every right to be proud. Truly worthy of being taken in as a disciple by the Grand Invocator. You are indeed a fledging phoenix; given time, you will surely soar the skies," the white snake praised.

"Ahem, there's no need for that, Senior. You flatter this junior far too much," Li Qi quickly demurred.

The tale of the phoenix and the owl[^1] was fine for self-deprecating jokes, but he couldn't possibly take such high praise seriously.

"Returning to the matter at hand," the white snake asked, "for what reason has Young Master summoned me this time? Is there a difficult problem you need me to resolve?"

"On the journey back to the mountain, I must naturally solve any problems I encounter on my own. How could I dare trouble Senior to intervene? Besides, my teacher probably wouldn't be pleased if I did," Li Qi hastily explained.

If he actually used his status as a Young Master to command these deities to solve his problems for him, his teacher would likely crush his head.

"Then what does Young Master require?" the mountain god of White Snake Mountain asked in confusion.

"It's like this: I would like to ask Senior, are you familiar with the mountain god of Green Mountain?" Li Qi inquired.

"Ah, I see. Young Master is here for information. The mountain god of Green Mountain... Jin Chi? I do know of him, and we share a passing acquaintance. Why do you ask? Have you offended Jin Chi?" the mountain god asked.

"Not at all. I am but a mere Rank 9; what qualifications do I have to offend a Rank 6 mighty figure? It's just that while passing through Clear Mist City, I accidentally offended his young master, Jin Buhuan, leading to a minor conflict. Since I don't know much about his background, I ventured to invite Senior here to ask a few questions."

"I understand. That boy from Jin Chi's family is indeed somewhat wildly arrogant. Since he has provoked Young Master, it is a good thing for him to be taught a lesson." The mountain god chuckled, seemingly grasping the situation.

However, Li Qi shook his head. "I dare not conceal this from Senior, but I am not preparing to simply teach him a lesson."

Upon hearing Li Qi's words, the mountain god paused in surprise.

"Young Master is...?" he asked hesitantly.

Did this Young Master truly possess such heavy killing intent?

"In order to force me to accept an apology gift, this person murdered dozens of people in cold blood. He intended to shackle me with their resentment, ruin my 'principles', and attack my Dao Heart," Li Qi explained. "He is cruel and unbenevolent, unjust in his dealings, unrighteous in his actions, and recklessly arrogant. Keeping him alive will only leave a scourge upon the land."

"Your words... carry a hint of the Human Dao about them. But considering Young Master hails from the Great Deer Kingdom..." The mountain god abruptly stopped mid-sentence. Instead of continuing, he quickly changed the subject.

Shifting his tone, he said, "Never mind that. Just ask whatever you need, Young Master. This minor god will naturally tell you everything I know. However, I beseech you not to mention my name in front of Jin Chi. He has a fearsome killing intent, whereas I am not skilled in combat. I was previously suppressed by those two demons, the curse devil and bear demon. If I were to face Jin Chi, defeat would be my only outcome..."

At this point, the mountain god's voice turned incredibly mournful. "Among Rank 6 entities, Jin Chi is likely among the most lethal and powerful. If this minor god were to face him, even twenty of me wouldn't be enough for him to beat..."

"Naturally," Li Qi immediately promised. "I will absolutely not reveal Senior's existence. Should he trouble you because of this, I will definitely remember it and intervene."

"Good. Then what does Young Master wish to know?"

"First, regarding Jin Buhuan's cultivation method..."

Li Qi began his line of questioning.

As he asked his questions, he once again discovered and confirmed a certain fact.

The mountain god of White Snake Mountain... was genuinely terrible at fighting.

Two hours later.

Li Qi returned from the out-of-body sensation brought on by the ritual.

When Shamans communicated with the gods of heaven and earth, they temporarily lost control over their physical bodies. To an outsider, they appeared dazed and delirious, as if they were performing some frenzied spirit dance.

Li Qi used to think this was utterly ridiculous, but now that he understood the mechanics behind it, he only felt helpless.

He really didn't enjoy performing spirit dances.

But there was nothing he could do about it. Perhaps in the future, when he could divide his attention, he would be able to maintain control over his body.

However, doing so would be disrespectful to the gods. Dividing one's attention always came across as perfunctory, and he certainly couldn't act like that during a formal invitation.

He opened his eyes and took a deep breath.

"Miss Shen, how goes the formation?" he turned and asked Shen Shuibi.

"Almost done. Don't rush me, don't rush me. I'm wrapping things up," Shen Shuibi replied absentmindedly, her head held high.

"Alright, I'll go take a look outside." Li Qi nodded. He stood up, pushed the door open, and walked out.

Outside, Lord Yan was still standing there, unmoving and unshaken.

And around him, there was not a single person left.

The crowds of onlookers had also vanished; everyone had evacuated the area.

Although it was still broad daylight, the entire street was completely deserted. Even the shops had closed their doors. Pedestrians took detours, absolutely terrified to step foot inside the street.

All the etiquette officials from the procession, and even the palanquin bearers and laborers who had previously stood beside Lord Yan, were entirely gone.

In their place lay a blanket of ash across the ground, with Lord Yan standing right in the center of it.

Between the palanquin bearers, the laborers, the etiquette officials, the masters of ceremonies, and various other attendants, the total number of people had easily numbered in the hundreds.

Now, all that remained was a pile of ash.

"Mr. Li, you've returned? Have you thought things through? Are you willing to accept my young master's apology gift?" Lord Yan asked politely. His attitude was quite respectful, and his tone extremely humble.

However, the words themselves caused Li Qi's face to darken, instantly shattering the facade of indifference he had put up.

Li Qi sighed. His ability to maintain his composure was still lacking.

"Lord Yan... was it?" Li Qi asked.

"Yes, Mr. Li may address me directly by name." Lord Yan appeared deeply respectful, though Li Qi could easily tell there was little actual reverence behind it.

However, whenever Lord Yan occasionally glanced toward the inn, his eyes held a profound amount of respect.

It was obvious that he didn't consider Li Qi worthy of his respect or attention, whereas he viewed Shen Shuibi as an admirable senior master.

This was perfectly normal. No matter how one looked at it, Li Qi was nothing more than an unremarkable Rank 9 cultivator. Shen Shuibi, on the other hand, acted more like his Dao Protector. She was the true expert.

Seeing Li Qi direct a question at him, Lord Yan tilted his head, a hint of helplessness flashing in his eyes.

Truth be told, Lord Yan didn't particularly care about this Rank 9 youth standing before him. Even if the young man had a high status and perhaps excellent innate talent, right now, aside from his background, there was nothing about him worthy of respect.

Yet, it was precisely because of this background that Lord Yan was forced to show deference to a young man who—aside from sheer luck—was inferior to him in every conceivable way, even having to address him as 'Mister'.

What did it mean to be a 'Mister'?

Those who are accomplished lead the way, and those who possess knowledge cultivate life; this was the meaning of 'Mister'. It was an honorific reserved exclusively for seniors who possessed profound talent, capability, and skill.

It was absolutely not meant for a junior like the one before him.

But simply because they had the good fortune to be born into a powerful family or to be taken in by a great master, they could ascend to the heavens in a single bound, becoming someone he was obligated to address with reverence.

Truly...

Unfair.

As Lord Yan thought this, he raised his hand. "What are your thoughts, Mr. Li?"

Li Qi vaguely thought he saw a cold gleam in the other party's eyes, but he couldn't be certain. Moreover, regardless of whether he was certain or not, it wouldn't change the undeniable fact that he couldn't beat this man in a fight.

The gap between Rank 8 and Rank 9 was simply too massive.

Therefore, Li Qi answered straightforwardly, "If I were to accept these things now, wouldn't all those people from before have died in vain?"

Lord Yan laughed. "It is only if Mr. Li refuses to accept that they will have died in vain. If you continue to refuse, my young master will fly into a rage, and I'm afraid even more people will have to die."

"Is people dying such a common occurrence?" Li Qi asked.

"Is it not common? Clear Mist City has a population of tens of millions. Hundreds die of natural old age every single day. Count in accidents and illnesses, and thousands of people dropping dead in a day is perfectly normal. The deaths of these hundred-odd people won't even cause a ripple," Lord Yan replied.

Then, he asked in confusion, "Did Mr. Li step outside just to say this?"

Li Qi shook his head and said, "Not exactly. I was just asking in passing. My main reason for coming out was because I wish to pay a visit to Young Master Jin Buhuan and speak with him face-to-face. Could Lord Yan be so kind as to introduce us?"

He then stated a request that left Lord Yan utterly shocked.
