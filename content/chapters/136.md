# Chapter 136: Sudden Incident

In this elegantly decorated inn, Li Qi asked with some curiosity, "Fight over it? What rank is this innate metal qi that it warrants the word 'fight'?"

He knew that Green Mountain did indeed nurture an innate divine weapon from time to time.

Innate meant naturally born, something that had not undergone any alteration.

As the saying goes: 'that which is endowed by destiny is innate; that which is achieved by human effort is acquired.'

Theoretically, most things in the world could be called 'innate.' After all, everything was born that way.

However, there were differences even among innate things. Just as some people were naturally smarter or stronger than others, some stones were naturally harder than the rest.

Only people or things born with such extraordinary traits were crowned with the title of an 'innate' existence.

Just like the current innate divine weapon. It was naturally nurtured by the concentrated metal qi of Green Mountain. Without any carving or forging, it naturally took the form of a weapon. Born with divine properties, it was a supreme armament for martial artists. Whether it was nourished over time or used as raw material for further forging, it would far surpass ordinary weapons.

Hearing this, the fox demon chuckled lightly at Li Qi. "When that metal qi was first born, the sky was brilliantly illuminated with five colors. Then, the light retracted before bursting forth once more, its crimson radiance piercing the heavens like the midday sun. A mighty figure from the Clear Mist Sect observed it and declared, 'The essence of metal can shine, while the impure cannot. This light shines without a trace of impurity.' Thus, it was determined to be an innate Rank 8."

_The essence of metal can shine, while the impure cannot._

Li Qi nodded upon hearing this assessment. That was an incredibly high evaluation.

"Shine" meant to emit a dazzling light, much like the bright light of day. The phrase meant that only the purest essence within the metal qi could radiate light, whereas impurities could not. Judging by the sheer purity of its radiance, this metal qi was undoubtedly flawless.

An innate Rank 8, perfectly pure metal qi that had naturally formed into a divine weapon—this was indeed exceedingly rare. Using the word "fight" to describe the ensuing scramble was certainly no exaggeration.

"Are you looking to inquire about the rumors surrounding this matter, Sir? Our establishment may be small, but street gossip always finds its way here. Why not order a bit more food, and I shall recount the details for you?" the fox demon asked elegantly. Her tone was gentle, and while she lacked obvious seductive intent, her words and mannerisms carried a faint, almost imperceptible charm.

Although she had reined it in significantly, Li Qi was a Rank 9 martial artist. With his keen senses, he could still detect it.

However, he didn't sense any deliberate provocation. It was merely a sliver of her natural allure leaking through despite her best efforts to suppress it.

Looking at the refined surroundings and the fox demon's conservative attire, she had likely chosen this modest lifestyle herself.

Ultimately, this had nothing to do with Li Qi. He shook his head and smiled. "My cultivation is too low to compete for such things. I'm afraid that if I hear too much, I won't be able to restrain myself."

The fox demon naturally understood his polite refusal and dropped the subject. Smiling, she finalized his lodging and meal orders, called for a waiter to lead Old Ma away, and went off to attend to other guests.

Li Qi and his companion sat in the center of the main hall, waiting to finish their meal before heading to their room.

Although they only had one room, they had spent the past year braving the wind and sleeping under the stars in all manner of rough lodgings. Sharing a single room was a frequent occurrence, and they had long since grown accustomed to it.

Taking his seat, Li Qi poured a cup of tea for himself and Shen Shuibi. He sighed with emotion. "Only after leaving the Great Deer Kingdom region did I realize that all the spirits of the world could actually coexist so harmoniously."

"Look at this Clear Mist City. Humans and demons mix freely, and spirits are everywhere. Yet no one finds it strange; they are completely accustomed to it. This is something you would never see in the Great Deer Kingdom, where demons are viewed as calamities."

Hearing Li Qi's words, Shen Shuibi covered her mouth and giggled. "We have, after all, entered the sphere of influence of Shaman God Mountain. Shamans do not care for racial divisions. I've heard that further west, beyond the Shaman Dao, lies the Buddhist Kingdom. Equality is taken even more seriously there. While the Shaman Dao still judges status based on strength, the Buddhist Kingdom supposedly reveres the equality of all living beings. Even the life of a livestock animal is considered equal to that of a cultivator."

"Does that mean the Great Deer Kingdom is the way it is because it's influenced by the Human Dao? Because the Human Dao dislikes demons?" Li Qi asked curiously.

"You could say that, but it's not entirely accurate. The Human Dao draws clear boundaries. Only if you identify yourself as a member of the Human Dao will you have a relatively stable path for advancement within their ranks without facing excessive discrimination. But if you do not belong to the Human Dao, then you are not of their kind, and your heart is guaranteed to be different," Shen Shuibi evaluated as she reminisced.

She was a victim of this very Human Dao ideology.

Once deemed an enemy, the Tang Emperor hadn't hesitated to order the eradication of Mount Luofu, showing absolutely no mercy.

"Identify yourself?" Li Qi was surprised by this description.

"Yes, just as I said before, the Human Dao isn't strictly bound by race; it only recognizes your self-identification. If you accept their ideology, even a demon is considered a comrade. If you reject it, even a human is treated as an enemy."

"Therefore, the Human Dao is extremely unified. They can easily gather the strength of the masses, allowing for rapid expansion," Shen Shuibi said with a sigh.

Seeing her demeanor, Li Qi immediately changed the subject. "Miss Shen, there are so many paths in the world. Besides the ones we frequently hear about, are there any other formidable Daoist traditions?"

"Formidable ones? Hmm... That's hard to say." Shen Shuibi pondered for a moment before replying, "As the saying goes, horses differ in coat yet share the same swiftness; jades vary in color yet are equally beautiful. Be it a chestnut stallion, a black courser, or a piebald steed, they can all run a thousand li in a single day. How can one possibly distinguish which is inherently better or worse?"

Li Qi nodded, agreeing with her reasoning.

Horses might have different coats, but they could all be fine steeds. Jades might have different hues, but they could all be exquisite gems. Regardless of their breed, those horses could all travel a thousand li a day. Therefore, no matter the Daoist tradition, each had its own unique merits.

"Thus, you must never grow complacent just because you hail from a major Daoist tradition. Oh, Li Qi, you've already seen through this point, so I won't nag," Shen Shuibi subconsciously reminded him as she spoke, only to catch herself. Realizing that Li Qi had long since ceased to be plagued by arrogance, she stopped talking.

While the two conversed, their food arrived. It was an array of exquisite dishes.

The first to be served was half a tender-pig. A tender-pig was a small pig weighing five or six catties, raised entirely on rice. Its bones were soft and brittle. Roasted whole, it emitted a mouth-watering peppery aroma. Sliced and eaten, its flavor was exceptionally delicious.

Next came bamboo shoots stir-fried with fatty pork. The fat rendered into a rich sweetness that infused the bamboo, transforming into a savory flavor of unparalleled freshness.

The main course was a bowl of Four Beauties Soup. It used terrestrial mushrooms and aquatic water shields to create the broth, blended with crab roe and fish ribs. Hence, the name "Four Beauties Soup."

Mushrooms were formed by the convergence of the qi from mountains, rivers, and vegetation. Consuming them was akin to absorbing the very essence of nature.

Water shields possessed a fragrant, crisp, yet smooth and tender texture. It slightly resembled fish marrow or crab fat, but was far superior in its light and refreshing quality.

Supplemented by the savory richness of crab roe and fish ribs, the taste was wondrously delectable.

The entire meal was incomparably fresh and savory, leaving Li Qi thoroughly satisfied. Though there were only two dishes and a soup, and the ingredients weren't particularly precious, the portions were generous and the taste was magnificent. As a result, he had no complaints about the two or three thousand copper coins the meal had cost.

After finishing their meal and returning to their room, Shen Shuibi sat down to meditate and cultivate. Li Qi, meanwhile, sat by the window to admire Clear Mist City's night scenery.

The lights twinkled like stars amidst the boundless mortal realm. Although the sky had darkened, the uniform glazed tiles covering the city reflected the glow of candlelight and luminous pearls.

Looking down at the city from this vantage point, a web-like network of roads spread out in all directions.

The city was supported by this dense, interwoven grid, resting in its center. Occasionally, people carried away by excitement galloped on horseback through the dazzling streets, singing and dancing in a lively, bustling display.

Only when night fell did Clear Mist City reveal its true form.

Beautiful scenery sustained by various spell arts filled the city. Li Qi even spotted merchants using mechanical puppets and illusions to attract customers, as well as numerous demons walking among the crowds.

They didn't seem worried about hiding their demonic traits, making it a common sight to see a plethora of animal ears, fur, and tails.

Yet, this was no fairyland. The demons possessed bizarre styles, and their human transformations weren't always aesthetically pleasing. Some hadn't fully shed their beastly forms, while others looked outright grotesque. Some hadn't even refined their hyoid bones yet, communicating through babbling noises and wild gesticulations.

And these were the ones that had actually developed spiritual intelligence. There were other exotic beasts that possessed no intelligence at all; they were pure animals. Even the demons didn't view them as kin, eating their meat and wearing their fur without the slightest hesitation.

To demons, spiritual intelligence was the sole metric for identifying their own kind. Only those with sentience were considered kin. Otherwise, even if it was the very beast that birthed them, they wouldn't care much for it.

For them, life began the moment their spiritual intelligence awakened, not at birth.

However, humans seemed to be much the same, the only difference being that humans were born with spiritual intelligence.

Speaking of which, Li Qi had read in books that humans weren't the only creatures born with sentience. The True Dragons of the Four Seas were also born with it. Many spirit beasts, including innate anomalies like Shen Shuibi, were naturally sentient from birth.

This was only considering All Under Heaven. If one looked out at the countless outer-realm worlds, the numbers were even greater.

There were many creatures born with spiritual intelligence, but there were far more born without it.

After comprehending this point, Li Qi entirely lost any attachment to the word "special."

In this world, no person was special, and no thing was special.

He was no exception.

There was nothing new under the sun, and there was no such thing as a truly unique person.

He watched the scenery outside while pondering these thoughts.

Thinking had now become a habit for Li Qi. During almost every moment of free time, he would ponder, read, and seek out new knowledge.

By now, Li Qi had already finished reading _[Book of Invocation]_.

Upon completion, his progress bar had directly granted him the knowledge of the entire book. It was as if he had memorized the text word for word, the memories incredibly profound.

This was the power of dao rhythm.

Just as Li Qi was immersed in his own world, he and Shen Shuibi suddenly snapped back to reality at the exact same moment. They both looked toward the door.

"Death qi. Did someone die?" Li Qi asked.

"Not just that, there's also death intent. It means they aren't dead yet, but they're fading fast," Shen Shuibi said with a frown.

The two immediately stood up and headed out the door to see what was happening.

Stepping out, they saw people emerging from other rooms as well. It seemed everyone had sensed the death qi.

Looking toward the source of the death intent, they saw a fox demon lying on the ground below. She was breathing in less than she was breathing out, looking as though she was at death's door.

Beside her, a frantic man was desperately clutching her wound, constantly screaming for his wife.

To Li Qi's surprise, absolutely no one stepped forward to help.

"This is strange. I didn't sense the killer. This wound... it's a curse art from the Shaman Dao, but I don't know if it's the work of a Diviner," Li Qi noted, his brows furrowing.

This was, after all, the territory of Shaman God Mountain. Although practitioners of the orthodox Shaman Dao were rare, there were still many who practiced obscure, branch lineages of the Shaman Dao. It was only normal that some among them excelled at curses.

"What should we do? It seems no one is willing to save her," Shen Shuibi said, glancing around.

"We can't just watch her die. Let's go take a look." Li Qi made his decision.

Regardless of what this proprietress had done, even if she had secretly committed unforgivable crimes, they could deal with that after saving her. They couldn't just leave her here to bleed out.

Decision made, the two immediately vaulted over the railing and dropped straight down to the lobby below.

At this moment, the panicked man was clutching the fox demon's stomach, his voice trembling as he yelled, "Wife! Hold on! I've sent Qi Ya to find a doctor! Just hold on!"

"Move aside, let me do it." Li Qi stepped forward and pulled the man away.

The man was stunned, but quickly realized that someone had stepped in to help.

Li Qi took one look at the wound and sensed the surrounding qi.

"It's definitely a curse. These are the methods of a Ghost Shaman," Li Qi confirmed after examining the injury.

"There is five elements qi lingering at the wound. This is the Five Ghosts curse art. Someone commanded five malicious ghosts, aligning them with metal, wood, water, fire, and earth. They laid down five types of restraining talismans to curse her first. Afterward, they dispatched the ghost corresponding to her weakness to hunt down the living victim's ethereal soul. That's why it erupted so suddenly," Li Qi explained.

"I will deploy my divine qi to drive out the ghost qi. But when it comes to injuries, you are far more skilled, Miss Shen. Please handle the treatment," Li Qi said.

Shen Shuibi nodded. "Alright."

With that, the two divided the work and cooperated.

Li Qi began setting up protective incantations. Aside from the divine qi of the four seasons within his body, he also inherently possessed the divine qi of the five household gods.

What were the five household gods? They governed the outer gate, the inner door, the well, the stove, and the central hall.

The inner door was sacrificed to in spring, the stove in summer, the outer gate in autumn, the well in winter, and the central hall in the sixth month. This secretly corresponded to the five elements.

Therefore, at the very beginning of his cultivation, Li Qi had offered sacrifices to the inner door gods, Shentu and Yulei. Their divine qi could exorcise ghosts. Because they resided on a giant peach tree, they were affiliated with wood. This was precisely why sacrifices were made to the inner door god during spring.

The stove represented fire, the outer gate metal, the well water, and the central hall symbolized earth at the center.

Li Qi deployed the divine qi of the five household gods, using their power to neutralize the ghost qi of the Five Ghosts and prevent the assailant from causing further harm.

On her end, Shen Shuibi examined the wound and couldn't help but marvel. "Wow, it really is the malicious ghosts of the five elements. The five evils have invaded her body. The external wound is just a tiny cut, but the five evils resonate within, inflicting damage both inside and out. That causes her qi to reverse and flow upward, which is why she's coughing up blood. How utterly ruthless. The qi of the five evils won't vanish when she dies; it will continue to pollute her divine soul. They are trying to tear her up by the roots and obliterate her divine soul entirely."
