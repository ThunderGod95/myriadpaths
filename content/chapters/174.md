# Chapter 174: Aftermath

In truth, just as Rank 9 martial artists were called 'Innate Martial Artists', the internal names for cultivation realms varied greatly among different cultivation methods, sects, and Daoist traditions.

For instance, within this traditional Divine Dao, Li Qi knew their cultivation realms were divided like this:

The unranked period began with the Heart Seed realm, which involved condensing a heart seed to sense believers.

Next was the Incense Fire realm, where one could formally accept worship and incense offerings.

After slowly accumulating incense fire to sculpt a golden body, the completion of the golden body marked formal entry into Rank 9, a stage they called the 'Golden Body realm'.

Following this were the Personal Descent realm, the Mountain and Water realm, the Unity realm, and the Divine Soul realm.

Only upon reaching the Divine Soul realm was one considered to have advanced to Rank 8.

Li Qi's Dao path was actually similar. Each time he finished condensing a specific type of internal deity, his cultivation base advanced a realm. Texts briefly outlined these as the Five Great Mountains stage, the Four Great Rivers stage, the Sun and Moon stage, and the Primal Spirit stage. The order of these four realms was not fixed; one could advance into any of them first, but all had to be completed.

The Martial Dao also had five realms: Tempering Skin, Tempering Bone, Tempering Sinew, Tempering Muscle, and Tempering Viscera. Completing all of these made one an Innate martial artist, which was Rank 9. Following that were three realms: Tempering Intent, Tempering Heart, and Tempering Spirit. Then, one would temper their Martial Dao divine will and advance to Rank 8, earning the description 'possessing both spirit and will, body and mind as one'.

Under All Under Heaven, there were countless Dao paths and dharmic methods, leading to an unknown number of distinct realm classifications. Trying to unify and convert them all individually would simply cost someone their life.

Therefore, a mighty figure had unified the standards of all Dao paths, dividing them into Rank 1 through Rank 9. This act was known as 'Standardizing All Under Heaven'.

One could imagine how difficult this undertaking was.

To create a convincing standard, the very first step required understanding the realm divisions of nearly every Dao path, knowing the reasoning behind those divisions, and then finding the commonalities across different realms for every path under All Under Heaven.

Only with commonalities could there be a premise for unified divisions. No one knew how much time the mighty figure who accomplished this had spent completely analyzing the Dao paths of All Under Heaven to find the common ground between different realms and paths.

However, since then, All Under Heaven possessed the nine-rank classification system.

Within your own Daoist Tradition, you could call them whatever you pleased. But when venturing outside and encountering those with different realm divisions, having a unified universal standard made communication possible.

Yet clearly, for some bottom-tier cultivators, the nine-rank classification system alone was an unknown secret.

What was common sense to Li Qi was an unreachable secret to these people.

The disparity in the world was just this massive.

After communicating with this water god, the other party fearfully memorized Shen Shuibi's aura. She then bowed to Li Qi, took her compensation, and dove into the water to send the message using a mountain-and-water transmission art.

Li Qi put away the severed finger, carefully storing it safely.

This item could be considered the most valuable thing on Li Qi's entire body. The physical body of a Rank 5 existence was the kind of thing no one would believe if spoken of, and it also held deep significance for Li Qi.

Every time he looked at this finger, he was reminded of that day, of the despair and powerlessness he felt when facing the Curse Devil King and the Bear Slaughter King.

Having finished this task, Li Qi vaulted back onto his horse and said to Old Ma, "Let's go, we're moving out again."

Old Ma let out a snort, accelerated once more, and rushed toward the Vast Yang City Prefect's manor.

---

Several hours later.

Dawn broke.

Li Qi squatted on a field ridge in the city outskirts, watching the morning sun rise. His brows were tightly knitted, his expression grave.

Old Ma grazed on tender leaves nearby, occasionally turning to glance at Li Qi. Seeing that he was still squatting there like a wooden stump, it continued to raise its head to munch on the newly sprouted leaves of a tree.

As for why Li Qi had ended up in this state...

It had to start with what happened last night.

Last night, Li Qi went to visit the Vast Yang Prefect.

He learned that the Vast Yang Prefect had returned to the royal capital to report on his duties.

He then changed his route to find the Minister of State, who was responsible for tax collection and organizing production.

The Minister of State was indeed present. However, he didn't even speak with Li Qi, directly driving him out.

Afterward, he had a porter pass along a message, informing Li Qi that the leading general of the White Rhino Army had defied orders on the battlefield and disrespected military commands. He had already been dismissed from his post and was currently in prison awaiting trial. Li Qi was told to return and pass on the order: immediately withdraw the troops and return to Vast Yang to garrison. Vast Yang City naturally had military rations to supply them.

Immediately after, treating Li Qi as a messenger of the White Rhino Army, he issued a new military order for the army: abandon the commoners and immediately rush back to Vast Yang City.

The military rations of the White Rhino Army, if solely consumed by the soldiers themselves, were absolutely enough to make it back to Vast Yang City.

Denied an audience once more, Li Qi had no choice but to retreat. He went outside the city and squatted from the night until now. Even as the sun rose, he hadn't changed his posture.

This continued until a farmer came out to weed and loosen the soil, only to see Old Ma chewing on the tender leaves of his jujube tree.

"That person over there! What are you doing!?" The old farmer charged forward to drive Old Ma away.

Old Ma whinnied twice, prompting Li Qi to hastily stand up. "My apologies, uncle. I didn't keep a close eye on my horse. Please accept this as compensation!"

Saying this, Li Qi quickly handed over a few copper coins, seizing the initiative to suppress the other party's momentum.

Following a barrage of apologies, he finally led Old Ma away.

Old Ma, on the other hand, wore an unconvinced expression.

It had only eaten the tender leaves and spared the flower buds. That was already giving enough face!

Li Qi could only smile bitterly, then let out a long sigh.

Having pondered all night, he had more or less figured out the situation in Vast Yang City.

Vast Yang City... no, it might even be Vast Yang Prefecture of the Hundred Forests Kingdom, had ordered the abandonment of all commoners near the border pass.

Therefore, when he first encountered that army led by a general, they hadn't brought any commoners with them because they had abandoned everyone!

The White Rhino Army, however, had protected a portion of the commoners. Clearly, for this sake, their leading general had personally journeyed to Vast Yang City to seek out his superiors, hoping for permission to bring the commoners along.

Yet, this action was not approved. Instead, he was directly stripped of his position and thrown into prison.

After that, it was the events Li Qi had encountered himself.

He had deduced the general sequence of events, but Li Qi didn't understand. What was the purpose of doing this?

Could Vast Yang City truly not support those hundred-odd thousand commoners? Impossible. Vast Yang City absolutely had enough grain. As for land, they could just have them reclaim wastelands.

Developed farmland might be scarce, but wasn't there plenty of wasteland?

Since they could afford to support them, why not accept them?

Moreover, judging from the military armaments of Vast Yang City, they did not lack strength. Holding the border pass was entirely viable for them. Why abandon it? On what grounds?

Ultimately, for what purpose would Vast Yang State choose to abandon the border pass and its commoners? Even when a general wanted to rescue and protect the civilians, he was directly dismissed and jailed.

This indicated that they had zero tolerance for the act of aiding the commoners.

No matter how much he thought about it, Li Qi couldn't understand. Why would Vast Yang State act like this?

There was no reason.

This was the point Li Qi found most baffling. However, none of that mattered now. Since official channels were a dead end, Li Qi had to first find a way to solve the temporary food crisis, and then address the long-term survival problem.

He could also choose not to solve it. Mirroring the attitude of the Vast Yang City government, he could just abandon those hundred thousand people.

However, obviously, Li Qi couldn't do that.

He didn't necessarily have to risk his life for this, but he had to at least try. What was wrong with making an attempt?

Thus, leading Old Ma, Li Qi walked back into Vast Yang City. This time, he didn't head anywhere near a government office, but instead made for a large restaurant.

He didn't eat here; he merely stood outside the restaurant, observing the deliverymen.

It was currently early morning. Having spent the night outside the city, the sun had only just risen, and thin mist lingered on the horizon. It was the time of the rooster's crow. The city had not yet come alive, but some were already beginning their labor.

The first batch to start working were those delivering goods.

Every day, fresh fruits, vegetables, and meats were delivered to major restaurants, pleasure boats, and large estates to supply the day's business.

Li Qi planned to see where these deliverymen came from, to locate Vast Yang City's distribution center for grain and vegetables.

This wasn't difficult. The grain distribution center itself was public, and nearby residents would even go in to buy groceries.

By following these delivering grain merchants, Li Qi quickly found the place.

Vast Yang City's grain distribution center.

Over six million tonnes of grain was gathered here. The daily turnover volume of this place alone was enough to cover all the grain Li Qi needed.

Of course, that was predicated on the premise that the entirety of Vast Yang City starved for a day.

However, with proper management, he could meet the demand without affecting anyone.

Li Qi squatted by the roadside, watching grain constantly being transported in and out of the distribution center.

Finally, he found a grain convoy that was sufficiently large.

Being sufficiently large was very important.

Then, he began trailing behind this convoy, wanting to see where their grain originated.

According to the customs of Baiyue that Li Qi was familiar with, the forces backing these grain transport convoys were gangs running this business, or rather, interest groups.

These groups cohered around a cultivation method as their core, then submitted to a higher-tier group. They stacked up layer by layer, until reaching the realm of true cultivators, where they finally dispersed to form an entirely different social structure.

Therefore, as long as he could trace them upwards layer by layer, he should be able to find the ultimate source of Vast Yang City's grain.

Once he found that, he would figure out a way to negotiate with the other party to resolve the food issue.

This was Li Qi's thought process, which was why he was currently following the grain convoy.

However, as they walked, they left the city, turned past a few rivers, and bypassed a few mountains. Suddenly, the convoy stopped.

A burly man stepped down from within. He walked out to the rear of the convoy, cupped his hands, and said, "May I ask which hero graces us? Why have you been following us? Have you lost your livelihood, or do you harbor ill intentions?"

"If you've lost your livelihood, we are all drifting in the Jianghu, and it isn't easy for anyone. Here are ten gold pieces as a toll, left for you brothers to buy wine. Let us remain at peace and go our separate ways."

"If you harbor ill intentions, my Golden Wave Sect is not one to be trifled with! You are more than welcome to try!"

As he spoke, he pulled out a pouch containing gold with one hand. With the other, he fiercely drew the scimitar at his waist and swung out a blade of wind, cleanly cleaving a large boulder by the roadside in half. The power was not insignificant.

Li Qi was somewhat surprised. He hadn't expected to be discovered.

Even though he was casually trailing behind, his personal cosmos was actually harmonizing with the external macrocosm. It gave off a faint feeling of blending in with all natural things, severely weakening his personal aura. While not quite invisibility, as long as he hid his figure well, it would be extremely difficult for anyone to detect him by sensing his aura.

At the very least, Li Qi felt this group wasn't capable of it.

But surprisingly, he had been found out.

Li Qi frowned and stepped out. "Gentlemen, I mean no harm."

Seeing Li Qi voluntarily step out, and entirely alone, the burly middle-aged man clearly breathed a sigh of relief. He cupped his hands and said, "Hero, you have been following us for at least half an hour. Such claims are likely hard to believe."

"My Golden Wave Sect runs a legitimate business. Though we don't have many ties with friends on the path, we have shared a cup of wine with the Black Mountain Wasps. We know the rules. There's no need for the hero to hide."

Li Qi wore an awkward expression and coughed dryly. "Ahem, I truly am not a highwayman. If you don't believe me, I will prove it to everyone."

With that said, Li Qi extended his hand and lightly pointed forward.

_Whoosh_. In the blink of an eye, two golden threads encircled the entire convoy several times.

In just one breath of time, the golden threads advanced a full five miles, flying two or three laps around the woods and rocks near the convoy.

The golden threads condensed in the air without dispersing, gleaming brightly, extraordinarily dazzling.

Then, the source of the golden threads flew back into Li Qi's body.

Before the others could react, another breath of time passed, and the golden threads slowly faded, dissipating without a trace.

_Rumble, crack._ Sounds rang out as the surrounding woods fell in swaths. Rocks shattered all over the hills, and even a blade scar was left on the cliff face above.

It was as if someone had drawn a ring nearby, and then this ring had expanded outward.

Everything along this ring was cleanly sliced in half, the cuts smooth as a mirror.

Trees toppled, boulders split. Pandemonium erupted all around as a myriad of small animals scattered, fleeing deeper into the forest. Startled birds burst from the canopy, snakes, insects, mice, and ants panicked, and the hearts and gallbladders of all sorts of small beasts were torn with terror.

Of course, it wasn't just small beasts.

It was the same for humans.

The man who had previously been speaking 'Jianghu slang' with such a proper air fell to his knees with a heavy thud.

Yet, his expression unchanging, he immediately shouted loudly with abundant vitality, "Senior's benevolence is great! This lowly one is in awe! If you have commands, we dare not disobey! It is our honor to work for Senior!"

Li Qi felt so awkward his toes curled.

He wasn't yet accustomed to using his strength to arrogantly order people around.

So he walked out. "There is no need for this. I trailed behind earlier simply because I didn't wish to reveal myself. May I ask how everyone discovered me? I ought to have completely concealed my aura."

The burly man spoke with reverence. "Senior might not know, but my Golden Wave Sect's cultivation method allows us to observe the source of a person's vitality. Though Senior did not leak any aura, your vitality is intense, like the blazing, brilliant sun, difficult to look at directly. It is like the light of a bright moon in the inky night sky; it truly cannot be hidden!"

Li Qi felt his heart twitch.
