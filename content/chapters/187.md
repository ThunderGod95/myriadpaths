# Chapter 187: Beyond the Boundary

Hearing this, Shen Shuibi immediately raised her hands, and the surrounding Yin and Yang lines rose with them.

Li Qi, however, grabbed her hand and shook his head, signaling her to stop.

The rabbit didn't understand why, but since Li Qi did this, she immediately lowered them. She only maintained a slight defense and no longer tried to actively attack.

Li Qi didn't know if he forgot or what, but anyway, he didn't let go, just holding on as he spoke. He continued to address the canopy-covered sky, "Great Master Chanzhi, the Gu Shaman Dao is but a minor path. Among the numerous branches of the Shaman Dao, it belongs to the kind that cannot be brought to the table. Its Dao path is sinister, twisting the meaning of the word 'sacrifice' and violating nature. If you use the Gu Shaman Dao as your reference, I'm afraid demonic thoughts will be deeply planted, and you will never be able to extricate yourself."

"If you truly want to learn the Shaman Dao's method of balance, you might as well come to me for help. Because I am the orthodox shaman." Sitting on Old Ma, Li Qi unfurled his personal cosmos.

The weather and seasons in the surrounding area gradually shifted toward Li Qi's location. The natural aura was gradually changing. Although still immature, it faintly exuded the oppressive feeling of 'I am Heaven and Earth.'

He had used this move against Jin Buhuan as well. By adjusting the rhythm between the external world and himself, his personal cosmos gradually diffused, forming an area akin to a domain around him.

In this area, the favorable climate and geographical position naturally tilted toward the shaman because it was inherently a part of his personal cosmos.

It sounded simple, but the Gu Shaman Dao did not possess such methods.

The focus of the Gu Shaman Dao lay in 'cultivation': cultivating Gu insects, cultivating demon retainers, and even cultivating parts of one's own body. To a shaman practicing Gu Shaman arts, a sacrifice was not a means of communication, but a means of cultivation.

This included a series of blood sacrifice methods, all of which were solely for extracting external resources to cultivate what they desired.

Sacrifice was no longer communication, but plunder.

This was the difference between Li Qi's sacrifices and the blood sacrifices of the Gu Shaman Dao.

Li Qi was certain that if his deduction was correct, then as long as this Great Master Chanzhi continued to dabble in the Gu Shaman Dao, his fall into the Devil Dao was an absolute certainty.

And what he was displaying right now was exactly what Chanzhi truly desired.

Therefore, after gradually altering the surrounding climate to adapt to himself, Li Qi grasped Shen Shuibi's hand and said in a low voice, "Don't come looking for me later. Go to Vast Yang City, find the Golden Wave Sect, and say that Li Qi is looking for Wang Boyan. Tell that person named Wang Boyan what happened today. Also, leave the matters of the White Rhino Army to Zhang Lan. You don't need to get involved. Zhang Lan and Wang Boyan will handle it later."

"Don't come looking for you? Where are you going?" Shen Shuibi immediately asked back.

Out here, without Li Qi to help her block so many things, she would have to proactively interact with people, which made her look extremely uneasy.

Even when facing a Rank 7 powerhouse, she felt almost nothing, but the thought of Li Qi leaving and having to take the initiative to go out and discuss matters with people made Shen Shuibi incredibly, incredibly uneasy.

But there was no other choice.

Because, before Li Qi could even speak, a vortex abruptly appeared in the sky.

Seeing this, Li Qi smiled. It was just as he expected.

Having witnessed the orthodox Shaman Dao, the other party, as a Rank 7, could easily recognize that this was exactly what he wished to pursue—the method to help him balance the conflict between Buddha and Devil.

This was the method Li Qi had come up with, the method to stop this blood sacrifice.

If they really fought a Rank 7, only a fool would choose to do so. Even Shen Shuibi couldn't win, and everyone present would be buried here.

It was better to risk himself and get everyone else out first.

As for Li Qi himself...

It was a certainty; he would absolutely be snatched away by Chanzhi.

Li Qi firmly believed that in Chanzhi's eyes, all of this was nowhere near as important as the method to resolve his Buddha-Devil conflict. He would definitely go to any lengths to achieve this.

Therefore, he arranged the tasks for Shen Shuibi. That was the backup plan regarding Chanzhi's matter.

Hopefully, Shen Shuibi wouldn't cause any mishaps.

When the vortex appeared, Shen Shuibi immediately tried to resist.

She shook off Li Qi's hand. Obviously, no matter what, she didn't want Li Qi to be taken away.

However, just as she made a move...

A giant tree suddenly appeared in their surroundings.

A towering Bodhi tree materialized here, incredibly resplendent. One could see its magnificent flowers and fruits, its radiant glow illuminating everything. There were also various Mani jewels in red, green, blue, and white; clustered gem chains, and adornments on various jeweled pillars.

Golden pearls and bells hung among the branches, with a precious, wondrous jewel net draped over it. Hundreds, thousands, millions of colors reflected off one another. Boundless, brilliant light shone limitlessly. All kinds of solemn adornments manifested accordingly.

A gentle breeze rustled the Bodhi tree, blowing through its branches and leaves, playing the sounds of countless wondrous Dharmas. The sound spread out, filling the surroundings, clear, melodious, subtle, and elegant.

On the branches of the Bodhi tree sat countless birds.

There were ducks, wild geese, mynas, parrots, mandarin ducks, peacocks, and various other birds.

There were Gandharva birds, Kalavinka birds, Jivanjiva birds, and so on.

The birds gathered like clouds, holding various flowers and fruits in their beaks.

However, these birds dropped the flowers and fruits in their beaks one after another, flying toward Shen Shuibi's grand array instead.

Clearly, those Yin and Yang lines were merely manifestations of energy without physical bodies, yet they were carried away by these birds!

In an instant, the array was no longer an array, the dharma was no longer dharma, the art was no longer an art—it lost all its power!

Shen Shuibi frowned and increased her speed. Her hands formed incantation seals, and her slender fingers twitched. The remaining Yin and Yang lines around her rapidly combined.

Yin and Yang lines were inherently the basic symbols that made up hexagram talismans. They were the most fundamental talismanic scripts of the Daoist Sects used to form the Bagua and Yin-Yang. _(Note: Yin and Yang lines refer to the long and short horizontal lines in the circle surrounding the Bagua diagram, which form different hexagram images symbolizing different situations.)_

At this moment, these Yin and Yang lines flashed continuously, manipulating Kan[^1] to fill Li[^2], converging on Shen Shuibi's body.

The dual qi of Yin and Yang creation merged into one, mixing the essence qi of the liver, gallbladder, heart, and kidneys. By operating the mechanism of the five elements generating and overcoming each other, the Samadhi True Fire within the gallbladder, corresponding to Bing[^3] and Ding[^4], could be unleashed.

However, before it could burst forth, Li Qi took the initiative to stand up. He left Old Ma, stepping out of the protection of the Yin and Yang lines.

Almost instantaneously, he was sucked away by the vortex.

The heavenly canopy enveloping the surroundings, as well as that Bodhi tree, vanished like an illusion.

Shen Shuibi's attack had already taken form, but Li Qi and the heavenly canopy had disappeared.

As if venting her anger, she pushed the gathered Samadhi True Fire forward. The mighty qi in her hands was fierce, purging ten thousand devils without the slightest hint of gentleness. The majestic wave of fire crashed out like a tsunami!

The mountains and rivers split open. A small mountain range was forcibly snapped in half by her from the middle!

Magma overflowed, and waves of fire surged to the sky.

Floating in the air, she looked up at the sky.

The rabbit was angry.

Although she didn't know who had taken Li Qi, she was very angry.

At this time, a figure rushed over rapidly from not far away.

It was Zhang Lan, who hadn't fled.

Although Zhang Lan had been blocked by the heavenly canopy earlier, he hadn't run away. Now that the canopy had vanished, he immediately rushed over.

"Miss Shen? Where is Brother Li?" Zhang Lan asked.

Shen Shuibi's previously blank expression changed into a serious, frowning look.

Originally, she didn't want to pay any attention to Zhang Lan.

But then she thought of the words Li Qi said before he left.

The rabbit rubbed her face very irritably, then said, "Li Qi said the matters of the White Rhino Army and these commoners are left to you. I'm going to look for someone. Goodbye!"

After saying that, Shen Shuibi seemed to flee, riding a rainbow alone and flying toward the direction of Vast Yang City.

First, go find that... Wang, Wang...

What was his name again?

Oh no, she didn't remember.

As for Zhang Lan, who was left behind by Shen Shuibi, he glanced at the commoners below and the White Rhino Army, whose military qi had almost completely dissipated.

This knight-errant also sighed.

Brother Li was daring enough to sacrifice himself to lure away that Rank 7, so this remaining mess could only be left to him.

The White Rhino Army's morale was scattered, and those commoners were even more panicked and at a loss for what to do.

"Fuck... me!" he cursed.

It was unclear whether he was cursing Li Qi, the suddenly appearing Rank 7, or cursing himself.

A Rank 7...

The upper echelons of the Righteous Sword Palace were only at Rank 7. Even in the Hundred Forests Kingdom, a Rank 7 could be considered a high-ranking regional official, a supreme ruler over ten thousand miles.

How many figures like this could there be?

A Rank 7 could even be the chief commander of their swordmasters, the chief wielder of the complete set of three hundred and sixty Heavenly Cycle Starry Swords.

However, just as Zhang Lan was lamenting, Old Ma suddenly rushed over.

He looked at Old Ma in confusion. "This is Brother Li's dragon colt? Why?"

Old Ma suddenly turned its body, revealing a letter placed on its back.

Zhang Lan picked it up and skimmed through it roughly. Then, his expression grew solemn.

"Follow me for now. After we settle these commoners, you and I will make a trip to the Righteous Sword Palace!"

---

Setting aside the events happening back there, regardless of what had transpired, the division of labor had already been arranged.

As for Li Qi, after passing through that vortex...

He suddenly felt a bizarre sensation.

His vision was completely pitch-black. He couldn't see anything, but it was precisely this darkness that conveyed a feeling of the grotesque, the maddening, and the loss of reason.

It was as if the surrounding area was a world that had already fallen into despair.

Faint murmurs seemed to echo by his ears, while something peculiar began to gradually rise, and his five senses progressively blurred.

His consciousness quietly sank into the darkness. The surrounding cold and deep dark gold color seemed to become warm, just like turning off the light and closing your eyes while lying in bed before going to sleep.

It was that kind of dark yet reassuring state.

It carried a sweet, fragrant scent that coaxed one into slumber.

Li Qi was almost about to fall asleep.

But in the next second, two golden lines lit up beside him. The golden lines swept through the darkness, and Li Qi, bracing himself with his last shred of lucidity, reacted.

But it had no effect.

The Rank 8 metal qi seemed completely unable to shatter these things.

However—

Countless progress bars flashed past his eyes, as if he were passing by countless events.

Just at this moment, Li Qi suddenly discovered that those progress bars—which he had long grown accustomed to and could already ignore—actually pierced through the darkness. These progress bars marked all the bizarre entities around him, placing them all right before his eyes.

The darkness was driven back by the progress bars, replaced by...

The starry sky.

A beautiful starry sky.

Not the kind of starry sky with little dots, showing a few bright specks on a backdrop.

Looking up at the starry sky from the ground, the sight was just like sesame seeds sprinkled on a black cloth.

Such stars seemed far too boring compared to this moment.

Now, the dao rhythm on Li Qi's body drove away the darkness for him, revealing an even more astonishing truth.

What appeared before Li Qi's eyes at this moment was an endless river of stars, a true spectacle visible to the naked eye!

The vast expanse of space was filled with boundless radiance. Hundreds of billions of burning spherical stars composed of superheated plasma!

But that wasn't all!

He saw a Great Peng with a wingspan the size of a star. On its back, it carried a magnificent cluster of palaces, seemingly large enough to hold several planets.

Who knows how far that Great Peng was from Li Qi; the light it emitted was only the size of a fist by the time it reached him, but the star beneath it could not be faked.

A translucent titan amidst the starry sky was leaping through the boundless river of stars. Every step he took crossed several galaxies, and before long, he disappeared from Li Qi's sight.

Logically, his speed should far exceed light, far exceeding Li Qi's reaction speed. Yet, Li Qi was somehow able to see the other party's movements. It seemed that what he was seeing wasn't light, but rather some more profound information.

There was also a terrifying giant crocodile swimming through the cosmic ocean, opening its massive maw to devour the galaxy. Dozens of stars were sucked into its belly in a single gulp, stirring the sun and moon as if startled, and sending the waves of the galaxy crashing over like a capsize!

And even more—upon rivers formed of indescribable energy, countless miniature worlds floated like small boats on an ocean. Myriad minor worlds bobbed up and down on this rainbow-like sea.

There were even larger worlds that surpassed that rainbow ocean. They almost occupied all of Li Qi's field of vision and came in bizarre shapes.

Some were spherical, just like planets, but they weren't planets. Instead, they were covered by a layer of crystalline membrane, concealing a grotto-heaven within.

Others were floating continents suspended in the void, drifting through the universe according to some specific pattern.

Still others were hemispherical, embodying the concept of a round heaven and a flat earth, with a canopy covering the land beneath.

Even, when Li Qi looked up, he saw an even more majestic, unbelievable existence.

A giant dragon composed entirely of stars. On its body, the stars were living things; galaxies were the writhing muscles on its claws, and planets were its cells.

In the eyes of the living beings of endless foreign worlds, his existence was merely a speck of starlight embedded in the celestial dome.

This giant dragon merely appeared before Li Qi for an instant before vanishing without a trace, and this was nothing more than a fleeting glimpse for Li Qi.

But he still recognized what it was.

The Azure Dragon! This was one of the Four Symbols, the Azure Dragon!

Within this boundless expanse, there were hundreds of billions of worlds. And beyond those hundreds of billions of worlds, there were hundreds of billions more!

Countless sceneries gathered together, transcending common sense!

Its beauty couldn't even be described. It was like a crystal-clear snowflake—though beautiful, if touched by a hand, that pure beauty would melt. Once it fell to the ground, that beautiful purity would become muddied. It could only truly be perceived in the sky.

Even to the point where, the moment Li Qi thought "I want to record this," weighing his words and trying to summarize that beauty with sentences, he could feel that the beauty lost its purity and became unbearably vulgar.

Li Qi knew.

He knew where he, having passed through that vortex, had arrived.

Beyond the Boundary!

This place was Beyond the Boundary!

[^1]: One of the eight trigrams (Bagua) of the I Ching. It represents the Water element. In Daoist internal alchemy and traditional Chinese medicine, Kan corresponds to the kidneys and the lower dantian, where the body's foundational essence (Jing) is stored. Visually, its hexagram (☵) has a solid Yang line trapped between two broken Yin lines.
[^2]: Another of the eight trigrams, representing the Fire element. It corresponds to the heart, the mind, and the spirit (Shen). Visually, its hexagram (☲) has a broken Yin line trapped between two solid Yang lines.

    **"Manipulating Kan to fill Li" (抽坎填离):** This is a core concept in Daoist Internal Alchemy (Neidan). It literally means "extracting Kan to fill Li." A cultivator extracts the hidden, true Yang energy from the Water of the kidneys (Kan) and drives it upward to replace the Yin weakness in the Fire of the heart (Li). This perfectly unites Water and Fire, reverting the body's energy back to a state of pure, primal perfection. In this scene, Shen Shuibi is forcibly aligning her internal organs to generate a terrifying amount of power.
[^3]: The third of the ten Heavenly Stems (a traditional Chinese system of ordinal reckoning and astrology). In the Five Elements theory, Bing corresponds to Yang Fire—fierce, explosive, active fire, much like the sun or a roaring inferno.
[^4]: The fourth of the ten Heavenly Stems. It corresponds to Yin Fire—subtle, internal, refined fire, like the flame of a candle, a forge, or the body's internal heat.
    
    **How they relate to the Samadhi True Fire?**
    
    The Samadhi True Fire is a legendary, inextinguishable flame, most famously weiled by the Red Boy in _Journey to the West_. Unlike normal fire, it cannot be summoned from the outisde; it must be ignited from within using the cultivator's own essence, qi, and spirit. Because **Bing** and **Ding** absolutely govern the concept of Fire, a cultivator must align the Yin (Ding) and Yang (Bing) fire element hidden within their own organs (specially the heart, liver, and gallbladder) to strike the "spark".
