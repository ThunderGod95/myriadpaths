# Chapter 125: Secret Plotting

Meanwhile, on Li Qi's side.

Li Qi had already set off. Taking Shen Shuibi and Bai Di with him, he headed out to meet his fellow disciple according to the details in the calling card.

In the car.

The Earth Oil World had no airplanes.

"Young Master, I still don't understand. Why did we let her go?" Bai Di had racked his brains but still couldn't figure out what the Young Master was thinking.

So, he simply asked.

Li Qi, sitting to the side, answered casually, "It's simple. To muddy the waters. This organization definitely has a Child of Destiny. They represent the final resistance of this world's Heavenly Dao; they are the local power."

"If I were to go to the meeting alone, it might look like I'm suppressing him with my momentum, forcing him to come see me. But what if he simply doesn't show up? Right? Furthermore, think a bit deeper. What if he's ruthless and sets up a trap on the way, intending to kill me outright? What then?"

Li Qi explained, then sat up straight. "Therefore, by introducing a third-party faction now, if I ever fall into a disadvantage, someone will suddenly jump out to help us."

"Ah? Are you that certain?" Bai Di asked in disbelief.

While the logic was sound, how could he be sure the other party would actually come?

Li Qi looked completely relaxed. "It's just preparing a backup plan. I don't expect them to come for sure. If they do, great; if they don't, it doesn't matter." He smiled. "At the end of the day, this struggle is nothing more than a game between me and that Young Master. So what if one of us loses? It's not like he can actually kill me. We both lay our cards on the table, and winning or losing just comes down to luck."

Hearing this, Bai Di felt relieved.

Indeed.

The true purpose of this trip had already been fulfilled. Even he had grasped most of what there was to know about the dao rhythm of this other world.

The rest was merely a matter of seeing who could snatch more rewards. The eventual Heavenly Dao recompense, or the differing dao rhythm after the world dropped in rank, were all just bonus prizes in this game.

To the Young Masters, a Rank 8 outer-realm world was nothing more than this.

Seeing Li Qi's relaxed demeanor, Bai Di couldn't help but feel a sense of admiration.

However, Shen Shuibi looked at Li Qi with a rather subtle expression.

It was as if some change had occurred within him.

But she shook her head and ultimately said nothing.

---

Late at night.

The moon hung high in the sky, and the stars shone brightly.

In the wasteland, this endless firmament was the only thing that offered any solace.

On the edge of this endless wasteland sat a small base.

Unexpectedly, the environment and atmosphere here seemed incredibly... peaceful?

Though it was a rebel base filled with strong, capable armed personnel, they almost all exuded a peculiar aura. For instance, most of them remained completely silent, and any occasional conversations were held in hushed tones. The majority of them were reading.

The books largely lacked covers, appearing to be handmade.

Yet, one could tell that their relationships with one another were quite good.

After all, emotion was something that could silently seep through the finest cracks in the heart, no matter how strong the person was.

And as it happened, human hearts were all flawed.

The return of the lioness Sha'er broke this tranquility.

Many people in the base came out to greet them.

She and Lu Li received a unanimous welcome. Those who had escaped earlier rushed over to ask about their safety.

"Platoon Leader! Are you alright?!" Her teammates asked her with deep concern.

"Don't worry, I'm fine." Feeling their care, Sha'er smiled and soothed their anxieties.

"Are you really okay? Platoon Leader, you were..." Her teammates still looked incredibly uneasy.

"I said I'm fine. Do I look hurt to you? Alright, if you have questions, ask Lu Li. I need to go report to the higher-ups," Sha'er said to her surrounding teammates, tossing them over to Lu Li.

Lu Li scratched his head, weighing what he could and couldn't say. He prepared his answers as best he could, bracing himself for the incoming barrage of questions.

Meanwhile, Sha'er left the crowd. Nodding to the onlookers from other platoons, she headed straight for "Number Three's" room.

In this organization, the highest-ranking leaders were uniformly addressed by codenames.

There were six in total, styling themselves from "Number One" to "Number Six."

According to Number One, this was a requirement for a certain ritual. If they were to die in the future, their successors had to inherit the codename; otherwise, the organization's reason for existing would vanish.

No one knew why, but Number One's prestige was extremely high, so everyone accepted this explanation.

Among these six leaders, Number Three was in charge of intelligence.

Sha'er needed to report her intel to him.

Arriving at Number Three's room, she knocked on the door.

In this base, everyone's quarters were roughly the same. No one was nobler than anyone else; people merely had different divisions of labor based on their abilities, but their treatment was largely identical.

"Come in," a voice called out from inside.

Sha'er pushed the door open and saw Number Three organizing files.

Intelligence work always involved a mountain of paperwork.

Sha'er walked in and said, "I'm here to report on the many things I encountered, specifically regarding the 'Young Master' we met."

Number Three was a very ordinary-looking man in his fifties. He didn't wear a mask or put on any mysterious airs. His codename was the only unusual thing about him; everything else was completely normal.

In fact, everyone knew his real name was Yuehan[^1].

Just as mentioned before, aside from the strange rules set by Number One, everyone here was fundamentally equal. They were all people gathered together for a single purpose.

And that goal was to rid themselves of the Exalted Immortals.

The Exalted Immortals were the venomous parasites driving this world toward gradual destruction.

"Regarding the 'Young Master' Number One spoke of? Number One said that if it's about this matter, you should go talk to her directly," Number Three said, looking up at Sha'er.

"Is that so? Then I'll head over to her place first." Sha'er took her leave.

"Mhm." Number Three nodded and buried his head back into his paperwork.

Sha'er changed her destination once more and arrived at Number One's room.

However, the door here was surprisingly open.

Frowning, she knocked, but there was no response.

She waited for a moment, but still, no one spoke.

She simply pushed the door open and walked in.

The moment she stepped through the doorway, the scene before her eyes suddenly shifted.

Then, she saw Number One suspended in mid-air.

Inside was not a room at all, but a strange space floating within the night sky. The night sky in all directions was as clear as a dream. The silver river rose from the southeast, with tens of thousands of points of light condensing and scattering in the air, like fluorescent light piercing through the crystal of the dark night.

Looking at this tranquil sky projected within the room, the stars seemed to possess actual shape and distance.

To mortals, the distance of the starry sky had never felt simultaneously so far away and yet within arm's reach. It all felt like a dream or an illusion, yet she was witnessing it with her own eyes.

Looking around, this inexplicably manifested starry sky was incredibly radiant. The silver river above looked as if it were about to cascade down, as brilliant as a jeweled eye gazing down upon the world with pity.

Number One, the familiar young girl, was floating amidst the star map. Her expression was agonized, her body drenched in sweat.

She looked like a seventeen- or eighteen-year-old girl, around high school age, wearing ordinary clothes. But as she stood suspended among the stars, her entire being radiated a sense of profound suffering.

Her veins bulged, her bones trembled, and her muscles spasmed. Sweat poured down like rain, and her expression was contorted, as if she were being subjected to some irresistible, agonizing torture.

Seeing this, Sha'er sighed.

Number One was doing this again.

However, it couldn't be helped; it was necessary.

According to her own words, she was communicating with the 'outer realms'—exchanging information with those worlds that had not yet been conquered by the Exalted Immortals.

It sounded incredibly mystical.

But Sha'er knew it was true.

Because her own modification surgery, the so-called "Type-A modified human manufacturing specification," had been obtained through this very method.

The modification process had been excruciatingly painful.

But it had genuinely granted her power that surpassed that of an ordinary Exalted Immortal.

It was just... they hadn't expected the Exalted Immortals to possess higher-tier beings like the "Young Masters."

Sha'er sat down within the starry sky, waiting for Number One to finish her communion.

Generally speaking, this kind of communication would end within an hour or two; it wouldn't last too long.

Because Number One's body couldn't withstand it.

Such communion was agonizingly painful and torturous. Every use was akin to cruel punishment and would shave years off her lifespan.

Sitting on the floor, Sha'er began to ponder her own situation.

Judging from this recent encounter, powerful individuals like the Young Masters had appeared among the Exalted Immortals.

Then... was it possible that they had existences even stronger than the Young Masters?

She didn't dare to imagine. A Young Master's abilities were already sufficient to decimate entire city blocks, right? If there were beings stronger than the Young Masters, could they flip over entire cities with ease?

With such a massive disparity... what were she and her people even striving for?

In the end, they still had to rely on Number One.

Sha'er looked up at the teenage girl.

It was this little girl, who grew up in an orphanage, who had founded the organization at the age of twelve and painstakingly expanded it to its current scale step by step.

She was the true Child of Destiny.

Despite her young age, everyone deeply respected her.

Sha'er felt that if everything the Young Master had said was true, then without a doubt, the Child of Destiny of this world was definitely Number One.

However, Number One never revealed her real name. She only ever referred to herself as Number One, claiming it was part of a ritual.

Nobody knew what kind of ritual it was.

Pondering these matters and mulling over the Young Master's words, time passed quickly.

Soon, the stars around them began to extinguish one by one.

The hovering Number One gradually descended to the ground.

"Haa... Haa." She was dripping with sweat, her eyes bloodshot, and she had practically ground her teeth to dust. The intense pain had turned her face a sickly, pallid blue, making her look terrifying.

It was like... the complexion of a pregnant woman undergoing a difficult labor, suffering massive hemorrhaging coupled with excruciating agony—a truly horrific ordeal.

And she had endured this silently while suspended in the air for hours.

"Big Sister Sha'er, you're back... What was the situation over there? I heard from the returnees that you encountered a 'Young Master'?" Number One stood up as the room reverted to its ordinary appearance.

She walked to the side, picked up a towel, wiped her sweat, and took a few deep breaths. She looked significantly better.

Once she finished wiping her sweat, Sha'er replied, "I documented everything that happened after we went over on my way back. I was originally going to give it to Number Three, but he told me to bring it directly to you."

With that, she handed over her written intelligence report.

"Alright, let me see." Number One took the report and read it carefully.

The more she read, the more solemn her expression became.

She said, "Big Sister Sha'er, you wouldn't lie. Which means everything written here is true. Those Young Masters truly possess the power to wipe us out directly... Aside from me, no one else would be able to withstand them."

Sha'er nodded. "Exactly. I faced off against one of the Young Masters directly. He didn't even use those abilities to manipulate matter; he defeated me with pure physical strength, and I didn't have the slightest ability to fight back."

She recalled her battle with Li Qi. The man had a long blade that he hadn't even unsheathed, relying solely on his fists to smash her to the ground.

Yet, that Young Master was relatively short, with ordinary musculature. How on earth did such a slender, fragile-looking body unleash such terrifying power?

Number One sighed. "I have been searching the outer realms for the origins of the Exalted Immortals. I've inquired in many worlds, but none of them seem to have ever had 'Exalted Immortals' present."

"But it doesn't matter. Regardless, we must drive the Exalted Immortals away. Now that we have two Young Masters fighting each other, it's a good thing for us."

Sha'er listened quietly to Number One's words.

"Big Sister Sha'er, prepare yourself. Since you said the two Young Masters are going to meet, we cannot pass up this opportunity. I believe that these two feuding Young Masters present our best chance to rid ourselves of the Exalted Immortals!"

"No matter what you intend to do, I will support you. But you have to tell me what your plan is," Sha'er replied.

"I have a secret weapon. It involves our codenames. This is something I've been stockpiling for a long time. I plan to exhaust the very last bit of this world's underlying foundation to alter our world's coordinates."

"Once the coordinates are changed, our connection to the Exalted Immortals will be severed. This is the only way to truly free our world and ensure the Exalted Immortals can never find us again!"

"You've had a way this whole time? Then why wait until now to do it?" Sha'er was bewildered.

"The accumulated foundation was always a bit lacking. My original plan was to stockpile for another ten years, and then we might have succeeded. Unfortunately, there's no time left. Those two Young Masters are preparing to drain the absolute last of our world's foundation."

"If they succeed, we will never have another chance," Number One gritted her teeth.

"But you said we hadn't accumulated enough... wait, is this why we've been assigned to attack the earth oil extraction plants all these years?" Sha'er suddenly realized something.

Was this so-called accumulation actually the earth oil?

"Yes. Our stockpile is still far from sufficient. But I believe that if we add the underlying foundations of those two Young Masters into the mix, it should be enough to activate the ritual! This is our final chance!"

"This ritual is called 'Severing Heaven and Earth'!"

[^1]: This name Yuehan (约翰 | Yuēhàn) is the standard transliteration for **John**. The characters were chosen for their sound in older dialects (like Cantonese or Middle Chinese), which originally sounded much closer to "Yohan" than the modern Mandarin "Yuēhàn" does.
