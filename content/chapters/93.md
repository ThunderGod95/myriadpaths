# Chapter 93: True Form

Since the matter was settled, all that remained was to act upon it.

Li Qi returned to the inn first to find Shen Shuibi. He intended to bring her along with him.

He made his way straight back to the inn.

Knocking on the door from the outside, he asked, "Miss Shen, are you busy?"

But he received no response whatsoever.

His heart tightened. Frowning, he pushed the door open without a moment's hesitation and strode inside.

Inside, he saw a rabbit.

A literal rabbit.

Its fur was smooth and glossy, radiating a healthy sheen. Its two ears flopped down over its face like a sleep mask. It was sprawled out on the bed, rump raised in the air, snoring softly.

Li Qi was dumbfounded.

Truth be told, this was... his first time seeing Shen Shuibi's true form.

It was kind of, well, cute?

But wait... this didn't seem to be a normal rabbit.

Li Qi could clearly see a certain type of luminous particle naturally emanating from Shen Shuibi's body.

His eyesight was currently exceptional. Abundant in Great Forest Wood qi, his liver, which belonged to the wood element, had been tempered to the absolute limit among his five viscera. Because the liver governed the eyes, the flourishing of his internal organs meant the flourishing of his senses. Thus, upon entering Rank 9, his vision had improved tremendously.

Focusing his gaze now, he saw that the glowing motes drifting around Shen Shuibi matched the exact descriptions found in the 'Beast Section' of the strange records within the [Book of Invocation].

_Her radiance is graceful and lovely, like a newly full moon of ice; this is the Moon Essence._

The so-called Jade Rabbit and Golden Crow were the spiritual manifestations of the dual qi of the sun and moon. The moon hid the Jade Rabbit, and the sun hid the Crow. These unique specks of light, concealing hidden runes, were the defining characteristic of the sun and moon's essence.

Shen Shuibi... certainly wasn't an ordinary rabbit. She was a Moon Essence Jade Rabbit.

This creature was no mere adorable pet. It was a spirit beast on par with the Golden Crow, a notable innate exotic beast. As the physical manifestation of the moon's spiritual essence, she represented a fragment of the Supreme Yin.

Supreme Yin was the convergence of all Yin qi between heaven and earth. Standing in opposition to the Supreme Yang, it was the key to universal harmony. A Moon Essence was born from shattered fragments of the Supreme Yin, making it a piece of the Supreme Yin itself.

"Truly incredible..." Li Qi sighed in admiration. He had long known that Shen Shuibi was far from simple, so seeing her original form for the first time only surprised him for a fleeting moment.

After marveling at her, he stepped forward and gave the rabbit a few good rubs.

Her fur was incredibly silky, feeling absolutely divine to the touch.

It was thrilling.

He rubbed her for a while, but Shen Shuibi remained fast asleep. She even wriggled her body, as if thoroughly enjoying the petting, and began to sleep even more soundly.

She seemed genuinely exhausted.

However, she would have to sleep on the road. Since they were departing tonight, he needed to wake her up so she could pack her belongings, check out of the room, and head over to the caravan.

Therefore, he tugged gently at the rabbit's ears, intending to rouse her.

Ah...

This texture was simply irresistible...

Still, Li Qi possessed a great deal of self-control. He tugged at her ears again. Sure enough, Shen Shuibi's ears suddenly twitched. She swatted his hand away with a _smack_, then began rubbing her eyes with her two tiny paws.

"Mmm... Mother, what is it?" she murmured softly, her voice thick with sleep.

"Your Mother isn't here. Wake up, Miss Shen. We need to leave." Li Qi scooped her rabbit form off the bed and placed her on the table.

Shen Shuibi still seemed a bit dazed, unable to fully pull herself from the depths of her dreams. The entire rabbit lay motionless on the spot, seemingly trying to gather her wits.

Li Qi waited patiently to the side.

He could clearly see an "Awakening" progress bar hovering over Shen Shuibi's body.

This interface couldn't display deeply profound information; it could only reveal the current action a person was undertaking. Therefore, it was mostly just something to look at, though occasionally it proved surprisingly useful.

Even the current action wasn't always completely detailed. For instance, if someone was speaking to him, he would only see an "Interacting" action and nothing more.

Wasn't that obvious? Of course Li Qi knew the person was interacting with him.

The sole advantage was that it provided Li Qi's perception with a minor cheat. As long as he wasn't completely oblivious, the progress bar would pop up. For example, during the time he had grabbed Shen Shuibi, if he could perceive even the slightest hint of her presence—even subconsciously—the progress bar would appear.

Yet, it was also quite useless.

If he truly couldn't see, hear, or feel anything, then he was completely out of luck.

The most direct example involved ghosts. If he didn't activate his qi-observing vision, he wouldn't see their progress bars at all. But the moment he activated his vision and perceived ghost qi, its action progress bar would materialize.

Overall, it was still helpful, aside from the times it frequently obstructed his line of sight.

He just had no idea where it came from.

Was it the effect of an Outer Dao artifact...?

It was hard to say. He was still too inexperienced to determine what this phenomenon was.

While he pondered these things, the progress bar had almost filled up.

Right as it reached completion, Shen Shuibi regained her consciousness. She abruptly propped herself up and shot Li Qi a vigilant glare.

Then, she let out a piercing, miserable shriek!

"Wh... what's wrong?!" The noise startled Li Qi, causing him to flinch.

"You! Li Qi, what are you doing?! You lecher! Get out of here right now!" Shen Shuibi cried, her voice a mix of terror, shame, and fury, as she began hurling teacups at him.

Li Qi was completely baffled, but since he was being pelted, he threw his hands up in surrender. He quickly retreated, pulled the door open, and slammed it shut behind him.

Following that, he heard rustling sounds from inside, like the rustle of clothing being put on.

After a while, Shen Shuibi's muffled voice drifted through the door. "You can come in..."

Li Qi pushed the door open again, his face a picture of pure confusion. Shen Shuibi had reverted to her usual human appearance, her expression a stormy mix of embarrassment and anger. She looked furious.

Biting her lip, indignation written all over her face, Shen Shuibi finally spoke. "Li Qi, how could you...!"

Just as she said "how could you", she seemingly realized whatever she was about to say was too indecent. She swallowed the words back down, looking positively fuming.

"Miss Shen... while it was my fault for entering while you were asleep, I truly don't understand. What exactly is the matter?"

"What's the matter? You have the gall to ask! The ancients said, 'Look not at what is improper!' How could you just look at me however you pleased!" Shen Shuibi's tone was so upset she sounded on the verge of tears.

Li Qi was even more perplexed.

"Ah... Miss Shen, but what did I look at?" he asked, scratching his head.

"I wasn't wearing any clothes!" Shen Shuibi finally steeled her heart and laid the facts bare.

Li Qi was stunned.

"But... don't you have fur?"

_Besides, weren't you in the shape of a rabbit?_ Li Qi silently complained in his mind.

Shen Shuibi immediately burst into tears of sheer anger. Snatching another teacup from the table, she hurled it right at him. "You have hair on your body too! Does that mean anyone can just look at you however they please?!"

Looking like a complete clown, Li Qi was pelted once more. He practically tumbled out of the room, hastily pulling the door shut behind him.

_Sigh... what kind of sin did I commit to deserve this..._
