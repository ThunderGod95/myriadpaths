# Chapter 98: Operating from a Supremely High Vantage Point

Watching from the sidelines, Shen Shuibi was dying of anxiety.

She had clearly marked out all the flaws and weaknesses, and even provided the method to disrupt the opponent's internal qi circulation. Yet, right from the start, Li Qi had fallen into a disadvantage under the fierce assault, utterly incapable of fighting back or utilizing any of the techniques she had given him.

_This Li Qi is so stupid!_

Clutching her clothes, she stamped her feet in frustration, wishing she could just jump in and fight herself.

As for the others from the Jing family's chamber of commerce, aside from lacking melon seeds and watermelon, they were the very picture of peanut-gallery spectators. Some had even started passing around small snacks.

Someone even handed one to Shen Shuibi. She accepted it, politely said thank you, and discovered it was a dried plum.

Sweet and sour, it was actually quite delicious.

These people were treating the fight exactly like a television program—sharing snacks, discussing the action, cheering at the exciting parts, and turning away to chat among themselves when it got boring.

They truly were treating it as a theatrical performance.

Inside the ring, however, Li Qi had no mind to pay attention to anything else.

He was met with an endless barrage of fierce attacks. He felt like a traveler caught in the rain holding an umbrella; the raindrops pounded down relentlessly, and all he could do was desperately hold the umbrella up to block the torrential downpour falling upon his head.

Every single 'raindrop' was an incredibly powerful thrust of the sword tip. If he failed to block even once, he would be facing the longsword head-on.

Yet, for Jing Yan, maintaining such an offensive was completely effortless. It was obvious she wasn't using her full strength.

"Your blade work is terrible, but the fact that you've blocked me until now shows you possess some strength," she remarked, still having the leisure to critique Li Qi's technique while maintaining her assault.

But Li Qi had no spare energy whatsoever. He didn't even hear Jing Yan's words, as his entire body and mind were focused on her attacks.

Numerous progress bars flashed and reset before his eyes—these were the intervals between Jing Yan's strikes.

What appeared to be a seamless, torrential rain of attacks was actually continuous cycles of internal qi circulation. With every cycle, Jing Yan could propel her body to execute the exact same thrust!

However, while each cycle consumed very little internal qi, it absolutely required a momentary pause!

This was one of the many flaws in her internal qi circulation that Shen Shuibi had mentioned.

But he couldn't grasp it.

He saw it, but he couldn't seize the opening.

She was simply too fast, so fast his eyes couldn't keep up. Even though the progress bar allowed him to perceive the time differential when she withdrew her qi and shed her force, he simply couldn't reach her in time.

_This won't do... I won't be able to hold on much longer at this rate. I have to think of a way—_ Li Qi grew anxious. They had been fighting for less than a minute, yet he felt as if his heart were about to explode.

To strengthen his physical movements, an overly massive amount of vitality was circulating rapidly through his body, and the organs supporting all this were the heart and the liver.

His liver had been refined and possessed abundant vitality, so it was fine.

His heart, however, was in absolute agony from the surging influx of massive vitality. It was already pumping as hard as it could, but an untempered heart struggled to bear such intense physical exertion.

It was fine for a short duration, but if the battle dragged on for more than two or three minutes, he would likely lose his combat capability very quickly.

If he ignored it and continued to push himself, he might even die from sudden cardiac arrest.

He had to think of a way. But what way?

Rely on his body to tank a hit, trading an injury for a chance to win? He would probably die. Even if he didn't, being injured and having his combat power compromised would be even more troublesome.

Activate the Ox Strength Art? Wouldn't that be broadcasting his identity as someone from the Shaman Dao? It was absolutely not worth it for a mere wager.

Then what other options did he have?

Amidst his frantic thoughts, a tiny flaw appeared in his defensive stance.

Jing Yan instantly spotted this flaw and thrust straight toward his core without hesitation! She was determined to defeat Li Qi with this single strike!

She had already gauged the level of Li Qi's blade work. No matter how he resisted this sword strike, the battle was guaranteed to end. She had calculated all fourteen possible blade trajectories he could use to counterattack; the moment she struck, everything was entirely within her control.

However, a brilliant light erupted from Li Qi's eyes!

`_Holy shit, she actually stopped suppressing me and started casting a skill!_`

This was a golden opportunity!

Li Qi immediately erupted with all his bodily strength. Gripping the long blade like a fire poker, he swung it downward, following the techniques he had stayed up late memorizing the night before.

This slash was thoroughly unremarkable; one could even say it completely lacked any method or order, a chaotic swing.

Yet, this very slash forced Jing Yan to retract her guaranteed strike.

Because Jing Yan realized that if this blade landed, her flawless internal qi circulation would be directly interrupted!

When martial artists fought, having one's internal qi circulation interrupted was tantamount to defeat.

However, there was still a chance to recover.

Taking a step back, she shifted her longsword from offense to defense. The previous torrential rain and howling gale transformed into a pliant qi force, circling her body like a moat to block the incoming attack.

As long as she weathered Li Qi's single move, she could launch an immediate counterattack.

But the next slash arrived.

The sword qi was effortlessly brushed aside, dissolving into nothingness!

"Hmm...?!" Jing Yan's pupils shrank. She had never expected that a defense capable of deflecting a ten-thousand-catty boulder would be pierced through so effortlessly!

Wait, it wasn't pierced—it was bypassed!

This Li Qi possessed incredibly shallow foundations; he hadn't even properly tempered his five viscera, and his face was flushed red from exertion earlier. Yet in this moment, he displayed a realm far surpassing his foundation!

Refusing to let the opportunity slip, Li Qi gripped the long blade tightly and stepped forward. In an instant, shadows of blades and flashes of swords filled the air. The ground bore the brunt of the devastation, forcefully caving in by half a foot!

Applause rang out from all around as the crowd watched in breathless amazement, clicking their tongues in wonder.

To think someone could fight that tigress to a standstill at Rank 9! It was widely known that the other Rank 9 deacons of the Martial Affairs Hall had all had their teeth beaten out by this tigress.

This young hero Li Qi really had some skill!

While the laymen enjoyed the spectacle, Jing Yan suddenly felt her pressure multiply.

What was going on?!

How had this person's blade work suddenly become so fierce and sharp?!

This was clearly a manifestation of having cultivated martial principles to an exceedingly high realm. When one cultivated martial principles and foundations to the absolute limit, they could casually invent sword moves at will, with each strike perfectly tailored to counter the opponent's attack.

Viewed in isolation, every single one of his slashes was incredibly clumsy, crooked, and completely devoid of power.

However, in that specific moment, every strike was a stroke of genius. They could be called moves specifically created to counter the exact situation at hand; they would be completely useless in any other scenario.

In other words, they were the most perfect moves to counter the current situation, capable of unleashing the most miraculous effects!

Every single move struck precisely at the breaks in her internal qi. She was stretched thin trying to cope, but no matter how she defended, Jing Yan's defense was like trying to catch rain with a sieve—full of holes. No matter how she altered her moves, they were effortlessly broken.

Her internal qi was nothing but weaknesses!

Her internal qi circulation, which she previously felt was flawless, appeared like scattered sand in the eyes of her opponent. He could strike its weak points at will, making her so agonizingly uncomfortable she nearly coughed up blood.

The tables turned. In a mere ten seconds or so, with a sharp _clatter_, the longsword flew from her grasp.

And the blade came to rest against Jing Yan's neck.

As for Li Qi, even he hadn't fully registered what happened. How had he won?
