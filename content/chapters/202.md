# Chapter 202: Outside Factions

Truthfully, Zhang Lan's intentions were good, and Li Qi had considered this very proposal quite a few times himself.

However, Li Qi had ultimately shelved the option for the time being, choosing not to voice it.

Now that it had been brought up, Li Qi replied, "We cannot. The Palace Master of the Righteous Sword Palace currently has important matters to attend to. If this situation ruins their grand scheme, it will backfire, and the blame will fall entirely on me."

Hearing this left Zhang Lan completely bewildered. He scratched his head. "Grand scheme? Why don't I know about this? Brother Li, you aren't even a member of the Righteous Sword Palace. How could you know?"

Li Qi immediately understood that Zhang Lan was unaware of his sect's major operation.

Come to think of it, this was perfectly normal.

At this very moment, the Palace Master of the Righteous Sword Palace was conspiring with their numerous elders. They planned to take advantage of the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord's journey to Beyond the Boundary to intercept and kill one of the Six Demon Kings of Heavenly Filth, thereby severing one of the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord's arms.

The Six Demon Kings of Heavenly Filth naturally formed a grand formation. With the six qi of heaven as their foundation, they could merge their power to form the true 'Qi of Heaven,' making them formidable when they fought together.

Before Li Qi arrived, the Green Mountain Kingdom Lord had told him that the Heaven-Devouring Demon Lord had gone to Beyond the Boundary for unknown reasons. The Righteous Sword Palace was seizing this opportunity to assassinate one of the six demon kings, aiming to permanently cripple the Six Afflictions divine ability they cast together.

That was a battle between Rank 6 experts. If he really diverted a Rank 6 here and caused their main front to collapse, Li Qi's sins would be unforgivable.

However, he could not share such secrets with Zhang Lan. If it leaked, the consequences would be catastrophic.

It was not that Li Qi distrusted Zhang Lan, but sometimes, a person's sheer willpower meant nothing. If certain beings wanted to pry your mouth open, you would be entirely powerless to resist.

Therefore, total ignorance was the best way to keep a secret.

Thus, Li Qi warned, "Do not concern yourself with this matter. Telling you will only bring you harm. Just know that you must not seek out your Palace Master unless absolutely necessary. Otherwise, you might ruin their grand operation, and you won't be able to afford the consequences even if you sell yourself."

Hearing this, Zhang Lan grasped Li Qi's meaning.

Li Qi likely knew something he did not, but was forbidden from speaking of it.

So, he asked, "Then, Brother Li, I have a question."

After speaking, Zhang Lan stared intently at Li Qi. "How do you know all this?"

Li Qi knew far too much and acted entirely too mysteriously. From their initial chance encounter to the myriad of events that followed, Zhang Lan had barely had time to process anything, yet Li Qi had navigated it all with effortless grace.

Not only had he arranged everything Zhang Lan needed to do, but he had even subjugated the underground factions of Vast Yang and personally investigated the origins of Monk Chanzhi.

How had he accomplished such unimaginable feats? What was his background?

In response, Li Qi threw his hands up and said simply, "I come from Shaman God Mountain."

Just like that, Zhang Lan's doubts vanished.

That name alone was enough to dispel all suspicion. It explained away every single abnormality about Li Qi.

"Shaman God Mountain... an outer affairs envoy?" Zhang Lan asked carefully.

Li Qi did not hide it. Since he was already laying his cards on the table, he spat out two more words: "Young Master."

Zhang Lan lost his footing and crashed into the wall. His qi and blood surged, stars danced in his vision, and he stammered, desperately trying to say something but completely failing to form the words.

The Righteous Sword Palace was considered a large sect. Although its main branch was in the Baiyue region, it was already regarded as a mid-to-high-tier faction there.

Which was exactly why he was terrified.

It took him a long while to regain his composure. With a bitter smile, he said, "It seems I've been reaching out of my league by calling you Brother Li. But, with such a background, why are you tangling yourself up in all of this here?"

"Just treat it as an exam," Li Qi said helplessly.

It was hard for him to explain, especially since he had no idea if his current situation was considered normal.

Would a normal Grand Invocator disciple be treated like this?

His teacher had tossed him two books and then ordered him to trek three million miles back to Shaman God Mountain.

He couldn't shake the feeling that a teacher with a remotely normal brain would never do such a thing.

But then again... back in the Earth Oil World, Bo Yue, another Young Master of Shaman God Mountain, had told Li Qi that his teacher was notoriously abnormal.

So encountering these bizarre events was actually par for the course?

It was normal precisely because it was abnormal.

It was convoluted logic, but that was the general gist of it.

However, with Li Qi's words, Zhang Lan instantly understood the reasoning behind everything.

An exam.

The Righteous Sword Palace had similar trials.

Throwing elite disciples into fiend dens and forcing them to slaughter their way out, and so on.

It was just that...

The Righteous Sword Palace typically threw them into fiend dens that matched their cultivation level, allowing them to temper themselves while slaying fiends.

They would never do something as insane as forcing a Rank 9 disciple to confront a Rank 7 entity.

That wasn't an exam; that was a death sentence.

But considering his identity.

Perhaps only a challenge of this magnitude qualified as an exam for someone like him.

Zhang Lan took a deep breath to calm himself. Maintaining his poise, neither servile nor overbearing, he didn't change his attitude due to Li Qi's status. He simply asked, "Then, Brother Li, why did you expend so much effort to bring me back?"

Li Qi didn't put on airs either. He pulled Zhang Lan to sit down in the courtyard and said, "I brought you over, Brother Zhang, because I naturally have an important task to entrust to you."

"Please speak. This concerns the lives of countless people in Vast Yang. If I can be of assistance, I will absolutely spare no effort." Zhang Lan grew serious, listening intently.

"First, listen to my analysis of Vast Yang's current situation."

Dipping his finger into the tea, Li Qi used the teacups as markers and began sketching on the stone table.

"First, we must recognize that Vast Yang has deteriorated to this state entirely because of Monk Chanzhi."

"I've investigated his goal. He came here for Shaman Dao cultivation methods. His current state is one plagued by devilish thoughts. An expert likely told him that Shaman Dao methods could alleviate his condition, which is why he traveled here."

"But he faces an awkward dilemma. For many orthodox Shaman Dao lineages, the inheritances themselves are heavily guarded. He lacks the ability to break through those defenses."

Zhang Lan nodded in agreement.

Many inheritances were like this. Numerous cultivation methods possessed abilities like mind-reading, memory extraction, or even directly devouring another's cultivation base. Yet, trying to obtain top-tier methods through such means was pure delusion.

Almost all orthodox inheritances had defensive countermeasures. Some required a crucial catalyst to practice, making the raw text useless. Others were far more brutal, instantly killing those who tried to steal the teachings.

For instance, the core cultivation method of the Righteous Sword Palace required one to initiate their training at the very center of the sect. If someone learned the method elsewhere and attempted to practice it, the generated sword qi would immediately spiral out of control. At best, their efforts would be wasted; at worst, they would explode and die.

Zhang Lan had even heard of a miraculous cultivation method belonging to a major sect overseen by a Rank 5 powerhouse. It was called the 'Sutra of Ingestion'. This method could not be memorized; the pactitioner would automatically forget it every day. It could not be transcribed either; any copied text would vanish the next day, leaving only blank paper.

Therefore, one could only cultivate it in front of the sect's original scriptures, reading and practicing day by day. They would practice while staring at the book, forget it, and return the next day to read it again, repeating the process until mastery was achieved.

Yet, even after mastering it, they merely gained a new divine ability; the actual cultivation method still could not be remembered.

As for what methods Shaman God Mountain employed to protect its teachings, that was unknown. But this was Shaman God Mountain... just thinking about it sent shivers down one's spine.

Naturally, Li Qi didn't know what Zhang Lan was thinking. He simply continued, "Because of this, Monk Chanzhi couldn't seek out profound inheritances. So, he set his sights on Vast Yang. The Vast Yang Prefect and Minister of War are both Rank 7 in the Shaman Dao, and Chanzhi is confident in his ability to break the restrictions on their inheritances."

"That is the root of Vast Yang's crisis. After arriving, Monk Chanzhi first leveraged the human qi of the tens of thousands of households here, then used karma to construct the framework of his five-tier Buddhist Pagoda. Following that, he fought one against three. He killed the Minister of War, exiled the Prefect, and suppressed the Minister of Works underground, leaving him terrified to show his face."

"As for the Rank 8 Minister of State, he was easily refined into a puppet afterward using a heart-eating method. Since then, the governance of Vast Yang has been under his absolute control. Hiding behind the scenes, he uses the authority of the Minister of State and the deceased Minister of War to slowly unearth Shaman Dao inheritances."

"However, because he devoured both the Minister of State's and Minister of War's hearts and consumed their memories, he has deciphered their Shaman Dao methods and begun to experiment with them. This is our opening," Li Qi concluded.

Zhang Lan, who had been focused on analyzing the political landscape, was taken aback.

How had this suddenly become an opportunity? Weren't they just discussing Monk Chanzhi's status?

"You might not know this, Brother Zhang, but I do. The Shaman Dao methods held by both the Vast Yang Minister of War and the Minister of State are actually Gu Shaman arts!" Li Qi declared with unwavering certainty.

He was absolutely positive. Back at the node of the landscape grand array, specifically at Lord Huangshan's location, he had clearly observed the array's construction. It was unequivocally built using Gu Shaman arts.

This was an established fact.

"Gu Shaman arts have strayed entirely from the orthodox path, essentially abandoning the Shaman Dao's methods of balance. Therefore, they are completely useless for purging the devilish thoughts in his heart. This is precisely why he targeted me."

"Along my journey, I performed good deeds everywhere, leaving my name behind far too often through sacrificial rites. I was too high-profile, which allowed Monk Chanzhi to discover me early on. He placed his hopes on me, desiring to obtain an orthodox Shaman Dao cultivation method to balance his Buddha and Devil hearts. To that end, he orchestrated the incident at the border town and step-by-step lured me into willingly creating methods for him. Now that I think about it, Brother Zhang, you were dragged into this mess because of me."

Zhang Lan shook his head. "This man condemned hundreds of thousands to death for his own selfish desires. He truly deserves to die! As for dragging me down, speak no more of it. Even if you weren't there, if I encountered such a situation, I would have charged in regardless. There is no dragging down. But you still haven't told me where our opportunity lies."

Li Qi cut to the core of the matter, "The opportunity lies within the Gu Shaman arts. Gu Shaman arts do not possess grand rites!"

"Their sacrificial methods have mutated into techniques for raising Gu and feeding demons. They are no longer orthodox rites. This means that Monk Chanzhi himself lacks the ability to complete a true grand rite."

Stopping here, Li Qi looked at Zhang Lan's utterly perplexed face and explained, "Brother Zhang, do you remember when we confronted those two Rank 8 cultivators trying to abduct the commoners?"

Zhang Lan recalled the event and said, "Of course. Afterwards, I think you mentioned it was something called... a Rat Soil Mound."

Li Qi slapped the table. "Exactly! The Rat Soil Mound!"

"Do you know the legends of the Rat Soil Mound, Brother Zhang?"

Zhang Lan shook his head blankly. "Naturally, I don't. What is it?"

"The Rat Soil Mound is a strange beast from the Buddhist Kingdom that craves incense fire from sacrifices. Based on my deductions, Monk Chanzhi brought it here. However, the rat king within the mound is at least Rank 7, so it cannot possibly be a subordinate of Monk Chanzhi. It is far more likely they traveled together as companions to this land governed by the Shaman Dao."

"In the legends, the Rat Soil Mound is obsessed with receiving sacrifices. The _Record of the Western Regions_ states: 'Thus, from monarchs down to commoners, all practice sacrifices to seek blessings. Approaching its burrow, one must dismount and proceed on foot, bowing in respect and offering sacrifices for good fortune. Offerings of clothing, bows and arrows, or fragrant flowers and delicacies, given with true sincerity, will yield great blessings. If it is denied its sacrifices, disaster will strike.' This clearly shows its nature!"

Sacrifice for good fortune, and receive great blessings.

Denied sacrifices, and disaster will strike.

These two lines perfectly encapsulated the Rat Soil Mound's disposition.

Shen Shuibi had told him all this knowledge. Chanzhi could never have anticipated that Li Qi possessed such obscure information!

Hearing Li Qi's explanation, Zhang Lan was suddenly enlightened!

He stood up in pleasant surprise and clapped his hands with fierce satisfaction. "Monk Chanzhi lacks the power of sacrifice, but you have it, Brother Li! You want to borrow the power of the Rat Soil Mound to force that demonic monk to eat his own bitter fruit?! Brilliant, brilliant! But how do you plan to execute this?"

Li Qi waved a hand, gesturing for him to calm down and sit back down to listen.

Suppressing his excitement and questions, Zhang Lan sat and continued listening.

He was finally beginning to understand. This Young Master of Shaman God Mountain likely didn't excel in martial prowess, but when it came to intelligence gathering and scheming, he was ten times Zhang Lan's superior!

Making a trip to Vast Yang, he had even written a letter to Zhang Lan on the way back. It was clear that even Monk Chanzhi's reactions had been within his calculations all along.

Now, he had effortlessly taken over two major factions in Vast Yang City: the Grain Alliance and the Lower Nine Gates, effectively securing over eighty percent of Vast Yang's underground power.

Although it seemed he had no ties with the officials in the open, who knew if someone would suddenly pop up revealing themselves to be his pawn?

And he had accomplished all this with ease within just ten days of arriving, without tapping into a single resource from Shaman God Mountain.

Furthermore, his ultimate goal was to tangle with, and even slay, a terrifying Rank 7 expert.

Were all the Young Masters of Shaman God Mountain like this?

Were these the only types of people capable of being chosen?

Amidst his excitement, Zhang Lan found himself increasingly unable to see through this Li Qi, who had been calling him 'brother' with absolutely zero pretension.

However, Li Qi didn't seem to notice this shift in perspective. He just continued, "This is exactly why I asked you to come, Brother Zhang. Let's return to the situation in Vast Yang."

"At this moment, the Minister of War and Minister of State are still the official authorities. I strongly suspect these two identities are still monitoring the situation in Vast Yang, which is why I've mostly utilized underground factions recently, avoiding official detection."

"Therefore, I need an official figure, someone who has a voice in the light of day in Vast Yang. That person is you, Brother Zhang. As a swordmaster of the Righteous Sword Palace, you are chivalrous, open, and possess an impeccable standing. You are perfect for this task."

"Gather some Jianghu warriors, bring along some official forces—preferably the army—and make a trip to the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest!"

"I want you to help me—"

"Contact the Rat Soil Mound! Tell them the one who truly understands sacrifice is not Monk Chanzhi, but me."
