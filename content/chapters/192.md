# Chapter 192: ....

The first floor of the five-tiered Buddhist Pagoda.

A full sixteen hours had passed since Li Qi first arrived.

Li Qi was currently observing the one hundred and eight devils. His expression was utterly captivated, completely immersed and unable to extricate himself.

He had even forgotten the perilous situation he was in.

_Too... too wonderful._

The one hundred and eight devils each possessed different spell arts, cultivation methods, and Daos. Their divine abilities, forms, and focuses varied entirely.

It felt as though if he could just thoroughly study these one hundred and eight devils, he would be able to comprehend a path to the heavens!

Li Qi's curiosity and his desire to seek the Dao were thoroughly ignited by these devils.

He felt as if he had returned to his school days. He didn't need to care about scheming or office politics; he only needed to pour his entire mind into the problem before him to achieve the greatest sense of accomplishment and earn everyone's praise.

Li Qi had already studied his way to the fifty-second devil.

With only the ever-deepening profound mysteries in his eyes, he felt as though he had already comprehended the cycles of life and death across the six realms.

He advanced boldly and diligently, contemplating with perfect reason. With every secret and puzzle solved, his mind became a fraction clearer, and his sense of peaceful joy deepened. Yet, at the same time, his curiosity grew that much stronger.

_I really wish I could keep studying them forever._

_Forever._

_If I continue to delve deeper, upon the dependent origination of all things, I can truly comprehend the nature of false attachments, and thus truly understand all formless Daos._

_If I continue to delve deeper, and truly comprehend the nature of dependent origination, I can truly understand all defiled methods._

_If I continue to delve deeper, and truly comprehend the perfect, ultimate reality, I can truly understand all pure methods._

_If I continue to delve deeper, and truly comprehend the formless methods within the nature of dependent origination, I can extinguish all defiled methods. By extinguishing the defiled methods, I can attain the pure methods._

_Such is the root of virtue._

_To truly understand all formless methods, defiled methods, and pure methods. By truly understanding the formless methods, one extinguishes all defiled methods. By extinguishing all defiled methods, one attains all pure methods..._

A wave of enlightenment rose within Li Qi's heart.

If so, he would attain a fruition status, earning the title of Bodhisattva Skilful in All Dharma Natures.

As long as he continued to solve the puzzles before him, as long as he continued to uncover the true faces of these devils and understand their spell arts, cultivation methods, and Daos, then the great status was right before his eyes.

_Just keep going._

_Just keep going._

Time ticked by, second by second. Li Qi had even forgotten that time was passing; he had no idea how long it had been.

Seventy-five devils.

Eighty-two devils.

One hundred and three devils.

All the way to the end, the one hundred and eighth devil.

Li Qi walked up to this final devil.

As long as he deciphered this last one, he would be able to...

He would be able to break the very first devil.

With clear eyes, Li Qi stepped forward. His previously suppressed consciousness unfurled, and the True Knowledge dao rhythm unleashed its power at this very moment.

Talismanic script flickered in the surroundings as Li Qi pressed a hand against the devil statue.

Suddenly, the statue rapidly came to life!

It was a hideous devil radiating the stench of decay. Its gaunt body was covered in leathery, black skin. Behind its protruding ears, a massive, curved horn rose from the back of its skull. A mouth filled with jagged teeth took up half of its atrocious face.

Its hands and feet were long and slender. Where its sharp fingers pointed, an inexplicable energy rippled outward. All of Li Qi's prior curiosity and desire to pursue the Dao erupted violently in this instant!

The inner demons accumulated from one hundred and seven devils coalesced into an unprecedentedly terrifying force!

Streams of energy were forcibly extracted from Li Qi's nose and mouth.

His pupils instantly turned a dead, milky white, like cataracts.

His 'curiosity', his 'Dao Heart of pursuit', and his 'memories' were all being forcibly drawn out!

The energy streams gathered into a sphere within the devil's hands before it swallowed it in a single gulp.

And then—

The devil began to bleed from its nose. It poured out unstoppably. Its body convulsed, and it let out an agonizing screech.

A pain so profound that even this devil could not suppress it surged into its mind and engulfed its body!

Its skin cracked open as the excruciating pain skyrocketed. It was as if boiling water had been injected into its stomach, scalding its internal organs to mush! Then, molten iron was poured into its wounds, melting its muscles and viscera! It was as if it had been plunged into a sea of molten magma!

"Ah—!" Unable to hold back, it howled in agony, falling to its knees and tearing at its own skin!

But in the next instant.

The torment abruptly vanished.

Because the devil's head had exploded like a watermelon.

To the side, Li Qi took a deep breath, returning to normal.

He opened his eyes, a bitter smile on his face. "This was just one devil? And I still had to rely on its greed, letting it actively devour my dao rhythm until it literally gorged itself to death."

That was right. Out of one hundred and eight devils, he had spent an entire day and finally managed to decipher just one.

This also allowed him to master some Buddhist Sect spell arts.

And he had mastered them through his True Knowledge dao rhythm.

"The Desire-Mind Devil. It's not just a malicious thought; it can also devour my memories and stimulate the deepest desires within my heart. If not for the True Knowledge dao rhythm, I'm afraid I really would have perished here."

Li Qi shook his head and sighed.

If he wanted to find balance, he first needed to understand the specific situation.

Just moments before, by borrowing the True Knowledge dao rhythm—which had somehow returned to normal—Li Qi realized he could analyze these devils' weaknesses, their power, and the forces they represented.

Yes, the forces they represented.

These devils seemed to be nothing more than... tentacles.

Li Qi didn't know if this was the right way to describe it, but from the perspective of the True Knowledge dao rhythm, these devils were merely the extending tentacles of some great entity.

Who knew how many of such tentacles existed? They were all branches of that great entity.

To decipher these devils was to analyze that great entity itself.

Therefore, the difficulty was exceptionally high, and the danger was immense.

In analyzing the Desire-Mind Devil this time, Li Qi had risked his life, absorbing its devilish thoughts into his heart bit by bit before striking back at the very last moment.

Before that, he had genuinely been bewitched. He had truly sunk into a false sense of satisfaction, drowning in the accomplishment of having his thirst for knowledge fulfilled.

Even his mind was unable to resist it; he could only claim victory by relying on his dao rhythm as a trump card.

And there were a full one hundred and eight of such devils.

No wonder Monk Chanzhi had gone mad.

It must be known that these devils had long since been slain and devoured by Chanzhi.

He had used the method of heart-devouring to eat one hundred and eight devils.

The devils remaining here now were merely the devilish thoughts rooted within his mind.

Li Qi seemed to have defeated one, but in reality, there were still one hundred and eight devil statues here.

The devilish thoughts were deeply planted within Chanzhi's Buddhist heart. As long as Chanzhi lived, the devils would exist forever.

But if the devils died, Chanzhi would likely not survive either.

_What a difficult problem._

No wonder he had traveled all the way from the Western Buddhist Kingdom to Baiyue, just to seek the shamans' Dao of natural balance.

Monk Chanzhi likely had no other options left.

"This is truly... too difficult." Li Qi rubbed his temples.

His original plan was to decipher these devils to understand Monk Chanzhi himself.

Because these devilish thoughts had already merged into one with Chanzhi, if he could comprehend them all, not only would he accumulate a vast foundation of knowledge, but it would also be equivalent to seeing through Chanzhi's weaknesses.

When that time came, he would turn the tables and seize the initiative.

However, Li Qi had severely underestimated the difficulty of this task.

For Chanzhi to so confidently let Li Qi attempt this, he had probably anticipated this outcome from the start.

Li Qi simply lacked the capability to accomplish this in a short amount of time.

"Let's see, it's already been... sixteen hours. I should rest first." Li Qi shook his head and sat down. He pulled out some cured meat and dry rations from his mustard seed pouch, condensed water qi into droplets, and controlled the fire to bring it to a boil. He made a pot of cured meat porridge and made do with that for a meal.

After eating, he went to sleep.

Since there were still many days ahead and he had already arranged his backup plans, all that was left to do was to protect himself and wait for rescue.

Until then, he would just see what he could manage to do.

Li Qi went to sleep.

Time flowed on. Although there was an hourglass for keeping time in the pagoda, there was no sun or moon, so it didn't matter when he slept.

However, while he was sleeping soundly, Li Qi suddenly sensed that something was wrong.

His shaman's sixth sense was frantically buzzing!

Wave after wave of premonitions were urging him: _Wake up! Wake up!_

Li Qi obeyed his instincts without hesitation, snapping his eyes open.

But upon waking, he found that his surroundings had completely changed.

The floor beneath his feet was spongy, no longer solid ground, but rather...

The intestines of some creature?

The entire corridor had become a biological intestinal tract, slick with a thick, yellow-green substance. Whether it was gastric acid or something else, it reeked with a nauseating stench.

The scenery had transformed completely. The large, intestine-like passageway was rapidly squirming, and the yellow-green gas it emitted sent stinging jolts of pain across Li Qi's body.

He was being digested!

"Fuck!" Li Qi cursed out loud. With a wave of his hands, two streaks of metal qi instantly pierced out.

This time, there were no flashy golden threads.

The afterimages of the golden threads were originally caused by poor control. Now that he possessed a primal spirit, Li Qi's control over himself had reached a pinnacle. Therefore, the golden threads had vanished, but their speed had increased by a full thirty percent!

The metal qi tore through the organs, and Li Qi flew out from the creature's stomach.

Without him even realizing it, he had been eaten.

But the moment he emerged, he was greeted by a horrifying sight.

Indescribable, bizarre monsters in staggering numbers.

Low snarls and roars echoed from all directions, and the sound of sharp claws and scales scraping against the floor was exceptionally ear-piercing.

Flesh and blood splattered everywhere, rendering this Buddhist Pagoda absolutely sickening.

Limbs were strewn about, and blood-like plasma and slime coated everything. It looked as though a crawling infant had been playing around, carelessly splashing the monsters' bodily fluids on the ground like paint.

The one hundred and eight devils had come alive!

The one hundred and eight Arhat statues now possessed dim golden bodies, shrouded in an aura of decay. Their divine might was gone, and instead, they were being suppressed by the fiends!

At this moment, Li Qi stood right in the middle of the animated devils.

He had just torn through a fiend's stomach to escape.

As soon as he landed on the ground, he saw a full one hundred and seven devils glancing sideways at him.

Both sides stared at each other in stunned silence, exchanging wide-eyed looks.

Li Qi's face instantly fell.

Metal qi flashed!

Countless tentacles immediately lunged at him. Li Qi reacted with lightning speed, scrambling and tumbling out of the way. In a split second, he dodged dozens of strikes.

Simultaneously, his two streaks of metal qi began to spin, severing more than a dozen tentacles.

The monsters' shrieks echoed throughout the area!

The devils charged after Li Qi!

Li Qi's expression turned exceedingly bitter. The first floor of the Buddhist Pagoda was now coated in slime and reeked of death.

Any fool could see the slime was highly toxic. He couldn't stay here any longer.

He continuously slashed the devils to pieces, and mustard-colored green blood sprayed from their wounds.

Li Qi could see countless mounds of flesh all around him. Massive and bloated, they possessed no humanoid form; they were just spheres of meat completely covered in human faces.

Li Qi's attacks seemed to sting them, causing the faces on the meat spheres to wail, creating a deeply terrifying sight.

With the monsters howling incessantly, Li Qi desperately weaved through them to run for his life, looking thoroughly wretched.

_The second floor! My only target is the second floor!_

_Only the second floor can save me!_

Even though Chanzhi had subdued these devils when he was still at Rank 9, meaning their strength was capped at Rank 9, Li Qi could only manage to fight and scrape by against one or two of them.

One hundred and eight of them? He would be beaten to a pulp.

_I'd just be waiting for death!_

_The only path to survival is the second floor!_

The second floor held Chanzhi's Bodhisattva heart, cultivated only after he suppressed these one hundred and eight devils.

_Only that place is safe!_

Fortunately, while the Buddhist Pagoda was large, it had its limits. It was only about one or two hundred meters across.

Li Qi was already considered an expert among Rank 9 cultivators. Under the protection of his metal qi, he rapidly carved a path through the devils' siege and charged up to the second floor.

The entire escape took only a few seconds, yet Li Qi had nearly perished several times.

Over a hundred devils had displayed their various divine abilities, nearly pinning Li Qi down and killing him on the spot.

"Damn it... Thank goodness I didn't cripple my Martial Dao cultivation, or I really would have died there." Having survived the ordeal, Li Qi looked down at the scene below with relief.

Down below, it was still a terrifying scene of a hundred ghosts walking in the night.

But the second floor was a scene of peace and serenity, radiating with brilliant, precious light.

"This Buddhist Pagoda is the manifestation of Monk Chanzhi's cultivation... Looking at this, Chanzhi's situation is truly severe," Li Qi muttered to himself.

To think that at night, this Buddhist Pagoda would experience an inversion where the hundred ghosts suppressed the Arhats!

Was the next step going to be the hundred ghosts devouring this Bodhisattva Hall?

Just as this thought popped into Li Qi's mind, he saw the Buddhist light on the second floor flicker.

Then, the Bodhisattva statue began to weep tears of blood.

The pure white Bodhisattva statue became a mottled mix of red and white.

The surrounding Buddhist light began to flicker madly, like a broken lightbulb.

Li Qi couldn't help but shudder violently! Cold sweat poured down his back.

He hurriedly picked up his pace, ascending four flights of stairs in a row to return to the fifth floor.

_None of the floors below are likely safe!_

However, the moment he reached the fifth floor.

Monk Chanzhi had vanished.

In his place sat a cross-legged devil god.

Its terrifying, oily body heaved in the air. Countless tentacles, covered in hooked suckers, had replaced its lower half.

Every single one of these writhing, maggot-like tentacles bore a human face. While the faces were twisted with cruelty, they surprisingly held a trace of lucidity.

Now, this devil god stared down at the approaching Li Qi with pure malice.

Li Qi froze in place, his hair standing on end as his heart skipped a beat.

_I'm screwed._
