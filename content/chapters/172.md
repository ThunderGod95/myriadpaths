# Chapter 172: Obsession

Upon reaching the fifth floor, all anomalies vanished.

Here, it truly became an ordinary wooden tower, devoid of the things that had made Li Qi feel so small.

Yet, this wooden tower was decorated with extreme luxury. Its four sides were adorned with gold and jade, agarwood served as canopies, glossy ganoderma covered the walls, and the caisson ceiling was crafted from powdered fungus. Hundreds of flower buds hung from above, while below sat many clay sculptures.

Clay sculptures of baldachins, pipas, devil-subduing pestles, nine-ringed staffs, flowing cloud trays, many-jeweled vases, pagoda bells, lotus petals, bumpa vases, dragon-patterned pearl plates, precious banners, square flags, damaru drums, and kapala bowls.

Not a single one was genuine. They were all fakes, molded from clay, painted, and then baked.

Beneath the numerous clay items sat a Buddhist throne, complete with a shrine, a throne seat, and a precious bed.

Enshrined within was a Buddha statue. It was less than a foot tall, exceedingly small.

However, despite its small size, it was crafted with a vast array of meticulous materials. The materials Li Qi could identify alone included animal glue, azurite powder, alum, ochre, cinnabar, realgar, orpiment, gamboge, rouge, red gold, and pure gold.

All these materials had been ground into powder and made into a slurry to paint the skin of the Buddha statue, rendering it so lifelike it resembled a real person. The skin looked as though it genuinely possessed a soft, tender touch.

The headband, floral crown, earrings, bracelets, and tassels on the Buddha statue were incomparably exquisite, appearing completely real.

Colorful murals surrounded the shrine, all painted in gold lacquer. The main subjects of the murals were the roaring lion, elephant, divine horse, divine mule, divine ox, foreign figures, Arhats, Bodhisattvas, and clerics.

The painting depicted a scene of harmonious coexistence among all living beings. Centered around the clerics, all things were in harmony, set amidst landscapes, trees, bridges, colorful clouds, and terrain.

Spreading outward from the edges of the many divine beasts, one could see encircling tigers, leopards, bears, dogs, sheep, wolves, cranes, orioles, and parrots, gradually expanding outward until the mural ended.

The entire shrine was six feet wide, two feet deep, and three feet high. In front of the shrine stood two people.

One sat cross-legged with an open scripture placed before him. His eyes were closed, his expression intoxicated and completely engrossed, and a golden light flowed from his body.

This person wore an official's uniform; it seemed he hadn't even found the time to change clothes before impatiently sitting here.

The other person stood. He wore white robes adorned with cloud patterns. With an elegant and free demeanor, he held a scripture in his hand, carefully reading something.

This standing man was exceptionally handsome, so much so that Li Qi felt somewhat eclipsed in his presence. That white attire accentuated his peerless temperament. Yet, judging by the style, this did not look like the attire of the Hundred Forests Kingdom, nor did it even resemble the clothing of Baiyue.

The pendants, cloud patterns, tailoring, and so forth on his body were all styles Li Qi had never seen before.

It was just undeniably pleasing to the eye.

Coupled with the man's striking appearance, it gave one the feeling of a refreshing breeze brushing past their face.

Compared to the middle-aged man in official robes who was entranced, nearly dreaming, and completely oblivious to Li Qi's arrival, this young, elegant young master immediately put away his book the moment he saw Li Qi.

"Ah, to think someone could pass through the four floors below. How interesting. Who are you?" The elegant young master put the book away, placed it on the altar table in front of the shrine, and walked toward Li Qi.

As soon as he approached, Li Qi felt an intense, oppressive pressure.

It was like...

Like what?

Li Qi couldn't describe it.

But he felt a sense of restraint. When facing this person, he was subconsciously constrained.

It was as if, when a normal person was alone, they would feel very comfortable and might even be unkempt.

But if they were speaking at a very important meeting, unless they truly possessed nerves of steel, they would likely feel somewhat restrained. They would pay attention to their posture and their words.

That was the feeling.

He felt as though he was subconsciously straightening his back and holding his head high.

This was a very bad feeling, causing Li Qi's expression to change. He channeled his Martial Dao internal qi, forcefully breaking free, and intentionally messed up his hair and collar.

His hair had originally been tied up, but pulling it loose left it disheveled. In doing so, the 'chaos' on his body outweighed the 'order,' and that feeling of restraint instantly vanished into thin air.

Despite breaking through this restraint, Li Qi's heart remained full of vigilance.

"And you are?" Li Qi subtly took two steps back, staring at the other party cautiously.

"I am Wang Boyan. My apologies for startling you. I was merely a bit astonished to see someone capable of passing through the four floors below," the elegant young master replied.

The white-robed young man who called himself Wang Boyan stood before Li Qi, his face full of curiosity. "On the four floors below, the Arhat Hall observes whether your mind is pure. The Bodhisattva Hall observes whether you bear karma. The Hall of the Three Buddhas tests your intellect and patience. Only the scripture section is truly without defense, though it can barely be considered a test of willpower."

He continuously evaluated Li Qi. "Walking all the way up without triggering any anomalies indicates that you harbor no baleful qi or stray thoughts in your heart, bear no karma from past or future lives, and possess the intellect and endurance to withstand the illumination of the Buddhist light. Furthermore, you do not covet the scriptures. To accomplish all of this, if you are not a natural-born Son of Buddha, you must certainly be an outstanding talent. Judging by your appearance, you don't look like someone in a high position. You have calluses on your hands, and though your clothes are clean, your spine is slightly curved. You must practice martial arts and have done heavy manual labor; you don't have the look of someone pampered."

"I'm guessing you were previously of low status, then seized an opportunity to rise up using your own talent, but your cultivation base hasn't improved much yet. Therefore, you are both an outstanding talent and a Son of Buddha. Brother, you possess root of wisdom," Wang Boyan sighed in admiration as he looked at Li Qi.

Li Qi sweated in embarrassment, but he had serious business to attend to. So, instead of continuing that line of conversation, he asked Wang Boyan, "You must be joking. I came here with a mission and have no time for jests. May I ask if the person over here is the Vast Yang Minister of War?"

As he asked, he pointed at the middle-aged man who was currently intoxicated by the Buddhist scriptures.

But Li Qi didn't let his guard down. He simultaneously observed this person.

This person also seemed to be Rank 9?

But what Dao path was he cultivating? Li Qi hadn't seen it before. He didn't seem like a martial artist, lacked the ghost-god qi of a shaman, and looked even less like someone from the Daoist Sects.

Yet, that feeling of imposing restraint upon others felt entirely familiar to Li Qi.

Fortunately, the other party was only Rank 9, so this restraint couldn't truly suppress him.

"Exactly, he is the Vast Yang Minister of War. However, he has already drowned himself in the Buddhist Dharma and lost all interest in the mundane world. I'm afraid you won't be able to wake him up right now," Wang Boyan told Li Qi.

"Won't be able to wake him?" Li Qi's expression sank. Ignoring Wang Boyan, he walked straight up to the Vast Yang Minister of War and gave him a push.

But with a gentle push, the man remained sitting cross-legged, his expression infatuated, ignoring Li Qi entirely.

Seeing this, Li Qi used a significant amount of strength and shoved hard.

This time, however, the golden light protecting the man's body burst forth with a repulsive force, neutralizing Li Qi's pushing force. He couldn't move him at all.

Li Qi took a deep breath, channeled his Carp Stream Intent, swung his fist, and erupted with tens of thousands of catties of tremendous force, smashing down with a punch!

But with this strike, the golden light erupted outright. Caught off guard, Li Qi was directly shoved away by the golden light, stumbling back over a dozen steps until he hit the edge of the wooden tower and finally stopped.

"You see, I told you, I'm afraid you can't wake him up," Wang Boyan said as he watched from the side.

"How could this be? Having a golden light for protection isn't an everyday occurrence." Uninjured, Li Qi pushed off the wall, his expression grave.

This was bad. What was going on here?

Having a golden light protecting the body while reading scriptures meant that he had deployed it himself. Usually, it signified that someone had just entered a crucial stage of cultivation and thus set up a layer of defense to prevent others from disturbing them.

In other words, the Vast Yang Minister of War was actively unwilling to be disturbed and had proactively shielded himself from the outside world.

"What urgent matter do you have?" Wang Boyan asked curiously.

But Li Qi didn't have the time to pay attention to him. Could this person sign for him on behalf of the Vast Yang Minister of War?

So Li Qi simply stood up. "There is a military emergency. Please forgive me for being unable to disclose the details. Farewell."

With that said, he immediately set off, heading downstairs without stopping.

He was going to find the monk Chanzhi.

This Heavenly Peace Temple was his territory, so naturally, such matters required seeking out the monk Chanzhi. If even the monk Chanzhi couldn't do it, then he would probably have to immediately return to the city and seek out the Vast Yang Prefect to make a decision.

Running all the way down the Buddhist Pagoda, Li Qi only felt that this was absurd.

Absurd, utterly absurd!

A state's Minister of War wasn't attending to his proper duties but had run here to comprehend the Buddhist Dharma, and he even acted as if he had reaped a great harvest.

If he really wanted to specialize in these things, he should just resign!

Occupying a position without doing any work; perhaps the chaos at the front lines was also due to him.

Neglecting his duties in such an important position was practically treating human lives like grass.

Li Qi ran all the way down, only to see the monk Chanzhi sweeping the floor.

He stepped forward and proactively said to Chanzhi, "Master Chanzhi, the Vast Yang Minister of War is drowned in the Buddhist Dharma and ignores the mundane world. Could you lend a hand to help me awaken him? I truly have crucial military intelligence. It's a matter of life and death concerning over a hundred thousand people. Along my journey, I've heard that the Master is a kind and good person who normally wouldn't hesitate to save even a single individual. Surely, you won't sit idly by and watch over a hundred thousand lives fall into peril?"

Hearing this, Chanzhi raised his head. "Almsgiver, could you tell this poor monk a bit more about the matter you speak of? If I do not know the specifics, I'm afraid it will be difficult to judge whether I should assist you."

Li Qi considered for a moment, then said, "The border pass has been attacked by fiends, prompting an emergency evacuation. Over a hundred thousand people need to migrate out, but the military rations are insufficient. We need Vast Yang City to allocate grain, yet all funds and provisions require the approval of Lord Minister of War. At this moment, he has sunken into cultivation, but the military situation is urgent. I beseech the Master for your assistance."

While Li Qi spoke, he continuously scrutinized Chanzhi. To be honest, he didn't quite trust this monk.

But unexpectedly, Chanzhi was incredibly receptive to Li Qi's words.

After hearing Li Qi out, he replied with equal urgency, "So that's how it is. It is indeed an urgent matter. This poor monk will go help you awaken him right away."

Then, he led Li Qi swiftly up the stairs.

Upon reaching the upper floor, he quickly stepped forward and gave the Vast Yang Minister of War a push. "Almsgiver Qian, Almsgiver Qian, wake up quickly. Wake up quickly."

The Vast Yang Minister of War's golden light burst forth once more, but this time, it was directly broken apart by Chanzhi, who then gave him a hard shove.

"Who is it!!?" The Vast Yang Minister of War flew into a rage and stood straight up.

Li Qi and Wang Boyan's expressions changed. The two closed their eyes simultaneously and fled toward the door.

The fury of a Rank 7 powerhouse wasn't something these two Rank 9s even had the right to look at. If they weren't careful, taking an extra glance could directly shatter their hearts and gallbladders.

It wouldn't kill them, but a massive loss in cultivation base was a certainty.

But before they could run, they saw the monk Chanzhi press his palms together and softly chant a Buddhist moniker.

Amidst the Buddhist resonance, golden light manifested beneath his feet.

Myriad wondrous lotus flowers, colored like Jambudvipa gold, spun like wheels of fire. A great, brilliant radiance transformed into countless precious canopies and banners.

Then came lotuses, perfectly round with exactly twelve petals, playing the voice of the Dharma. The subtle and wondrous sounds preached the paramitas of suffering, emptiness, impermanence, and non-self, surging with a subtle, golden brilliance. Every petal of the lotuses shimmered with the colors of a hundred treasures, possessing eighty-four thousand veins like a heavenly painting. Each vein emitted eighty-four thousand rays of light, clear and distinct, visible to all.

There were also musical instruments suspended in the void, like heavenly precious canopies, sounding without being struck. Among this chorus of voices, all spoke of mindfulness of the Buddha, mindfulness of the Dharma, and mindfulness of the Sangha.

This illusion flashed past in an instant. Then, the Vast Yang Minister of War's anger completely vanished. His mind clear, he wore a look of shame. "I lost my composure. Master, may I ask why you awakened me?"

"Good man, this is not the fault of sentient beings; it is the blame of afflictions. The wise observe thoroughly and do not direct their wrath at sentient beings. Naturally, I have no reason to blame you. However, to constantly stir up anger allows filth to fill the heart, obscuring the pure mind necessary for achieving sainthood and preventing one from seeing all things clearly," Chanzhi said to the Vast Yang Minister of War, his hands pressed together.

"Qian Bin has received your teachings. However, I wonder what matter the Master awakened me for?" The Vast Yang Minister of War calmed down and asked.

Seeing this, Chanzhi finally turned around and looked at Li Qi and the other, who had just been dodging. "Almsgiver Qian has been awakened. Please, almsgiver, quickly explain the situation. This poor monk will excuse himself for a moment."

Having spoken, the monk Chanzhi prepared to exit the fifth floor.

Li Qi hurriedly offered his thanks. "Thank you, Master, for your assistance, and thank you for your protection."

"Many thanks for the protection." Wang Boyan also nodded in gratitude.

If Chanzhi hadn't intervened earlier to block the fury, the two of them would have definitely suffered a mishap here.

"There is no need. Does Almsgiver Wang wish to head down together with this poor monk? After all, Almsgiver Qian and this Almsgiver Li seem to have an important matter to discuss," the monk Chanzhi said.

"That's fine. I'll head down with the Master then."

Upon hearing this sentence, however, Li Qi took a deep breath.

He hadn't told Chanzhi his name.

How did Chanzhi know? And to call it out so casually?

But he didn't point this out. Instead, he watched the other party leave, then closed the door. He walked up to the Vast Yang Minister of War and, under the other party's puzzled gaze, said, "Lord Minister of War, urgent military intelligence!"

After saying that, without waiting for the Vast Yang Minister of War to reply, he dumped the entire situation regarding the White Rhino Army and the migration of over a hundred thousand commoners.

Next, he brought out his token and military banner, along with the official document he had prepared in advance. "I beseech Lord Minister of War to sign and stamp your seal, so that I may go and withdraw the rations."

"Just for this? You came to disturb my quiet cultivation for this? Complete nonsense! In the future, seek out the Minister of State for matters like this. There's no need to come to me!" The Vast Yang Minister of War seemed furious but barely suppressed his anger. With a sweeping stroke of his brush and the stamp of his seal, he waved his hand to dismiss Li Qi, while he himself picked up the Buddhist scripture once more.

Seeing this, Li Qi sighed but said nothing more.

He headed straight downstairs and bid farewell to Chanzhi.

Wang Boyan seemed to want to strike up a conversation with him, but Li Qi didn't have the spare time. He gave a few simple, perfunctory replies, then quickly descended the mountain, mounted Old Ma, and rushed toward the military ration warehouse.
