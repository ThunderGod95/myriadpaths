# Chapter 102: Investigation

It was already spring. Over in Li Prefecture, the snow had long since melted, giving way to flourishing growth and abundant vitality.

The same could not be said for the vicinity of White Snake Mountain. While the spring breeze had indeed begun to stir the earth below, the mountain itself remained blanketed in pristine white snow.

This was only the foot of the mountain, where the thaw was simply delayed, usually melting away by late spring or early summer. As for the higher peaks, it was said the snow never melted year-round, forever coiled around the summit like a white snake.

In the bleak chill of the evening, as night descended, the air turned bitingly cold. Even taking a breath induced a dull ache in the head, as if the wind itself carried hidden needles.

Li Qi inhaled deeply and exhaled, his eyes unconsciously tracking the plume of white vapor. In an instant, the frosty wind scattered it into nothingness; the gales here seemed to blow without end.

He did not return to sleep right away. Instead, he found a water source and began his cultivation. He practiced the Carp Stream Intent within the water, simultaneously absorbing the water qi and refining it into internal qi through his movements.

Cultivation required relentless daily effort; it could not be neglected simply because they were traveling.

The temperature was freezing, and the mountain winds howled fiercely.

Yet, even in such harsh conditions, traces of human life endured. Occasional mountain-harvesting platoons could be seen passing by, and the smoke from village cooking fires drifted in the distance, though there was scarcely any lamplight.

After cultivating deep into the night, Li Qi dressed himself and made his way back to his wagon.

His living conditions were far better than those of most Jing family members; at the very least, he had a private ox cart.

Due to a shortage of carts, some of the Jing family had to pitch tents outside, enduring a far more bitter cold.

He returned to his cart and carefully pulled open the door.

Shen Shuibi was already asleep.

The rabbit was curled up on the bedding. As the cold wind from the open door brushed against her, she twitched as if startled, sniffled, and shrank into an even tighter ball. However, she didn't wake up at all, continuing to sleep soundly.

_Wasn't she immune to heat and cold? Does she still feel the chill?_

Li Qi felt a twinge of confusion but didn't wake her. He quickly shut the door and lay down himself.

Having already washed himself clean at the water source during his cultivation, he went straight to sleep.

Staring at the roof of the cart, he took a slow, deep breath and glanced over at Shen Shuibi.

The rabbit had kicked off her blanket. _Probably a natural talent,_ he thought. _The rabbit's kick._

Reaching out, Li Qi gently pulled the blanket back up over her.

As he did so, he could feel its warm, soft texture. It exuded a faint, fragrant scent from the expensive essential oils sprinkled over it to ward off mold and insects.

Li Qi had purchased these oils directly from the Jing family caravan, and Shen Shuibi was quite fond of them.

_Right, time to sleep._

The remainder of the journey went smoothly. With two Rank 9 experts serving as escorts, they were unlikely to encounter any danger in these parts. The four days passed in a flash, and Li Qi received his payment of four gold pieces.

After helping the Jing family caravan load all their goods onto the cargo ships, the family members bid Li Qi farewell. Jing Yan and Manager Jing also expressed their gratitude for the guidance Li Qi and Shen Shuibi had provided.

After exchanging pleasantries, Manager Jing cupped his hands and said, "Warrior Li, if you and your sister ever have the chance, you must visit the Song Kingdom. We will certainly treat you with the utmost hospitality."

"We will definitely come if the opportunity arises. I wish you and Big Sister Jing a safe journey. Make sure not to act impulsively again, Big Sister, lest you find yourselves fleeing in the dead of night once more," Li Qi joked.

Jing Yan gave a cold snort and didn't reply, but judging by her attitude, she probably wouldn't hold back if a similar situation occurred in the future.

Following their goodbyes, Li Qi watched as their ship set sail. It would travel via the Li River, leaving White Snake Prefecture for Li Prefecture, before continuing along the river all the way back to the Song Kingdom. The vessel was loaded to the brim with mountain goods and treasures from White Snake Mountain—items entirely absent in the Song Kingdom. This single trip would undoubtedly yield a massive profit.

As the ship departed, Li Qi turned to look at Shen Shuibi. "Miss Shen, let's go! We're heading back. Old Ma should be just about rested and fattened up by now. We'll head back and set off once more!"

"Mhm!" Shen Shuibi perked up the moment hitting the road was mentioned.

The two immediately turned around, preparing to trek back to Snake Tail County on foot. Li Qi waved his hand, and Shen Shuibi immediately understood, leaping onto his back so he could carry her.

Ox carts were slow and couldn't match a person's pace.

Even carrying Shen Shuibi, a journey that would take an ox cart four days would take him two days at most to traverse.

Li Qi carried the rabbit, and the rabbit carried the bamboo basket. In this bizarre formation, the two set off swiftly on their return journey to Snake Tail County.

---

Almost at the exact same time.

In Snake Tail County, back at the inn with the stables where Li Qi had previously rented a room.

"Sir... this, this is the horse of that, that person," the innkeeper stammered, sweating profusely as he continuously bowed and saluted the constable standing before him.

"Eight feet at the shoulder, a coat of pure black. A fine steed. I wonder which noble family's scion could afford to ride such a magnificent horse? Did he ever mention it?" The constable cast a sidelong glance, questioning the innkeeper casually.

The innkeeper, however, dared not answer casually. He hurriedly cupped his hands and replied, "I never heard him mention it, but he was accompanied by a woman of extraordinary beauty. I imagine she is not a concubine that any ordinary household could afford to keep."

"Extraordinary beauty? Just how beautiful? How old is she? Did she have any accessories on her? What material were her clothes made of?" the constable pressed.

"As beautiful as a celestial maiden, with skin as tender as mutton-fat jade. Her face looked as if it were glowing. However, her accessories were a bit shabby; she wore only a single copper hairpin. Her clothes were made of cotton, but on her, they draped like silk and gossamer, giving her an incredibly ethereal air," the innkeeper answered truthfully without hesitation.

The constable frowned, suddenly uncertain of how to proceed.

A towering stallion, a beautiful concubine, and a Rank 9 cultivation base himself—no matter how one looked at it, he had all the makings of a noble scion. Someone who could not be easily offended.

Yet, he dressed plainly and traveled without an entourage.

What kind of logic was that?

_Perhaps his cultivation method has certain taboos? Are there precepts he must strictly observe?_ the constable guessed internally.

That was a possibility.

But if that were the case, why would he associate with the Jing family over a bit of money? Did he truly go to all that trouble just for a few pieces of gold?

One possibility was that he actually had no money and perhaps no status at all; the beautiful concubine and the horse were simply picked up along the way.

But that was highly unlikely, to the point it could be entirely ruled out.

Then, the second possibility was that he knew about the conflict between the Jing family and the Assistant Magistrate, and intentionally went to help.

But if so, what was his reason for leaving the horse behind?

A warning?

Intimidation?

Or was there some other reason, like him being completely oblivious?

The horse was emaciated and still recovering its weight, clearly having just endured a long journey. Did this have anything to do with the man's goals?

_Too many suspicious points..._

_Well... best not to alert the snake in the grass just yet._

He decided he would report this information to the Assistant Magistrate and let him make the decision. It wasn't his place to jump to conclusions.

With this thought, the constable coughed dryly. "Then take good care of it. I won't disturb your business any longer. Remember to pay your taxes on time; no delays."

The innkeeper hastily agreed, then respectfully saw the constable out of the establishment.

Afterward, he let out a long sigh of relief, feeling somewhat puzzled.

Usually, these constables couldn't be bothered to lift a finger. Why had one come knocking on his door today?
