# Chapter 143: Jin Buhuan

Clear Mist City, within the most luxurious mansion in the center of the city.

It covered a massive area, practically a city within Clear Mist City. The surrounding doors and windows resembled city battlements. Its light illuminated the sky, attracting crowds of onlookers. Spanning three to four li in circumference, it looked like a grand palace from afar.

Inside, there were flying eaves and painted beams, exquisite from every angle. Looking from a distance, one could see countless masts and the smoke of a thousand households. Even the servants' quarters spanned five or six square li, densely packed with buildings that housed around a thousand people.

In other directions, it was even more spacious. Flowers and bamboo formed lush canopies. There were over ten square ponds, the water so clear that the swimming fish could be counted. Each pond was about thirty acres, planted with thousand-petal lotuses and surrounded by weeping willows, interspersed with peaches and plums. Occasionally, there were fragrant plums, green paulownias, and weeping willows, all contrasting beautifully among the fresh blooms and ancient trees. Even in winter, the scenery remained vibrant and magnificent.

Looking elsewhere, there were paths lined with clusters of chrysanthemums, deep stone corridors, and swirling pools. The hall's front courtyard could seat a thousand people, boasting the beauty of pavilions over water and the glory of ten thousand flowers.

Pavilions and terraces were partially hidden, their crimson and emerald colors fresh and bright. Entering the gates, the fragrant ponds were like mirrors, and outstanding pavilions stood at varying heights. With green locust trees lining the paths, it felt exactly like stepping into the pavilions of an immortal mountain a thousand miles away.

And this place was Jin Buhuan's mansion.

His father, the mountain god of the Green Mountain, was a White Tiger born atop the Green Mountain.

The White Tiger was not a tiger of wild beasts, but an innate exotic breed similar to the Jade Rabbit and the Golden Crow. It was formed from metal qi, a spirit of slaughter and conquest.

Aside from slaughter, the White Tiger also possessed the ability to gather wealth.

As the saying went: _When the White Tiger holds power, it gains momentum. Sitting upon the noble seals of wealth and authority, the day master must be supported by vital qi. Or, if authority is prosperous and timely aided, even without visible wealth or authority, using authority ensures nobility, and using wealth ensures riches._

A poem stated: _When the White Tiger holds the world, Yin and Mao are strong. If it descends upon the realms of Si, Wu, Wei, or Xu, encountering it in the four wilds brings great wealth and nobility. It is destined to become a pillar of the imperial capital._[^1]

Because the White Tiger belonged to metal, and metal itself was a symbol of wealth and nobility.

These aspects roughly summarized the general nature of the mountain god of the Green Mountain.

Cold, harboring profound killing intent, and immensely wealthy.

Jin Buhuan, who had inherited his father's trait of wealth, was currently standing in the great hall.

In front of him hung a massive topographical map, seemingly depicting the entirety of the Green Mountain Kingdom.

Looking at this map, the territory of the Green Mountain Kingdom was shaped like a dumbbell. Both the northern and southern regions were vast, while the passage in the middle was extremely narrow.

And this middle section was precisely the hub connecting the north and south, comprising an entire mountain range.

Without a doubt, the range connecting the north and south was the Green Mountain.

Clear Mist City was located about two thousand li away from the Green Mountain, serving as the transportation hub for the entire Green Mountain Kingdom.

Jin Buhuan stood with his hands behind his back, his expression gloomy as he faced this enormous map. He listened to the intelligence personnel behind him continuously report the information they had gathered.

After listening, he turned around and tapped the table. "So, aside from his address, you haven't managed to find out a single piece of information?"

Although his tone was calm, the servants behind him immediately knelt down and lowered their heads. "Replying to the young master, there is one more thing we haven't reported yet! It's... it's that Li Qi is also raising a dragon colt. According to an equine appraiser who saw it, this horse possesses the potential to transform into a dragon."

Jin Buhuan smacked his lips, somewhat dissatisfied. "The potential to transform into a dragon? Tsk, he really has all the good things. Something to resist divination... something to stop curses, and even such a fine horse. Yet he casts it aside like worn shoes, raising it only on ordinary meals, and hasn't even sought out a cultivation method for it?"

"Yes... The appraiser was wringing his wrists and sighing at the time. If it had been nurtured well previously, this dragon colt would definitely be able to transform into a dragon. Reaching Rank 9 would be a certainty, and even Rank 8 wouldn't be out of the question," the servant said with his head bowed.

The room fell silent for a moment.

Jin Buhuan sank into deep thought, while the servants below kept their foreheads pressed to the floor, trembling with fear.

At this very moment, this young nobleman showed none of his usual frivolity. Instead, he possessed the profound depth and majesty of a ruler.

After a while, he waved his hand. "Alright, you may step down."

The subordinates felt as if they had been granted a great pardon. They hastily stood up, placed their hands on their knees, bent their waists, lowered their heads, and slowly backed out the door.

As for Jin Buhuan, he stood up and began to pace back and forth across the room.

At this time, the maid from outside walked in.

She still had that same cold and aloof appearance. She was extremely beautiful, with a jade flute hanging from her waist. Her temperament was outstanding, yet she was incredibly inconspicuous.

Yes, truly inconspicuous. If she didn't speak or move, most people would probably subconsciously ignore her existence.

However, she now took the initiative to speak. With a slightly puzzled expression, she asked, "Young Master, since you are so wary... then, why not strike first and gain the upper hand?"

As she spoke, she made a downward chopping motion with her hand.

Jin Buhuan quickly reverted to his previous frivolous and arrogant second-generation heir persona. With a snap, he opened his folding fan. "Strike first to gain the upper hand? Hehe, that doesn't align with my Dao."

The maid frowned, her eyes filled with confusion.

"What? Looking down on me?" He swayed his folding fan and lay down on a reclining chair.

"The sages of the Human Dao have said that even thieves have their Dao. As the so-called Dao of Thieves goes: Deducing what treasures lie at the destination is the sagehood of a thief; being the first to enter and fight is the courage of a thief; being the last to leave and covering the rear is the righteousness of a thief; knowing where one can strike and where one cannot is the wisdom of a thief; dividing the spoils evenly afterwards is the benevolence of a thief."

"Even when acting as a thief, one must follow the Dao of Thieves and achieve sagehood, courage, righteousness, wisdom, and benevolence. If one lacks these five, they cannot become a great thief, only a petty burglar. If even thieves have their Dao, where in the world is there no Dao? As a second-generation heir, could I possibly not have my own Dao?"

The maid looked on with great interest, covering her lips with one hand, her eyes sparkling. She was somewhat curious about this concept of 'where in the world is there no Dao'.

She knew the phrase meant, 'Where could there possibly be no Dao?'

But she didn't know how her usually eloquent young master planned to explain his Dao as a second-generation heir.

Because it sounded utterly ridiculous.

Seeing the maid's expression, Jin Buhuan laughed and explained, "Haha, you don't understand this, do you? The Dao is a standard, a rule. The Dao between heaven and earth is the law by which all things operate. The Dao within a person is the principle one must follow as a human being."

"All things in the universe operate according to the Dao, which is how heaven and earth function stably without falling into disorder. When people act according to the Dao, they avoid falling into chaotic and perverse situations."

"Since I am a second-generation heir, naturally, I cannot deviate from this rule."

"Making my elders believe that I can inherit their wealth without causing trouble is the sagehood of a second-generation heir."

"To protect my right to inherit property, fearlessly facing the consequences when a threat appears and decisively strangling it in the cradle is the courage of a second-generation heir."

"Fulfilling my responsibilities as a second-generation heir and defending the wealth I am destined to inherit is the righteousness of a second-generation heir."

"Understanding what poses a threat to my inheritance and knowing what actions can increase my chances of inheriting my father's wealth is the wisdom of a second-generation heir."

"Rationally planning and managing the wealth I inherit, not squandering it recklessly, and using it methodically is the benevolence of a second-generation heir."

"The 'sagehood, courage, righteousness, wisdom, and benevolence' of a second-generation heir... As long as one achieves these five points, they can be considered a qualified second-generation heir. They will absolutely be able to inherit their elders' wealth without any accidents occurring."

Speaking up to here, he leaned back in his chair and sighed. "If one cannot achieve these five points, then the status of a second-generation heir will only invite disaster."

"Without sagehood, one will be abandoned by their elders and family, losing this status."

"Without courage, one becomes indecisive and will sooner or later be eliminated by others in the competition."

"Without righteousness, one lacks the big picture. Not knowing what principles a second-generation heir must uphold, they will definitely suffer a great loss for a small gain in the end."

"Without wisdom, not knowing friend from foe and lacking a sense of priority, they will inevitably perform actions that harm themselves to benefit others."

"Without benevolence, the inherited wealth won't last long before being completely squandered, turning into nothing more than a pipe dream."

He sat up from the reclining chair and looked up at the sky. "Isn't it precisely because my two older brothers didn't follow the Dao of the Second-Generation Heir that I was able to surpass them? Now, I am the liaison between the Green Mountain and Clear Mist City, while they are forced to stay home and act as guards for the gold mine."

"You see me having fun and playing around all the time, but when has my leisure ever violated those five principles? It's precisely because I strictly remember and follow the Dao of the Second-Generation Heir that I can sit securely in this position. Even the Rank 7 Sect Master of the Clear Mist Sect must treat me with courtesy, and Rank 8 cultivators are willing to work for me."

"This is the benefit of following the Dao," Jin Buhuan said with a beaming smile.

Hearing this, the maid put away her previous expression and looked at her young master seriously. Her expression carried a hint of surprise, as if she were knowing him for the first time.

"Let me analyze it further for you. Just listen," Jin Buhuan said. Having sat up from his chair, he faced the expansive courtyard outside, his demeanor relaxed. "I have absolutely no need to clash with Li Qi, do I?"

"First, he poses no threat to my inheritance of the family business. If we don't clash, he and I will most likely just be passersby in each other's lives. If I provoke him out of a momentary fit of anger and needlessly make an enemy, it would instead give my two brothers an opportunity. Blindly making enemies means failing to distinguish friend from foe and lacking a sense of priority. This is known as lacking wisdom."

"Second, he is also someone with a background. A minor conflict with him has already left Lord Yan severely injured. The medical treatment alone cost three days' worth of tax revenue from Clear Mist City. How many 'three days' are there in a year? If I really kill him, although it would vent my anger, what about my wealth? What about the dead servants? This is a failure to cherish one's wealth, which is known as lacking benevolence."

"As a typical second-generation heir, how could I do something so lacking in benevolence and wisdom?"

"Therefore, not only will I not make an enemy out of him, but I will also send people to his door with compensation to let this matter pass. It would be best if nothing else happens and we just end things here," Jin Buhuan said.

The maid nodded to show she understood. However, right after, she asked in confusion, "I see. But why has the young master said so much today? Usually, you would just act without explaining."

Hearing this, Jin Buhuan tilted his head back and laughed loudly. He lay back down on the rocking chair, swaying his body. He pulled the maid into his arms and laughed, "Haha, saying all of this to you today is what I call 'sagehood'!"

---

After Jin Buhuan spent several hours indulging in daytime debauchery.

The maid washed her body, adjusted her clothes, and quietly left to return to her own room, shutting the door behind her.

After confirming the door was closed, she formed hand seals and silently chanted until the entire room was sealed by spell arts. Even if someone tried to peek inside, they would see absolutely nothing.

Then, she removed her clothes and changed into a new set. Once completely dressed in white, she offered up ceremonial vessels. She knelt in place, striking a copper instrument and loudly singing praises to the White Emperor of the West.

If Li Qi were here, he would be able to tell that this was a set of sacrificial etiquette. The object of the sacrifice was likely a spirit of the metal element or a god of the West.

However, this set of etiquette was far too subservient, not something an orthodox Invocator would use.

Orthodox Invocators like him, while observing hierarchy and status, would not use etiquette that excessively humiliated themselves. As someone who 'established the positions of heaven and earth and offered sacrifices to all the gods,' an Invocator naturally possessed their own dignity and status. Even when paying respects to a great god whose status far exceeded their own, they would only do so with an attitude of reverence, not one of subservience.

Regardless, after going through the entire procedure, the grand rite was completed.

The western god and spirit of metal here was naturally the mountain god of the Green Mountain, the White Tiger.

The sacrificial rite was a means of communication.

However, this rite was too vastly different from the orthodox rites of Shaman God Mountain that Li Qi used. It was entirely incapable of constructing the kind of chaotic space Li Qi used to house the will of the gods.

Instead, only a phantom image of the Green Mountain's mountain god appeared above the altar, allowing for a temporary conversation.

Looking at that phantom image, it was a great god with a tiger's head and a human's body. He wore a brocade robe and looked refined and easygoing, but the sharp teeth in his mouth were rather terrifying.

His eyes narrowed slightly. His gaze was calm yet extremely penetrating. Even though it was just a phantom, it exuded an immense sense of oppression.

The phantom pondered for a moment before speaking to the maid. "Hmm... is it time for the routine report? Speak. What has he done recently?"

The maid voiced her compliance and began recounting Jin Buhuan's recent activities.

First, the business deals he had arranged, the entertainment he engaged in, the luxury goods he bought, and the gambling games he participated in.

Then, what he had gained from them and the consequences that followed.

Next, she mentioned his conflict with Li Qi and how it was handled afterwards.

Following that, she recounted Jin Buhuan's "Dao of the Second-Generation Heir" word for word.

Finally, the maid asked, "The young master said at the very end that the reason he spoke so much today is what he calls 'sagehood.' My Lord, what does this mean...?"

Upon hearing this, the mountain god of the Green Mountain first smiled and nodded repeatedly. Then, hearing the maid's question, he chuckled softly and asked in return, "What is the meaning of 'sagehood' according to him?"

"Making his elders believe that he can inherit their wealth without causing trouble is the sagehood of a second-generation heir," the maid repeated his exact words.

The mountain god of the Green Mountain replied, "You see, by conveying these things to me through your mouth, isn't he trying to persuade me and make me believe that he can become a qualified heir? This is his 'sagehood.'"

The maid understood and spoke no more.

But at this moment, the mountain god sighed. "However, his killing intent has still not ceased."

The maid didn't know, but he could hear the hidden meaning behind his son's words.

Usually, his son wouldn't boast about himself like this. Saying it now was simply to make his father value him more.

Only after gaining that importance would he have more resources to eliminate that passing traveler.

[^1]: This poem references the Four Pillars of Destiny also known as Bazi. The White Tiger symbolizes the Metal element. Yin and Mao are Earthly Branches representing Wood. In Chinese fortune-telling Metal conquering Wood signifies the generation of wealth. The branches Si Wu Wei and Xu represent Fire and Earth which symbolize authority and support. An astrological chart with this exact configuration dictates a life of supreme wealth and high political office.
