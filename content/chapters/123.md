# Chapter 123: Too Scientific

Why would the heavenly punishment miss its mark?

It was very simple. When Li Qi was halfway there earlier, he bent down and carved a 'substitute' on the ground using his own blood and true name. In essence, the heavenly punishment was aiming at that spot.

This trick could be used to evade divination and confuse a Diviner's senses. When paired with a technique to conceal one's aura, it would cause the enemy to 'see incorrectly'.

Although it wasn't guaranteed to be useful, an experienced Diviner would certainly have their own methods to unravel it. At that point, it would simply be a matter of whose skills were superior.

However, to the Heavenly Dao of this world—which was already weak and on its last breath—this substitute was the only coordinate it could lock onto. It possessed no method to unravel the trick, nor was it powerful enough to do so.

If one tried to play this trick on the Heavenly Dao of All Under Heaven, they would most likely die a miserable death.

As for this substitute method, it was one of the one hundred and twenty spell arts recorded in the book given to him by his teacher, the Grand Invocator.

Of the spell arts recorded in that book, only two or three were truly grand spell arts. The rest were mostly minor tricks similar to this one. Young Master Zhu himself had written in the book that they were "petty tricks of thieves and scoundrels."

Nevertheless, they were very useful.

This was the 'borrowing power from mountains and rivers' that Invocators excelled at. An Invocator was a profession dedicated to communicating with the deities of heaven, earth, and nature. This communication involved far more than mere flattery.

Sacrifices were merely a means of communication; as for the content of that communication, it varied from person to person. Invocators certainly didn't consider themselves inferior to the ghost-gods.

In most situations, things could be discussed amicably. The Invocator would offer the sacrifices required by the ghost-god in exchange for their power and assistance. But, of course, there were still those "minority of situations."

But that was digressing.

Getting back to the matter at hand, among the desert convoy, aside from Bai Di and Shen Shuibi, no one else could look at Li Qi with a calm gaze anymore.

Although Shen Shuibi had somewhat ruined the atmosphere, it ultimately did no real harm.

Under the might of the heavens, no one could remain calm.

"You two, surely you understand now that I truly harbor no ulterior motives toward you, right? My purpose in coming here is very simple: to observe the operation of the Heavenly Dao here. I have no other intentions."

"Since I've already spoken to this extent, you don't still think I'm genuinely out of options to deal with you, do you? Up to this point, I have treated you with courtesy and respect. I hope you can show me some respect in return."

"Whether you refuse or agree, you have to give me a clear answer. Your current posture is truly not the attitude of someone willing to communicate."

Li Qi said to Sha'er and Lu Li with a smile.

Still reeling from the previous shock, Lu Li didn't know how to respond for a moment.

However, he didn't dare to utter sarcastic remarks like before either.

This was because he could clearly recognize that the 'Young Master' of the Exalted Immortals standing before them was akin to a god in their eyes.

The fact that this Young Master was willing to communicate and converse, rather than opting for direct martial conquest like the other Exalted Immortals, was already their 'good fortune'.

Even if they didn't particularly want this good fortune.

On the other hand, the lioness, Sha'er, appeared even more composed. After witnessing all of that, she took a deep breath. "Then... may I address you as Young Master?"

"Of course," Li Qi replied.

Although he didn't really like the title, now was the time to maintain his imposing aura. It was best not to cause any unnecessary complications.

"Speaking only for myself, perhaps I can offer the Young Master some assistance," the lioness Sha'er said.

Her words were extremely cautious.

But Li Qi smiled; this was enough.

Because, while he also cared about the Heavenly Dao's recompense, or the dao rhythm that would emerge after the Heavenly Dao dropped in rank, compared to those things, what he wanted to know even more was—

Was someone truly plotting against him behind the scenes? Was there really someone preparing to backstab him... or was he simply fighting a battle of wits against the thin air?

It was still necessary to be a bit more cautious.

Li Qi didn't have persecution paranoia. Rather, he felt that ever since being flattered by so many people, a persistent sense of unease had lingered in his heart.

Lacking the strength corresponding to his status, not even having entered the ranks of the Shaman Dao, yet bearing the title of a direct descendant of the Shaman God Mountain—this was not a good thing.

A tall tree catches the wind. With the title currently hanging over his head, someone was absolutely bound to target him.

Just like how some people would scour the entire map every day to farm elite monsters.

---

Setting those matters aside, although Sha'er and Lu Li had agreed to help or provide some information, Li Qi and the others wouldn't delay their main business for a minor interlude on the road.

What was their main business?

Naturally, it was to observe the differences in this other world's Heavenly Dao, using it to learn and comprehend a different dao rhythm.

This was Li Qi's true purpose in coming here.

Compared to this, fighting a battle of wits with the thin air wasn't something that urgently needed doing right now.

Li Qi simply ordered the people from these industrial companies to gather intel for him, had Bai Di spend some effort supervising them, and then enthusiastically awaited the process of observing the 'Heavenly Dao'.

Along the way, Li Qi had been looking forward to seeing what it would feel like. Would it be like what the books described, where simply sitting in meditation would cause countless wondrous sensations to emerge, followed shortly by a massive increase in cultivation? Would his perception of the world enhance multifold, suddenly leading to immense progress?

Harboring these expectations, Li Qi first arrived at a bizarre modern city along the way, and then traveled non-stop to the headquarters of the so-called 'industrial company'.

He encountered quite a few strange sights on the road. For example, although 'industry' existed here, the city's aesthetic meant that most buildings featured sweeping eaves, yet they were built extremely high, making for quite an odd appearance.

The roads weren't entirely filled with cars either; there were even animal-drawn carts.

The people also wore an alternative style of ancient clothing. It suited the local aesthetics without being too bulky. Only the models on street advertisements or the statues wore those particularly solemn, sweeping robes.

This other world scenery made Li Qi look like a country bumpkin entering the city for the first time.

However, seeing it was enough. This scenery wasn't the main point; the main point was still to observe the dao rhythm.

Upon arriving at the headquarters, a crowd of people came out to welcome him, seemingly all high-level executives. But Li Qi merely gave a brief greeting before leaving, heading to the earth oil extraction site not far from the city.

Yet, these people acted as if they were overwhelmed by an unexpected favor, and all of them proceeded to follow Li Qi to the earth oil well.

After arriving, they took an elevator straight deep underground.

Deep beneath the earth, one could observe the most wondrous aspects of this other world's Heavenly Dao.

However, when Li Qi stepped out of the elevator and arrived at the observation point, he froze.

Laid out before him was not a meditation cushion or some tranquil meditation spot.

Instead... it was a basement. The room was filled with interconnected copper and glass pipes. An unknown liquid flowed through them, distilling drop by drop into test tubes.

The copper pipes and experimental apparatus spread throughout the area. Various materials, gemstones, arrays, herbs, and minerals were too numerous to count. A strange odor permeated the air, seemingly a blend of many different substances.

Why did this look so much like... a laboratory?

"No, Miss Shen, what is going on here? Comprehending the Heavenly Dao... is this how it's done?" Li Qi was somewhat stunned, a strange expression on his face.

Displayed before him were a pile of instruments, blackboards, books, and... mathematical formulas.

"Huh? How else would you do it? If you don't rely on these right now, how are you going to comprehend the Heavenly Dao?" Shen Shuibi looked even more confused than he did. "Could it be that you can perceive the Heavenly Dao's dao rhythm on your own? Do you possess dao rhythm sensory organs?"

Seeing the perplexed expression still on Li Qi's face, Shen Shuibi patiently began to explain. She first asked, "Li Qi, tell me, what is the Dao?"

"The Dao is the law by which all things in the world operate..." Li Qi answered according to what he had read in books.

Upon saying that, Li Qi felt as if he had been enlightened, a sudden realization washing over him!

So that was it!

Seeing that Li Qi understood, Shen Shuibi was also pleased. "See? You get it now, right? The Dao is the foundation of this universe's operation. Everything relies on the 'Dao' to function. Therefore, at the very beginning, when we don't possess the means to directly perceive dao rhythm, the only way is to rely on tools to observe different phenomena."

Having heard this much, Li Qi naturally understood Shen Shuibi's meaning. Furthermore, he gained an even deeper level of comprehension.

Indeed, he currently had no idea what the existence of the "Dao" even was. If he really just closed his eyes and meditated, how would that be any different from sleeping? How could he possibly perceive the differences in dao rhythm?

Thus, if he wanted to perceive the differences in this other world's dao rhythm, he could only do so through phenomena.

By conducting an experiment using the same method, the different 'Daos' would yield two different results in the two worlds. Although the variance would generally be minuscule—so small it was almost invisible—it was still a difference.

By observing this variance, one could deduce the minute differences between the two worlds, allowing for comparison.

This was why certain worlds had 'specialties'. It was because those specialties could inevitably only be born under those specific rules.

However, there was one problem.

Li Qi suddenly thought of it and asked Shen Shuibi, "Since that's the case, and the rules of different worlds are different, it means the foundations of material formation are also different. Wouldn't entering such a world kill you?"

Imagine if a world lacked electromagnetic force. All matter would cease to exist. The instant Li Qi entered, he would crumble into a pile of atomic nuclei.

Hearing this, Shen Shuibi immediately explained, "Ah, there's no need to worry about that. The universe itself possesses an eternally enduring 'Dao'. This is the Great Dao that supports the operation of myriad worlds, and even the universe itself. Even these worlds rely on the true Great Dao to exist."

"Therefore, the different dao rhythms in each world just mean there are very, very small distinctions. It's not to the extent that fundamental things like material foundations are completely different."

"I see. I've learned something new. Thank you, Miss Shen," Li Qi thanked her.

Since he understood the situation, the next step was to honestly conduct the experiments.

Moreover, when Li Qi actually got his hands dirty, he realized that the experiments themselves weren't that important. They were simply a more intuitive representation to help him understand better.

Because... predecessors had already performed all the experiments capable of demonstrating the differences between All Under Heaven and the Earth Oil World. All the records and data were already complete.

Any civilization possessed a skill called 'inheritance'.

The earliest wise men exerted all their strength just to solve simple little things like the Pythagorean theorem, or to propose some unbelievable conjectures.

Knowledge was just like that.

It was like in Li Qi's previous world. When he was in middle school, he learned the three laws of motion, the Pythagorean theorem, and two-dimensional geometry. How simple was it? How easy?

A few simple lines of text, perhaps only requiring half an hour of reading to comprehend.

Yet behind it lay the efforts of the top minds of the human world, spending decades or even their entire lifetimes exploring and summarizing, just to arrive at that one simple line of small text in a book.

Knowledge was this kind of thing.

Before mastering it, it seemed unreachable, unknown, and mysterious, as if it were some special, ultimate, unparalleled super secret.

But once you truly grasped a certain piece of knowledge, you would find that everything was simply a matter of course. The principles seemed so simple.

Yes, so simple that a single hint was enough to see through it.

Yet, it required an unimaginable amount of time, material, and vital energy to accomplish such a simple thing.

These prices had already been fully paid by those who came before.

"These books... these data and records, this is the difference between the 'Daos' of the two worlds?" As Li Qi read through the documents, the things summarized by predecessors long ago simply flowed into his mind.

Before coming here, he had thought about how mystical comprehending dao rhythm would be.

But looking at it now, some things were truly universally applicable.

Experiencing an other world's dao rhythm... was truly far too scientific.

However, merely reading wasn't intuitive enough. Coming here still required intuitively conducting experiments, making summaries, deducing what consequences the different Daos of the two worlds caused, locating the differences, and making comparisons.

Finding the differences, and then finding the similarities. By understanding these, one would naturally develop a clearer comprehension of the universe's singular Great Dao.

This wasn't some mystical, unspeakable concept. It was clear and obvious experimental records, crystal-clear summaries and analyses. It could be understood at a glance, and learning it brought one closer to the truth.

As Li Qi read the books and conducted experiments, his heart was filled with endless sighs of emotion.

This was the power of inheritance. The wise sages risked their lives to forge a tiny path, and thus, ordinary people could walk those paths. They could walk them smoothly and simply, without needing to pay the same price as those wise men.

It was just like how he currently cast certain spell arts and rites. He didn't need to understand the principles behind the spell arts and sacrifices, nor did he need to study how these things worked. All he needed to do was form hand seals, chant incantations, guide his qi, and pair them with specific techniques. Then, he could unleash them, exerting power that far exceeded human limits.

The great, ancient wise sages had used vital energy unimaginable to ordinary people, and made incredible sacrifices to explore the laws of this world bit by bit. They summarized the foundational 'Dao', developed fundamental techniques, and then spread their teachings to All Under Heaven.

Those who came later only needed to follow the prescribed steps to walk a smooth and steady path.

This kind of inheritance was what truly built the civilization known as 'All Under Heaven'.
