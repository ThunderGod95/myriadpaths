# Chapter 133: The Judgment

Beyond the Earth Oil World, a dharma body suddenly appeared.

It was colossal, hundreds of thousands of miles high. Its pupils alone filled the entire visible sky. With one hand gently raised, it held the entire Earth Oil World in its palm as a mortal might hold a basketball.

Number One, the Child of Destiny of this world, stared blankly at the gargantuan figure in the heavens.

This magnificent dharma body looked as if it could shatter their world with a single slap.

"You see now, don't you, miss? As I said, you have absolutely, utterly, no chance of winning," Li Qi said with a sigh, glancing at the girl's stunned face.

He had anticipated this, which was why he had tried to warn her earlier.

In the end, his true opponent in this world had never been some Child of Destiny, but Bo Yue, the young Diviner.

"This… this is impossible. How can a person possess such power? And if he could, why would he even pay attention to us here?" Number One’s face was a mask of disbelief, her tone bordering on hysteria.

Bo Yue looked at her with a hint of pity and shook his head. "Ordinarily, he wouldn't. But with the two of us here, there will obviously be someone keeping a close eye on things to prevent any accidents."

"However, there wasn't going to be any accident anyway. You thought the formation you set up was something rare, but it's nothing more than a dime-a-dozen Human Dao ascension formation,"

He elaborated with an air of nonchalance, "You used code names, Number One through Number Six, to correspond to your satellite cities. Then, you used the formation to obscure the heavenly secrets, replacing the cities' karmic fortune with human bodies to usurp the human qi and channel it all into yourself, allowing you to ascend to the Rank 8 of the Human Dao. Am I wrong?"

Number One's eyes widened in shock. She bit her lip but said nothing.

"Mere parlor tricks," Bo Yue said, shaking his head with a sigh. "Did you know that your plan could never have succeeded? Did you really think that the Heavenly Dao of this world was still some naive, nascent world-consciousness?"

Hearing this, Li Qi's eyes went wide with astonishment. "Could it be?"

Bo Yue nodded. "Exactly. As soon as I arrived, I tried to divine the condition of this world's Heavenly Dao, but I couldn't get a read no matter what I did. So, I immediately guessed the situation. Someone must have refined the Heavenly Dao of this world. When you and I communicate with it, every single move we make is entirely visible to them, seen with crystal clarity."

He then glanced at Number One, who was still biting her lower lip. "So your attempt to obscure the heavenly secrets was nothing but a fool's dream. The moment you tried to advance to Rank 8 and perform the Severing Heaven and Earth ritual would have been the moment of your death."

At this, Number One finally spoke, retorting through gritted teeth, "Impossible! I am the Child of Destiny! I was born with this knowledge, and I can even communicate with other worlds! If the Heavenly Dao had been refined, how could I have been born?"

Li Qi frowned. "That's a good point. And what about the heavenly punishment I summoned earlier?"

"Refining the Heavenly Dao is not the same as replacing it. The Heavenly Dao of this world still exists and has its instinctual reactions; it is merely under someone's control. As for why a Child of Destiny would be born?" Bo Yue rolled his eyes.

Then he explained, "You fool. Did you really think you were the first Child of Destiny of this world?"

_Did you really think you were the first Child of Destiny of this world?_

At these words, all the color drained from Number One's face.

Even Li Qi felt a chill run down his spine.

_He's right. How many Children of Destiny had died before this girl?_

Looking at her now, she was truly…

Though he had known from the start that she had no chance, the reality of this higher-level blow was far too cruel.

After all, saying something was one thing, but seeing it happen with one's own eyes was another entirely.

This also further corroborated Li Qi's theory.

The Great Deer Kingdom’s actions would only cause the Earth Oil World to weaken further, until it eventually became a wasteland, an unranked world where only a tiny fraction of life could eke out a meager existence.

It would be like the desert that now covered so much of this world. The only difference was that there were still a few habitable places left, places where people had built satellite cities, separating the world within the walls from the world without.

But Li Qi believed that eventually, even the satellite cities would perish, until the entire world became a barren waste, unable to produce even a drop of earth oil, a place where even life itself would disappear.

As they spoke, the sky gradually returned to its normal state.

The colossal dharma body was slowly descending.

Its body shrank, returning to the size of a normal person. When it landed, it was revealed to be a middle-aged man in ornate robes, his beard neatly trimmed, exuding an aura of considerable authority.

Behind this dharma body stood a flippant young man dressed as a scholar, a smile on his face.

Li Qi performed the rite of a disciple, bowing to the young man. "Greetings, Teacher!"

Then he bowed to the middle-aged man. "Greetings, Your Majesty."

Bo Yue, who had been speaking so freely just moments before, was startled when he heard Li Qi address the young man as 'Teacher.' Frightened, he hurriedly performed the salute of a junior. "Greetings, Grand Invocator. Greetings, Your Majesty."

_What is going on? I was surprised enough that the sovereign of Great Deer came, but why is a Grand Invocator here in person?_

_Just how favored is this Li Qi by his master?_

Though his heart was in turmoil, his etiquette was flawless, and he maintained a calm exterior.

But Young Master Zhu paid no heed to such formalities.

"It's fine, dispense with the formalities," he said with a wave of his hand. "Don't get bogged down in such trifles. This is hardly a formal occasion. Let's all sit and talk."

He completely ignored the sovereign of the Great Deer Kingdom standing beside him, acting as if he were the guest of honor.

As he spoke, tables and chairs materialized from thin air for them to sit, with a seat even for Number One.

They all sat, but everyone, including the sovereign of Great Deer, remained silent, waiting for Young Master Zhu to speak.

"Well done, my boy! I haven't seen you for a few days, and not only have you attained a clear Dao Heart, you've even found a path to practice. You truly are my student! Haha." After sitting down, Young Master Zhu slapped his thigh in delight, as if Li Qi had brought him great honor.

Li Qi felt a bead of sweat on his brow. "Teacher, didn't you leave? Why are you here?"

"I was planning to leave. But that Grand Diviner from earlier, that old geezer who came to rescue Lady Luofu... he happened to have a disciple gaining experience around here. That guy right there, his disciple." Young Master Zhu pointed at Bo Yue.

Bo Yue immediately lowered his head, his previous confidence and flair gone.

Young Master Zhu continued, "He rushed over because of the Lady Luofu situation and planned to check on his disciple while he was at it, intending to take him back to the mountain once things wrapped up."

"I figured you could give it a try too, so I arranged for Little Qi here to set one up for you. As for me, I decided to stay an extra month to see how you'd do," Young Master Zhu said frankly.

Then he looked at Bo Yue. "By the way, your master was originally going to stay and watch over you, but when I showed up, he found me too annoying and just left."

Bo Yue quickly nodded. "It is no matter. This disciple can return to the mountain on his own."

As he spoke, sweat began to form on his brow.

He had heard his master speak of this Young Master Zhu, of his extreme unreliability and his mercurial temperament.

He even dared to call others "old geezers" simply because he was the youngest Rank 3 in Shaman God Mountain—so young it was almost preposterous.

Because of his genius, even the Shaman Gods doted on him, giving him free rein.

As a result, his libertine ways had earned him the attention of the other Grand Invocators and Grand Diviners—or rather, their helpless displeasure.

For example, the fact that the sovereign of Great Deer was here and his own master had left was, he was one hundred percent certain, this man’s doing…

_Li Qi is this man's disciple?_

With that thought, he shot Li Qi a look of profound pity.

_How tragic. I should buy him a meal sometime. Can't let him grow disillusioned with Shaman God Mountain._

_What if he defects from the sect one day? You couldn't even blame him…_

While Bo Yue’s mind was filled with sorrowful sighs, Li Qi seemed completely unfazed. He simply bowed to Young Master Zhu and said, "Teacher, you must have already heard my conversation with my Senior Brother, haven't you?"

"Of course I did. I was listening the whole time. You spoke very well. What you've reasoned out, what you've considered, is the very operation of the Heavenly Dao—righteous, but not compassionate. That's why I'm so pleased."

"But, in practicing this path, you will surely face many obstacles. Because the wolf will not want to give the sheep a chance. Just like you. Before, did you give your enemies a chance?" Young Master Zhu said.

Li Qi shook his head. "Not so."

At these two words, both the sovereign of Great Deer and Bo Yue quickly bowed their heads.

_Is this man insane? How dare he? To openly refute a Grand Invocator! Who does he think he is? When he dies, I hope his blood doesn't splash on me…_

_This kid… no wonder he’s this man’s disciple. Just as lawless._

But Young Master Zhu was not offended. He tilted his head. "Oh? And why is that?"

Li Qi, feeling nothing was amiss, answered directly. "The wolf and the sheep do not refer to a single individual, but to a group. Between one individual and another, it matters not what is done."

"Therefore, I believe the righteousness I must uphold is a righteousness for a large group, such as a world or a race, not for any single individual within it," Li Qi said.

Hearing this, Young Master Zhu burst out laughing. "Hahaha! You're only Rank 9, and you already dare to speak of influencing worlds and races? Should I call you far-sighted, or should I call you conceited?"

"It is not conceit, Teacher. Am I not influencing a world right now? And in the future, I will only be stronger than I am today, will I not?" Li Qi replied.

"Alright, then let me ask you another question. You speak of the group, but is not the individual a part of the group?" Young Master Zhu's smile faded, and he asked Li Qi with a hint of his usual flippancy.

"A hundred people is a group, one person is an individual. So are ten people not individuals? Before ten thousand people, are those hundred not a group? Are smaller groups not individuals in the face of larger groups? Your righteousness, to what size of group does it apply? What number is required to be considered a group?"

Young Master Zhu easily pointed out the flaw in Li Qi’s words, a flaw in the very 'Dao' he sought to practice.

But Li Qi was not shaken. "I wonder if Teacher has heard of the concept of a percentage?"

"Whether something is a group is not determined by its number, but by the proportion it occupies," Li Qi answered.

"Fine, we'll accept that for now. Let's move on. The group is made up of individuals. So how can you value the group while ignoring the individual? Without a strategy for the individual, how can you influence the group?" Young Master Zhu pressed.

"That is why the path I have conceived is not only righteousness, but also justice."

"Righteousness is for the group. Justice is the path for the individual, or for smaller groups," Li Qi replied.

"Then what is justice?" Young Master Zhu asked, intrigued.

"Justice is a system of laws and morals that is suitable for the masses. I treat others with righteousness, and I conduct myself with justice. In this way, the Dao is complete, and I will not arbitrarily abuse my own power," Li Qi answered.

To treat the world with an attitude of righteousness, and to hold himself to the standard of justice.

This was the answer Li Qi had arrived at.

At least, for now.

Hearing this, a look of delight spread across Young Master Zhu's face, and he laughed heartily.

The sovereign of the Great Deer Kingdom’s eyes widened in astonishment, looking at Li Qi in disbelief.

Bo Yue fell into contemplation. Clearly, he had only considered righteousness, but had not considered justice.

After he had finished laughing, Young Master Zhu walked over to Li Qi and clapped him on the shoulder. "Haha, good! To have thought this far, you won't have to worry about your Dao Heart wavering before Rank 7! You truly are my disciple, so much more talented than those old fogeys' students! I have high hopes for you, kid."

"As you are now, you only lack cultivation, not state of mind. From now on, you can focus on raising your cultivation without worry. With a clear Dao Heart, inner demons will not arise, distracting thoughts will be none, and power will no longer be an obstacle in your pursuit of the Dao. The path to Rank 7 is now a smooth road for you!"

Hearing this, a look of shame crossed Bo Yue’s face.

It was official. He had lost.

Li Qi looked up. "Does that mean Teacher thinks my conclusion is correct?"

"There are ten thousand paths to the Dao. How can there be a right or wrong? Any path one can walk to the end is the right one," Young Master Zhu said with a playful smile.

He continued, "Well, now that we've covered all this, there's nothing left for me here. I have my own business to attend to. As for you, Little Qi, you understand, don't you?" He shot a sidelong glance at the sovereign of the Great Deer Kingdom.

"This disciple understands perfectly. I will immediately abandon the heretical Human Dao methods and govern this world with the righteousness of the Shaman Dao. I will also leave this world a thread of life and hope," the sovereign of Great Deer immediately declared.

"See, that's more like it. The Great Deer Kingdom was founded on the Shaman Dao. For you to be promoting heretical Human Dao methods at a time like this, it makes me wonder, you know?" Young Master Zhu said with a smile.

Then, he pointed at Number One. "Little girl, you heard our conversation, didn't you? By my decree, you are this world's thread of life! I will grant you a fortuitous encounter and send you to the outer realms, so that Little Qi here will never be able to find you!"

"What becomes of this world in the future will depend on your fortunes in the outer realms!"

With that, he tore a rift in the fabric of the world and kicked Number One through it.

Everyone was stunned.

But before anyone could react, he cried out, "My work here is done! I'm off!"

With that cry, he suddenly soared into the sky, transforming into a streak of light and disappearing without a trace.

Li Qi was dumbfounded.

_My teacher's insanity seems to have reached a new level!_
