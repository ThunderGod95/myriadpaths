# Chapter 170: Temple

Li Qi was a man of action. Although he could be indecisive during the planning stage, once his mind was made up, he executed his plans with absolute resolve.

The lives of over a hundred thousand people could not be delayed. It was best to leave early, return early, and make haste.

Thus, after simply feeding Old Ma and bidding farewell to Shen Shuibi, he swiftly set out for Vast Yang City.

Li Qi had been to Vast Yang City before, though only for a brief stay to restock supplies before leaving. It was approximately fifteen hundred miles away from the border pass here.

Given the average physical constitution of the people in this world, and the fact that a large contingent would inevitably slow down the march, a journey of fifteen hundred miles would take about ten days.

Covering over a hundred miles a day was, truthfully, a terrifying pace. This was entirely reliant on the fact that everyone possessed a cultivation method—even the peasant farmers—which allowed them to maintain such marching speed.

For Li Qi, however, it was far less troublesome. Ever since Old Ma had practiced the [Song of the Solitary], it could travel two thousand miles a day. It would only take ten hours to reach Vast Yang City, and even a round trip traveling day and night would take no more than twenty hours.

Since he could return by tomorrow, there was naturally no need to overthink it.

Li Qi packed his belongings, spurred his horse, and hurried on his way.

The journey was naturally uneventful. After all, his cultivation base was no longer shallow; ordinary demons or bandits were unlikely to court his dark qi and invite trouble.

His destination was Vast Yang City.

Vast Yang City was a prefectural city of the Hundred Forests Kingdom. It was similar in size to the Li Prefecture's capital. The governor resided here, commanding the entire prefecture while also overseeing the defensive operations of the border pass.

The military pay and rations for the border pass soldiers naturally fell under the governor's purview as the chief official, while the Vast Yang Minister of War was responsible for the specific execution and logistics.

Riding like the wind and lightning, shadows of thunder trailed behind them. Bolstered by the [Song of the Solitary], the True Dragon bloodline within Old Ma surged, leaving a trail of sparks and lightning that tore auspicious clouds and azure thunder across the sky.

Although it looked incredibly impressive, it was actually a sign of inadequate mastery.

When a true dragon traversed the heavens, the clouds followed naturally. It would pass without a trace, its movements completely imperceptible.

A display like Old Ma's, practically begging everyone to know its location, was considered inferior.

Traveling non-stop and entirely unhindered, they arrived swiftly at Vast Yang City ten hours later.

With ten hours passed, the sky had grown dark. It was already night.

Under normal circumstances, Li Qi would have rested for the night before paying a visit. However, with the lives of over a hundred thousand people at stake, he could not afford such luxuries.

In a major city like Vast Yang, flying inside was strictly prohibited. Therefore, Li Qi landed directly outside, took Old Ma by the reins, and walked briskly toward the Minister of War's Manor.

The position of Minister of War controlled the prefecture's soldiers, along with the regulations of their chariots, horses, weapons, and armor, and managed the classifications of the various military steeds and armaments.

In practice, this role was the supreme commander of the prefecture's military forces. The Minister of War oversaw the movements of the soldiers, managed their logistics, and held personnel authority—meaning the ability to "manage the classifications of military steeds and armaments" translated to the power to independently promote talent.

It was an office of immense power and overwhelming prestige. As the highest military official of the prefecture and one of the Three Ministers, the Minister of War stood equal with only a handful of others, subordinate only to the governor.

The so-called Three Ministers were the Minister of War, the Minister of State, and the Minister of Works.

The Minister of War commanded military logistics, supplies, personnel transfers, external campaigns, and internal defense, among other duties.

The Minister of State was responsible for the mapping of the state's lands and the census of its people. They also compiled educational texts, distributed official posts, managed taxation and finance, and served as the core of the standard bureaucratic system. They held the power to monitor and recommend local officials while simultaneously crafting and executing specific policies concerning the livelihood of the people.

The Minister of Works was in charge of infrastructure. Land surveying for farmers, road construction, water conservancy maintenance, urban planning, and the preservation of industrial techniques—all foundational engineering projects fell within the Minister of Works' jurisdiction.

Together, these three were known as the Three Ministers. They answered directly to the governor. Besides the governor, they represented the absolute zenith of power within the prefecture.

As far as Li Qi knew, aside from the Minister of State—who held the position with a Rank 8 cultivation base due to exceptional intelligence and outstanding talent—the other two were both Rank 7 experts.

To pay a late-night visit to a Rank 7 powerhouse who was also a high-ranking official, Li Qi naturally would not just show up empty-handed and devoid of manners.

He still needed to prepare some sort of gift.

The selection of the gift required careful consideration.

Li Qi could not afford anything too expensive; he simply had no money.

Nor could he just casually throw something together. If he offended them, he might be thrown out onto the street.

On the way there, Li Qi had pondered this. Outside the city, he had hand-picked some wild cotton, beating it into rough cloth. Upon entering the city, he found a tailor and spent a few dozen copper coins to have it woven into a curtain.

A black tent made of cotton cloth was known as "Guzhong." Cotton cloth was thicker than silk and much warmer. The reason it was used within the imperial palaces was to symbolize the virtue of frugality.

This item came with a historical anecdote: "The Emperor uses Guzhong, for he reveres frugality. If even the imperial palace honors frugality in such a manner, then the households of scholars and commoners should know moderation!"

This way, he had a gift prepared and subtly flattered the man. As the saying went, one does not strike a smiling face. With this setup, going to ask for a signature shouldn't pose much of a problem.

With everything considered, all that remained was the visit itself.

Li Qi led his horse and walked all the way to the Minister of War Manor.

The nightlife in Vast Yang City was quite vibrant. As Li Qi walked along, despite the late hour, the entire city was brilliantly illuminated. Taverns lined the streets. Pedestrians strolled through the city in wooden clogs, the scenic beauty of the mountains and lakes reflecting in their joyful expressions, as they feasted on cooked fish and bamboo shoots, drinking and chatting freely.

However, where some enjoyed life, others naturally had to work.

All along the road, various peddlers carried goods on their shoulders and by hand, endlessly transporting supplies into the city.

Vast Yang City boasted a population of a million. The daily consumption was incalculable, naturally requiring countless laborers to handle the daily transport and hauling of supplies, while peddlers and shopkeepers distributed the goods through retail.

Li Qi also passed by a wondrous sight. A wealthy household had erected a massive pillar outside their gates. Atop the pillar was an awning to provide shade and shelter from the rain for pedestrians below. There was also a water-gathering talisman that constantly condensed and sprayed out a stream of water, providing free, clean water for those gathered beneath it.

The massive pillar stood two hundred feet tall and bore the inscription: "Blessed Land of the Zhang Family."

Tens of thousands of bats were carved into the pillar, leading passersby to call the area "Ten Thousand Blessings Come to Pay Homage." It was a place established by this wealthy family to accumulate merit and pray for blessings.

But when Li Qi passed by, hundreds of beggar children were tightly packed beneath the pillar. The stench was overpowering as they fought over the clean water and sought shelter from the wind and rain. There was not the slightest hint of 'blessings' to be seen; it had long since devolved into a beggar's den.

Finding it strange that Vast Yang City would have so many beggars, Li Qi asked a nearby street vendor.

Hearing this, the vendor, who was busy deep-frying snacks, smiled and said to Li Qi, "A countless number of refugees recently retreated here from the border pass. Some found a way to make a living, but those who couldn't work naturally just gathered in this area."

"Countless refugees? What happened over at the border pass?" Li Qi asked curiously.

The stall owner seemed quite talkative and replied, "You must be joking, good sir. We just set up stalls in the city; how would we know what happened at the border pass? I only heard people say that the soldiers over at the border retreated and brought back a lot of fleeing refugees. It's been better these last few days, but half a month ago, it was swarming with people inside and outside the city. Now, it's just some beggars left asking for food. Oh, and places like the Spring Fragrance Pavilion have gotten quite a few new young ladies. Seeing as you're traveling alone, good sir, you might want to give it a try. The prices are incredibly low."

"Let's not talk about that. Give me two skewers of tofu and fry up two skewers of cabbage. Oh, and give me one of those tiger-skin eggs, and some chicken feet too. By the way, based on what you're saying, an event this major happened, and the Minister of War's Manor hasn't put out any official notices?" Li Qi ordered some fried skewers and asked out of curiosity.

"What notices could the Minister of War's Manor possibly put out? Lord Minister of War has been running off to Heavenly Peace Temple every day lately. He probably hasn't even touched his official duties!" the vendor laughed heartily.

"You even know about that?" Li Qi was greatly surprised. He had only asked offhandedly to see if there were any government notices, yet this street vendor knew so much?

Back when he was a boat tracker at the bottom of society, he had never been privy to such information. Just getting a glance at an official notice was considered lucky.

"Ah, the good sir must be new to Vast Yang, right? This isn't something I needed to snoop around for. Just ask anyone in the city; who doesn't know that Lord Minister of War is a devout believer in Buddhism? He practically lives at Heavenly Peace Temple now," the vendor said as he fried the tofu for Li Qi.

Li Qi, however, suddenly frowned.

_Wait. Buddhism? Heavenly Peace Temple?_

_Buddhist schools? Damn, I haven't heard of this before._

Having been in this world for so long, he had almost forgotten that Buddhist Sects even existed.

The books did mention that in the extreme west of All Under Heaven, there was a Supreme Boundless Buddhist Kingdom. But because it was so far away, Li Qi basically treated it as a fairy tale.

And yet now, right within the sphere of influence of the Shaman God Mountain, the Minister of War of a kingdom's prefectural city was a devout Buddhist, and a temple had even been established here. This was absolutely extraordinary.

"Seeing your expression, good sir, you haven't heard of them, have you? I hadn't heard of temples or Buddhism before either. But they claim to be no different from the shamans and spirit mediums we normally see. They can heal illnesses, pray for blessings, and so on. Over time, people just let them be. It's not like they're doing anything evil anyway. They often come into the city to give out alms. Even I've eaten the food they hand out," the vendor babbled on, his face still bearing a bright, sunny smile.

The vendor added, "I reckon they're pretty similar to Daoist priests, all coming from the outside. It's just that they are much better than the Daoist priests. Those Daoist priests squat in the mountains all day, and on the rare occasions they show up, they have the faces of dead men. It's too gloomy. They can't compare to those big bald monks. Every one of them has a kind face and a benevolent gaze. They're always smiling, friendly to everyone, and they don't even charge money to cure sicknesses."

As they chatted, Li Qi continued to ask for information about this so-called Heavenly Peace Temple. By the time his fried skewers were ready, he had asked just about everything he needed to. He paid the money, took Old Ma by the reins, and prepared to leave.

He still planned to go check out the Minister of War's Manor.

According to the vendor, Heavenly Peace Temple had arrived about ten years ago.

In the beginning, a group of senior monks arrived. They were all incredibly thin and gaunt, calling themselves ascetic monks. Their mission was to travel across All Under Heaven to spread the voice of Buddha.

This group of gaunt monks numbered around four to five hundred, but they only left one of their own behind. The rest didn't linger and quickly departed.

According to them, traversing All Under Heaven was an arduous task that could not be delayed. They would only station a single ascetic monk in places they deemed to possess 'destined affinity,' leaving them there to spread the Buddhist teachings.

The ascetic monk who stayed behind called himself Chanzhi. He remained to spread Buddhism, but he didn't start preaching right away.

First, Chanzhi went around 'seeking alms.' While it was called seeking alms, it was essentially begging. Seeing how thin and frail he was, some people took pity and gave him food.

In return, he would leave something behind—perhaps a hand-written page of Buddhist scripture. When he didn't even have a page to spare, he would sit at their doorstep and chant a passage of scripture, claiming it was to pray for the household's blessing.

This sort of behavior won people's favor. After all, while most beggars would also say a few words of blessing, none possessed his serene and gentle disposition. Despite being old and emaciated, his eyes remained as calm and warm as still water.

Moreover, the typical auspicious phrases everyone was already tired of hearing could not compare to the profound and mystical aura of his chanted scriptures.

After a period of time had passed, one of the families that had offered him alms suddenly suffered a disaster. A fierce ghost broke into their home in the dead of night, seeking to take their lives and drain their lifeblood.

But just as the fierce ghost crossed the threshold, the family's house erupted in a brilliant golden light. A chorus of Sanskrit chanting rang out with a thunderous boom. The ghost instantly let out a wail, utterly losing its ability to resist.

Later, when the local Devil Suppression Division rushed to the scene, they were completely astonished. Thus, the tale began to spread.

Now, the entire Vast Yang City knew that a monk named Chanzhi possessed genuine abilities. As long as you gave him a meal, he would protect you and pray for your blessings.

For a time, Chanzhi went around explaining to everyone that Chanzhi was his Dharma name, not his birth name. Furthermore, he claimed he was not worthy of being called a high monk, but rather just a simple cleric.

The masses, however, cared little for such distinctions. They didn't know the difference between a high monk and a Dharma name. All they knew was that Chanzhi was a man of true skill. People flocked to offer him food, delivering all sorts of mountain delicacies and rare ingredients right to his lips. Wealthy families invited him over as an esteemed guest, eager to throw grand banquets in his honor.

However, after this incident, everyone realized they could no longer find Chanzhi.

He vanished for a full six months. Only after the Chanzhi craze had died down did he reappear. And when he did, he stood before the door of a poor household, continuing to 'seek alms' by asking for some leftover food from the previous night.

That family wasn't wealthy to begin with. Their meals were no different from what Li Qi had eaten during his days as a boat tracker—exceedingly shabby.

Furthermore, they had no idea that the man they had encountered was the famous Chanzhi.

But out of simple, honest kindness, they still shared their own food with him.

After eating, Chanzhi chanted his mantras, prayed for their blessing, and left behind a single piece of paper, just as he always did.

Later, when word of this got out, the price of that single page skyrocketed to twenty thousand coins. It was purchased by a wealthy family, who took it home to enshrine. The poor family's life improved significantly as a result, and only then did they realize they had met Chanzhi.

A day later, Chanzhi paid them another visit, hoping to take a single wooden plank from their roof.

Having just earned twenty thousand coins because of him, the family naturally wouldn't refuse and hurriedly offered it up.

Chanzhi accepted the plank and then resumed his daily alms-seeking. Once a day, he would visit a home—sometimes a wealthy estate, sometimes a poor hovel. He never discriminated, nor did he have any dietary restrictions. He ate whatever he was given.

According to him, seeking alms arose from karmic destiny. He was simply going around to resolve the karma from his past lives. The households he visited were all ones he shared a destined affinity with from a previous life.

However, since his reappearance, he would always plead with his hosts for a wooden plank. If they agreed, he accepted it and expressed his sincere devotion and gratitude. If they refused, he wouldn't say a word, simply withdrawing after finishing his meal.

After several years of this, he actually used the wooden planks gathered from ten thousand households to build a temple upon a barren mountain.

That temple was called Heavenly Peace Temple.
