# Chapter 139: Bustling Street Scenes

As he asked this, Li Qi picked up a chopstick-full of dry-tossed shredded chicken and looked Piaoxu up and down.

The fox demon was already beautiful, possessing a timid disposition that made people want to bully her. Now, under Li Qi's gaze, she grew even more flustered turned her face away, making it obvious to anyone that she was harboring fear in her heart.

"Well, we plan to visit the market to see if we can buy some Windwalk Talismans or some cultivation methods for demon beasts. But Miss Piaoxu, don't you still have a business to run?" Li Qi asked amiably, not wanting to make things too difficult for her.

Hearing this, Piaoxu hurriedly replied, "There are assistants in the shop, and my... that chang ghost will naturally manage things. I also have Qi Ya to help. It will only delay the dine-in service for half a day, it won't be a hindrance."

Li Qi then asked, "Speaking of which, that chang ghost... did you strip away his sentience? Did you reduce him to a puppet?"

"Of course not. If he were turned into a puppet, how could he still appear so lifelike? It's just that when I inherited that tiger demon's chang ghost, he had already been refined into this state. Although he still has his sentience, his speck of true spirit is entirely at my mercy. He can no longer resist in the slightest, which is why I ordered him to act as my disguise..."

A chang ghost was exactly this kind of entity. They possessed sentience, wisdom, the ability to think, and even seemed capable of feeling emotion. However, all of their joys and sorrows were manipulated by the tiger demon. Even if ordered to lure their own flesh and blood to fill the tiger's belly, they would do so without hesitation and without a shred of guilt.

"If you think..." she hurriedly tried to add after her explanation.

"Forget it, it's your own possession, deal with it yourself. The chang ghost is long dead. Strictly speaking, its sentience is also fake; it's nothing more than a puppet. However, since it won't delay your business, could I trouble you, Miss Piaoxu, to lead the way for us? Naturally, we will compensate you to make up for your losses," Li Qi said.

Piaoxu hastily replied, "There's no need for compensation. You two gentlemen saved my life, so it is only right that I repay you. How could I accept any payment?"

Li Qi shook his head and took out a small, translucent bead. "Accept it. I treat people with righteousness; things are as they should be. Saving your life was nothing to us, so we cannot accept excessive compensation. Staying here for free is already enough. Asking for more would be unjust."

Li Qi spoke very seriously. After all, this concerned the Dao he identified with, so he brooked no refusal.

Hearing Li Qi's tone, Piaoxu didn't dare say anything more and had no choice but to accept the translucent bead.

The bead had a transparent outer shell, and inside, five streaks of qi in different colors constantly spiraled around. It looked extremely beautiful, but upon touching it, it felt incomparably freezing, and no amount of holding it could warm it up.

"May I ask, sir, what is this?" Piaoxu asked in confusion.

"The five streaks of ghost qi that tried to kill you earlier. The first time, it succeeded. The second time, I captured it using divine qi and sealed it into this small bead. If you want to find your enemy, this might provide some clues. I think it is quite fitting to use this as compensation," Li Qi explained.

"Thank you for the trouble, sir." Piaoxu bowed in gratitude.

"There's no need for that. Go and pack your things. We will eat first, and when we head out later, you just need to familiarize us with the routes. Let's not waste too much time." Li Qi then picked up his bowl, ready to eat.

There was no helping it.

Just in the time he spent chatting with Piaoxu, the rabbit had almost finished all the food!

If he didn't eat now, there would be nothing left!

Seeing this, Piaoxu chuckled softly and stepped away. A short while later, she returned carrying a tureen of crab roe soup.

The crab roe was fresh and plump, sweet yet rich, white as jade and yellow as gold, having already achieved the absolute pinnacle of color, fragrance, and taste. Piaoxu had mixed it with oil, salt, and bean powder before pan-frying it. The more one chewed, the sweeter it became. Its luscious flavor was almost like cheese, yet soft, smooth, and boneless. It was truly the ultimate delicacy.

Naturally, another scramble for food ensued.

Li Qi failed to snatch any, because Shen Shuibi had even resorted to using Daoist spells.

He only managed to eat one spoonful; the rest was entirely demolished by the rabbit.

He seethed with impotent rage.

His good mood for the morning was completely ruined.

Setting that aside, after about half an hour, the group headed out to the market.

He wouldn't have known if he hadn't come, but upon arriving, Li Qi finally experienced the true prosperity of Clear Mist City.

This was a level of vitality and flourishing commerce that places in the Great Deer Kingdom and the Earth Oil World could never hope to match.

Shen Shuibi didn't seem to have seen anything like this either. In the past, she had always cultivated in seclusion on Mount Luofu, rarely leaving her home in her entire life, so she observed everything very intently.

She could instantly recognize the raw materials and the principles behind these goods, but she had never seen them produce such dazzling effects. They were truly beautiful, keeping her eyes glued to them.

As for a country bumpkin like Li Qi, he couldn't get enough of it. These extraordinary techniques and spell arts left him dumbfounded; they looked far more spectacular than any special effects.

For instance, a roadside flower shop displayed blossoms for sale. Branches and leaves sprawled out, jutting over the eaves and brickwork, filling the entire interior. There were dozens of flowers blooming at once: goose-yellow, oriole-bright, pine-blossom, steamed chestnut hues, jasmine, pollia, pearl orchids, mountain orchids, white jasmine, and sickle senna. The whole room was bursting with life, the calyxes and petals overflowing in lush, abundant clusters. Some were as large as small peck measures, emitting an intoxicating fragrance. The wind carried the scent, resembling graceful, lovely beauties vying for attention.

Seeing this flower shop, Li Qi finally understood where the intensely fragrant floral dew in his morning porridge had come from. It was clearly winter, yet these flowers continued to bloom without wilting. They must have used a secret technique.

And this was merely a roadside flower shop.

Walking further ahead, some people were playing chess, but not with ordinary pieces. The chessboard was like a vast plaza, containing thumb-sized miniature people acting as the pieces. Each piece stood ready with lance and halberd, as if nations of a thousand chariots were slaughtering each other.

With every move, three hundred and sixty lines shifted simultaneously, mimicking the numbers of the heavenly cycle. The pieces were evenly divided into black and white, symbolizing Yin and Yang.

The state of the game—life and death, survival and ruin—affected every movement of the little figures. Playing this game was like genuinely manipulating the treacherous plots and bloody, cruel warfare between two nations.

Passing by a lake within the city, it seemed to be a residential area for nobles. The winter days had just passed, but the snow had not yet melted, and the rime-covered trees were shrouded in a thick, sprawling white mist.

Looking into the lake, the sky, clouds, mountains, and water merged into a single expanse of white. Yet, within the white, a few specks of black could be seen. Upon closer inspection, it was someone boating on the lake. The black amidst the white consisted only of the faint trace of a small boat and a single dot representing a pavilion in the center of the lake.

The contrast of black and white resembled an ink wash painting. The scenery was extraordinary.

By the roadside, there were also vendors boiling tea and dairy drinks. The cheese was pure white and steamed with heat. At first glance, it looked like jade liquid and pearly glue, plump as snow and smooth as frost. Its fragrance seeped straight into the lungs, and sprinkled with honey and cane syrup, it was incredibly enticing.

Shen Shuibi clamored for some, so Li Qi bought three cups, one for each of them.

After taking a taste, it was indeed sweet, delicious, and of supreme quality.

Looking further ahead, there were houses newly built for the New Year. The newly constructed pavilions used precious and magnificent materials. Setting aside the use of silk and jade, the fine gold-traced paintings showcased the utmost skill and craftsmanship. All of these structures were meticulously designed and built by cultivators possessing cultivation methods.

Below, an acting troupe had been hired to celebrate the new houses. They played instruments, sang, and danced. The intermingling of strings and woodwinds was profoundly captivating, filled with a jubilant atmosphere and the deafening clamor of gongs and drums. Countless spectators gathered by the roadside, and even Li Qi and his group joined in the fun.

Not far away, street performers were putting on illusion shows.

Looking at the illusion play, the stage was initially covered by dark curtains. Suddenly, a thunderclap rang out, and scenes materialized on the black canopy. Using a ram's horn to dye five-colored cloud qi, light and shadow were projected onto the black cloth. The flames of light were a dark, greenish-black, colored like the first light of dawn. They scattered to form bridges, then scaled the lunar caverns. The realm was magical, flickering in and out of existence with endless bizarre illusions that defied imagination. The plot was also meticulously crafted, making one forget it was merely a play. It was practically a grand holographic movie, although it didn't last long, only a quarter of an hour.

When the illusion play ended, even Li Qi couldn't help but applaud. Shen Shuibi even pulled out money for a tip, earning a scolding from Li Qi for being a spendthrift wife.

They then passed through a small street bazaar. The entire street was filled with merchants. There were shops and stalls; outside the stalls were awnings, and outside the awnings were more stalls. Every inch of space was occupied, blanketing the entire street.

Walking into it, they found themselves packed tightly together, shoulders rubbing and heels touching the people in front of them, all of whom were there to browse.

Within the bazaar, there were all sorts of ingenious crafts and rare exotics from distant lands. There was rouge, hairpins, earrings, ivory rulers, scissors, small dharma treasures, decorations, children's toys, musical instruments, and books.

Some items were genuine, others fake. Merchants and customers haggled with one another, creating such a ruckus that even the winter's cold qi was dispelled. The air was sweltering, making people feel the bustling energy, though it inevitably felt a bit noisy as well.

Li Qi only had about a dozen gold coins on him to begin with. After taking a single lap around this market, half of it was gone.

As it turned out, while Shen Shuibi was looking around, she spotted a white jade hairpin themed around the Jade Rabbit. She seemed rather happy to be used as a motif.

Seeing that Shen Shuibi was still wearing the woven copper-wire flower hairpin he had bought for her a year ago, Li Qi's heart was moved, and he purchased the jade one.

Shen Shuibi was delighted. She clutched it to her chest, laughing and chatting non-stop, looking as if she intended to change into it right after taking a bath when they returned.

In short, during this journey of barely an hour, Li Qi had fully witnessed the prosperity of this place.

Let alone other pleasures—such as fine sights and enchanting music, visiting brothels, constructing grand mansions, or howling at the moon and riding the winds—this level of opulence alone had already surpassed Li Qi's prior understanding.

A den of indulgence was truly worthy of the name. And this was merely the scene Li Qi experienced when he had no money and could only stroll through the streets.

If one were wealthy and overbearing, the joys to be found here would likely be beyond Li Qi's wildest imaginations.

So this was the joy of the rich.

Half an hour later, Li Qi and the others passed through several markets, only to find themselves arriving at an... arena?

One could even faintly smell the scent of blood here. There were several circular buildings, and constant noises drifted out from inside. Did it seem like fighting rings?

However, Piaoxu, acting as their guide, stopped right here. "We've arrived, gentlemen. This is the place where you can buy Windwalk Talismans."

"Huh? Here?" Li Qi looked at the arena, somewhat surprised.

There were indeed things being sold here, such as admission tickets and various standardized dharma artifacts. There were also quite a few portraits of gladiators hanging up, presumably for endorsements or something similar.

Speaking of standardized dharma treasures, Li Qi really had underestimated the level of industrialization in this world. During his time as a tracker, it was merely that no one could be bothered to pay attention to people as insignificant as dust. But in reality, rapid-production industrialized dharma artifacts also existed here. It was just that... they weren't very popular.

After all, dharma treasures generally needed to be custom-made or crafted by oneself to be the best. When it came to a person's personal cosmos, every individual was unique.

Whether one used someone else's dharma artifact or a so-called standardized dharma artifact, they could only unleash sixty to seventy percent of their efficacy. Only an item perfectly attuned to oneself could display its full power.

This was perhaps the reason why workshop-style handicraft industries still existed. Ultimately, items custom-made according to one's own conditions were the ones that truly suited them. Although standardized dharma artifacts were cheap, their effects left much to be desired.

For example, the Tang saber in his hand was a typical standardized weapon. Every Rank 9 soldier in the Tang army had one, along with matching armor.

However, with the techniques of the Tang Empire's Reason Dao, even this kind of standardized weapon was vastly superior to the vast majority of Rank 9 dharma artifacts.

By now, Li Qi had gained a modicum of understanding regarding the Tang Empire. The Tang Empire was primarily dominated by three major Daoist Traditions.

These were the Human Dao, the Daoist Sects, and the Reason Dao. Among them, the Human Dao held absolute authority. The Human Emperor, acting as an existence above Rank 1, had concurrently established a pact with the Reason Dao, reducing the Reason Dao to a vassal of the Human Dao.

For some unknown reason, the True Immortals of the Daoist Sects had also joined the Tang Empire's camp.

As a result, the sheer quantity and quality of the Tang Empire's equipment and pills were unimaginable. The Daoist Sects were supremely skilled in alchemy, and the Reason Dao's research into artifacts had reached the absolute pinnacle. This was what created the Tang Empire's tens of millions of iron cavalry that swept across All Under Heaven, never suffering a true defeat.

Therefore, the Tang saber in Li Qi's hand was far stronger than the standardized weapons before him. It could probably cleave through dozens of them consecutively without any effort.

Nevertheless, his true trump card was still the willow branch tied around his waist, which contained the divine qi of a Grand Invocator. To this day, it had never failed him.

Hearing the doubt in Li Qi's voice, Piaoxu quickly explained, "Sir, don't just see this place as an arena for gladiatorial combat. In truth, the interior holds the largest trading center for dharma artifacts and talismans below Rank 9 in Clear Mist City."

"I see. Then please lead the way," Li Qi said with a nod.

Piaoxu nodded and led the two of them further inside, arriving in the midst of the market.

Naturally, it was incredibly bustling, even more awe-inspiring than the free entertainment outside. Various craftsmen and refiners were showing off their skills and products. The sounds of clashing metal rang incessantly, and bursts of light scattered frequently. It felt like standing amidst steel and raging flames, with lethal weapons soaring through the air all around them.

Passing through the specialized zones for weapons and armor, the trio soon turned into the area where talismans were traded.

Meanwhile.

In the VIP spectator stands of the outer arena.

This was an entire private box. From here, one could command a sweeping view of the whole arena and observe the situation below.

And right now, inside this circular arena, the competition was named the "Ever-Victorious Warriors."

It was also the cruelest form of gambling here.

Why was it given this name?

Because, under this competition format, every single gladiator was an ever-victorious champion. There had never been a precedent of someone who had lost a match ever stepping back onto the field.

The reason was very simple: the loser was generally slain on the spot. That was the bloody spectacle the crowd loved to watch the most.

Every single time, the loser was eliminated, leaving only the victor behind.

Therefore, this format was named the "Ever-Victorious Warriors."

Inside the private box, a gorgeously dressed man suddenly opened his eyes. "Elder Si, I seem to have sensed your ghost qi. It's right down below. What's going on?"
