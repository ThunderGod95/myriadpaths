# Chapter 95: Minor Skill

However, the owner of that longsword was far less pleased.

It was a woman dressed in martial attire. She was tall, with solid, well-defined muscles. Her facial features were rather plain, and combined with a physique far more robust than an average woman's, she lacked traditional femininity.

She appeared to be in her thirties, exuding a valiant aura. Though not beautiful, she possessed a unique bearing that immediately proved she was no mere decoration.

She glanced at her severed longsword, then at Li Qi, defiance evident in her eyes.

"You only relied on the sharpness of your weapon," she stated. "That blade of yours is a Rank 9 sharp weapon, isn't it? Without it, you would already be dead."

But Li Qi couldn't be bothered with her. He turned to Shen Shuibi. "Miss Shen, are you alright? What happened? Why are these people making trouble?"

"I... I was just watching her practice the sword and muttered a few words to myself..." Shen Shuibi replied, looking deeply aggrieved.

She hadn't intended for anyone else to hear. Besides... what she said wasn't wrong.

Hearing this, the woman whose sword Li Qi had broken frowned and raised her head. "Muttered a few words to yourself? Little girl, you have no cultivation base, yet you noisily pass judgment on my sword techniques. I merely intended to teach you a lesson. Do not spout nonsense about things you do not understand."

"But... I do understand," Shen Shuibi mumbled softly, shrinking her neck and cowering behind Li Qi.

"Enough, elder sister," Li Qi said, raising his head to interrupt the exchange. "Let this matter end here. I—"

Before Li Qi could finish, the robust woman cut him off. "End here? I have practiced the sword for twenty-five years. I entered Rank 9 through the Sword Dao, and my swordsmanship has reached the realm of meticulous detail. Yet, she comes up and slanders a lifetime of my sword techniques, calling it a minor path. She must apologize."

Hearing this, Li Qi finally grasped the whole story.

As it turned out, this elder sister had likely been practicing her swordsmanship when Shen Shuibi spotted her.

Who was Shen Shuibi? Before crippling her own cultivation, she was a spirit beast of Mount Luofu—one of the Daoist Sects' top ten grotto-heavens. She was the Moon Essence Jade Rabbit, Lady Luofu's cherished companion, and a practitioner of orthodox profound sect cultivation methods at Rank 5!

As a genuine immortal being, watching Rank 9 swordsmanship naturally looked like child's play to her.

But her private muttering had been overheard by this elder sister.

This woman had practiced the sword her entire life. Hearing someone dismiss her life's pride, and then realizing the critic was a weakling with no cultivation, naturally spurred her to confront the girl and demand an apology.

This... it seemed neither side was entirely in the wrong. It was just a misunderstanding.

Furthermore, the other party was a Rank 9, and a woman. Could this be the Martial Affairs Hall Deacon, Jing Yan?

Having figured this out, Li Qi gave an awkward grin. He cupped his hands and said, "Then I will apologize to you on my little sister's behalf, elder sister. We spoke out of turn. Why don't we let this matter go?"

However, upon hearing Li Qi's words, Shen Shuibi lowered her head, pressed her face against his back, and grumbled, "I was just kindly reminding her that she was walking the wrong path..."

These words were once again overheard by the woman.

Her eyes widened, her face contorting with rage. "You dare say it again!"

With that, she reached out, grabbing directly toward Shen Shuibi!

Li Qi's expression darkened, and he immediately moved to block her.

This time, neither side used weapons. The moment they engaged, Li Qi felt a pliant force. Though it seemed soft, it clung like maggots to bone, redirecting him like a flowing current.

Fortunately, Li Qi was best at dealing with flowing water.

The Carp Stream Intent, which he practiced for over six hours every day, came into play. Like a carp sliding upstream, he carefully parted the currents and swam smoothly through them. He easily neutralized the pliant force and took the opportunity to observe the progress bar. Seizing the momentary gap as she withdrew her qi, he swatted her reaching hand aside. This left the careless woman's guard wide open, exposing her centerline!

Li Qi roused his internal qi once more, erupting with Carp Stream Intent. As he threw a punch, the speed of his fist skyrocketed!

Carp Stream Intent was fundamentally a cultivation method focused on evasion and maneuvering, followed by a sudden, explosive strike like leaping over the dragon gate when the enemy was unprepared.

This caught the woman entirely off guard and landed a direct hit.

However, Li Qi knew his limits and pulled his punch. Transforming his fist into a palm, he planted it squarely on her face, converting the immense striking power into a pushing force, and shoved the woman hard.

Fueled by an explosive force exceeding twenty thousand catties of strength, the woman immediately locked her waist and dropped into a horse stance to stabilize her center of gravity. Yet, she was still staggered by Li Qi's shove. She stumbled back several steps, stamping deep footprints into the stone floor before finally coming to a halt.

"Good martial arts. Who are you? Your blade work is mediocre, but your unarmed combat methods are decent. You have a good grasp of timing." Having suffered this loss, the woman was somewhat surprised. Yet, instead of being angry, she offered Li Qi a word of praise.

Thanks to the progress bar, Li Qi excelled at seizing opportunities. While others relied on instinct, he could intuitively see the progression of an opponent's moves, pinpointing the exact moment when their old force was spent and their new force had yet to generate.

Li Qi appraised the woman. It seemed she wasn't petty-minded; she simply placed immense value on the swordsmanship she had honed her entire life, which was why she had grown so furious.

"I am Li Qi. I was hired by Manager Jing to guard the caravan. Elder sister, could you be Jing Yan?" Li Qi responded, cupping his hands.

"I am Jing Yan. But trying to get close to me won't work. Your little sister spoke far out of turn. Ask her yourself what she said! I began practicing the sword at age seven, and after twenty years of cultivation, I finally entered the ranks. I am now thirty-two, having practiced the sword for twenty-five full years—a span longer than she has been alive. What right does she have to critique my Sword Dao?" Jing Yan had not forgotten about Shen Shuibi. Though polite to Li Qi, she still looked highly displeased when glancing at the rabbit-turned-girl, absolutely insisting on an apology.

But Shen Shuibi absolutely refused to apologize. The rabbit seemed to possess her own pride regarding her statements, continuing to shrink behind Li Qi.

She looked incredibly cowardly.

Yet, as she cowered, she uttered the most hurtful words in the most timid tone imaginable. "What Dao... Swordsmanship is merely a minor skill. It's just a weapon; how dare it be called a Dao? You don't know the immensity of heaven and earth..."

That single sentence instantly ignited Jing Yan. Flames of fury roared to life in her eyes. Though unarmed, she took a step forward, looking as though she was about to strike again.

Seeing this, Li Qi immediately shielded Shen Shuibi, stepped forward, and cupped his hands. "Please quell your anger, Elder Sister Jing. My little sister is not as frail as she appears; it's simply that the Dao she cultivates is ill-suited for combat. She merely pointed out your flaws out of the goodness of her heart. As a martial artist, can you truly not even accept this much?"

"Point out my flaws? Her?" Jing Yan raised her chin, not believing it for a second. However, with Li Qi blocking her path, it was difficult for her to attack.

"Yes, her. If you don't believe it, Elder Sister Jing, how about we make a wager?" Li Qi suggested, his eyes darting thoughtfully.

He had a plan—one that could resolve this misunderstanding while simultaneously bringing them closer together.
