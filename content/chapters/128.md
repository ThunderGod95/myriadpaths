# Chapter 128: A Chance Encounter

Before the two sides had even met face-to-face, the first round of their clash had already concluded.

Li Qi had fallen slightly short, but fortunately, with Shen Shuibi present, the outcome wasn't too disastrous.

However, Li Qi had found a blessing in disguise. He managed to purify his Dao Heart, ridding himself of the unsettling arrogance he'd been developing and laying a solid foundation for his future progress.

This was far more valuable than any dao rhythm from an other world.

And according to the rabbit, his arrival in this world for tempering was actually arranged by his teacher.

That made everything make sense.

It explained why the ruler of the Great Deer Kingdom had suddenly made room for him, and why the Governor of White Snake Prefecture, a dignified Rank 6, had actively invited him.

He had genuinely believed it was due to his own prestige, but looking back now, it was all part of the arrangement.

Since that was the case, he needed to perform even better.

Shen Shuibi had told him that two-thirds of their purpose for coming here was already fulfilled. The remaining one-third definitely wouldn't just be the easily obtainable dao rhythm.

What could it be?

What was the remaining trial, or rather, the test?

While pondering this situation, Li Qi also marveled at his teacher's grand methods.

To set up a mere test, he had thrown him into a world roughly the size of Earth to play around in.

This was truly...

While he was lost in these thoughts, night fell, and the vehicle came to a stop within a city.

"Young Master, will we be resting here for the night? That Zhang Le has booked a hotel for us," Bai Di said as he opened the car door.

"You all head to the hotel. I want to take a walk by myself," Li Qi nodded and replied.

"Understood." Bai Di didn't say anything more.

Shen Shuibi stretched her back. The rabbit was ready for sleep. Protecting Li Qi all day had thoroughly exhausted her.

However, she cast a glance at Li Qi.

Looking at his current state.

The remaining one-third should be completed soon as well.

As for how she knew?

Naturally, it was because she had already walked this path herself.

Ah, it felt like an older sister who had already graduated university watching her younger brother struggle with quadratic equations.

It was somewhat cute.

"_Yawn_...!" Shen Shuibi let out a wide yawn.

These matters could wait for later.

Sleep came first!

On Li Qi's end, he didn't follow Shen Shuibi and Bai Di straight to the so-called hotel to rest.

He suddenly felt that ever since arriving in this world, he had been entirely caught up in trivial, superficial matters.

He hadn't stopped even once to take a proper look at this world.

This was something he had realized earlier.

An Invocator was one who presided over sacrificial rites, the person responsible for communicating with heaven, earth, ghosts and gods.

Since he was an Invocator who had come to this realm, did he truly understand its heaven, its earth, and the people, ghosts, and gods living within it?

If he couldn't even achieve a basic understanding, how could he possibly coordinate heaven, earth, humans, and gods?

Undoubtedly, it was impossible. Without understanding, there could be no coordination.

If he couldn't do that, could he still be considered an Invocator?

The Dao was the law of the external heaven and earth, as well as the guiding principle of human behavior.

If he failed to fulfill the duties of an Invocator and didn't hold his actions to an Invocator's standards, wasn't this behavior akin to losing the Dao?

As mediators and communicators, Invocators inherently walked the world by borrowing the power of heaven, earth, mountains, rivers, ghosts, gods, and even all myriad things.

Could one who had lost the Dao obtain such power?

Li Qi had once heard a saying: "He who loses the Dao finds little support." It meant that if you lost your righteous stance, no one would come to your aid.

What was a righteous stance?

First was being upright—upright in character and upright in reason. Just as Li Qi had once reprimanded the Rank 8 Ghost-God Old Man Li, one had to be impartial and coordinate things fairly.

Next was righteousness. Through his reflections, Li Qi concluded that righteousness should be the principle that aligned with the masses and with the entire world.

The Invocator texts stated that righteousness was the focal point of sacrifices. The ancients said, "In the court, clarify the righteousness of ranks and titles to lead the order of young and old."

The righteousness here referred to the appropriate laws and ceremonial rites, which were intrinsically the most crucial aspects of any sacrifice.

So, was upright righteousness the Dao of an Invocator?

Or, to put it another way, was upright righteousness what Li Qi believed to be the Dao of an Invocator?

Or perhaps this wasn't the Dao of an Invocator, but rather Li Qi's very own Dao?

Walking the streets of this unfamiliar other world, Li Qi's mind swirled with countless questions regarding cultivation.

Yet, he couldn't grasp an answer no matter how hard he tried.

The truth was, the answer wasn't fixed. He could choose anything, but once he chose, he had to act upon it and walk the path he had decided on.

His inability to choose an answer simply meant that Li Qi himself didn't know how to proceed on his future path.

"Alas, my life experience is still too shallow," he sighed.

_How could someone at Rank 9 possess absolute unwavering certainty about their future? I should just observe more and learn more. As long as I maintain reverence and vigilance in my heart at all times, keeping my Dao Heart clear, I will naturally find my own answer after experiencing more of the world. And that will become my Dao._ Li Qi silently etched this thought into his mind.

Having figured this out, he took a deep breath, feeling his entire body become significantly lighter!

But when he turned his head, he realized he had wandered into some unknown place.

While deep in thought just now, he seemed to have walked too far without watching where he was going, ending up in the middle of nowhere.

There were no longer towering skyscrapers around him. The bustling city lay far behind, standing alone in the middle of the wilderness.

Li Qi suddenly came to a realization.

In the Earth Oil World, the various cities seemed incredibly prosperous, but the expanses between them appeared to be entirely desolate wastelands like the one he was in right now.

Vitality only existed within the cities.

However... this wasteland still contained traces of human qi.

Using his qi-observing spell art, he could spot a human settlement not far away.

Wherever humans gathered, there was human qi. Naturally, this human qi would form a barrier, warding off various wild miscellaneous qi like poison qi and miasma.

This was why humans needed to live together in settlements; those who ventured alone into the wilderness would likely have their bodies ruined by the miscellaneous qi.

"How could there be human qi in a place like this? People actually live out here?" Li Qi was a bit curious and walked in that direction.

Over the past few days, his arrogance had blinded him from truly observing this world, which nearly led him into a trap. That was exactly why he wanted to change.

Since he had declared his intent to change, to see more and learn more, he should start right now.

The best time to plant a tree was ten years ago; the second best time is now.

He would go and actively understand this world right now.

Li Qi was a man of action; once he made a decision, he executed it immediately.

Accelerating his pace, he darted rapidly across the wasteland.

Soon enough, Li Qi genuinely spotted a human settlement out in the wild.

These people didn't seem to be permanent residents, because Li Qi saw... a convoy.

Yes, a convoy.

It included two tanker trucks, four pickup trucks, seven or eight small sedans, and a few motorcycles.

Most of the people wore tattered clothes and gave off a foul stench. Their skin was rough, caked in grime and dust, looking as though they hadn't washed in a very long time.

The convoy consisted of roughly twenty to thirty people—men, women, and even children—resembling a small, mobile village.

Li Qi didn't rush over recklessly. Instead, using his senses that far surpassed those of ordinary mortals, he stood in the dark and listened in on their conversations.

Within that makeshift camp of vehicles.

They had arranged the cars into a circle, barely blocking the direct brunt of the chilling winds outside.

It was still bitterly cold, but it was bearable.

Since the surrounding area was full of dead forests, there was no shortage of fuel. They had chopped down some withered branches and easily started a bonfire.

Canned food was roasting over the fire, sizzling audibly. It was likely scavenged from the cities.

Almost everyone had some sort of battered firearm or rusted cold weapon in their hands.

It looked exactly like... a scene from a post-apocalyptic wasteland.

Yet, it still felt vibrant with life.

The women and children stayed together, talking and laughing in a rather heartwarming manner.

A few younger men lingered nearby, chatting with different women. At times, a comfortable silence would fall between them, filled only with shared smiles and unspoken glances.

On the other side of the camp, four or five robust middle-aged men were huddled together, discussing something.

Among them, a particularly muscular man suddenly looked up and asked the person next to him, "Number Two, how much water do we have left?"

"Not much. Enough for two or three days," the middle-aged man called Number Two replied.

"For using or drinking?" the muscular man pressed.

Number Two answered truthfully, "Drinking. If we use it for washing, it won't even last a day. A few days ago, when those boys got hurt, we had to use water to clean their wounds, so our reserves are running low."

"What about food?" the man asked again.

"We have enough food. The transport truck we hijacked the other day was full of it; the pickups are packed tight. We can last a month no problem. But we're low on canned goods. It's mostly biscuits and sugar—it must have been a dessert transport. Without water, though, that stuff is hard to swallow," Number Two replied.

The middle-aged men fell into a collective silence.

Water was incredibly precious. It only existed in areas where the earth oil hadn't completely dried up.

In any place where the earth oil was depleted, the land turned entirely into a desert, forests withered away, and all life ceased to exist. For drifters like them wandering the wilderness, supplies were an eternal crisis.

Ever since earth oil was discovered and harvested over a hundred years ago, the desertification had spread uncontrollably.

These ordinary people didn't know the exact reason; they only knew it was tied to the earth oil.

However, Li Qi, observing from the sidelines, naturally understood.

Without earth oil, the earth qi and water qi ceased to exist. This caused the qi of the Five Elements to weaken and dry up, ultimately turning the area into a dead land.

"Those corporations... they really don't care about human lives as long as they get their money!" One man slammed his fist into the dirt, looking utterly enraged.

The leader waved his hand, motioning for the man to calm down, and then said, "Alright, complaining won't do us any good. Let's focus on how to get water. Do we dare hit an earth oil extraction site?"

Earth oil extraction sites definitely had water, and usually featured large lakes and rivers.

"People will die," Number Two frowned.

"If we don't go, even more will die. In two or three days when the water's gone, who's going to have the strength to lift a gun? Or run? We might as well do it now while we still have energy," the muscular man argued.

"That makes sense, but it's an earth oil extraction site..." Number Two said, clearly troubled.

Earth oil extraction sites were incredibly dangerous places. The quality and equipment of their guards were on a completely different level compared to the usual convoys traversing the wasteland.

"Let's discuss tactics. If we target a smaller earth oil extraction site, we might not suffer casualties. We can totally join forces with other convoys and storm it together," the muscular man finalized the decision.

Following this, the group—who were clearly the decision-makers of the convoy—huddled close to hash out tactics and discuss their armaments.

However, their discussion had barely begun when suddenly, many people throughout the camp perked their ears and looked toward the nearby darkness.

A short distance away stood a person dressed in bright, pristine clothing, his body completely spotless.

Compared to the grime and raggedness of everyone else, he was simply too clean.

_Shwa!_ Instantly, a barrage of guns were raised and trained on Li Qi. Those wielding cold weapons tightened their grips on their hilts—even the children were no exception.

"It's a city dweller!" the muscular leader immediately shouted.

He then shot a quick look to Number Two beside him. Number Two nodded, quietly slipping away into the darkness to scout the perimeter.

City dwellers absolutely never left their cities!

If they did, they were guaranteed to be accompanied by guards.

Was this a guard squad sent to wipe them out? They had to scout the surroundings first.

With a multitude of guns pointed at him, Li Qi decisively raised his hands. "Relax, I came alone."

"Why would a city dweller come out here?!" The leader aimed his gun at Li Qi, his face deadly serious.

As he spoke, he stepped forward.

In the background, the women and children immediately began packing up their belongings and boarding the vehicles.

The men, on the other hand, all readied their weapons, finding cover, scouting, and setting up covering fire lines.

The few decision-makers gripped their firearms and approached Li Qi with utmost vigilance, ready to open fire at the slightest sign of trouble.

"I mean no harm. I was just a little curious about you all, that's it. I don't have any guards with me," Li Qi said loudly, keeping his hands raised.

"I just want to ask you a few questions."

But the opposing side didn't relax in the slightest. Their muscles remained taut, their wariness unabated.

Only after several minutes did the scouts return. Number Two ran back and whispered something into the leader's ear.

Only then did the leader lower his weapon. He frowned at Li Qi and asked, "You want to ask questions?"

"That's right, I just want to ask a few questions. And, if you answer them, I will reward you with clean water!"

As soon as Li Qi said those words, the crowd perked up immediately.

"Water? Where's the water!"

"Where is it?! Let us see it first!"

Several young men aimed their guns at Li Qi again, shouting loudly for him to hand over the water.

"I obviously don't have it on me, but I do have water. However, you need to answer my questions first," Li Qi emphasized once more.

A few of the younger men lost their patience, preparing to rush forward and beat Li Qi up.

Answer questions? Fat chance. If he wouldn't talk, they'd beat it out of him!

There was no need to be polite to a city dweller!

But the middle-aged leader barked, "Get back here!"

The young men reluctantly stepped back, though they still glared fiercely.

The middle-aged man, however, walked up and scrutinized Li Qi. He then said, "Are you a reporter? I've heard that city dwellers have this kind of job. You always run around trying to interrogate people, only to take their answers out of context."

"Uh... I guess you could call me a reporter," Li Qi replied, his expression turning slightly awkward.
