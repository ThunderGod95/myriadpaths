# Chapter 194: The Terrifying Rabbit

Outside the Buddhist Pagoda.

Two days had already passed.

Shen Shuibi was burying her head in drawing an array formation diagram.

Beside her, Wang Boyan wore an expression of deep suffering. "Horse King Shen, the resources you're requisitioning are things the entire Vast Yang City couldn't possibly afford. I had to personally ask the main branch just to gather this many materials..."

"Is the _Profound Gate's Scripture of the Treasure Sea_ not enough? That can't be right. Is the Rank 8 standing behind you really that blind to its value?" Shen Shuibi casually replied, not even lifting her head.

"It's... it's enough." At the mention of this, Wang Boyan felt his teeth ache, and his expression grew even more bitter.

He was currently at a bit of a loss as to what kind of sacred being this new Horse King in front of him actually was.

Wang Boyan had investigated this new Horse King's methods.

Her schemes were complex and colossal, each link tightly interlocked, much like a grandmaster maneuvering pieces on a chessboard.

It took her a mere sixteen hours to effortlessly seize all power over the Lower Nine Sects, firmly grasping everything within her hands.

According to rumors, she had planted informants in every single branch and business of the Lower Nine Sects.

She knew every conspiracy aimed at her like the back of her hand. She constantly maintained an aura of mystery, was perpetually temperamental, and somehow held the weaknesses of everyone she met, even if it was their very first encounter.

She could always grasp the ultimate goal within the labyrinth of power struggles. Her train of thought was utterly unrestrained, leaving her subordinates completely unable to predict her next move.

Because of this, whether they were her subordinates or her enemies, everyone lived in a constant state of fear and anxiety.

If slaughtering every last person in the Lower Nine Sects had a difficulty of one, then thoroughly controlling the Lower Nine Sects had a difficulty of at least ten.

Because not everyone was willing to be controlled. Some people would rather die to uphold their traditions and gang rules.

Yet, none of this could hide before her.

No one could forget that just four hours after she became the new Horse King, the Lower Nine Sects immediately launched a series of counterattacks. This included power struggles, assassinations, poisonings, using tradition to force her to step down, and inciting the lower ranks. Yet, she emerged completely unscathed. She even turned the tables, inciting defection among her enemies or outright destroying them. This undoubtedly proved the sheer cunning of this new Horse King to everyone else.

Especially during conversations, she loved using a casual, careless tone, as if she were accidentally bringing something up, to shock the majority of the involved parties into a cold sweat.

For example, when facing Wang Boyan, she lightly mentioned the Wang Clan of Jiannan.

When such things happened, those 'warned individuals' would quickly realize they had no idea how this new Horse King knew these secrets. They would immediately perceive that they had no choice but to treat her with the utmost respect.

It was as if she held the secrets of everyone in the palm of her hand.

Furthermore, she would sometimes leak absurdly fake information. However, these rumors would inevitably turn into reality, as if they had been true from the very beginning.

For example, the moment they met, she declared that the old Horse King looked like he was about to die.

This startled the old Horse King on the spot. Enraged, he wanted to kill her, but because the fury attacked his heart, he lost consciousness and collapsed before he could even finish giving the order.

No one believed the old Horse King had merely succumbed to a fit of rage. The old Horse King was a Rank 8 expert; how could he be so fragile?

This was absolutely a trick played by this new Horse King!

But no one knew how she managed it. She even went up to save him with a face full of exasperation, claiming that the old Horse King's cultivation method had gone awry, damaging his heart meridians more the longer he practiced it. She suggested he submit to her, and she would help improve it.

This was clearly a threat!

Countless people discovered that their leverage and weaknesses had long been seen through by her, and the only path to liberation had long been padlocked.

And the key—was firmly grasped in her hand.

She played everyone in the palm of her hand.

This was a terrifying woman.

Wang Boyan deeply understood the terrifying nature of this person, so hearing her previous remark made his teeth ache again.

When did she find out that a Rank 8 from the main branch had arrived?

Did she even have informants planted within the Grain Alliance?!

What else did she know?

Wang Boyan couldn't possibly know the answers. The only thing he knew for certain was the unfathomable depth and temperamental nature of this new Horse King.

She truly was moody and unpredictable; you simply couldn't gauge whether she trusted you, liked you, or loathed you.

In most situations, she was extremely cold to everyone. Yet, occasionally, she would suddenly toss out a heavy gift to someone—such as an advanced cultivation method, or guidance to solve a bottleneck that had plagued them for years. These were monumental boons, capable of changing a life... or even the destiny of an entire clan.

A superior cultivation method—how many generations of a clan would normally have to struggle to earn such a thing?

But now, she casually tossed them away as if they were nothing.

Yet, at other times, she would become extremely irritable. Especially when her eyes turned red, many people would suffer as a result. Though they weren't beheaded, they lost their positions.

You must absolutely never assume that just because she gave you a cultivation method, she valued you. If you presumptuously tried to curry favor, you would most likely be met with a cold face, or she might not even remember who you were.

Moody and unpredictable, yet controlling the entire board, knowing the secrets of countless people. It was as if her spies and scouts were stationed right beside everyone.

You didn't even know if your friends, your brothers, or even your wife were spying on you. Were they taking orders from this new Horse King?

Because there were many things that only the most intimate people should know.

But as it turned out, this new Horse King knew them too, and she would casually say them out loud, terrifying listeners to their cores.

Incomprehensible. Indescribable.

Exactly what kind of person could do such things?

But these thoughts were things Wang Boyan only dared to keep bottled up inside his stomach.

At this moment, standing before this new Horse King, he could only smile bitterly and reply, "Horse King Shen, while the price is indeed sufficient in comparison... to transport items from the Tang Empire all the way to Baiyue, we still have to bypass informants in many regions. Taking it slowly might be fine, but you need it urgently. This exceeds the scope of my abilities. It's not something that can be achieved just because the price is right..."

"Then send over however much you have," Shen Shuibi replied, still not lifting her head.

"Naturally. It's just that I still need a two-day buffer. With you needing it so urgently, even I am at a loss for solutions," Wang Boyan complained bitterly.

"Fine," Shen Shuibi replied concisely.

Seeing her agree so readily while remaining buried in the work at hand, Wang Boyan didn't say anything more and obediently retreated.

For the entire day, Shen Shuibi had been busy with her task: the array formation diagram she was currently drawing.

This array formation could bypass the Buddhist Pagoda's blockade and establish contact with Li Qi inside.

At present, restoring contact with Li Qi was the most important thing. Thus, Shen Shuibi had essentially set aside all other matters. She had even suspended her deductions on the cultivation method to restore her Yang Spirit, dedicating all her efforts to researching and developing this array diagram.

She didn't know why, but without Li Qi by her side, she felt terribly anxious.

Even though she used to always be alone.

It was just that the rabbit had never realized that she had never truly been alone in the past.

Back in the Mount Luofu grotto-heaven, whether she was physically alone or not, she always had a sense of security in her heart.

She knew that the Lady was always watching her, and that Yang Ning could find her no matter what.

Therefore, having stayed on Mount Luofu since birth, even when she was alone, she wasn't afraid. Her heart was completely relaxed.

But things were different now.

Mount Luofu was gone.

The Lady was gone.

Yang Ning had gone to a faraway place.

Previously, Li Qi had still been by her side, able to help her solve so many things.

But now, Li Qi was gone too.

From birth until now, this was the first time Shen Shuibi had ever encountered such a situation.

For the first time, she was truly alone.

Because of this, her heart was filled with an inexplicable anxiety. She frantically wanted to regain contact.

Fortunately, she could still maintain her basic Dao Heart and understood what she needed to do at this moment.

She still had one way to contact Li Qi—by using a method Monk Chanzhi could never anticipate to bypass the blockade of his Buddhist Pagoda.

Thus, everything was progressing in an orderly manner.

---

Meanwhile, on the other side, Li Qi was also engaged in grueling mental labor every day.

He was also pondering how to establish contact with the outside world.

In fact, after a full two days had passed, he had already come up with a solution.

The next step was implementation. Right now, he was right under Monk Chanzhi's nose and couldn't do anything that would arouse suspicion, otherwise things would go south very quickly.

Monk Chanzhi was very amiable toward him, but Li Qi wouldn't dare act presumptuously.

His little life was more important.

Another twenty hours passed. Li Qi had finished analyzing the third devil, named the Vehement Fury Devil.

Just like the Desire-Mind Devil, the Vehement Fury Devil also manifested a mental trend.

It was the trend of an orderly state of mind gradually tilting toward a stage of disorder.

No matter how good one's patience was, it would gradually wear away. Anger would only mount, accumulating more and more extensively until it absolutely had to erupt to be quelled.

Li Qi had already analyzed three devils, and all three shared this commonality.

They all represented a trend...

No, not represented. They _were_ the trends themselves.

These devils naturally existed within all living beings possessing the capacity for thought and spiritual wisdom.

Were these... the kins of the Heavenly Devil?

According to Chanzhi, what he had devoured were the kins of the Paranirmita-vasavartin Devaputra Mara[^1], commonly known by others as the kins of the Heavenly Devil.

These devils could hinder a person's supreme goodness, hate and envy the dharmas of sages, and cause all sorts of disturbances. They were most fond of sneaking into a cultivator's mind, devouring their Dao Heart, and becoming a part of their devilish thoughts. Ultimately, they would completely destroy the cultivator's Dao Heart and cause them to fall into the Devil Dao.

Furthermore, these were merely kins.

The true Heavenly Devil was incomparably terrifying; they weren't as simple as Rank 9 entities.

According to what Li Qi had read in books, the minions of the Heavenly Devil were spread across all the heavens and myriad realms. They were an incredibly massive and horrifying faction.

But none of that had anything to do with the current Li Qi. The were extremely rare in All Under Heaven. It was said that Almighty beings had intervened to expel the Heavenly Devil, resulting in them only being able to run rampant Beyond the Boundary.

As for the cultivation method to break the Vehement Fury Devil, Li Qi had formulated it shortly after finishing his analysis. It also utilized the Shaman Dao's balancing methods, accumulating the qi of the Winter King to clarify the inner mind and suppress the fury.

Following that, he once again ascended to the fifth floor and demonstrated the dharma for Monk Chanzhi.

Monk Chanzhi naturally waited for him with a smile, presenting him with a brand new bed, bedding, and some food.

These were the items Li Qi had requested earlier, asking for something to eat and a bed to sleep on.

Monk Chanzhi likely felt these were perfectly normal requests and had prepared them for Li Qi.

Once the dharma demonstration was complete, Li Qi exchanged a few pleasantries with Monk Chanzhi, then hoisted the bed onto his shoulders and carried it downstairs, setting it up right in the center of the second floor.

He then obediently lay down on the bed and went to sleep.

At this moment, Monk Chanzhi's mind should be entirely focused on experimenting with the new cultivation method.

However, Monk Chanzhi, who knew nothing of the Shaman Dao, could never have anticipated this.

A bed was also a type of dharma altar.

It even possessed its own dedicated gods, much like the inner door god and the stove god. As the saying goes, 'Buy sweets to welcome the Stove Emperor, pour water to worship the Bed Lord.'[^2] These sayings all alluded to the mystical nature of the bed.

The Shaman Dao possessed a myriad of profound and wondrous methods, making it impossible to defend against them all.

This was the unique characteristic of the Shaman Dao; no one else could learn it.

Li Qi had placed the bed in the exact center of the second floor, with the headboard facing north. This placement was meticulous. The center of a room corresponded to the Five Yellow of the _Luoshu Square_, the supreme position among the Nine Palaces.[^3]

This was called 'lifting the four corners,'[^4] unifying the four directions upon the bed.

Originally, a bed should face east or south. This was known as 'sleeping always with the head to the east, resting in alignment with vital qi.'[^5] The [Sayings of the School] stated: 'The living face south, the dead face north.'[^6]

All these arrangements were designed to align this dharma altar more closely with the aspect of Yin.

Only in this way could Li Qi accomplish what he wanted to do.

That is, his ethereal soul and corporeal soul leaving his body, perhaps even attracting ghost messengers!

He was going to use his Rank 9 cultivation base to attempt an astral journey Beyond the Boundary!

Ordinarily, doing this was a guaranteed death sentence, because traveling Beyond the Boundary presented three major problems.

First, the ethereal soul and corporeal soul leaving the body would normally result in death. Only the Yin Spirit of the Daoist Sects and the golden body of the Divine Dao could safely leave the body. The Shaman Dao didn't possess such a technique. Forcibly separating the souls from the body would certainly result in the physical body's death.

Second, the souls needed to resist the various dangers Beyond the Boundary. Souls separated from the body were incredibly fragile; even a gust of wind could inflict heavy damage. Only a few Dao paths could offer resistance. The Yin Spirit did not fear the wind, the Yang Spirit did not fear fire, and the golden body was immune to external stimuli. Once one reached the Yang Spirit realm, they could freely wander astrally, and the outside world could no longer harm their souls.

A shaman's soul was very fragile and would be injured by the slightest stimulation.

Third, one still needed to establish a navigational anchor.

If one got lost Beyond the Boundary, they could probably forget about ever coming back in this lifetime.

With good luck, one might find another minor world to stay in, then slowly figure out a way to locate All Under Heaven. With bad luck, one would simply drift Beyond the Boundary until death.

The sights of Beyond the Boundary that Li Qi had witnessed were all illusory in their scale. They looked vast, but Li Qi estimated the distances were measured in light-years. Even flying at the speed of a soul, he couldn't traverse them in a lifetime.

But it was precisely because doing this would lead to death that Monk Chanzhi wouldn't be on guard against it.

He had imprisoned Li Qi's physical body, and Li Qi's souls couldn't escape either. This gave him peace of mind.

For the past two days, Li Qi had also been obediently breaking the devilish thoughts for him, using the Shaman Dao and the True Knowledge dao rhythm to help him resist the devils.

In Li Qi's view, there was no way the monk could imagine he would actually dare to put his life on the line.

Moreover, even if he did guess it, Li Qi still had to try.

Trying didn't cost money. Over the past two days, he had already proven his value. Monk Chanzhi wouldn't casually kill him.

Li Qi also had confidence he wouldn't die.

Because he still had a hidden trump card.

He was now someone who possessed living shrines in ten thousand households.

Among those hundred thousand commoners, some were already devoutly praying to Li Qi, establishing shrines and making offerings to express their gratitude to their benefactor!

Li Qi planned to take an astral journey Beyond the Boundary and consecrate himself to become a god!

Relying on the prayers of these commoners, he would be able to use the Divine Dao's cultivation methods to forge a temporary golden body for himself, resisting the external dangers.

Then, using those commoners' prayers as an anchor, he could locate All Under Heaven and contact Shen Shuibi.

Once that was done, everything else would be easy to handle.

This was Li Qi's plan.

One could say good deeds reap good rewards. If Li Qi hadn't gone out of his way to help them, he certainly wouldn't have this trump card now.

But then again, if he hadn't gone to help them, perhaps Monk Chanzhi wouldn't have targeted him in the first place.

A drink and a peck are all preordained by karma. Who could really say for sure?

[^2]: This refers to ancient Chinese folk religion, where everyday household objects and spaces are overseen by specific minor deities.
    
    **The Stove Emperor (Zao Shen / Kitchen God):** He watches over the household's daily affairs. Before the Lunar New Year, he ascends to Heaven to report on the family to the Jade Emperor. Families traditionally offer him sticky sweets (like malt sugar) to 'sweeten his words' so he only reports good things, or to stick his jaws shut so he can't speak ill of them.
    
    **The Bed Lord (Chuang Gong):** The deity (often paired with a Bed Mother) who oversees the bedroom, marital harmony, and the safety of sleeping individuals and children. Offering a simple cup of water or tea is a humble, traditional gesture of daily reverence to keep the sleeping space peaceful.
[^3]: This is rooted in Chinese numerology, Feng Shui, and the I Ching (Book of Changes).

    **The Luoshu Square (洛书):** A sacred 3x3 mathematical square where the numbers in every row, column, and diagonal add up exactly to 15. It maps the qi flow grid of the universe.
    
    **The Nine Palaces (九宫):** The 9 individual squares within the Luoshu grid, each corresponding to a direction, element, and qi type.
    
    **Five Yellow (五黄):** The number 5 sits in the absolute, exact center of the Luoshu Square. In Feng Shui, the center represents the Earth element, the Emperor, and the supreme anchor point of any space.
    
    By placing the bed in the exact center of the room, Li Qi is claiming the "Emperor's seat" of the room, giving his makeshift altar maximum authority over the surrounding space.
[^4]: The center commands the periphery. By occupying the ultimate central point (the Five Yellow), Li Qi conceptually 'lifts' or takes control of the four corners (Northwest, Northeast, Southwest, Southeast) and the four cardinal directions. He is locking down the entire room's qi to serve his ritual.
[^5]: In the Five Elements (Wuxing), the East is associated with the Wood element, Spring, the rising sun, and Sheng Qi (vital, life-giving, generating energy). Traditional Chinese medicine and Feng Shui dictate that pointing your head East while sleeping allows your body to absorb this revitalizing energy to heal and grow.
[^6]: The South is associated with fire, summer, warmth, and ultimate Yang energy. Emperors always sat facing South to rule the living. Therefore, the living are meant to align with the South.

    The North is associated with water, winter, cold, shadows, and ultimate Yin energy. Graveyards and tombs are traditionally aligned on a North-South axis, with the deceased's head pointing North.
