# Chapter 149: Arrangements

Inside the inn.

Jin Buhuan flourished the flawless, innate divine weapon, unable to suppress the excitement surging in his heart.

For so many years, he had tracked clues everywhere, paying an unimaginable price, even going so far as to seek out Elder Si specifically to perform a divination for him.

Finally, he had restored this divine weapon to completion.

_This is the very foundation of my standing in the world!_

With this divine weapon in hand, his strength truly made him the most powerful among all his brothers. It utterly solidified his position as the heir, ensuring no one could threaten his standing.

_No..._

_Wait, didn't another streak of Rank 8 metal qi recently manifest in Green Mountain?_

_Both of my older brothers are currently stationed at Green Mountain._

_Tsk, so this is what Li Qi meant by 'trouble ahead.' Is this the omen of 'yin and yang shifting positions, separating the two polarities'?_

_If I don't handle this, and one of those two brothers obtains that other streak of Rank 8 metal qi, won't they have the capital to challenge me again?_

Jin Buhuan feared neither of them individually. But if they joined forces, armed with a Rank 8 innate divine weapon of their own...

_Tsk, how troublesome._

_However, this also indirectly prove that Li Qi's words hold merit._

_Once the matters here are settled, I must return to Green Mountain._

_Furthermore, it seems this Li Qi isn't quite as deserving of death anymore._

_Hmm... I'll let him off the hook. Making a friend out of him isn't a bad idea, though I still need to test him and keep my guard up._

_After all, this man is still actively using the method of 'treating the heart as a drumhead and qi and blood as drumsticks to generate disruptive sound and agitate qi,' thereby masking his surface thoughts to prevent me from reading his mind._

While pondering these matters, Jin Buhuan turned to Li Qi with a face full of smiles. "Well then, Brother Li, you mentioned wanting to spar with me. I won't hold back, then. We'll spare each other's lives, but otherwise, there are no restrictions!"

He then turned his head back and added, "Elder Si, keep an eye on us. Don't let either of us fail to pull our punches and damage our amiable relationship."

"Of course." Elder Si nodded, agreeing to the request.

Li Qi also cupped his hands in a martial salute. "Let us begin, then. Perhaps Brother Jin can help me break through to the next level?"

"Haha, I hope so! Come, if you please!"

With those words, Jin Buhuan extended a hand and lightly tapped his long blade.

A crisp _ding_ rang out, followed by an eruption of metallic reverberations that caused Li Qi's heart to skip a beat!

Despite being a long blade, it emitted the grand cacophony of a dozen cymbals interspersed with the reedy, mournful wail of a bamboo flute. The sound was enough to dry up one's heartblood, induce full-body tremors, and shatter one's wits. For a brief moment, Li Qi lost his focus entirely.

Li Qi immediately roused his qi and blood, releasing a thunderous shout! With that, he finally snapped out of his stupor.

Yet, when he refocused his gaze, countless blade edges had already materialized before him, whistling as they slashed downward!

The myriad blades whipped left and spun right, moving as swiftly as a tempestuous storm.

Li Qi instantly sank his qi to his dantian. Channeling the heavenly qi and earth qi within his body, he unleashed another mighty bellow!

Earth qi ascended, heavenly qi descended. Yin and yang ground against each other, heaven and earth clashed together, drumming up the force of thunder.

Utilizing the qi of his personal cosmos, Li Qi merged the energies within his lungs and expelled a voice like roaring thunder.

His qi burst forth like a thunderclap, utterly obliterating the countless blade edges formed from the soundwaves.

But in the very next instant, the soundwaves transformed again!

The blade had seemingly turned into a percussion instrument. Both the edge and the flat of the blade projected entirely different sonic torrents, carrying countless bursts of sharp metal qi. These lacerated the ground into a ruined mess and shredded all surrounding furniture into sawdust.

Jin Buhuan stood rooted in place, continuing to tap out a rhythm on the blade.

However, the scene playing out before Li Qi's eyes was entirely different.

Before him, the soundwaves boiled and churned like a cosmic cauldron boiling the stars themselves.

It sounded as though cannons were detonating right next to his ears. The fire of his consciousness flickered erratically, like a lone candle in a dark, stormy night.

Everything in his vision flashed with the brilliance of lightning and cascading golden sparks, dazzling the eyes. It felt as catastrophic as a thunderous axe cleaving a cliff face, sending him plunging into a bottomless abyss, terrifying enough to strip a spectator of their soul.

Before Li Qi could react, the illusion shifted into ten thousand raised candles. The reflective firelight and starlight boiled and shook the world around him. It was like a nightmare, a fever dream, stripping him of his hearing and speech.

He could clearly hear a cacophony of infinite sounds, yet simultaneously felt as though he could hear absolutely nothing.

His mind grew hazy; his mental defenses were crumbling.

However, despite momentarily losing his ability to react, he still managed to successfully rouse his qi once more. Channeling the Carp Stream Intent, he forcefully erupted with power, shattering the illusions obstructing his vision!

Jin Buhuan was slightly surprised. _Hmm? He recovered from that, too? What resolute willpower. A standard Rank 9 cultivator should have fainted by now._

_Regardless, it matters not._

_Even if he broke through the technique, he is merely an arrow at the end of its flight._

He lightly flicked the blade's flat once again.

But following a vibrating hum, Jin Buhuan was astonished to find that the sound was trembling, unable to form a cohesive rhythm.

_What's going on?_

_Why was my spell art interrupted?_

Before he could shake off his shock and react, Li Qi had already charged right in front of him.

_So fast!_

The moment those two words popped into his mind, a massive, pot-sized fist had already arrived at his face.

The protective golden light surrounding him shattered inch by inch, and a string of golden stars burst across his vision.

Li Qi's heavy strike landed squarely, instantly leaving him dizzy and disoriented!

A Rank 9 of the Martial Dao didn't possess many flashy tricks.

It simply meant tremendous strength and blinding speed, the pure might of a fleshly body tempered to its absolute limits.

All the methods within the Martial Dao revolved around how to eat to increase one's power, and then how to flawlessly unleash that power outward!

As long as they were of the same rank, no other Daoist Tradition could avoid suffering a disadvantage once a martial artist closed the distance.

Jin Buhuan had never expected that Li Qi actually possessed a cultivation base in the Martial Dao, let alone that he had already attained a rank!

He forced himself to stabilize his stance, preparing to strum the blade once more.

But before he could even steady his feet or raise his hand, Li Qi had already pursued him, delivering another brutal punch.

This time, he was sent flying entirely out the door. Saliva mixed with streaks of blood trailed through the air like thin threads. His body became a blur as he smashed into the ground outside, pulverizing the stone paving.

He had been knocked back exactly ten paces by Li Qi.

It was the perfect distance.

Within ten paces, Li Qi was absolutely certain that he was faster.

Like a howling gale, Li Qi surged forward again, sweeping toward his opponent like a violent bolt of lightning!

His gaze was frigid. Every muscle in his body quivered with subtle, precise tremors, unleashing an explosive surge of strength.

_Thud._

_Thud._

_Thud!_

Three consecutive punches accurately bypassed Jin Buhuan's protective golden light, driving the explosive force of the fists directly into his opponent's body.

The Shaman Dao granted Li Qi precise visual acuity, expansive knowledge, and the ability to manipulate certain amounts of qi.

The Martial Dao, on the other hand, granted him terrifying close-quarters combat capabilities.

Most importantly, his progress bar provided him with an unparalleled ability to seize the perfect opening.

Every time Jin Buhuan attempted to rally his defenses, Li Qi would interrupt him at the exact right moment, pummeling him into the ground punch after punch!

Accompanied by the thunderous roar of his fist wind, Li Qi's instantaneous movements were so fast that the sheer friction generated explosive sonic booms in the air.

Every single strike landed solidly upon Jin Buhuan's flesh. Yet, whatever his body was made of, he hadn't sustained any obvious external injuries despite the brutal beating.

He was, after all, a divine scion.

Jin Buhuan was no human.

"Enough!" Having taken over a dozen punches in a row, Jin Buhuan roared in absolute fury!

He violently struck his own chest. His previously elegant and aristocratic appearance vanished completely, and his head morphed directly into that of a tiger.

A bizarre entity with the body of a man and the head of a tiger unleashed a ferocious roar, causing the metal qi surrounding him to roar in tandem!

Brilliant golden rays wove around his form, confined by some sort of potent energy field. His body, in turn, stimulated this mass of energy until it teetered on the very edge of detonation.

Waves of visible heat encircled Jin Buhuan, radiating a dazzling luminescence. It looked like liquefied gold, emanating a bright and resplendent hue.

Layer upon layer, wave after wave—countless energy fluctuations resonated around him, forming concentric ripples of power that propagated ceaselessly across the earth.

It was like a pebble being tossed into a tranquil pond, sending ripples outward, only for a barrage of more stones to be hurled in immediately after! The once calm surface was churned into violent turbulence!

Li Qi was directly shoved back by these intense fluctuations.

However, his feet dug into a firm horse stance, rooting him steadily to the ground. He linked his earth qi with the terrain below, borrowing the earth's leverage. Utilizing the Martial Dao's sense of balance and sturdy lower body, combined with his connection to the earth qi, he prevented himself from being toppled by the sudden, overwhelming force.

Even so, he was still pushed back over fifty feet.

His feet plowed a pair of long, deep trenches through the stone bricks.

Li Qi barely managed to stabilize himself. Before he could even reset his posture, he felt a chilling sensation brush past his neck.

"I yield. Brother Jin is truly formidable." Li Qi cupped his hands, dropping his combat stance.

Jin Buhuan gently waved his hand.

The long blade transformed into a streak of flowing light, returning directly to his grasp.

"Brother Li is not lacking either. If not for my extraordinary bloodline and the advantage of my weapon, I truly might not have been able to best you," Jin Buhuan replied with a smile.

At this moment, his appearance bore no trace of the elegant young master. Instead, much like his father, he possessed the head of a tiger and the body of a man. Protruding fangs and a vicious, predatory gaze were accompanied by an aura of golden nobility, interlaced with the slaughtering intent of clashing metal halberds.

His tone of voice, however, remained as gentle and refined as ever.

"This battle has benefited me greatly. I won't see you off, Brother Jin, as I need to meditate deeply on what I've learned," Li Qi said hastily. Without even offering a proper farewell, he ducked back into the inn and slammed the door shut with a loud crash.

He completely ignored the disastrous mess outside.

The street pavement and the treasure chests loaded onto the carts had all been overturned and scattered by the aftershocks of their battle, littering the entire road. Quite a few were thoroughly destroyed.

Jin Buhuan didn't spare the wreckage a single glance. Snapping his fan open, he declared, "Let's go! We are heading back as well!"

As he walked away, he kept his eyes fixed on the doors Li Qi had just shut.

_This probing test... no matter how I look at it, this man seems like nothing more than a brainless martial brute. Was his earlier demeanor completely faked?_

_When we were conversing earlier, he appeared quite shrewd and calculating. Yet, occasionally, his actions shift to an entirely different style._

_Is it an act? Or is he truly just a man who doesn't care about the minor details?_

_Even if it is an act, he undeniably helped me restore this streak of metal qi to completion, and even informed me about the yin-yang metal qi._

_I'll shelve my murderous intent for now. After all, the other party has been highly cooperative. My goal was the metal qi; now that the goal is achieved, there's no need to hound him relentlessly._

_Whatever steps I take next can wait until later._

_My most pressing priority must be my two brothers over in Green Mountain. They are the true threats capable of shaking my foundation._

_As for this passing traveler... be he friend or foe, he poses no fundamental threat to my status as the heir. If he wishes to make a move against me, the strength he's displayed today is still far from sufficient._

_Besides, is his supposed backer... actually real?_

_Today's battle sussed out his foundation. This man is actually cultivating both the Shaman Dao and the Martial Dao simultaneously..._

_What kind of faction passes down such bizarre inheritances?_

Jin Buhuan recalled that the Shaman Dao and Martial Dao could be compatible in their early stages. In the later stages, however, the aggressive nature of the Martial Dao would reveal itself. A martial artist's inner qi would attempt to devour any qi of a different nature and origin from itself.

The Martial Dao demanded absolute sincerity and prized pure, specialized focus. Every drop of qi within the body would be condensed into one's own exclusive inner qi, leaving no room for anything strange or foreign.

This fundamentally contradicted the Shaman Dao's philosophy of accommodating heaven and earth within one's own body.

Because of this, the Shaman Dao possessed all sorts of weird and wondrous methods, whereas the Martial Dao's methods were singular and straightforward. This was a divergence in their foundational ideologies; the two paths simply could not coexist.

Cultivating both of them at the same time was incredibly bizarre to begin with.

_Does he truly have a powerful background?_

_If nothing could be found in our investigations, could it be that he's merely a lucky vagrant who stumbled upon a fortuitous encounter?_

_Whatever, it doesn't matter. I need to hurry back to Green Mountain._

"Elder Si, summon the swift winds. We are returning to Green Mountain to see if the rumors of the yin-yang metal qi are true," Jin Buhuan ordered.

Elder Si furrowed his brow. "Young Master, if we head to Green Mountain, no matter how fast we are, we won't be able to return until tomorrow. If the city is left without oversight during this time..."

"It's merely a single day. What could possibly go wrong in such a short time? But if I ignore the other matter, the threat posed by those two worthless brothers of mine is far greater."

"This brooks no delay. Let's move."

---

Having watched Jin Buhuan depart, the smile vanished from Li Qi's face.

He then walked straight to the backyard, coming to a halt before Piaoxu and the others.

Piaoxu's face was etched with worry. The moment Li Qi entered, she immediately stepped forward. Though she tried her best to keep her tone calm, an uncontainable anxiety bled through her words. "Sir, how did things go?"

"I will explain the details later. Where is Qi Ya? Where is she?" Li Qi asked.

Hearing her name, Qi Ya hastily bounded over. The little fox reared up on her hind legs, staring intently at Li Qi while squeaking softly.

"Qi Ya, I have a task for you," Li Qi said. "If anyone else goes, they might be tailed. But you haven't yet transformed into a demon; you are still a wild beast, which makes you much more inconspicuous. This matter is somewhat dangerous. Are you willing?"

Qi Ya looked somewhat bewildered. She glanced at Li Qi, then at her older sister. After a moment of thought, she let out a squeak and nodded her head.

"Misty Rain Pavilion, the Lu Manor on West Main Street, Lihu Fragrance Market, the Dragon Spout Fountain at Yanghe Spring, Plum Blossom Bookstore, and the Junzhi Pavilion at Fengmen. I need you to make a trip to these locations. Deliver a written message for me, instructing the heads of these households to gather at the inn within two hours."

As Li Qi spoke, he waved toward the outside. "Miss Shen, come help me draw a concealment talisman. I am going to write the letters."

Hearing this, Piaoxu tightly furrowed her brows.

She seemed to realize what Li Qi was planning to do.

Moreover, he was dragging Qi Ya into it.

Thus, Piaoxu stepped forward. "Sir, I can transform into my original form, and my size is quite small. Qi Ya is still too young and might ruin your plans. Please, allow me to go in her stead."

Li Qi cast a glance at her. "You have already become a demon, which means you carry demon qi on you... You would likely be discovered, and you would die."

"I also know some spell arts. Between Qi Ya and me, we each just have our own advantages and disadvantages," Piaoxu immediately countered.

Seeing her resolute demeanor, Li Qi did not try to stop her. "Very well, we'll do it your way. There will be compensation for you afterward. I leave the hard work to you."
