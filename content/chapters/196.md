# Chapter 196: The Horn of Counterattack

In an instant, Li Qi understood.

Shen Shuibi naturally didn't know the method to summon him, nor the divine title he had just claimed.

Therefore, she opted for exhaustive trial and error.

Take the formation disk beneath Li Qi's feet, for example. Based on his rudimentary understanding of formations, he could tell it was a divine descent summoning system.

It had been fed with all the parameters Shen Shuibi knew about him, creating an automated name-calling mechanism. It continuously generated possible honorifics that might apply to Li Qi, calling out to him without pause.

And this was just one of thousands of formations on this formation disk.

The entire formation disk was densely packed with these automated calling mechanisms. Various paths, various methods, thousands of formations, and precious materials all combined to form this incredibly expensive summoning machine.

Every second, it generated hundreds of thousands of different summoning methods. This included the Divine Dao's [Divine Descent], the Shaman Dao's [Covenant], the Reason Dao's [Flowers in a Mirror, Moon in the Water], the Human Dao's [Heart-Linking] method, the Demon Dao's [Blood-Calling] art, and so on.

Thousands upon thousands of methods were operating autonomously, constantly attempting to summon Li Qi.

Li Qi could discern the effects of these formations, but his understanding stopped there.

A rough analogy would be Li Qi knowing how to use a mobile phone, whereas Shen Shuibi had built the phone by hand, soldering every single transistor and manually forging all the chips and the screen.

Let alone the technique involved, Li Qi couldn't even recognize some of the materials used. The vital energy required to integrate so many formations into one single entity was unimaginable.

Furthermore, this thing was truly expensive.

Setting aside the materials he didn't recognize, just the ones he did were invaluable. Li Qi certainly couldn't afford it himself, even if he sold his Rank 8 metal qi. There were four or five Rank 8 materials visible to the naked eye alone.

Not to mention the technical expertise embedded within it. The ability to seamlessly gather thousands of formations together was completely beyond his comprehension. If this were used to craft a dharma artifact, it would absolutely reach the absolute zenith of Rank 8, perhaps even bordering on half-step Rank 7.

Even for Shen Shuibi, completing this was by no means an easy task, right?

Especially since her cultivation had long been crippled, and she was currently painstakingly re-cultivating.

She might have been able to figure out the technical aspects on her own, but she abhorred secular, mercantile affairs. Just imagining her having to negotiate business deals and scrounge for materials made Li Qi realize exactly how much effort she had poured into this.

Realizing all this, Li Qi felt deeply moved.

However, looking at the rabbit who had turned her back to him in a fit of pique, he quickly snapped out of it. He hurried over, grasping her hand with his divine soul's golden body, and said, "Miss Shen—"

The rabbit stopped and looked back, still puffing her cheeks in anger.

Li Qi had always been silver-tongued. He had already brewed up a myriad of sweet words in his mind, but upon seeing her turn around, he choked them all back, unable to utter a single one.

After a moment of stunned silence, he awkwardly offered, "You've worked hard."

Yet, the moment those words left his mouth, the angry pout vanished from the rabbit's face.

She lowered her head, looking a little embarrassed, but let out an irrepressible, smug hum. "As long as you know it was hard work..."

It was a feeling of wanting to show off, yet feeling too shy to do so.

Seeing this, Li Qi smiled, letting out a sigh of relief.

Growing bolder, he reached out and patted the rabbit's head. "Alright, leave the rest to me."

To his delight, Shen Shuibi made no move to resist.

Of course, in reality, she simply had no reaction at all. It was as if Li Qi hadn't done anything; she was neither pleased nor repulsed.

The rabbit merely nodded. "Okay, I'll leave it to you."

With that said, she reverted to her usual blank demeanor. She stood obediently in place, effectively activating her follow mode.

It was as if that lively, expressive person from just a moment ago had been a complete illusion.

Li Qi was dumbfounded.

He didn't even understand the full situation yet... How could she just zone out like this?

He patted Shen Shuibi's shoulder. "_Ahem_, Miss Shen, wake up for a second. Could you please tell me what happened over the last few days?"

Shen Shuibi regained her focus. She glared at Li Qi with clear displeasure, her eyes screaming, _You're so annoying._

Nevertheless, she quickly proceeded to recount the events of the past few days, trying to finish as fast as possible so she could wash her hands of the matter and get back to her own things.

She was truly terrible at, and deeply despised, dealing with mundane chores. She also hated interacting with the outside world.

The rabbit's favorite thing to do was to immerse herself in her own world, quietly bustling about with her own projects.

To put it nicely, it was an ethereal, otherworldly grace, untainted by mortal dust.

To put it bluntly, she had severe social anxiety and was completely closed off.

But the bunny was cute, so it wasn't a problem.

Furthermore.

Because of this trait, a certain dynamic had emerged—one that Li Qi had realized, but Shen Shuibi was still completely oblivious to.

The bunny had always pawned these responsibilities off onto others. She habitually relied on this someone else, content to just obediently follow along.

In the past, this someone else was Lady Luofu.

For who knows how many years, she had lived exactly like this.

But now, that someone else was Li Qi.

Therefore, when she had no one to rely on, the rabbit had fallen into that state of extreme, restless frenzy. Yet the moment Li Qi returned, she immediately calmed down.

She didn't even realize it herself, but after Li Qi arrived, the anxiety in her heart evaporated. It was as if a massive weight had been lifted, allowing her to retreat right back into her comfort zone.

As for Li Qi, he naturally welcomed this development.

He was absolutely thrilled!

---

A few hours later, Li Qi fully understood everything Shen Shuibi had done.

The bunny, meanwhile, had re-entered power-saving mode. She had overloaded herself these past few days, so she simply reverted to her original form, curled up in Li Qi's arms, draped her ears over her eyes, and fell sound asleep.

For her, completing the formation disk and taking over the entire Lower Nine Sects had drained a massive amount of vital energy, the latter especially so.

She was thoroughly exhausted, so she nestled into his embrace to rest.

However, despite being in her rabbit form, she was still wearing tiny clothes.

Truly meticulous.

Now that the baton was passed, Li Qi immediately took charge of the situation and formulated his own plan.

The first order of business, and his most pressing priority, was to find Wang Boyan.

Based on Shen Shuibi's account, combined with Wang Boyan's previous actions, Li Qi was certain.

That scheming bastard!

He didn't care about the refugees at all. His goal was to make Li Qi destroy the blood sacrifice, incite the fiends to attack Vast Yang City, and then sit back as an outsider, selling out both sides to reap the profits!

However, even Wang Boyan hadn't anticipated that the blood sacrifice wasn't actually meant to resist the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest, but was merely Monk Chanzhi's method to save himself.

Now, the entire situation was crystal clear. The previous confusion had vanished entirely as Li Qi mapped out the web of events.

First, excluding himself, there were four factions currently present in Vast Yang.

The first faction was the Jianghu forces led by the Righteous Sword Palace.

Li Qi felt these people could be trusted. He could tell from Zhang Lan that the Righteous Sword Palace, just like himself, was fighting entirely out of their own pocket. They confronted the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest without asking for any reward, driven purely by a chivalrous heart.

Zhang Lan's character was also excellent. For the sake of the refugees, he had taken the initiative to battle two Rank 8 experts, fully prepared to go down with them. He truly deserved the title of a great hero.

If they knew what was happening here, Li Qi couldn't fathom a reason for them not to intervene.

That was why he had left a letter for Zhang Lan before departing, acting as a backup plan.

It probably wouldn't be long before he could take the next step.

Second, the local faction of Vast Yang City was basically paralyzed. Based on his own experiences, Li Qi knew the Vast Yang Prefect had returned to the capital to report on his duties!

Previously, Li Qi hadn't understood, but looking at it now, it was as clear as the sun breaking through the clouds.

Nonsense, of course he ran—was he supposed to stay and wait for death?! The man had absolutely rushed back to the Hundred Forests Kingdom's capital for help. If he had stayed, he would undoubtedly have become one of Monk Chanzhi's puppets as well.

Therefore, the Prefect must have discovered that the Vast Yang Minister of War and the Minister of State had become Monk Chanzhi's puppets. He had decisively ordered the army to withdraw from the garrison pass, set up the landscape grand array to fend off the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest, and then hastily departed for the capital to fetch reinforcements.

However, for some unknown reason, no one had returned to rescue Vast Yang City. It was unclear what had transpired at the Hundred Forests Kingdom's capital. If the Kingdom Lord intervened, capturing a mere Rank 7 like Monk Chanzhi should have been effortless.

Third, the Human Dao faction led by Wang Boyan.

This meant the Wang Family of Jiannan. Their goals and methods were now evident. There were two reasons why Wang Boyan approached Li Qi. The first was to utilize his abilities as a Shaman to commune with the Heavenly Dao, helping them secure a minor world to serve as their personal resource world.

The second reason was to use Li Qi's hands to destroy the landscape grand array and the blood sacrifice. He had linked the two together, planning to mislead Li Qi into dismantling Vast Yang City's defenses.

In doing so, he and Li Qi would save the commoners and spread their good names. At the same time, he could drag Li Qi—a disciple of the Shaman God Mountain—down with him to shield against the Shaman Dao's retaliation. When the Shaman Dao forces arrived, he could just shove Li Qi forward as a meat shield and say, "Who the hell are you lackeys?! A disciple of the Shaman God Mountain and I did this together. Do you intend to defy the Shaman God Mountain?"

Li Qi would even be forced to stay and fight the invading Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest alongside him.

This way, he would use another's knife to kill, crippling Vast Yang's local faction. The Human Dao could then plant deeper roots in Vast Yang Prefecture, subtly completing their other schemes in the region.

It was all calculated perfectly, but Wang Boyan hadn't expected that the only reason Vast Yang's defenses were so feeble was due to the existence of that thing called Monk Chanzhi!

The blood sacrifice had nothing to do with the landscape grand array; it was Monk Chanzhi's conspiracy. Consequently, even though the blood sacrifice was stopped, the landscape grand array remained intact. Vast Yang's defenses hadn't faulted, and the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest couldn't breach them.

Due to Monk Chanzhi's appearance, Wang Boyan's meticulous schemes had amounted to nothing but drawing water with a bamboo basket.

This brought him to the fourth faction.

That was Monk Chanzhi himself.

A cultivator of the Dakini Dao Lineage under Mahakala, he had attained the Rank 7 Dao. Moreover, he was an incredibly terrifying powerhouse even among Rank 7 experts, capable of single-handedly facing the Vast Yang Minister of War and the Prefect—both Rank 7s themselves—killing one and driving off the other.

The Minister of War he killed had become his puppet, a pawn to manipulate Vast Yang's political landscape. The Prefect had fled and had yet to return.

But none of this was his true objective. His real goal was to obtain authentic Shaman Dao cultivation methods to balance the Buddhist heart and devilish thoughts within him, laying a solid foundation for his advancement to Rank 6.

Li Qi had finally figured it out.

He had only guessed half of the truth back then.

This entire sequence of events was a trap. There were no coincidences!

That bald thief had probably had his eyes on him from the very beginning!

The hundred thousand commoners, the garrison pass, the White Rhino Army, and everything else that had transpired—it was all a trap laid by that demonic monk to force him to voluntarily offer up the Shaman Dao secrets.

He had been targeted the moment he started running around the Hundred Forests Kingdom doing good deeds.

All these so-called coincidences were actually his arrangements.

He had noticed long ago that Li Qi was from the Shaman God Mountain. Thus, he had made early preparations, intentionally ensuring Li Qi ran into the White Rhino Army. Playing on Li Qi's habit of acting as a do-gooder, he had woven this blood sacrifice scheme specifically for him.

He used the blood sacrifice to corner him, forcing him to ultimately surrender the Shaman Dao cultivation methods of his own volition.

Looking at his behavior, Li Qi could more or less guess the reason.

Chanzhi clearly possessed heart-devouring methods, yet he refrained from using them to cleanly extract the Shaman Dao secrets from Li Qi's mind.

Despite the threats, he never completely tore away all pretense of civility.

Evidently, there were two possibilities. First, he dreaded the Shaman God Mountain and thus didn't dare to outright kill Li Qi.

Second, even if he devoured Li Qi's heart, he wouldn't obtain the Shaman Dao methods; it had to be voluntary.

Li Qi believed it was a mix of both.

The [Book of Invocation] and the divine will inheritance of the spell arts in his mind—if they could be snatched just by eating a heart, the Shaman God Mountain might as well pack up and quit. How could they face the world while standing tall in the southwest of All Under Heaven?

Li Qi had ample reason to believe that the moment the other party ate his heart and learned the methods, he would drop dead the very next second.

Therefore, Chanzhi wasn't even planning to directly learn the Shaman Dao methods. Instead, he was coercing Li Qi into working for him, using him to develop a method to suppress his devilish thoughts rather than outright stealing a Shaman Dao cultivation method.

The Tang Empire was located in the center of All Under Heaven, its prestige shaking all four directions.

The Buddhist Kingdom was in the extreme west, hailed as the Great Western Paradise Buddhist Kingdom.

The Shaman God Mountain was no different. Occupying the southwest of All Under Heaven, they were a major power capable of standing firmly in the world.

The entirety of All Under Heaven was divided by the cardinal directions and the center, split into eight extremities and one central domain, making nine parts in total.

For the Shaman God Mountain to occupy one of those parts, they absolutely wouldn't allow their cultivation methods to leak out. It was safe to assume that this monk actually couldn't do much to him; he could only resort to these roundabout threats.

So, summarizing the desires and capabilities of the four factions, as well as his own position in the middle of it all.

A clear plan naturally took shape.

Li Qi had used a split soul's divine descent to escape while Monk Chanzhi was experimenting with the methods. This meant he had never planned to fight Chanzhi head-on from the start.

Damn it all! A Rank 7 bullying a Rank 9? If he wanted to play the big guy picking on the little guy, he couldn't blame Li Qi for calling in some backup.
