# Chapter 167: Aftermath

Li Qi and the others naturally didn't stay idle. They shared a tacit understanding, saying little as they simply cleaned up the battlefield.

Surviving the calamity left most of them feeling deeply fortunate. Though some mourned fallen brothers, life and death were fickle on the battlefield, a truth these soldiers had long since accepted.

They began by clearing out the fiends.

Once not a single living fiend remained, they turned their attention to the corpses.

The bodies were sorted into distinct categories. Human remains were gathered for burial, while the demons were dismantled, stripped of useful materials, and the rest consigned to the flames.

A contingent of soldiers dispersed to comfort the commoners. Despite their desperate fight, hundreds had still perished.

The vast majority had died in the panicked stampede, trampled underfoot as they fled blindly.

A small fraction had quite literally died of fright from the Rank 8 fiend's terrifying presence.

An even smaller number were executed by military officers for inciting panic and disrupting the defensive formations.

Only a sparse few had actually fallen victim to the shockwaves of the battle itself.

The army had absorbed almost the entirety of the collateral damage.

The initial force of over ten thousand soldiers had lost nearly a third of its ranks. Those who remained were almost universally wounded, their eyes heavy with grief. Yet, their hands never stopped working. They even dedicated men to console the grieving families among the populace.

Their own brothers had just fallen, and with no one to comfort them, they still shouldered the burden of comforting others.

It wasn't until nightfall that the battlefield was thoroughly swept clean.

However, clearing the debris was far from the end; much work remained.

The casualties here were too massive to simply walk away. Left unattended, this land would eventually curdle into a haunted battlefield.

Vengeful spirits would roam free, drawing in dense Yin qi. It might even manifest a ghost city that would blight the surrounding lands. Should a Ghost King emerge from such a place, it would unleash endless calamities upon future generations.

Thus, the army chose to make camp for the night. They needed to purge the lingering baleful qi and death qi, delivering the wandering souls into the cycle of reincarnation.

The military shaman typically responsible for this rite had fallen in battle, so Li Qi volunteered to take up the mantle.

Now, Li Qi stood atop the sacrificial altar.

Below the altar gathered thousands of soldiers, ready to participate in the ritual.

Zhang Lan, unfamiliar with sacrificial rites, stood to the side. Under Li Qi's direction, he acted as the unmoving anchor of the ceremony, utilizing his righteous qi to suppress any encroaching evil.

Aloft on the altar, Li Qi gripped an incense burner and chanted a true mantra. Below him, the army's military qi coalesced to bolster his power.

This was the method to honor spirits slain in combat.

The ghosts born of the battlefield harbored intense killing intent and baleful qi from their final moments. They were not ordinary ghosts. Worse, the spirits of the demons now dominated the area, most of them fiercely violent. It was imperative to first summon the souls of their fallen soldiers and deliver them to peace.

Once only the tens of thousands of demon spirits remained, they would be suppressed. Those who submitted would be delivered into reincarnation; those who resisted would be obliterated on the spot, forever preventing them from harming the mortal realm.

On the altar, Li Qi struck a golden gong.

Below, the soldiers commenced a martial drill.

The thundering of hooves and the march of infantry swept across the plains like a surging tide. Formations shifted, banners fluttered in time with the drums, advancing and wheeling with the ferocity of a tempest, displaying the strict discipline of a marching army.

The cavalry rode tall, the spearmen marched in unified ranks, exuding an aura of pure, vital vigor.

As the formation moved, the military qi gathered. Stirred by the familiar echoes of their living ranks, thousands of lingering souls drifted forth to join the drill, moving precisely as they had in life.

Seeing the spirits of their dead comrades, tears streamed down the faces of some soldiers. Not daring to break rank or make a sound, they choked back their sorrow and poured even more strength into the exercise.

Li Qi, however, could not let these spirits expend their strength. Though devoid of consciousness in death, fueling the military qi still drained them. Souls were like water without a source; once depleted, they would scatter into oblivion.

Wasting no time, Li Qi invoked the bridging rite. Celestial light descended, and a Yin Bailiff answered his summons.

The manifestation of the Yin Bailiff brought chilling gusts of Yin wind. Its terrifying presence eclipsed even the combined might of Shen Shuibi and Zhang Lan.

Yet, the bailiff took no hostile action. It stepped forward, offered a respectful bow to Li Qi, and then set to its task. It marked the souls, drawing them out of the gloom and ushering the heroic spirits into reincarnation.

The first phase of the sacrifice was complete.

Moving into the second phase, Li Qi abandoned the gong in favor of a heavy drum.

The rolling thunder of the drumbeats resonated for thirty miles.

He seized a prepared battleaxe and brought it smashing down onto the battlefield.

Upon this signal, the waiting soldiers roared in unison. Baleful qi erupted. They moved with terrifying majesty, a crushing force that sought out hidden evils, seizing ghouls and tearing apart phantoms. The atmosphere blazed with intensity, and the very military qi above them took on a bloody, crimson hue, appearing as if it were weeping blood.

Under Li Qi's command, this fusion of killing intent and baleful qi took physical form as the army marched into the heart of the battlefield, heads held high, weapons drawn.

Their heavy, synchronized footsteps struck the earth like localized tremors, marching directly over the grounds where countless demon corpses lay buried.

Instantly, a cacophony of wailing ghosts and howling beasts erupted from beneath the soil. Things writhed below the surface. Mangled flesh, shattered bone, and dark blood pushed against the dirt, desperate to claw their way out.

Li Qi struck the drum once more. The elemental energies of his personal cosmos synchronized with the heaven and earth, discharging arcs of lightning that crackled through the air.

A continuous, deafening rumble echoed out, striking with the relentless rhythm of war drums.

The soldiers unleashed deafening war cries. Their dense Yang qi violently mixed with the baleful qi, terrifying the emerging spirits and forcing them back into the dirt.

Raising his hands again, Li Qi gripped the drum rim with his left hand and raised the mallet in his right. He struck a resonant, snapping beat, slicing through the ghostly presence with sheer qi.

He delivered another blow, mimicking the clap of thunder, while the soldiers' roars surged to match the thunderous rhythm.

He repeated this three times.

After the third strike, the fiend souls beneath the earth lost all will to rebel, remaining obediently suppressed beneath the soil.

The suppression complete, Li Qi toppled the altar. He cast aside the axe that symbolized slaughter and dropped his drum mallet. In a booming voice, he issued an edict to the fiend souls, commanding them to return to the cycle of reincarnation and forbidding them from lingering.

Not daring to disobey, countless fiend souls gathered to depart. The multitude of souls coalesced into a howling Yin wind, obediently fleeing the battlefield and descending toward the Nether Court.

However, what awaited them was not a gentle rebirth, but swift punishment.

To enter the Nether Court weighed down by heavy karmic debts meant facing judgment. Virtuous karma would be rewarded, but those stained with malignant karma were forced to atone for their sins before re-entering the cycle. Such was the iron law of the Nether Court.

Every soul had to be reborn completely unburdened, stripped bare of all karmic ties. Thus, the good must be rewarded and the wicked punished, wiping the slate clean for reincarnation.

This was the absolute justice of the Nether Court.

Of course, this all operated on the premise that one actually went to reincarnation.

If a soul refused to go, Yin Bailiffs would be dispatched to apprehend them.

If one was so overwhelmingly powerful that even the Yin Bailiffs were helpless against them, then the cycle of reincarnation meant very little.

But should someone arrive to deliver judgment at that level, they would certainly be an entity far more terrifying than the mere cycle of reincarnation.

Having finished the grueling ordeal, Li Qi let out a long breath and collapsed onto the ground.

To be perfectly honest.

This was his very first time presiding over such a massive sacrificial ceremony.

The cascade of arrangements—coordinating the soldiers' movements, planning his own actions, gathering the required materials, and adhering strictly to proper etiquette—required meticulous calculation. It was far beyond the capabilities of an ordinary person.

Regardless, it had concluded flawlessly.

The moment Li Qi fell back, Zhang Lan and Shen Shuibi rushed forward.

However, seeing Shen Shuibi approach, Zhang Lan stopped in his tracks.

_Better to let his own people help him up._

He waited until Shen Shuibi had supported Li Qi to his feet before walking over. He offered a deep bow and said, "You are truly formidable, Sir. Your cultivation may be shallow, but you possess immense courage and intellect. Not only did you save the lives of a hundred thousand people here, but you also commanded a sacrificial rite for thousands of soldiers. Even some Rank 8 shamans I know would be incapable of such a feat, yet you succeeded. It is utterly astonishing!"

Li Qi hurriedly waved his hand, gesturing for the man to rise. "As long as I could be of help. Senior need not be so courteous; my cultivation is lacking, I hardly deserve—"

"Of course you deserve it. Setting aside your life-saving grace, you delivered the souls of thousands of comrades, which alone moves me to tears. Beyond that, you suppressed tens of thousands of fiend souls, stopping a ghost city from ever forming and saving countless lives in the future. With merits such as these, what does cultivation level even matter? Your achievements and virtue command my utmost respect!" the Rank 8 cultivator said, bowing in deep reverence.

Li Qi managed to stand properly, only to see the surrounding soldiers uniformly bowing their heads, paying him their highest respects.

Seeing this, Li Qi raised his voice and called out, "There is still much to be done! The commoners have not yet been settled, and our subsequent route remains undecided. You brave men need not crowd around me—please, return to your duties!"

At that moment, an officer was nudged forward by his peers from within the ranks. He stepped ahead of the soldiers, dropped to one knee, and cupped his fists respectfully toward Li Qi. "The Hundred Forests Border Guard's White Rhino Army thanks you for your righteous grace, Sir! You saved our brothers, protected our people, and delivered the souls of our fallen kin. May we ask for your name? The White Rhino Army will engrave it in our hearts and never forget!"

He quickly added, "Our army cannot march without the Tiger Tally, so I dare not pledge the entirety of the White Rhino Army to your service. However, once we strip off our armor and sheathe our blades, every man among us is willing to lend you aid! Please, Sir, accept our signal token. I dare not claim we will arrive the very instant you call, but if we are within a thousand miles, we guarantee our arrival within three days."

With those words, he condensed military qi onto an arrow etched with intricate runes. Once triggered, the arrow would undoubtedly streak into the sky, transmitting a message to anyone bearing the matching qi.

"I will accept this token, and I deeply appreciate your sincerity. But you all have duties to uphold, so there is no need to dwell on this. The people are what matters most. Please, return to your posts." Li Qi limped forward and received the arrow.

Refusing would be inappropriate, and though he did not seek any repayment from these men, accepting it would put their minds at ease. He simply wouldn't use it in the future.

As for the limping...

He couldn't help it. Commanding such a grandiose sacrificial rite had left him utterly drained.

Sacrifices demanded a steep toll, and seeing one of this scale through to the end was undeniably exhausting.

Having presented the token, the officer bowed once more, turned around, and offered similar words of gratitude to Zhang Lan.

Zhang Lan, however, refused it outright, firmly declining to accept the token.

Left with no alternative, the officer offered his thanks once again before leading the other soldiers away, dispersing to carry out their respective duties.

By the time Li Qi finally sat down to rest, the moon had already climbed above the treetops.

Even while resting, his mind remained active. He turned to Zhang Lan. "Senior Zhang, judging from how they spoke to you, it seems you have no formal ties to this army. Are you not their commanding general?"

Only now did Li Qi have a moment to carefully scrutinize this Rank 8 cultivator.

The man's appearance was rather unseemly, falling far short of even being considered plain. He had a flat nose, a bulging forehead, and pockmarks dotting his face. Dressed in an ordinary yellow robe, he carried a sword box on his back, though it appeared hollow. Considering he hadn't drawn anything from it throughout the entire ordeal, it was likely empty.

Li Qi, naturally, was not one to judge a person by their looks.

Hearing Li Qi's inquiry, Zhang Lan shook his head. "Of course not. Just like you, I was merely passing through."

"Senior is truly righteous. Encountering injustice on the road, you were willing to fight a battle to the death." Filled with profound respect, Li Qi immediately cupped his hands in praise.

"Why put on such a display? Did you not do the exact same?" Zhang Lan laughed, somewhat curious about Li Qi's reaction.

"There is a vast difference. I never undertake tasks without a guarantee of success. If I were in your position, Senior, and witnessed such a scene, I would have silently memorized the fiend's appearance, retreated, and sought vengeance at a later date," Li Qi admitted with stark honesty.

He had always been this way. Doing a good deed was perfectly fine, provided it posed no threat to his own safety.

But if it demanded risking his life, he had to carefully weigh the odds to determine if the endeavor was truly worth it.

If risking his life was futile and guaranteed a hundred percent chance of death, Li Qi would flee without a moment's hesitation.

As long as the green mountains remain, there will never be a shortage of firewood. Li Qi wasn't about to carelessly throw his life away. Even righteous acts had to be measured against one's own capabilities. He had a bright future ahead of him; he couldn't afford to die in a place like this.

Li Qi firmly believed that as long as he drew breath, he could claw back any losses, no matter how severe.

When he had first arrived in this world, he was illiterate, unable to speak the language, and trapped in a frail body that had never touched a cultivation method. Yet, he fought desperately to survive. Aside from sheer luck, it was this unwavering conviction that carried him through.

"Haha, my master also tells me I'm entirely too reckless. But given the circumstances, where was the time to think things through? If I had paused to observe and weigh my options, I fear many more would have died," Zhang Lan replied.

"That is exactly why Senior's actions are worthy of admiration. Compared to my habit of hesitating, fearing wolves ahead and tigers behind, your straightforward and open demeanor proves you are a true champion of justice," Li Qi said.

Zhang Lan repeatedly waved his hands in denial. "It was merely the act of a brute. I am only good for fighting and killing. How could that compare to your brilliance, Sir? The moment you acted, you resolved the crisis, and you have effortlessly settled the myriad of troubles in the aftermath. These are feats I could absolutely never accomplish."

Li Qi eagerly rebuffed the praise. "Deliberating again and again is nothing more than a lack of courage. It is an act of sheer indecision."

Zhang Lan was just about to retort.

But at that moment, Shen Shuibi spoke up from the side, cleanly severing their exchange.

The rabbit-girl wore an expression of deep bewilderment. She tilted her head and asked in a thoroughly confused tone, "Um... you two. Tearing yourselves down just to heap praises onto the other—do you find this fun? Or... is this also part of the sacrificial rite?"

It was glaringly obvious that she was genuinely baffled.

With those words, the two men, still locked in their posturing, were instantly struck dumb. Awkwardness flooded their faces, their expressions shifting into something highly complicated.

"Hahahaha—"

"Hahaha..."

Then, they both let out stiff, forced laughs.

Midway through the strained chuckles, noticing they were doing the exact same thing, they clumsily cut themselves off.

Shen Shuibi's brow furrowed tightly, her watery eyes swimming with utter mystification.

_What in the world are these two doing?_

Catching sight of Shen Shuibi's expression, the two men were finally, genuinely amused. They threw their heads back and burst into unrestrained laughter.

"Alright then, there is no need to call me Senior anymore. Since we have hit it off like old friends, we shall address each other as equals!" Zhang Lan declared decisively as his laughter subsided.
