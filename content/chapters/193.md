# Chapter 193: Hard Labor

_This is bad._

When that devil god locked its gaze on him, Li Qi shivered. His hairs stood on end as his entire body mobilized for action!

He knew that life and death hung in the balance of this very moment!

Never in his wildest dreams did Li Qi imagine Monk Chanzhi's condition had deteriorated to such an extent. Just by taking a nap, the monk's devilish thoughts had erupted, plunging him entirely out of control!

If he couldn't think of a solution in the next second, he was dead!

His primal spirit and mind began to race frantically, countless fragments of information flashing through his thoughts!

Then, in the very next second, right before that devil god could make a move, Li Qi immediately sat down cross-legged and shouted, "The wondrous principles of the four seasons exhaustively comprehended, the flourishing and withering within the five elements inherently endowed. With a pure body, speech, and mind, I traverse all dharmas without remainder."

"Grandmaster Chanzhi, please observe my dharma demonstration!"

After sitting cross-legged, Li Qi ceased all other thoughts and focused entirely on demonstrating the dharma.

Demonstrating the dharma meant physically manifesting his methods for others to witness.

It was akin to a martial artist performing a martial routine, allowing others to understand their methods. It could provide inspiration, or even serve as a deterrent.

There was a higher level known as demonstrating the Dao, but Li Qi lacked the proficiency for that. His Dao, at present, could be summed up in a short phrase: unqualified to be shown.

This was not Li Qi's Shaman Dao cultivation method. Rather, it was the Buddhist Dharma he had just learned from the Desire-Mind Devil through the analysis of the True Knowledge dao rhythm.

"All dharmas lack self-nature. They are neither born nor destroyed. They are originally tranquil, possessing the nature of nirvana."

As Li Qi finished his recitation, the dharma demonstration officially began, and sudden phenomena arose all around him.

First, a Bodhi tree sprouted beside Li Qi. Initially just a tender sapling, it rapidly grew, its branches spreading in all directions, its dense foliage obscuring the sky.

Beneath the tree, Li Qi sat upright, reciting dharmic verses, merging in harmony with the Bodhi tree.

The fully grown Bodhi tree radiated a dazzling golden light, manifesting the appearance of a full moon, adorned with brilliantly shining pearl necklaces.

Beneath the Bodhi tree, myriad things flourished. Butterflies danced through the air, bees navigated among the flowers, a doe grazed on the grass, and wild hares frolicked.

Dappled sunlight scattered across the ground, painting a scene straight out of a dream. Li Qi sat upon a thick tree root, the air around him filled with the sweet fragrance of blooming flowers, lush plants, and earthy soil.

This was a pure land.

What was a pure land?

Externally untainted by the six dusts of the mundane world, internally free from the concepts of self and others, and unbound by the extremes of annihilation—thus, it is called a pure land.

It resembled a paradise untouched by the mortal realm, free from worldly interference. Pure, flawless, and exceedingly beautiful, it was like a perfect spring day.

However, in the very next moment, the tree grew increasingly massive, nearly stretching to the top of the five-story Buddhist Pagoda.

Soon enough, the tree's growth reached its absolute limit.

The devil god that Monk Chanzhi had transformed into stared blankly at the pure land, as if hypnotized.

Suddenly, the earth shook with eighteen distinct tremors, and the sound of heavenly drums echoed in the sky, instilling panic and dread in the heart.

A fierce gale whipped through the air, striking the wind wheel. As the wind wheel moved, the waters churned. As the waters churned, the earth quaked.

From the midst of this upheaval, a devil was born. Its appearance was familiar; it was the very Desire-Mind Devil that Li Qi had encountered.

At this moment, the inner devil rampaged wantonly beneath the Bodhi tree.

First, it drilled into the belly of the doe. The doe subsequently became pregnant, and shortly after, gave birth to a fawn.

The fawn was born with sharp fangs, its coat patterned with black spots, radiating a profound wicked qi.

The fawn initially suckled on the doe's milk, but then bit down with its fangs, mixing blood with the milk.

The doe suffered in pain, but driven by maternal love, she merely trembled and refused to leave.

The fawn's cruelty escalated. Not only did it suck her blood, but it also began tearing at her flesh. Still, the doe did not flee.

It tore through her skin, gnawed on her meat, ripped open her internal organs, and finally, consumed her heart.

Experiencing agonizing pain, the doe wept streams of tears. Her body was covered in blood, yet she never abandoned her young, allowing herself to be eaten alive.

The fawn continued to gorge until its belly was completely bloated, rupturing its stomach sack. It then collapsed and died.

From the corpse of the fawn, two inner devils emerged.

The devils grinned maliciously before diving into the bees. Suddenly, the bees raised their stingers, violently piercing the nearby butterflies before pulling them out.

A bee's stinger was naturally connected to its innards; this violent extraction directly ripped out their own internal organs.

Both bees and butterflies perished.

The devils multiplied into four.

The devils scurried frantically in all directions. All living things drank of their foul taint, and the common creatures became their minions. Filth steamed upward, and pestilence ran rampant. The once pure land was besieged by the devils, transforming—

The living beings of the pure land wept and lingered in sorrow, consumed by suffocating anguish and regret. They beat their chests and cried out, wailing in profound misery.

Observing this from the outside, the devil god roared with laughter, overjoyed. The clarity in its eyes gradually dimmed, while its tyrannical nature expanded, threatening to completely hijack its body.

However, at this very moment, the cycle of heavenly time turned. The spring day was no more, shifting into summer.

Within the pure land, the four seasons began to rotate.

Yet, the devils paid little mind to this. Following their demonic instincts, they continued to wreak havoc.

The heavenly qi shifted again, arriving at autumn.

The devils ran rampant. The living beings now had nowhere to turn; all living things collapsed to the ground in despair.

At this moment, Li Qi, sitting beneath the Bodhi tree, chanted an ancient proverb: "Though Mount Sumeru is high and vast, it will ultimately grind down to dust. Though the great ocean is deep and boundless, it too will eventually dry up. Though the sun and moon are bright, they will soon set in the west. Even if one wears pearl necklaces and adorns oneself with fragrant flowers, impurity remains hidden within, like fire concealed beneath ashes, waiting for the foolish to tread upon it."

The proverb was chanted, yet no living beings heard it.

Only the vicious voices of the devils spread. The living beings feared death, their tears falling like rain, everyone seized by great terror.

The devils, completely deaf to the words, even surged toward Li Qi.

But then, the time shifted to winter.

The bleak, harsh winter descended, and all living beings and devils fell into absolute silence and extinction.

The devil god watching the dharma demonstration from the outside blanked for a moment.

After the silent extinction came spring once more.

Following the beauty of spring, the devils were born again.

Then came summer, autumn, and winter.

And so the cycle continued.

After cycling ten times, Li Qi chanted once more: "To purify the land, one must first purify the mind. The impermanent suffering of the world is all but the devil of the mind."

"Because the mind is pure, the land is pure. To speak of a solemn and pure Buddhist land is not true speech. Beauty is not true beauty. A beautiful pure land—is not beautiful, therefore it is called beautiful. In the true nature, there is no such beauty, thus this solemnity is merely an empty name, not reality. Only the true nature is real!"

Hearing these words, the devil god's body trembled violently.

Li Qi's words were exactly the method to shatter the Desire-Mind Devil. The Desire-Mind Devil did not solely exist in darkness; it also resided within beauty.

The essence of the Desire-Mind Devil was not as simple as a mere devil. Rather, it was a trend—a force that drove the human heart downward, corrupting all that was beautiful.

This was the true terror of a devil.

It existed within all beautiful things. The more beautiful something was, the stronger the Desire-Mind Devil became.

Because beauty was destined to fall, just as a vigorous young man would inevitably age. A work of art is most radiant the very moment it is newly brought into the world; from that second onward, every following moment is a state of decline.

The Desire-Mind Devil was the trend of corruption.

If this trend was not eliminated, the devil could not be eradicated.

Therefore, this was the crux of Li Qi's dharma demonstration. The trend of decline could not be erased. If it could be erased, then all the Desire-Mind Devils All Under Heaven could be exterminated.

One could only resist it. And the method of resistance was the Shaman Dao's method of rotating heavenly time.

The underlying principle was simple: adapt to the heavenly qi, constantly transform anew, and suppress the birth of the Desire-Mind Devil. Whenever something decayed, simply rotate the cycle and start fresh, restoring it back to its original, pristine state.

This was the Dao of Rotation.

It could not eradicate the Desire-Mind Devil at its roots, but it could offer temporary resistance. If the rotation was executed properly, one could act as if the Desire-Mind Devil didn't exist at all.

The principle sounded very straightforward.

The Dao, once discovered, was usually quite simple to explain.

But the truly important part was the cultivation method.

It was like... everyone knew what a pulley was, and everyone understood the principle behind a pulley.

But when it came to the specific cultivation method, one would need to construct an entire, complex set of multi-stage pulley blocks to actually apply that principle.

And that was not something everyone could accomplish.

The same principle, the same Dao, could evolve into tens of thousands of cultivation methods, which could then branch out into countless strategic applications of those methods.

This was also the purpose behind Li Qi's dharma demonstration.

He was demonstrating to the devil god that Monk Chanzhi had become the cultivation method to resist the Desire-Mind Devil. All the phenomena were merely visual aids to showcase the cultivation method he had just invented.

To be honest, Li Qi himself remained skeptical about this cultivation method.

But he had no other choice.

If he didn't use it now, he'd never get the chance. He was about to be turned into a kapala bowl.

However, to his surprise, the clarity within the devil god's eyes fiercely surged.

Immediately after, the devil god pressed its hands together. The cycle of the four seasons abruptly manifested around it. Hundreds of flowers bloomed in an instant; the four seasons were gathered into a single moment. Amidst the gentle warmth of spring, the bleak harshness of autumn was unleashed. At the time of soothing warmth, the chilling commands of winter were issued.

The four seasons existed simultaneously; the heavenly qi was thrown into utter chaos!

Li Qi jumped in fright at the sight. This demonic monk was not a shaman! Even as a Rank 7 expert, he was completely incapable of controlling the rotation of his own heavenly qi! He was about to completely succumb to the devil!

Terrified, Li Qi rushed forward, hastily setting up a dharma altar as he shouted instructions. "Spring's growth relies on Jia and Yi occupying Yin and Mao; only then can one not fear Geng and Xin!"[^1]

"Summer's flourishing relies on Bing and Ding riding Si and Wu; therefore, one need not evade Ren and Gui!"[^2]

"Earth generates the four seasons in their proper time; its damage is harmless. Do not use your dharma power, use earth qi!"[^3]

"The heavenly qi must transform into structured forms; you must distinguish between decline, prosperity, and equilibrium. The coordination must be rigorous—do not let it run wild!"[^4]

While shouting out the core formulas, he used the altar's rituals to intervene in the division of the four seasons, smoothing out the rotation.

With Li Qi's assistance, down on the first floor, the body of an Arhat statue suddenly emitted a brilliant golden light. Shifting from being suppressed by the devil, it turned the tables, forcefully suppressing the devil back down!

However, there were one hundred and eight Arhat statues, corresponding to one hundred and eight devils. Currently, only a single statue had regained its luster.

Yet, with the recovery of that one Arhat statue, all four floors of the five-story Buddhist Pagoda trembled. Buddhist light blazed brilliantly, pure as glazed glass. Myriad images and phenomena appeared in all their glory.

Sudden phenomena emerged. Groves of purple bamboo and sandalwood formed lush forests. Peacocks and kalavinkas sang Sanskrit chants. Phantoms filled the ten directions, manifesting worlds as numerous as grains of sand, while myriad images glowed with the light of a Buddha land.

The Bodhisattva and Buddha statues all pooled their power into that single, liberated Arhat statue. Using it as an anchor, one Arhat statue after another regained the initiative under this overwhelming support.

It was like a person trying to lift a perfectly smooth iron ball. If the ball was just resting on the ground, it was incredibly difficult to exert force, making it impossible to lift.

But if that iron ball was given a handle, providing a point of leverage, the task became infinitely easier.

The Arhat statue Li Qi had helped liberate served as exactly that—a handle for Monk Chanzhi.

Thus, Monk Chanzhi fiercely exerted his strength, forcibly lifting the iron ball and once again suppressing the devilish thoughts!

The devil god's massive body gradually shrank, withered, and faded. Then, from within its husk, the appearance of Monk Chanzhi emerged.

He took a deep breath and chanted a Buddhist title, "Om Ah Hung, Mahakala-ya, Hung Phat."

Li Qi trembled upon hearing this.

This demonic monk wasn't chanting the Buddhist title of Amitabha or Mahavairocana Tathagata, but the exalted title of Mahakala!

Monk Chanzhi pressed his hands together and nodded at Li Qi. "Many thanks for your assistance, young friend. Ordinarily, when my devilish thoughts erupt, it takes this poor monk two to three months to recover. But with your intervention this time, I have recovered in a single night. It seems your words were not empty. The orthodox methods of the Shaman Dao can indeed help this poor monk balance his Buddhist and devilish thoughts."

His form of address for Li Qi had shifted from 'Benefactor' to 'Young Friend.'

However, before Li Qi could even speak, the monk continued, "It is just that... my young friend has only completely comprehended the method to break one devil. There are still one hundred and seven remaining. You must continue to work hard."

The corner of Li Qi's eye twitched.

_Listen to that tone? Are you planning to use me for free hard labor?_

One devil had taken Li Qi sixteen hours, nearly a full day.

One hundred and eight devils would require one hundred and eight days.

And that wasn't even factoring in the subsequent difficulties, such as conflicts between the different cultivation methods, resolving the resulting chain reactions, and a myriad of other complex issues.

Seeing Li Qi's expression, Monk Chanzhi revealed an amicable smile. "Young friend, you previously stated your willingness to offer me the wondrous methods of the Shaman Dao to resolve my conflict between Buddha and Devil. Could it be that you are planning to go back on your word?"

Looking at Chanzhi's smile, Li Qi's mind instantly calmed. He smiled back and said, "How could that be? Entrusted by Grandmaster, this junior will naturally go all out. It is but a mere half a year. To be able to resolve a major hidden danger for Grandmaster is truly this junior's great fortune."

Then, Li Qi pumped himself up, throwing his arms wide. "When traveling abroad, isn't mutual assistance the most important thing? Today, I help Grandmaster; tomorrow, Grandmaster helps me. The friendship between Mahakala and the lineage of Invocators and Earthly Divinities shall begin with the two of us!"

"Hahahaha!" Chanzhi laughed heartily.

"Hahahahaha!" Li Qi chimed in, issuing a bright and hearty laugh of his own.

After the laughter subsided, Li Qi turned around and quickly walked down the stairs.

Staying near this demonic monk made his skin crawl.

That devil god form the man took was absolutely not something he had just developed upon falling to the devil.

He _was_ a devil god to begin with! That form was merely what he reverted to when he lost control, similar to how a snake demon couldn't stop itself from turning back into its true form after consuming realgar.[^5]

This demonic monk's cultivation method and Dao path were originally inching closer and closer to Mahakala. Right now, Li Qi was still useful, so they could still exchange smiles.

If he ever lost his usefulness, he was destined to become a kapala bowl.

But just as Li Qi turned, he heard Chanzhi say from behind, "If you need anything, young friend, you may freely tell me. You are also a cultivator, so do not fret over a mere two or three years. Whatever items you require, simply state them."

"I don't need anything else for the time being. Grandmaster can just prepare some food for me. If possible, it would be best to get me a bed," Li Qi said, stopping and looking back.

Chanzhi smiled gently and nodded.

However, Li Qi faintly saw a gloomy haze lurking behind the monk.

It was like a venomous ocean reeking of blood. Monstrous waves of blood churned, and a devil god stood behind him, staring directly at Li Qi.

Ordinarily, he shouldn't have been able to see it. Li Qi wasn't qualified to sense the other party's aura.

But the True Knowledge dao rhythm had granted him senses beyond the typical five, and it was precisely these senses that detected this horrifying vision.

Li Qi suppressed a shiver.

He didn't say a word, silently making his way downstairs.

A Rank 7 expert possessed limitless lifespan. In many worlds, such beings were powerful enough to become Buddhas and founding patriarchs.

For now, he couldn't afford to provoke him.

However, the other party was currently wholly focused on testing the cultivation method he had just handed over.

This was his chance.

[^1]: _"Spring's growth relies on Jia and Yi occupying Yin and Mao; only then can one not fear Geng and Xin!"_

    * **Jia (甲) and Yi (乙):** These are the first two of the Ten Heavenly Stems. They correspond to the Wood element.
    * **Yin (寅) and Mao (卯):** These are Earthly Branches that also correspond to the Wood element and the season of Spring.
    * **Geng (庚) and Xin (辛):** These are Heavenly Stems that correspond to the Metal element. In the Five Elements cycle, Metal chops/destroys Wood.

    Li Qi is saying that if the Wood element (Spring) is perfectly rooted in its natural time and foundation (Jia/Yi paired with Yin/Mao), it becomes overwhelmingly robust. When Wood is this strong, it no longer needs to fear its natural enemy, Metal (Geng/Xin).
[^2]: _"Summer's flourishing relies on Bing and Ding riding Si and Wu; therefore, one need not evade Ren and Gui!"_

    * **Bing (丙) and Ding (丁):** Heavenly Stems corresponding to the Fire element.
    * **Si (巳) and Wu (午):** Earthly Branches corresponding to the Fire element and the season of Summer.
    * **Ren (壬) and Gui (癸):** Heavenly Stems corresponding to the Water element. Naturally, Water extinguishes Fire.

    Just like the previous line, if the Fire element is perfectly aligned with its peak season and foundation (Bing/Ding riding Si/Wu), it blazes so intensely that even its natural counter, Water (Ren/Gui), cannot extinguish it.
[^3]: In the traditional Chinese Five Elements system, Earth does not have a dedicated season of its own like the other four elements. Instead, it sits in the center and governs the transitional periods between the seasons (specifically, the last month of spring, summer, autumn, and winter). It acts as the anchor and mediator.

    Because Earth is the ultimate stabilizer that harmonizes the clash of the other elements, Li Qi is ordering Monk Chanzhi to stop using his own chaotic, erratic dharma power. Instead, he must channel earth qi to force the chaotic seasons back into order.
[^4]: This refers to the Twelve Phases of Qi in Chinese astrology. Qi is never static; an element transitions through stages such as 'Birth,' 'Prosperity' (its absolute peak), 'Decline,' and 'Death.'

    Monk Chanzhi is currently causing all four seasons to manifest at maximum power simultaneously, causing a catastrophic clash. Li Qi is telling him he must structure the energy. He has to consciously manage which season is at its 'Prosperity' peak and which ones are forced into 'Decline' or 'Equilibrium,' ensuring they flow in a rigorous cycle rather than exploding all at once.
[^5]: This is a direct callback to one of China's most famous folktales, The Legend of the White Snake (Baishe Zhuan). In the story, a powerful and benevolent thousand-year-old white snake demon named Bai Suzhen takes on a beautiful human form and marries a mortal man.
    
    Realgar is an arsenic sulfide mineral. In traditional Chinese medicine and folklore, it is considered a potent repellant for snakes, insects, and evil spirits. During the Dragon Boat Festival, people traditionally drank or wore realgar wine.
    
    In the legend, Bai Suzhen is tricked into drinking realgar wine. Because she is fundamentally a snake, the realgar suppresses her disguise, forcing her to painfully revert into a giant white snake, terrifying her mortal husband.
