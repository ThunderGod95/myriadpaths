# Chapter 154: Interception (II)

However, Jin Buhuan's expression instantly shifted.

"Traitor!" Jin Buhuan roared!

Accompanying his roar, a massive sun materialized in the sky!

The heavens gave birth to a second sun, and the ground below cracked and fissured. Despite being an early spring morning, the heat felt like the height of summer, and the piercing sunlight forced everyone to shut their eyes!

The blinding, fiery red light was entirely overwhelming.

But from Li Qi's vantage point, he could clearly see that the massive sun in the sky was actually a violently surging spear of fire.

The fire spear soared thousands of meters in length, aimed directly at Jin Buhuan's location. To Jin Buhuan, looking up, it appeared as though a blindingly intense sun had manifested directly above him.

Then, the fire spear plunged downward! Aiming straight for Elder Si's heart!

Elder Si let out a bellow of rage. His entire body suddenly emitted a blinding light as several divine figures flew out from within him.

These were the Shaman Dao's gods within the body.

Within a Shaman Dao cultivator existed a personal cosmos. This micro-universe contained the sun, moon, five elements, four great rivers, and five great Mountains. It formed a complete world of its own and could even project its influence into the external world.

And from within this personal cosmos, gods could naturally be born. These gods were governed by the cultivator's 'thought'; they possessed divine abilities but lacked sentience, acting entirely under the cultivator's command.

The greatest difference between Rank 9 and Rank 8 in the Shaman Dao was precisely these internal gods. Only by fully manifesting all the gods within one's body could a cultivator advance to Rank 8.

As for Li Qi... he hadn't condensed a single one.

But that didn't matter anymore. This was no longer a battle between him and Lord Yan.

It was Elder Si versus Lord Yan.

The internal gods flew out, and their divine abilities were unleashed!

Rainbows illuminated the heavens, winds stirred the clouds, qi rushed forth with enough force to rend heaven and earth, and their sheer might shocked the demons and terrified the ghosts!

There were the mountain gods of the five great mountains, the water gods of the four great rivers, and the heavenly gods of the sun and moon. Each guarded a specific direction. Ten thousand streaks of auspicious light circled into the sky, multicolored clouds brushed past, and propitious qi surged upward. The heavenly principles circulated endlessly in a flawless cycle.

With the internal gods protecting his body and the pure qi of heaven and earth swirling around him, Elder Si's personal cosmos began to subtly alter the surrounding environment, shifting the natural laws in his favor.

This was exactly what Li Qi had done earlier—expanding his personal cosmos to modify the local environment and tilt the odds in his favor.

This was the Shaman Dao's method of "Suppressing the Unfavorable."

As heaven and earth suppressed the unfavorable, the natural laws leaned toward Elder Si while simultaneously suppressing Lord Yan. The battle became a chaotic exchange of "you come, I go."

The difference of one rank completely separated the two combatants by two distinct tiers of power.

An internal god struck with full force, and the fire spear was directly shattered by the fist of a Five Great Mountains mountain god.

But Lord Yan rapidly formed hand seals. The spirit beast fire dove flew out from behind him, spewing its spirit beast heart fire.

The portion of the fire spear that hadn't completely dissipated was stimulated by this heart fire. It violently contracted into a spiral before detonating with a deafening boom, blasting one of the Five Great Mountains mountain gods into a mangled mess of blood and gore.

The surrounding area was instantly annihilated. Mountains were shattered, rivers were destroyed, and everything within a two or three-mile radius was completely overturned.

Fortunately, Elder Si had shielded Jin Buhuan, while Li Qi had long since run off without a trace.

Everything was going according to his plan. He was well aware that he absolutely could not withstand the aftershocks of a battle between Rank 8 experts.

Just how massive was the gap between Rank 8 and Rank 9?

According to Li Qi's own estimation, in a standard cultivation novel, entering Rank 9 was roughly equivalent to successfully establishing a foundation. Anything before that was considered unranked.

The jump from early Rank 9 to early Rank 8 was roughly equivalent to cultivating all the way from Foundation Establishment to the Tribulation Stage.

The disparity was truly that terrifying.

This was why Rank 8 cultivators were one in a million, and why challenging someone a rank above oneself was a near impossibility.

With an internal god injured, Elder Si's face paled as he recalled the mountain god back into his body.

However, the other internal gods were uninjured. They charged forward again, their fists hammering out like beating drums. They whipped up countless astral winds that blew Lord Yan's flames into a flickering, swaying mess. The spirit beast fire dove also retreated to Lord Yan's dove cage to avoid being battered.

"Heaven and earth suppressing the unfavorable? Tsk, a mere personal cosmos dares try to suppress me?" Lord Yan sneered coldly, no longer holding back. In the space surrounding him, a thousand fiery snakes abruptly sprang forth. The golden flames intertwined with the divine abilities of the internal gods across the plains, producing a ceaseless cacophony of booms and crackles. The sheer energy of their collisions turned the surrounding plains red-hot; stones melted into slag and were blown away by the shockwaves.

"Lord Yan! You dare commit betrayal? Do you not fear death?!" Elder Si demanded with a darkened expression, questioning Lord Yan even as he constructed spell arts and commanded his internal gods.

Lord Yan curled his lip. "The path of pursuing the Dao has always been a mixture of life and death. Since when has anything been entirely without risk?"

Elder Si was exasperated and furious. "What does this have to do with pursuing the Dao!? You treacherous, backstabbing scoundrel! Not being willing to lend a hand is one thing, but how could you betray and bite the hand that feeds you? Do you not fear the Jin family's retaliation!?"

"Retaliate for what? I'm not killing Young Master Jin. I'm merely killing an old man like you," Lord Yan replied matter-of-factly.

Even as they exchanged words, the battle intensified. Streams of flames spewed forth, dazzlingly brilliant, sending sparks flying in all directions.

These streams of fiery snakes crisscrossed like a spiderweb, or more accurately, like a silkworm cocoon. Wrapped in thousands of silken threads, the red filaments of flame gleamed brilliantly under the sunlight, weaving together to form a cage that suppressed Elder Si within.

Trapped deep within this heavy snare, Elder Si found that even the slightest movement caused countless fire doves outside the cage to spew sparks. Like ten thousand flying swords, the sparks shot directly at him, rendering him increasingly miserable and bedraggled.

Countless fire snakes formed an inescapable net that stretched endlessly. Looking down at the plains from the distant Clear Mist City, it appeared as though a massive red orb had materialized out of thin air. Boundless streams of flame, centered on Elder Si, formed a colossal sphere that blotted out the sky.

Tens of thousands of silken threads completely locked down any space for him to maneuver or dodge. It was as if a massive sun had suppressed and sealed Elder Si within a cage; he was visibly being pushed into a desperate situation.

Elder Si's originally perfected personal cosmos cracked like fragile porcelain, displaying countless fissures. It looked ready to shatter from within and vanish into nothingness at any moment.

His internal gods were still attempting to resist. Every collision with the cage sparked countless red embers, resembling infinite fireflies scattering across the horizon—a truly beautiful sight.

At this moment, Jin Buhuan, still under Elder Si's protection, finally spoke up.

He currently looked incredibly weak. Having just suffered an invasion of Rank 8 ghost qi, the ordeal had been too much for him. Yet, he forced himself to speak. "Lord Yan, although you haven't personally moved to kill me, your actions today have already offended me. Just what kind of benefits did it take for you to do such a thing?"

"Do not blame me, Young Master. I know you are protected by the mountain god's metal qi, so your life is in absolutely no danger. That is the only reason I dared to do this. Besides, whether Elder Si lives or dies has nothing to do with you," Lord Yan offered in his own defense.

Indeed, he knew of Jin Buhuan's trump card.

Hidden deep within Jin Buhuan's body was a wisp of the Green Mountain God's metal qi, capable of saving his life in a moment of critical danger.

This was also the Green Mountain God's consistent child-rearing strategy. As long as they didn't die, they could mess around however they pleased. Children could only truly grow by stumbling and crawling through the harsh realities of the world.

Therefore, Lord Yan wasn't the least bit concerned about Jin Buhuan's safety.

"The one who truly wants to kill you is Li Qi. I am merely intercepting Elder Si; beyond that, this has nothing to do with me," Lord Yan stated. "As for the reason... it's quite simple."

"The senior by Li Qi's side was willing to explain the flaws in my cultivation method and help me patch them up one by one."

"Young Master Jin, the weight of the Dao surpasses all external possessions. And aside from external possessions, you can give me absolutely nothing. So, do not blame me. You can only blame yourself for being one step behind; your planning was simply not as thorough as Mr. Li's."

Lord Yan spoke with an expression and tone that suggested his actions were entirely justified.

He had merely been hired in the first place; it wasn't as if he was bound to sell his life for Jin Buhuan.

Jin Buhuan let out a sigh and called out to Elder Si, who was still fiercely resisting. "Elder Si, stay your hand. If you continue fighting, I fear you won't be able to suppress your injuries..."

It was true. Elder Si had already intervened multiple times during the fight for the Rank 8 metal qi. Even if his oil lamp wasn't entirely dry, he was at the very least heavily injured.

If he truly engaged in a death match with Lord Yan, his strength would likely fall short of his will.

Now, Lord Yan's interception effectively neutralized Elder Si as a Rank 8 combatant.

Elder Si sighed, naturally aware of this fact. He ceased his resistance and allowed Lord Yan to seal and suppress him.

Having accomplished this, Lord Yan bowed toward Jin Buhuan. "Well then, Young Master Jin, I shall take my leave. It's just a pity that we shall likely never meet again in this lifetime. I won't know the outcome of your battle with Li Qi; what a shame."

However, having said that, he seemed to recall something and added, "Considering that Young Master has treated me well, I will offer a few more words. It's not completely impossible for Young Master to turn the tables. Li Qi knows I won't act to kill you. Thus, with Elder Si removed, if you and Miss Xiao can slay Li Qi, then all of his prior calculations will amount to nothing."

Having reminded Jin Buhuan, Lord Yan felt he had fulfilled his moral obligations. He turned away, transforming into a streak of escape light as he departed.

Shen Shuibi had already provided him with the corrections for his cultivation method. All he needed to do now was find a safe place and meticulously patch up all of his previous flaws.

As for the affairs of Clear Mist City...

Let them be.

His own path toward the Dao was the most important thing.

He, Lord Yan, had always been this way.

Jin Buhuan sighed, his expression turning gloomy.

As expected, his previous words had failed to steady Lord Yan; he had still been swayed by Li Qi's subordinate.

Before leaving his residence, he had instructed Lord Yan, promising to find a way to mend the flaws in his Dao path in the future, even if it meant utilizing his father's connections.

The very reason he hadn't brought Lord Yan along was precisely due to this concern, arranging for him to act as backup here instead. After all, Lord Yan had already proven somewhat unreliable; keeping him close might have posed a danger.

Having him wait here as backup not only eliminated that danger but also allowed his other forces within the city to keep an eye on him. Jin Buhuan only had two Rank 8s directly under his command, but he had more than a few allies. If Lord Yan truly rebelled, they could still suppress him.

But to think such a thing would happen before he even made it back to Clear Mist City... Truly...

None of his allied factions had stepped in to help. There was definitely a higher power involved. Was it the Clear Mist Sect?

Where did the Clear Mist Sect find the nerve? Weren't those two dead foxes afraid his father would skin them alive and turn them into shawls?

Jin Buhuan felt a surge of quiet frustration.

However, what's done is done; excessive regret served no purpose.

Things had already reached this point.

When it was time to risk his life, he had to risk it.

"Elder Si, just watch for now. No one will kill you anyway. In the end, the burden of unraveling this scheme still falls upon my shoulders." Jin Buhuan let out a long breath and snapped his fan open.

The maidservant by his side quickly stepped forward, helping Jin Buhuan to his feet.

"Xiao'er, step back and return to Green Mountain. Given Li Qi's demeanor, I don't believe he will make things difficult for you. Once you reach Green Mountain, someone will arrange a way out for you." Jin Buhuan waved his fan, signaling for the maidservant to retreat.

"But Young Master..." The maidservant seemed to want to say something.

"No 'buts'. Go. The disparity even within Rank 9 is massive. Your strength won't be of much help in deciding the outcome. You've served me for many years; don't throw your life away here." Jin Buhuan waved his hand dismissively. "Go. This is my order; do you dare disobey?"

The maidservant bit her lip, turned around, and began hurrying toward Green Mountain.

They were two thousand miles away from Green Mountain. Without Elder Si to carry her through the air, the journey would not be easy.

Having driven away his maidservant, Jin Buhuan violently struck his own chest. His body erupted with a massive surge of metal qi, forcefully propping up his form—which had been severely eroded by the Rank 8 ghost qi—and returning him to his peak combat prowess.

He was exchanging an extreme detriment to his physical health for a temporary return to his peak state.

Using this method carried a high probability of reducing the user's lifespan and lowering their future cultivation potential.

It was essentially forcing one last breath for a desperate struggle when the body had already given out.

After all, Jin Buhuan knew that if he didn't risk his life now, he wouldn't have a life left to risk later.

Winning meant losing a bit of his lifespan; losing meant losing 100% of it.

Even a fool would know which to choose.

Jin Buhuan stepped forward, his head held high, striding with a confident gait. He tapped his folding fan against his chest, having fully regained his confident, unbothered demeanor.

With a tiger's head and a human body, rings of golden light continuously spun around him. They melted together, transforming into a protective divine light that rapidly swept across his form.

A massive amount of metal qi dissipated outward, snapping the grass and trees and causing all living things to wither.

Simultaneously, Li Qi, who had just been running for his life, finally made his way back.

Compared to Jin Buhuan, who had returned to his normal state, Li Qi didn't look nearly as dignified. Having desperately dodged back and forth to avoid the battle's aftershocks, he was exhausted as a dead dog. Covered in sweat and panting heavily, he barely managed to drag himself over.

Jin Buhuan snapped his folding fan open and began fanning himself. "Brother Li, compared to the grand scale of your schemes, you look rather pathetic right now."

Li Qi wiped a handful of sweat from his brow, panting heavily as he spoke. "I've made a fool of myself before Brother Jin... I had no choice. Just figuring out what to do took everything I had; I really didn't have any energy left to care about my demeanor."

"Huff... puff... but since Brother Jin is saying this, you must have already figured out what's going on, right?" Li Qi asked between gasps for air.

Though phrased as a question, he was really just trying to catch his breath and delay Jin Buhuan a little longer.

Jin Buhuan was forcefully propping himself up; it was impossible for him to sustain that state for long. Li Qi himself had slightly overdrawn his stamina from his frantic sprint, so this was the perfect opportunity to recover some qi.

"If I hadn't figured it out by this point, I would be far too slow on the uptake. I'm just a bit puzzled—are you truly not afraid of my father? If all your actions fail to kill me, or fail to sever my father's intervention, everything you've done will ultimately be for naught." Jin Buhuan didn't launch a sneak attack like Li Qi had; instead, he asked Li Qi a genuine question.

He already understood exactly what Li Qi was doing.

Inciting Lord Yan to defect, convincing the Clear Mist Sect, rallying the local Clear Mist City factions that opposed him... Perhaps he even used some clever rhetoric to persuade them to suppress Jin Buhuan's own forces for a single night. Then, Li Qi himself would step forward to bear the Green Mountain mountain god's wrath and claim Jin Buhuan's head.

The scheme itself wasn't complicated; the difficulty lay in the execution. What exactly had Li Qi done to make everyone believe in him? Was that fox, the Clear Mist Sect Master, really someone so easily duped?

And how did he have the nerve to actually show up? What method did he possess to resist his father's terrifying might?

Jin Buhuan understood *how* he did it, but he couldn't figure out *where* his confidence came from.

Listening to his words, Li Qi nodded. "It's completely normal to have those doubts. But talking about it now is useless. Once this matter is settled, you'll understand when you see me face the Green Mountain God yourself."

"True enough. Come then. After half a day of scheming, it eventually has to come down to action. Let our true skills decide the outcome." Jin Buhuan folded his fan and tossed it behind him.
