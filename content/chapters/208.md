# Chapter 208: Desperate Struggle

Indeed, in Vast Yang, there had previously been no one capable of presiding over the landscape grand array.

This was also a reason Monk Chanzhi was at ease.

No one could control the landscape grand array; the only possible candidate was currently imprisoned by him within the Buddhist Pagoda.

As for other shamans, if they wanted to reach the level of controlling the landscape grand array, they would need to be at least the cream of the crop among Rank 8, or simply Rank 7 for it to be possible.

If such an expert had truly arrived, he would definitely have sensed it. Auras were not so easily concealed.

All things were formed by qi, and the qi of cultivators was exceptionally vast and radiant, highly conspicuous.

Even if someone specialized in concealment and hiding their aura, sneaking in secretly, if they specialized in concealment, could they still specialize in sacrificial rituals?

Hosting a sacrifice was no simple matter. All the taboos, precautions, the coordination of various parties, and whether one could convince ghosts and gods were all factors to consider.

Therefore, Monk Chanzhi concluded that the landscape grand array must be in a dormant state. Aside from his own Rank 7 puppet, there was no other entity in Vast Yang City capable of forcefully activating it.

Normally, this line of reasoning was correct and hard to fault.

But the mistake lay in—

No matter who it was, they could never have anticipated that in the tiny Vast Yang, two young masters of Shaman God Mountain would be present, and that they had joined forces.

Back when his separated divine soul was summoned by Shen Shuibi, the very first thing Li Qi did was touch Shen Shuibi's tail, even pulling on it to test how it felt.

At that time, his Covenant called out to Bo Yue, someone with whom he had previously established an alliance.

Both were Rank 9, both were young masters of Shaman God Mountain. Even if Bo Yue specialized in the path of divination, controlling a landscape grand array was still no problem for him.

And so, Monk Chanzhi fell into the trap.

Bo Yue stood a thousand miles away at the center of the landscape grand array, tugging the strings of the entire grand net with a single hand.

Beside Bo Yue, a trembling minor demon was biting her lip, utterly terrified.

"There is no need to fear. Since you are Li Qi's subordinate and even guided the way for me, I naturally won't make things difficult for you. Relax," Bo Yue said indifferently from his position.

He had changed his clothes and was now wearing an ordinary gray robe. He still looked like a young man as always, his long hair tied up and draped over his front.

His expression was incredibly detached, making it completely impossible to discern his thoughts. Yet, he did not exude a cold aura; his temperament was rather peculiar.

Because the vast majority of his energy was currently devoted to hosting the sacrifice, his reactions to the outside world had become rather sluggish.

Torrents of information flashed before his eyes, as if the very essence of the world was drawing closer to him.

Countless streams of information flooded his mind. Every minute detail in the surroundings—every mountain range and water vein in Vast Yang, the earth qi and water qi—were all perceived with crystal clarity.

He could hear the rushing of all the water veins, sounding just like blood flowing through blood vessels.

He could hear the mountains pulsing, much like a person's heart beating forcefully.

Just over a month ago, when Li Qi used the Covenant to call upon him, he hadn't planned on coming.

Why should he go out of his way to help him?

However, Li Qi told him that there was an unowned landscape grand array here, waiting for him to use.

And so, he came.

The allure of controlling an ownerless landscape grand array was simply too great for him. This was a genuine opportunity to commune with myriad deities, a chance to simultaneously observe them all while comprehending the flow of earth qi across an entire prefecture.

To a diviner, this meant access to a vast repository of data.

In the path of divination, apart from the gleaning heavenly mysteries, everything else was pure calculation.

Whether it was the Buddhist Sect's Three Lives Deduction Method, the Shaman Dao's divination, the Daoist Sects' Yin-Yang numerology, or the Human Dao's data statistics, they all operated on this principle.

Taking the Three Lives Deduction Method as an example: this technique could observe a past life, examine the present life, and ultimately deduce the form of the next life. It could calculate exactly where someone would reincarnate and what they would become. It was a profoundly wondrous technique, unparalleled for tracking people down.

It was precisely because of this method that the Buddhist Sect could constantly play their tricks with true spirits reincarnating and rebuilding cultivation across lifetimes. They could calculate exactly where the next reincarnation would take place.

This was no simple task.

Normally, finding out where someone reincarnated required hiring a Rank 5 expert to escort the soul the entire way. Furthermore, one had to bribe the Yin Bailiffs and grease the palms of the Ten Yama Kings and the judges. Otherwise, not even an immortal would know where the soul ended up.

Even the Ten Yama Kings themselves didn't know the exact destination of a reincarnation. Only the escorting Rank 5 expert would know by keeping a close watch the whole way. Securing those underworld connections merely allowed the escort to tag along with the remaining true spirit.

Reincarnation did not just occur within All Under Heaven.

Instead, it encompassed the entire cosmos and endless worlds. Following a soul all the way was no easy feat; it required at least a Rank 5 cultivation just to keep up, and the escort would also have to endure the backlash of the reincarnation cycle.

Under normal circumstances, no one could follow a soul the entire way, as doing so violated the rules of reincarnation.

However, as long as one could afford the price of breaking the rules, there were no rules to speak of.

Due to this massive cost, even major powers found it difficult to mobilize so many Rank 5 experts to escort souls individually. Thus, aside from crucial figures, the post-death fates of most people were left to chance.

The Buddhist Sect, however, benefited from the Three Lives Deduction Method to achieve this. They could continuously orchestrate theatrics like ten-life reincarnations and nine-life cycles.[^1]

The reason the Three Lives Deduction Method could accomplish this hinged entirely on the word 'deduction.'

Through some unknown method, it could observe the transformations across three lives and deduce the next.

The most critical aspect was observation—gathering sufficient information—before calculating, extrapolating, and ultimately reaching a conclusion that closely mirrored reality.

Let alone the Yin-Yang numerology, which lacked any elements of the profound whatsoever. It relied purely on deductive arrays and sheer mental calculations to reach conclusions.

Although the divination arts of the Shaman Dao contained relied heavily on interpreting the profound, the calculation component was also significant.

Therefore, borrowing the landscape grand array of a prefecture to observe the distribution of its myriad deities, earth qi, and water qi veins was immensely tempting for Bo Yue. To him, this was equivalent to being directly provided with countless first-hand data points.

By utilizing this data, he could draw inferences and thoroughly grasp related concepts. In the future, his accuracy when divining and calculating earth veins and water veins would skyrocket.

Normally, such opportunities were practically nonexistent.

Landscape grand arrays were strategic weapons of their respective regions; no one would activate them idly for fun. Even as a young master of Shaman God Mountain, he couldn't just act willfully.

If he truly relied on his status to act tyrannically, the first person to discipline him would be his own master.

But Li Qi had given him this chance.

So he came without the slightest hesitation.

After arriving, he discovered that Li Qi had already prepared everything for him.

A mountain god transformed from a butterfly—who, despite not yet being ranked, already possessed the embryonic form of a golden body—came to welcome him personally.

This mountain god then led him here and presented the formation blueprints of this landscape grand array.

Bo Yue truly didn't know how Li Qi had managed to get his hands on them.

Since Li Qi had prepared so thoroughly, Bo Yue was more than happy to save his energy.

Working with smart people was always a pleasure.

Besides, Li Qi only asked him to do one thing.

Connect with the myriad deities, activate the landscape grand array, and hold a grand blood sacrifice that would be visible across the entire Vast Yang Prefecture, perhaps even the Hundred Forests Kingdom.

And the sacrifice—

Was Monk Chanzhi.

The deities receiving this sacrifice would be all the demon rats of the Rat Soil Mound.

The landscape grand array was slowly activating.

This was not the previous civilian array used to regulate earth qi.

This was the final trump card specifically constructed to resist the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

Once the sacrifice commenced and the myriad deities joined forces, even a Rank 7 expert would face instant annihilation.

Thinking of this, Bo Yue suddenly realized something.

"Ah, right. Li... Li Zhufang, was it?" Bo Yue looked at the twelve- or thirteen-year-old girl behind him.

Although she looked twelve or thirteen, based on the lifespan of butterfly demons, she had probably only been alive for four or five years.

In other words, she was still just a child.

He didn't know how Li Qi had tricked her into coming, but her talent was quite good. To have already stepped onto the Divine Dao at this age meant she could likely break through to Rank 9 within a year or two.

If Bo Yue remembered correctly, this was a characteristic of insect demons, right?

Insect demons with poor talent had no right to take human form. Their lifespans were only a few months; the shortest among them lived and died in a single day. Without talent, they wouldn't even have the chance to become demons.

"Exactly so. I wonder what instructions you might have, sir?" Li Zhufang hurriedly bowed, asking cautiously.

"Nothing much. Hide further away, lest you be caught in the crossfire." Bo Yue waved his hand.

Originally, he wanted this minor demon to provide some assistance, but seeing how young she was, he decided against it. It was better to let her stay far away.

"Hide further away? There shouldn't be any shockwaves, right?" Li Zhufang was slightly confused.

"Normally, there wouldn't be any." Bo Yue nodded. "However, if someone fights me for control, that's a different story."

"Retreat a mile away. A fight is about to start." Bo Yue smiled, then devoted his entire being into the landscape grand array.

---

At the exact moment Bo Yue spoke.

The revival of the landscape grand array suddenly stalled.

The intense summoning that could be felt throughout all of Vast Yang halted abruptly, stuck awkwardly in the middle!

Another force had forcefully intervened halfway through.

The myriad landscape deities could all sense that the person hosting the sacrifice was no longer one individual, but two!

How could there be two people?

They were all bewildered.

The previously arranged formations and the already-connected myriad deities were thrown into confusion.

They didn't know who to listen to anymore.

Wasn't activating the landscape grand array's sacrifice supposed to be a deeply solemn matter? It was a monumental feat that required every single deity to reach a consensus to complete. Yet now... why were the two leaders clashing?

The multitude of deities had no idea why this was happening.

However, Monk Chanzhi and Bo Yue knew.

Just as Bo Yue initiated the sacrifice, Monk Chanzhi finally tore away all his disguises.

He now understood that Li Qi fully intended to put him to death right here.

Although he didn't know how a mere Rank 9 cultivator had managed to rally so much assistance, there was no doubt the other party was exceedingly well-prepared.

But Monk Chanzhi realized that Li Qi had miscalculated one thing.

He still had a puppet.

The Vast Yang Minister of War, who cultivated the Gu Shaman Dao.

This puppet was also capable of controlling the landscape grand array.

Therefore, Monk Chanzhi immediately split his focus. On one hand, he controlled the Minister of War to directly intervene and halt the landscape grand array's activation. On the other, he unleashed his full power to confront the Rat King!

*Bastard!* Monk Chanzhi cursed inwardly, consumed by fury!

Seizing control of the landscape grand array was no easy task.

The reason he hadn't used it all this time was simply because he didn't know how!

As a monk, he was fine with leading Buddhist rites and ceremonies. But when it came to hosting a sacrifice to the mountains and river, he was completely clueless.

However, if he allowed the other party to successfully control the landscape grand array right now, he would truly meet his end here.

Thus, even if it meant being driven to do something beyond his abilities, he had to stop them.

He didn't seek to seize control of the landscape grand array for himself; he only aimed to make the other party fail.

He had to obstruct this person!

Simultaneously, he struck out without hesitation.

He had to defeat the Rat King as quickly as possible and kill everyone present! Then, he would track down that newly emerged Shaman and kill him too!

This was his only remaining chance.

And that Li Qi—he couldn't be spared either!

Truly refusing face when it was given! He had already been extremely lenient. If Li Qi had just felt genuine gratitude and cooperated properly for a year or two, he would have naturally set him free. Everyone would have been happy; it would have been a win-win situation.

But he just had to court death!

Once this matter was settled, he would light Li Qi up as a sky lantern, burning his divine soul and physical body to ashes bit by bit! He would let the flames of the Nether Prison consume his body, torturing him for a century!

This was the price for scheming against him!

Did Li Qi really think this was enough to kill him?

Keep dreaming!

A mere Rank 9 cultivator simply couldn't fathom his true strength!

Monk Chanzhi's fury burned as killing intent surged within his heart. His tyrannical malice grew exponentially. His body instantly expanded a thousandfold, crushing mountains and collapsing rivers as he transformed directly into a massive devil!

Seeing this, the Rat King, who had been tangling with Monk Chanzhi, felt its fur stand on end and immediately retreated!

It understood Monk Chanzhi very well. After all, they had traveled together all the way from the Western Heaven to Baiyue.

When Monk Chanzhi emitted this kind of aura, it meant he was about to risk his life!

Then, with one hand holding a flower and the other pressing its palm upward, he chanted loudly: "The bodies of all living beings perish and their lives end! The three calamities of Beasts, Hungry Ghosts, and Asuras descend upon the world, devouring all living things without exception! Not a single mortal remains! This world rots and descends into catastrophe!"[^2]

As soon as these words were spoken, his three-inch devil kingdom shattered entirely, transforming into a swirling vortex.

From within the vortex, absolute disaster surged out.

The doors to the lower three paths of the Six Paths of Reincarnation[^3] had opened.

It was no longer just the Nether Prison aspect born from the three-inch devil kingdom.

In truth, the Nether Prison aspect was merely an illusion formed by the divine souls Monk Chanzhi had killed and collected within his devil kingdom.

What opened at this moment was the true, genuine catastrophe.

The sentient beings from the Path of Beasts, the Path of Hungry Ghosts, and the Path of Asuras within the Six Paths of Reincarnation swarmed out from within!

A massive black tempest erupted! Countless beasts, hungry ghosts, and green-faced, sharp-toothed Asuras lunged out. They began devouring the divine souls within the devil kingdom, instantly filling the world with agonizing, piercing wails.

Almost visibly to the naked eye, the divine souls in Monk Chanzhi's devil kingdom began to dissipate. As these souls were eaten by the swarming hungry ghosts, beasts, and asuras, it directly caused Monk Chanzhi's cultivation to regress in tandem.

However, it was worth it.

It was just sacrificing some cultivation.

In a moment, not a single living person would remain in the entire Vast Yang Prefecture.

As long as he prevented the landscape grand array from being activated, he would regain everything he lost!

[^1]: **Ten-life reincarnations and nine-life cycles (十世轮回, 九世轮回)**

    In Buddhist mythology, high-ranking monks or divine entities often undergo multiple reincarnations to temper their souls, accumulate karmic merit, and shed mortal attachments before achieving ultimate enlightenment.
    
    The significance of these specific numbers stems from Eastern numerology, Daoist philosophy, and the I Ching (Book of Changes):
    
    * **Nine (九):** As the highest single-digit odd number, nine is the ultimate "Yang" number. It represents the absolute extreme or peak of a progression. Reaching the "ninth" stage of a cycle (like a nine-life cycle or a nine-petaled lotus) implies pushing a concept to its absolute limit right before a fundamental, qualitative transformation must occur.
    * **Ten (十):** While nine is the extreme limit, ten represents flawless perfection and a completely closed cycle. Reaching ten signifies that a process is completely fulfilled and has returned to the source to begin anew.

    In the context of this novel, this passage serves to critique the Buddhist Sect's monopoly on the cycle of rebirth. Because they possess the "Three Lives Deduction Method" to calculate exactly where a soul will land, their elites can deliberately orchestrate these perfect nine- or ten-life grinds to build flawless foundations. Ordinary cultivators and mortals, on the other hand, drink Meng Po's soup, forget their past lives, and are left entirely to the mercies of random chance.
[^2]: This chant is a twisted inversion of traditional Buddhist sutras.

    _"The bodies of all living beings perish and their lives end!"_
    
    **Orthodox Meaning:** A reflection on the impermanence of mortal flesh, meant to detach a monk from earthly desires.
    **Chanzhi's Meaning:** A literal death sentence. He is enforcing the end of their mortal lifespans.
    
    _"The three calamities of Beasts, Hungry Ghosts, and Asuras descend upon the world, devouring all living things without exception!"_
    
    **Orthodox Meaning:** A warning against the karmic retributions of giving in to ignorance (Beasts), greed (Hungry Ghosts), and wrath (Asuras).
    
    **Chanzhi's Meaning:** He is physically tearing open the boundaries to these realms, using their inhabitants as a cataclysm to consume the living.
    
    _"Not a single mortal remains! This world rots and descends into catastrophe!"_
    
    Chanzhi's ultimate goal. By destroying the mortal world and creating a vacuum of death, he intends to violently seize control of the landscape grand array and crush his enemies with the raw weight of karmic disaster.
[^3]: **The Six Paths of Reincarnation (六道轮回 - Liùdào Lúnhuí)**

    The Six Paths of Reincarnation is the core concept of Samsara in Buddhist cosmology. It dictates that all sentient beings are trapped in a continuous cycle of birth, death, and rebirth, placed into one of six realms based on the karma (good or bad deeds) accumulated in their previous lives.
