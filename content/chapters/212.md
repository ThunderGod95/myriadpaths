# Chapter 212: Aftermath

Half an hour later.

Li Qi, Bo Yue, and Le Ya sat together in the secret manor of the Lower Nine Sects.

However, even now, the vast majority of people in Vast Yang City remained completely unaware of what had just transpired. Aside from a mild commotion caused by the recent tremors and the flashes of light beyond the horizon, it was... quite peaceful.

Of course, the leaders of the major factions were surely aware of the matter concerning the young masters of Shaman God Mountain. After all, Le Ya had bowed to him in full view of the public; that could not be faked.

The ordinary commoners, however, were entirely clueless. They had no idea they had nearly perished just moments ago, nor did they know that another group of people had fought to the death to carve out a path of survival for them.

Dying without knowing why, and living in a daze—this was likely their current reality.

Not that Li Qi had any intention of publicizing his deeds. He wasn't particularly concerned with their recognition.

Right now, what he cared about more was that he had finally established a connection with Shaman God Mountain...

He truly... wanted to cry.

It was a bitter feeling.

Someone was finally backing him up.

The bitterness, however, came from the fact that his backing was merely a byproduct.

Le Ya had only come because he sensed Bo Yue was in danger.

Even though Li Qi had invited Bo Yue with this exact intention—guessing that Bo Yue would have a Dao Protector—seeing it in person...

It certainly left him feeling a bit unbalanced.

But those were minor details.

The moment they sat down, Li Qi immediately spoke up.

"Senior Le Ya," Li Qi said, clasping his hands in a respectful greeting.

Le Ya waved his hand, stopping Li Qi from continuing.

Li Qi was stunned but obediently fell silent.

Beside him, Bo Yue was coughing up blood, his face deathly pale. The backlash from having the landscape grand array forcefully interrupted was far too severe. He had yet to recover and could currently only listen, not speak.

His personal cosmos had fractured, stripping away half his life. It was purely by luck that he had some precious spiritual medicines on him; swallowing them had tethered him to life. Otherwise, he might have genuinely died on the mountain.

Yet, even as he wiped away the blood, he pricked up his ears, listening intently to Le Ya.

Both of them understood very well that although Le Ya treated them with respect—bowing upon meeting and addressing them as young masters—he was a Rank 5 entity. He was a presence that absolutely commanded reverence.

To become so arrogant over mere status and potential that one lost sight of reality was the mark of the most mediocre of fools.

Seeing both young masters halt their words to listen, Le Ya gave a slight nod. "Let us start with Young Master Bo Yue. My appearance here today means that, this time, you have failed."

Bo Yue offered a bitter smile.

He coughed up more blood as he smiled, looking exceptionally miserable.

"However—" Le Ya immediately pivoted.

The sudden shift caused Bo Yue to cough violently. His face paled another shade, and he hurriedly produced a medicine bottle to take another gulp.

Watching from the side, Li Qi felt a pang of sympathy, inevitably struck by a sense of shared sorrow.

"However, I have gathered a general understanding of what transpired. The two of you joined forces and, relying only on simple local resources with almost no support from your sect, merely borrowed a name to intimidate your foes. As Rank 9 cultivators, you fought against Rank 7 opponents. Such a battle record is exceedingly rare and can be considered a paragon of defeating the strong as the weak. In the end, the opponent's master descended—a Rank 5 expert arriving in person. There was nothing you could do. Therefore, your failure is excusable."

These words brought a rush of color back to Bo Yue's face, and Li Qi felt his own tension ease.

"But!" Le Ya's face hardened once more.

Li Qi reacted swiftly, stepping forward to support Bo Yue. He channeled his Great Forest Wood qi to soothe Bo Yue's vitality, lest the constant shock outright kill him on the spot.

Failing to die on the battlefield only to be scared to death by their own side—that would truly go down in the annals of history.

It was a good thing Bo Yue couldn't speak right now, otherwise he probably would have started cursing.

Le Ya seemed entirely oblivious, continuing on his own. "But this battle must still be counted as a loss. Young Master Yue lost due to an unforeseen accident, which is forgivable. But this young master..."

"I am Li Qi!" Li Qi immediately released Bo Yue, clasping his hands and stating his name.

Le Ya's expression turned stern. "Indeed. Young Master Qi, Young Master Yue followed your plan and encountered an irresistible force. His failure is excusable. But you have no such excuse."

"A Rank 9 challenging a Rank 7—your ambition is commendable, your courage praiseworthy. But did you not assess the risks? To execute a perilous gambit purely to prove yourself is absolutely not the action of a wise man."

"You were the one laying out the plans and acting as the tactical strategist. Yet, you focused only on showing off your skills, calculating human nature, and plotting strategy, while committing an error right from the start that a wise man never should."

"You should have anticipated the arrival of a Rank 5 long beforehand. Why did you not prepare in advance? Must you stubbornly cling to your pride? Where is your Dao Protector? Why did you not invest more resources?"

"A planner's duty is to coordinate and dispatch resources, placing every object and person in their most optimal position. You must predict risks, assess the situation, and make the decision that best aligns with the strategic goal."

"You executed everything else exceptionally well. Whether in dispatching, commanding, or even predicting, it would be hard to do better. But tell me, why did you not anticipate the descent of a stronger foe? Why was there no contingency plan? If I hadn't come, wouldn't the two of you be dead? And whether I came or not was not up to you."

"Failing to prepare for the worst-case scenario while clearly possessing resources, choosing instead to place yourself in peril for the sake of personal glory and a battle record—this is the act of a brave warrior, not a wise strategist. Now, Young Master Qi, what do you have to say in your defense?"

Le Ya maintained his stern visage, awaiting Li Qi's response.

Hearing this, Li Qi could only manage a bitter smile. He offered no excuses, simply clasping his hands and replying, "Senior's lecture is correct."

Seeing Li Qi's receptive attitude, Le Ya's expression softened slightly, though he still spoke with earnest gravity. "You are both favored children of heaven. In the future, you will strive to reach Rank 3. You are entirely different from someone like me, whose path on the Dao has already reached its end."

"I know you possess outstanding abilities and staggering talent. It is natural that your pride is high and you constantly wish to prove yourselves."

"Therefore, you must be even more cautious. No matter what you do, you must carefully plot every step. On the Dao, a single misstep leads to ruin. You are still young, which means you must be extra vigilant not to let your eyes be clouded."

To defeat a Rank 7 as a Rank 9 through pure planning and strategy was an earth-shattering achievement, yet in Le Ya's eyes, it was still a failing grade.

These standards... were unbelievably strict.

Nevertheless, Li Qi nodded. "Thank you for the guidance, Senior. I will engrave it in my heart."

Observing Li Qi's sincerity, Le Ya nodded in reassurance. "Then I ask Young Master Qi to step outside for a moment. I must heal Young Master Yue. After all, continuously coughing up blood is hardly a good thing."

"Understood." Li Qi stood up and patted Bo Yue's shoulder. "I'll step out first. We can talk privately later."

That single pat caused Bo Yue to spew another mouthful of blood, his wide eyes now glazed over.

Seeing this, Le Ya waved his hand. Li Qi was instantly swept back, bursting through the doors to land smoothly outside.

He shrugged helplessly and went off to find Shen Shuibi.

There was still much aftermath to deal with; he couldn't afford to stop now.

---

Once Li Qi had left.

Le Ya placed a hand on Bo Yue and began the healing process.

He was reconstructing his personal cosmos.

All the cracks were mended with incredible speed.

An injury that would have ended a cultivator's path in a place like the Righteous Sword Palace was completely fixed in under two minutes.

"Phew..." Bo Yue let out a long breath, his breathing finally growing smooth.

Le Ya did not rest. He stared at Bo Yue and said, "Alright, having addressed Young Master Qi's issues, it's time to address yours, Young Master Yue. When you were invited to participate in this, did you not realize—"

Bo Yue cut Le Ya off, shaking his head helplessly. "Wait, Grandpa Le, you wrongly blamed him. I feel embarrassed on your behalf... You asked why he didn't mobilize more resources. Do you even know who his master is?"

"Who is it?" Le Ya frowned.

Bo Yue held up three fingers and whispered, "Invocator..."

Then, he stopped abruptly, refusing to say any more.

But Le Ya froze.

Then, he stood up and slapped his hands together in sudden realization.

So that was it!

No wonder!

He had thought Young Master Qi employed such a dangerous gambit purely for glory, to forge a battle record, and to showcase his own abilities.

Now that he knew the identity of his master.

It all made sense—

No wonder, no wonder! Young Master Qi's entire layout had seemed exceedingly cautious. Despite taking a risk, he had suppressed the danger as much as humanly possible, factoring every possible variable into his plans.

Someone capable of such meticulous arrangements was certainly not the type to recklessly gamble.

He had assumed the youth was just arrogant and eager to prove himself. Clearly, he had misunderstood.

He wasn't acting out of youthful arrogance.

He simply had no other choice!

So, roping in Young Master Yue was also part of his plan? The objective was to secure both an assistant and a safety net when Yue arrived.

And that safety net was him.

And indeed, because of Young Master Qi's arrangement, he had rushed here and blocked the final danger for him.

Every link perfectly connected.

Brilliant!

He had completely misjudged the boy. Far from being ill-prepared, he had calculated everything with staggering thoroughness!

It was just that, aside from a perilous gambit, he truly had no other options.

Being the disciple of *that* entity, he must have suffered a great deal, hadn't he?

Seeing Le Ya's reaction, Bo Yue pursed his lips. "It's a good thing he didn't expose you to your face, otherwise you'd have been thoroughly humiliated."

"I'm not afraid of losing face; I'm afraid of misunderstanding him. No, I must go apologize immediately. I cannot let a false judgment stand." Hearing this, Le Ya hurriedly got up and pushed open the door to leave.

Bo Yue slapped the table from behind. "Hey! You are my Dao Protector!"

Le Ya stroked his beard in annoyance. "You aren't going to die sitting here!"

With that, he hurried out the door, leaving Bo Yue sitting alone in the room.

He didn't dare move, either.

His injuries weren't completely healed. He'd likely have to sit here for an hour or two, waiting for the microscopic fissures in his personal cosmos to mend themselves.

*Damn it, he didn't even help me lie down!*

*This old man!*

---

As for Li Qi, he was walking along the road.

Suddenly, he saw Le Ya run up from behind, stopping in front of him to offer a solemn apology.

Li Qi had not anticipated this at all.

He hurriedly waved his hands. "No, no, no, Senior, your words are too heavy. It is indeed my fault for failing to prepare adequately. Although this appears to be a total victory, the process was fraught with luck and guesswork. The casualties are also severe."

At this thought, Li Qi sighed.

"Tens of thousands of Earthly Divinities in Vast Yang are gravely injured or dead. The White Rhino Army and other local forces have suffered nearly fifty percent losses. The Vast Yang Minister of Works is severely wounded, and the Rat King sustained minor injuries. The landscape grand array has shattered, and its reconstruction is nowhere in sight. Vast Yang's earth veins have been severed; there likely won't be any crop yields for a decade. During this time, wild beasts will be barren, rivers will run dry, and fish and insects will perish."

They said he had saved Vast Yang, but... was Vast Yang truly saved?

Li Qi sighed, looking up at the street.

The people of Vast Yang had absolutely no idea what was about to happen.

In the future, unless the citizens of Vast Yang fled as refugees to other prefectures, they would likely all starve to death.

Le Ya, having just apologized, saw Li Qi in this state and gave a slight nod. "Since that is the case, does Young Master still remember the duties of an Invocator? Do you still remember the path you have chosen?"

Li Qi immediately clasped his hands and answered solemnly, "To connect heaven, earth, humanity, divinities, and ghosts. To facilitate the exchange of what is needed, and to mediate from the center to perfect the Dao of Righteousness."

Upon hearing this response, Le Ya nodded. He raised a hand, pointing toward the main street beyond the courtyard. "In Vast Yang Prefecture at this very moment, heavenly qi is scarce and earth qi is exhausted. The Earthly Divinities have suffered heavy blows, and the demons and ghosts are severely wounded. Only humanity has been preserved. Given the current situation, what should be done?"

Li Qi jolted in realization!

That's right!

Why hadn't he thought of this earlier?!

An Invocator did not simply stand on the side of humanity.

He needed to maintain the balance of the entire world, the entirety of the *Dao*. Only then would the outcome be beneficial for everyone.

If the world itself tilted out of balance, it would only march toward destruction.

The Dao of Balance was the key to the survival of all things!

When the realization hit Li Qi, it was like a flash of sudden enlightenment.

He hastily clasped his hands and said earnestly, "Thank you for the guidance, Senior. I shall go make preparations at once!"

He was going to rally the multitudes, gather human qi, and perform a Grand Rite to offer sacrifices to heaven, earth, and the ghost-gods, thereby replenishing the primordial qi of Vast Yang.

This was also for the good of everyone.

If Vast Yang truly failed to produce crops for ten years, could the people living here possibly survive?

The entire ecosystem, the entirety of heaven and earth, were inherently interdependent.

The righteous path was the method that allowed the entire world to continue operating smoothly.

Seeing Li Qi remain as sharp as ever, understanding immediately from a mere hint, Le Ya stroked his beard and nodded. "Go then, Young Master. However, presiding over a ritual for an entire prefecture is still too taxing for your current cultivation base. If you need assistance, do not hesitate to seek me out. I will remain here for the next few days. As compensation for wrongfully blaming you earlier, please accept the corpses of those two monks to use as sacrificial offerings. After all, human qi alone will likely not be enough."

"Thank you, Senior. I will go handle this immediately." Li Qi gave a solemn bow and hurried away.

He would leave the wrapping up to Wang Boyan. Although that scoundrel would undoubtedly skim some profit off the top, if anything went wrong, he would just beat him until he coughed it all back up.

The most pressing matter was exactly what Le Ya had mentioned.

He was going to prepare a ritual.

A Grand Rite involving at least ten million people. He would gather human qi to give back to heaven and earth, dragging the ruined mountains and rivers back from the brink of extinction!
