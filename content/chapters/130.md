# Chapter 130: Covenant

In the distance, it began to rain.

Meanwhile, Li Qi was presiding over a rite on the other side, about a dozen miles away.

The ancient texts stated: "When the sun reaches the winter solstice, water follows it; when the sun reaches the summer solstice, fire follows it. Thus, in the fifth month, fire is at its peak while water leaks forth. Therefore, at the summer solstice, the yellow marshes flow, and stone essence emerges."

What did this mean?

During the summer solstice, although Yang fire was at its most vigorous, Yin water invariably accompanied it. Because the fire qi of the fifth month was so intense, water qi seeped from its source. From time to time, yellow marshes would flow, and the stone essence would also exude water qi.

As such, this was a time when "floodwaters swell, the earth is damp with muggy heat, and heavy rains fall in their season. By burning the weeds and dispersing water, one benefits from killing the overgrowth. Do this, and by this decree, a sweet rain will fall three times this month."

This meant that during this season, when the earth was naturally moist and the weather humid, one could simply burn dried wild grass, sprinkle water upon the ground, and scorch it with the fire qi of the Vermillion Bird. Like boiling water, doing this would bring timely rain and wash away the dark qi.

This was the rite for rain.

It was not a prayer for rain, but rather manipulating the circulation of qi to artificially create it.

This was the 'clean water' Li Qi had promised the caravan.

However... Li Qi knew this was merely a drop in the bucket.

The ancient texts also recorded: "All life is not born from the transformation of a single qi. When qi is discordant, the wind and rain will be ill-timed, the sweet rain will not fall, frost and snow will be out of season, and the cold and heat will be disproportionate. The Yin and Yang will lose their order, the four seasons will reverse their rhythm, the people will suffer from excesses and become frail, birds and beasts will fail to gestate and multiply, flora will stunt and fail to flourish, and the five grains will wither and yield no harvest. The populace will be plagued by disease, the roads littered with abandoned infants, the blind, bald, hunchbacked, and lame will multiply, and all manner of monstrosities will be born."

This meant that the existence of life could not be sustained by just one type of qi. When qi fell out of balance—much like the current state of the Earth Oil World—it would lead to unseasonable weather. The rain would not fall, the waters would dry up, frost and thunder would strike out of turn, and temperatures would wildly fluctuate. The natural order of Yin and Yang would be shattered, and the progression of the four seasons upended. The people would be unable to produce, animals unable to breed, and vegetation too stunted to grow. Crops would wither without bearing fruit. Illness would run rampant among the commoners, roads would be lined with abandoned infants, and all sorts of bizarre anomalies would spawn.

The texts had already explained the phenomena occurring in the Earth Oil World with absolute clarity.

By using the qi of the Vermillion Bird to draw upon the water qi from the nearby city, Li Qi invoked a light drizzle through his rite. It could only relieve their immediate distress.

At best, he had simply fulfilled his promise and provided them with clean water.

Yet, their accounts, combined with the barren wasteland he saw with his own eyes, were enough to prove what the Great Deer Kingdom had done here.

But... did he have the right to save them?

To the ruler of the Great Deer Kingdom, the Earth Oil World was a vital resource domain. The earth oil produced here would be shipped to countless other worlds, even to that overarching All Under Heaven.

Such a crucial resource world—could he just tell them to stop, and they would? He was merely a disciple of the Grand Invocator, not the Grand Invocator himself. He didn't have that kind of face.

But if they didn't stop, how could he possibly change this world?

Suppose he forced the Zhang Corporation to shelter these refugees from the outside. Would the people in those satellite cities agree? Even if they were forced to comply, could they truly accept them?

When the time came, would these refugees be able to accept those "city dwellers"?

Forcing them together would only bring more harm than good.

Even a rainmaking rite like this didn't create water out of thin air. It still relied on the world's inherent water qi.

The water didn't materialize from nothing; he had summoned the rain by borrowing the water qi from within the city.

If he were deeper in the wasteland, he wouldn't have been able to make it rain at all.

This world simply could not support this many people.

He thought it over and over, but there were no solutions.

"Sigh, I'm still too weak. If I possessed the vast dharma power to transport earth qi and water qi from the void to replenish the emptiness here, this situation wouldn't exist," Li Qi murmured with a touch of melancholy.

But the moment the words left his lips, a sudden shiver ran down his spine. His body went rigid, horror blooming in his chest!

Li Qi jerked his head up to the sky, staring at the canopy of stars, the constellations of the heavens, and the four-aspected Heavenly Gods!

One must know, the celestial phenomena of all worlds were identical!

And these celestial phenomena guided the circulation of a world's qi, even bringing in extraneous qi.

_Holy shit? Don't tell me it's exactly what I'm thinking!_

Although a vague hypothesis had formed in his mind, he had no proof. The texts hadn't recorded such a thing, so Li Qi simply filed the speculation away in his heart.

After finishing his tasks, he packed up the dharma altar used for the rain rite and prepared to leave.

Right at that moment, he suddenly frowned.

The ancient texts stated: _Heaven and Earth resonate, Yin and Yang interact; this is called qi. All things take form below, while their qi corresponds above. Therefore, when a heroic warrior enters the court, a white rainbow pierces the sun; when a king is about to emerge, crimson clouds blanket the sky above. Thus it is said: Observe the qi to know the event, look at the clouds to know the person._

This meant that qi was something that resonated with the Yin and Yang of Heaven and Earth. Every type of qi corresponded to something truly existing in the material world. For example, earth oil was formed from the combination of earth qi and water qi through specific laws.

The same applied to living creatures. Thus, when a heroic warrior harboring intense emotions stepped into the great hall, the corresponding qi outside would manifest as the anomaly of a white rainbow piercing the sun. When a person possessing the aura of a sovereign appeared, red clouds would crown their head.

Therefore, by observing the corresponding qi, one could deduce the corresponding event. By simply looking at the qi radiating from a person, one could perceive their true capabilities.

This method of observing qi was practically the signature skill of Shamans. A significant portion of the Diviners' path of prophecy was built upon this very foundation.

While Invocators did not specialize in this area, they still understood the fundamental principles. For instance, Li Qi had previously observed the presence of military qi, using it to predict the impending calamity of war he was about to suffer.

And now, he observed something similar.

In the sky above, the Earth Star encroached upon the Root constellation, the Fire Star encroached upon the Southern Dipper, and the Army Breaker  star was poised to slay a general. It was an omen of great famine and great plague.

There was also a shooting star transforming into a cloud—a sign of flowing blood and mountains of bones.

Yet, surrounding all of this was pure and bright qi, completely devoid of dregs. It was yellow-white and lustrous, carrying an air of majesty and virtue. A hero was emerging.

This wasn't the result of a divination, but... an omen.

It was an omen naturally manifested by this very world.

“A civil war, born from the conflict between us young masters?” Li Qi murmured.

For these worlds, it certainly seemed that way.

From this world's perspective, the existence of Li Qi's group and the Great Deer Kingdom was an absolute scourge. Their presence brought not a single shred of benefit.

Li Qi let out a sigh.

Suddenly, his desire to compete with the young Diviner felt much weaker.

_This time, I won't bother trying to strike first._

_Wait, since the other party could attack me from afar, even nearly making me lose directly by exploiting a flaw in my Dao Heart... then it should be possible to converse now, right?_

For a Diviner, long-distance communication should be a simple matter.

Acting on the thought, Li Qi immediately began setting up the ceremonial items and the dharma altar.

"'To forge a Covenant is to lay out one's words beneath the clear square of the earth, invoking the divine.' If I do this... he should sense it, right?" Li Qi mumbled to himself as he arranged the altar.

The phrase Li Qi chanted meant: The invocation spoken before the idols of the gods was the "Covenant."

The so-called Covenant referred to an agreement reached between two parties under the gaze of an Invocator and the witness of the Heavenly Gods.

Only an agreement of this caliber could be called a "Covenant," and the parties involved were termed "Allies."

In the rites, such agreements were divided into two types: Oaths and Covenants.

An Oath was a vow, formed with the intent of self-restraint.

A Covenant, however, had to be mutually established by both parties under the witness of an Invocator.

Therefore, their binding power was vastly different.

_Using the rite of a "Covenant" to send a remote invitation... as a Diviner, the other party should be able to perceive it, right?_

Li Qi pondered as he finalized the rite.

Meanwhile, far away on a polar snow mountain on the other side of the world.

The Diviner, who had been resting, suddenly snapped his eyes open.

"A Covenant under the gaze of the Heavenly Dao? And the invitation is for... me?" His expression shifted, his gaze instantly turning intrigued.

"Is it a trap? Or a simple chat?" he murmured.

But he quickly shook his head, rejecting his own theory. "Impossible. A Covenant is a grand rite; it cannot be used so frivolously! Not unless he wants to be severely reprimanded by his master."

Even this Diviner knew the ancient texts recorded: "The grand essence of a Covenant must be initiated in times of crisis. It points to the Nine Heavens as the ultimate arbiter, requiring a reverent and immaculate sacrifice. The Invocators merely facilitate the dialogue, for true sincerity lies in absolute solemnity."

This meant that the primary characteristic of the "Covenant" rite was that it must be utilized during critical moments. It required the Heavenly Dao as a witness. It was a grave sacrifice that rested upon the moral integrity of the officiant, and the sincerity of that morality was rooted in solemnity. This was the duty of an Invocator.

As an Invocator, it was impossible for him to employ such a crucial rite lightly.

Thus, after only a moment of deliberation, he decisively used his power to accept the Covenant's invitation.

At this moment, the sheer versatility of the Shaman Dao was on full display. By leveraging all sorts of rites, the divine abilities and spell arts of Shamans were incredibly bizarre, completely defying standard logic.

A martial artist of the same Rank 9 would absolutely be incapable of communicating across half a world like this.

However, at the same rank, it wouldn't be unusual for a martial artist in close quarters to beat two Shamans to death.

Every Dao path had its own characteristics. The Shamans' defining traits were their erratic unpredictability and endless arsenal of tricks.

The Covenant entered its coordination phase.

The function of the Invocator began to take effect.

As mentioned many times before, the duty of an Invocator was "communication."

It was that familiar chaotic space once again.

The two disciples of Shaman God Mountain met for the first time.

To outsiders, they were the lofty, unreachable young masters of Shaman God Mountain. But Li Qi, having grasped the clarity of his Dao Heart, was no longer swayed by this title.

And this Diviner surprisingly didn't seem to take it seriously either. It appeared his own Dao Heart had undergone significant tempering as well.

"A pleasure, a pleasure." The Diviner looked rather frivolous. He strolled over and offered a casual clasp of his hands as a greeting. "Junior Brother, calling out to me so suddenly with the rite of a Covenant... whatever for?"

Li Qi, however, was incredibly solemn. He didn't take offense at being called 'Junior Brother'; instead, he straightforwardly accepted it. 

"Senior Brother, you are a Diviner. I wonder if you have sensed it—the phenomena tonight of the Earth Star encroaching upon the Root constellation, the Fire Star encroaching upon the Southern Dipper, and the shooting star transforming into a cloud."

Even as an Invocator, he had sensed it. As a Diviner who specialized in this exact field, the other's perception was undoubtedly much stronger, and he knew far more than Li Qi did.

"Naturally. But isn't the reason quite simple? To this world, the conflict between the two of us is a cataclysm. Aren't we both trying to push this Heavenly Dao into a rank-dropping conclusion?" the Diviner said, sounding utterly unbothered.

It was true. Both were attempting to force the Heavenly Dao to drop in rank, shift the blame onto the other party, and ultimately step in to "save" the Heavenly Dao. This would allow them to observe a portion of the dao rhythm generated by a falling Heavenly Dao while also reaping its karmic rewards.

It was a classic case of "I want it all."

The loser would not only walk away with nothing but would also offend the ruler of the Great Deer Kingdom. And the consequences of offending him would likely not be pleasant.

Both were individuals who had already shed their arrogance. They naturally understood just how vast the gap was between themselves and the ruler of a kingdom once their identities as Shaman God Mountain disciples were stripped away.

Let alone the ruler of a kingdom, even the Rank 6 Governors of the various prefectures were god-like existences to them.

They were respectfully addressed as "young masters" not because of their personal strength, but due to their prestigious backgrounds. But background was merely an external factor. A status unsupported by personal power would not last.

Li Qi looked at the Diviner before him and spoke earnestly. "Senior Brother, your cultivation and age are both greater than mine, and your experience is surely deeper. You know these omens signify that this world is about to plunge into catastrophic suffering, and all of it is starting because of us two. I truly cannot bear it. Thus, I have come to request of you, Senior Brother: the dispute between Diviner and Invocator is one thing, but implicating the lives of tens of millions of people is another. We really don't need to escalate matters to such a devastating scale."

"Tch, the mercy of a woman. Do you think the Great Deer Kingdom only has one world like this? How many exist across All Under Heaven? How many can you possibly save?" Hearing this, the Diviner scoffed, a hint of disdain creeping into his gaze as he looked at Li Qi.

Bearing innate dao rhythm and guided by their masters, they were destined to hold high positions in the future, standing above countless mortals.

This wasn't arrogance; it was a clear-eyed understanding of their own reality.

Even now at Rank 9, they could effortlessly stir the winds and clouds of an entire world. After ascending to higher ranks in the future, walking the myriad heavens, plucking the stars and moon, and establishing their own monumental merits would be child's play.

Harboring the mercy of a woman for a petty little world at this juncture was truly foolish and cowardly.

"It is not a matter of how many to save, but that we must not actively inflict such destruction," Li Qi said, clasping his hands. "And I do this not out of kindness or mercy, but out of righteousness and justice."

"Oh? How so?" The Diviner looked at Li Qi, curious to hear his reasoning.

“Whenever an Invocator offers up the proper rites and speaks the words, is there any god who does not respond? Why is that?” Li Qi asked the Diviner.

The Diviner naturally understood the meaning. "It is because the power of the Grand Invocators suppresses the myriad heavens. Moreover, the Shaman God reigns supreme, ruling the southwest of All Under Heaven. Therefore, the myriad gods dare not disobey," the Diviner stated proudly.

Although it was the power of the Invocators, it was still a part of Shaman God Mountain. The dispute between Diviner and Invocator was, at the end of the day, an internal conflict within Shaman God Mountain that wouldn't affect the outside world, so he remained proud of it.

But Li Qi shook his head. "I believe that is incorrect. I believe that when an Invocator lay out their sincerity, it is to reward loyalty and filial piety, to pledge mutual survival and ruin, and to unite hearts and efforts. Their words truthfully inform the gods, and the abundant sacrifices are purely fragrant, rooted in luminous virtue—it is because of this that the myriad gods submit in admiration."

Upon hearing this, the Diviner frowned in silence, but did not voice a rebuttal.

Because he didn't know how to refute it.
