# Chapter 105: Close at Hand

Old Ma tread upon the clouds, galloping into the distance.

Li Qi was still somewhat bewildered.

He had expected a fierce battle, so he announced his background in an attempt to make the opponent retreat in the face of difficulties.

Who knew the reputation of Shaman God Mountain was so terrifyingly vast that it literally scared the opponent witless?

"This thing... it's a double-edged sword," Li Qi sighed.

Shen Shuibi had recognized the opponent's roots. The old man was also a practitioner of the Shaman Dao, known as a Ghost Shaman.

Shamans who allied with ghosts were therefore Ghost Shamans.

It meant that when Shamans formed cliques and joined forces with ghosts, such Shamans were referred to as Ghost Shamans.

Ghost Shamans were not orthodox Shamans. In his understanding, orthodox Shamans possessed no methods involving blood sacrifices or controlling ghosts.

Thus, he could be certain the opponent was not an orthodox Shaman. He recited a passage from the [Invocation] regarding Ghost Shamans to establish his identity and flaunt his backing.

Even though Shaman God Mountain probably didn't even know someone like him existed, he was just pulling a tiger's skin to serve as a banner... Besides, he wasn't lying. Young Master Zhu had said he would take him in as a disciple.

But the effect was simply too good.

So good that Li Qi was filled with worry.

Li Qi naturally liked to look ten steps ahead for every step he took. Sometimes his overthinking even made him seem indecisive. After this incident, he began contemplating how to handle the title of a Shaman God Mountain disciple.

_While a large tree provides good shade..._

_It also attracts the wind..._

_Forget it. I don't have enough information, so it's better to keep a low profile for now. I'll consider these things once I see Shaman God Mountain. Until then, I shouldn't reveal my identity if I can avoid it._

It was just that... a mere title was enough to awe an Assistant Magistrate of a county.

_Truly terrifying._

However, this also made Li Qi understand more clearly the difference between orthodox Shaman Invocators and these rural Shaman Invocators.

An Invocator could borrow the power of a Ghostly God, but the prerequisite was mutual consent, unlike a Ghost Shaman's method of directly controlling ghosts.

Because ghosts lacked the qi of the Five Constant Virtues, they had no sanity to speak of. Ghost Shamans could subdue them through certain means, driving them as ghost slaves.

A true Invocator would never do such a thing. If they encountered these ghosts, they would most likely disperse them directly, helping them return to the cycle of reincarnation.

If true Invocators opened the floodgates of ghost taming, it would be a massive problem.

Malicious ghosts... could be artificially created.

Grievances? Hostility?

They were so easy to manufacture.

Li Qi absolutely did not believe that the malicious ghosts in that Assistant Magistrate's hands had all died innocent and unjust deaths.

Judging by his words and actions, and listening to what the Jing family had said, this person was definitely not a good sort in Snake Tail County.

Li Qi did want to stand up and administer justice, but considering he probably couldn't win right now, he had no choice but to run.

_There will be plenty of time in the future. Better take care of myself first._ Li Qi considered himself a passably good person, but not to the extent of throwing his life away to right an injustice he stumbled upon.

He would return to Snake Tail County.

Along the way, Li Qi once again urged his horse into a wild gallop. This time, he didn't care about the stares of others, brazenly driving Old Ma forward. Treading upon cloud qi, they flew along the path.

The speed was incredibly fast. An ordinary mountain-harvesting platoon would only feel a gust of strong wind blow past, only able to see the exhaust trails of Old Ma in the pitch-black night.

Occasionally, a powerful mountain-harvesting platoon might catch a glimpse, but they wouldn't give chase.

_Are you joking? Chase a dragon colt?_

_Can a human outrun a horse?_

When traveling long distances, a dragon colt could cover eight hundred li in a day, or one thousand six hundred li traveling day and night. This was its cruising speed, meaning it could maintain this pace without harming its body.

When needed for combat or fleeing, Old Ma's burst speed could easily reach six or seven hundred li in half a shichen. Pushed even harder, it might be even faster.

However, utilizing burst speed not only consumed stamina but also caused great harm to Old Ma. It required a vast amount of food to replenish its strength so it wouldn't suffer a loss of essence qi.

And it couldn't be ordinary food; it had to be natural treasures containing pure essence qi.

Sprinting for a minute or two during combat was fine, but traveling for hours on end would truly exhaust the horse. Running at that speed for one shichen required a full day of rest.

_A person can't rely on hundred-meter dashes to travel long distances either; a steady jog is needed._

But even so, it was still a speed that left others choking on their dust.

A night passed, and another city appeared before Li Qi.

"Looking at the map, this should be Azure Mountain County." Li Qi fiddled with his map, determining their bearings.

"Miss Shen, how much further are we from Lady Luofu?" he asked Shen Shuibi over his shoulder.

"Hmm... Let me feel it. It seems we still have about ten thousand li to go? Our direction is correct. In half a month at most, we should be able to see Her Ladyship!"

"Moreover, Her Ladyship hasn't seemed to move from her position for the past half month. She must have settled somewhere," Shen Shuibi said happily.

"That's good. We traveled all through the night, so let's rest here in Azure Mountain County for now and set off again tomorrow. Once we depart this time, we won't stop until we reach Her Ladyship's side!" Li Qi arranged.

He then patted Old Ma. "Don't complain about being tired, old brother. The one we are meeting this time is Lady Luofu. By then, even if she plucks a single hair from her body, it will be enough for you to transform into a dragon."

Shen Shuibi pinched Li Qi, saying in dissatisfaction, "Her Ladyship wouldn't pluck her hair!"

"Let's put it another way then: whatever leaks through Lady Luofu's fingers would be enough to benefit us endlessly!" Li Qi laughed heartily, then spurred his horse into the city.

Yet, although Li Qi was laughing and smiling, there was no mirth in his eyes.

They were about to meet Lady Luofu.

_But is it truly a good thing?_

Li Qi always felt that meeting such a monumental figure was basically equivalent to walking into a dragon's pool or a tiger's den.

While there were rewards, the risks were equally high.

Not to mention, walking into a dragon's pool or a tiger's den meant your life or death was in your own hands, and your harvest depended entirely on your own effort. It was dangerous, yes, but at least you controlled your own fate.

However, when meeting a major figure, whether it was rewards or life and death, it was all held in someone else's hands...

_Besides, once we meet her, Shen Shuibi will have to leave, right? After all, staying by Lady Luofu's side is her favorite state. Not to mention, she is an expert of Rank 5. Me being able to travel with her is purely down to my good luck._

Up until now, he had benefited immensely from her pointers. He hadn't taken a single wrong detour in his cultivation; his foundation was solid, and his cultivation speed was incredibly fast. It was all thanks to Shen Shuibi's unreserved guidance.

_After this, there won't be any more of it._

_How should I put it... I feel a bit uncomfortable._

Regardless, the journey still had to be made.

There wasn't anything particularly special along the way. Li Qi wasn't some unique existence who would stumble upon fortuitous encounters and various crises the moment he stepped out the door. In short, after resting for the night, he continued his journey.

Fifteen days passed in the blink of an eye.

Braving the wind and sleeping in the dew, even though Li Qi used the excuse of taking care of Old Ma to rest quite a bit along the way, the distance was inevitably shrinking.

Finally, on the sixteenth day, following Shen Shuibi's guidance, Li Qi arrived deep in the mountains.

Li Qi was very vigilant.

Because, unlike the major roads between the county towns earlier.

This was a true, remote primeval forest.

"Right ahead! Twenty li at most!" Shen Shuibi, however, was very excited.

They were almost there.
