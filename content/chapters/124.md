# Chapter 124: Child of Destiny

Two days passed quickly, and Li Qi had indeed gained a much clearer understanding of the laws governing this world.

As expected, combining practical experiments with theoretical study was the fastest way to progress.

By comparing various data sets and witnessing the formation process of earth oil in this world with his own eyes, he benefited immensely.

He even began tentatively applying this knowledge to his spell arts. With a few minor adjustments, he adapted his sacrificial rites to better suit this world, significantly amplifying the power of his spell arts and sacrifices here.

_If I can instantly grasp these relatively simple changes in every world and make the corresponding adjustments, then I'll have the home-field advantage no matter where I go... This should be a viable path for advancement, right?_ Li Qi pondered.

He then walked out of the laboratory to witness the spectacular scene of earth oil extraction.

Tens of thousands of people crowded the landscape, working in tandem with various machines to extract the earth oil.

Forklifts, conveyor belts, and immense manual labor converged on this massive construction site. The earth oil gushing from the drilling wells was collected barrel by barrel and transported away.

Subtle differences in this world's rules allowed for the creation of a bizarre substance like earth oil. Furthermore, in accordance with the universe's singular Great Dao, matter taken to another world would not collapse into nothingness.

This resulted in a rather unique phenomenon. Every world possessed its own exclusive resources, and All Under Heaven, as the most powerful entity, ruled over all the outer-realm worlds within its territory. Various factions would extract resources from their respective worlds, trading some to fulfill their needs and keeping others for their own use.

It was truly a wondrous sight.

In All Under Heaven, the Great Deer Kingdom maintained a backward and primitive ecosystem. Yet, in the outer realms, they had initiated industrialization, wantonly plundering the resources of these outer-realm worlds.

The extracted earth oil was transported to a spatial passage opened by the ruler of the Great Deer Kingdom. From there, it was shipped to various destinations—sometimes to other outer-realm worlds, and sometimes back to All Under Heaven itself.

However...

Li Qi sensed that something was amiss.

_What is going on with this qi?_ Standing on the massive work site, Li Qi could see an endless amount of human qi converging through the laborers' toil.

And the convergence point was right near him.

In fact, it was drawing closer!

Li Qi went on high alert. He secretly mobilized his Vermillion Bird divine qi while observing the various progress bars around him to guard against a sneak attack.

Even though he was at one of the industrial company's main headquarters, he remained vigilant.

At that moment, a man's voice called out to him. "Young Master need not be so wary. I am merely here to deliver some documents at Mr. Bai Di's behest."

As he spoke, a middle-aged man walked up the nearby stairs.

Li Qi could clearly see that the man's body was linked to the qi of all the laborers.

It was like... a giant formation.

Noticing Li Qi's expression, the man took the initiative to explain. 

"This is a cultivation method imparted to me by the Lord of the Great Deer Kingdom. It is a Human Dao technique that allows one to gather the strength of the masses for one's own use."

"I use 'contracts' as a medium to establish a connection with them, allowing their power to serve me and form a Human Dao collective. This way, I can reach Rank 9. The Lord said that once I unify all the companies in the Earth Oil World, even Rank 8 will be just around the corner."

Hearing this, Li Qi was slightly surprised. "So, you are the president of the industrial company here?"

"I dare not accept such a respectful title from the Young Master. You may simply call me by my name. I am Zhang Le." He looked somewhat apprehensive.

"Then I'll just call you President Zhang. You mentioned you were here to deliver documents. Is it the intelligence I requested earlier?"

Zhang Le bowed his head and produced a file folder. "Exactly. Please take a look, Young Master."

"Thank you." Li Qi took the folder and began flipping through it.

Zhang Le stood quietly to the side, waiting with a respectful demeanor as Li Qi reviewed the materials.

Meanwhile, Li Qi focused on the contents of the documents.

They listed the two largest earth oil industrial companies in this world: the current Zhang Corporation, and another entity known as the Polar Industrial Development Corporation.

The Zhang Corporation controlled inland earth oil extraction, while the Polar Development Corporation monopolized extraction in the polar and oceanic regions.

These two companies viewed each other as mortal enemies and were locked in fierce competition.

The cultivation method given to them by the ruler of the Great Deer Kingdom destined them to compete. There was no such thing as coexistence in this wicked Human Dao art; both sides were bound to fight over the other's population, labor force, and earth oil extraction sites.

"So, you came to tell me that the Polar Industrial Corporation also has a 'Young Master' on their side?" Li Qi frowned after finishing the documents.

He had guessed right; there really was a competitor.

Moreover, the other party had no intention of playing nice or minding their own business.

"That is correct," Zhang Le confirmed. "This is not merely a struggle between two Young Masters... The Zhang Group is willing to assist you in defeating the other party. In doing so, wouldn't you also receive more rewards, Young Master?"

"The rewards are secondary... they aren't of much use anyway. However, someone is preparing to make a move on me, and a heavy-handed one at that, without even meeting me face-to-face. I have no choice but to strike back," Li Qi said thoughtfully, putting the documents away.

"Exactly! Therefore, our group will definitely fully cooperate with your actions, Young Master, to crush the Polar Industrial Corporation!" Zhang Le expressed his resolve.

Li Qi glanced at him.

He understood that Zhang Le's true motive was fear. Without the protection of another Young Master, the Zhang Industrial Corporation risked being suppressed and ultimately defeated by the Polar Industrial Corporation.

Now that Li Qi was here, Zhang Le desperately needed to cling to his coattails to restore the previous stalemate.

However, this was also beneficial to Li Qi. At the very least, operating alone in this world wouldn't be as smooth as having the support of the local top dog.

"Fair enough. Let's do that. I'm definitely going to deal with him, and having your help will be nice." Li Qi nodded.

"Zhang Le will absolutely not disappoint you, Young Master!" he immediately pledged his loyalty.

"No need to be so formal. First, go and deliver a calling card for me. It shouldn't be too far from here to the Polar Industrial Corporation's territory, right? Can we get there in half a day?" Li Qi asked, patting him on the shoulder.

"Ah? A calling card? You want to venture deep into the enemy camp?!" Zhang Le instantly realized Li Qi's intentions.

"What do you mean 'deep into the enemy camp'? Look at how you phrase things," Li Qi chuckled.

"We are all fellow disciples, not enemies. Your Earth Oil World is also a subordinate world of the Great Deer Kingdom. We're merely competing with each other; we aren't mortal foes."

"Since that's the case, what's the harm in paying a visit?" Li Qi said with a relaxed expression.

---

Soon, a calling card was sent via email from Li Qi's side at the Zhang Industrial Corporation straight to the Polar Industrial Corporation.

Back on that snowy mountain, inside that small room.

"This is a provocation," the middle-aged man from before said to the Diviner Young Master nearby.

His identity was now obvious: he was the boss of the Polar Industrial Corporation.

"No, this isn't a provocation. He wants to negotiate with me," the Diviner Young Master replied, shaking his head with a smile.

_What a brilliant move._

The struggle between Invocators and Diviners was also a Dao struggle, but it wasn't a fight to the death. It was merely a battle for authority within Shaman God Mountain.

It was unlike the conflict between the Shaman Dao and the Human Dao.

"Negotiate?" The boss of the Polar Corporation frowned in confusion.

He didn't understand. Was there really room for negotiation between these two Young Masters?

"Yes, negotiate. He doesn't want to clash with me, so he wants to meet and have a proper talk. That's why he's being so open and honest. However, while he calls it a negotiation, I'm afraid he just wants to suppress me with his momentum," the Diviner Young Master said, a smile on his face as he toyed with the dagger in his hand.

It was a black stone dagger that looked as if it had been chipped into shape. It bore mottled strike marks, appearing to be newly made rather than a stone tool smoothed out by long use.

"Invocators are incredibly powerful. They can borrow the power of mountains and rivers, making them formidable in frontal clashes. Look at the intelligence you gathered last time—that massive crater, comparable to the explosion of an earth oil extraction rig, was his doing."

"If his strength has already reached that level, then meeting him for this negotiation would place the outcome of our contest entirely in his hands. There won't be any room for me to talk then," the Diviner Young Master muttered to himself, weighing the options.

The boss of the Polar Corporation remained silent.

This Young Master loved talking to himself, and he would grow angry if anyone interrupted him.

"But if I don't go, I'll appear overly petty. It won't sound good when word gets out." The Diviner frowned deeply, looking quite displeased.

He refused to lose face.

He wanted to have his cake and eat it too, securing a complete and total victory.

The room fell quiet, the only sound being the continuous clicking of him toying with the dagger.

However, after a while, he suddenly looked up at the boss of the Polar Industrial Corporation.

"I recall you are at Rank 9 of the Human Dao, correct?"

"Y-Yes."

"Good. I will use a substitution technique to temporarily transfer my qi onto you. You will go meet him in my stead."

"But my appearance is vastly different from yours, Young Master..."

"Just use an illusion to change it. The point is to deliberately let him recognize you," the Diviner Young Master waved his hand impatiently.

"Deliberately let him recognize me?" The boss looked up, deep in thought.

"Exactly. Isn't he being overly arrogant, wanting to meet in person to suppress me with his momentum? If I go, I take a risk; if I don't, I lose face and look petty." The Diviner Young Master stood up from his chair. "Then I won't hold back either. Since he dares to put himself in danger, I'll make sure to arrange enough danger for him."

"When he's lying on the ground waiting to die, I'll personally make an appearance and have a friendly 'negotiation' with him. Won't I have both my victory and my face then? It's decided. You, go and do as I've arranged!"

Hearing that the Young Master had made his decision, the boss of the Polar Industrial Corporation immediately bowed. "As you command!"

With that, he hurried away, not even waiting for the Young Master to explain the specific details.

Because he knew that in a little while, the Young Master's 'plan' would emerge from his own shadow.

This Young Master seemed to be everywhere.

---

While the two Young Masters of Shaman God Mountain were scheming and plotting against each other, Sha'er and Lu Li were looking utterly bewildered. They had been given a car, a few days' worth of food and water, and were simply told to leave.

Yes, Li Qi had let them go just like that.

His exact words had been: "Go back and tell your companions to stay quiet for a little while. Don't make any rash moves. I will help resolve the matter regarding your Heavenly Dao. However, I will need to use you when the time comes. By then, you should already know whose side you're on."

"For now, just go back. Return and report everything you've learned here to your leader."

After saying that, he had someone escort them out.

And then, they were truly sent on their way, receiving food, supplies, and a brand-new car.

The car drove through the wasteland.

Kicking up a cloud of dust in its wake.

The two looked at each other in dismay, completely unable to figure out what that Young Master was thinking.

Was he just acting on a whim? Or was there some deep, hidden conspiracy behind this?

They didn't understand.

But one thing was certain.

The other party was an Exalted Immortal, and an incredibly powerful one at that. Moreover, he harbored no hostility... or at least, he hadn't shown any yet. Therefore, they shouldn't make an enemy of him.

A trail of dust lingered over the wasteland.

The car would occasionally pass by dried-up riverbeds, and sometimes they would come across a patch of dead, withered woods.

Decades ago, these places had been lush with green hills and clear waters.

But with the extraction of earth oil, the ley lines became obstructed, and the water qi was severed. Consequently, the world's water qi circulation was gradually cut off, leading to the birth of these massive expanses of wasteland.

This wasn't a so-called desert terrain; it was an absolute, utter wasteland where no life could survive.

A desert was merely a type of terrain. In reality, a desert possessed abundant earth qi and continuous water veins, harboring groundwater, geothermal heat, and a variety of life and flora.

But in this kind of wasteland, life couldn't find a single shred of sustenance. The 'qi' here had been severed. Earth qi was not generated, and water qi did not flow. The mineral veins formed by metal qi had been mined to exhaustion, and the wood qi had long withered away. It was dry and bitterly cold, lacking even subterranean fires.

Of the five elemental qi—metal, wood, water, fire, and earth—not a single one could be found in this desolate land.

This was the consequence of recklessly plundering a world's resources with no regard for the future.

The five elements mutually generated one another. Originally, they could slowly elevate their own scale and sustain existence. But now, the five elements had been severed, turning the area into a dead zone.

This was the plight of the Earth Oil World.

It was also the plight of all resource worlds subjected to endless extraction.

And the Earth Oil World was just an incredibly insignificant one among them. Its Heavenly Dao was incredibly weak. Even a Rank 9 like Li Qi, relying on a wealth of knowledge and the profound foundation of All Under Heaven, could toy with this place in the palm of his hand.

"Platoon Leader, do you think... does that Young Master have some ulterior motive, or...?" Lu Li asked as he drove, glancing at the lioness Sha'er curled up in the backseat.

Sha'er was too massive, so she had to curl up just to fit inside the vehicle.

"Don't overthink it," Sha'er replied in a muffled voice. "We have no way to resist the power of an Exalted Immortal."

"Therefore, we can only rely on Number One."

"She is the true Child of Destiny."
