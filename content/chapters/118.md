# Chapter 118: Departure

Setting aside the trivial matters along the journey, twelve days later, Li Qi followed the ceremonial guard of the governor of White Snake Prefecture straight from that valley all the way back to White Snake Prefecture City.

Seven days had passed since they arrived here.

White Snake Prefecture.

The governor's estate.

Li Qi was observing the celestial phenomena in the south.

For summer had arrived.

The south aligns with fire. Its sovereign is the Flame Emperor, and his attendant spirit is Zhu Ming. Holding the Balance, they govern summer. Its deity is Yinghuo, its beast the Vermillion Bird, its note is Hui, and its days are Bing and Ding.

"At the summer solstice, the Big Dipper points south along the meridian; Yang qi reaches its peak, and Yin qi begins to sprout..." Li Qi murmured to himself as he watched the sky.

The meaning of his words was that during the summer solstice, the Big Dipper pointed south toward the Wu and Chen positions, aligning with the prime meridian. At this moment, Yang qi reached its absolute limit, and Yin qi began to stir.

At the summer solstice, fire followed. Upon reaching this time, one no longer needed to offer sacrifices to the Azure Dragon, but rather to the Vermillion Bird, one of the four celestial phenomena.

The Supreme Tenuity presides over the Vermillion Bird of the South, while the Purple Palace held the Dipper and rotated leftward, moving one degree a day to complete its circuit of the heavens.

The celestial phenomena were correct.

Then, the sacrifice could begin.

To sacrifice to the Vermillion Bird, one needed to face south, revere the color red, and use a Red Zhang to honor the south.

That is to say, using a red jade tablet to sacrifice to the god of the south, the Vermillion Bird.

Furthermore, one also needed to offer crimson silks, a light red fabric. Both of these were favored by the Vermillion Bird.

Offering these two sacrifices, arranging the items, reciting the prayers, moving according to the regulations, and chanting—the sacrificial rite was completed.

The rite was finished.

The qi within the sacrifices dissipated. The beautiful jade turned into an opaque stone, and the crimson silk became coarse and powdery, as if it had been weathered for a very long time.

And Li Qi had acquired the second of the qi of the four seasons.

The divine qi of the Vermilion Bird of the South.

Fire governed the south, blazing fiercely to transform all things. Between spring and summer, the wood qi had not yet fully dissipated when the fire qi was born. In spring and summer, excess fire brought about fierce punishment. If the scorching heat of midsummer erupted, it was called the fire of 'Entering Office'; with wood above to fuel it, its qi flourished.

The Si month marked early summer, its fire growing ever brighter, representing the extreme of the six Yangs. The fire qi obtained in early summer was called the Fire of Flourishing Qi.

From this name alone, Li Qi could sense the immense destructive power contained within.

This wisp of fire qi could not be used on himself like the divine qi of the Azure Dragon; instead, it was to be unleashed outward.

Presumably, its power would not be small.

With the fire qi entering his body, his personal cosmos had completed two of the qi of the four seasons.

Once all four were complete, it would be time to enter the ranks.

"Phew, it's finally over. That wasn't too difficult," Li Qi said, sensing the changes within his body and the intensity of that wisp of Flourishing Qi Fire.

"Young Master possesses extraordinary talent and has earned the favor of the Grand Invocator. A trifling matter like entering the ranks naturally poses no difficulty for you." Beside him, a guard stepped forward and flattered Li Qi.

"Haven't I said it already? Brother Bai, there's no need to call me Young Master. Just call me Li Qi," Li Qi said with a bitter smile to the incredibly handsome man beside him.

He was truly handsome.

Although called a guard, the man was nine feet tall, ethereal and transcendent. Dressed entirely in white, his black hair cascaded like a waterfall. He possessed the bearing of fine jade and an outstanding temperament. Standing next to Li Qi, he made Li Qi look like a lowly menial servant in comparison.

This transcendent young hero in white was named Bai Di, the nephew of the governor of White Snake Prefecture.

After arriving in White Snake Prefecture City, he had been assigned to serve as Li Qi's guard.

Li Qi could roughly figure out the specific intentions behind this. It was likely the hope that this peer of his—two young men in their twenties—could develop some sort of friendship, which would undoubtedly bring tremendous benefits to White Snake Prefecture in the future.

As for why a nephew was chosen, it was probably because if this person didn't get along well with him, the governor could easily wash his hands of the matter. After all, he wasn't a direct descendant; if he needed to be discarded, he could be discarded.

Moreover, under the guise of being a guard, he could conveniently observe what Li Qi did on a daily basis.

It couldn't be called surveillance, but it would at least reveal Li Qi's usual preferences.

At the very most, there was a high probability that Bai Di could follow him into that extra-territorial realm, securing a spot under the name of a guard. This counted as one of the governor's selfish motives.

After all, only upon arriving here did Li Qi learn that the other world was not under the governor's final say. Instead, each prefecture recommended their outstanding talents and sent them to the ruler of the Great Deer Kingdom, who would make the final decision.

It was, after all, the Great Deer Kingdom's own other world.

However, his own status was not low. The ruler of the Great Deer Kingdom most likely wouldn't dare to cross his name off the list.

In this way, by recommending him and attaching a nephew, the governor secured a free spot with absolute certainty.

He could only say that the governor truly lived up to his position; he was a shrewd fox.

Since everyone was helping each other out, Li Qi had no reason to refuse bringing someone along. He could figure all of this out, so he felt there was no problem in agreeing and gave his consent.

But after the man arrived, Li Qi regretted it.

Bai Di was simply so excellent that it made him feel somewhat ashamed...

In terms of height, he was a full head taller than Li Qi.

As for his cultivation, he was also at Rank 9 of the Martial Dao, but it was extremely profound. He was only one step away from Rank 8. It was said that he was only a hair's breadth away from condensing his Martial Dao divine will.

Once he broke through that hair's breadth difference, he could directly enter Rank 8.

His way of treating people and handling affairs was impeccable, as gentle and refined as jade.

He could be considered an outstanding hero handpicked from the hundreds of millions of people in White Snake Prefecture, excellent enough to make Li Qi feel inadequate by comparison.

Yet, he was exceptionally respectful towards Li Qi. He always addressed him as Young Master, accompanied him wherever he went, and never showed the slightest breach of etiquette.

"How could I dare address the Young Master by name?" Bai Di hurriedly lowered his head and said.

"Forget it, we'll each call the other what we want. Brother Bai, we should be setting off today, right? It really has been a bother making you wait for me until now," Li Qi said.

"The Young Master's cultivation is important. A delay of a day or two is nothing. Furthermore, my ability to head to the extra-territorial realm for tempering relies entirely on the Young Master. How could I be disrespectful to you?" Bai Di answered immediately.

"There really is no need to be so formal..." Li Qi scratched his head, his face full of helplessness.

Originally, the journey to the extra-territorial realm could have begun earlier. However, Li Qi felt it would be safer to wait until he obtained the fire qi of the Vermillion Bird, so he had delayed it until now.

This also caused Bai Di to wait for him until now, which made him feel quite embarrassed.

At this time, a person suddenly walked in from outside.

Looking closely, it turned out to be Shen Shuibi. Waving her hand, she said to Li Qi, "Li Qi! Are you done? Are we setting off then?"

"All done, let's go!" Li Qi responded, then turned his head to Bai Di and said, "Brother Bai, let's head out."

Bai Di cupped his hands. "I will follow the Young Master's word."

_Damn it, it's impossible to talk with this guy!_

Helpless, Li Qi could only take the lead. He walked out of the courtyard and headed towards the nearby residence of the governor of White Snake Prefecture.

"Thank you for your trouble, Governor," Li Qi bowed.

"It is no trouble at all," the governor returned the bow. "My family is entirely basking in the Young Master's reflected glory to obtain a spot. Waiting a bit longer is only proper."

Having said that, he brought out a jade talisman and infused it with a streak of divine qi.

The jade talisman cracked open. Swaying in the wind, it transformed into a portal, opening a door of light from within.

Seeing this, Li Qi knew that this was the door that crossed into the other world.

He quickly stepped inside, followed closely by Shen Shuibi and Bai Di.

The tempering in the extra-territorial realm had begun.

Watching their retreating backs, the smile on the governor's face receded.

The steward behind him stepped forward. "My lord, is this really for the best?"

"What is there to question? It's merely mutual benefit. Besides, Bai Di is the only promising one among the younger generation. What's wrong with having him go out to temper himself?" the governor said as he looked at the steward.

"My lord should know that's not what I meant," the steward said. "I feel that Young Master Bai Di is also exceptionally outstanding. He is only lacking a trace of innate dao rhythm compared to that young master from Shaman God Mountain. He could have snatched a spot on his own; there was no need to attach him to the young master. Doing it this way makes us seem petty. I fear he will be looked down upon by others, and it will also deal a blow to his state of mind."

"Under normal circumstances, that would be the case, but this time is different." The governor shook his head and said, "This time... there is more than just one young master from Shaman God Mountain coming to snatch a spot."

"Bai Di is the hope of our Bai family's new generation. I don't want him to perish prematurely from confronting the young masters of Shaman God Mountain. It's better to let the descendants of those big shots fight it out amongst themselves like dragons and tigers. I only hope for him to be a dog that guards the house; I don't want him directly encountering fierce tigers descending the mountain or flood dragons emerging from the sea," the governor lamented.

---

Meanwhile, on Li Qi's side.

He felt a bout of weightlessness.

Afterwards, he landed on the ground, his feet planting firmly on solid earth.

Looking up, he saw... _Uh, what the fuck is this?_

Li Qi clearly saw a massive metropolis.

There were cars, highways, power lines, and towering skyscrapers.

_The art style here seems a bit off, doesn't it?_

Li Qi's face was completely filled with shock.

Shen Shuibi, however, upon seeing all this, spoke with an air of worldly experience: "Ah, it's this kind of resource world. Then it mostly doesn't have much of a future. This world is probably close to being hollowed out, isn't it?"

"What... what are these objects? Has the Reason Dao developed in this world?" Li Qi asked, looking astonished.

Ever the knowledgeable one, Shen Shuibi said to the two men, "Ah, no. It's because the path of cultivation in this world isn't developed enough, so they have no choice but to rely on external objects. Moreover, these external objects aren't without their merits. At the very least, they are exceptionally excellent at extracting resources. Because of this, many worlds have developed these things."

"Many of the resource worlds governed by our All Under Heaven need to rely on these rapidly reproducible external objects for mining. This way, there's no need to mobilize high-level cultivators, and resources can be extracted at a very fast pace."

Shen Shuibi explained.

Li Qi frowned. "Then, Miss Shen, why doesn't our All Under Heaven use this kind of technology? If they did, wouldn't they have long since developed past that primitive agrarian stage? So many resources are buried underground..."

Shen Shuibi rolled her eyes at Li Qi and asked in return, "Do you really think that extracting resources faster is a good thing, Li Qi?"

"What we call cultivation resources are the foundation of a world, its fundamental essence. Within a certain limit, humans can take them, and demons can take them. But if they are extracted endlessly, it will only result in the world becoming hollowed out. Eventually, the dao rhythm will dissipate, and it will be reduced to a dead land where only mortals can survive, incapable of supporting the existence of cultivators."

"I mentioned before that the grade of a world is calculated based on the highest level of cultivator it can support. If a world cannot even support cultivators, then it is unranked. Only mortals can eke out a meager existence there, and it basically has no future to speak of."

"Do you think the True Immortals, Shaman Gods, and Great Sages of the demon race don't know how to rapidly explode with a multitude of combat forces? But doing so comes at the cost of consuming the world's fundamental essence. It's better to maintain the status quo, allowing All Under Heaven to slowly accumulate power and achieve further development."

Having said this, Shen Shuibi looked at Li Qi and said, "I once heard Her Ladyship say something."

"What is it?" Li Qi asked.

Shen Shuibi repeated Lady Luofu's words to Li Qi: "The reason All Under Heaven is the way it is, is because the True Immortals want it to be this way."

_The reason All Under Heaven is the way it is, is because the True Immortals want it to be this way._

The True Immortals, Shaman Gods, Great Sages of the demon race, and Sages of the Human Dao didn't lack knowledge of so-called 'advanced' societies. Yet, they still chose to let the world remain as it was.

What kind of staggering audacity was this?

However... through these words, Li Qi suddenly realized something.

The conflict between Shaman God Mountain and the Tang Empire.

Could it be that... the Shaman Gods felt All Under Heaven should be one way, while the Sages felt All Under Heaven should be another way?

And that was why the two sides had gone to war?

_Things are bad. This is much more terrifying than some resource conflict theory!_

A conflict of philosophy and ideology, when boiled down, wasn't that a 'Struggle of the Great Dao'? That meant a battle to the bitter end.

"Miss Shen, Young Master, let's continue this conversation on the road. We should head to our arranged location first and get some rest," Bai Di said from the side at this moment.

Li Qi nodded and said to this noble young master, "We are unfamiliar with this place. But since it is a resource world belonging to the Great Deer Kingdom, Brother Bai must surely know some things. You might as well tell us about it on the way."

"How could I dare disobey?" Bai Di immediately bowed.

Then, the three of them set off, walking towards their designated location.

Old Ma hadn't come; he had gone off to put on some fat.

He had gained over a hundred pounds in the past few days, which was terrifying. It was the first time Li Qi had ever seen a horse with a double chin.

Along the way, Bai Di began to introduce this world.

Back in 'All Under Heaven,' resources were regarded as the foundation of the world. With entities like Shaman Gods and True Immortals imposing restrictions, arbitrary mining was forbidden, to the point that the development of lower-level civilizations was restricted for this very reason.

Therefore, if one wanted massive amounts of cultivation resources, they could only develop the extra-territorial realms.

This particular realm contained several worlds and was an extra-territorial realm developed by the Great Deer Kingdom. The ruler of the Great Deer Kingdom had named it the Realm of Hesitation, and within it, there was a world called the Earth Oil World.

In its entirety, it was spherical, roughly the size of a single prefecture in the Great Deer Kingdom.

Which meant... it was about the same size as Earth.

Moreover, it wasn't a flat earth beneath a round sky; it should be a stellar system?

It also possessed a Moon and a Sun, each orbiting on their own.

Forty percent of it was land, and sixty percent was ocean. There was only one continent in total, along with some archipelagos isolated across the sea.

This was a resource world because it was rich in 'earth oil.'

This wasn't petroleum, but something else entirely. It was a physical substance condensed from earth qi and water qi. Resembling droplets of oil, it flowed within the ley lines, almost like the blood of the earth itself.

Earth oil could carry massive amounts of earth qi, and with water qi acting as an insulating container, it wouldn't easily dissipate and was easy to store. Furthermore, whether used for artifact forging, alchemy, or direct consumption, it was highly beneficial to cultivators. Because of this, it was one of the Great Deer Kingdom's most vital resource lands.

Therefore, the Great Deer Kingdom had developed 'industrial corporations' here. Using wicked methods from the Human Dao to control the populace, they had dismantled the original nations and transformed them into massive industrial corporations. They mobilized all the natives of the entire world to extract earth oil, turning it into the economic lifeline of the entire world.
