# Chapter 121: Child of Destiny

The Heavenly Dao of this world was wailing.

The four wisps of the Grand Invocator's divine qi within his body were clearly telling Li Qi this fact.

It wasn't that they were actually transmitting a message, but rather something he deduced through observation.

The sun, moon, stars, and constellations represent the heavenly timing. Therefore, the movement of the sun, moon, stars, and constellations in the heavenly cycle must not deviate from their designated paths. They must not lose their guiding principles, and they must maintain their original state as the norm.

What did this mean? It meant that the movement of the sun, moon, stars, and constellations constituted the heavenly timing. Whether it was the position of the sun, the path of the moon, the locations of the stars, or their corresponding points of intersection, they all had to be absolutely precise. They could not deviate from their original measures. How they were at the very beginning was how they should be at the very end.

A correct heavenly timing should look exactly like this.

The 'constellations' among the sun, moon, stars, and constellations referred to the points where the sun, moon, and stars intersected at different times. It was precisely because of this characteristic that the concept of 'shichen'—two-hour time periods—existed. This was because every single shichen marked a time when the astronomical phenomena changed.

There was once a saying: _'At that time, Xihe subverted his virtue, sinking into the chaos of wine. He abandoned his post and neglected his duties, disturbing the heavenly disciplines and forsaking his office. Thus, on the first day of the last month of autumn, the celestial bodies did not gather in the Fang constellation. The blind musicians beat the drums, the petty officials galloped, and the commoners ran. Yet Xihe, occupying his office like a corpse, heard and knew nothing, completely stupefied regarding the astronomical phenomena.'_

What did this mean? It meant that there was a deity named Xihe in charge of the movement of the sun, moon, stars, and constellations. He neglected his work, habitually drowning himself in wine and abandoning his post. He threw the movement of the celestial bodies into chaos and gave up on his designated duties.

Therefore, on the first day of the last month of autumn, the sun and moon failed to meet at their proper intersection point in the Fang constellation, resulting in a solar eclipse[^1]. This caused the musicians to beat their drums and the petty officials to hastily gallop forth with offerings to revere the gods. The masses ran around in a panic to perform their labor duties, but Xihe knew nothing of this. He was ignorant of the astronomical phenomena, plunging All Under Heaven into great chaos.

A disruption in the movement of the sun, moon, stars, and constellations would cause such a situation.

Later on, of course, he was executed.

However, one could learn from this incident that if the astronomical phenomena fell into disorder, the entire world would inevitably experience turmoil as a result.

Moreover, Li Qi knew one more thing.

That was, regardless of these outer-realm worlds, no matter which world it was or how the specific rules differed, one thing was absolutely the same.

That was the fixed and unchanging nature of the astronomical phenomena.

No matter the world, there were the Four Phenomena[^2] of heaven. There were always the familiar stars like Taibai[^3], Yinghuo[^4], Suixing[^5], and Zhenxing[^6]. They all had the Eight Winds[^7], the Twenty-Eight Mansions[^8], the Five Officials[^9], the Six Palaces[^10], Purple Palace[^11], Supreme Tenuity[^12], Xuanyuan[^13], Xianchi[^14], the Four Guards[^15], and Tian'e[^16].

When a world's astronomical phenomena became unstable, the professional instincts of an Invocator—a path dedicated to communicating with the deities of heaven and earth—would definitely tell them that this world was undergoing a cataclysm.

Standing in the center of the group, Li Qi spoke slowly, "Therefore, I feel that the bitter suffering of this world is currently giving birth to a group of rebels. And they are the final resistance of this world's Heavenly Dao."

"I'm wager your companions must all possess firm convictions and exceptional talent, or perhaps they frequently encounter fortuitous events? Your luck should be quite good across the board, right? Well, except for when you ran into us."

"Because, to you, we are people of the Outer Dao. It is difficult for the Heavenly Dao of this world to interfere with us. Hmm, thinking about it this way... Miss Shen, I'm guessing that when a world's Heavenly Dao drops in rank, the dao rhythm we observe will become even more intense, clear, and obvious, right?"

Shen Shuibi tilted her head, a bit confused, but she honestly answered Li Qi's question. "Well, yes, it would, but Li Qi, are you the kind of person who would do such a thing?"

Bai Di also cupped his hands and said, "Young Master... Are we going to catalyze this world's drop in rank? I'm afraid that wouldn't be appropriate. According to the texts, when a world drops in rank, there should be natural disasters. I fear it would result in countless deaths and injuries..."

Bai Di was also a kind-hearted person, so he began to persuade Li Qi at this moment.

"Who said I wanted to make this world drop in rank?" Li Qi looked at everyone strangely. Did he look like some mass-murdering grand villain?

"But Young Master, just now...?" Bai Di was perplexed. Li Qi's words clearly meant he was preparing to let this world drop in rank to make it easier for him to observe the dao rhythm.

"The person I was talking about wasn't me," Li Qi said, holding up a finger.

"Ah! I understand! Li Qi, you're saying that someone else is currently accelerating this world's rank-dropping process, and their actions have provoked the resistance of this world's Heavenly Dao. Therefore, the Heavenly Dao gave birth to a batch of 'children of destiny' to come and stop us people of the Outer Dao!" Shen Shuibi still had the best rapport with Li Qi. She reacted immediately, understanding his meaning!

That was exactly what Li Qi meant.

There was another group of people secretly stirring up trouble, preparing to ruin this world and force it to drop in rank. Being able to observe the intense reaction of the Heavenly Dao during this process would be the most profitable outcome for them.

"This couldn't possibly be the work of the Great Deer Kingdom's officials themselves. To them, keeping this world in a half-dead Rank 8 state is the best option. I imagine that's exactly what they've been doing. Brother Zhang, have the Exalted Immortals been making you pay a lot of attention to environmental protection recently?" Li Qi looked toward Zhang Deming.

"Y-Young Master's calculations are divine. The Exalted Immortals have indeed asked us to slow down our mining recently," Zhang Deming immediately bowed his head and bent his waist, speaking respectfully with a trembling voice.

"So, let's deduce this. Who would dare to completely disregard the Great Deer Kingdom's deterrence and single-mindedly push a world to its death for their own benefit? And completely disregard trivial matters like how many people will die or whether they'll offend the Great Deer Kingdom?" Li Qi asked the people around him.

The native inhabitants belonging to this world's industrial companies naturally didn't dare utter a single word.

As for the two rebel terrorists, they exchanged continuous glances. It was unknown if they had telepathy, but they could actually convey their thoughts to each other just through their eyes.

However, Bai Di and Shen Shuibi both began to ponder just like Li Qi.

"It's quite simple, right? Obviously, there's only one kind of person who would dare do such a thing."

"First, they must be extravagant enough, capable of mobilizing the resources required to cause a world's Heavenly Dao to drop in rank. Second, they must be confident that after doing such a thing, the Great Deer Kingdom will suffer the silent loss and not dare to make a sound, having no choice but to accept defeat. Third, they must have enough ability and knowledge to be aware of all this from the very beginning, and completely disregard the casualties of the commoners." Li Qi listed three conditions.

"Then... wouldn't that be you, Young Master?" Bai Di said after hearing the three conditions.

Li Qi's expression darkened. "I already said it, do I look like the kind of person who would do something like this?"

However, he immediately revealed the answer he had deduced, "I'm guessing it's someone with a status similar to mine, but they aren't nearly as kind-hearted as I am."

"I was only planning to observe the dao rhythm and then leave. But he is planning to dissect the Heavenly Dao of the Earth Oil World and conduct a live experiment to learn," Li Qi sighed.

---

Almost at the exact same time.

In another hidden location within the Earth Oil World.

The sun in the sky shone blindingly bright. Many laborers busily rushed back and forth. The intense, frantic atmosphere and the sweat dripping from their foreheads almost created the illusion that it was incredibly sweltering here.

However, the moment the cold wind blew, the freezing weather made people's skin sting. The overly frigid air even caused a hallucinatory ringing in their ears, as if the wind carried a whimpering sound.

Atop this snowy mountain, neither the heaters nor the sun could easily warm anyone up.

Only continuous physical labor could maintain their body temperature.

Seeing the time shift from early morning to noon, the sunlight outside grew brighter and brighter. The snow continuously reflected a piercing white light, making it impossible to keep one's eyes open.

Seeing that the time had come, the diligent laborers all began to rest, seizing the moment to eat a meal.

They had to work continuously until late into the night before they could rest. Basically, apart from major holidays, they worked year-round without a break. This was their life. Having one full meal followed by going hungry was a common occurrence; after all, they had no spare cash.

However, these matters were of no importance, and no one would pay attention to these commoners. They weren't like the people in the slums whose shocking plight would attract the help of various philanthropists, nor did they have the luxurious lives of the rich. They were the largest demographic in the Earth Oil World, and also the most neglected.

And they were the main workforce mining the earth oil.

It was just that, as always, nobody cared about them.

Right now, the attention of almost every important figure in the entire base was focused on a small room not far away.

Inside this small room was an altar that had only recently been constructed.

Two attendants looked up with reverence at the young figure above them.

"Young Master, is this... the art of shifting blame?" one of the attendants asked in astonishment, seemingly having just witnessed something inconceivable.

The young figure walked down from the altar, washed his hands clean, and nodded. "This is the art of shifting blame. I have just redirected the fury of this realm's Heavenly Dao onto my newly arrived fellow sect member."

He looked to be only in his twenties, around the same age as Li Qi and the others. His appearance was ordinary with deep-set eyes, and his attire was plain, lacking excessive adornment.

Seeing him walk down, the other attendant below immediately began to flatter him: "As expected, the Diviner lineage is superior to the Invocator lineage! In my opinion—"

However, before he could finish, his shadow suddenly opened a massive maw and swallowed the man whole!

He let out a shrill, agonizing howl, but it was completely useless. Very quickly, the entire person was devoured, skin and bones and all, leaving absolutely nothing behind.

The shadow itself quietly slithered away, blending into the large patches of shade in the room.

The young man addressed as 'Young Master' glanced over and said in displeasure, "The superiority or inferiority within the Shaman Dao is for us of the Shaman God Mountain to contest with our own strength. What status do you have to dare speak out of turn?"

The others fell as silent as winter cicadas, hastily kneeling on the ground, not daring to utter another word.

But at this moment, the door to the secret room was pushed open.

A middle-aged man walked in, smiling as he said, "Alright, why must the Young Master be so angry?"

Having said that, his face hardened, and he spoke in a deep voice to the people kneeling around them: "All of you, get out."

The kneeling crowd immediately got up and retreated backwards, exiting the room in an orderly fashion, terrified of angering this 'Young Master' again.

The Young Master, meanwhile, sat down at a table to the side.

"Come, sit. Did you bring the item I asked for?" He looked at the middle-aged man who had just entered.

"The natural permafrost ice crystal you requested is ready." The middle-aged man walked over and produced a pure white gemstone.

Natural permafrost ice crystal was a luxury item in the Earth Oil World. Naturally frigid, it resembled a block of ice, but it was actually a type of gemstone. Therefore, unlike ice, it wouldn't melt in warm places.

This type of gemstone had an extremely low yield. Even for the immensely wealthy or grand nobles, possessing a piece merely the size of a thumb was a collection worthy of showing off. However, despite being miraculous, apart from looking beautiful and having an innate chilling effect, it had no other uses. It was often only used to chill drinks and show off, just like right now.

The value of the single piece he held in his hand already exceeded the combined annual wages of all the laborers outside.

"In my hometown, we call this thing Cold Jade. It contains profound water spiritual qi and has a calming and soothing effect." The Young Master poured himself a cup of wine, raised his cup, downed it, and then poured himself another.

It seemed he quite enjoyed the wine here.

The middle-aged man didn't reply because he knew the Young Master wasn't actually speaking to him, but rather talking to himself.

Replying at this moment would not only make the Young Master uncomfortable but also bring punishment upon himself.

After a while, the Young Master suddenly spoke. "Another Young Master from the Shaman God Mountain has arrived. I initiated a Grand Rite earlier, transferring the Heavenly Dao's fury onto him. Right now, he should be under attack by the Heavenly Dao's resistance."

"If we go gather intel now, we will be able to clearly find traces of his whereabouts."

The middle-aged man looked up in surprise, asking in disbelief, "Go gather intel? But are there still things in All Under Heaven that you do not know?"

"Do not use the term 'All Under Heaven' so carelessly. Is this minor world worthy of being called 'All Under Heaven'?" the Young Master frowned and reprimanded him.

"I misspoke." The middle-aged man immediately slapped himself across the face. His cheek swelled up instantly, a bright red handprint stark against his skin. It was obvious the strike contained great force, as blood even seeped from the corner of his mouth.

"To mortal fools like you, I naturally know everything. But the other party shares the same status as I do. My master is a Grand Diviner. He saw a sliver of innate dao rhythm within me, which is why he took me in as a disciple. To you, my divination arts can naturally perceive exactly what you are doing with perfect clarity."

"But this person must also possess innate dao rhythm to be highly regarded by a Grand Invocator. It's only natural that he can evade my divination arts." The Young Master shook his head.

"However, if someone thinks I am a useless person without my divination arts, they are underestimating me. Do as I said. Even if he can obscure divination, could he possibly avoid leaving behind a single trace? Once I find his trail, I can still toy with him to death from thousands of miles away."

"A Diviner is inferior to an Invocator in a head-on battle, even more so at Rank 9. But I cannot allow my master to lose face in the dispute between Diviners and Invocators." It was unclear whether the Young Master was talking to himself or the man.

But after he finished saying all this, he waved his hand. "Go, get to work. See how the children of destiny nurtured by this realm's Heavenly Dao managed to find them, and then bring the intelligence to me."

[^1]: Failed to meet... resulting in a solar eclipse: This passage quotes the Book of Documents (Shangshu), recording one of the earliest known solar eclipses in Chinese history. In ancient Chinese cosmology, the sun and moon were supposed to cross paths harmoniously during a new moon. An eclipse was perceived not as a physical overlap, but as a failure of the celestial bodies to gather peacefully, resulting in a cosmic clash. Thus, "failing to meet (harmoniously)" became the traditional phrasing to describe a solar eclipse.

[^2]: **Four Phenomena (四象 - Sìxiàng):** In traditional Chinese astronomy, this refers to the four mythological creatures guarding the cardinal directions of the night sky: the Azure Dragon of the East, the White Tiger of the West, the Vermilion Bird of the South, and the Black Tortoise of the North. Note: In the context of this world's broader cosmology, these directional guardians are a constant astronomical fixture, distinct from Li Qi's personal "four qi" (the Sun, Moon, Stars, and Constellations).

[^3]: **Taibai (太白):** The ancient Chinese name for the planet Venus. It literally translates to "Great White."

[^4]: **Yinghuo (荧惑):** The ancient Chinese name for the planet Mars. Meaning "Sparkling Deception," its erratic red glow was historically viewed as an omen of war, disaster, or the death of emperors.

[^5]: **Suixing (岁星):** The planet Jupiter. Known as the "Year Star," ancient Chinese astronomers used its roughly 12-year orbit to establish the 12 earthly branches of the zodiac.

[^6]: **Zhenxing (镇星):** The planet Saturn. Known as the "Quelling Star," it is associated with the Earth element and the center direction in the Five Elements (Wu Xing) theory.

[^7]: **Eight Winds (八风 - Bāfēng):** The winds blowing from the eight compass directions (the four cardinal and four ordinal points). In ancient times, they were closely observed for meteorological divination, agriculture, and predicting the fate of the empire.

[^8]: **Twenty-Eight Mansions (二十八宿 - Èrshíbā Xiù):** The Chinese equivalent of the zodiac. It is a system of 28 constellations spaced out along the moon's orbit, divided into four groups of seven that correspond to the Four Phenomena.

[^9]: **Five Officials (五官 - Wǔguān):** In the context of celestial mapping, these refer to specific cosmic ministries within the heavenly court, mirroring the bureaucratic structure of the mortal imperial government.

[^10]: **Six Palaces (六府 - Liùfǔ):** Celestial storehouses within the heavenly court, serving as the cosmic equivalent to the mortal imperial government's storage and bureaucratic structures.

[^11]: **Purple Palace (紫宫 - Zǐgōng):** The center of the cosmos and the residence of the Celestial Emperor (represented by the North Star). It is the central part of the Purple Forbidden Enclosure, which inspired the name of the mortal Forbidden City.

[^12]: **Supreme Tenuity (太微 - Tàiwēi):** Also known as the Supreme Palace Enclosure, it is one of the three major macro-constellations in Chinese astronomy. It represents the celestial government and the imperial court where ministers advise the Heavenly Emperor.

[^13]: **Xuanyuan (轩辕):** A major constellation named after the legendary Yellow Emperor (Xuanyuan Huangdi), widely considered the mythological ancestor of the Chinese people.

[^14]: **Xianchi (咸池):** The "Pool of Harmony." In Chinese mythology, this is the legendary pool where the sun bathes after setting in the west; it is also represented as a constellation.

[^15]: **Four Guards (四守 - Sìshǒu):** Minor constellations acting as celestial checkpoints, representing the imperial bodyguards of the heavens.

[^16]: **Tian'e (天阿):** Literally "Heavenly Ridge" or "Heavenly Boundary." This constellation acts as a cosmic border or geographic feature in the star map.
