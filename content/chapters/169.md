# Chapter 169: Mortals

Fang Cuiyuan huddled on her uncomfortable pallet. The wind howled around her, carrying a biting chill that forced her to constantly curl her body tighter.

The little girl tossed and turned, unable to fall asleep no matter how hard she tried.

If it were the past, her mother would have draped a quilt over her, scolding her for not covering up properly.

Unfortunately, she no longer had a mother.

Truly too frozen to sleep, Fang Cuiyuan had no choice but to sit up.

Her father was a hunter. Fang Cuiyuan remembered him saying when he was drunk that in the mountains, when it was so cold at night that you couldn't sleep, you must absolutely not fall asleep. Otherwise, at best, you would fall gravely ill; at worst, you would never wake up again.

A companion who used to hunt with them had died for exactly this reason.

However, tonight was a bit strange.

First, those soldiers had come to herd them, making them shift their formation so that everyone formed a massive circle.

One hundred thousand people arranged in a ring was a spectacular sight, but she had absolutely no idea what purpose it served.

Still, she constantly felt that staying within this ring was much more comfortable. Her health had never been good, and she was terrified of the cold. Her mother used to say that she caught a chill right after being born, resulting in a weak constitution and deficient Yang fire. The moment she got cold, she would cough.

But inside this ring, even though it was cold, she miraculously didn't cough. It was truly wondrous.

Sitting on the ground, she wrapped her body in her only piece of thick cloth and gazed into the short distance.

Soldiers were standing guard over there. Just earlier that day, these very soldiers had risked their lives to protect them. Hiding in the back, she had watched with her own eyes as a young soldier who had greeted her that morning was torn to pieces by a fiend.

If it weren't for them, she and the others truly wouldn't have known what to do.

Fiends...

During this fiend attack, out of an entire village of six to seven hundred people, only a scant few dozen had survived.

Her entire family was gone. Her parents, her younger brother, her older brother—she was the only one left alive.

At this thought, she lowered her head.

Yet, the tears simply refused to flow.

In the very beginning, she had washed her face with tears, weeping as if she were going to go blind.

But now, she couldn't cry anymore.

It seemed heartache and sorrow also had their limits.

Back then, the agony had been so soul-tearing that she thought it would be etched into her bones forever. Yet, it had only been a few days, and she already felt almost nothing.

Perhaps this was what it meant to be heartless.

Thinking about it now, it seemed like just another occurrence.

Aside from sudden pangs of sorrow while spacing out, it felt like nothing much.

It was just like—

Right now.

In the past, when she sat outside admiring the moon wrapped in a bedsheet, her older brother would walk over and scold her, telling her to return to her room because her health had always been poor.

The moment this thought arose.

Her heart clenched.

It felt as if even breathing had become difficult.

Yet, she couldn't cry.

She couldn't cry.

Sorrow and anguish knotted in her chest, but tears simply would not fall.

It was just like what happened at her second uncle's house next door in the past.

She had seen it before.

Her second uncle's son, her younger cousin, was playing in the courtyard, climbing a tree to pick jujubes. He accidentally slipped from the tree and fell into the water vat.

The vat was too deep and its edges too slippery. Unable to climb out, he drowned.

Her second aunt wept until the sky turned dark and the earth spun, but her second uncle merely kept a dark expression and stayed silent. He only repeatedly told her second aunt that at worst, they would just have another one; at worst, they would just have another one.

At the time, she felt that her second uncle had a remarkably thick skin to actually feel absolutely nothing.

However, recalling it now.

It wasn't actually that he felt nothing.

He just couldn't cry.

She suddenly remembered that back then, as usual, she had secretly sneaked out wrapped in her bedsheet. She saw her second uncle holding a rock at night, circling the water vat over and over.

He had raised the rock, attempting to smash it again and again, but ultimately gave up. He threw the rock to the ground, then squatted beside the vat, puffing on his pipe.

After all, buying a new vat wasn't cheap.

As her mind wandered with random thoughts, an old farmer walked over and squatted beside Fang Cuiyuan. "Cuiyuan, why haven't you slept yet?"

The old man's skin was coarse, and the joints of his limbs were exceptionally thick. One glance revealed he had done his fair share of heavy labor; calluses were layered thickly upon his hands.

"Ah, Second Uncle, I can't seem to fall asleep." Fang Cuiyuan finally looked up, realizing her second uncle had come to her side.

It was this very second uncle.

This time, just like her, he was the only survivor of his entire family.

Her two newborn cousins and her second aunt had all been eaten by the fiends this time.

"It's fine to cry, but don't let it truly break your heart," the old man said, his voice a soft rasp. "No matter what, life has to go on. Our Fang family only has the two of us left now. You are my niece, don't be afraid. So what if eldest brother died? Isn't your second uncle here? As long as I am around, you won't go hungry." The old man smiled, chattering away to Fang Cuiyuan.

"Mhm... second uncle, where are we running to? Did those soldiers say?" Fang Cuiyuan asked.

"I went and asked. They said they're escorting us to White Peak Mountain. They said it's sparsely populated with plenty of wasteland, enough for us to farm. When the time comes, I'll build another house. A bit of exhaustion is fine; I'll open up fifty or a hundred acres of fields. Then, I'll find you a good family to marry into and have a few babies. You can adopt one out to me as a grandson so he can carry the Fang surname and continue the incense lineage. Then we can consider ourselves to have truly set down roots." The old man smacked his lips as he spoke to Fang Cuiyuan.

"A person separated from their homeland is cheap... No matter how sparsely populated it is, there are still other people there. Us just moving over like this—will it really be that smooth?" Fang Cuiyuan asked with concern.

The future her second uncle described sounded too stable, too perfect, which naturally filled her with apprehension.

"There's over a hundred thousand of us. If they don't let us settle, does that mean we just won't settle? We still have to live our days, we still have to eat. There will always be a way," the old man said nonchalantly, wearing a completely unconcerned expression.

"Then how long will it take to reach White Peak Mountain?"

"I don't know, but those soldiers said that until we arrive, they'll provide the food. We don't have to worry about going hungry, so as long as we don't get sick on the road, there's nothing else to fear," the old man replied.

At this point, he seemed to suddenly remember why he came here. He hurriedly stood up and pulled out a bundled blanket. "Right, I was just worried you'd get sick. Your constitution is weak, so I brought you a blanket."

"The fiends came so fast at the time, time was tight, and there wasn't much chance to grab anything. I just snatched up some copper coins and this blanket. Your aunt bought it, it's fur. Damn it all, it cost me over a hundred coins—the half-year harvest of over twenty acres of land, gone just like that."

"She had no fortune to enjoy good things. She bought it, and it was gone before she could even use it for two days. Take it. Let the two of us just take care of our own lives. Only the two of us are left of the Fang family. I'm old, so the ancestors' incense lineage depends on you. It can't be delayed." The old man stuffed the fur blanket into Fang Cuiyuan's hands without taking no for an answer.

Fang Cuiyuan wanted to decline, but her second uncle simply shoved it at her, then walked away without looking back, leaving her to wonder how he would manage tonight.

At this moment, the moon had risen above the treetops; it was the exact middle of the night.

Shen Shuibi dangled the silver-furred rat, pondering for a long time but unable to figure it out.

So she simply gave up thinking about it and also went to sleep.

The night passed without further incident.

However, out of the more than a hundred thousand people, including the soldiers, many could not sleep.

On this journey, there was no telling how many would die along the way.

By the next day, Li Qi and Zhang Lan had awakened. Li Qi's energy had been somewhat replenished. Although he hadn't fully recovered, his personal cosmos was capable of spontaneously generating some qi, so he no longer felt so empty.

As for Zhang Lan, it was unknown. Li Qi's understanding of the Martial Dao was not deep. Since he didn't comprehend Zhang Lan's foundation, he naturally didn't know what he relied upon to recover.

The lieutenant general temporarily appointed by the White Rhino Army also sought out Li Qi bright and early.

Carrying a steaming basket, he stepped forward to offer greetings. "Mister Li, Mister Zhang, did you two rest well? And this..."

"Shen. Her surname is Shen," Li Qi said.

"Miss Shen," the lieutenant general said respectfully. He then opened the basket, revealing dried meat, rice, and leafy green vegetables boiled in plain water, which appeared freshly picked.

It seemed this was the best food they could prepare under the current conditions.

"Please make do with this for breakfast. We have no female cooks in the army; it was thrown together by the mess cooks. It's just coarse tea and plain rice, please forgive the inadequacy." He looked somewhat embarrassed.

Zhang Lan, however, was carefree. "No matter. What haven't I eaten while braving the wind and sleeping in the dew? There's even meat here; it's more than enough."

Li Qi nodded. "Food and drink are unimportant; the lives of over a hundred thousand people take precedence. General, do you have any arrangements?"

The three men didn't stand on ceremony, sitting cross-legged on the ground. Only Shen Shuibi politely declined the invitation to eat, instead running to the side to write and draw on the ground, seemingly calculating something.

"Don't mind her. Her cultivation method possesses its own marvels, negating the need for food and drink. Moreover, her personality is somewhat aloof, and she dislikes worldly affairs, so there's no need to bother her. We can just talk among ourselves," Li Qi said with a smile. Seeing the other two hadn't picked up their chopsticks, he took the initiative to grab his utensils, picked up a bite, and placed it in his mouth.

Since Li Qi had done so, the other two relaxed and began to eat.

The food couldn't be considered good. Ordinary dried meat chopped and boiled, wild vegetables boiled in plain water, and cooked millet—almost everything was boiled, then seasoned with a bit of salt.

"Misters, the most pressing matter we must resolve is the military rations. The White Rhino Army originally numbered twelve thousand five hundred men, and now stands at eight thousand three hundred and forty-one. According to past standards, a single man is allotted nearly a kilo and a half of rice per day, meaning the army consumes roughly seventeen tonnes daily. Previously, military rations were distributed monthly. At this moment, we have over three hundred tonnes of rations remaining."

"These commoners aren't like us big eaters; half a kilo a day is sufficient for them. However, we still can't last for many days. Even if we control it well, it can only support us for so long. From here to White Peak Mountain, the area is completely desolate, with only the single city of Vast Yang in between. If we drive the populace there to resupply, it will take about ten days, and a portion will undoubtedly starve to death along the way. This is the most critical issue at hand. Do you misters have a solution?"

"Where are your military rations stored?" Li Qi asked.

"Within mustard seed pouches. The army possesses several specially made mustard seed pouches," the lieutenant general replied.

"I have a dragon colt. If it is Vast Yang City, I can reach it in five hours. I also have a mustard seed pouch of my own. Give your mustard seed pouches to me, along with a credential, and I can rush to Vast Yang City to request rations," Li Qi said.

Zhang Lan immediately clapped his hands. "This is excellent! I was worried that if I went to request rations, there would be no one to guard the camp in case of another fiend attack, which would be a massive problem. Little Brother Li has a dragon colt, so his speed isn't slower than ours. His departure won't affect the overall combat strength either; with two Rank 8s remaining, we can still provide comprehensive protection."

Li Qi listened, his face darkening with black lines.

This big brother was a bit too honest, wasn't he? Did he know how to speak? What did he mean by 'his departure won't affect the overall combat strength'? He was still the third strongest here, for crying out loud!

Second only to the two Rank 8s, the other Rank 9 military officers essentially couldn't last a single exchange against him.

However, his words might be coarse, but the logic was sound. No matter how strong he was, he was still a Rank 9, and his effectiveness on a large-scale battlefield was limited.

An army was not a disorganized mob.

If it were just ten thousand-plus unranked brutes, sparsely mixed with a few Rank 9s, Li Qi might get injured from exhaustion, or perhaps even falter if he were careless, but at the very least, he could slaughter them until rivers of blood flowed.

But if it were an army of over ten thousand led by a dozen Rank 9 military officers, the situation would be completely different.

If Li Qi were to clash head-on with the White Rhino Army right now, all his methods would find it extremely difficult to shake their military qi. He would even struggle to flee and would simply be swarmed and hacked to death.

This was the difference between a highly trained iron-blooded army and a disorganized mob.

Such an army, possessing a commander and a military banner, could rely on the might of its military qi to violently clash with a Rank 8.

During the previous battle, that eagle-headed fiend had marshaled the fiend military qi from below to break Zhang Lan's divine ability, which was enough to showcase its power.

It was indeed more suitable for Li Qi to ride the dragon colt to transport rations.

"_Ahem_, then leave the matter of transporting the rations to me. General, could you provide me with a credential?" He coughed lightly to suppress his awkwardness, then spoke to the lieutenant general.

The lieutenant general understood. He handed a rolled-up bolt of cloth to Li Qi. "This is one of our army's banners. It cannot be lightly entrusted to others, as it represents the face of the White Rhino Army. Take it, Sir. The Minister of War of Vast Yang City will naturally recognize that you are not an imposter."

"With the matter of military rations resolved, the second matter is: which route shall we take to White Peak Mountain?" The lieutenant general brought out a military topographical map and unfurled it before the group.

The officers did not make these decisions privately; instead, they deliberated with both Li Qi and Zhang Lan.

Over the course of the morning, the three finally ironed out all the major and minor details.

Zhang Lan remained in charge of martial defense, while military matters were left to the original officers. The task assigned to Li Qi was far simpler: to acquire the military rations.

As for Shen Shuibi, she was free to move about and do as she pleased; no one assigned her any tasks.

The affairs of the commoners were also delegated layer by layer. Managed by the army, they enlisted the help of village elders and other prestigious individuals among the populace to assist with governance.

In short, once everything was arranged, Li Qi prepared to depart.

Just as he pulled Old Ma along and prepared to set off, Shen Shuibi suddenly appeared by his side. She asked somewhat nervously, "Um... Li Qi, can't I go with you?"

Li Qi laughed, "I'm heading to a massive human city, what could possibly happen? Your cultivation is profound and your combat strength is high; it's better if you stay here to protect the people."

"Oh..." Though Shen Shuibi agreed, she was clearly a bit dejected.

"It's only a ten-hour round trip. Even if there's a delay, I'll be back by tomorrow at the latest. There's no need to worry," Li Qi reassured her.

Without further delay, it was best to depart early and return early. It was only ten hours, after all.

It wasn't as if he would leave and never come back.
