# Chapter 131: In the Distance

What Li Qi was referencing meant that an Invocator's supplications require sincerity. They reward the virtues of loyalty and filial piety, forming pacts of life and death with the gods, demanding united hearts and strength. Using invocations utterly devoid of falsehood, they address the divine with absolute honesty. They bind the gods with pure, open candor and offerings redolent of a noble and beautiful character. It is for this reason that the gods yield willingly and offer their power.

"Indeed, the power of Shaman God Mountain is an indispensable component. Whether demonstrating virtue or sincerity, it must be done on a foundation of equality. Otherwise, it only holds sway among gentlemen. To make those without virtue adhere to justice, power is required as a pillar of support," Li Qi added.

"However, I believe that while power is vital, it is not the true reason an Invocator can act as the nexus of communication between Heaven, Earth, humanity, gods, and ghosts," Li Qi said solemnly. "If we speak of raw power, the Martial Dao cultivates it exclusively. The Daoist Sects and the Human Dao are no weaker. I have heard there are many Dao paths across All Under Heaven that excel at combat, such as the War Dao and branches of the Reason Dao. Yet, why can they not become the ones who command the conviction of all creation?"

"Previously, Senior Brother used a spell art to breach a flaw in my Dao Heart, but it only spurred me to contemplate further. Since a Dao Heart is a strict necessity on the path of seeking the Dao, then what exactly is this 'Dao' we are chasing?"

"I pondered relentlessly. Though I could not divine the absolute correct answer, I arrived at two answers that, for me, can never be wrong. The first is righteousness, and the second is justice."

"Righteousness is the attitude of upholding fairness and integrity. Justice is the law and morality that suits the multitudes."

"Upon grasping this answer, my mind and body felt instantly transparent. Thus, I am preparing to put it into practice. I do not have the ability to stop the ruler of the Great Deer Kingdom, but I believe I must begin with what is within my power."

"I cannot stop the Great Deer Kingdom from continuing to excavate this world, but I can stop the conflict between the two of us, allowing more people to survive," Li Qi concluded.

The Diviner stared at Li Qi. The previous disdain in his eyes had morphed into intense gravity.

"Your words hold merit. Though I do not fully agree, it is evident you have found the path you must walk," the Diviner said.

Li Qi remained silent, waiting for the Diviner's next words.

Discarding his earlier contempt, the Diviner spoke with a solemn demeanor. "However, the Dao I perceive differs from yours. The Dao of the Diviner establishes the rites of divination to interrogate the spirits. Therefore, the virtue of the Invocator is round and spiritual, while the virtue of the Diviner is square and intellectual. You take righteousness and justice as your path, but I claim intellect and power as mine. Regarding our stance on this Rank 8 world, I find myself unable to concur with your methods."

"Moreover, you treat this world with righteousness and justice, but do you think this world will treat you the same way in return?" the Diviner challenged.

"Previously, I viewed you as an enemy, so I was unwilling to tell you. But after hearing your words, I am willing to view you as a rival. As such, I must inform you truthfully: among the results of my divinations, one pointed directly to your current location, Satellite City 4."

"A great omen of disaster lies there. It seems that Child of Destiny has already locked onto both of us, and you are the primary target," the Diviner declared.

He then explained, "In my original arrangement, using the devil seed to scramble your Dao Heart was a roundabout way to force you to linger in that spot. I had used the Dao of divination to lay down two absolute killing moves there in advance. The moment that Child of Destiny attacked you, I would trigger my trap and defeat you."

"Calculating the time, they should be arriving right about now. Once you witness firsthand the attitude this world's Child of Destiny holds toward you, will you still be able to persist in treating them with righteousness and justice?" the Diviner asked.

Li Qi felt his scalp go numb.

_Damn it, Diviners really are a bunch of scheming bastards._

They were useless in a straight fight, but they could always set up lethal killing moves ahead of time.

"Thank you for the warning. Since Senior Brother is unwilling to accept this Covenant, I shall withdraw the rite first to focus on the enemy," Li Qi said.

"Very well. I have my own methods to observe your location. You may withdraw on your own. I am curious to see if you can truly live by the path you have conceived for yourself." The Diviner sized Li Qi up as he spoke.

Although Li Qi's logic was highly structured and his words persuasive, if the principles he spouted could not be put into action, he was nothing more than an empty braggart.

For a Dao seeker, mere talk was never enough.

The connection between the two severed.

The 'Covenant' was a grand rite. If it failed to form, the Invocator would inevitably suffer a loss. After all, this set of rites relied on the Invocator's own credibility and power as a guarantee. Both parties forged the agreement under the watch of the Invocator and the Heavenly Dao, giving it far stronger binding power than an Oath. Consequently, if the Invocator failed to facilitate the Covenant, they would suffer a backlash.

After Li Qi cut the connection, a wave of tightness seized his chest.

However, the backlash wasn't incredibly severe, as the other party had politely declined rather than violently severing it.

"I lost some of my reserved qi... but it's fine. My personal cosmos is largely unaffected." Li Qi examined the damage and evaluated the outcome.

"That Diviner said the Child of Destiny would appear here and attempt to kill me," Li Qi muttered to himself. "He wouldn't go so far as to lie about this, which means that Child of Destiny is truly preparing to ambush me here."

"I better hurry back. It's safer with Shen Shuibi and Bai Di around. This world's Child of Destiny is definitely not someone to be trifled with."

Yet, while still in the wilderness, just as he was about to move, he suddenly felt the city before him grow distant.

Wait, it wasn't the city moving farther away; the surrounding space was expanding!

The barren wasteland was stretching, unfurling!

The original distance of roughly twenty li had suddenly been violently stretched to over a hundred li in the blink of an eye!

Accompanying this phenomenon was a cold, detached announcement.

A woman's voice drifted out, "Young Master of the Exalted Immortals, you will die here today."

Li Qi immediately felt a headache coming on.

He had _just_ figured things out, and now trouble was knocking on his door?

_Hey, I'm not the one who ruined this place! Why are you coming after me? Go look for the ruler of the Great Deer Kingdom!_

But Li Qi also knew complaining would achieve absolutely nothing.

Thus, he immediately clasped his hands and called out, "O Child of Destiny, why don't we have a chat! I did not come here to oppress your world!"

"Perhaps we can negotiate a solution that benefits everyone!"

However, as Li Qi shouted these words, he could keenly feel that the other party hadn't stopped their assault.

The spell art stretching the distance was still actively expanding.

"Sigh." Li Qi let out a breath.

Clearly, the fight had already reached his doorstep. Trying to persuade the other party with talk of 'righteousness and justice' now was visibly impossible.

Though Li Qi had clearly defined his direction in his heart, he was not a pedantic fool. He knew when to reason, and when to reason only _after_ the fighting was done.

Just like how he had dealt with those people in the past.

Justice could convince people, but the prerequisite was possessing the power to uphold that justice.

You had to be a person of both power and credibility to compel the masses and practice the Dao of the Invocator.

Otherwise, pure justice was nothing more than a joke. Many would leap out to smack you in the face, trample the righteousness and justice you insisted upon beneath their feet, and use brutal reality to teach you that justice was worthless.

Just like right now.

The Exalted Immortals—the people of the Great Deer Kingdom—dominated this world and had brought endless suffering to it.

Trying to persuade this world's Child of Destiny at a time like this was glaringly impossible.

Reasoning would have to wait until he beat the other party to the point where they were willing to listen.

Bai Di was practicing his martial arts, counting with every strike.

"Four hundred and ninety-seven..."

"Four hundred and ninety-eight..."

"Four hundred and ninety-nine..."

"Five hundred...!"

He completed exactly five hundred sword strikes. The muscles on his bare upper torso bulged, qi and blood surging within them as sweat poured off him like a fountain.

Only then did he stand straight, wipe away his sweat, and take a swig of water.

This training room had been specially prepared for him by the Zhang Corporation. It was exceptionally high-end, equipped with combat dummies, various exercise equipment, assorted weapons, a boxing ring, and even a healing pod to provide a place for body recovery after extreme overexertion.

Furthermore, there were two sparring partners on standby at all times.

But he didn't need any of it. He merely trained his own way—by swinging his sword.

Five hundred swings might sound like a paltry number, but he utilized the full strength of his entire body for every single strike.

This included his muscles, his internal qi, and all of his accumulated intent.

Every strike demanded total concentration, unleashing his bodily strength to the absolute limit. Each swing left him in a state of extreme exhaustion. A regular martial artist would only be able to execute one such strike a day before being paralyzed for four hours, only regaining the strength to fight by the next day.

Moreover, they would be completely unable to retract their force. The unleashed attack would fly out uncontrollably, causing massive environmental damage.

Yet when Bai Di swung his sword, he could perfectly split the force into two exact halves every single time, making them cancel each other out.

Any force was, fundamentally, a resultant force composed of countless component forces. Therefore, as long as one controlled the force perfectly, they could make the net force of the two halves equal zero, causing absolutely zero impact on the outside world.

Swinging his sword five hundred times in this manner was his daily regimen.

At the very beginning, he hadn't been able to control his force either. Qi blades would burst outward, destroying his surroundings, and he could only manage a single strike per day.

But after years of grueling training, he was completely different from his past self. He was a cream-of-the-crop talent even among Rank 9 martial artists, lacking only the final step to break through to Rank 8.

It was just that...

"Martial Dao practice is finished. Time to try out the rites." He set his sword down and rested for a moment.

His cultivation in the Shaman Dao was still unranked to this day. Much like Li Qi, he was an unranked Shaman.

The difference was that Li Qi's ranking was imminent. Once his qi of the four seasons was fully rounded, he would inevitably ascend in rank. But Bai Di... he had been stuck here for many years.

Because his heart wasn't in it. He didn't like the Shaman Dao, nor did he possess much talent for it.

However, the Great Deer Kingdom was founded upon the Shaman Dao. As a scholar of the Bai family, he was obligated to cultivate it. In fact, because his Martial Dao progressed so rapidly while his Shaman Dao stagnated, his uncle had severely reprimanded him several times.

In their eyes, the Martial Dao was nothing more than neglecting his proper duties.

Though Bai Di appeared elegant and refined, he harbored his own hidden grievances.

But he didn't change because of it; he had long grown accustomed to it.

_I should still practice. Uncle and the others have a point._

_The Martial Dao I love is ultimately a minor path. There is no supreme Martial Dao sect in this world akin to Shaman God Mountain._

_I'm not a child anymore. It's time to be realistic._

Shen Shuibi was presently swallowing the Supreme Yin qi.

Cultivating in the Daoist Sects was not like the path of the Invocators.

The Dao of Invocators utilized rites and protocols to cultivate. Everything they gained was the perfectly pure qi of the world and divine spirits. It required no refinement of their own; they could wield it directly.

More accurately, Invocators were like merchants conducting business. They roamed between Heaven, Earth, gods, and ghosts, relying on their own skills and credibility to earn qi through various means for their own use.

The Daoist Sects, however, were like toiling miners. Whatever they needed, they had to excavate themselves, absorbing and hoarding it bit by bit from the fabric of the world.

As a Moon Rabbit born from the essence soul of the Supreme Yin, the speed at which she absorbed the Supreme Yin qi was simply astounding.

At this rate, the accumulation that would take an ordinary Daoist disciple decades or even a century to achieve would only take her three to five years to fulfill.

However, even after fulfilling the requirements, restoring herself to Rank 8 was still a massive hurdle. It wouldn't be that simple.

The promotion of every rank required something unique to that specific rank; otherwise, it would be impossible to categorize them into ranks at all.

Rank 8 of the Martial Dao required comprehending the Martial Dao divine will.

The Shaman Dao required nurturing a god within the body.

The Daoist Sects were also straightforward: Rank 9 meant achieving the Golden Core, while Rank 8 meant forming the Yin Spirit.

The Yin Spirit could wander in spirit form, capable of traversing a hundred li in an instant and commuting across a province within a day. Its only flaw was that it could not interfere with reality. It was a construct of pure mental energy, though it could still cast a few spell arts.

Reaching a higher realm allowed one to condense the Yang Spirit. The Yang Spirit was the byproduct of refining the spirit to return to the void. Cultivation allowed one's essence qi to solidify without dissipating. Only by cultivating both nature and life simultaneously could one turn the void into reality, transforming divine will into a physical entity. It could freely roam Heaven and Earth, interfere with all creation, and exist independently of the main body. Even if the main body died, the Yang Spirit would survive.

The Yang Spirit was a genuine lifeform with flesh and blood, capable of altering reality. It could essentially be considered a Daoist priest's second body.

"Sigh, both my Yin Spirit and Yang Spirit were shattered. Reforging them now is going to be so hard..." Shen Shuibi couldn't help but sigh. Her rabbit head bobbed up and down, her face entirely masked in gloom.

The first time she cultivated, her body had been completely intact. All she had needed was to comprehend the critical junctures.

Now, she understood every critical juncture, but her personal cosmos was littered with fragmented debris. Every session of cultivation felt like trying to put together a puzzle.

The cultivation method of Mount Luofu positioned the self as the celestial pole, with the external universe revolving around it.

But right now, this celestial pole was inherently unstable, making the formation of a Yin Spirit incredibly difficult.

"I need to think of a way, or I'll be stuck at Rank 9." The rabbit curled into a tight ball, racking her brains in bitter contemplation, her pretty face scrunching up completely.

But before she could formulate a plan, she suddenly glanced out the window.

"The 'So Near Yet So Far' spell art? What's going on?"

So Near Yet So Far—what seemed right before one's eyes was actually worlds apart.

This spell art could stretch space, creating the effect of hiding massive objects within a minuscule area.

However... this was not a spell art a Rank 9 could master.

No amount of external force could make up the difference.

Shen Shuibi heaved a sigh.

"Ah, something's happened. Someone definitely went out of their way to target Li Qi! Ugh, that idiot. If he doesn't even look where he's going, how did he manage to get trapped by something like this? He has absolutely no eye for danger, yet he insisted on running off all by himself. Honestly!"

But she instantly returned to normal, muttering, "Still... he won't be in any real danger anyway. Whatever, I'll just ignore him."
