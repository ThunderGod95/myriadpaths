# Chapter 164: A Chance Encounter

Shen Shuibi dozed off by the roadside.

Well, she wasn't actually dozing off. She just hadn't told Li Qi what she was really doing.

In reality, her Yin Spirit was traveling afar, observing the situation over by the border.

That's right, her Yin Spirit. She had already recovered her strength to Rank 8.

After that night, she and Li Qi had talked until dawn. Opening up about many things had significantly lifted her spirits.

Then, the very next day... she had dismantled and swallowed the Moon Corporeal Soul fragment that carried her memories.

The memories had been spoken aloud; they were no longer something exclusively hers. Since the vessel carrying those memories was no longer the only one, what was the point of keeping the Moon Corporeal Soul?

Shen Shuibi thought with absolute clarity. She decisively consumed the Moon Corporeal Soul fragment, relying on the dense Supreme Yin qi within to mend a portion of the cracks in her physical body, smoothing the path to her Rank 8 recovery.

She knew this was Li Qi's intention all along. The Moon Corporeal Soul was inherently capable of repairing her body, but its usefulness was limited to her current state. Later on, such a mediocre material would be utterly useless.

If she didn't use it now, she would be wasting Li Qi's goodwill, and Shen Shuibi did not want that.

She didn't know how he figured it out, but he seemed to have seen through this truth, which was why he found the Moon Corporeal Soul fragment for her.

Regardless, since that day, their relationship seemed to have grown much closer. In Shen Shuibi's eyes, aside from the Moon Corporeal Soul itself, Li Qi was the only person who shared her memories of Mount Luofu.

After restoring her Rank 8 cultivation, the journey had been relatively uneventful. Li Qi's progress was astonishingly fast; he seemed innately gifted at mediating among the masses.

However, they were about to arrive at the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest. Rumor had it that at Bright Lake within the nest, there was a treasure capable of helping Li Qi temper his primal spirit.

The Daoist Sects also cultivated something called a primal spirit, but it was entirely different from the Shaman Dao's version.

In the ancient texts, the word 'Yuan'—the Primal—originally referred to a person's head. Because the head sits above all else, the concept naturally evolved to signify the 'chief,' the 'first,' and the 'absolute beginning.'

Because of this, the Daoist Sects spoke of the "Primal Beginning," signifying the very first cause that split heaven and earth, the driving force behind every "effect" in all creation.

Thus arose sayings such as, "The Primal is the beginning of qi," "The Primal Heaven is the origin that brings prosperity," and "To call the One the Primal is to look to the great beginning and seek the true root."

When applied to cultivation, this word generally referred to the foremost, most vital aspect.

Therefore, the "primal spirit" of the Daoist Sects was the starting point of one's cultivation, the origin of the Spirit treasure among the three treasures of Essence, Qi, and Spirit. Correspondingly, there was primal qi and primal essence. Together, these were called the three primals.

When these three primals gathered, they possessed the profound capacity described as: "The Dao originates from the unbegun; through resonance, it births the Wondrous One. From the Wondrous One, it divides into the three primals, which turn into the three qi, which birth the Three Realms. As the Three Realms flourish, all things are completed."[^1] This was the root of the Daoist Sects' personal cosmos, capable of expanding to encompass all things in the world.

Projected across the reflection of the personal cosmos and the external macrocosm, it manifested as the three primals. In heaven, they were the three lights of sun, moon, and stars; on earth, they were the three essentials of water, fire, and earth; within man, they were the three substances of essence, qi, and spirit.

This perspective and method of cultivation had fundamentally nothing to do with the Shaman Dao. If someone who didn't understand the underlying principles merely picked up a few manuals, started cultivating, and mixed the two systems together, they would inevitably suffer cultivation deviation and kill themselves.

For instance, that ghost-god Old Man Li they met in the Great Deer Kingdom. Despite being a majestic Rank 8 cultivator, he died in his fifties or sixties.

The primal spirit of the Shaman Dao was much purer. It simply referred to the "internal deity of the head."

The ancient meaning of Yuan was head, so the primal spirit naturally meant the god of the head. Its function was to control the brain and act as the central dispatcher for all the internal deities. It was the leader of the internal deities, much like the brain controlled the rest of the physical body.

The first internal deity formed represented a cultivator's area of focus during the Rank 8 stage.

If one prioritized tempering the Heavenly Gods of the Sun and Moon, their eyes would become bright, they would gain synesthetic abilities, and their sixth sense would become incredibly sharp, occasionally granting sudden premonitions of fortune and disaster.

Diviners generally formed these deities first.

If one first tempered the mountain gods of the Five Great Mountains, their physical body would become immensely solid. Their stamina and endurance would experience massive growth, their personal cosmos would become much tougher, and they would gain many divine abilities related to Earthly Divinities, such as earth traversal.

This had been Li Qi's original choice. After all, he belonged to the Earthly Divinity branch, and tempering these deities made travel convenient.

If one chose to first temper the water gods of the Four Great Rivers, their personal cosmos would be able to accommodate more originally incompatible qi. The four rivers connected the qi of all four directions, bolstering the shaman's power in spell arts while providing divine abilities related to water manipulation.

If one chose the primal spirit, they would possess sharp ears and bright eyes, as well as a perfectly clear mind. The body's functions would become smooth and harmonious, capable of coordinating the power of all internal deities. Their physical control and reaction speed would skyrocket.

Li Qi's choice to prioritize the primal spirit was likely driven by his desire to perfectly control those two streams of metal qi while still at Rank 9. However, tempering the primal spirit was extremely difficult. Had he chosen any other internal deity, he probably would have succeeded in forming one within the last half year.

The first god merely dictated a cultivator's focus; there was no hierarchy of superiority. It entirely depended on the shaman's personal preference.

Li Qi's decision to prioritize the primal spirit was undoubtedly so he could perfectly manipulate that metal qi.

Shen Shuibi naturally offered her full support. Thus, she accompanied him on his journey. After traveling for half a year, they were finally about to enter the territory of the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

Crossing the border of the Hundred Forests Kingdom would place them right inside the demon nest.

Spanning hundreds of miles, Shen Shuibi's Yin Spirit was observing the situation near the border.

Suddenly, she felt someone shaking her physical body.

_Hmm? Li Qi is back?_ Sensing this, Shen Shuibi's Yin Spirit stopped resisting the physical pull of her body.

The distance of a hundred miles was crossed in an instant.

In just a split second, her Yin Spirit snapped back into her mortal flesh.

"You're back~," Shen Shuibi opened her eyes and looked at the returning Li Qi with a smile. "Are you done handling everything? Are we setting off now?"

Li Qi nodded. "It's all taken care of. Let's head for the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest."

Their exchange was brief. Li Qi mounted Old Ma, and Shen Shuibi sat behind him as they rode toward the border.

Chatting about random trivialities along the way, Old Ma soon arrived at the border region.

After all, the current Old Ma could already be considered a demon. Although the horse hadn't officially entered the ranks, its dragon blood had been awakened. Its speed had skyrocketed by two or three times; it could now travel two thousand miles a day, and four thousand if traveling day and night. It was incredibly fast.

No wonder Young Master Zhu said it wouldn't take ten years.

As long as Li Qi kept progressing, three million miles didn't seem that far away at all...

"By the way, Li Qi, when my Yin Spirit was out roaming earlier, I saw that things over by the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest seemed really quiet. Nothing special stood out at all. It's just... very quiet."

"Quiet?" Li Qi was stunned.

_How could it possibly be quiet?_

"A battleground at the border is quiet? Are they not at war?" Li Qi asked.

"No, it doesn't even look like there's a garrison. The fortress is completely empty," Shen Shuibi replied.

Cold sweat immediately broke out on Li Qi's back.

_Holy crap, has it fallen? What the hell, I just got here and the border garrison is already an empty ghost town?_

"Hmm... I did see people evacuating on the road. They looked like soldiers, all military men. But they didn't seem like they were under attack. It looked like a peaceful withdrawal. Should we go ask?" Shen Shuibi suggested.

"Sure, let's go ask. Which direction?" Li Qi said.

"To the west." She pointed the way.

Old Ma didn't need further prompting. The horse launched into the sky, stepping on auspicious clouds, and galloped off rapidly.

_Chasing the wind and fast as lightning, Dayun... ahem_, anyway, Old Ma sprinted west at breakneck speed.[^2]

Barely fifteen minutes later, an army regiment indeed appeared in Li Qi's vision.

The army marched in neat, strictly disciplined formations. They were well-equipped, their morale seemed solid, and their military banner bore the emblem of a majestic eagle.

In terms of cultivation, the vast majority were unranked mortals, though almost all of them had some foundational cultivation method. Those at the level of a thousand-man commander appeared to be at Rank 9.

He couldn't spot the supreme commander directly, but there was a general's flag and a carriage, suggesting the commander was inside. As a result, Li Qi couldn't gauge their cultivation.

This regiment numbered around ten thousand in total—not a massive force.

The soldiers marched together at a steady, unhurried pace. There were no wounded, and they hadn't discarded any supplies. There was no sense of panic or a chaotic rout; it merely looked like a routine deployment.

However, as Li Qi passed by, the army seemed to notice him as well.

After all, the current Li Qi was a bit too conspicuous.

He was handsome to begin with, standing seven and a half feet tall with a well-proportioned build and a striking face. His clothes were impeccably clean, and he rode a dragon steed that soared through the sky.

As the dragon steed moved, it was trailed by auspicious clouds and wreathed in lightning. Mortals might not notice it from afar, but any Rank 9 cultivator would hear the roaring wind, much like the thrum of an airplane flying overhead.

If one looked up and had sharp enough eyesight, they would also see a beautiful woman sitting sideways behind him. No matter how one looked at it, it resembled a young noble scion out on an excursion, exuding a carefree and elegant air.

For someone presenting such an image to pass by an army, drawing attention was only natural.

Seeing that he had been spotted, Li Qi told Old Ma, "Go down, I'll go talk to them."

Hearing this, Old Ma descended to the ground, snorting and neighing. Serpentine bolts of lightning instantly danced around them, startling the soldiers into immediately raising their spears and forming up their battle arrays.

"Wait here for a moment. I'll go ask about the situation," Li Qi said in a low voice. He swung down from the horse and slowly walked toward the army.

Stopping about a mile away, Li Qi cupped his hands toward the wary regiment. "Brave warriors, may I ask if this is the garrison of the Hundred Forests Kingdom?"

Whispers suddenly broke out within the ranks, but they were quickly silenced by the reprimands of the officers. The soldiers maintained their defensive formation.

Once the army's formation locked into place, visible military qi began to drift. Crimson qi swelled to block out the sun, spiraling around the military banner to generate rings of spiritual light. The auras linked together like intertwining vines, forming a massive, illusionary eagle with razor-sharp talons that glared fixedly at Li Qi.

Soon, a military cultivator at the rank of a thousand-man commander stepped forward. He was at Rank 9 and possessed considerable strength.

Gripping his long spear, the commander addressed Li Qi, "The Hundred Forests Kingdom garrison lies straight ahead. What are your intentions, sir?"

"That is good. I was only worried I had taken the wrong path. I am Li Qi. I intend to head into the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest to sever a few demonic heads as a trial of experience. However, I found the garrison deserted and grew suspicious. But as long as you confirm this is the way to the garrison, that is enough. Since I haven't gone astray, I will continue onward." Li Qi cupped his hands and turned to leave.

However, exactly as Li Qi had anticipated, the commander immediately called out to stop him. "Young hero, please hold your steps!"

Li Qi naturally knew the other party would try to stop him.

For an army withdrawing from its defensive perimeter to see someone heading straight into the war zone and say absolutely nothing would be far too abnormal.

If they hadn't tried to stop him, Li Qi would have secretly followed the army instead to uncover the truth behind their actions.

Seeing the commander ask him to stay, Li Qi turned back. "Yes, warrior?"

"You are heading to the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest? Do you have any idea what has happened over there recently?!" the commander bellowed across the distance.

"Has something changed in the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest?" Li Qi frowned. He quickened his pace, walking directly toward the army.

The soldiers nervously raised their spears, but without an officer's command, no one made a move.

The commander was also on guard. He was just about to shout for Li Qi to halt and not approach recklessly, as it could easily cause a misunderstanding.

The other party was a cultivator; who knew if he was a demon or devil in disguise? The general hadn't spoken yet.

But right at that moment, a voice suddenly drifted from the carriage behind him. The general spoke.

The voice wasn't loud, yet it echoed throughout the surrounding mountains and fields. "Did you say your name is Li Qi? The recently renowned shaman, Li Qi?"

"Ah? Renowned?" Li Qi was dumbfounded. He genuinely hadn't heard anything about his own fame.

But it made sense. After all, he had been rushing forward this entire time without ever looking back.

"Even at the front lines, I have heard the rumors. The shaman Li Qi has risen to fame within the Hundred Forests Kingdom. He frequently performs chivalrous deeds without asking for reward, traveling with a carefree and unrestrained grace. To think your ultimate destination was the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest to slay demons... it is truly admirable." A commanding officer stepped out from the carriage. Clad in heavy armor, he stood over nine feet tall. Li Qi only reached his chest.

Not only was he tall, but he was also massively built. He had the back of a tiger and the waist of a bear, with knotted, bulging muscles. Veins coiled beneath his skin like slithering snakes. He radiated a thick baleful qi, and his martial internal qi was practically bursting from his body.

Li Qi could tell at a single glance—this was a Rank 8 of the Martial Dao! As expected of the lord of an army.

This was his first time seeing a living Rank 8 Martial Dao cultivator!

The reason wasn't hard to understand. The path of the Martial Dao was absurdly difficult to walk.

To be honest, Li Qi was planning to cripple his own Martial Dao cultivation right after tempering his primal spirit. For no other reason than it was simply too agonizing.

One had to practice their forms daily; skipping a single day would result in regressing cultivation. It also demanded an extreme level of comprehension. It was purely a Dao meant for beasts of burden.

Further along the line, it would eventually conflict with the Shaman Dao anyway, so Li Qi was prepared to simply discard it.

Because of this, seeing a living Rank 8 Martial Dao expert left him utterly astounded.

_What kind of beast of burden... ah no, what kind of divine being is this?_

Facing a Rank 8 expert, Li Qi maintained a respectful attitude. He immediately cupped his hands and asked, "I dare not boast before a General. May I ask why you have stopped me?"

"It's nothing. I simply wanted to meet the renowned young hero for myself. And, as a passing favor, I wanted to warn you: the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest has undergone a massive change. Going inside recently might not be a wise choice."

[^1]: This passage speaks of classical Daoist creation cosmology. It describes the evolution of the universe starting from absolute nothingness ("the unbegun"). From this void emerges a singular, unified origin point of existence ("the Wondrous One"). This singularity then fractures into fundamental cosmic forces (the primals and the qi), which ultimately form the structure of reality—traditionally represented as Heaven, Earth, and Humanity (the "Three Realms" or San Cai).
[^2]: Dayun is a reference to a famous Chinese television commercial for Dayun Motorcycles. Their iconic advertising slogan is "Chasing the wind and fast as lightning, Dayun Motorcycles." Li Qi, a transmigrator from modern Earth, reflexively recalls this jingle due to the horse's incredible speed before catching himself.
