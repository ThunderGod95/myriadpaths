# Chapter 91: Putting on Weight

Don't ask; he booked two rooms.

This move left even the inn's waiter stunned.

He had already prepared their finest double suite, yet this gentleman opted for two separate rooms.

_What's going on? A true gentleman?_

_Or is he simply lacking in manhood?_

But Li Qi ignored the waiter's bewildered gaze. He paid for the rooms and told Shen Shuibi that he was going out for a walk. He gave her permission to move about freely but warned her not to wander off too far.

The rabbit nodded eagerly, like a chick pecking at rice, looking extremely obedient.

Putting his mind at ease, Li Qi packed away his belongings. He carefully re-wrapped his saber in coarse cloth so that it resembled a simple wooden staff, and then headed out the door.

What was he doing going out? Looking for an opportunity to make money, of course. He couldn't just sit around and eat through his savings.

Old Ma couldn't put the weight back on in just a day or two; he needed at least half a month of proper rest. Fortunately, he didn't require new horseshoes, otherwise the expenses would be even greater.

But there was no need. A dragon colt treaded on clouds, so its hooves suffered very little wear and tear. Furthermore, its hooves didn't grow continuously like those of an ordinary spirit horse, which could lead to illness.

A dragon colt's hooves remained a fixed size. If chipped, they would grow; once full, they stopped growing entirely. It was extremely convenient.

They were both called horses, but in reality, it felt like they belonged to completely different species.

It made sense when he thought about it. One was a horse; the other was half-dragon.

With these random thoughts swirling in his mind, Li Qi strolled through the streets of Snake Tail County. He looked around, observing the county's layout and casually conversing with the locals to gather common knowledge he was previously ignorant of.

The air here was incredibly fresh and quite humid.

Human settlements were invariably built near water. A clear river ran right through Snake Tail County, fed by the melting snows of White Snake Mountain.

Just as the entirety of Li Prefecture relied on the Li River for its livelihood, White Snake Prefecture relied entirely on White Snake Mountain. After all, those living on a mountain lived off the mountain, and those living near the water lived off the water.

The melting snows formed countless streams that meandered downward, creating a dense water network that nourished all of nature. The existence of White Snake Mountain itself was a treasure trove.

Unlike the barren forests of Li Prefecture, White Snake Mountain produced countless heavenly treasures and possessed extending mineral veins. Because of this, White Snake Prefecture was quite wealthy.

Moreover, this wealth was built upon the backs of numerous adventurers.

Whether it was prospecting for veins, foraging for heavenly treasures, or undertaking other ventures, they had to leave the city and brave the wilderness. Merchant caravans waited back in the city, ready to buy whatever goods these adventurers managed to haul back.

Once purchased, the goods were loaded onto boats, ferried along the rivers down to the Li River, and transported from there to other regions.

White Snake Mountain and the Li River formed a perfect ecological chain, supporting the basic needs of hundreds of millions of residents across two prefectures in the Great Deer Kingdom.

However, while the people of Li Prefecture could earn a living from farming and collecting taxes within the city walls thanks to the Li River, making money in White Snake Prefecture required stepping out of the city limits.

Li Prefecture had the Li River. As long as they planted crops outside the city and settled near the riverbank, abundant resources, taxes, and livelihoods would naturally deliver themselves. Water was incredibly gentle to humanity; one only needed to stay quietly by its side, and provisions would flow down with the current.

The mountain, however, was different. It was bountiful, yet severe. It wouldn't simply wash provisions down to you like a river. You had to prepare your own gear, risk your life, and harvest its bounties yourself.

For this very reason, a strong martial ethos permeated the region. Many households owned swords, sabers, and armor, frequently banding together to head into the mountains and seek their fortune.

White Snake Mountain was an inexhaustible treasure trove. Whenever one lacked money, they simply went in to harvest.

Of course it was. A mountain range stretching for thousands of li covered an area of millions of square kilometers—essentially the size of a continent on Earth. It would be a miracle if it could be exhausted.

Snake Tail County's population was relatively small, numbering only a few hundred thousand. Yet, just by walking around and observing, Li Qi could acutely feel the culture of the entire White Snake Prefecture.

There weren't as many gangs as in Li Prefecture. Instead, there was a surplus of 'mountain-harvesting platoons' and 'martial arts dojos.'

These were the most common organizations in Snake Tail County.

People could frequently be seen practicing martial arts on the streets, and arenas were set up in various locations. The locals took joy in sparring and testing their martial skills. The winner earned roaring cheers, while the loser stepped down without discouragement, making way for the next challenger.

It looked more like a communal game where winning or losing mattered little; the true goal was mutual improvement.

Li Qi could tell that most of these individuals were significantly stronger than the gang members in Li Prefecture City. Many carried baleful qi and bore scars—likely the result of years spent exploring and fighting in the mountains, having slaughtered both beasts and men alike.

Because of this, the forests around White Snake Prefecture were highly developed.

Li Qi realized that unlike in Li Prefecture, it would be difficult to gallop through the wilderness here for years without encountering a single soul.

If he were to ride Old Ma at full speed here, he would likely run into a mountain-harvesting platoon every few miles and leave them eating his dust.

"It's not suited... for being too flashy here. Otherwise, it will be easy to expose the dragon colt's identity." Li Qi shook his head, evaluating the situation around White Snake Mountain and contemplating his next move.

A tall spirit horse and a dragon colt were two entirely different concepts.

Unless someone was an expert in judging horses like Shen Shuibi, simply walking wouldn't easily give them away. However, if Old Ma took flight and galloped, anyone with even rudimentary knowledge would instantly recognize it as a dragon colt worth a thousand gold—a beast over a hundred times the price of an ordinary spirit horse.

_But making money should be quite convenient. The Martial Dao thrives here, and many mountain-harvesting platoons are recruiting. However, those are just ordinary platoons, and their strongest members haven't even achieved a rank. The pay is too low; it's a waste of time._ Li Qi walked down the street, analyzing how to earn some travel expenses to buy extra feed for Old Ma.

As a newcomer, he shouldn't expose his abilities as an Invocator. Yet, leveraging his Martial Dao Rank 9 cultivation should guarantee him a decent income.

After all, despite wandering the streets for an entire noon, he hadn't spotted a single Rank 9 expert. The strongest person among those patrol platoons was merely a burly, bearded man.

That man had only just finished tempering strength in his skin, muscles, and bones, and was barely beginning to refine his internal organs. Judging by his age—somewhere in his forties or fifties—his qi and blood were already declining. He had zero hope of reaching Rank 9 in this lifetime.

Achieving a rank was simply this difficult for ordinary people—so excruciatingly hard that dedicating one's entire life might still end in failure.

In fact, setting aside achieving a rank, the mere act of accessing a cultivation method capable of granting a rank filtered out ninety-nine percent of the population.

This realization made Li Qi marvel once more at his own good fortune.

Therefore, he had to seize this opportunity firmly.

Li Qi continued strolling down the street, listening to the shouts of a crowd.

Right then, someone hurried over and took the initiative to call out to him, "Brave warrior! Please, wait a moment!"

Hearing this, Li Qi turned his head to look.

He saw a merchant dressed in silk garments, though he had no idea why the man had stopped him.
