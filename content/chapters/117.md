# Chapter 117: Minor Matters on the Dao

The _Chapter on Golden Core_ stated: "If the five grains can sustain human life—granting life when consumed and death when denied—how much more so for superior, divine medicines? Would their benefit to a person not be ten thousand times greater than that of the grains?"[^1]

In other words, humanity could rely on external objects to fortify their physical forms. By absorbing the miraculous properties of external substances like pills through specific pathways, the human body could achieve immortality.

Therefore, within the Daoist Sects, cultivation was divided into two distinct paths: Inner Alchemy and Outer Alchemy.

Inner Alchemy demanded the dual cultivation of nature and life force, forged entirely through one's own cultivation base. This was the essence of the saying: "The precious cauldron of the dragon and tiger is the body and mind itself. The body is the furnace, the heart is the spirit chamber, and the saliva is the blooming pool."[^2]

If a cultivator lacked the necessary talent and could not form an inner core on their own, they had to rely on external power. By using the converging qi of heaven and earth to form an Outer Alchemy pill, they could swallow it to assist their body in forming its own core.

Some utilized ordinary alchemy cauldrons, while others used the bodies of others as their furnace. The methods were endlessly varied.

The practice of Inner Alchemy required strict self-discipline. It sought power only from within, placing modest demands on worldly resources but extremely high demands on one's temperament and mental state.

Conversely, the path of Outer Alchemy required continuous exploration and experimentation to discover which external materials were useful for personal core formation. It demanded an astronomical amount of resources, and its requirements for mental fortitude were not low either.

For an unranked cultivator, entering the ranks through Inner Alchemy meant relying on slow, methodical practice. Through grinding effort, their primal spirit would eventually reach perfection, allowing them to ascend into the ranks.

An unranked Outer Alchemy cultivator seeking to enter the ranks had to design their own pill recipes. Through countless experiments, they would eventually synthesize an Outer Alchemy pill effective for their specific constitution. Only after swallowing it could they enter the ranks.

Ultimately, the cultivation of the Daoist Sects could not escape a core philosophy: absorbing the essence of the sun and moon, gathering the qi of heaven and earth, focusing the mind to condense cinnabar[^3], and tempering the physical form to seek transcendence—taking in much, and expending little.

The only difference was that Inner Alchemy drew these resources into the body, using the physical form itself as an alchemy cauldron for slow, steady cultivation.

Alternatively, Outer Alchemy refined and prepared these resources in an external alchemy cauldron before consumption, granting the benefits directly.

Both methods culminated in the Golden Core. There was no innate superiority between them; it solely depended on the path one chose to walk.

For Shen Shuibi, she had originally walked the path of Inner Alchemy. However, with her physical body currently shattered to such an extent, Inner Alchemy was no longer viable. Since her body could no longer serve as a furnace, walking the path of Outer Alchemy was the only solution.

The concoction she was refining was called the "Evil-Sweeping Righteous Return Pill."

This pill was an orthodox product of Outer Alchemy, refined according to Golden Core methods. Consuming it would allow one to form a Golden Core.

Refining such a thing was a breeze for her. The only difficulty lay in the preliminary preparation phase.

Yang Ning's collection had already helped her supplement the necessary resources. After finishing the refinement over the past few days, she made her preparations and began to consume it.

A single golden pill was swallowed into her belly.

Instantly, righteous qi surged from various acupoints across her body—the Crimson Palace, Guanyuan, Qi Sea, Seabed, Mingtang, and the Heavenly Bridge. Tranquil yet vast and mighty, she used her foundational spirit to command her intrinsic qi, merging it with the qi of the void. Water and fire achieved equilibrium, dragon and tiger converged, and her power immediately began to skyrocket.

Pursuing the dual qi of sun and moon along the ecliptic, gathering the three natures in the origin palace, assembling the five elements, and harmonizing the Four Phenomena[^4]. Amidst the roar of the dragon and the howl of the tiger, the jade cauldron boiled and the golden furnace blazed, finally allowing the profound pearl to manifest its form.

In an instant, anomalous visions materialized around Shen Shuibi. Numerous dao rhythms resonated loudly, and a leisurely purple qi descended alongside auspicious blessings. An illusion of the Supreme Yin descended from above, teeming with vibrant Moon Essence!

However, all of these phenomena were contained within three feet of her body. The outside world sensed absolutely nothing.

Rank 9 was achieved.

There were no bottlenecks or unexpected accidents to speak of. For her, it was simply a matter of course.

Yet, at that exact moment, the governor of White Snake Prefecture, who was resting inside his carriage, suddenly opened his eyes.

Shrinking the earth to inches, his body crossed the distance in the blink of an eye, arriving rapidly at Shen Shuibi's side.

He arrived just in time to witness the anomalous vision of that bright moon.

_Leisurely condensing light, cold dew falls; a single note of heavenly music chills the flesh and bone._

_Wind and dew drift freely, bursting with crystalline brilliance; cicadas sing amongst withered leaves beneath the graceful moon._

What a magnificent crescent moon.

Solitary, elevated, and beautiful. It illuminated the supreme purity, having watched the mortal realm rise and fall for untold millennia.

All color instantly drained from the governor's face. He hurriedly bowed to Shen Shuibi. "I did not know a senior was here. Forgive my intrusion... No, I apologize for my prior neglect. I beg senior not to take offense."

Such a dao rhythm... it was undoubtedly Rank 5!

_A supreme expert of Rank 5 was acting as that young master's maid?_

_Good heavens!_

_Just who exactly are these people?_

"You haven't intruded; rather, I was the one who disturbed you, wasn't I? I didn't expect to cause such a commotion. I tried to restrain it, but since you have already reached Rank 6, it seems you noticed it anyway," Shen Shuibi replied politely. She didn't bear a shred of arrogance, appearing somewhat embarrassed instead.

She had, after all, disturbed someone's peace.

It was quite troublesome.

"N-not at all," the governor stammered, drenched in a cold sweat. "If I may be so bold as to ask, is senior the young master's Dao Protector? Was it inappropriate for me to presumptuously arrange an extraterritorial journey for him?"

He was deeply worried.

What if he had accidentally stepped on a landmine?

This was a Rank 5 entity.

A supreme expert of Rank 5, striking with full force, possessed enough power to directly collapse an extraterritorial world, obliterating billions of living beings in the blink of an eye.

The higher one climbed on the path of cultivation, the more acutely they could feel the massive chasm between each rank.

It was just... why did this Rank 5 expert's aura feel so excessively weak?

He couldn't help but steal a glance upward, verifying the dao rhythm once more.

It was genuinely Rank 5.

_Perhaps she deliberately concealed her power to keep the young master unaware of his Dao Protector's true strength._

_It's just a bit strange._

_Why would a direct descendant of Shaman God Mountain have a Dao Protector from the Daoist Sects?_

_Forget it. These matters aren't something I should meddle in. Asking too many questions will only get me killed._

"Ah? Extraterritorial? Do whatever makes you happy. His teacher already said to let him temper himself out in the world, so I won't interfere much. Did you come looking for me just to ask about this?" Shen Shuibi tilted her head.

"Exactly so. If senior finds no fault with it, then I can set my mind at ease," the governor of White Snake Prefecture said, bowing low.

Of course, he could never admit that he had come to investigate because he sensed the presence of a high-level entity. That would make him seem far too tactless.

"I see," Shen Shuibi murmured, tilting her head again.

"Then this junior wouldn't dare disturb senior any further. I will take my leave." The governor of White Snake Prefecture immediately slipped away, retreating to his own carriage.

Before leaving, he didn't forget to cast a glance at Li Qi in the other carriage.

His eyes were brimming with envy.

_I truly envy these favored children of heaven._

_Not only do they have elders acting as Dao Protectors along the way, but they are also born with access to top-tier methods leading straight to Rank 1. They have an inexhaustible supply of resources and fortuitous encounters._

_Moreover, judging by the aura radiating from that young master, does he actually possess a trace of innate dao rhythm?_

_That would mean he has stood above others since the moment he was born. I suppose this is exactly why a Grand Invocator accepted him as a disciple._

_And it guarantees that, in the end, he will rightfully stand at the absolute pinnacle._

---

A little while later, Shen Shuibi bounded cheerfully over to Li Qi's side.

Li Qi had been engrossed in the [Book of Invocation]. Seeing Shen Shuibi approach, he withdrew his focus and looked up at her.

What he saw gave him quite a fright.

A dense aura of Supreme Yin qi wreathed Shen Shuibi's body, not yet fully dissipated.

"Li Qi! I'm at Rank 9!" Shen Shuibi announced happily.

_Rank 9 already?_

Li Qi was startled.

_Damn, entering the ranks really is nothing to these favored children of heaven, is it?_

_Oh, wait. That's not right._

_Shen Shuibi was originally a Rank 5 expert. Returning to Rank 9 should naturally be as easy as breathing for her._

"Congratulations on your return to Rank 9, Miss Shen," Li Qi said politely.

"Mhm~ Oh, by the way, I made a little gift for you too." Shen Shuibi looked exceedingly pleased, hiding her hands behind her back, her face beaming with smiles.

"A gift?" Li Qi was curious.

"This right here!" Shen Shuibi stretched out her hand, presenting a golden pill about the size of a pinky finger.

"What is this?" Li Qi tilted his head, not quite understanding.

"I made it for you while I was refining my alchemy. I brewed it right alongside my Outer Alchemy!" Shen Shuibi wore an expression that practically demanded praise.

"Outer Alchemy?" Li Qi frowned.

According to his shallow understanding, Outer Alchemy involved highly flawed items with massive side effects. Things like using the inner cores of demon beasts to refine an Outer Alchemy pill, which would supposedly sever one's future prospects upon use.

"Yeah, Outer Alchemy. What's wrong?" Seeing Li Qi's reaction, Shen Shuibi had no idea why he suddenly looked so stern.

"Are Outer Alchemy methods actually usable?" Li Qi asked.

"Ah? Oh, I know what you mean." Hearing Li Qi's question, Shen Shuibi immediately understood his concerns.

Thus, the rabbit began to explain.

"What exactly is Outer Alchemy?"

"In ancient times, the Most High Primordial Lady transmitted the Dao to the mortal realm, instructing on the principles of heaven and earth's ascent and descent, and the cyclical harmony of the sun and moon. Since then, alchemy scriptures have filled the world, allowing mortals to hear the Great Dao. Extracting mercury from cinnabar and silver from lead, they likened mercury to the Yang Dragon and silver to the Yin Tiger. By adjusting the moon's fire, gathering the qi of the eight directions, and attuning to the seasons, they used sulfur as a medicine to harmonize with spiritual cinnabar. Once an Outer Alchemy core reaches great completion, it allows one to ascend freely, acting like a mighty warrior stretching his arms to traverse thousands of li. Even if it cannot return one to Penglai, it grants immortality amidst the calamitous mortal realm. How could such a thing not be usable?"

"As for those rumors you've heard, they were probably spread by ignorant charlatans, weren't they?"

"Mortals are fond of petty tricks and fail to scrutinize the depths of the Dao. External medicines are not unusable. In fact, someone in my condition has to use Outer Alchemy because my bodily injuries are simply too severe."

Shen Shuibi fiddled with the pill in her hand as she continued, "The body contains two critical points: the root of qi and the source of vitality. If the qi root is not deep, its leaves will not flourish; if the vitality source is impure, its stream will not flow far. This is where the concept of 'foundations' originates. With my body in poor shape, my foundations are far from solid, making Inner Alchemy impossible to form. Therefore, if I wish to cultivate, I can only rely on Outer Alchemy."

"By merely borrowing the properties of the five metals and eight stones, and accumulating effort over days and months, one can refine a Rank 9 Dragon Tiger Great Pill. Taking it connects one with true qi, tempering the form to anchor oneself in the mortal world, allowing one to leap lightly as if flying, and smoothly enter Rank 9."[^5]

Shen Shuibi spoke of it lightly, but Li Qi was terrified upon hearing this.

"Wait, so this is an Outer Alchemy pill? If I eat this, I'll advance straight into Rank 9 of the Daoist Sects?" Li Qi was astounded.

_According to Shen Shuibi, couldn't anyone just pop a pill to reach Rank 9? Damn, who would even bother cultivating then?_

However, upon hearing his words, Shen Shuibi rolled her eyes. "What are you thinking? Outer Alchemy pills must be refined by oneself to work."

"Ultimately, Outer Alchemy is just the alternative chosen when your personal cosmos cannot be utilized. Though the personal cosmos seems simple, it is, after all, a 'cosmos.' How could an outsider possibly examine it thoroughly? Even I cannot map out every single detail of another person's body."

"Many things can only be clearly perceived by oneself. Therefore, an Outer Alchemy pill can only be refined by the individual according to their own unique conditions; it cannot be delegated to an outsider."

"Otherwise, if the refined Outer Alchemy pill clashes with one's personal cosmos, consuming it will not only yield no benefits, but the chaotic qi of the metals, cinnabar, and mercury will kill you. Even if you've entered Rank 9 of the Martial Dao, possess a robust physique, and have strong renal metabolism, eating a mismatched Outer Alchemy pill won't end well for you. You'd likely be bedridden for three to five months."

Hearing this, Li Qi rubbed his chin and asked, "Doesn't that make an Outer Alchemy pill highly toxic, then? What if we get our hands on a couple to use for a sneak attack against someone?"

"Outer Alchemy pills are extremely expensive... If you want a poison, wouldn't conventional poisons work just fine, and with better effects? ...Besides, once someone reaches Rank 8 or above, their personal cosmos stabilizes. Taking an Outer Alchemy pill won't negatively affect them, though it wouldn't offer any benefits either." Shen Shuibi was rendered entirely speechless.

If one wanted to poison someone to death, there were plenty of actual poisons available. Why waste an Outer Alchemy pill? These pills could catapult a compatible person straight to Rank 9. Their immense value went without saying. Using one as a poison was a truly absurd notion.

Much more potent poisons were also far cheaper than an Outer Alchemy pill.

At this point, Shen Shuibi cut the topic short. "Alright, enough about that. Take a look at this!"

"So, what is it?" Li Qi also dropped the subject of Outer Alchemy.

Since Shen Shuibi claimed it wasn't an Outer Alchemy pill, what was it?

"This item is called the Consumption-Extracting Worm. It's a pill recorded in the immortal prescriptions of the Shangqing Purple Court. It can fortify your qi and blood, nourish your five viscera, and suppress any lingering consumption qi within your body. You were invaded by the wicked qi of those two Rank 6 demons earlier, and I've been worried about you suffering lasting side effects. So, I made this for you while I was at the cauldron!" Shen Shuibi explained, handing the pill to Li Qi with a beaming smile. Her expression was incredibly tender, her eyes crinkling with a mirth that made them sparkle like stars.

Looking at her smiling face, Li Qi felt deeply moved.

_She genuinely cares about me._

"Thank you, Miss Shen," Li Qi said in gratitude.

"It's nothing, as long as you're okay. If it weren't for you this time, Her Ladyship would have been in real danger. I should be the one thanking you. Anyway, you take your medicine; I'll be going now." Saying this, Shen Shuibi stood up to leave.

Li Qi suspected nothing. After watching her leave the room, he immediately swallowed the pill.

Instantly, he clearly felt a stream of qi travel from his tailbone and ascend along his Governing Vessel.

The qi stream was intensely warm. Wherever it passed, his qi and blood would boil, his muscles would bulge and harden, and his blood flow would rapidly accelerate.

The scorching current then traveled past his spine to the Niwan and Baihui acupoints at his crown, before descending along the Conception Vessel straight down to his perineum.

_Wait, perineum?_

Li Qi suddenly broke out in a sweat.

Such an immense surge of qi, blood, and heat caused all his muscles to harden involuntarily, which naturally meant...

_Hey._

_The blood-fortifying effects of this thing are way too intense, aren't they?!_

Just then, the door abruptly opened. Shen Shuibi popped her head back inside. "Oh, right, Li Qi! I forgot to mention one thing. Make sure you prepare a tub of ice water and soak in it before taking the pill, otherwise your qi and blood will boil over. It doesn't have any actual side effects, but it will be a bit uncomfortable."

Having delivered her warning, she pulled her head back and shut the door. She skipped away, presumably off to handle something else, without glancing back once. It was impossible to tell if she had seen his current state.

Left inside, Li Qi stood hunched over, his face flushed completely red, staring blankly in absolute shock.

_God fucking damn it, you stupid rabbit!_

[^1]: The "five grains" (五谷, wǔgǔ) is a traditional Chinese classification for the primary staple crops (typically rice, wheat, soybeans, and two types of millet).
[^2]: This passage describes the core philosophy of Inner Alchemy (Neidan). Rather than using physical ingredients, the cultivator uses their own body. The "dragon and tiger" represent the opposing forces of Yin and Yang (or water and fire) within the cultivator. The "furnace and cauldron" are the physical body where the core is forged. The "spirit chamber" is the heart or middle dantian (the seat of the mind), and the "blooming pool" (Hua Chi) refers to the mouth, where the generation and swallowing of saliva is practiced to nourish the body's internal energy.
[^3]: In historical Daoist Outer Alchemy, cinnabar (a red mineral ore) was the primary ingredient used to forge physical elixirs of immortality. In Internal Alchemy and cultivation, "condensing cinnabar" is a metaphor for gathering, purifying, and compressing one's internal Qi to form the Golden Core (Jindan).
[^4]: This passage describes the precise internal process of Core Formation (Inner Alchemy) using classical Daoist terminology:
    * **Three Natures:** Refers to the "Three Treasures" of human life—Jing (Vitality/Essence), Qi (Energy), and Shen (Spirit/Mind).
    * **Origin Palace:** The lower Dantian, the spiritual center located below the navel where the elixir is forged.
    * **Five Elements:** Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, and Water. In internal alchemy, this means gathering and unifying the energies of the five major internal organs (liver, heart, spleen, lungs, and kidneys).
    * **Four Phenomena:** In Ch. 115, the "Four Phenomena" were the sun, moon, stars, and the constellations. However, "Four Phenomena" (四象, Sì Xiàng) is a deeply layered concept that changes meaning depending on the specific text, era, or philosophical system.
        In traditional Inner Alchemy (Neidan), the Four Phenomena are almost always mapped to the four cardinal directions, and their guardian beasts.
[^5]: "The five metals and eight stones" (五金八石, wǔjīn bāshí) is a classical Daoist term for the physical, often highly toxic, mineral ingredients used in Outer Alchemy (Waidan). The five metals typically included gold, silver, copper, iron, and lead, while the eight stones included minerals like cinnabar, sulfur, realgar, and mica. By purifying these raw materials in a crucible, an alchemist sought to forge a "Dragon Tiger" (Yin and Yang balanced) elixir that could grant superhuman physical prowess and earthly immortality.
