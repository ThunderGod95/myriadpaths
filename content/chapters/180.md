# Chapter 180: Clues

However, the true instigator of this incident was currently in the sky, galloping past on a dragon colt.

Wang Boyan followed alongside, thoroughly astonished. "Whoa, whoa, whoa, Brother Li! I thought you were a reasonable man who would go down, interrogate them one by one, weigh the details, and finally deliver a just verdict. Why did you just kill him outright? That doesn't fit your usual style at all."

Atop Old Ma, Li Qi shook his head. "The imperial palace was shrouded in death qi. Everyone was in peril, trembling in constant fear. On our flight over, we saw a world where the people are destitute, the fields are littered with bleached bones, and nine out of ten houses are empty. The scars of war are everywhere. That alone is enough to show what happened."

"Yet when we arrived at the palace, it was utterly magnificent and excessively extravagant. The population of a massive outer city couldn't even compare to the number of attendants serving within those walls. The evidence is irrefutable. There was no need to agonize over the details; killing him was the cleanest solution."

"You really draw a clear line. So, what do you plan to do next?" Wang Boyan asked.

Li Qi immediately laid out his arrangement. "I've already located the military granary here. There are ample provisions—roughly hundred thousand tonnes, likely meant to sustain the entire palace for several months. I'll take the required grain and leave the rest for the native inhabitants of this world."

"As for you, the Heavenly Dao of this realm has designated an area for you on the islands of the Southern Seas. You can grow your crops and mine your ores there without hindrance, but you must not take a single step beyond those boundaries." Li Qi explained the situation.

Wang Boyan narrowed his eyes. "Wait. How do you know all this?"

"I asked the wronged spirits here and learned the granary's location," Li Qi replied. "As for the southern islands, the Heavenly Dao of this realm conveyed it to me from the unseen world."

Hearing this, Wang Boyan fell silent for a long moment.

After a while, he flicked his folding fan open. Flying alongside Li Qi, he offered a sighing appraisal. "...Truly worthy of being a real shaman. Is this an Invocator? To commune with all things—whether it is the lofty Heavenly Dao, the various Earthly Divinities, or even ghosts that have lost their minds. It is a staggering power."

"Brother Wang, this is the second time you've marveled at 'real shamans'." Li Qi turned to look at Wang Boyan. "What are you implying?"

"Nothing. I couldn't help but sigh in admiration. After all, this is the first time I've ever seen a shaman other than those wild, rural practitioners," Wang Boyan said.

Li Qi remained noncommittal and said no more.

There was no need to speak; he only needed to stay guarded in secret.

Li Qi had never trusted this man.

The ensuing matters proceeded smoothly.

Li Qi descended and packed the grain he needed into his mustard seed pouch, while Wang Boyan received the coordinates of the southern island where he was permitted to cultivate his crops.

He happily named the small island "Little Penglai."

This earned him a few extra glances from Li Qi, but Li Qi did not voice any complaints.

In the later generations of this minor world, rumors would spread of a Penglai Immortal Island overseas, inhabited by immortals possessing vast divine abilities.

Upon this immortal island were countless grains of spirit rice, immortal fruits, and divine herbs. Yet it stood isolated across the sea, its inhabitants never taking a half-step beyond its shores. The mortal world would never see them, save for the rare lost ship that might stumble upon it by sheer luck.

But the number of people who witnessed it was so minuscule that it would forever remain a mere legend.

Although he had slain a tyrant, Li Qi had never intended to make a name for himself in this world. From beginning to end, this realm remained entirely ignorant of his existence.

All he left behind was the myth of an immortal.

Perhaps that was how the legends of many worlds began.

Setting that aside, the duo did not delay. Once their tasks were complete, they swiftly departed from the minor world and immediately went their separate ways.

Wang Boyan busied himself with the Golden Wave Sect's relocation and agricultural efforts, while Li Qi hurried back non-stop toward the White Rhino Army's encampment.

Everything was going incredibly smoothly.

So smoothly that Li Qi found it exceptionally strange.

He hadn't encountered a single obstacle. He was returning with the grain without a hitch.

Still, a smooth journey was a good thing. There was no point in going out of his way to look for trouble.

Having bid farewell to the increasingly eccentric Wang Boyan, Li Qi carried the grain and set out on his return journey.

---

Li Qi rode Old Ma, speeding through the sky.

As they traveled, he calculated the time.

In about six hours, he would be able to return to the White Rhino Army's location.

The entire round trip had taken just over forty hours—a little less than two days. There shouldn't be any issues.

However, as they flew, Old Ma's speed began to drop.

_Neigh!_ Old Ma's tongue lolled out as he glanced back at Li Qi with a miserable look in his eyes.

Li Qi understood his meaning at a single glance.

For the past forty-plus hours, Old Ma had been running almost without pause. First rushing to Vast Yang City, then sprinting everywhere to find grain, running through the night in that minor world, and then bolting straight back out to keep running until now.

He was on the verge of collapsing from exhaustion.

"Alright, alright, we'll rest!" Seeing Old Ma in such a state, Li Qi felt a sudden rush of embarrassment.

He quickly directed Old Ma to descend from the sky and land on the ground to rest for a while.

The terrain of Baiyue was highly mountainous. From the sky, the undulating landscape below didn't seem too imposing. But once on the ground, the rugged reality of the mountain forests became fiercely apparent.

These low-lying hills were all the same—they looked flat enough from above, but navigating them on foot taught a person the true meaning of suffering.

Traveling by land in a place like this was no easy feat.

Fortunately, Old Ma could fly.

If they had to actually run across the ground, the distance they needed to cover would at least double, perhaps even triple.

Land travel required constantly winding around mountain paths, whereas in the sky, they could fly in a straight line.

But no matter the route, Old Ma had been running for forty hours. Even a horse made of iron would have dropped dead from fatigue, and a dragon colt couldn't withstand such relentless squeezing either.

Landing on the ground, Li Qi patted Old Ma's neck. "We'll rest for two quarters of an hour. Then we'll push to make it all the way back in one go, and you can rest properly after that!"

Hearing this, Old Ma paused to think.

Then he spun around and delivered a vicious backward kick, planting his hoof squarely in the center of Li Qi's chest!

Li Qi hastily threw himself backward to dodge, narrowly avoiding having his clothes ripped open by the blow.

After kicking Li Qi away, Old Ma earnestly drew a horizontal line in the dirt with his hoof.

"One...? You want to rest for one double-hour[^1]?" Li Qi steadied himself, looking at the character for 'one' drawn on the ground.

_Neigh, neigh!_ Old Ma nodded solemnly.

"Fine, fine. One double-hour it is." Left with no choice, Li Qi conceded.

There was nothing else he could do.

To him, Old Ma was not a mount, but a friend.

He had been running for forty hours; a rest was well deserved. Li Qi knew that Old Ma wasn't just being lazy—he was genuinely exhausted.

It was just that Li Qi was feeling anxious.

Delaying by an hour wouldn't theoretically cause any problems, but he still worried about Shen Shuibi's situation.

Pushing those thoughts aside, Li Qi pulled out Old Ma's fodder from his mustard seed pouch. It consisted of dried beans, eggs, and an assortment of spirit flowers and seeds graciously provided by Jin Buhuan.

Out of the eight hundred spirit flowers, they still hadn't finished them all.

After mixing it evenly in a basin, Old Ma's meal was prepared. Li Qi sat to the side, waiting for Old Ma to eat and rest.

Having sat down with nothing else to do, Li Qi began his daily reading time.

Studying was cultivating; it required relentless daily effort to yield any harvest.

As Old Ma ate and Li Qi read, sunlight slanted down through the gaps in the leaves. The Tyndall effect created beautiful pillars of light, contrasting sharply with the deep shade of the canopy.

Amidst the mountain forests of Baiyue, Li Qi had unexpectedly found a rare moment of tranquility.

This peace did not last long, however. A short while later, Li Qi sensed a surge of demon qi approaching him.

It was a very fresh, clean demon qi.

Indeed, demon qi could be fresh. After all, demons were a specific category of cultivators. Those who truly exuded the foul stench of baleful qi and bloodlust were generally called devils.

Speaking of which, Li Qi had always found it strange. Why were so many different animals collectively referred to as 'demons'? Who had coined such a sweeping term?

It couldn't just be an abrupt, unspoken consensus, right?

Cultivators of the Dragon race, cultivators of the Human race, and even certain innate anomalies or spirits born of heaven and earth were not called demons, nor did they possess demon qi.

Yet, when ordinary flora and fauna cultivated, they were invariably labeled as demons. Furthermore, they genuinely radiated 'demon qi'—even if they practiced cultivation methods from entirely different Daoist traditions.

This point was truly bizarre, as if some entity in the unseen world had decreed it to be so.

But Li Qi hadn't read broadly enough yet, so the answer eluded him.

Digressions aside, Li Qi stopped reading and looked toward the source of the demon qi.

The demon qi was exceptionally faint and crisp, typical of a minor demon that had only just gained sentience.

Opening his eyes, he spotted a butterfly flapping its wings vigorously as it flew toward him.

"A butterfly? Now that's rare." Li Qi was slightly taken aback.

It was rarer for plants to become demons than beasts, and rarer still for insects.

Insects generally lived short lives. As the saying went, a summer insect cannot discuss ice; many bugs never even saw winter. Let alone mayflies, which are born in the morning and die by dusk, their adult lifespans lasting a mere few hours.

The same was true for butterflies. The entire process, from caterpillar to butterfly, spanned just a month or two. Some long-lived species might endure, but their maximum lifespan was merely a year.

To become a demon within such a narrow window of time was incredibly difficult.

Li Qi could tell that this butterfly demon had been cultivating for some time and had broken free from its natural lifespan constraints. It had likely lived for five or six years, attained spiritual sentience, and was on the verge of shapeshifting.

The early cultivation speed of insects was generally quite rapid.

This wasn't due to any special abilities or racial talents. Rather, the slow ones died of old age. Only those with extraordinary aptitude who cultivated swiftly could survive long enough to attain sentience.

It was survivor's bias.

"Hm? How did you get injured? This wound has severed your path to shapeshifting?" Li Qi's brows furrowed as he realized the butterfly was flying over to seek help.

"I see. It is not easy for a butterfly to cultivate. Since we met today, I will lend you a hand," Li Qi said.

He raised his hand, allowing the butterfly to approach and alight upon his palm.

The butterfly demon already possessed sentience; it couldn't shapeshift yet. Understanding Li Qi's intent, it immediately landed on his index finger.

After examining it, Li Qi discovered that the butterfly had had too much of its essence qi drained away.

Though he didn't know the culprit, having identified the root cause, he proceeded to treat the condition.

"In early spring, the rains arrive. This water carries the generative qi of the spring sun, yet it harmonizes with the plum rains. It stews in damp heat—people catch illnesses from this qi, and objects grow moldy. It holds little benefit for humanity, but it is excellent for insects and fungi. As the saying goes, 'spring snowmelt breeds insects and easily spoils'; this is the principle behind it."[^2]

"Open your internal organs. Although it is not spring, and there are no natural plum rains of heaven and earth, I will merge the vitality of the Azure Dragon's divine qi with the spring qi of my personal cosmos to generate spring water. This will be able to replenish your depleted essence qi."

However, as Li Qi formed the spring water, he added, "But you must be careful not to absorb too much. It is better to let a portion of the damage heal naturally. 'Loss' is a sign of shedding rigidity to gain flexibility. In nature, nothing exists without loss. Only when the body suffers a loss can new life emerge. Within this cycle of loss and birth lies metabolism—this is the natural Dao of flourishing and decaying."

The butterfly was drinking, swallowing the pure water Li Qi had conjured into its body.

Then, it heard Li Qi's words.

The butterfly froze. Its wings, which had been constantly fluttering to maintain balance, halted, and the insect tumbled downward.

"Hm?" Li Qi murmured in confusion.

Suddenly, a burst of spiritual light flared. Spiritual qi radiated outward into the surroundings. The tiny butterfly had broken through, transforming into a pupa brimming with vibrant life force, seemingly incubating something within.

It was as if it had attained a sudden epiphany. Armed with a sufficient foundation and aided by Li Qi's spring plum water, this breakthrough allowed it to successfully begin shapeshifting, entering a chrysalis state.

Li Qi was surprised at first, then burst into laughter. "Hahaha! A butterfly's shapeshifting is the act of transforming the old into the primeval, pushing away the stale to bring forth the new. A larva becomes a butterfly, and a butterfly becomes a demon, shapeshifting into another creature of innate sentience. It is all the same principle! It seems your foundation was already solid enough. My single sentence enlightened you, allowing you to truly become a demon. Good, good! Then I will lend you one more hand!"

With that, Li Qi mobilized the qi of the four seasons from within his personal cosmos. "The renewal of an old body is like the shifting of the four seasons. One must harmonize with the four seasons to regulate cold and heat. I will use the qi of the four seasons to establish your foundation."

Li Qi's two consecutive interventions were perfectly measured—neither excessive nor lacking. They provided the exact catalyst needed for the transformation. The pupa split open, and a young girl emerged from within.

The girl appeared to be around twelve or thirteen years old, radiating the innocent bloom of early youth. Being the shapeshifted form of a butterfly, she was naturally extraordinarily beautiful. Her entire back was covered in a sprawling tattoo of butterfly wings, suggesting she had drawn them into her body to achieve this form.

However, having only just completed her transformation, she was entirely naked without a single thread to her name. Seeing this, Li Qi hurriedly covered his eyes.

Look not at what is improper!

Inexpressibly overjoyed by her successful shapeshifting, the little girl immediately dropped to her knees and kowtowed to Li Qi. "Thank you, benefactor, for piercing the fog of my confusion and helping me shapeshift! I... I..."

Having just assumed human form, she possessed neither a name nor the vocabulary to articulate graceful pleasantries. She stammered and stalled, trembling with excitement but unable to voice the words.

"Stop. Put some clothes on before you speak," Li Qi commanded, not allowing her to continue. He hastily dug out a spare set of his own clothes and tossed them to the butterfly-girl.

Yet, in that exact moment, Li Qi sensed something.

_Wait. There was a peculiar thread attached to the butterfly's body, continuously drawing away her essence qi?_

The young butterfly-girl draped Li Qi's garments over herself, wrapping her body up like a cloak.

Then, she kowtowed once more. "Thank you, benefactor! I am eternally grateful. I... I don't know what to say. I only beg my benefactor to grant me a name. I wish to engrave it upon my heart for the rest of my life, and I will not dare to forget it!"

But Li Qi cut her off sharply. "Wait, leave that for now. Why is this thread attached to your body? This is the tether of a landscape grand array. Have you witnessed any blood sacrifices nearby!?"

[^1]: The unit used here in "shichen." 1 _shichen_ = 2 _hours_
[^2]: Spring is associated with rising Yang energy and rapid growth. When this explosive vitality mixes with extreme humidity of the East Asian "plum rains" (late spring monsoons), it creates a pathogenic "damp-heat" environment. Unlike pure winter snow, which was traditionally gathered to brew tea, spring snowmelt is considered highly impure because this intense generative energy causes it to quickly stagnate, breed insects, and spoil.
