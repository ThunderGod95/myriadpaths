# Chapter 153: Interception

Jin Buhuan took his first step forward.

Li Qi met him with a smile.

Second step. Both men looked at each other amicably, their expressions conveying mutual admiration and approval.

Step by step, the two drew closer in a friendly manner.

They walked until they were face-to-face.

Li Qi lowered his voice. "Brother Jin, lean in closely. I have something to tell you."

Jin Buhuan leaned in, offering his ear.

In a split second, Li Qi violently struck out!

A completely unhesitating sneak attack.

Yet, in the very next instant, Jin Buhuan had somehow raised his innate divine weapon well in advance.

With a crisp _ding_, he blocked Li Qi's ambush.

"Sigh, Brother Li, you've thrown away the chance I gave you," Jin Buhuan shook his head.

"Did you forget that I am a metal spirit? Metal governs slaughter; I am extremely sensitive to things like killing intent," he sighed.

Li Qi thought he had hidden it perfectly, suppressing his killing intent entirely. He had even ensured that the emotions radiating from the outward expansion of his personal cosmos were gentle and natural.

But as a metal spirit, Jin Buhuan could still clearly sense his killing intent.

However, he felt it was perfectly normal. Harboring killing intent didn't necessarily mean one would act upon it. Countless people in this world harbored killing intent toward him, yet the vast majority remained utterly deferential in his presence.

After all, people couldn't just let their emotions dictate their actions.

Thus, the magnanimous Young Master Jin decided to give Li Qi a chance.

If he passed, Li Qi would be a worthy subordinate or ally.

If he failed...

Then he could just go die.

While Jin Buhuan was busy sighing, Li Qi wasn't standing idle.

Without hesitation, he launched a barrage of ferocious attacks.

The Carp Stream Intent erupted! Li Qi's fist tore through the air, whistling as it smashed into Jin Buhuan's shoulder blade! The immense force dislocated the joint, leaving Jin Buhuan momentarily unable to move.

Jin Buhuan forcefully endured the strike, not even batting an eye as he continued his lamentation over Li Qi's choice.

Li Qi's next attack arrived in the blink of an eye.

His other hand drew a tang saber, slashing at Jin Buhuan with a ruthless momentum and explosive speed.

Jin Buhuan had a layer of protective golden light around him, but the tang saber easily sliced through it. The whistling sound of it tearing through the air was incredibly ear-piercing, brimming with murderous intent.

However, just like the scenario from before...

Jin Buhuan's body violently erupted with terrifying energy, transforming him once again into his tiger-headed, human-bodied form.

His strength and agility instantly jumped a tier. The aura of authority and threat radiating from him was almost palpable—unbelievably dense!

The ensuing shockwave shoved Li Qi backward.

Just like before, Li Qi maintained his horse stance as he was pushed back fifty feet. His feet seemed rooted to the ground, plowing two deep trenches in the earth.

And the following sequence of events played out exactly as it had before.

A flash of golden light.

Silent, traceless.

A paper-thin streak of metal qi swept past Li Qi's throat before he could even mount a meaningful reaction.

He couldn't even perceive it.

In the next moment, his head would be severed.

This was a tactic Jin Buhuan had executed countless times, and it had never failed.

Unsurprisingly, Li Qi's head was indeed severed.

The head is the convergence of all yang energy; severing it guarantees death.

Except, it was severed, but not completely severed.

Li Qi's charge did not halt!

"Wait, it's the Shaman substitution technique! Young Master, be careful!" Elder Si instantly saw through Li Qi's trick and called out!

Li Qi's speed suddenly skyrocketed. His head regrew, and the severed head on the ground transformed into a bloodstained block of wood.

Jin Buhuan immediately prepared to dodge.

But caught in close-quarters combat with a Rank 9 of the Martial Dao, how could he possibly evade?

Caught off guard for a mere moment, Jin Buhuan was pierced through the body by the tang saber.

For some unknown reason, the blade of the tang saber was now wreathed in a pitch-black halo of light. A strange qi flooded into Jin Buhuan's wound. A chilling cold constructed of shadows enveloped his heart and began spreading through his blood vessels to his limbs, bringing with it agony, weakness, and tremors.

With a heavy thud, Jin Buhuan collapsed to the ground. As the black energy spread throughout his body, the expression on his face gradually shifted from confusion to shock. The creeping shadows increasingly paralyzed him.

His body went rigid involuntarily, his complexion turned a sickly, ashen blue, and cold sweat poured from his entire body. It felt as though something was violently squeezing his heart, threatening to crush it completely with just a slight squeeze. His ability to control his own body began to fade away.

_Ghost qi enters the body, divine soul fails to attach._

_The soul unattached to the body, as if plummeting, as if falling._

This was the very ghost qi of the Rank 8 Elder Li from back then!

Seeing this, Elder Si erupted in fury!

To think a mere Rank 9 petty Shaman managed to successfully pull off a sneak attack right under the nose of a Rank 8 Shaman like himself!

"Damned brat!" Elder Si immediately struck out, preventing Li Qi from delivering the finishing blow.

On the other side...

Li Qi had intended to land one final whip strike on Jin Buhuan's divine soul. But before he could act, five malicious ghosts surged toward him from all directions!

One was covered head to toe in blade wounds, its flesh flayed open to reveal interconnected white bones. With all its skin and muscle gone, it was nonetheless charging toward Li Qi with great strides.

Another was covered in contusions, as if it had been beaten alive into a bloody pulp by clubs. Not a single inch of its skin or bones remained intact. Lacking both skin and bone, it could only squirm forward using its mangled flesh, yet its speed was terrifyingly swift.

A third was deathly pale all over, its skin swollen from soaking in water; a single press would leave a deep indent. Its entire body exhibited signs of post-mortem bloat as it dragged its massive, distended belly forward.

A fourth was engulfed in flames that tore at the surrounding air. The fire burned fiercely, alternating with intense waves of heat. Its skin, flesh, tendons, fat, organs, bones, and marrow were all being scorched, inflicting unbearable agony.

The fifth was completely stiff, looking like a dried corpse. Parched and famished, there wasn't a drop of moisture left in its body. Its belly contained nothing but yellow earth, yet it could neither drink nor eat.

Li Qi instantly abandoned all offensive actions and retreated explosively! He barely dodged the ghosts' initial pounce.

He recognized them: these were the malicious ghosts of the five elements.

This was exactly how Elder Si had used the ghost qi of these five elemental ghosts to curse Piaoxu.

If they touched him, he would be instantly dragged into the purgatory of the five elements, forced to suffer the endless torment of the five ghosts, tasting every ounce of agony they bore without any hope of release.

But the five elemental ghosts immediately resumed their pursuit of Li Qi, while Elder Si rushed to Jin Buhuan's side.

Elder Si had also realized that ghost qi had invaded Jin Buhuan's body, causing his body and soul to separate.

Fortunately, he was a Ghost Shaman, so dealing with ghost qi was not difficult for him.

He examined Jin Buhuan carefully and discovered that the ghost qi within him was dormant, not yet active. However, his soul aura was highly agitated and unable to reattach to his body; his divine soul was on the verge of detaching entirely.

_Rank 8 ghost qi! What incredibly high quality!_

He immediately took out a stalk of pill grass, lit it, and waved it over Jin Buhuan's head like incense.

The aroma of pill grass calmed the mind and stabilized the corporeal soul. It specifically warded off evil qi, misfortune, and ghost poison, and could treat intense fever.

Ghost Shamans always kept these on hand to prevent backlash from the malicious ghosts surrounding them.

He lit the pill grass and used shamanic methods to offer the incense, successfully expelling the ghost qi. Jin Buhuan erupted into a violent fit of coughing as the chilling sensation throughout his body completely dissipated.

"Kill him!" Jin Buhuan roared loudly, consumed by uncontrollable rage!

_I already gave him a chance!_

_I gave him face, but he didn't want it!_

_Then go die!_

Elder Si nodded. "Although my five elemental malicious ghosts are all Rank 9, when arrayed together, they possess the combat power of a Rank 8. He won't last long... What's going on!?"

Elder Si was dumbfounded. He looked up in utter astonishment, only to realize that one of his five elemental malicious ghosts had already been dispersed!

He had exhausted his lifeblood and spent decades finally gathering all five elemental malicious ghosts. Yet, in their very first clash, within less than five breaths, someone had actually beaten one of them to death?

How was that possible!?

The malicious ghosts of the five elements were formed from souls that died tragically through methods related to the five elements. The souls themselves needed to possess a certain level of cultivation base. Their deaths had to be excruciatingly painful, and their resentment overwhelmingly massive to be used as materials.

Then, the materials were taken and tempered using the Yin qi of the Nether Court. They were slowly refined and ground down until their consciousness was entirely erased, then arranged as array cores.

Through painstaking, daily effort, they were forged into a mobile array. The five ghosts hidden within were incredibly ferocious. While their raw destructive power wasn't particularly high, they excelled in insidious cruelty and were practically impossible to defend against. Furthermore, they were bizarre, hidden, and difficult to detect. Formed into an array, they could match a Rank 8; against a Rank 9, they would almost certainly score an instant kill.

This was Elder Si's ultimate trump card.

But why? What was going on?

What kind of Rank 9 could whip one of his five ghosts to death in a single clash!?

It was important to know that the greatest strength of the five elemental malicious ghosts lay in the completeness of the five elements. That was what allowed them to form an array and cycle endlessly.

If the array was broken, they were nothing more than five peak Rank 9 malicious ghosts.

While Elder Si was reeling in shock, Li Qi's side of the battlefield appeared relaxed and effortless.

_A Rank 8 dharma treasure?_

_Did you think you were the only ones who had one?_

_Did you think Jin Buhuan was the only one with Rank 8 metal qi, and you, a Ghost Shaman, were the only one with five elemental malicious ghosts comparable to Rank 8?_

_I, Li Qi, have one too! And the quality is far higher!_

Li Qi flourished the willow branch in his hand. With a single crack of the whip, he lashed the leading metal-element malicious ghost directly into a wisp of green smoke!

The willow branch, imbued with a sliver of the Grand Invocator's divine qi, had reached the absolute peak of Rank 8. Not even the metal qi in Jin Buhuan's hand could compare; only if the two streaks of metal qi merged their yin and yang could they surpass it.

Moreover, the only reason this willow branch was relegated to Rank 8 was simply because its material was merely a branch Young Master Zhu had casually snapped off a tree, which naturally imposed a limitation on its power.

The reason a willow branch was chosen, besides its symbolic meaning of "plucking a willow branch to bid farewell," was likely just because there happened to be a willow tree nearby at the time.

However, Li Qi had kept this item tightly concealed, never once revealing the slightest hint of its power.

The moment he stepped out the door and invited Jin Buhuan to meet him, preparing to challenge him, he had already plotted out this exact scenario.

From the time he locked himself in the inn, remaining silent for hours, to the moment he finally pushed the door open, he had completely mapped out his every move.

Elder Si was a Ghost Shaman. When it came time to kill Jin Buhuan, if malicious ghosts appeared, this item would catch them completely off guard!

The might of the Grand Invocator's divine qi was unrivaled. A single whip strike instantly shattered one of the five elemental malicious ghosts.

Li Qi immediately continued whipping. The branch moved like a dragon, devoid of any martial routine; it was purely chaotic, indiscriminate lashing.

But even so, all five malicious ghosts were completely dispersed!

Elder Si coughed up a mouthful of blood. With the total annihilation of the five elemental malicious ghosts, his divine soul suffered a severe backlash due to its connection to them.

The five ghosts were linked to his divine soul, allowing him to command them as easily as his own limbs.

A portion of the impact suffered by the five ghosts reverberated back to him, hitting him with waves of dizzying concussions.

Li Qi seized this opportunity and sprinted outward with explosive speed.

"Damned brat! You will pay with your life!" In his rage, Elder Si directly cast Jin Buhuan aside, throwing the weakened young master away before violently clenching his fist toward Li Qi.

Within his grasp was a wisp of qi.

This wisp of qi carried a faint trace of Li Qi's aura, as well as a drop of his blood.

The blood had been hastily snatched from the wooden substitute Li Qi had used moments ago.

Using blood to locate the target and mimicking the aura of life—this was the Shaman Dao's curse of "Capture the Living, Substitute for the Dead."

Techniques like stabbing straw dolls or smashing stone figures were all manifestations of this method. The core principle was linking the target's life force to another object; destroying the object would then indirectly extinguish the target's life.

The fundamental principle was exactly the same as Li Qi's substitution technique—making another object bear the consequences—just with the process reversed.

Yet, when this wisp of aura was crushed, Li Qi showed no reaction. Instead, he accelerated, running even further away.

Conversely, the wooden substitute on the ground shattered completely.

Elder Si instantly realized what had happened.

_He anticipated this! So he deliberately left an opening, baiting me into taking the blood, but the blood was actually fake!_

Meanwhile, as Li Qi ran, he continued to execute the next phase of his plan.

Deceiving Elder Si had been well within his expectations.

The Shaman Dao aimed to maintain balance and harmony among all things in the world. The prerequisite for achieving this was understanding all things.

If one couldn't even achieve understanding, how could they balance anything?

Therefore, the Shaman God Mountain's training manual... that [Book of Invocation], recorded countless foundational concepts. It was roughly equivalent to a Shaman Dao version of a universal encyclopedia.

And Li Qi, utilizing his dao rhythm, had memorized every single bit of it.

Compared to the orthodox Invocator inheritance of Shaman God Mountain...

Elder Si? A Rank 8 Shaman?

_Hehe, nothing but an illiterate._

But Elder Si knew none of this. He only knew he had failed twice, and that his opponent also possessed a Rank 8 dharma treasure, seemingly specialized against divine souls.

A Rank 8 failing against a Rank 9 twice?

It was an utter insult.

He got serious and struck out once more.

Elder Si raised two fingers and swiped them aggressively. A miniature altar appeared before him, its candles igniting spontaneously.

He cupped his hands in a raised salute and chanted, "I invite the Lord of Earth and the calamitous spirits to manifest. I trouble you with this task; there will certainly be a reward afterward."

The Lord of Earth was the lowest tier of Earthly Divinity—essentially a local land god.

Calamitous Spirits referred to minor ghosts that flew about causing mischief.

Using a deity invocation method, Elder Si demanded these two ghost-gods manifest to intercept the fleeing Li Qi.

He needed these two ghost-gods to pinpoint Li Qi's location so he could cast his spell and curse him to death once more. This time, escape would be impossible.

Li Qi noticed this as well.

However, Elder Si's action took the time of a single sentence—roughly a few seconds.

A few seconds was enough for him to flee over a mile away. The Martial Dao lacked many things, but its instantaneous burst speed was truly phenomenal.

But the terrain began to grow rugged, becoming increasingly steep, slowing him down more and more.

Fireflies also began to appear in the surroundings, making him increasingly conspicuous.

However, Li Qi suddenly stopped.

He halted and looked back. _This should be enough, right?_

Just as Li Qi stopped and looked up at the sky...

As expected, fiery clouds suddenly ignited on the horizon.

Jin Buhuan revealed a triumphant expression.

A vortex formed of flames suddenly materialized and engulfed Elder Si.

Accompanied by a violent explosion and a scorching shockwave...

Jin Buhuan and Li Qi simultaneously shouted aloud, "Lord! Yan!"
