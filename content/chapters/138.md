# Chapter 138: Future Arrangements

After pondering for a moment, Li Qi decided he couldn't just listen to one side of the story.

As an Invocator, he always existed as a middleman. Even if he were to uphold justice for the sake of 'righteousness,' he should only act after hearing both sides.

"Let's wait and see for now. We will be staying here in the meantime, so there's no rush," Li Qi said. "If the culprit doesn't return, we won't interfere any further. After staying here for a while, we'll take our leave."

"If they do come, I will make my own judgment."

After stating this, Li Qi added, "Your injuries are no longer life-threatening, but you need to rest. Let your _Chang_ Ghost handle the business. Focus on recuperating."

With that, Li Qi prepared to stand up and leave.

It was just a trivial matter to begin with. If there was no follow-up, he was ready to wash his hands of it here.

Shen Shuibi also rose at the right moment, following Li Qi out the door.

Piaoxu immediately called out, "Thank you, Sir, for saving my life. If you don't mind, please stay here in peace. I will surely attend to your needs with the utmost care..."

"Your food is pretty good," Li Qi remarked over his shoulder before pushing the door open and leaving.

Hearing this, Piaoxu was overjoyed and immediately lowered her head in respectful farewell.

She knew this meant they had agreed to stay.

Stepping out the door, Li Qi saw a small red-furred fox and a doctor carrying a medical box. The doctor was still sweating profusely, seemingly having just arrived.

Only then did Li Qi recall that the _Chang_ Ghost had shouted earlier, telling 'Qi Ya' to fetch a doctor.

"Ah, Doctor, the patient is inside. Hurry in," Li Qi said, pointing the way.

"Thank you, thank you..." The doctor hastily expressed his gratitude and rushed inside with his medical box.

The small red-furred fox also bowed repeatedly. Though it couldn't speak, it still expressed its gratitude for Li Qi pointing the way.

Li Qi shrugged and pulled the rabbit back to their room.

They still had to do their daily cultivation exercises.

Cultivation was just like that.

There weren't that many sudden epiphanies or fortuitous encounters.

There was only the ceaseless, day-in and day-out grinding effort, stretching over decades, centuries, or even millennia.

After the doctor rushed inside, he saw Piaoxu lying on the ground, being tended to by her husband.

He rushed over, immediately squatted down, opened his medical box, and began to examine her and take her pulse without a single word of nonsense.

The patient was still lying on the floor. Hadn't he run all the way here just to save a life?

But the moment he began his diagnosis, he froze.

Following that, the doctor exclaimed in a hushed whisper, "The... the five evils invaded her body, and someone actually resolved it? And there are absolutely no side effects! How is that possible!"

"Doctor? Is something wrong?" Piaoxu frowned. She still hadn't realized exactly what she had just experienced.

"What's wrong? An injury like this, even the City Lord of Clear Mist City couldn't handle it so cleanly! Who treated you!? Was it those two who just walked out? Who are they!?" The doctor grabbed Piaoxu, his expression frantic, firing off question after question. He looked as if he had completely lost control of himself.

And it was impossible to maintain control.

The strongest cultivator of the Medicine Dao in all of Clear Mist City was the Clear Mist Sect Master, Hu Xian'er. She simultaneously cultivated the Medicine Dao and demonic arts, having reached Rank 7. Once, someone had metal qi invade their body to the point where even their divine soul began to crystallize into metal, yet she managed to save them.

She was also the most skilled doctor in treating incised wounds and external trauma within a ten-thousand-li radius. Even the imperial physicians of the Green Mountain Kingdom could not compare to her in this regard.

But the invasion of the five evils, each with its own strange characteristics and interlocking with the rest... someone had suffered this exact injury before. They had paid a massive price to invite her to intervene. In the end, their life was saved, but their cultivation was completely ruined because their personal cosmos had been utterly destroyed by the qi of the five evils. Their path of cultivation was permanently severed.

Yet now, someone possessed medical skills even more brilliant than hers? Someone who had resolved the affliction entirely without any side effects?

Which divine physician of the Medicine Dao was passing through?

One had to remember, the Clear Mist Sect Master was a Rank 7 expert. What kind of immortal figure was that?

Yet now, someone had surpassed her, and they seemed to have done it effortlessly.

Seeing the doctor's reaction, even Piaoxu seemed to understand what she had just encountered.

_Those two... have massive backgrounds?_

_No wonder they dared to meddle._

It was a known fact that when traveling, the most troublesome thing was meddling in others' affairs. Drawing one's sword to help upon seeing an injustice was ultimately not a common occurrence.

Therefore, Piaoxu immediately pulled the doctor closer and lowered her voice, "Doctor, please listen to me—"

Piaoxu knew in her heart that while those two might not fear exposing their identities, she absolutely could not leak the information. Otherwise, not only would she bring trouble to them, but she would also invite a catastrophic disaster upon herself.

"I can introduce you to that person, but you must swear an oath upon your sect that you will not leak a word. Otherwise, you should know the power of such figures. If they place the blame, I fear both of us will die without a burial place," Piaoxu warned in a hushed tone.

The doctor was calmed down by these words.

His previous fervent excitement was extinguished by reality. He knew that anyone capable of such a feat was bound to have immense influence.

"I understand. Thank you for the reminder, Proprietress. I will publicly announce that you merely suffered external injuries that I healed, and that the previous treatment was only to alleviate the pain. Even if there were witnesses earlier, I imagine they do not understand the Medicine Dao and will not grow suspicious," he replied immediately.

"As for the introduction, let's just forget it." The doctor sighed. "Just thinking about figures of that level terrifies me. We are nothing but sparrows pecking at flowers; how could we dare seek an audience with a dragon in the clouds? It would only invite disaster."

Both of them knew it was best not to delve too deeply into this matter. After making a mutual pact and swearing an oath, they dispersed as agreed. They publicly announced that the proprietress had only been cursed by enemies and the matter had been reported to the authorities, who would naturally investigate.

Upstairs, Shen Shuibi's ears twitched.

_So many people are talking about me._

_Mhm... but they're all praising me._

_Happy._

And so, carrying the cheerful mood of being praised after saving a life, the rabbit went back to pondering how to reforge her Yin Spirit.

As for Li Qi, he was actually just like the rest of the onlookers—he had no idea whatsoever just how shocking a feat the rabbit had accomplished.

After all, he didn't understand medicine either. He only knew the rabbit had saved someone. And over the past year, Shen Shuibi had actually done this quite a few times.

There were so many people who knew medicine; was it really that strange to have one by his side? The rabbit even knew alchemy. She carried a small alchemy cauldron with her and had stashed away quite a few pills.

_I'm better off reading more books._

Knowledge. In cultivation, knowledge was the most important thing.

Hailing from Shaman God Mountain, his greatest advantage wasn't some lofty status, but rather its vast ocean of books. Just the two books in his head were enough for him to study extensively.

_I should take advantage of this rest period to keep triggering them._

Of the three-million-li journey, he had already traveled over a hundred thousand miles. Tang Seng's entire journey to the West was roughly this distance.

He quickly cast Piaoxu's matters to the back of his mind and focused on real business.

Now that he had reached Rank 9, their speed wouldn't have to rely entirely on Old Ma. He first needed to figure out how to craft a few wind-traveling talismans here. With them, Old Ma's speed could be further increased.

If conditions allowed, he should also acquire a cultivation method for Old Ma.

Old Ma possessed a fine bloodline. With proper cultivation, the chances of him transforming from a wild beast into a demon were very high.

Their speed would be even faster then, and he could rush back to Shaman God Mountain even quicker.

He was a dragon colt, possessing excellent innate talent. It couldn't go to waste.

However, the prerequisite for all these plans was making more money.

Otherwise, he couldn't just resort to robbing, right?

---

No one came to disturb Li Qi. In the blink of an eye, it was already the second day.

Guided by his sixth sense, Li Qi had brought Shen Shuibi to Clear Mist City to rest and regroup.

He had three primary objectives for coming here.

The first was to rest. Running wildly through the wilderness for so long had exhausted both Old Ma and himself. They needed to find a city to properly rest, recover their physical and mental strength, and prepare to hit the road again.

The second was to find an opportunity to acquire some wind-traveling talismans.

These talismans were something he had recently read about in a book—tools that only Rank 9 cultivators could craft.

They weren't exactly rare. Aside from the meatheads of the Martial Dao, practitioners of other Daoist traditions could basically craft them as long as they could manipulate "wind." It simply depended on whether they knew how.

Clearly, Li Qi did not know how. Thus, he planned to buy them.

He had looked into it. Crafting these talismans required mastery over the qi of the Eight Winds.

The Eight Winds were heavenly qi. Heavenly qi transformed into frost, dew, wind, and rain.

Heavenly qi manifested as wind to correspond with the four seasons.

In the northeast blew the Scorching Wind, born of Gen qi; it governed heat, bringing scorching temperatures and drought wherever it passed.

In the east blew the Surging Wind, born of Zhen qi; it was the wind of the sea, often found along the coast.

In the southeast blew the Balmy Wind, born of Xun qi; it was a warm wind, intoxicating and deeply soothing to experience.

In the south blew the Colossal Wind, born of Li qi; it was ferocious, destroying houses, ruining crops, and also known as the tornado, capable of moving mountains and draining seas.

In the southwest blew the Bitter Wind, born of Kun qi; it was a cold wind, often accompanied by bitter rain and misfortune.

In the west blew the Soughing Wind, born of Dui qi; it was the wind of autumn, governing desolation, causing leaves to fall and fruits to ripen wherever it swept.

In the northwest blew the Fierce Wind, born of Qian qi; it was a fierce, unparalleled gale.

In the north blew the Frigid Wind, born of Kan qi; wherever it passed, all living things withered and vegetation perished.

One had to specifically study and comprehend these Eight Winds, harmonizing them with the pitch pipes and aligning them with the eight sounds to craft the wind-traveling talisman. Though common, it wasn't something one could learn purely through self-study. Therefore, he didn't plan to learn it himself; he would simply buy it.

(TL Note: To get a detailed breakdown of the Eight Winds: [The Eight Winds and the Harmonic Dao](/myriadpaths/mythology/eight-winds/))

As for its effects, they were simple and clear: it ensured that one always ran with the wind, reducing air resistance to the point where it practically vanished.

Naturally, this only applied below a certain speed limit. Go too fast, and it would lose its effectiveness.

Don't be fooled by its seemingly minor effect; it provided an astonishing boost to movement speed, generally increasing it by over twenty percent. Moreover, it was a passive effect. A single talisman could easily last half a year or more.

However, the talisman was extremely fragile. Even a mortal could tear it easily. In combat, unless the power gap was massive, it would shatter from the mere shockwaves. It had no combat value, but it was excellent for traveling.

Li Qi planned to buy three to five of them, attaching them to Old Ma, himself, and Shen Shuibi, while keeping the rest as spares. This would make Old Ma significantly faster.

His third objective was to find a cultivation method for Old Ma to see if he could start cultivating.

It didn't need to be amazing; as long as it helped him build a foundation and get started, that was enough.

After all, any cultivation method found in these parts wouldn't be top-tier anyway. Once they reached Shaman God Mountain, he could definitely find far better ones. For now, the goal was simply to give Old Ma a bit of cultivation, allowing him to transition from a normal beast into a demon.

With his decisions and plans finalized, the next step was execution.

The 'rest' objective was going quite well. He was very satisfied with this inn. Plus, not only had they not charged him yet, but Piaoxu had even returned the money he paid earlier. They were essentially eating and lodging for free.

Li Qi didn't refuse. After all, they had saved her life; he would treat it as their medical fee.

Refusing what one was rightfully owed was not in accordance with the principle of 'Righteousness.'

Li Qi had determined his path to be the righteous one. He would not engage in self-indulgent, superficial kindness, as that would only burden both sides.

The Dao of Righteousness emulated the movement of the heavens—centered, impartial, and unbiased.

Just as he was pondering his goals, a knock sounded at the door.

"Come in," Li Qi called out, breaking his train of thought.

Shen Shuibi twitched her nose and lifted her head happily. "Breakfast is here!"

Indeed. Li Qi also knew it had to be Piaoxu delivering food.

The tender-pig, Four Beauties Soup, and bamboo shoots stir-fried with fatty meat they had eaten right upon arriving had all been cooked by Piaoxu herself. It was clear her culinary skills were superb, capable of turning ordinary ingredients into incredible delicacies.

Last night's dinner had also been a feast of exquisite flavors. Thus, Li Qi was quite curious about their second meal here.

"Sir, Madam, here is today's breakfast. Please enjoy," Piaoxu said, bringing the food in herself.

Shen Shuibi could hardly wait. The food was genuinely delicious, making it one of the rare things capable of stirring her emotions.

Most of the time, the rabbit looked rather spaced out, but she was actually deep in thought. Anything that could make her temporarily abandon her contemplation was undoubtedly a high-quality item.

Li Qi looked over curiously, inspecting the new dishes for the day.

The main course today was congee. But this was no ordinary congee; it was steeped in the floral essences of roses, citrons, and osmanthus. The water and rice blended perfectly, much like rice fermented into wine, emitting a refreshing fragrance.

Accompanying the congee was a side dish made from the meat of chicken, fish, and shrimp. The meat had been sun-dried until perfectly brittle, shredded into fine threads, and thoroughly mixed with fresh bamboo shoots, fragrant mushrooms, sesame seeds, and Sichuan peppercorns. It was savory and fresh without being greasy—the perfect pairing for congee.

Then there were six or seven goose feet, plump, sweet, and nearly an inch thick. Resting in the soup bowl, they smelled distinctly of common and crucian carps. It seemed the broth had been boiled using these fish, seasoned with melon and ginger. Once the broth turned as white as milk, the goose feet were added and simmered vigorously until tender. Consequently, the rich flavor was entirely locked within the webs, not a single drop wasted.

Such a modest breakfast, yet brimming with variety. Li Qi couldn't help but marvel.

"Miss Piaoxu, your craftsmanship must surely be the foundation of this inn's appeal. To maintain a foothold in a gold-squandering den like Clear Mist City for fifteen years with such a tranquil little shop... it truly possesses its own unique charm," Li Qi praised after taking a bite.

"Right, right, right!" Shen Shuibi hastily raised her chopsticks in agreement. She grabbed a goose foot and buried her head, nibbling away.

"It is but a minor culinary trick. I am glad you enjoy it, Sir," Piaoxu smiled bashfully. Then she asked, "May I ask what arrangements you two have for today? I have lived in the city for a long time and am familiar with the area. I can save you some detours."
