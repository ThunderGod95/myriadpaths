# Chapter 168: Rat Soil Mound

The two sides finally relaxed and began to introduce themselves.

Li Qi's introduction was simple. He claimed to be a shaman who had experienced a few fortuitous encounters and hailed from the Great Deer Kingdom. The Daoist Sects cultivator beside him was Shen Shuibi, his good friend. The two were traveling together to the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest, intending to claim the heads of two fiends.

As for his lineage, he directly glossed over it, neither confirming nor denying if he had one. As long as he didn't say it, it wasn't a lie.

Zhang Lan also began his introduction.

Only then did Li Qi understand Zhang Lan's circumstances.

This plain-looking fellow named Zhang Lan was a Swordmaster of the Righteous Sword Palace.

The so-called Swordmaster was an official position within the Righteous Sword Palace.

In the beginning, the Palace Master of the Righteous Sword Palace paid a hefty price to invite an artifact refiner to forge three hundred and sixty-five Heavenly Cycle Starry Swords to match the heavenly cycle. All of them were Rank 8 weapons. Following this, the position of Swordmaster was established.

Any disciple who cultivated to Rank 8 and did not wish to stay within the sect to hold an administrative role could apply for the position of Swordmaster. After passing numerous assessments, they would receive one of these precious swords and learn a corresponding formation.

Swordmasters typically wandered the outside world, upholding justice and spreading the renown of the Righteous Sword Palace. They were also responsible for gathering genius disciples to bring back to the mountain. Essentially, they acted as the sect's public face and handled external affairs.

After all, many external matters were far from simple.

Although standard disciples also traveled down the mountain to gain experience, their cultivation was too shallow to handle major events.

Other Rank 8 cultivators all had their own duties and held positions within the sect, making it difficult for them to leave their posts on a whim.

As for the higher-ups above them, it was even harder for them to make a move.

Therefore, the Swordmaster role existed. They received a sect sword to prove their identity and could share resources with one another. Tasked exclusively with roaming the world, they enjoyed a great deal of freedom.

As a Swordmaster, Zhang Lan was known for his eagerness to help others in need. He had also been heading to the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest to slay demons. However, he encountered many bizarre occurrences along the way. For instance, the border garrison of the Hundred Forests Kingdom had almost entirely withdrawn, yet the commoners were left behind. Only a small fraction of the army was honestly assisting with the evacuation.

Thus, he temporarily halted his journey to the demon nest to aid these people in their evacuation. The White Rhino Army was already the third army he had assisted.

Hearing this, Li Qi's mind filled with doubts. He asked, "Brother Zhang, is this... a widespread phenomenon?"

"What? Oh, you mean these migrating commoners? Yes, indeed. As far as I know, the entirety of the Hundred Forests Kingdom bordering the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest seems to be like this. I find it very strange as well," Zhang Lan answered.

"That's not right. What about their commanding general? I noticed earlier that the commander of an army should be at Rank 8, yet I haven't seen the main general here. An army without a general is like a host of dragons without a leader. How can this be?" Li Qi asked, perplexed.

Zhang Lan explained, "From what I've gathered, the main general of the White Rhino Army has temporarily left for the capital to report on his duties. Before leaving, he ordered these soldiers to assist in evacuating the populace. Seeing that they barely numbered ten thousand, I knew it would be far too difficult for them to relocate over a hundred thousand commoners, which is why I stepped in to help. _Sigh_, it is a pity that so many good men were buried in this battle. Now, only a little over six thousand remain."

"Bizarre, truly bizarre. Would a general reporting for duty arrange such a thing in advance?" Li Qi frowned. It was all too illogical, but with insufficient clues, he couldn't pinpoint the exact reason.

"I know it's bizarre as well, but regardless of the reason, we can't just abandon the commoners. Little Brother Li, how about we travel together for now? The three of us can escort these hundred thousand people to a safe place, and then we can head to the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest together to investigate the truth of the matter. Sister Shen, what do you think?" Zhang Lan looked at Shen Shuibi and asked.

In Zhang Lan's eyes, although Li Qi was not weak and appeared quick-witted and adaptable—even earning his admiration through his actions—he was still only Rank 9. If they were truly going to join hands and enter the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest, he still had to consult this woman who had been spacing out from beginning to end.

After all, she was the Rank 8.

Addressed so suddenly, Shen Shuibi was momentarily at a loss. She panicked for a split second before pointing at Li Qi. "Ask him. I'm following him."

Having said that, she hurriedly hid behind Li Qi.

Shen Shuibi's social anxiety was extremely severe.

Li Qi had long since experienced this firsthand.

Zhang Lan wore a stunned expression, quite surprised.

This woman was a dignified Rank 8. It was one thing to previously assume she and Li Qi were on equal footing, but looking at it now, it seemed she actually deferred to Li Qi?

Just what was the background of this Li Qi? He hadn't mentioned coming from a major sect in his introduction.

Even if he was from a major sect, the Dao Protector dispatched to guard him would certainly be a senior; it was absolutely impossible for them to follow his lead.

They didn't seem like a married couple either. Li Qi showed no intimacy toward this Miss Shen. In fact, his every word and action were quite restrained, utterly devoid of any impropriety, highlighting his strict adherence to courtesy and boundaries.

It was Miss Shen, instead, who frequently displayed natural, intimate gestures toward Li Qi. Whether it was dusting off his clothes, supporting him without shying away from physical contact, or occasionally stealing a glance at him, it was all incredibly natural, as if she were completely accustomed to it.

_Could it be..._

_Miss Shen was the one pursuing him?_

_This Li Qi really has some impressive skills._

With these thoughts, the look in Zhang Lan's eyes when he looked at Li Qi instantly changed.

Previously, he had felt profound respect for Li Qi's ability to preside over the sacrificial rite and his righteous willingness to step forward, which was why he was willing to address him as an equal brother.

But now, his gaze toward Li Qi held an even deeper level of reverence.

Li Qi felt a chill run down his spine. Why had Zhang Lan's gaze become so... uh, how should he describe it?

If he had to find an adjective, it would probably be _worship_?

What did he possess that was worthy of this man's worship?

Unable to figure it out, Li Qi coughed dryly to change the subject. "_Ahem_, Brother Zhang's words are reasonable. Then I will conduct another sacrifice to dispel the sickly qi here and improve the health of these commoners. We shall set off tomorrow."

In any case, he had plenty of time. Li Qi didn't mind a slight delay; it would take half a month at most.

He had spent nearly half a year traveling here from the Green Mountain Kingdom. A mere half month was no issue at all.

He was already a cultivator; his lifespan was beyond compare to an ordinary person's. He needed to gradually develop a proper sense of time step by step.

"You can actually perform a rite to dispel sickly qi? Truly impressive. Someone like me who only knows how to fight really cannot compare," Zhang Lan praised.

The Righteous Sword Palace was a Martial Dao sect. They cultivated the Martial Dao, and Zhang Lan was naturally a Rank 8 of the Martial Dao. However, he seemed to have dual-cultivated some other methods as well.

Hearing this, Li Qi voiced the doubt in his heart. "Right, speaking of which, Brother Zhang, I can see that you cultivate the Martial Dao. You indeed lack flashy methods. However, earlier I saw you employ the Greater Yang Lung Meridian Geng-Metal sword qi. That is clearly a Daoist Sects method. The weapon in your sword box should be the Heavenly Cycle Starry Sword, so why didn't you use it during the battle?"

If Zhang Lan followed the Martial Dao, it meant he hadn't revealed his true strength earlier. He had been holding back, not even drawing his sword. What was the meaning of this?

Zhang Lan burst into laughter upon hearing this and explained to Li Qi, "Haha! Little Brother Li, you don't understand this, do you? If I went all out from the start, those fiends absolutely wouldn't have fallen for my trap. They would have definitely prepared their defenses."

"By attacking with my secondary Daoist Sects methods, I made them misjudge my strength. Closing the distance at the critical moment with my Martial Dao cultivation and drawing my sword—that was where my sliver of survival lay. Kill one first, then fight the other to the death. According to my estimations, I had a thirty percent chance of counter-killing them and saving these hundred thousand people. Moreover, I had absolute certainty of dragging both of them down to the grave with me," Zhang Lan said, lifting his chin with confidence.

Only then did Li Qi reach a sudden realization.

Although the other party was reckless, anyone capable of cultivating to Rank 8 couldn't be entirely brainless. It turned out he had thought everything through before making a move.

However, Zhang Lan immediately added, "But this time, I still had to rely on you, Little Brother Li. _Sigh_, my Martial Dao divine will still isn't up to par. I didn't even notice someone else holding the line in the rear. If it weren't for you discovering the third one in advance, I'm afraid I would have truly perished this time."

Speaking of this, Zhang Lan asked curiously, "What's your deal, Little Brother? To be able to detect the concealment techniques of a Rank 8 fiend while only at Rank 9—how did you manage that?"

"I have a wisp of dao rhythm within my body that excels at observation. As long as there is the slightest trace, it absolutely cannot escape my eyes," Li Qi answered truthfully.

"So that's how it is." Having received a satisfactory answer, Zhang Lan asked no further questions. He stepped back a bit, preparing to end the conversation and begin meditating to regulate his breathing.

Li Qi, however, still had one unasked question, so he finally inquired, "Before you rest, Big Brother, could you resolve one more doubt for me? You Swordmasters travel the outside world year-round holding precious treasures. What happens if you lose the sword? For instance, if it's stolen, or if you simply die—what becomes of the sword?"

Hearing this, Zhang Lan laughed aloud as if he'd heard a joke. "What is there to worry about? If a Swordmaster encounters an accident outside, the sword will naturally fly back to the sect."

"Not only will it fly back, but before leaving, it will record the surrounding situation. Once it returns, the sect will handle it naturally. It's incredibly convenient. Even if you die, there's nothing to fear; someone will definitely avenge you!"

His tone was entirely unconcerned, leaving Li Qi momentarily speechless, with nothing to say in response.

"No one can stop it?" Li Qi asked again.

Zhang Lan replied, "To stop the self-return of a Heavenly Cycle Starry Sword requires at least a Rank 6. If a mighty Rank 6 expert acts against a Rank 8 like me, isn't that a massive loss of face for them? Besides, within a radius of a hundred thousand miles, every Rank 6 is a known figure. When the time comes, our sect will visit their doors one by one to ask. They will eventually be found."

Well then, Li Qi finally understood the operational style of the Righteous Sword Palace.

Truly worthy of a Martial Dao sect...

They were all more or less mentally deranged.

The two temporarily ended their conversation. Zhang Lan went to heal his wounds, while Li Qi spent the night setting up an altar.

What he was setting up this time was a sacrifice to dispel sickly qi, which he could handle alone.

He first laid out wine and food, then blew an ox horn and struck a gong. He wove multicolored silk threads with various flowers and medicinal herbs to form a floral wreath.

Then, he ordered the soldiers to have the commoners form their camp in a ring formation to rest, using the floral wreath to symbolize the human ring.

Next, he summoned metal qi and cut his own arm. Using his blood, he painted nine talismans. Eight were placed in the eight directions around the floral wreath, and one was set in the center.

He buried the talismans in the earth, covering them with dirt to a depth of two feet and five inches each.

The sacrificial rite was complete.

The qi of the medicinal herbs and the hundred flowers was pure.

The blood qi of the shaman was divine.

The two were supplemented by talismans. With the floral ring symbolizing the human ring, they were linked together through Shaman Dao methods. Resonating the pure qi of the flowers with the blood qi of the shaman, the qi of the hundreds of flowers and herbs dispersed throughout the entire ring of pitched tents, driving away the original sickly qi.

The shaman's blood qi transformed into phantom copies of Li Qi. Like divine generals, they guarded the eight directions and the center, preventing the sickly qi from returning.

Thus, the effect of mass disease-dispelling was achieved.

If the floral wreath and blood qi phantoms in this process were replaced with something else—such as various highly toxic herbs and the foul blood of fiends—then what would be unleashed was a curse born of sacrifice, capable of cursing these hundred thousand people to death.

Of course, whether it was cursing or dispelling sickly qi, the shaman himself had to bear all the consumption and consequences. In reality, it was not an easy task.

After Li Qi finished, he was utterly exhausted. He simply lay down on the ground and went to sleep.

Sleep was the best method for recovery.

Not sleeping was naturally possible, but it was akin to the personal cosmos lacking a night, which would only result in the defensive qi failing to enter the Yin.

If defensive qi could not enter Yin and constantly lingered in Yang, then Yin qi would become deficient. One would be perpetually agitated, the fires of the seven emotions would flare, and the mind would be restless.

Therefore, maintaining sleep was extremely important. As the saying goes, 'In wakefulness, the spirit dwells in the heart.' For this reason, anyone who forewent sleep would undoubtedly suffer side effects.

As cultivation increased, the personal cosmos became more robust, naturally allowing one to endure longer without sleep. However, slumber was ultimately necessary; otherwise, it would cast an adverse effect upon the personal cosmos.

Deep sleep and awakening were fundamentally the transformations of Yin and Yang, implicitly aligning with the transitions of heaven and earth in the external macrocosm. Forcibly reversing this wasn't impossible, but it was unnecessary.

Everyone went to rest. After the soldiers arranged for all the commoners to sleep as well, the dead of night soon arrived.

In the dead of night, only Shen Shuibi had not rested. In her hand, she clutched a silver-furred rat. It was precisely the Rank 8 fiend she had killed, the one that had been holding the line.

Even though it was the strongest among the three Rank 8s, it was truly insignificant in front of Shen Shuibi.

"Rat Soil Mound..." Shen Shuibi tilted her head, recognizing the creature's origins.

The so-called Rat Soil Mound was a unique cluster of `exotic beasts`.

It was recorded in the _Record of the Western Regions_: "In the middle of the main desert road, there are earthen mounds, named Rat Soil Mounds. The rats within are as large as hedgehogs, and their fur boasts strange colors of gold and silver. Whenever the chieftain of the swarm leaves the burrow to roam, the swarm of rats follows."

Long ago, two nations were at war. A great nation led an army of hundreds of thousands, plundering border cities until they encamped beside a rat mound.

The other, smaller nation possessed only tens of thousands of troops and was powerless to resist. However, they had long known of the peculiar rats in the desert and treated them as divine beings.

Within a few days of battle, the small nation's cities were conquered, its lands seized, and it faced utter annihilation. With nowhere to turn for help, the monarch and officials were terrified and bereft of plans. In a desperate act, they set up an altar, burned incense, and prayed to the rats, hoping the creatures possessed divine awareness and would lend a fraction of military might.

That night, the king of the small nation dreamed of a giant rat, which spoke: "Respectfully, I wish to assist you. Prepare your troops early. If you engage in battle at dawn, you shall certainly achieve victory."

Knowing he had divine protection, the small nation's king mobilized his horses, issued orders to his generals and soldiers, and marched before dawn, driving deep to launch a surprise attack.

Hearing of this, the great nation was overjoyed at the prospect of battle. Just as they prepared to saddle their mounts and don their armor for war, they suddenly discovered that their saddles, clothing, bowstrings, armor links, and every strap and fastening had been gnawed through by the rats.

The enemy troops had arrived, yet they were left completely unarmed. They had no choice but to surrender and be slaughtered. Thus, the small nation killed their generals and captured their soldiers.

The great nation was struck with terror, believing the small nation was protected by deities.

Deeply grateful for the rats' profound grace, the king of the small nation built a shrine and established sacrifices. Generation after generation adhered to this reverence, holding them in profound esteem. Thus, from the monarch down to the commoners, the entire nation practiced these sacrifices to seek blessings. Whenever they passed by the burrows, they would dismount and walk, bowing in respect and offering sacrifices for good fortune. Be it clothing, bows and arrows, or fragrant flowers and fine meals, as long as they offered their sincerity, they received many boons. If the sacrifices ceased, disaster would strike.

"This should be a cluster of exotic beasts only found in the western Buddhist Kingdom, even further west than Shaman God Mountain... How could they appear in the Baiyue region?" Shen Shuibi was somewhat puzzled.

Shaman God Mountain was only in the southwest of All Under Heaven, whereas the Buddhist Kingdom lay due west, at the extreme western edge of All Under Heaven.

"Forget it, perhaps they migrated here... I won't worry about it." She stored the silver-furred rat into her mustard seed pouch.

This thing was still somewhat useful to Li Qi.
