# Chapter 145: Coercion

The moment this treasure was revealed, Li Qi could feel gazes shooting over from across the entirety of Clear Mist City.

_One... two, three, four,_ Li Qi counted silently to himself.

_Nine... ten._

_Twenty-four, twenty-five._ Li Qi opened his eyes, finished with his counting.

A full twenty-five gazes. Furthermore, he estimated that every single one of them belonged to a Rank 8 expert. Otherwise, they would not have instilled such a sense of trepidation within him, to the point that his personal cosmos had begun to tremble slightly.

This was by no means a good omen.

Li Qi cupped his hands in greeting. "I must ask you to return these gifts, old sir. The matter between your young master and myself was nothing more than a minor misunderstanding. I cannot accept such an extravagant apology."

These treasures were astonishing to behold. Had it been anyone else, their head likely would have spun with greed.

It was as if a distant uncle had suddenly left behind an inheritance of two hundred million for you to claim; few could maintain their composure in the face of such a windfall.

But Li Qi possessed a clear and luminous Dao Heart, unmoved by external material wealth, so he refused without a second thought.

Money was unimportant to him. Furthermore, that long saber was equally useless to him.

He simply did not know how to wield a saber...

No matter how exquisite a weapon might be, what good was it if he couldn't use it?

Right now, his combat skills were largely limited to throwing punches.

So, what was the meaning behind Jin Buhuan suddenly offering him so many things? Was he truly afraid, hence this visit to offer an apology?

Highly unlikely. Even Li Qi didn't buy that excuse.

"This..." The etiquette official looked troubled. He hurriedly bowed and said in a trembling voice, "Please do not make things difficult for me, sir. If I fail to have you accept these gifts, the young master will certainly punish me."

"Punishment is a matter between you and your young master, something I cannot interfere with. However, I will do my best to plead for leniency on your behalf," Li Qi answered earnestly.

As he spoke, he retrieved a brush and paper, swiftly writing out a handwritten letter.

He always carried writing implements to record his insights and experiences. After all, the faintest ink was better than the best memory. Daily cultivation was not merely blind practice; it required constant contemplation.

Even a blunt path like the Martial Dao required comprehension and the integration of one's own thoughts during practice, let alone the Shaman Dao he cultivated.

This particular path was famously demanding of the intellect, requiring far more memorization than what a regular person could handle.

Fortunately, he had his cheats, sparing him from spending too much effort on rote memorization. But the time saved couldn't be spent idling; he still needed to deepen his understanding of the concepts.

Cultivation itself was a process of learning and elevation. Rote, mechanical training would never amount to anything significant.

Having finished writing, he tore off the page and handed it to the etiquette official. "Please accept this, old sir. Should your young master seek to punish you, show him this letter. This way, the blame will not fall upon your shoulders."

The official grew even more frantic. "Mr. Li, you do not understand... The young master, he—"

However, before the elderly official could finish his sentence, his entire body suddenly convulsed. A burst of roaring flames erupted, instantly swallowing him whole!

Li Qi instinctively moved to save him, but before he could marshal the qi within his body, the old man was consumed by the fire, reduced to drifting ash that scattered across the ground.

Li Qi's expression instantly darkened.

Right at this moment, a middle-aged man with red hair and a red beard descended from the sky.

It was none other than Lord Yan.

The left sleeve of Lord Yan's robe hung empty, a stark reminder of the arm Shen Shuibi had forced him to sever.

Offering a one-armed salute, he spoke impassively to Li Qi, "Mr. Li, I suggest you accept the gifts. The young master has decreed that should you refuse, it can only mean the etiquette official was lacking in manners. For a servant to offend a distinguished guest in such a way, they must be executed before your eyes as an apology. Otherwise, it would reflect poorly on our ability to discipline our subordinates."

Staring at this Rank 8 entity, fire qi surged within Li Qi's heart.

Consequently, his tone turned rigid. "Lord Yan, he was not lacking in manners. It is simply that I cannot casually accept these gifts..."

Before Li Qi could finish speaking, another figure stepped forward, trembling uncontrollably. It was a woman in her forties with graying hair. She shook like a leaf, her teeth chattering ceaselessly, yet she forced herself to grit her teeth and bow deeply toward Li Qi. "Please accept the gifts, sir."

Li Qi took a deep breath, turned around, and walked away.

Lord Yan waved his hand. The woman let out a wretched wail before she, too, was consumed by raging flames.

From within the procession of gift-bearers, a young man immediately rushed out, weeping bitterly and crying out for his mother.

By now, countless onlookers had gathered along the street.

Some could not bear the sight, shedding tears and sighing softly.

Some were furious but dared not voice their curses, able only to glare at Lord Yan.

Some watched Li Qi with keen interest, curious to see what choice he would make next.

Still others hurried away, seemingly unwilling to be dragged into such an affair, refusing to even watch.

But the majority simply watched as bystanders, devoid of any superfluous emotion, purely enjoying the spectacle.

After all, such scenes were a rare occurrence.

Inside Piaoxu's inn, Qi Ya was so terrified that she dared not move. Every hair on her body stood on end as she huddled in Piaoxu's embrace, shivering violently.

Piaoxu's face was etched with worry. She looked at Li Qi, fraught with anxiety.

Even she could tell that these visitors came with ill intentions.

The rabbit was still engraving her talismanic incantations, completely oblivious to the outside world. She held a compass, a brush, and paper, continuously writing and calculating Yin and Yang numerology. After all, the establishment of a formation strictly depended on such calculations.

She seemed to feel nothing at all, reacting to nothing, exactly as she always was.

Outside, Li Qi's expression was dark. He said absolutely nothing, striding straight back into the inn and slamming the door shut with a loud crash.

Watching from outside, Lord Yan shook his head. He then signaled with his eyes: next.

Another young girl, no more than seventeen or eighteen years old, hastily stepped out from the procession. She kowtowed toward the inn, begging Li Qi and Shen Shuibi to accept the gifts.

She was terrified out of her wits, weeping until her voice grew hoarse. She struck her forehead against the ground until it split open, blood streaming down her face.

Li Qi exhibited no reaction. His face remained cold and detached, as if he hadn't heard a thing.

Without his word, no one dared to go out and open the door. The inn's handymen and Piaoxu exchanged helpless glances, standing stiffly in place.

The girl begged for over ten minutes. Li Qi ignored her completely. With another wave of Lord Yan's hand, she was incinerated.

Then, came the next.

The next was a young man. After pleading to no avail, he was killed.

Following him was a middle-aged man in his forties. Two frail, young children clung desperately to his legs as he wiped away his tears, hesitating to step forward.

Simply because he hesitated, Lord Yan muttered that they were in the way, and the two children were summarily executed.

The man stood stunned for a moment before letting out a furious roar, lunging at Lord Yan in a desperate bid for revenge.

However, he hadn't even entered the ranks yet; how could he possibly match a Rank 8 expert? A single glance was all it took to reduce him to ash.

Rank 8. In the Earth Oil World, such a level of cultivation was enough to become the master of a domain, a lofty mighty figure commanding hundreds of millions of people.

After killing another four or five in succession, Lord Yan seemed to grow weary of the slaughter. He looked toward the inn. "Does Mr. Li not pride himself on his benevolence? You were even willing to save a tiger demon that possessed a fox's body. Why, then, do you allow these people to die?"

Li Qi maintained his dark expression, but when he spoke, he deliberately forced a casual, indifferent tone. "I did not save her out of benevolence, but merely because it was convenient. Besides, aren't the people who just died all servants of Young Master Jin? If you choose to kill your own people, you naturally have your reasons. It is not my place to interject. After all, I am but an outsider; how could I interfere with Young Master Jin carrying out family discipline?"

Saying this, Li Qi sat down.

He was acutely aware of two facts at this moment.

First, if he wished to avoid being threatened, he could not afford to be soft-hearted.

Second, if he wanted more people to survive, his best course of action was to ignore the situation entirely.

Once human lives became his vulnerability, his enemies would only use more lives to threaten him. In the end, he would either sacrifice himself or cause even more people to die.

Li Qi naturally understood what had to be done.

However, there were still a few underlying issues.

The primary issue was a matter of face.

To be precise, it wasn't merely 'face', but the superficial 'principles' of the situation.

As it was often said, only when one's principles are straight can their conviction be strong.

To establish affairs and uphold righteousness, one must rely on principles to make decisions.

The transformation of the world's qi is the principle of the Dao.

The proper execution of laws and regulations is the principle of governance.

The appropriateness of etiquette is the principle of righteousness.

The intricacies of human relationships are the principles of emotion.

When the essence aligns with these principles, clarity is achieved, and thus true 'reasoning' is formed.

Li Qi conducted himself with righteousness, meaning he needed to place great emphasis on 'principles'. The principle of righteousness demanded conformity with the accepted norms of the masses. This was the foundation of good faith and the root of his Shaman Dao. The other party's 'apology' fell squarely under the 'appropriateness of etiquette'.

Li Qi already knew what his opponent was attempting to do.

If he were to accept such an apology, it would mean that, 'in principle', he had forgiven them. Whatever they chose to do afterward could no longer be considered 'unjustified'.

This wasn't some abstract mysticism, but a tangible reality. Being in the wrong affected the hearts of the people, and the collective will of the people was a power in itself—one that could very well impact Li Qi's path in the Dao.

Clearly, Jin Buhuan had discerned that Li Qi walked the Shaman Dao. Thus, he was deliberately using this underhanded method to disgust him, forcing Li Qi into a position where he was in the wrong, potentially damaging his reputation with certain deities.

Li Qi could practically hear Jin Buhuan's mockery through this staged spectacle.

'Save them, then! You saved the person this young master wanted to kill in the market, didn't you? Aren't you benevolent? I'd like to see just how many you can save!' Jin Buhuan was using this charade to hurl such taunts at him.

Furthermore, Li Qi had ample reason to suspect that the gifts themselves were tampered with. If he accepted them, there would undoubtedly be subsequent traps lying in wait, leading only to further trouble.

He didn't want to gamble on whether his keen eyes could spot the flaws in the gifts, even though he was confident in his own breadth of knowledge and had Shen Shuibi to help appraise them.

No longer the naive fledgling who had just stepped out into the world, he understood the principle of 'never underestimating the heroes of All Under Heaven'. Therefore, he would not let his guard down.

Aside from the primary issue, there was a secondary problem.

If he truly allowed the other party to continue this slaughter, eventually, the mounting death toll would put him in the wrong.

He could even feel the lingering resentment latching onto him.

It was the grudge of the dead, resenting him for refusing to capitulate.

'If only you had agreed, so many people wouldn't have had to die. Why didn't you agree?'

'Our deaths are your fault.'

'You heartless wretch, how could you watch us die and remain so indifferent?'

He felt as though he could hear the wailing of ghosts, although there were none in reality.

Because Lord Yan's flames incinerated the divine soul alongside the body, no ghosts could remain. These voices were merely the manifestations of the resentment he sensed.

Li Qi stood up and rolled his shoulders.

The clinging resentment was shaken off, dissipating into nothingness.

He was already Rank 9, having transcended mortality. Such resentment could not taint him in the slightest.

Ordinary people were always like this. They often blamed those who failed to stand up for them, rather than directing their anger at the true perpetrators.

It was much like when women and children were trafficked; the masses would grieve, but their condemnation usually focused on the authorities for not solving the issue sooner, rather than targeting the wicked culprits themselves.

Therefore, he didn't fault the emergence of this resentment.

However, Jin Buhuan had miscalculated on one crucial point.

This entire charade would not make Li Qi feel unjustified. As for any deity who might hold this against him—to be frank, Li Qi looked down on them even more and couldn't be bothered to entertain them.

Everything that had transpired so far had only resulted in one single consequence.

It had caused Li Qi to add one more objective to his agenda in Clear Mist City: "Take down Jin Buhuan."

Originally, his plan had been to convince Jin Buhuan to let Piaoxu go, after which he would quickly depart, avoiding any further trouble.

But now, it seemed he couldn't simply walk away.

"Miss Shen, how goes the establishment of the formation?" Li Qi turned his head, looking toward Shen Shuibi, who was still drawing her talismanic incantations.

"Six hours! Just give me six more hours!" The rabbit raised a paw before diving back into her frantic work, tapping, writing, and drawing in various spots, her paws pitter-pattering rapidly all around the room.

Li Qi nodded, then turned to Piaoxu. "Miss Piaoxu, I fear a battle is about to break out here, and it will likely damage your inn. I wanted to warn you in advance. I will surely compensate you for any damages at market value once this is over. For now, I must ask you all to leave temporarily, lest you lose your lives."

But Piaoxu adamantly refused. "You offended Jin Buhuan to save my life, sir. How could I possibly demand compensation from you? Please take back those words. I may lack great ability and possess none of your immense divine abilities, but I still have my pride."

She then respectfully offered a small throwing knife. "My cultivation methods are unrefined, but I have had my share of fortuitous encounters. When I assumed my demon form, I obtained a wisp of metal qi at Green Mountain. It manifested into this divine weapon, left behind by the tiger demon after its death. It moves according to the user's will and can cross two miles in an instant. It has already reached the absolute pinnacle of Rank 9. If you detonate it for a single explosive attack, its power will increase tenfold. My cultivation is shallow and my strength is meager, preventing me from taking the battlefield, but I wish to offer this weapon to aid you against the enemy."

Li Qi inspected the throwing knife. It glimmered with a golden light and overflowed with baleful qi. It was indeed a top-tier artifact among Rank 9 weapons, comparable in quality to his own Tang saber.

It was just that... this knife looked somewhat familiar.

He couldn't shake the feeling that he had seen something similar somewhere before.

Pushing the thought aside, he said, "Then I shall accept it. This may very well come in handy."

Li Qi pointed to the back door. "Go hide in the rear courtyard. Miss Shen's formations there should already be complete; the shockwaves of the battle won't reach you."
