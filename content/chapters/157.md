# Chapter 157: Recuperation

Two hours later.

When Li Qi walked into Clear Mist City, gripping his tang saber with Jin Buhuan's severed head impaled on the tip, the entire city fell dead silent.

After a moment, the city erupted into a boiling frenzy.

Some panicked, scrambling frantically to flee.

Some were overjoyed, singing and cheering in uncontrollable ecstasy.

Some merely sighed, their faces twisting in conflict—perhaps they shared some hidden connection to Jin Buhuan.

Some wore expressions of deep anxiety, their brows furrowed as they sank into heavy contemplation.

Some stepped forward with polite courtesy, offering Li Qi their congratulations and inviting him to celebratory banquets.

Some wept bitterly, rushing up to kowtow before Li Qi and hailing him as their savior.

And others gnashed their teeth in hatred at Li Qi, yet lacked the courage to approach, forced to swallow their fury in silence.

A thousand people wore a thousand faces. With millions residing in Clear Mist City, the myriad reactions were countless. Who could say truly how many rejoiced, how many seethed with anger, or how many drowned in sorrow?

Jin Buhuan's death would trigger a massive reshuffling of every power faction within the city. Naturally, some would celebrate while others despaired.

But one thing remained universal.

And that was their awe and dread toward Li Qi.

Even Rank 8 experts could muster nothing but profound reverence when they looked at him.

Li Qi kept his gaze fixed straight ahead. Bearing Jin Buhuan's head aloft, he walked under the scrutiny of countless eyes until he reached the entrance of his inn.

At the inn—

Devastation marred the surroundings. Corpses littered the entire street.

The defensive formations erected by Shen Shuibi had suffered significant damage. Fortunately, no one had managed to breach the perimeter.

It was Shen Shuibi, after all. Li Qi had absolute faith in her.

While Li Qi was out fighting, there were naturally those who attempted to raid the inn or assassinate him in the shadows.

As he had traversed the streets, an unknown number of unseen forces had fought—some defending him, others seeking his life. Every time Li Qi turned a corner, four or five bodies would drop.

Blood had spilled from the alleyways, and the flash of blades had occasionally reflected across his face. As he had methodically knocked on each door, every step had been taken in a pool of blood.

The clash between these hidden factions was undoubtedly spectacular, but unfortunately, Li Qi had lacked the time to observe them closely.

Too much had transpired this night. It was no exaggeration to say the very heavens and earth had been overturned.

Thankfully, the dust had finally settled.

The moment Li Qi appeared at the city gates with Jin Buhuan's head, all overt hostilities ceased. All that remained was the redistribution of wealth and power.

_Redistribution._ What a peaceful-sounding word.

Yet behind those few syllables lay the rise and fall of countless clans, a tapestry woven with hatred, love, and bitter partings.

None of that concerned Li Qi anymore.

He stepped up to the inn's door and knocked three times.

With a creak, the door opened.

Shen Shuibi stood there, offering Li Qi a warm smile.

For a fleeting moment, an overwhelming sense of peace washed over him.

"You're back?" Shen Shuibi tilted her head, briefly glancing at Jin Buhuan's severed head.

"Yeah... I'm back. I can't hold on anymore. I'll leave the rest to you..." Li Qi let out a long breath. Tossing the head aside, he collapsed straight toward the ground.

Shen Shuibi scrambled to catch him, crying out in panic. "Hey, hey, hey! Don't die! How did you end up like this?!"

But no matter what, the ordeal had finally come to an end.

Li Qi slumped heavily into her embrace. She barely managed to catch him before he smashed into the floorboards.

"Honestly... what on earth did you do to get hurt this badly?" she sighed.

From Shen Shuibi's perspective, the night had been exceedingly exhausting.

More than one Rank 8 expert had launched attacks against her here. She had barely managed to fend them off, and eventually, other unknown parties had intervened to block the assailants, ensuring she never fell into true peril.

There was only one downside.

While she was busy blocking the onslaught outside, she had asked Piaoxu to make her a late-night snack.

Piaoxu's hands had trembled so badly that the food's flavor had deteriorated significantly.

She was unhappy about that.

Though it was understandable.

But she was still unhappy.

However, seeing her casually eat a snack while simultaneously fending off the combined siege of several Rank 8 experts had caused the remaining attackers to retreat. Perhaps seeing her enjoy her food so much had made them crave a late-night snack as well, prompting them to head home.

Looking at it that way, eating certainly had its benefits.

She waited until morning, and Li Qi finally returned.

She had initially been delighted, but he fainted the moment he arrived. After hurriedly checking his pulse, she realized Li Qi was hovering on the brink of death.

His injuries were agonizingly severe. So severe, in fact, that she wondered how he had even made it back alive.

His heart meridian was shattered, and his fire qi was nearly drained, causing an inversion of his Fire elemental alignment. As a result, his Yang qi was exhausted, allowing Yin qi to invade.

His wood qi was far too dominant, resulting in severe muscle spasms and volatile surges in his liver meridian. This liver corruption was encroaching upon his heart, while Yin sank into his metal and water pathways. With symptoms like these... he would be dead within a day.

He couldn't even rely on natural healing. His physical stamina and qi had already been entirely diverted into his personal cosmos. His personal cosmos had sealed itself off, seemingly suppressing something within.

This seal was continuously siphoning his life force to maintain the suppression.

The Five Elements within his body were completely inverted, yet his vitality was still being relentlessly drained.

_What a massive headache._

Then again, there seemed to be plenty of medicinal herbs outside. There were rare spiritual flowers, exotic breeds, piles of ores, and various spiritual metals.

They were all the gifts Jin Buhuan had sent over the previous day.

_Might as well put them to use, right?_

Shen Shuibi pushed open the doors and gathered everything she needed. Cradling the materials carefully in her arms, she returned to the inn and shut the door behind her.

Next, she summoned the Three-Five Cauldron.

It was time to begin alchemy. Otherwise, Li Qi was going to die.

She had to hurry.

Two days passed in a flash.

Li Qi sat on his bed, still looking half-dead.

Before him sat a bed tray.

Atop the tray was an extravagant feast, consisting entirely of incredibly potent, restorative delicacies.

However, Li Qi's expression suggested he wasn't exactly thrilled.

On the contrary, his face was drenched in a cold sweat.

For the main course, there was his favorite crab porridge. The crabs were bursting with rich roe, their meat as flawless as pale jade. The golden roe was sweet, the white flesh tender, leaving no residue upon chewing. Its exquisite flavor went without saying.

Beside it sat a pot of spiritual flower soup. It was brewed from young chicken broth and simmered with a medley of water shields, cattails, lotus roots, bamboo shoots, ferns, fresh blossoms, mugwort, and lotus chrysanthemums. Dried razor clams, spiral shells, and wine-lees fish had been tossed in alongside a handful of sweet goji berries. The broth was extraordinarily rich, packed with medicinal spiritual flowers meant to rapidly replenish Yang qi.

Next was a humble plate of chive-fried eggs. Though simple, its aroma was intensely concentrated. Crisp and refreshing, it possessed a natural sweetness that easily rivaled the finest cuts of meat.

All of this was still relatively normal.

What truly made him sweat were the dishes that followed.

First came a pot of organ soup, featuring the phalluses of oxen, sheep, pigs, dogs, and donkeys. If that weren't enough, the most absurd addition was an actual tiger phallus. It had been boiled into a thick stew alongside white mutton, raw rehmannia, dried ginger, borneol, millettia vine, aconite, deer antler, and angelica root. Just looking at the intense concoction made Li Qi's kidneys ache in phantom pain.

Then came a hearty poultry braise, stir-fried with ginseng, cinnamon, tiger bone, bamboo sparrows, and wild goose legs.

Following that were deer kidneys mashed with epimedium and paper mulberry fruits. Heavily spiced, the mixture was stuffed into the bellies of squabs, roasted until the skin was perfectly crisp, and then slow-stewed to completion.

In short, just staring at this tray of hyper-masculine restorative tonics made Li Qi feel physically unwell.

"Miss Piaoxu, isn't this a bit much? Who told you to make all this?" Li Qi rubbed his temples to soothe a growing headache, looking helplessly at Piaoxu, who had brought in the food.

"Er, Mister Shen told me to," Piaoxu explained. "She said you've yet to recover from a grave condition. Since the root of your illness lies in your life force being drained, a severe lack of Yang qi, and the complete exhaustion of your fire qi, she insisted you need to aggressively replenish your Yang qi to recover as quickly as possible."

"She initially planned to rely entirely on pills, but after feeding you a few earlier, some pill toxins began to accumulate in your body. She decided that dietary supplements would be a safer alternative."

"No, you know that's not what I mean. Miss Shen might not understand the implications of these specific foods, but surely you do!" Unable to stand, Li Qi could only sigh helplessly from his bed.

Piaoxu covered her mouth, stifling a giggle. "But those were Mister Shen's strict orders, and I must obey them. Your health is the priority. You'd best eat up, sir."

With that, she hastily pushed open the door and darted out, doubled over and covering her mouth. Anyone who didn't know better would think she had just stolen something.

She left Li Qi alone on his bed, staring down at the table of questionable delicacies. His expression was incredibly subtle, as was his mood.

"Sigh... Qi Ya, oh Qi Ya. Right now, I can only move my hands. If I actually eat all of this, what on earth am I supposed to do with all that pent-up energy?" Li Qi tilted his head, directing his question to the little fox resting on the windowsill.

"Squeak?" Qi Ya tilted her head in utter confusion.

The un-transformed little fox had completely failed to grasp his meaning. Moreover, since Li Qi couldn't move, he was rather boring to be around. She promptly hopped out the window, leaving Li Qi completely alone in the room.

Now he was truly isolated.

_Forget it._

_Eat._

He picked up his chopsticks and began his meal.

There were no tough dishes on the table; everything was either a soup or a soft broth, specially prepared to make eating effortless for him.

This was his first meal since waking up.

After returning that night, he had fallen into a coma for over a day. Shen Shuibi had exhausted every method at her disposal just to pull him back from the brink of death.

As expected, attempting to control two streams of Rank 8 metal qi simultaneously was pushing his limits far too much.

Still, he had reaped a considerable harvest, hadn't he?

Li Qi extended his hand. Two streams of metal qi surged directly from his fingertips. They effortlessly sliced the various phalluses in his soup into thin, chewable pieces.

Yes. Those two streams of metal qi had been fused into one, balanced by the harmonious Yin and Yang of his personal cosmos. They now belonged entirely to Li Qi.

Normally, they could remain separated. But when driven by his personal cosmos, the Yin and Yang could fuse into a single breath of qi, erupting with terrifying power. Its speed could cross ten miles in a single breath, slicing through anything in its path. It moved as an extension of his own will, limited only by his reaction speed.

However, Li Qi's current reaction speed wasn't enough to manipulate it flawlessly. The twin streams of metal qi were simply too fast. When unleashed at full force, he couldn't control the blast radius at all.

Because of this, Li Qi temporarily altered the direction in which he condensed his internal deities.

To advance from Rank 9 to Rank 8, a cultivator needed to manifest twelve internal deities.

These consisted of the five mountain gods of the five great mountains within the personal cosmos, the four water gods of the Four Great Rivers, the two Heavenly Gods of the Sun and Moon, and one primal spirit to oversee the entire personal cosmos.

A total of twelve deities, quietly corresponding to the twelve Earthly Branches[^1].

If an Invocator studied the Heavenly God branch, they only needed to condense ten internal deities, corresponding to the ten Heavenly Stems[^2].

If they followed the Human Ghost branch, they condensed six deities, corresponding to the six internal spirits. The common idioms _six spirits lacking a master_ or _six spirits uneasy_[^3] referred precisely to this concept.

The deities of the Human Ghost branch shared a connection with one of the Daoist Sects. The two sides seemed to have borrowed concepts from one another while maintaining independent systems. Li Qi wasn't entirely clear on the specifics.

He belonged to the Earthly Divinity branch, after all.

The three Invocator lineages—Heavenly God, Earthly Divinity, and Human Ghost—shared common ground, but they each possessed highly specialized disciplines. Li Qi had noticed that his instructional texts contained practically nothing regarding the specialties of the other two branches.

Most likely, those secrets were heavily guarded and not easily shared.

Speaking of which, Li Qi had originally planned to start his condensation with the Five Great Mountains and the Four Great Rivers, as doing so would maximize the structural integrity of his personal cosmos.

Now, however, he intended to start with the primal spirit. If he could manifest his primal spirit first, he would be able to perfectly command the two streams of metal qi, wielding them as effortlessly as his own limbs.

The word _primal (yuan)_ in primal spirit represented the absolute beginning, similar to the concept of _Qianyuan_—the origin of heaven.

(TL Note: Qian means 'Heaven', and Yuan means 'origin, primal'.)

Once he possessed a primal spirit, controlling the metal qi would be seamless. This was a feat not even Jin Buhuan could achieve. Whenever Jin Buhuan utilized them, it came at the heavy cost of severely damaging his lung meridians.

Yet now, utilizing the lofty status and profound harmony of his personal cosmos, Li Qi could deploy these two divine weapons without any backlash. Separated, their power hovered in the mid-tier of Rank 8. Merged, they reached the absolute pinnacle of Rank 8. The difference was staggering.

The gap in power even within the same rank could be absurdly vast.

A newly advanced Rank 9 cultivator facing experts of Li Qi or Jin Buhuan's caliber would be slaughtered by the hundreds in mere minutes, without the victors even breaking a sweat.

If he could completely master these two streams of metal qi, Li Qi could confidently claim that his combat prowess had reached the absolute zenith of Rank 9.

He wouldn't dare call himself invincible. All Under Heaven was vast, teeming with countless heroes and geniuses who harbored terrifying trump cards. Li Qi was no longer as arrogant as he had been during his early days in the Jianghu.

The more he learned, the smaller he felt.

Jin Buhuan and Elder Si had been incredibly arrogant largely because they were profoundly ignorant.

Lost in thought, Li Qi used his metal qi to cleanly slice the tough ingredients, grabbed his chopsticks, and methodically fed himself.

He wondered how Jin Buhuan would feel if he could see his prized weapons being used to cut up soup ingredients.

Right at that moment, the door was abruptly pushed open, accompanied by a sudden, muffled sound: "Pfft..."

Li Qi instinctively whipped his head toward the door.

He saw a breathtaking beauty covering her mouth with a delicate hand, clearly unable to hold back a soft chuckle.

Li Qi glanced down at the piece of tiger phallus currently in his mouth, then looked back up at the woman standing in the doorway.

His face instantly darkened.

The beauty stifled her laughter and sauntered into the room, her hips swaying seductively with every step. "You certainly have discerning tastes when it comes to your diet, sir. You seem to be a man who appreciates vitality nourishment. Coincidentally, my Clear Mist Sect possesses a few related secret techniques. I wonder if you might be interested?"

"Senior, please... please don't tease me," Li Qi stammered, his face burning bright red.

Truth be told, it was incredibly awkward.

"Such matters are only human nature. Why should you feel ashamed, sir? Have you forgotten the line of business our Clear Mist Sect is involved in?" the beauty smiled, taking a seat opposite Li Qi.

Li Qi knew exactly who she was.

Her beauty was not a fraction inferior to Hu Xian'er, the Sect Master of the Clear Mist Sect, yet her aura was the exact opposite. If Hu Xian'er was like a secluded plum blossom, exuding a quiet and elegant fragrance, this woman was a vibrant, blooming peony—alluring, intoxicating, and dripping with raw sensuality.

Her skin was as smooth as polished jade, her waist impossibly slender. Her elegantly arched brows resembled distant mountains, framing enchanting, liquid eyes that sparkled with charm. A phoenix hairpin rested at a delicate tilt within her dark, cloud-like tresses.

She was a beauty beyond worldly compare, radiating an intoxicating magnetism that made one yearn to run their hands along her soft, luscious skin.

This was the second-in-command of the Clear Mist Sect, another Rank 7 demon—Hu Mei'er.

Li Qi didn't know what to say. Unsure of whether to swallow the tiger phallus in his mouth or spit it out, he sat frozen on his bed in sheer embarrassment.

"You are quite bold, sir. Those two streams of metal qi are veritable treasures, yet you wield them as dining knives. You truly possess the demeanor of an unmatched master." Hu Mei'er gracefully shifted the conversation, rescuing the room from dead silence.

"They are merely external tools. If not for use, what good are they?" Li Qi finally recovered from his embarrassment. Swallowing hard, he asked, "Why has Senior come to visit me?"

[^1]: **Earthly Branches (十二地支):** A traditional Chinese system of timekeeping and astrology consisting of twelve terms. Historically used to track years, months, days, and the twelve two-hour periods of a day (shichen). They are closely tied to the twelve animals of the Chinese Zodiac. In cultivation concepts, the twelve branches represent a complete, grounded, and cyclical earthly foundation.
[^2]: **Heavenly Stems (十天干):** A Chinese system of ordinals consisting of ten terms. They are fundamentally linked to the Five Elements (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water), with each element having a Yin and a Yang manifestation (5 * 2 = 10). While the Earthly Branches govern the terrestrial, the Heavenly Stems govern the celestial, fitting the "Heavenly God" lineage.
[^3]: **Six spirits lacking a master (六神无主):** In traditional Chinese medicine and Daoist internal alchemy, the human body and its vital organs (Heart, Liver, Spleen, Lungs, Kidneys, and Gallbladder) are believed to be inhabited and governed by distinct spirits. "Six spirits lacking a master" is a common real-world Chinese idiom used to describe someone who is panicking, utterly confused, or paralyzed by fear—as if the guiding spirits of their body have scattered. The author is cleverly recontextualizing this everyday idiom into a literal stage of cultivation.
