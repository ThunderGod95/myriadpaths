# Chapter 127: Dao Heart

At this very moment, in the outside world.

On the other side of the continent, that Diviner young master clicked his tongue in displeasure. "Tch, what incredible luck."

"However, it wasn't completely without gains."

He spoke as he toyed with the shadow in his hand.

This was a sliver of his innate dao rhythm.

The Devil Seed.

This was the very root of how he had directly entered the gates of Shaman God Mountain, earned the favor of a Grand Diviner, and become a personal disciple.

The Devil Seed could pierce directly into an opponent's primal spirit, invade the weakest point of their inner heart, and forcefully drag them into a devil realm.

Within that realm, everything was identical to reality, and everything that transpired was entirely real.

It was just that the opponent would fall into different states of mind depending on which weakness had been invaded.

Under those circumstances, if the opponent died, they truly died. Furthermore, every technique the opponent utilized would be recorded in his mind, making it tantamount to a simulated battle.

Conversely, if he lost—whether the opponent saw through the illusion or killed his projection within it—everything would revert to the very beginning, as if nothing had ever happened.

It equated to a forced battle where the victim had to win and could not afford to lose. Furthermore, it forcefully attached itself to the flaws in their Dao Heart, leaving them muddled and dominated by sheer instinct.

His master had told him that the essence of his Devil Seed was to see through the flaws of a Dao Heart and then drag the victim into a fragment of cosmic time. What occurred within that fragment was half-real and half-illusion.

If one willed it to be real, it became real.

If one willed it to be fake, it remained nothing but an illusory dream.

Because this sliver of dao rhythm had already touched upon cosmic time, it provided immense aid to the path of divination. This was exactly why the Grand Diviner had taken him in as a disciple.

However, it possessed a limitation: it could only be used on a single person twice. After that, the target would become completely immune to the Devil Seed.

Ever since he left the sect to temper himself, those capable of breaking or resisting the Devil Seed had been exceptionally few.

This Li Qi truly lived up to his status as the Grand Invocator's disciple. He was indeed a worthy adversary.

_And then there was... that Bai Di. Looking at his sword, it appears to be the Xiezhi Sword. Hmm, the Xiezhi from White Snake Prefecture? Could he be from there? Is he Li Qi's follower?_

_Also, that woman named Shen Shuibi—just who exactly is she?_

_Why could she see through all my spell arts at a glance? She shouldn't be from Shaman God Mountain. Could she be from another Daoist Tradition, making her more knowledgeable?_

_Which traditions have disciples with that kind of ability at Rank 9?_

The Myriad Tomes Pavilion of the Human Dao? Or something else? Or perhaps she also possessed an innate dao rhythm that he had simply failed to detect?

"I don't know. Regardless, I'll keep watching." He stowed away the Devil Seed and continued his observation.

His master had once taught him that an inability to find an answer merely meant he lacked sufficient clues. As a Diviner, he should employ his methods to gather more clues, striving for absolute infallibility.

As for using the Devil Seed on Shen Shuibi or Bai Di, the thought hadn't even crossed his mind.

There was no need to drag outsiders into the struggle between Diviners and Invocators. Once the Devil Seed was unleashed, failing to break it meant certain death. Unless absolutely necessary, there was no reason to kill and needlessly offend others.

He didn't treat the inhabitants of these minor worlds as human beings, but he still afforded that basic respect to the people of All Under Heaven.

---

Meanwhile, on Li Qi's side.

He opened his eyes.

He found himself still sitting in the vehicle.

Bai Di sat in the front passenger seat, completely oblivious to what had just transpired.

Li Qi realized his back was drenched in sweat. Furthermore, a pair of hands were pressing against his temples.

The hands felt incredibly soft to the touch. At the same time, he noticed a barrier glowing before his eyes, isolating the back seat from the outside world and preventing any sound from escaping.

Turning his head, he saw it was Shen Shuibi.

She had adjusted her posture to rest her hands on Li Qi's temples. It was exactly this action that had provided Li Qi with the crucial warning within the devil realm.

Had she not done so, Li Qi likely would have fallen prey to the trap.

"Thank you, Miss Shen. I owe you once again for this..." Li Qi immediately expressed his gratitude.

Shen Shuibi, however, kept her hands on his temples and replied, "I noticed something was amiss earlier. But I felt this might not necessarily be a bad thing for you, so I didn't say anything."

"It truly wasn't a bad thing..." Li Qi's eyes were bloodshot. He slumped weakly against the seat, allowing Shen Shuibi to channel a stream of cooling qi into his body, letting it circulate around his mind.

"I was too arrogant. Over the past few days, I was always standing at the pinnacle, acting as if I was orchestrating everything from behind the scenes. Arrogance and conceit... I had somewhat lost myself." Li Qi took a deep breath.

"I thought that by remaining poised and not boasting in front of the Governor and the others, I was maintaining my humility. Little did I know, arrogance never stems from one's attitude toward superiors, but from one's attitude toward subordinates."

"When I was in All Under Heaven, I understood the vastness of the world. Thus, I was cautious and prudent, not daring to overstep and constantly reflecting on myself."

"But after arriving in this minor world and learning of its shallow foundations, I even used a bit of petty cleverness to toy with the Heavenly Dao once. Because of that, I felt an immense sense of superiority over Zhang Deming and the rest. I became smug about my title as 'Young Master', thinking that while I might be nobody in All Under Heaven, I was a genuine big shot here." Li Qi voiced the conclusions of his deep introspection.

He then sighed, "Alas, arrogant without realizing it, haughty without self-restraint. My Dao Heart had already developed a flaw."

Li Qi had truly recognized his mistakes and perceived the flaw in his Dao Heart, which was why he appeared so weak and drained.

Hearing this, the persistent worry that had clouded Shen Shuibi's expression vanished. Instead, a hint of joy entered her eyes. "Being able to realize this proves you now have the opportunity to mend that flaw."

"I didn't speak plainly earlier because matters of the Dao Heart cannot be addressed directly. Speaking too much can easily provoke resistance; one must reach enlightenment on their own. But it seems you're quite impressive after all, Li Qi!" she said cheerfully. "Flaws in the Dao Heart constantly plague even many mighty figures. Many can recognize their flaws yet remain forever incapable of mending them. For you to awaken with just a minor reminder from me shows that two-thirds of the purpose of this otherworldly journey has already been achieved," Shen Shuibi said with a smile.

Hearing Shen Shuibi praise him, Li Qi felt a spark of secret delight. However, he immediately forced himself to calm down, questioning the source of this joy. Was it born from arrogance, or something else?

After this ordeal, he had become vigilant. He understood the absolute necessity of constant self-reflection. Only then could he avoid sinking into the flaws of his Dao Heart, slowly deteriorating until he was no longer himself.

However, after a moment of thought, he suddenly caught onto a specific detail. "Two-thirds of the purpose of coming to this outer realm? Wasn't our goal to study dao rhythm?"

"Ah. Well, do you feel that outer realm dao rhythm is particularly difficult?" Shen Shuibi asked in return.

Li Qi recalled his experiences and had no choice but to admit the truth. "...It's very simple."

"Exactly. The otherworldly dao rhythm of such minor worlds holds no inherent difficulty to begin with. By studying it step by step and observing it, you will naturally come to understand it. There is nothing extraordinary about it."

"The true purpose of all otherworldly journeys is to force disciples of major sects like yourself to shift your mentality. It's to observe how your mindset changes once you leave All Under Heaven, and to teach you how to stabilize your Dao Heart."

"Comprehending dao rhythm and acquiring knowledge can be done at any time; there's no need to specifically venture out for tempering just for that. Stabilizing the Dao Heart, however, is a ceaseless task that will accompany you for millions of years along your path. Therefore, your first lesson after descending the mountain will inevitably be this."

"I actually realized this a long time ago. The moment the Governor extended the invitation, I knew this tempering experience was definitely orchestrated by the Grand Invocator."

"The Dao Heart is the absolute foundation of cultivation. Without a sufficient Dao Heart, a martial artist will lose the perseverance to practice their forms day in and day out for decades. A Shaman will lose their reverence for heaven, earth, and nature. Members of Daoist Sects will no longer adhere to tranquil and ascetic cultivation. And those who cultivate the Human Dao will become volatile and oppress the people."

Shen Shuibi paused here before adding, "Without the steadfastness of the Dao Heart, cultivating to the end is nothing more than chasing an empty void. When that time comes, all your cultivation will no longer truly belong to you."

_No longer truly belong to him?_

This phrase struck Li Qi as somewhat bizarre.

Even if his Dao Heart developed a flaw, it shouldn't mean he would cease to be himself, right?

Thus, Li Qi humbly sought guidance. "Why do you say that? Does having a flaw in the Dao Heart mean one is no longer themselves?"

Shen Shuibi looked at Li Qi and spoke as if it were the most natural thing in the world. "It's quite simple. When an ordinary man flies into a rage and commits murder on the streets out of fury, do you think the decision to kill was truly made by him?"

Hearing this question, Li Qi frowned and sank into deep thought.

_Was it?_

_It should be._

Even if rage had clouded his mind, the decision should still be his own.

But... was it really?

No.

An ordinary man flies into a rage and kills on the streets out of fury.

In truth, when that man acts out of seething street-level fury and commits murder, he is acting in a moment of hot-headedness. He is being driven by rage, driven by base instinct. His true heart has already been blinded by his anger.

At that exact moment, the decision he made was no longer truly his own.

"So, think about it a little deeper. What is the difference between the rage of a nobleman—which leaves two corpses and blood spilled over five paces—and the rage of an ordinary man?" Shen Shuibi asked him again.

Her tone was patient and guiding, leading Li Qi step by step to deduce the answer.

Li Qi continued to ponder.

The difference between an ordinary man killing in a street rage, and a nobleman's rage that leaves corpses and spilled blood?

Both involved killing in anger. From the outcome alone, there was no difference.

But what about the reality behind the acts?

The ordinary man merely suffered a rush of blood to the head and rushed in to stab someone to death. It was highly likely he would feel unfathomable regret afterward. He would be consumed by fear and sorrow, hating himself for being so impulsive in that moment.

Because the ordinary man acted without thinking. While driven by fury, he failed to see that better solutions existed. Only after the fact would he realize that the murder was entirely unnecessary.

The nobleman's rage, however, would never breed regret. Before making a move, he had already deliberated deeply. He understood the cause and process behind his decision and concluded that this was the absolute best course of action. Thus, he acted with resolute determination, capable of facing nine deaths without a single shred of remorse.

This was the distinction between 'blind, hot-blooded courage' and 'acting where righteousness lies'.

It was also the exact difference between making a decision with a clouded Dao Heart and making one with a clear Dao Heart.

With such clarity, one could achieve liberation. When walking as a Dao seeker, they would fear neither life nor death, dread no powerful foe, and could live for the Dao and die for the Dao.

The mortal masses did not cultivate the heart, hence they were not free. Right and wrong were inverted; they mistook the false for the true. Every single person was like a slave—shackled and subjugated by desires, impulses, and a myriad of turbulent emotions.

In this manner, under the binding chains of desire, they lived without control over themselves, died without control over themselves, loved and hated without control, and toiled exhaustingly for an entire lifetime. They neither knew why they lived this way nor how to break free from it all. They merely existed out of inertia—experiencing birth, aging, sickness, and death. Constrained by their shackles, they were driven by endless external forces, forever denied true freedom.

But at this point, another doubt arose in Li Qi's mind.

"Miss Shen, following this logic, does cultivating the Dao require abandoning the seven emotions and six desire, turning oneself into an emotionless puppet? If one analyzes the pros and cons of absolutely everything, wouldn't that just make them a mercenary seeking only personal gain and security? How could that still be considered cultivation?" Li Qi asked, his brow furrowed in utter confusion.

Shen Shuibi tilted her head, looking utterly perplexed. "Why would you think that?"

"Telling you not to be driven by desire is not the same as telling you to abandon desire entirely. Desire itself is an intrinsic component of the 'heart'. If you discard it, would your heart still be whole?"

"The so-called clarity of the Dao Heart—what exactly is clarity? It is allowing you to use wisdom to introspect and constantly reflect. Through reflection, you achieve inner and outer brilliance and purity. You avoid being driven by pure desire and come to recognize your original, true heart."

"By recognizing your original, true heart, you clear away confusion and cast aside anger and obsession. You understand exactly what your desires are, what it is you truly want, what provokes your wrath, and what the true motives and reasons behind your actions are."

"When your inner heart is clear and you understand all of this, how could you possibly commit acts blinded by desire?"

"It's just like contrasting your current self with how you were just moments ago. You were blinded by arrogance, which completely clouded your Dao Heart. Because of that... do you really believe that the actions you took were things you would normally do?"

Shen Shuibi threw out a rapid succession of questions, once again forcing Li Qi to deeply ponder.

Eventually, Li Qi had to concede that her words made perfect sense.

Being driven by instinct to run rampant, allowing one's desires to endlessly bloat—was that truly freedom? No, that was merely being a slave to desire; it couldn't be called freedom in the slightest.

Clarity of the Dao Heart meant not being confused by greed, anger, or ignorance; not being shackled by karmic ties; not being hijacked by inner doubts, fear, or rage. It meant knowing one's true heart and acting strictly in accordance with it.

It meant walking one's path without a shred of hesitation in the heart, clearly understanding what one genuinely loved and what one truly sought, and then pouring all of one's vital energy and wisdom into realizing that ultimate goal.

This was the heart of a Dao seeker. This was the 'Dao Heart'.

Every Dao seeker had to possess such a heart to press forward with unwavering perseverance. Only then could they sustain their original passion throughout their long lives. Otherwise, over the vast stretch of time, they would inevitably become puppets controlled by power and desire.

Upon thinking this through, Li Qi suddenly felt a multitude of confusions within his heart vanish completely.

The thick layer of dust that had clouded his heart—accumulated from acquiring power and stepping onto the path of cultivation—seemed to have been swept entirely clean.

The Dao in the outside world was the underlying law governing the operation of the vast cosmos.

The Dao within the body was the fundamental principle ruling the personal cosmos.

At this moment, with the old dust swept from his Dao Heart, his personal cosmos seemed drastically clearer and purer. The circulation of his various inner qi and the divine qi within his body became remarkably smooth and unimpeded.

Seeing this, Shen Shuibi happily dispelled the barrier.

Not a single soul had realized what had just transpired.
