# Chapter 101: Tang Empire (Part 2)

Li Qi was so shocked by the affairs of the Tang Empire that he was somewhat speechless.

But this wasn't the end of it.

The conversation continued.

Even more shocking and horrifying revelations followed, completely overturning Li Qi's understanding of this world.

The Reason Dao had developed numerous things. Besides 'Flowers in a Mirror, Moon in the Water,' there were also the magnificent 'Heavenly Palaces.' These were artificial stars hanging in the sky. Ordinarily, they could regulate the weather, but they could also launch direct attacks. Anyone below Rank 7 would die instantly upon contact.

_Artificial satellites? Space-based kinetic weapons?_

Li Qi plunged into a state of utter bewilderment.

There were also Earth-Flow Carriages built upon the ley lines. Even mortals could ride them, traveling three thousand li in a single day. However, the construction costs were exorbitant. They required opening up the ley lines and linking the earth qi. Furthermore, an Earthly Divinity was required to personally guard and suppress the lines every thousand miles, preventing potential earth qi riots and earthquakes.

For this reason, there was currently only one track extending from the Tang Empire to the capital of the Song Kingdom.

Moreover, the Tang Empire harbored an extreme disgust for illicit shrines and wild gods. As soon as they arrived, they obliterated all temples that demanded human sacrifice, exterminating all those mountain spirits and wild monsters. Only those who were benevolent to humans, abstained from eating people, and protected their local domains were spared.

After the slaughter came the conferral of titles. Some were local benevolent gods, some were heroic spirits of the Tang Empire who had died in the war, and others were the ghosts of highly respected locals who had died of old age.

The imperial court of the Tang Empire personally issued these decrees. Only after receiving a long scroll stamped with a grand seal and accepting the edict could they be considered 'Orthodox Gods.' From then on, they served the Emperor of the Tang Empire as their liege. They had to work diligently for the people and obey the dispatch of local officials. Only by bringing peace to their region and protecting the land and water could they enjoy incense offerings.

The previously mentioned Earthly Divinities were exactly like this. They were all Orthodox Gods sanctioned by the Tang Empire, responsible for suppressing the thousand-li stretch of Earth-Flow Carriage tracks within their jurisdiction. They worked tirelessly and never dared to abandon their posts.

Any illicit wild god not recognized as an 'Orthodox God' either fled for their lives, abandoning the incense they had cultivated for so long, or was directly killed and turned into raw materials.

Following this purge, although there was minor unrest, order was eventually established.

After that came the massacre of the shamans.

Show no mercy. Every practitioner of the Shaman Dao, anyone who held a rank, was arrested and thrown into the grand prisons.

According to the Tang Empire, the existence of shamans and the Shaman Dao was the root cause of illicit wild gods. Anyone following the Shaman Dao was destined to bring ruin to the nation. Therefore, they had to either sincerely reform or die.

Next came the implementation of their laws.

All the aforementioned actions were rooted in the law. Everything they did followed legal precedent; there were absolutely no extrajudicial killings.

The laws of the Tang Empire were incredibly detailed and heavily regulated. Although the populace gained protection, favorable weather, and freedom from oppression, they absolutely could not overstep their bounds or break the law. Otherwise, the punishments were exceedingly severe.

However, according to Jing Yan, while the laws were humane, the punishments showed no leniency whatsoever. People from the Great Deer Kingdom would likely find it very difficult to adapt. Initially, many people in the Song Kingdom couldn't adapt either, subconsciously breaking the law out of habit.

Then, after six months of strict enforcement, harsh crackdowns, and legal education campaigns, nearly ten percent of the Song Kingdom's residents—roughly over a hundred million people—had suffered imprisonment and punishment. The rest, naturally, were terrified into silence and never dared to offend again.

In summary, life in the Song Kingdom over the past two or three years had undergone an earth-shattering transformation.

To the Song Kingdom, the Tang Empire was an entirely new world, existing on a completely different level.

As for Li Qi, just listening to this description made him break out in a cold sweat.

_Damn it._

_How the hell is anyone supposed to fight that?_

_Can Shaman God Mountain even hold out against them?_

_It sounds incredibly precarious._

_Did I cling to the wrong thigh? Is it too late to switch sides now?_

Fortunately, the conversation moved on. Before long, it shifted from describing the changes in the Song Kingdom to the events that had transpired back in Snake Tail County.

Li Qi suddenly remembered something and asked Jing Yan, "By the way, from what I've seen, Manager Jing is amiable, fair, and very principled and methodical in his business. Why would he offend the Assistant Magistrate and have to flee Snake Tail County in such a hurry? He doesn't seem like the type to offend people."

At this, Jing Yan sighed. "Manager Jing naturally treats others with kindness, but I simply cannot stand seeing filthy deeds. I was the one who provoked the Assistant Magistrate."

"What happened?" Li Qi pressed, his curiosity piqued.

"It was nothing extraordinary. The son of the Assistant Magistrate of Snake Tail County tried to forcefully take a family's daughter as a concubine. I bumped into him and gave him a beating. That's all," Jing Yan explained.

"That sort of thing... definitely should be stopped. But you all just left like that? By leaving, doesn't it mean you effectively did nothing? You just left that girl to suffer the Assistant Magistrate's malice for nothing. She probably won't be able to escape later anyway." Li Qi's expression turned subtle as he raised an eyebrow.

_You beat someone up and then ran. You got away, but what about the girl?_

"I considered that as well. So, I simply castrated him, packed my bags, and brought the girl and her widowed mother along with the caravan. This way, he can't harm anyone else in the future, much less find that girl," Jing Yan said.

Li Qi wiped away a cold sweat.

_Holy shit, lady, you really don't care about the rules when you do things, do you? No wonder Manager Jing had to flee in the middle of the night. Doing something like that in broad daylight—do you think you don't have enough trouble already?_

However, looking at it now, the Jing family didn't seem to think she was in the wrong. He hadn't seen anyone giving Jing Yan the cold shoulder.

Quite the opposite. Even though they all avoided her and cheered for Li Qi during their sparring match, it was clear that no one harbored any true malice toward her.

From Li Qi's perspective, the Jing family had an excellent family ethos. They all seemed to treat others with kindness. Despite differences in personal temperament—ranging from the fierce Jing Yan to the gentle Manager Jing—they were incredibly united. They were gentle and reasonable, yet not weak. When faced with a crisis, they could make decisive choices without hesitation.

It was just like how, after Jing Yan stuck her neck out for someone, Manager Jing didn't utter a single complaint upon hearing the news. He immediately arranged the manpower to flee the city that very night, taking the back roads to the ferry crossing before the other party could mount a blockade.

_Only a family like this could manage to expand their business to a foreign place..._

"Fleeing like that isn't exactly wrong. It's getting late, so I'll take my leave. I won't disturb your sword practice any longer, big sister." Li Qi checked the sky. During their conversation, it had grown completely dark. It was time to head back.

"Alright." Jing Yan was just as straightforward, directly seeing her guest off.

On his way back, however, Li Qi pondered whether he ought to run away as well.

_I was recruited right on the main street... Even though we fled overnight, someone must have seen me joining the caravan... If the other party really is the Assistant Magistrate, will I be targeted too...?_ he thought to himself.

If the other party truly was petty and vindictive, returning to Snake Tail County might actually bring trouble to his door.

But Old Ma was still back there.

_Tch. This is a bit tricky._
