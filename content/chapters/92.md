# Chapter 92: Employment

Li Qi cupped his hands, his manner exceedingly polite. "May I help you?"

He was currently dressed as a commoner, wearing cotton clothes accented with a bit of silk. In his hand, he held a long blade wrapped in coarse cloth. At first glance, he looked like an ordinary passerby leaning on a cloth-wrapped walking stick. He didn't appear to possess any martial foundations.

However, there were always those with a discerning eye.

The merchant hurriedly stepped forward. "I saw you wandering nearby for quite some time, warrior. It looks like you are seeking employment. It just so happens that my merchant caravan is in need of an expert who can hold the line. Would you be willing to lend me a hand?"

He recognized Li Qi as an expert with a single glance, proving his eye wasn't poor. Perhaps he was a cultivator himself, just one who hadn't yet entered the official ranks.

"This journey will take no more than four or five days. I am willing to offer one gold piece per day." He immediately added the compensation.

One gold piece a day was a considerably generous price. But... a willingness to offer such a sum indicated that the mission's risks matched its reward.

Nobody handed out money for nothing.

Therefore, Li Qi didn't accept the offer rashly. Instead, he inquired, "Where do you intend to travel, sir? If our paths do not align, I'm afraid I must decline."

The merchant was a shrewd man and naturally understood the implication. Glancing around at the heavy foot traffic, he kept his words brief and extended an invitation. "There are too many people here to speak freely. Why don't you accompany me to my caravan to discuss the details, warrior?"

"Very well. After you." Li Qi extended a hand, gesturing for the other man to lead the way.

The merchant immediately set off.

The caravan wasn't far. After walking for less than a minute, they arrived at a commercial inn.

Inns of this kind were rented out to merchants. They featured courtyards, stables, and storehouses for stacking goods and parking vehicles, along with places for the oxen to rest.

Naturally, there were no horse-drawn carriages. Spirit horses were far too expensive and ill-suited for pulling carts. Ox-drawn carts were the standard; old oxen were strong and steady, capable of hauling heavy loads. Their slow pace mattered little, so long as their steps were sure.

Upon arriving, Li Qi noticed the caravan was quite large. It consisted of a full fifty or sixty men and women, accompanied by over twenty ox carts. Each cart was fully loaded with mountain goods from White Snake Mountain.

This was to be expected. For many small caravans, an entire trip might only yield a profit of two or three gold pieces. Only a massive operation of this scale could afford a daily rate of one gold piece.

Everyone greeted the merchant and Li Qi. Li Qi nodded in return.

The merchant led Li Qi into a guest room and closed the doors and windows.

"If my old eyes do not deceive me," he said, "you are a Rank 9 of the Martial Dao, correct?"

"I thought you were going to call me an innate martial artist," Li Qi replied with a playful smile.

"Only country bumpkins use that term. For those who have officially stepped onto the path of cultivation, who doesn't use the nine-rank system? Without it, there's no way to compare the strengths of different Daoist Traditions." The old merchant poured Li Qi a cup of tea and brought out a plate of fruit and pastries.

Li Qi stared at the fruit in surprise.

They weren't fresh, but dried—and he was very familiar with them.

They were shriveled and emerald green, coated in a layer of something resembling white frost. The plate didn't hold many, only a few dozen pieces.

Noting Li Qi's surprised gaze, the merchant explained, "Please do not take offense at the meager portion, warrior. These originate from the Western Regions as a specialty of Chechen, located over a hundred thousand miles from the Great Deer Kingdom. Here, a single piece can fetch a copper coin, and even then, they are hard to come by. This small dish alone is worth over a hundred copper coins."

Over a hundred copper coins was enough to buy meat, yet these few dozen dried fruits weren't even enough to fill a handful.

Li Qi gazed at the dried fruits with a mix of surprise and a touch of nostalgia.

Since they were dried, they no longer possessed the plumpness of a fresh harvest. However, after special processing, their sugars had condensed within their shriveled forms, filling the air with a sweet aroma.

Unable to resist, Li Qi picked one up, placed it in his mouth, and said, "I know what these are. They are called grapes."

Raisins existed in this world too...

"You are truly a man of wide experience, warrior, to recognize even this foreign specialty." The merchant praised Li Qi's knowledge with genuine amazement.

Li Qi wasn't particularly proud. Beneath his nostalgia, a sliver of panic set in.

Why did this world share so many similarities with his original one?

Setting aside the various myths, written characters, spoken languages, and the existence of places like the Tang Empire, the presence of the Western Regions and grapes filled him with apprehension.

However, worrying was useless. He had no right to be apprehensive. To a lowly Rank 9 cultivator, such grand mysteries were as incomprehensible as the vastness of the universe—meaningless to ponder.

He quickly suppressed these stray thoughts and smiled at the merchant. "I have simply read a few more books than most. Why don't you tell me our destination, sir, so we can see if our paths align?"

"Very well, then I will not hide it from you. Our caravan intends to travel to the ferry crossing at the source of the Li River. From there, we will board a ship and transport our goods down the Li River to the Song Kingdom," the merchant revealed their route.

"So... you need me to escort you to the ferry crossing?" Li Qi asked.

"Exactly," the merchant confirmed.

"The route from Snake Tail County to the ferry crossing is a wide, flat highway with many fellow travelers. It is a journey of merely two or three days. Why would you need my protection?" Li Qi immediately sensed something amiss.

"Ahem. To tell you the truth, warrior, I am afraid our caravan cannot take the main road. We must travel through the narrow mountain passes, which is why I estimated it would take four or five days." The merchant coughed dryly, offering an explanation.

"What happened?" Li Qi frowned, lowering his voice.

"Due to a moment of carelessness, I offended the Assistant Magistrate of Snake Tail County, and he has been targeting me ever since. If we take the main highway, the commercial taxes alone would flay a layer of skin off my bones. Taking the mountain trails is a choice born of absolute desperation..." The merchant cupped his hands with a bitter, helpless smile.

This was a plausible reason. Merchants inherently sought stability; they wouldn't detour through dangerous mountain paths for no reason.

The merchant continued, "But you need not worry. My trading firm has taken on a massive order this time, so we already have another Rank 9 Martial Dao cultivator serving as a guard. It's just that I felt uneasy relying solely on her. It is fortuitous that fate brought us together today. With two ranked experts present, this journey will be practically guaranteed to succeed."

"So there is another person. That certainly makes things safer. I will accept the job. When do we depart?" Li Qi asked.

"There is no time to delay. After all, we are still in Snake Tail County... someone else's territory. We hope to leave this afternoon. Do you have any other matters to attend to, warrior? If you need to delay, we can wait a bit." Overjoyed, the merchant relayed their schedule.

"I have no other business. However, I have a companion I wish to bring along. I wonder..." Li Qi requested to bring an additional person.

"No problem at all, it's merely a matter of adding an extra pair of chopsticks. Then it's settled." The merchant naturally agreed, finalizing their agreement.
