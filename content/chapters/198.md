# Chapter 198: Devilish Thoughts

Wang Boyan's face cycled between flush red and pale white. His heart was tied in knots, agonizing over potential gains and losses.

Li Qi, seeing his state, hardened his expression. "Brother Wang, I'll gift you a phrase."

Li Qi stood up from his stool and swept his arm outward, declaring, "Listening to advice is the prerequisite for an endeavor; planning is the timing of an endeavor! How can anyone in this world who schemes but misses the timing ever secure lasting peace?!"

Then, he lowered his arm and slammed his hand on the desk, reprimanding, "Timing! Oh, the timing! Opportunities pass without waiting for deliberation! One must gather rations and mount their steed, fearing only falling behind! Brother Wang, you've plotted back and forth—could it be that all your scheming has only resulted in petty penny-pinching?!"

"If you lose this time, will you die? Do you not have a single trick to save your life?"

"Challenging a Rank 7 alongside a Young Master of the Shaman God Mountain—when your family hears of this, will they scold your recklessness or praise your bravery? With this decision, even if you lose, once word spreads, will it damage your reputation, or will it make you renowned across All Under Heaven?"

"Not to mention, what if we succeed?"

"Can't you figure these things out? No, I actually think you see it very clearly."

"To know and yet not to act—is there such logic? To possess the wisdom to understand, yet lack the courage to execute—this is the root of disaster in all endeavors!"

"Therefore, wisdom lies in decisive action, while hesitation is the bane of all deeds! By agonizing over infinitesimal details, one forsakes the grand calculus of a lifetime!"

"The hesitation of a fierce tiger is inferior to the sting of a wasp; the faltering of a thoroughbred stallion cannot match the steady pace of an old nag. Brother Wang, compared to a mediocre man, you are the fierce tiger to the wasp, the thoroughbred to the nag. Do you not even have this level of decisiveness?"

"Can't you wrap your head around it yet? The moment you extended a hand to help the new Horse King, you were already tied to my ship!"

"Do you think that if Monk Chanzhi wins, he won't figure it out? Will the Wang Family not uncover the truth when they investigate the aftermath?"

"Endlessly hesitating, sitting idle to watch others succeed or fail—doesn't this invite disaster by dawn or dusk? When that time comes, what will be Brother Wang's ultimate fate?"

Wang Boyan was startled by Li Qi's reprimand. He subconsciously took a step back, a flash of irritation crossing his face.

However, before he could wallow in his anger, he heard Li Qi's subsequent words.

Then, Wang Boyan suddenly realized he had overlooked a crucial point.

_That's right._

From the moment he requested resources from his family to aid that new Horse King, he had essentially stood on Li Qi's side.

In all endeavors, the greatest taboo was being indecisive and playing both sides.

Especially when neither side was someone he could afford to provoke.

His train of thought immediately surged forward.

If he helped Li Qi, then in the eyes of his family, he would be entangled with the forces of the Shaman Dao. This wasn't a good thing.

This was also the primary reason he had been unwilling to help at the very beginning.

He had come to Baiyue as a nail driven by the forces of the Human Dao.

Just like the Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest.

That Heaven-Devouring Demon Nest was a nail driven into Baiyue by those great demons.

Only, the Demon Dao acted crudely. They didn't understand the principle of moistening things silently. They had forcefully smashed a peak Rank 5 straight in. Although they managed to establish a foothold through sheer brute force, they were tightly locked in place by the combined efforts of the Righteous Sword Palace and four kingdoms, unable to spread outward.

_Wait... Li Qi is hinting at something!_

Reaching this thought, Wang Boyan suddenly realized the implication!

The phrase _'Challenging a Rank 7 alongside a Young Master of the Shaman God Mountain'_ inherently represented a stance! It meant he had forged a connection with a Young Master of the Shaman God Mountain!

This was true silent moistening of things! Being able to work alongside a Young Master of the Shaman God Mountain represented a multitude of things in itself. The family branch... no, even the main family would only feel that compared to a paltry place like Vast Yang, this connection was undoubtedly far more important.

Li Qi seemed to be persuading him, but it was both a transaction and a threat!

He had no other path to choose.

_A miscalculation!_

_Wait! Back then, that woman—the one who became the Horse King—she explicitly stated that Li Qi sent her!_

_Motherfucker! Li Qi had already planned to drag me down into the muddy waters back then!_

His reaction had been a step too slow. He thought it was just a simple transaction, but the moment the deal was struck, the Human Dao forces in Vast Yang City were shackled to Li Qi. The only way out was to relieve Wang Boyan and appoint a new master.

But things having escalated to this point, could he even be removed?

Having extended his hand that one time, only a single path remained before Wang Boyan: sparing no effort to assist Li Qi, even if it meant risking every single asset he had accumulated in Baiyue over the years.

If his assets were gone, he could still give the main family an explanation. With the main family's backing, making a comeback was merely a matter of time.

Not to mention, there was actually a minuscule probability—

What if... perhaps it was truly just a "what if."

This Young Master, what if he...

_What if he won?_

If he won, then this time, Wang Boyan would likely become far more than a mere supervisor of a single prefecture.

Myriad thoughts raced through his mind, but in reality, only a fleeting instant had passed.

Wang Boyan was not an indecisive man. One could clearly see his true nature from the fact that he had schemed against Li Qi from the start, attempting to meddle in numerous affairs.

Just now, he had simply been terrified by the prospect of a Rank 7.

Nothing more. In his eyes, Li Qi's proposal was absolutely nothing but courting death.

A Rank 9 challenging a Rank 7—such a thing had never occurred in all of All Under Heaven.

But thinking carefully, was it truly just a Rank 9 challenging a Rank 7?

They controlled a considerable amount of Rank 8s and possessed abundant resources.

In essence, this was an ambush—an assassination attempt on a Rank 7 orchestrated by a Young Master of the Shaman God Mountain, utilizing the resources at his disposal.

It was still earth-shatteringly shocking, but it was far more comprehensible than a one-on-one duel.

If this endeavor succeeded, it would still be enough to be recorded in the annals of history, perhaps even written into the scrolls of sages.

Since that was the case...

Wang Boyan gritted his teeth and steadied his stance. "In all worldly affairs, to focus on the trivial while forgetting the grand will inevitably lead to harm! To be suspicious and hesitant will inevitably lead to regret! But to be decisive and dare to act, even ghosts and gods will give way, and success shall follow!"

"The Young Master's prestigious name is well-known, and all submit to you. Today, the demonic monk acts with tyranny, leaving Vast Yang in a state of panic, its people harboring dread and terror. Currently, many harbor schemes and long to fight back. It is already my greatest fortune to receive your favor! Thus, I dare to speak my mind fully—I am willing to meticulously ponder this plan for the Young Master and dedicate all my strength! Wherever the Young Master points his banner, how could I dare not respond?!"

His words were sonorous and firm as he announced his decision.

Li Qi burst into hearty laughter. Saying no more, he departed in high spirits, still clutching the rabbit in his arms.

He boarded his palanquin once more, departing with effortless grace amidst the beating of gongs and drums.

With Wang Boyan sorted out, the next target to seek was the Righteous Sword Palace.

Fortunately, the Righteous Sword Palace definitely wouldn't be hard to find. After all, he had left a letter for Zhang Lan on Old Ma.

Li Qi always had his own calculations brewing in his heart.

It was just that, much of the time, he appeared indecisive and hesitant precisely because he thought too much.

Several days ago, while strolling through Vast Yang City, ascending the five-story Buddhist Pagoda, visiting the minor world with Wang Boyan, and wandering the mountain forests with Li Zhufang, he had already been pondering all sorts of possibilities.

Now, it was time to put them into action.

---

Time flew by. A full seven days had passed.

Over these seven days, Li Qi had been coordinating the specific progression of his plans.

For instance, how Wang Boyan's resources were to be practically deployed, and what exactly the people of the Lower Nine Sects needed to do.

The grand plan was more or less set in stone. To execute a plan, it had to be divided into the decision-making level and the execution level.

Since the decisions were made, the next step was execution. Unfortunately, Li Qi didn't have any trustworthy subordinates to handle the execution, so he had to do everything himself.

Seven days later, the general arrangements were finally finalized.

During these seven days, Li Qi had been splitting his focus.

On one hand, his true body was continuously laboring for Chanzhi, analyzing the characteristics of various devils for him, and then using Shaman Dao methods to create corresponding arts to counter them.

This was difficult. Exceptionally difficult.

Normally, the vast majority of Rank 9s were simply endlessly learning the methods of others. They rarely pondered the essence of spell arts and didn't seek a deep understanding of cultivation methods.

To them, learning how to use it was enough.

Delving into the principles behind a spell art was considered a meaningless endeavor.

A thoroughly researched and mature spell art was akin to a fully manufactured rifle.

Hand it to an ordinary person—even if they don't understand the mechanics, as long as they pull the trigger, the weapon's power will naturally be unleashed from their hands.

For these spell arts, one simply needed to follow a step-by-step process to gather the required qi, then process it according to the specific method, and naturally, they could unleash various spell arts.

Even if they disassembled the rifle and comprehended all of its mechanics and working processes—essentially understanding the "Law" hidden within the "Art"—it would hold no meaning.

It wouldn't make the spell art any stronger, nor would it increase one's combat power.

A sharpshooter doesn't need to understand the mechanics of a gun to hit the bullseye with every shot; they just need to learn how to use it.

Thus, for a Rank 9 with a limited lifespan, this was a completely unnecessary investment of time. It was better to accelerate the improvement of their cultivation base, and slowly delve into such studies only after extending their life.

However, in reality, once someone grew accustomed to superficial understanding, they would lose the drive to pursue the truth any further.

Li Qi had always understood this principle. He had never fallen into that vicious cycle. Instead, he diligently studied books, continuously upgrading his knowledge and learning capabilities.

But he had never pushed himself to such extremes before.

Under external pressure, Li Qi was forced to embark on an investigative process. Throughout this ordeal, he constantly familiarized himself with the True Knowledge dao rhythm and honed his research and development skills.

It had only been ten days since Chanzhi captured him, yet he felt that his confusion regarding the Buddhist Sect, devilish thoughts, and many aspects of his own Shaman Dao had suddenly cleared up. He had accumulated a wealth of experience.

Originally, he had planned to learn slowly, setting aside whatever he truly couldn't grasp to study gradually in the future.

But humans were cheap like that—they needed a bit of pressure to get their brains working.

Over the last ten days, under the immense pressure from Monk Chanzhi, Li Qi's progress was meteoric.

First was the True Knowledge dao rhythm.

After truly mastering this dao rhythm, Li Qi could now confidently pat his chest and say that as long as the opponent was below Rank 8, absolutely no spell art could conceal its underlying principles from him.

If the progress bar could interrupt an opponent's physical movements.

Then the True Knowledge dao rhythm could directly interrupt the opponent's spell art itself.

He could achieve something similar to Shen Shuibi's feat: with a single point of a finger, the opponent's spell art would instantly plunge into chaos and self-destruct.

He had grown accustomed to analyzing spell arts. The devilish thoughts of these devils were constructed from spell arts. They shifted and morphed every passing second. Li Qi had to adapt to these changes, constantly perceiving their fluctuations, and then finding commonalities. Only then could he judge what "trend" the opponent leaned toward, and subsequently devise a method to suppress that trend.

It was like a random algorithm. Bolstered by an algorithm, these devilish thoughts continuously generated random numbers. Merely memorizing these random numbers was useless.

One needed to crack their essence, uncover the pattern between the random numbers, deduce the core algorithm, and finally shatter the algorithm itself.

This was exactly what Li Qi was doing every single day now.

The True Knowledge dao rhythm served as his tool of observation. Observe, contemplate, draw a conclusion, and then personally act to verify that conclusion. If he was wrong, he reflected on it until he finally got it right, securing the results.

When one repeats this process enough times, they'll find themselves growing increasingly intelligent.

Moreover, the knowledge they command will grow ever vaster.

More and more of the things he had read in books were transforming into skills he had mastered.

In just ten short days, Li Qi felt his comprehension of spell arts elevate to an entirely new level. The True Knowledge dao rhythm helped him store countless memories, ready to be accessed at any moment.

All this experience became purely his own.

Li Qi felt that these devils couldn't stump him anymore.

The remaining devils had merely become mechanical labor. As long as he kept working, continuing wouldn't yield any further gains.

Just as Li Qi was continuing his work, a sudden chill swept past his back.

He instantly dropped his task and turned his head to look behind.

Monk Chanzhi stood by the staircase, gazing amicably at Li Qi.

A shiver ran down Li Qi's spine. He hurriedly stood up. "Grandmaster, what brings you here?"

"Nothing much. I am merely astounded by your progress, my young friend. To think you have already reached the realm of the Mother of Buddhas." Monk Chanzhi pressed his hands together in prayer. With a gentle expression, he walked over to Li Qi.

Li Qi's heart skipped a beat as he asked, "May I ask what Grandmaster means by this?"

"The path of Arts spans endlessly in all directions; its quantities exceed the dust and sand, like the sands of the Ganges River[^1], immeasurable. Therefore, focusing purely on Arts brings no benefit. One must focus on the essence—this is the 'Buddhist Dharma'."

"Buddhas take the Dharma as their teacher; the Dharma is the Mother of Buddhas. 'Buddha' refers generally to all Buddhas, while the word 'Mother' signifies the origin of birth. Therefore, the Buddhist Dharma gives birth to all Arts. Being able to perceive the root of all Arts means you have stepped into the realm of the Mother of Buddhas. It has only been ten days. Such comprehension... truly shames this poor monk," Monk Chanzhi sighed in admiration.

Hearing this, Li Qi put on an expression of great joy and said happily, "So that is why Grandmaster came. I am honored by your praise! At this rate, I will be able to resolve your predicament much sooner, Grandmaster. Everyone is happy—truly a joyous occasion for all."

However, Chanzhi didn't look particularly pleased. He shook his head. "Although this is a good thing, something unfortunate has befallen you, my young friend. That is why this poor monk came looking for you."

"Ah? May I ask Grandmaster what it is?" Li Qi asked in confusion.

Chanzhi unclasped his hands and stepped right in front of Li Qi, saying, "It's like this. Perhaps you've been too tired recently, my young friend. Out of curiosity today, I observed your condition and discovered that your spirit isn't in very good shape."

Staring intently into Li Qi's eyes, Monk Chanzhi said in a soft, gentle tone, "My young friend, your divine soul seems somewhat damaged, doesn't it? Is it caused by exhaustion? Or is it due to the erosion of devilish thoughts?"

The moment this question was raised.

Li Qi's heart skipped a beat.

_My split soul... has been discovered?_

[^1]: Ganges is the holy river of Hindus.
