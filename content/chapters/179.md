# Chapter 179: Concealing Identity and Name

On the other side of this minor world, in its capital city.

Night had already fallen over the imperial palace.

Illuminated by the last rays of the setting sun, the vermilion roofs gleamed an even more vibrant crimson. It was as if real flames were dancing across them, or like a dazzling stream of blood cascading downward.

The palace boasted the utmost heights of architectural grandeur. Gold and jade shone brilliantly together, adorning purple doors and yellow pavilions. Carved balustrades sat upon jade steps, framing the crimson imperial staircases and painted courtyards.

Yet, it felt exceedingly cold.

The sweltering heat of the day had rapidly faded, replaced by an increasingly biting night wind that left one's hands and feet stiff.

Palace servants scurried about with extreme caution. There were many of them, but each moved gingerly and in a hurry, running errands ordered by their masters. Beyond that, not a single one dared utter a word.

Chilly, rigid, and suffused with an agonizingly suffocating pressure—this was the atmosphere of the imperial palace.

Burly, towering imperial guards patrolled everywhere. Under their watchful eyes, the palace servants kept as silent as cicadas in late autumn.

However, in a secluded corner, two servants carrying hot water whispered to one another.

"Did you hear? General Yang is dead."

"Ah? What happened? Didn't he just pacify the northern barbarian army and return in triumph? How could he be dead?"

"It seems His Majesty ordered his death. He committed suicide right there in the throne room."

"Ah? Suicide? What did he do wrong? Furthermore, I recall the General fought Balahan for six hours straight and both his arms were crippled. How could he commit suicide?"

"I heard His Majesty ordered him to bite down on a sword and smash his head against a pillar to his death."

After exchanging those words, the two servants fell dead silent, quietly continuing their trek through the brightly lit palace.

But they hadn't taken more than a few steps before a tall guard strode over and viciously kicked one of the servants to the ground.

The kick was incredibly heavy. The struck servant spat a mouthful of blood and passed out without even making a sound.

"L-Lord Guard, what is the meaning of this?" The remaining servant was scared out of his wits, falling to the ground and trembling like a leaf.

"The meaning? Recklessly discussing His Majesty is a capital offense," the guard stated expressionlessly. Drawing the blade at his waist, he swiftly and cleanly ended both their lives.

Then, he shot a look backward, and people soon arrived to carry the corpses away.

This scene seemed to have occurred many times before. The surrounding servants merely buried their heads lower and continued dutifully performing their tasks.

The only difference was that the atmosphere throughout the entire palace grew even more oppressive.

Among the countless servants who witnessed this, one trembled so violently he could barely hold the tray in his hands.

He was a pale, beardless young man of about twenty years old. Completely devoid of color, his lips were somewhat purplish-blue. Standing amongst a crowd of servants, he looked entirely unremarkable.

Seeing this, the old eunuch beside him spoke in a sharp, displeased voice. "What are you shaking for? Hold it steady, all of you!"

"Do you young ones understand the rules of the palace or not? You aren't holding trays of handkerchiefs; you are holding your own heads!"

"If the tray drops, your head drops! So regardless of what happens to anyone else, just mind your own business. Keep your heart calm, your hands steady, and everything else has nothing to do with you."

"Control your mouths, control your hands. Do that, and you'll have secured a foothold in this palace. Understood?"

The old eunuch lectured the group of trembling servants with his chin held high, thoroughly relishing his authority.

There were about ten or so servants in this group. Every one of them held a mahogany tray piled high with bathing supplies.

There were scented soaps, hot water, towels, floral dew, and the like.

However, the quantities were surprisingly massive.

Take the towels, for instance: there were four stacks of twenty-five, making exactly one hundred towels. All were embroidered with golden silk thread, depicting a yellow dragon playing with a pearl. The posture of the yellow dragon on each towel was completely unique.

Some had their heads raised, some were spraying water, some toyed with clouds, and some scaled mountains. Every single one boasted exquisite craftsmanship and immense value. Yet, there were an entire hundred of them here.

Finishing his lecture, the old eunuch watched as the servants all stood up straight and steadied their grips before nodding in satisfaction.

He then continued, "Remember this: when wiping His Majesty's body, a towel can only be used once. If it gets dirty, immediately hand it back to the person collecting them. You four are responsible for wiping his limbs. Remember to be slow and gentle, wiping until every pore is open. Understood?"

"Understood!" the four servants responsible for wiping the limbs replied respectfully.

"And you lot, have you memorized the signals? The moment the signal is given from within the room, you must enter to change and add water to the tub. Water cools quickly at night, so remember to be light and swift when adding the hot water."

The servants responsible for adding water immediately voiced their compliance as well.

Highly satisfied, the old eunuch inspected them one by one, issuing instructions like, "The water in the tub must be perfectly clean," "Balance the basin on your heads," and "No matter how exquisite the socks, they are only worn once and absolutely never a second time."

Finally, having finished his instructions, he waved his hand. "Go in. Remember, if you make a mistake, you'll be beaten to death without question!"

The servants hastily complied. Then, forming a line, they headed out to bathe His Majesty.

A task as simple as bathing required over twenty people to line up and attend. Every person carried a pile of incredibly exquisite items that were entirely single-use.

Such was the extravagance; such was the cruelty.

The procession of servants marched at a measured pace. The soles of their shoes brushed against the floor without making a sound. Every movement was perfectly measured, neither hasty nor clumsy. Seeing this, the leading old eunuch nodded in satisfaction and ceased his chattering.

It was obvious that this was the result of long-term training. Who knew how many servants had died before these elites were selected, deemed worthy to 'closely attend' to that Emperor.

The servant with the purplish-blue lips was among them, walking cautiously all the way into the inner palace.

Once inside.

Everything proceeded methodically.

First, four servants spread out to stand on all four sides. They wore identical clothing and possessed identical appearances.

Led by the presiding old eunuch, they knelt and offered their greetings to the sovereign.

After greeting him, the trays were taken by other servants. Oilcloths were laid out in the room, the bathtub was carried in, and warm water was poured. Then, they requested the Emperor undress.

The Emperor seemed somewhat exhausted. He stood perfectly still, allowing the eunuchs and maids to remove his clothing.

What followed were incredibly tedious rules. There were strict regulations for which basin and which cloth to use. Every servant kept their duties firmly in mind.

Only the Emperor appeared relaxed and natural. He utilized his servants as naturally as a modern person used household appliances.

As for what the servants treated as smart appliances thought? Who cared?

If they couldn't perform, they died.

In the year since the establishment of the Great Xin Dynasty, far too many servants had died for this very reason.

Under such immense pressure, the servants had been strictly trained in their every movement. In the process of being domesticated as tools, some even developed a twisted sense of pride. They acted much like the old eunuch, closely monitoring those who had not yet developed this pride, worried that they were unqualified as tools.

When it was the turn of the servant with the purplish-blue lips, he stepped forward slowly. In his hands, he held a tray carrying a stack of twenty-five towels.

The first fifty towels had already been used. He was in the third rotation, responsible for passing fresh towels to the wiping servants and carrying the dirty water out.

He walked all the way to the very front.

Then, he suddenly dropped his tray.

The expression on the endlessly chattering old eunuch's face instantly shifted to absolute terror.

_Oh no, it's a beheading for me._

The old eunuch thought to himself.

But before he could panic any further, he noticed that hidden among the stack of towels on the dropped tray was a razor-thin blade.

In the blink of an eye, the pale-lipped servant snatched the blade and hurled it forward.

A hum filled the surroundings. Countless ripples violently erupted across the surface of the bathwater. An ear-piercing screech echoed out, instantly shattering the surrounding glass windows.

The servants frantically covered their ears, and the once-orderly room descended into absolute chaos.

But the pale-lipped servant paid them no mind. In his eyes, there was only the Emperor.

Of everyone present, only the Emperor managed to react.

Grabbing a tray, the Emperor flipped it up. Over a dozen towels flew into the air, tangling together.

The blade was incomparably sharp, but it was a mundane object in the end. Piercing through over a dozen towels consecutively, much of its rotational force was sapped, and its speed slowed significantly. In that brief window, the Emperor had already leapt out of the bathtub.

It takes time to tell, but all of this occurred in the span of a single breath.

The old eunuch finally registered what was happening when the Emperor stood up. In his shrill voice, he screamed, "Assassi—!"

He couldn't finish the word. The servant violently swept his sleeves twice. A barrage of wooden splinters burst forth from within, scattering outward and instantly killing all the surrounding servants. A portion of the splinters shot directly at the Emperor.

Unflustered, the Emperor simply continued waving his towel, swatting all the incoming splinters out of the air.

Closing the distance in two rapid strides, the servant struck again, shaking two more razor-thin blades from his robes and launching another assault.

But the Emperor was prepared. This time, it was his turn to wave his hand.

The Emperor flicked two glass beads from the bathtub out of his hand. Tearing through the air with the force of a gale, they struck with a crisp _ding_. Sparks flew, and the two blades in the servant's hands snapped in half.

The servant frowned slightly, abandoning the throwing strategy.

If throwing knives were broken, their speed would be drastically reduced, rendering them useless for killing this man.

So, he decisively discarded them and changed his approach. Flicking a blade resembling a flexible sword from his belt, he brandished it and slashed toward the Emperor.

The Emperor, however, merely shook his head. "This level of skill... you must be Wind-Listening Blade Xiao Ming. To think a first-rate knight-errant of the Jianghu would come to assassinate me. What a pity. Your martial art is unrefined; I'm afraid you won't achieve your great endeavor today."

As he spoke, the Emperor even had the leisure to deflect the attacks while putting on his clothes.

Xiao Ming, the Wind-Listening Blade, slashed three times in rapid succession, all of which were effortlessly evaded. The Emperor even used that time to finish donning his dragon robes.

The fourth slash descended from above.

Veins bulged on Xiao Ming's forehead. This was the fastest strike he had delivered in his entire life.

In the past, his blade always made a sound.

This strike was completely silent.

Or rather—the sound couldn't catch up to the blade.

Even the previously relaxed Emperor was taken by surprise.

Unable to dodge in time, a gash was sliced across his dragon robes.

But this also ignited the Emperor's fury.

Forming his hand into a dragon's claw, a powerful suction force erupted from his palm. Xiao Ming was unexpectedly pulled right into it, only to be blasted away by a palm strike!

His ribs shattered instantly. Xiao Ming spat a huge mouthful of blood and flew thirty feet through the air, crashing hard into the wall.

The formerly ferocious assassin had instantly lost all combat capability.

The Emperor leisurely straightened his robes. "Weak. Is this the strength of a first-rate Jianghu expert? Truly far too weak."

Shaking his head, the Emperor spoke with a hint of boredom. "And here I actually had some expectations for you lot. Sigh. Aside from Balahan, is there truly no one in this world who can match me? Oh, there was also General Yang, but he's dead too. In today's world, there is no longer anyone who can be my opponent."

"What a pity. Looking at you, it seems that to assassinate this king, you actually became a eunuch?"

"Castrating yourself, entering the palace, and suffering for a whole year just to finally get an opportunity to approach me—only to discover that you were absolutely no match for me. Well? Are you frustrated?" the Emperor asked with a smile.

Xiao Ming spat a mouthful of bloody foam. "Bah! Tyrant!"

"You call me a tyrant? Yet I have unified the world." The Emperor wasn't in a rush to kill him. Instead, he struck up a conversation with keen interest.

"You unified the world, but the people feel nothing but hatred for you. Kill Xiao Ming today, and another Xiao Ming will come tomorrow. Unless you slaughter every person in the world, you will never kill us all." Xiao Ming's breath was weak. It was clear he wasn't going to survive, but he glared at the Emperor with absolute hatred until the very end.

"Hatred is useless. I don't need to slaughter every person in. I only need to slaughter your generation. The children of the future will all study in my schools. Your children will all become my loyal servants."

"By the way, seeing as you are about to die, I'll tell you a secret. Haven't you martial world folks always wanted to know where my peerless divine skill came from?"

"I come from another world. An accident brought me here, and only then did I realize that this place is far too weak."

"You lack proper methods, and your cultivation methods are garbage. That is why I was able to unify the rivers and mountains in just over a year."

"The martial art of your world is too pathetic. For me to accidentally arrive here and conquer this world... truly, heaven is helping me."

"Alright, having heard all that, it is time for you to die. From now on, this world will be my familial empire. Everything belongs to me."

"You castrated yourself and entered the palace to assassinate me. I will find your son, castrate him as well, and have both father and son serve me. Consider it a touching tale."

"I truly thank you. I have held this secret in my heart for far too long. Now that I have spoken it out loud, I feel much better."

Having rambled through his speech, the Emperor's expression lightened considerably.

Then, he stood up, preparing to finish the assassin off. However, the assassin named Xiao Ming hadn't even lasted that long; having heard the monologue, he had already succumbed to his severe wounds.

Unable to end the man with his own hands, the Emperor looked somewhat disappointed.

Yet, at that exact moment, a golden thread abruptly descended from the heavens.

The head of the previously wildly arrogant Emperor simply dropped to the floor.

Dead as dead could be.

Historical records of later generations, _The Primary Biography of Emperor Li_, documented:

"Emperor Li of the Xin Dynasty acted contrary to propriety and violated teachings, harming culture and tyrannizing the people. Ripping fetuses from wombs and severing shins were considered insufficiently cruel by his measure. To use all the bamboo on the mountains would not be enough to record his transgressions; to exhaust the rabbits in the mountains and marshes would not be enough to write out his sins. Since the dawn of civilization, among all the tyrant monarchs and brutal emperors recorded in the annals, there has never been one as extreme as he."

"When both men and gods lacked a true master, the altars of soil and grain were in peril, the world boiled over, the common people were displaced, the populace lived in terror as if crushed under a falling mountain, and the masses wailed with nowhere to seek refuge. Yet, there arose the righteous man Xiao Ming. Complying with heaven and acting in accordance with the people, he executed heavenly punishment. Castrating himself to go into hiding, he infiltrated the imperial palace and gave his life to assassinate Emperor Li, saving the people from the depths of suffering."
