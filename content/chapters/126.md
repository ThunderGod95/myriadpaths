# Chapter 126: Caught Off Guard

The vehicle moved at a crawl.

Technology in the Earth Oil World was not nearly as advanced as on Earth. Radio communications were only just beginning to emerge.

Consequently, it took Li Qi and his companions several days of continuous travel to finally reach the territory of the Polar Industrial Corporation.

Stepping out of the vehicle, the group surveyed the world of ice and snow before them.

It truly lived up to the name of a polar region.

Fortunately, everyone present possessed cultivation bases and had no fear of the biting cold.

However, this was still a city. Li Qi and the others had merely disembarked on its outskirts. Buildings surrounded them, yet not a single soul could be seen. The populace had likely been evacuated.

The moment his feet touched the ground, Li Qi sensed a massive surge of qi rushing toward him.

He immediately drew his willow branch and lashed it through the air!

A sharp crack echoed out. The encroaching qi abruptly began to recede, seemingly startled by the snap of the whip.

Li Qi naturally knew the reason behind this.

His willow branch contained Grand Invocator divine qi. Although the whip itself was of a low grade, merely Rank 8, it was more than enough to inspire awe and reverence among the disciples of Shaman God Mountain.

Lowering the whip, Li Qi called out, "Junior Brother, why not show yourself?"

By addressing the other party as Junior Brother right off the bat, he was taking deliberate advantage.

If the title of senior and junior were acknowledged, the strict etiquette of the Shaman Dao would force the opponent to bow their head in submission.

"Haha, how long have you even been in the sect to demand I call you Senior Brother? Why don't we lay out our lineages and properly sort out our seniority?" At this moment, a figure stepped out onto the nearby road.

Shen Shuibi cast a single glance at the figure and murmured to Li Qi, "It's a fake."

Li Qi turned to Bai Di. "Bai Di, go."

"I would not dare disobey the Young Master's command," Bai Di replied.

Then, he made his move.

The longsword left its sheath with a sharp ring!

Like a white swan gliding across the water, the strike was graceful yet utterly lethal. The approaching figure was instantly shredded to pieces!

Only after the figure shattered did the delayed, explosive cracks and booms of the sword breaking the sound barrier echo through the air.

Bai Di was a genius martial artist who was only a hair's breadth away from advancing to Rank 8 in the Martial Dao. Within Rank 9, his strength had arguably reached the absolute pinnacle.

He only lacked the comprehension of Martial Dao divine will to breakthrough to Rank 8.

However, after that initial figure crumbled away, countless more began pouring out from the surrounding buildings.

They all looked exactly the same.

Every single one of them was that ordinary, plain-looking man.

This entire area had been rigged with an illusion array!

"Haha! Junior Brother, attacking the moment you arrive? Didn't you say you wanted to negotiate? To strike so directly, could it be you fear me? Or perhaps negotiations were just a pretext, and you always intended to strike first? Is your mind full of nothing but treacherous schemes?" The countless figures spoke in unison. Their voices merged into a booming reverberation that echoed off the surrounding architecture.

This Diviner was brimming with confidence.

Woven from the power of the Human Dao and layered with his karmic interference, a Rank 9 Invocator would find it entirely impossible to see through this crowd illusion.

If the opponent couldn't locate him, he would claim the first victory. Li Qi would either have to admit his skills were inferior and inevitably stumble into subsequent traps, or rely on brute force to break the array.

Using brute force would be a massive loss of face, and it would leave him vulnerable to the Diviner's other hidden arrangements.

By then, victory would be firmly in his grasp.

He wore a faint smile, observing everything unfolding around him.

But what he hadn't anticipated was the woman standing beside the Invocator whispering into his ear. "Observe its patterns—it appears smooth and soft, neither rushed nor volatile. Yet, its exterior belies its true nature. The overarching formation is as direct as a thrown spear and as taut as a bowstring. It is evident that everything here is false. In such circumstances, where momentum follows form in a continuous cycle, the true hiding spot of this illusion array is right there."

"They are all fake. The real one is over there," Shen Shuibi concluded, using a spell art to mark the target for Bai Di.

Bai Di nodded in thanks. In the next instant, sword shadows danced wildly through the air!

Suddenly, a dark shadow surged from the empty air!

The shadow was a perfect mirror image of Bai Di. Wielding an identical sword, it materialized behind him in a flash and viciously slashed downward!

Bai Di deftly sidestepped the shadowy blade. Using the flat of his own sword, he parried the descending strike with ease. Simultaneously, he stepped forward, driving his knee between the opponent's legs to instantly close the distance. Crashing into the shadow's chest, he expertly hooked his foot behind its calf to sweep its leg.

Struck squarely in the chest by Bai Di's shoulder and tripped by the sweep, the shadow tumbled toward the ground. Yet, its reactions were incredibly swift. It wildly swung its sword with one hand to deter any follow-up attacks, while planting its other hand on the earth. Dropping to a half-kneel, it executed a fluid backward roll, sprung back to its feet, and reset its stance at a safe distance.

"What manner of thing is this?" Bai Di frowned.

Before he could ponder further, the sword-wielding shadow launched another fierce assault.

Bai Di could not afford to ignore the lethal strikes. Reluctant as he was, he had no choice but to meet the attack head-on.

Sword shadows blurred as a relentless barrage of ringing steel echoed through the air. The two combatants became a tangled whirlwind of blades, perfectly matched in a bitter stalemate.

"A shadow? Ah, this spell art... it's a Mirror." Shen Shuibi recognized it immediately.

"What is that?" Li Qi asked.

For the moment, Bai Di didn't seem to be in mortal peril, but the opponent's methods remained a mystery.

"A Mirror is exactly that—a looking glass. Ancient texts mention this as a method of your Shaman Dao. The Master of Fire used the sun-kindler to draw bright fire from the sun, and the moon-mirror to draw bright water from the moon, all to provide pure offerings for the sacrificial rites."

"His spell art is derived from this concept. He likely harnessed the principle of projecting the bright moon to forge an identical, illusory duplicate of his enemy," Shen Shuibi explained.

"Then how do we break it?" Li Qi asked as soon as she finished.

Shen Shuibi leaned in close and whispered the solution.

Nodding in satisfaction, Li Qi extended his hand. Wood qi surged forth.

This wisp of wood qi was refined from the Azure Dragon divine qi of springtime.

He then ignited it with a sliver of Vermillion Bird divine qi.

This was Flourishing Qi Fire, cultivated through the resonance of wood.

The Dragon Star was the essence of wood. When it appeared in the eastern sky during spring, its heart marked the zenith of fire; thus, it was used to seal flames.

The wood qi of late spring manifested in the dragon and birthed fire.

Such a concentrated fire qi could utterly disrupt the clear mirror.

Li Qi executed the spell art, using wood qi to draw forth the fire qi. Instantly, brilliant light flooded the surroundings. The shadow was immediately dispelled, and the mirror's projection shattered into chaos.

This was a true spell battle—exchanging techniques and dismantling the opponent's spell arts step by step.

Both Li Qi and the Diviner knew full well that the moment one party's spell art firmly took hold, the victor of the battle would be instantly decided.

They both wielded the spell arts of Shaman God Mountain, techniques that possessed incredibly terrifying power.

However, Li Qi merely glanced at the rabbit and smiled.

Having Shen Shuibi by his side during a spell battle was tantamount to outright cheating. The rabbit possessed vast, encyclopedic knowledge and piercingly sharp insight. With her, he was practically invincible.

With the shadow banished, Bai Di's hands were finally freed.

Seizing the opening, he lunged straight for the array core Shen Shuibi had marked. His sword light flared brilliantly, piercing the formation like a needle popping a balloon and shattering it utterly.

The countless figures thronging the surroundings disintegrated into nothingness.

The illusion array dissipated completely.

Seeing this, Li Qi shouted, "Junior Brother, do you still wish to fight? What other tricks do you have hidden away? You might as well bring them all out!"

"Do not call me Junior Brother." The Diviner stepped out from the shadows, his face dark and sullen.

"Your skills are too weak; you are unworthy of being my Senior Brother. I thought you would at least be Rank 9, but to think you haven't even entered the ranks. You relied entirely on that woman to break my array." The Diviner walked forward, looking exactly like his illusory copies. "Why don't you rely on your own strength to exchange a few moves with me?"

Li Qi couldn't help but laugh out loud. "What, are you jealous?"

To drive the point home, he deliberately pulled Shen Shuibi into a close embrace to show their intimacy.

Shen Shuibi didn't resist, but she looked up at Li Qi with visible worry in her eyes.

Her expression conveyed a very real, deep-seated anxiety.

Li Qi, however, noticed none of it.

He casually released her and strode forward. "So, Junior Brother, doesn't your throat hurt from shouting all the way over there? Why not take a few steps forward and sit down for a proper chat?"

"It's still a fake," Shen Shuibi warned once more. "But this time, there's a physical entity hidden inside."

"Bai Di," Li Qi commanded.

Bai Di nodded, his hand slicing down in a vicious chop.

The sword launched.

This time, it was a flying sword.

The blade shot forward with explosive force. Sword qi roared like thunder, and surging sword intent swept through the air, transforming mid-flight into a colossal beast. The beast held jade in its maw and wore heavy armor across its body—it had actually awakened a strand of divine qi!

This sword was also a dharma treasure. It was a Rank 9 dharma treasure gifted to Bai Di by the Bai family—the Xiezhi Sword, which contained a faint trace of Xiezhi divine qi.

The Xiezhi was a divine beast. Its bright, piercing eyes could discern loyalty from treachery, embodying the concept of 'Clarity'.

The single, unbending horn upon its brow represented 'Justice'.

This was a sword of Clarity and Justice. Once it locked onto a target, its strike would never miss; it would not return until it found its mark.

It seemed that upon hearing Shen Shuibi's warning about a 'physical entity,' Bai Di had realized the situation was treacherous. Rather than risking his own body in melee combat, he chose to attack with the giant beast forged from his sword qi.

"Young Master, I can't keep up the act!" the illusion suddenly bellowed. The facade ruptured, revealing a man within. Swathed in the providence of the Human Dao, he exerted his absolute limits to forcefully deflect the Xiezhi Sword.

Though knocked away, the Xiezhi Sword did not fall to the earth. Instead, it carved a sharp arc through the sky, whipping around to violently thrust at its target once more.

Bai Di instantly closed the distance, engaging the man in a fierce melee.

Surrounding buildings crumbled into dust as the battle descended into total chaos.

Yet, at this very moment, Li Qi seized the initiative to strike.

He had finally sensed it himself.

The Diviner's true location.

After the illusion shattered, his internal progress bar had clearly pinpointed the enemy's coordinates.

Thus, Li Qi launched a preemptive assault.

"Tch..." The Diviner spat in frustration.

"Today, your victory is unearned. You won solely because of that woman." Having said his piece, he actually... ran away.

That's right, he ran.

Li Qi clearly watched a world gate tear open, allowing the opponent to cleanly abandon this realm altogether.

Diviners were notoriously weak in direct combat. Engaging in a head-on clash with a charging Invocator—especially one who dual-cultivated the Martial Dao—was a mark of sheer stupidity. Fleeing was the far smarter choice.

Li Qi stood frozen in place.

_I won?_

Glancing over to the other battlefield, the Rank 9 of the Human Dao had already been slain beneath Bai Di's blade.

Bai Di was leaps and bounds stronger than his opponent, and armed with a dharma artifact, this outcome was hardly surprising.

"Haha, just as I thought. I won without even having to reveal my trump cards," Li Qi laughed, looking utterly self-satisfied.

Even a Diviner was incapable of withstanding a single bout against him.

"Bai Di! Excellent work. Go prepare the car; we're heading back!" Li Qi was in high spirits as he summoned Bai Di and issued his orders.

Bai Di naturally obeyed.

Soon after, the entire world was thrown into an uproar.

The Polar Industrial Corporation had fallen and was wholly assimilated by the Zhang Industrial Corporation.

Li Qi and his companions were treated with the utmost reverence.

The president of the Zhang Industrial Corporation successfully broke through to Rank 8. Naturally, Li Qi was hosted as an honored guest of the highest caliber.

The entire world now knew the name 'Li Qi.'

Li Qi, however, paid little mind to a mere Rank 8 like Zhang Le. That man had already reached the absolute limit of his potential in this lifetime, while Li Qi still had an incredibly boundless future ahead of him.

He had even managed to play the Heavenly Dao of this world in the palm of his hand.

In this world, he was truly invincible.

A week later, Li Qi found himself lounging atop a towering skyscraper.

The scenery was spectacular, the decor was opulent, and there was even a luxurious rooftop pool.

Having just finished his cultivation session, Li Qi was relaxing on a lounge chair, standing alone at the pinnacle of the world.

"Bai Di, pour me a glass of water," Li Qi demanded, lazily extending a hand.

This time, however, Bai Di did not hand over a glass.

Li Qi frowned in confusion.

Normally, Bai Di was instantly at his beck and call. What was going on now?

A spike of irritation flared within him. He stood up and barked, "Bai Di! What is the meaning of this!"

Yet, total silence answered him.

As he stood there, a sudden wave of dizziness washed over Li Qi.

Where was everyone? Even if Bai Di wasn't here, shouldn't there be other attendants waiting on him?

Where was Shen Shuibi? Why had the rabbit vanished as well?

_Wait, why... why is there no sun in the sky?_

A torrent of terrifying questions crashed into his mind.

At that exact moment, a faint sigh seemed to drift down from the distant horizon.

Li Qi's eyes snapped to the small table beside him. A piece of paper had suddenly appeared out of thin air.

He walked over, picked it up, and discovered a single line of text scrawled across it.

_"Since when did you start calling him Bai Di, instead of Brother Bai?"_

Upon reading those words, Li Qi was instantly drenched in a cold sweat.

_That's right... when did I start addressing him solely by his full name?_

_When did I become so unfathomably arrogant?_

The realization struck him like a bucket of freezing ice water poured over his head.

_Since when?_

_Was it when the Grand Invocator accepted me as his apprentice?_

_Was it after witnessing the apocalyptic battles between demon kings?_

_And when did I start mindlessly turning to Shen Shuibi the second I encountered a problem? In that previous battle, I didn't use a single ounce of my own brainpower. I just threw out orders and asked her for answers._

_No, this isn't me!_

_And who left me this warning?!_

Suddenly, Li Qi remembered every tiny inconsistency he had noticed recently.

_Was it all because of... my arrogance?_

The quiet hubris festering in his heart had become a fatal flaw.

Arrogance had completely clouded his judgment.

He had truly started to view himself as some untouchable, high-and-mighty 'Young Master', treating those beneath him as lesser beings.

He had even subconsciously begun to look down on Shen Shuibi.

_How dare I? What right did I ever have to act like that?_

Shaken to his core and sweating profusely, Li Qi immediately sat down cross-legged, shut his eyes, and began to deeply reflect on himself.

None of this was real.

Yet, he finally recalled the repeated, meaningful glances Shen Shuibi had been shooting him within the illusion.

The disturbing anomalies surrounding him now were definitely not flaws in the construction of the illusion array.

Regardless of the mechanics, this spell art had explicitly taken root in the vulnerabilities of his Dao Heart.

It had latched onto the suffocating arrogance that had suddenly swelled within him recently.

Smooth sailing, endless flattery from all manner of great powers, a beautiful woman constantly at his side, the blind adoration of an entire world, and everyone he met bowing and addressing him as 'Young Master'.

This was exactly how the past few days had played out, wasn't it?

Even someone like Li Qi couldn't help but lose himself and become profoundly conceited under such intoxicating circumstances.

"Is this my fatal flaw... my recent hubris?" Taking the harsh lesson to heart, Li Qi sank into deep introspection.

There was still time to fix this.
