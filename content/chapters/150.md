# Chapter 150: The Assembly

Li Qi sat in his room, tapping his fingers against the table as he pondered the situation.

Jin Buhuan's personal strength was more than a tier beneath his own, but the power of that streak of metal qi was truly formidable. As long as the might of that metal qi erupted, all of Li Qi's defensive methods would be rendered useless, as flimsy as paper.

Even dodging was highly improbable. His reaction speed simply could not keep up with the velocity of that metal qi.

In short, with a streak of Rank 8 metal qi in hand, Jin Buhuan could treat any number of Rank 9 cultivators like worthless weeds, rapidly harvesting their lives, provided his opponents lacked a countermeasure. By merely circulating the metal qi and taking a stroll, every enemy in his path would lose their head.

This was the power of Rank 8... Even if it wasn't a cultivator, but merely a weapon.

Li Qi fancied himself as having few rivals among Rank 9, but facing a Rank 8 innate divine weapon, he could only offer his neck to the slaughter.

At times like this, it became glaringly obvious just how absurd Shen Shuibi was.

How had she managed to nearly beat another Rank 8 to death relying solely on a Rank 9 cultivation base?

However, Jin Buhuan's metal qi should not be easy to unleash.

Using a Rank 9 cultivation base to drive a Rank 8 innate divine weapon was no simple task. His inability to control his divine body was likely one of the side effects.

By utilizing the Shaman Dao's ability to observe the flow of qi through all things in the world, Li Qi could clearly see why Jin Buhuan had departed while maintaining that tiger-headed, human-bodied form.

It was because he could not control it.

According to Li Qi's own deductions, an ordinary Rank 9 would find it extremely difficult to wield a Rank 8 divine weapon. Jin Buhuan was the same, but he possessed another trump card: his father was the mountain god of Green Mountain.

Mountain and water spirits were all classified as Earthly Divinities, which happened to be Li Qi's area of expertise. He was well aware that most cultivators on the divine path possessed certain anomalies. As a spirit of metal nurtured by Green Mountain—a native-born White Tiger—his child should also inherit some of those traits.

As a divine scion, Jin Buhuan relied on forcefully activating his traits as a metal spirit to barely manage the manipulation of the Rank 8 metal qi. The side effect, however, was an imbalance of the metal element within his body, causing metal qi to overflow and saturate him.

The imbalance of the five elements within his body resulted in his previous state. He was unable to retract his alternate form, and his voice carried a hoarse quality, as though he had fallen ill.

This was because the lungs belonged to the metal element. As the imbalanced metal qi expanded, his lung meridians dilated with it, resulting in his heavy panting and the metallic timbre in his voice.

As Li Qi understood it, metal qi converged in the lungs, and an excess of it would afflict the liver. When the metal element became too dominant, the lungs themselves would sicken.

Jin Buhuan's activation of the Rank 8 metal qi would damage his lungs and liver. It did not seem like this was his first time using it, so Li Qi could reasonably deduce that both his lung meridians and his liver had suffered varying degrees of metal qi damage.

This was his weakness, and it should be exploitable.

Furthermore, there was his cultivation method.

Metal sound...

Li Qi did not know which Daoist Tradition his cultivation method belonged to, but his training was clearly focused on this aspect.

Li Qi knew that making sacrifices to the Western White Emperor—the Metal Emperor among the Heavenly Gods—required singing lyrics with metal sounds and playing the _shang_ note. The _shang_ note also belonged to the metal element.[^1]

The *shang* note resonated like chime stones, its intent was joyful, and its meridian was the hand Supreme Yin. If it reversed into the Bright Yang meridian, the defensive and nutritive qi would be blocked, yin and yang would invert, Yang qi would strike inward, and Yin qi would cause external injury.[^2]

The metal sound was bleak and murderous, and the _shang_ note was tyrannical. It was like a severe wind thrashing stalks and heavy frost withering leaves; striking it would resound through the heavenly gates[^3]. Therefore, his habitual fighting method was to use this bleak, tyrannical sound, combined with his dao rhythm, to shock the opponent's heavenly gates[^4] and mind, followed by a sudden, decisive strike.

Moreover, looking at his tapping technique.

Every time he exerted force, he invariably tapped the metal qi in his hand to match the pitch pipes' musical tones.

Was he mimicking a _bo_ bell[^5]?

Legend had it that Ling Lun cast twelve bells to harmonize metal sounds, and the _bo_ bell was the very first metal-sounding instrument. Jin Buhuan was using the innate divine weapon to imitate the sound of a _bo_ bell, fulfilling the requirements of his cultivation method while simultaneously retaining the advantage of sudden, lethal strikes.

So, what were the weaknesses?

The first weakness: he could not constantly activate the Rank 8 metal qi, as doing so would damage his cultivation foundation. If he overused it, his life might even be in danger.

The second weakness lay within the metal sound itself. Ancient texts recorded that regarding metallic sounds, if the metal was too hard and rigid without being tempered by fire, the person would become mute. Solid metal was inherently silent.

This meant that metallic objects were originally soundless and needed to be processed and cast to produce noise. Just like the original 'Ling Lun casting the twelve bells', without processing, a bell would remain a silent object.

To unleash his power, he definitely needed an instrument. He currently held that streak of Rank 8 metal qi, but he surely had other backup tools as well. He couldn't just pick up anything and use it.

If he could be stripped of his equipment, ninety percent of his combat prowess would likely be eliminated. The remaining ten percent would probably rely on his vocal cords, issuing the _shang_ note from his lungs, which might still allow him to put up a fight.

He definitely had other protective items on him, but his other combat capabilities had to be whittled down first to force out his trump cards.

This was the method to physically destroy Jin Buhuan.

But to truly topple Jin Buhuan, physical destruction was far from enough.

Jin Buhuan represented Green Mountain's deeply entrenched faction within Clear Mist City. Green Mountain itself was the iconic landmark of the Green Mountain Kingdom, and could even be considered its true pillar.

The Green Mountain god had achieved the Dao at Rank 6. According to the White Snake mountain god, he was a pinnacle existence even among Rank 6, and within the territory of Green Mountain, he could even contend against a Rank 5. At the very least, the lord of the Green Mountain Kingdom could do nothing about him.

As a metal spirit, he was a natural-born anomaly with tremendous killing power, and rumors said his inheritance was extremely profound.

One had to remember... the Four Symbols of the Heavens, the four supreme Heavenly Gods governing the four quadrants of the firmament above, included the White Tiger.

The firmament across the infinite outer-realm worlds was identical. No matter which world one was in, they all shared the same starry sky and the same Four Symbols of the Heavens.

What kind of majestic power was that?

Although they were both White Tigers, and one absolutely could not compare to the other, the Green Mountain god definitely had his own unique trump cards and could not be underestimated.

So, what would be the result of this?

Jin Buhuan's conduct within the city had long since earned him a pile of enemies.

At the same time, did the actions of the Green Mountain god, Jin Chi, within the Green Mountain Kingdom earn him no enemies?

Take the existence of Clear Mist City alone—did the lord of the Green Mountain Kingdom exert no effort in its creation?

Clear Mist City took root right on the edge of Green Mountain. Because it was exactly on the border, the Green Mountain god couldn't reach his hands into it, yet it was positioned perfectly to become a commercial and transportation hub.

If this was merely a coincidence, Li Qi would chop off his own head.

This proved that the lord of the Green Mountain Kingdom had long harbored grievances against the Green Mountain god. He simply was unwilling or unable to tear away all pretense of cordiality due to certain matters or a lack of strength.

But Clear Mist City and the Clear Mist Sect should both be chess pieces he placed to restrict Green Mountain, that massive dragon.

Initially, Li Qi had no desire to get involved. He just wanted to finish his business and run away. However, Jin Buhuan's actions had already breached his bottom line of righteousness. Therefore, despite the immense risks, he had to carry this through.

He needed to unite every force that could be united.

He, Li Qi, could serve as the sharp blade the lord of the Green Mountain Kingdom thrust at the Green Mountain god.

Actually, there was a much simpler solution: directly reveal his identity and declare himself the young master of Shaman God Mountain.

The moment he threw out that title, both the lord of the Green Mountain Kingdom and the Green Mountain god would instantly change their attitudes.

A mere Jin Buhuan might be immediately stripped of his heir status, and perhaps his severed head would even be presented to Li Qi as an apology.

But that wouldn't do; it wasn't right.

The young master of Shaman God Mountain was just a title. It didn't mean he was actually all that powerful.

Parading around all day using the title of Shaman God Mountain's young master would certainly force many to back down, but it would also attract individuals who were truly capable of challenging such a young master.

If he ran his mouth about being the young master of Shaman God Mountain every day, eventually an existence capable of challenging Shaman God Mountain would come knocking for trouble. At that point, he would painfully learn the harsh realities of the world.

Right now, the one he had provoked was just Jin Buhuan. If he loudly proclaimed himself to be from Shaman God Mountain, the next enemy to arrive might be ten or twenty times stronger than Jin Buhuan.

A reputation that exceeded reality would inevitably invite disaster.

Until he truly possessed the strength to match the title of Shaman God Mountain, Li Qi would shoulder the consequences of any trouble he stirred up himself.

This could be considered a form of experiential tempering.

If he had to rely on Shaman God Mountain's resources to resolve everything, wouldn't he just be a piece of trash?

Since Li Qi chose to meddle in these affairs, it was only natural that he resolved them himself. He couldn't just stick his nose into things and then go crying to his elders for help the moment trouble arose.

What kind of look would that be? He'd be absolute trash.

He had to shoulder the burden of the meddling he chose to do.

Even if it meant potentially provoking the Green Mountain mountain god.

But managing this affair inherently required settling things with the Green Mountain mountain god anyway.

Finding a way to prevent the mountain god from intervening, or finding someone who could protect him if the god did intervene—that was what Li Qi needed to do after bringing down Jin Buhuan.

"The strategy is set, then. I just need to wait for the others to arrive." Li Qi let out a long breath, stood up, and stretched his back.

Piaoxu had already gone to deliver his messages. Those addresses actually belonged to the people who had previously invited Li Qi, attempting to recruit him to their faction to oppose Jin Buhuan.

These people would become the force that toppled Jin Buhuan.

Li Qi was responsible for destroying Jin Buhuan physically, while they would be responsible for dismantling his faction.

"Calculating the time, they should be arriving soon, right?" Li Qi shook his head, muttering to himself.

Right at that moment, Shen Shuibi's voice suddenly rang out from behind him. "Li Qi!"

"Ah, what is it, Miss Shen?" Li Qi turned his head, temporarily casting aside the schemes swirling in his mind. Putting on a smile, he looked at Shen Shuibi.

"The formation is all set up. At the very least, our personal safety shouldn't be an issue. It's just that I didn't have any treasures on hand to reinforce the formation... I could only rely on the favorable timing and terrain." Shen Shuibi walked over, letting out a long sigh, her face plastered with an expression that practically screamed for praise.

Li Qi responded, "You've worked hard. I'll have Piaoxu make you an extra meal tonight using the ingredients out there. They're all good stuff. I'll leave them to Piaoxu to handle; I won't fight you for them. I'll even show off a few of my own cooking skills for you!"

As he spoke, he pointed toward the gift carriages outside.

Hearing this, Shen Shuibi's eyes narrowed into joyful crescents, and she skipped outside to pick out ingredients.

She had already forgotten what she originally came in for.

She had actually come to complain.

Li Qi was too good at bossing people around, casually asking for things that were incredibly difficult. Fortunately, she understood it all!

_Hmph, even Yang Ning probably wouldn't be able to set up this formation._ With no suppression items and only a Rank 9 cultivation base, it was constructed purely through sheer technique.

It was a pity Li Qi didn't understand formations.

If he did, he might have understood that Shen Shuibi had essentially built a functioning CPU out of sand with her bare hands.

The level of difficulty was so high it bordered on the unimaginable.

Relying purely on talismanic script and guiding the momentum of heaven and earth—without formation disks, formation flags, or precious materials, essentially creating something out of nothing to complete a formation capable of temporarily withstanding a Rank 8 assault—was nothing short of appalling.

There was a formation of a similar grade in Jin Buhuan's manor. Jin Buhuan had bled profusely to get it. The formation master he hired had walked around with his nose in the air, charged an exorbitant fee, and spent half a year meticulously surveying and deducing. It had drained Jin Buhuan's years of savings just to set it up.

Even then, Jin Buhuan felt he had scored a massive bargain. Formations capable of blocking frontal attacks from Rank 8 experts were exceedingly rare, and formation masters who could construct them were treated as highly treasured assets.

Who knew what he would think if he found out that Shen Shuibi had demanded nothing, and within a single day, slapped together these exact same results with her bare hands.

Perhaps he would regret offending Li Qi.

---

Putting those matters aside, very soon, individuals with covert movements began to appear before Li Qi one by one.

The myriad of methods they employed left Li Qi marveling in astonishment.

The first to arrive was through the tea in Li Qi's hand.

He had just been waiting when the tea suddenly split into two masses. It flew up, coiling and twisting, spiraling and bending. After folding over itself several times in midair, it surprisingly transformed into a water mirror. An illusory figure was projected from within, and then the mirror collapsed. The figure cupped his hands toward Li Qi. "Has Young Master Li finally made up his mind?"

"The cultivation method of the Misty Rain Pavilion is truly profound and mysterious. With such assistance, how could I possibly swallow my anger?" Li Qi replied with a smile.

A short while later, the talisman bamboo plant placed in the corner of the inn suddenly sprouted new shoots, bloomed, and bore fruit.

The flowers were as pure as mutton-fat jade and as delicate as crane feathers. The fruits were a plump, snowy pink, dotted with star-like specs of red and purple.

A fruit split open, revealing a tiny person inside. Although he was only the size of a thumb, his clothes were neat, and his features were handsome.

The moment he appeared, the tiny person spoke in a resonant voice, "Young Master issued a sudden invitation, and the Junzhi Pavilion would not dare refuse to come. However, I didn't expect the Misty Rain Pavilion to be here as well."

Upon hearing this, a voice suddenly rang out from the air. "Haha, there's more than just the two of you!"

Following that, a large character appeared in midair. Looking closely, it was a massive character for 'Door'.

As soon as the 'Door' character materialized, someone pushed it open from the inside. A man dressed as a scholar stepped out, an ink brush in his hand. "The Plum Blossom Bookstore has arrived. Are there any other friends present?"

Before long, various methods were deployed, bringing about ten people in total. All of them arrived in utmost secrecy—whether through concealment, projection, or teleportation—ensuring no one would detect them along the way.

As for the room itself, Shen Shuibi's formation was already active, meaning the outside world could not easily pry into what was happening inside.

If Shen Shuibi hadn't placed a marker on the concealment formation she carved onto Piaoxu to provide them with a method of entry, even these people's spell arts would not have been able to penetrate the defenses, preventing them from entering entirely.

Seeing that everyone had gathered, Li Qi cupped his hands. "Everyone, I will skip the idle chatter and be direct. Tonight is the night Jin Buhuan dies."

[^1]: Ancient Chinese music was built on a 5-note scale (roughly sounding like Do, Re, Mi, Sol, La). Shang is the second note (similar to "Re"). Because it is tied to the Metal element and autumn, it does not sound soft or flowing. Instead, it has a sharp, clear, and heavy metallic resonance, like the lingering ring of a struck bronze bell or stone chime. Culturally, it evokes the feeling of a harsh autumn wind—carrying a bleak, mournful, and "murderous" martial intent.
[^2]: These are concepts from Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM). The Metal element governs the lungs, and the "Hand Supreme Yin" refers specifically to the Lung Meridian. "Bright Yang" (Yangming) refers to its paired meridian (the Large Intestine).
[^3]: Here, "heavenly gates" literally refer to the gate of the celestial realm where the Heavenly Gods reside. "Heavenly Gates" is used to describe the sheer, terrifying magnitude of the _shang_ note. It means the sound is so powerful, piercing, and tyrannical that its echoes reach all the way to the highest heavens, demanding the attention of the gods themselves.
[^4]:
    Here, the meaning shifts from the universe to the human body.
    
    In Daoist inner alchemy and Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), the human body is a viewed as a miniature universe (a "personal cosmos").
    
    In this context, "heavenly gate" refers to the spiritual center of a person. It is usually associated with the top of the head (specifically the Baihui acupoint at the crown), which Daoists believe is the gateway where spiritual energy enters and exits the body, and where the soul or consciousness resides.

    In summary, the sound is loud enough to shake the heavens, and precise enough to directly stun a cultivator's soul.
[^5]:
    Bo Bell (镈钟 - bózhōng) is an ancient, massive Chinese bronze bell used in grand state rituals and ceremonial music. Unlike smaller, melodic chime bells, a bo bell is exceptionally large, hung vertically from a sturdy wooden frame, and struck with a heavy wooden beam or mallet.
