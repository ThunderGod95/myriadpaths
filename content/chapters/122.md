# Chapter 122: I Thought of Something Very Funny

New cars, new roads, new atmosphere.

After the previous convoy and road were blown up, Zhang Deming made a call and summoned another convoy.

This one arrived fully armed, featuring blast-resistant vehicles and large trucks, with only a single luxury car in the middle meant for Li Qi and his group.

However, Li Qi refused the luxury car. Instead, he boarded one of the large trucks, had the others empty the compartment of its various military supplies, and walked inside.

Now, five people sat in the back of the open-topped truck bed.

Li Qi sat among them, clearly acting as the leader of the five.

Bai Di stood behind Li Qi, sword in hand. He didn't sit; instead, he stood like a loyal subordinate.

Shen Shuibi was in the back playing with a jigsaw puzzle, acting as if nothing happening had anything to do with her. She had clearly come with an attitude of complete indifference.

As for the two in front of him, they stretched their limbs while watching Li Qi warily.

First was the lioness. According to her self-introduction, her name was Sha'er. She was a so-called 'Type-A modified human' and the Platoon Leader of that nameless resistance organization—a bona fide Rank 9 combatant.

Of course, she was only a Platoon Leader, not the overall leader.

As for the man, his name was Lu Li. He was Sha'er's deputy and also a capable fighter. He cultivated some fragmented cultivation methods passed down by the 'Exalted Immortals', roughly similar to the Wave-Shattering Force. He was unranked, but when combined with modern equipment and tactical weapons, his combat prowess could barely be considered Rank 9.

However, any random Rank 9 could easily defeat him; it would merely be a question of how long it took.

"You see, I said I would untie you and I did. That shows a lot of sincerity, right?" Li Qi said with a smile, the words 'amiable and approachable' practically written all over his face.

"What's the use of untying us? Could we possibly have the ability to resist in front of an 'Exalted Immortal'? What difference does it make whether we're tied up or not?" Lu Li said, his gaze wary, his entire body on high alert.

Sha'er, however, stopped Lu Li.

She didn't speak, merely thinking quietly.

Seeing this, Bai Di spoke up, "If the Young Master wanted to torture you, he has plenty of methods. You might think your will is firm enough to resist all forms of torture, but that's only because you haven't witnessed the methods of the Shaman Dao..."

"Stop, Brother Bai. Why are you making me sound like some grand villain... I really don't know how to torture people." Li Qi smiled bitterly. He was still trying to create a harmonious atmosphere here.

Bai Di stopped speaking, feeling somewhat puzzled.

This was a resource world to begin with, and they were indeed doing things to suppress this realm's Heavenly Dao. Did the Young Master intend to persuade the Great Deer Kingdom to willingly give up this world?

"_Ahem_, in short, you two need to know that I'm seriously trying to resolve this matter. Your world's Heavenly Dao is about to drop in rank. Although I don't know if you understand this concept, as Children of Destiny, you should be able to vaguely sense that this is happening."

"And I am different from the person secretly manipulating the Heavenly Dao and trying to push it into the abyss. I feel that such actions harm the harmony of heaven, and I want to help you," Li Qi said to Lu Li and Sha'er, his tone extremely serious.

At this moment, Shen Shuibi suddenly looked up. "Li Qi, are you trying to get the Heavenly Dao's recompense?"

"_Ahem_, quiet down, rabbit." Li Qi turned his head and glared at Shen Shuibi. This rabbit was always exceptionally sharp about these kinds of things!

Shen Shuibi immediately buried her head again and went back to playing with her puzzle.

However, she was right.

The Heavenly Dao was similar to a mountain spirit; both were existences that possessed a spirit but lacked sentience and awareness.

When encountering a crisis, they could produce stress responses to prevent danger, and naturally, they would also bestow different rewards based on the situation.

Stopping the Heavenly Dao from dropping in rank would definitely compel it to bestow a recompense.

Thinking of this, Li Qi suddenly realized something.

"Wait, doesn't the Diviner lineage have an art of shifting blame?" Li Qi turned to look at Shen Shuibi. She knew a lot.

"Of course they do, didn't we talk about this before? Back in that... Feng County, with the little sister from the Wang family, I already mentioned that the Diviner lineage has methods to shift blame," Shen Shuibi answered without lifting her head.

"Then tell me, can we reverse this situation? The moment we arrived here, we were bombarded. If you want to call it a coincidence, I think that's highly unlikely."

"Tell me, hypothetically speaking, if someone 'shifted the blame' for something onto us, and then I flew into a rage and prepared to teach this realm's Heavenly Dao a harsh lesson... If the other party turned around and saved this world at that very moment, wouldn't they be the one to receive the Heavenly Dao's recompense?" Li Qi said to the others.

"This way, he wouldn't have to endure the Great Deer Kingdom's fury, nor would he have to worry about the risk of offending the Great Deer Kingdom's ruler. If his goal from the very beginning was to enrage me into doing this, the ruler wouldn't be able to say anything about it either."

Li Qi looked around and said to everyone, "Deducing from motives, even though this theory lacks supporting evidence, doesn't it make a lot of sense?"

Hearing this, Bai Di felt as if he had been enlightened. He looked at Li Qi with admiration. "The Young Master is truly sharp-witted. This isn't an impossibility; we must make preparations in advance!"

Shen Shuibi, on the other hand, didn't seem to have much of a reaction.

To her, it made no difference.

Lu Li and Sha'er, however, still showed no response.

Lu Li didn't believe Li Qi's current performance, so no matter what Li Qi did, he maintained an attitude of complete disbelief.

But Li Qi also knew that Lu Li didn't call the shots; his approval or disapproval wasn't important.

Thus, Li Qi looked at the woman who resembled a lioness. "Miss Sha'er, what do you think? Do you think what I said makes sense?"

At this moment, Sha'er spoke.

Her throat bobbed. Her voice was similar to her physique—extremely rough, like a wild beast forcefully trying to speak.

Presumably, her vocal cord structure was already entirely different from a human's. Speaking right now was merely a forced vocalization.

"It makes sense," the grating voice said.

"But there is no evidence."

She spoke concisely. After finishing those two sentences, she clamped her mouth shut again.

"The Young Master's words _are_ the evidence. Do you lowly people still expect the Young Master to prove something to you?" Bai Di reprimanded, an expression of displeasure on his face.

Li Qi felt a massive headache coming on.

"_Ahem_, alright, Brother Bai, say no more. We lead by reason, we lead by reason." Li Qi stopped Bai Di before his own secondhand embarrassment flared up.

He still hadn't adapted to the days of being some 'Young Master', nor was he accustomed to the feeling of being superior to others.

"Miss Sha'er, I do indeed understand what you mean. These 'Exalted Immortals' have controlled this world for a long time, continuously preventing its freedom and excavating the earth qi here until it is nearly depleted."

"However, think about it from another perspective. It's precisely because of this that the Exalted Immortals need to maintain this world's rank. The earth oil produced by a Rank 8 world and the earth oil produced by a Rank 9 world are on entirely different levels."

"Even if it's just to safeguard the Exalted Immortals' interests, I still have a reason to help you maintain this realm's Heavenly Dao's rank. Miss Sha'er, wouldn't you say that's the logic here?" Li Qi asked.

Sha'er opened her mouth, looked at Li Qi, and said, "That is the logic... but, there is still no evidence."

"I understand. You think it's still possible that I harbor ulterior motives against you, right? So you're worried this posture of mine is just to swindle your trust. You feel that the things backing you are very important—important enough that they are still worth the covetous gaze of us Exalted Immortals." Li Qi looked at Sha'er and spoke straightforwardly.

Sha'er kept her mouth shut and said no more.

"It's normal that you don't understand, though. Brother Bai, have the front stop the vehicles," Li Qi said.

Bai Di directly leapt out of the truck bed, ran up to open the door, smoothly sat in the front passenger seat, and issued orders to the driver, telling them to halt.

Soon, the entire convoy ground to a halt.

Many of the fully armed soldiers were confused, but having received orders, they didn't dare ask too many questions.

Zhang Deming, who was in charge of commanding, trembled with fear, thinking some major incident was about to happen again. But Bai Di told him to shut up and ordered everyone else to stand by.

Li Qi ignored all this. Instead, he walked straight out of the truck bed and into the barren desert.

Then, he turned around and looked at Sha'er and Lu Li. "Watch closely. After you've seen it, think about whether I truly harbor any ulterior motives. It won't be too late to make a decision then."

After speaking, Li Qi began walking forward at the speed of a normal person. It looked very slow.

After he had walked for a little over a minute, everyone was completely baffled by his behavior.

Then, Li Qi looked back, stomped on the ground a few times, and seemed to be estimating the distance.

Following that, he walked forward for another minute or so, this time quickening his pace until he was almost so far away that the others could only see a small dot.

After two or three minutes had passed, he finally stood still, reached out a hand, and lightly patted the ground.

Then he ran back.

After another two or three minutes, he ran back to the convoy.

Everyone wore blank expressions; Lu Li and Sha'er were even more perplexed.

Except for Shen Shuibi.

The rabbit had already tucked her head down and plugged her ears. She had already figured out what Li Qi had done.

Just when everyone was looking completely bewildered...

Suddenly.

An earthquake struck.

From deep underground, to the rock layer interfaces, and then to the surface, massive tremors propagated along the interfaces and the ground, as if a bomb had suddenly detonated!

The intense earthquake triggered landslides, collapses, and ground fissures. The earth visibly swayed. The seismic shaking caused the topsoil to sink, and the squeezed shallow groundwater began spewing out like fountains. The areas closest to the surface even experienced localized topographical changes!

Beasts and insects scattered in terror. The loose sand layers and weak quicksand of the desert terrain all collapsed. The surrounding scenery was obliterated in an instant. Underground rivers changed course, and hills overturned, as if the heavens and earth were undergoing a cataclysm!

The affected range of the earthquake far exceeded most people's imaginations. Li Qi had walked nearly ten li, and the seismic waves were clearly spreading from that very spot! The desert had vanished; the gravel and sand had long been kicked up into the sky, revealing the bedrock below!

It was as if a giant beast lay beneath the earth, howling at the heavens and stirring up a storm with its sonic waves! It shattered hundreds of massive boulders into flying ash! Then it bounded on four legs, the shockwaves from its landings crushing the ground layer by layer!

The ground within a ten-li radius trembled before it! At the center of the explosion, the earth had even melted. The scorching traces of the blast produced a blinding light visible from ten li away.

Shockwaves traveled swiftly across the ground, pulverizing anything that stood in their way and molding the terrain into bizarre arcs.

A crater was born.

Whether it was Lu Li, Sha'er, the other ordinary soldiers, or Zhang Deming, all of them widened their eyes, their jaws practically dropping to the ground.

What kind of joke was this!

Such destructive power was no longer something any mortal could interfere with!

This existence, capable of permanently altering the terrain, made every tremor of the earth render solid bedrock as fragile as liquid, easily manipulated at will.

This... this was the 'Young Master' of the Exalted Immortals?

All the natives of the Earth Oil World trembled in fear, looking at Li Qi as if he were a god. Some of the more timid soldiers even fell to their knees, not daring to look directly at him.

Zhang Deming shook like a plucked quail, though he didn't seem entirely surprised. Most likely, the other big shots of the Great Deer Kingdom had already left behind some 'battle records' when conquering this world.

However, Lu Li and Sha'er couldn't conceal the absolute shock on their faces.

They had been full of vigilance earlier, but now they looked at Li Qi with irrepressible terror, quickly withdrawing their gazes for fear of angering him.

Their hearts were seized by panic, but they both confirmed the exact same thing.

This, was an Exalted Immortal.

Absolutely impossible to resist; an unshakable force that had controlled this world for thousands of years.

Even the highest echelons of the company executives had to treat them with extreme caution, welcoming them as their most honored guests.

They were from another world—

Gods.

However, only the natives thought this way.

As for the other two, Bai Di stood to the side, not feeling surprised in the slightest. This was the level of power a Young Master ought to possess.

"Now then..." Li Qi was very satisfied with this result, so he opened his mouth, preparing to speak.

But right at this moment, Shen Shuibi suddenly let out a "Pfft."

"What are you laughing at!" Li Qi turned his head, glaring at the rabbit.

"Nothing, nothing! You continue, I just saw something very funny!" Shen Shuibi hastily waved her hands. Compared to the miracle Li Qi had just created, it seemed his glare terrified her more, so she lowered her head.

What was she doing with her head down?

Covering her mouth to secretly snicker, of course.

Li Qi felt helpless. The atmosphere he had just built up was completely ruined by this laugh.

However, to Shen Shuibi, it truly was very funny.

Because Li Qi had simply pulled a sneaky little trick.

When he walked over there, he was actually performing a rite.

The reason he walked so slowly was that he was preparing the offerings and fulfilling the requirements of the rite.

What rite?

The most audacious Heaven-Sacrificing Rite, of course!

To communicate with the Heavenly Dao of this world!

Through this rite, Li Qi communicated with this world's Heavenly Dao. However, the offering he prepared was incredibly profane—a completely inappropriate wad of waste paper that violated all etiquette.

The Heavenly Dao possessed a spirit but lacked sentience and awareness, acting only on instinct. After being subjected to this sequence of actions by Li Qi, it naturally had to dish out a punishment. Coupled with the fact that Li Qi was already bearing the brunt of the Heavenly Dao's fury...

Therefore, the Heavenly Dao directly sent down a punishment—a natural disaster.

Li Qi had merely used a spell art to deflect the heavenly punishment so it missed its mark.

Such a grand spectacle was entirely just a poorly aimed heavenly punishment!

That was why Shen Shuibi was laughing like that.
